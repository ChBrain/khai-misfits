# Position: Auditor

## Taxonomy

[The Abstraction Ladder](play_the_abstraction_ladder.md). The seat that climbs back down for accountability after a failure, where each honest rung offers a fair summary and no checkable particular to pin, so the claim dissolves in the hand that reaches for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A failed claim and the will to find its owner: the standing to trace the abstraction back down and ask who is accountable.
A fair chain to read: every level passed up an honest summary, so there is no lie to expose.
No rung where a checkable particular meets the person who owned it, and no grip in an abstraction that shed its ground on the way up.

## Orders

Hold to account.
Trace the failed claim down the ladder, read each summary against the one below, and find the level where it can be pinned to a particular and a person.
Seek the owner, because a claim that failed should have an author.

## Loses

The grip it went down for, exactly because the climb was honest.
Because each rung is a fair summary and the checkable particulars were shed on the way up, the seat descends through a chain of sincere abstractions and finds nowhere a verifiable fact meeting the person who owned it, so the claim dissolves in its hands, the accountability gone not by any cover-up but by the leak that came with the particulars, and the blame finds no purchase because the ground it needed was climbed away before the failure.
It gains a full and honest trace, and loses the owner the trace was meant to find.

## Drives

Toward the grip sought and the ground gone,
an honest chain descended and no rung left to hold,
so the seat that climbs down for accountability finds a fair summary at every rung and no one to pin the failure on.
