# Process: Slipping

## Taxonomy

[The Abstraction Ladder](play_the_abstraction_ladder.md). The accountability leaking out with the particulars, the grip that closes on air when a failed claim is traced down a chain of fair summaries and finds no rung to hold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A failed claim and an auditor climbing down for its owner.
At [the summit](place_the_summit.md) and down, the auditor tracing the abstraction rung by rung, looking for the level where it can be pinned.

## Direction

Toward the grip finding no purchase.
Because each rung is a fair summary and the checkable particulars were shed on the way up, slipping lets the failed claim dissolve as it is traced down, every level honest and none holding a verifiable fact met by its owner, so the blame slides through the whole chain and finds nowhere to hold, the accountability having leaked out with the particulars long before the failure came.

## Lever

The honesty of every rung.
Because no level lied and each passed up a fair account, slipping runs not on any cover-up but on the plain absence of a checkable particular anywhere the claim can be pinned, so the ungrippable claim is assembled from no concealment, only from a chain of sincere abstractions in which the ground that accountability needs was climbed away in good faith.

## Echo

A claim dissolved, and no owner found.
The auditor has traced an honest chain to its end, so what reads as no one being to blame is, in truth, the accountability shed rung by rung with the particulars, the failure real and the owner nowhere, not hidden but abstracted away.
