# Play: The Abstraction Ladder

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Abstraction Ladder.
There is a ladder in every report, and everyone climbs it, because they must. At the bottom is the concrete particular: this weld on this beam failed this shift, this customer waited forty minutes on Tuesday, this line of the ledger is a number you could go and check. And above it is the next rung up, the summary: welds are failing on the night shift, wait times are up, the region underperformed. And above that, higher: quality is slipping, service is degraded, the unit is challenged. And higher still, at the top, where the vocabulary is all strategy and alignment and headwinds and challenged and delivery: language so abstract it could mean almost anything, and commands the room precisely because it does. And climbing is not a fault. No one can carry every particular up a hierarchy: the top cannot read every weld report, so the specifics must be summarised, and summarising is abstracting, and abstracting up the ladder is how an organisation communicates at scale at all. So the report climbs, rung by rung, each level sincerely compressing what it got from below into something the level above can hold, and no one lies, no one hides, every summary is a fair account of the one beneath it. It is only that each rung up sheds the particulars, and the particulars were the checkable part. A weld you can inspect; a slipping quality you cannot. The number in the ledger you can verify; the challenged unit you cannot. So as the claim rises it gains authority, because the abstract sounds strategic and commands, and it sheds accountability, because there is nothing concrete left in it to hold anyone to. At the top the claim directs the whole enterprise and cannot be checked against a single fact, and no one can be pinned to it, because by the time it reached the summit it had been abstracted past the point where a name or a weld or a Tuesday still clung to it. And when something goes wrong and an auditor climbs back down looking for who is accountable, the claim dissolves in their hands: every level passed up a fair summary of the level below, each abstraction sincere, and nowhere is there a rung where a checkable particular meets a person who owned it, because the particulars were shed on the way up and the accountability leaked out with them. Nobody concealed anything. The reports were honest, the summaries fair, the abstraction necessary, and the top genuinely could not have run on the raw particulars. It is only that a claim high enough to command a hierarchy is a claim abstract enough that nothing checkable survives in it, so the language that lets the top act is the same language that lets no one be held, and the ladder every honest report must climb is the ladder accountability falls off.

## Arc

The bend is this: a report must climb the ladder of abstraction to be usable at the top, and each rung up sheds the particulars that made it checkable, so the higher a claim rises the more it commands and the less anyone can be held to it, accountability leaking out not by concealment but by the climb itself.
It runs on an abstraction that is necessary.
No hierarchy can carry every concrete particular upward: the top cannot read every weld report or every ledger line, so specifics must be summarised, and summarising is moving up the ladder from the concrete to the abstract (Hayakawa, on the abstraction ladder, language ascending from the particular thing to the general class). So the climb is not a fault but the only way an organisation communicates at scale, and this is what makes it a trap rather than a failure, because the abstraction the top needs is the abstraction that sheds the check.
And each rung up sheds the checkable particular. The concrete is what can be verified: a weld inspected, a number confirmed, a named customer on a dated Tuesday; the abstraction that summarises them, slipping quality or a challenged unit, cannot be checked against any single fact, because the facts were the particulars left on the rung below (Korzybski, on levels of abstraction, the map that omits more of the territory the higher it goes; Scott, on the legible summary that sheds the local particular knowledge). What climbs is fair, and what climbs is uncheckable.
And the abstract gains authority as it sheds the check. The higher, vaguer claim sounds strategic and commands the room, so the abstraction that can be held to nothing is exactly the language that directs the enterprise, psychological height and linguistic abstraction rising together (Trope and Liberman, on construal-level theory, distance construing the world in more abstract terms). The claim rules more the less it can be pinned, authority and unaccountability climbing the same ladder.
And accountability leaks out with the particulars. Because the checkable specifics are shed at each rung and the claim that reaches the top has nothing concrete left in it, no one can be held to what it says, so when an auditor climbs back down after a failure the claim dissolves in their hands, every level a fair summary of the one below and nowhere a rung where a checkable particular meets the person who owned it (Wendell Johnson, on dead-level abstracting, high-level language that never cashes out in referents). The blame finds no purchase, because the ground it needed was shed on the way up.
And no one concealed a thing. The reports were honest, the summaries fair, the abstraction necessary, and the top genuinely could not run on the raw particulars, so the accountability leaves by the climb and not by any hand: it is undeliberate, the shedding a property of the ladder and not a choice to hide. What each rung did in good faith, summarise for the level above, all rungs did to the enterprise, float its command free of its ground.
Descend the ladder: demand the concrete particular under the abstract claim, sample the ground the summary stands on, and hold the report to a checkable fact and a name, and the accountability can be kept on the ground: but this means reading the particulars the abstraction was built to spare the top, distrusting a claim that sounds strategic because it sounds strategic, going down to the weld and the Tuesday when the summit runs on slipping-quality and challenged-unit, and being the one who drags the clean high language back to the weeds it rose above, against every reason the ladder was climbed, so the ground is held only by paying, continuously, the cost of reading the particulars the climb existed to shed.

## Stakes

Whether a report can stay checkable as it climbs, when the top can only act on the abstract and the abstract is where the check falls off.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the claim abstracted upward, which the Reporter must do and the Principal rewards, and away from the particular held on the ground, which would ask the top to read the specifics the climb existed to spare it and to distrust the strategic language it runs on. The production cannot make the abstract carry its check, because a claim high enough to command a hierarchy is abstract enough that nothing verifiable survives in it, and the particulars that would hold it are exactly what each rung must shed to be usable above, so the leak is the ladder working as a ladder must, not a concealment: no one hides anything and every summary is fair. It can only show that the reports were honest and the abstraction necessary and the top genuinely could not run on the raw particulars, that the claim gained its authority and lost its accountability on the very same rungs, and that the one move that keeps the ground, descending the ladder to the concrete particular and the name, must be made by reading the specifics the climb was built to shed and dragging the clean high language back to the weeds, against every reason it rose, so the report is honest and the abstraction is needed and the command that runs the enterprise is the claim that no one can be held to.
