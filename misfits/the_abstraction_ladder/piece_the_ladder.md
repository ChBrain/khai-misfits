# Piece: The Ladder

## Taxonomy

[The Abstraction Ladder](play_the_abstraction_ladder.md). The rungs from the concrete particular up to the highest abstraction, the climb every report must make and the climb accountability falls off.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Stands from [the ground](place_the_ground.md) up to [the summit](place_the_summit.md), each rung a level of the hierarchy.
Descended at [the sounding](place_the_sounding.md), where a claim is dropped back toward the concrete.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no climb and no shedding, and a report stays as concrete and as checkable as the ground it came from.
With it the trap has its structure: a ladder from the particular weld up to the abstract challenged, each rung more general and less checkable than the one below, that every report must climb to be usable at the top (Hayakawa, on the abstraction ladder, language ascending from the concrete thing to the general class; Korzybski, on levels of abstraction). The Ladder is load bearing because it is the mechanism itself: the rungs on which a claim gains generality and loses ground, so the piece carries the necessary ascent that makes a report usable above and uncheckable there, the same steps up carrying the authority higher and the accountability off.

## Apparent

Just how reporting works, the natural chain of summary from floor to board, nothing but communication at scale.
Seen at the surface, the Ladder looks like plain organisational plumbing, information flowing upward as it must, and the truth that each rung sheds the checkable particular, that the climb which makes a claim usable is the climb that makes it unholdable, that authority and unaccountability rise the same steps, stays hidden behind how ordinary and necessary passing a summary upward looks.

## Yearbook

Climbed by [the reporter](persona_the_reporter.md) through [abstracting](process_abstracting.md), descended by [the grounder](persona_the_grounder.md) through [grounding](process_grounding.md), the rungs the report rises on.
It is the mechanism of the trap: the Ladder turns [the particulars](piece_the_particulars.md) into [the summary](piece_the_summary.md), lifts it into [the command](piece_the_command.md), and sheds the ground so [the grip](piece_the_grip.md) finds nothing, the ascent at the centre of [the cloud](piece_the_cloud.md).
