# Persona: The Auditor

## Taxonomy

[Auditor](position_auditor.md). The one who climbs back down after a failure to find who is accountable, and finds each rung a fair summary of the one below and no rung where a checkable particular meets the person who owned it, the claim dissolving in their hands.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Diligent. After the fact. Looking for the grip.
Is the one who seeks accountability when something goes wrong, the reviewer, the investigator, the board asking who is responsible for the claim that failed.
No zeal to blame in it: the Auditor wants only to find who owned the decision and hold them to it, the reasonable wish that a failed claim have an author.

## Action

Traces the failed abstract claim back down the ladder, rung by rung, looking for the level where it can be pinned to a particular and a person.
Reads each summary against the one below and asks who is accountable for what was said.
Does what an auditor does: it climbs down for the grip, and finds, at each rung, a fair summary and no checkable particular to hold.

## Shadow

Not thwarted by any cover-up, a diligent tracer finding only honest rungs, and that is the whole difficulty: nothing was concealed, and the accountability is gone all the same.
Because each level passed up a fair summary and the checkable particulars were shed on the way, the Auditor descends through a chain of honest abstractions and finds nowhere a rung where a verifiable fact meets the person who owned it, so the claim dissolves in their hands, the blame finding no purchase not because anyone hid it but because the ground it needed was climbed away, the accountability having leaked out with the particulars long before the failure.
It goes down for the grip in good faith, and finds that the honest climb left nothing to grip.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "everyone up the chain reported fairly, and no one is accountable."
Says it having found no lie and no owner, and cannot point to the rung where the claim became someone's, because there is none: it was abstract at every level that mattered.
