# Piece: The One-Time Gain

## Taxonomy

[Seed Corn](play_seed_corn.md). The asset sold, and its proceeds worn as performance.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the estate](place_the_estate.md), in the sale of the thing that used to earn its keep.
The family silver turned to cash: the building sold and leased back, the division divested, the patent licensed away, the stake cashed in. A lump of proceeds that lands in the period as if it were the fruit of the period's work.

## Load Bearing

Without it a shortfall too large to cut or pull must simply be missed.
With it no shortfall need ever be missed, because the One-Time Gain lets the outfit sell a piece of itself and book the price as a result: a productive asset, which would have earned a little every year, is exchanged for a single sum that makes this one year, and the future stream it would have thrown off is gone with it. It is the deepest harvest, spending not next quarter's demand but the earning base itself, and it can be done only once per asset, so it buys the biggest present at the cost of the most future.

## Apparent

Unlocking value and sharpening focus.
It looks like shrewd portfolio management: monetising an underused asset, shedding a non-core unit, releasing capital tied up in bricks. The story is plausible and sometimes even true, which is the cover, because a sale that funds the future and a sale that merely feeds the quarter look the same in the period they close, and only the years after show which it was.

## Yearbook

Made by [the stripper](persona_the_stripper.md) through [divestment](process_divestment.md), counted as a triumph and never repeatable.
It is the harvest of last resort: the One-Time Gain converts [the seed corn](piece_the_seed_corn.md) at its most fundamental, the earning base itself, into [the number](piece_the_number.md), and leaves [the bare cupboard](piece_the_bare_cupboard.md) not just short of a crop but short of the field.
