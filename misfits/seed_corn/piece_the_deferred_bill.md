# Piece: The Deferred Bill

## Taxonomy

[Seed Corn](play_seed_corn.md). The future cost of a present gain, real and not yet due.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Between [the quarter](place_the_quarter.md) and [the handover](place_the_handover.md), in the gap where a cost is owed but not yet posted.
The invoice with a later date: the breakdown the deferred maintenance will cause, the lost sales after the channel is stuffed, the earnings the sold asset will no longer bring, the product gap the cut research leaves. Certain, and simply not here yet.

## Load Bearing

Without it eating the seed would show its cost at once and no one would do it.
With it eating the seed shows only its gain, because the Deferred Bill is a liability the present-tense sheet does not carry: the harvest posts its benefit immediately and its cost on a future page, so at the moment of the decision the ledger shows profit and no offsetting loss, and a rational reader of that ledger harvests. It is the accounting shadow of time itself, the reason a real cost can be invisible, and it is what makes every seed-corn choice look like free money in the quarter it is made.

## Apparent

No cost at all.
It looks like the gain came clean, because on the day it is booked there is nothing on the other side of it: the maintenance not done costs nothing this month, the pulled-forward sale returns nothing this quarter, the sold asset's lost yield is a subtraction from years that have not started. The absence of a visible cost reads as the absence of a cost, and the bill sits quiet until it comes due on someone else's watch.

## Yearbook

Run up by every harvest, and presented, at last, to [the heir](persona_the_heir.md) in [the handover](place_the_handover.md).
It is the hinge between gain and ruin: the Deferred Bill is what lets [the number](piece_the_number.md) be made from [the seed corn](piece_the_seed_corn.md) at no apparent cost, and it is the same bill, matured, that empties [the bare cupboard](piece_the_bare_cupboard.md).
