# Play: Seed Corn

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Seed Corn.
A quarter delivered exactly on target, every year, by whatever it takes ... and what it takes is next year's crop: the research cut, the upkeep deferred, the demand pulled forward, the asset sold, the future eaten to feed the present, until a winter arrives that there is no seed left to plant.

## Arc

The bend is this: measure only the present, and you get the present, bought with the future.
It runs on a true number and a hidden bill.
You get what you measure, and this period is what is measured, so the number is real, made in full, and honestly reported. Nothing on the sheet is false.
But the ways to make the number when the work alone will not are all withdrawals from tomorrow: cut the research and book the saving, defer the maintenance and pocket the gap, pull next quarter's sales into this one, sell the asset and call the proceeds performance. Each is a present gain paid for by a future loss that is real but not yet due, and so does not show.
The one who makes the number is rewarded and moves on. The one who inherits the depleted base pays the winter, and by then the seed is gone and the person who ate it is somewhere else, congratulated.
Measure the future too, and put its cost on today's sheet, and eating the seed stops paying: what you measure is the story.

## Triggers

**[Number](plot_number.md)**
The quarter is what is scored, and the quarter is what is rewarded. The Scorer measures on the period, the Deliverer is told to hit it, and the work alone will not get there. Closes when the present is all that is measured and the number must be made regardless.

**[Cut](plot_cut.md)**
Cue: the shortfall needs closing, and the fastest saving is a future one. The Pruner cuts the research and defers the upkeep, booking the gap as this year's saving. Closes when the seed corn is eaten and the lab goes dark, and the number is made.

**[Pull Forward](plot_pull_forward.md)**
Cue: more is needed than the saving gives. The Harvester pulls next quarter's demand into this one, and the Stripper sells the asset and calls the proceeds performance. Closes when tomorrow's revenue and the family silver are booked as today's result.

**[Bare Cupboard](plot_bare_cupboard.md)**
Cue: the winter arrives, and the person who made the numbers is gone. The Heir opens the books and finds the base depleted, the deferred bill now due, the cupboard bare. Closes when the future is named as the thing that was spent ... or the same number is made the same way again.

**[Long View](plot_long_view.md)**
Cue: someone puts the future on today's sheet and measures it too. The Steward rewards the base kept whole, not just the quarter hit, and eating the seed stops paying. Closes when the cost of the future is made visible now ... or the long view is too slow to reward, and the seed is eaten again.

## Stakes

Whether the future's cost is ever put on the present's sheet.
Every plot moves the same way: toward the number, which is measured and paid now, and away from the base, whose loss is real and deferred and lands on someone else. The production cannot make a manager value what no one measures. It can only show that no one lied, that every number was made in full and reported true, and that the one figure that would have stopped the harvest, what it cost the future to make the present, was the one figure the sheet was never built to carry.
