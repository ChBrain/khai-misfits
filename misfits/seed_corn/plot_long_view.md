# Plot: Long View

## Taxonomy

[Seed Corn](play_seed_corn.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The horizon](place_the_horizon.md), and someone who has stopped scoring the quarter alone.
The question has lengthened: not what did we deliver this period, but what did delivering it cost the years to come, and how would the sheet read if that cost were on it.

## Action

[Stewardship](process_stewardship.md): [The Steward](persona_the_steward.md), holding [the custodian's seat](position_custodian.md), puts the future onto today's measure, rewarding the base kept whole and the horizon held, not just the number hit.

## Stage

[The Steward](persona_the_steward.md) tables [Measure the Future](plan_measure_the_future.md): price the harvest on the sheet where it is made, hold the maker to the year the bill matures, and reward the crop grown over the crop eaten, so [the short-termism](piece_the_short_termism.md) loses its footing and feeding the quarter from the larder stops paying.
With the future on the measure, [the seed corn](piece_the_seed_corn.md) is worth more planted than eaten, and the knife goes back in the drawer.

## Tension

The seed is spared, and the fast, clean reward goes with it.
Measuring the future works, and it costs: the long view is slower to score, harder to define, and easier to argue than a quarter's earnings, and it defers the manager's reward to a horizon they may not be standing on to collect. It trades a crisp, immediate number everyone understood for a patient, contestable one that asks people to be paid later for a base kept whole, and that trade runs against the grain of every quarter ... or the long view is judged too slow and too soft, the sheet reverts to the period alone, and the seed is eaten again.
