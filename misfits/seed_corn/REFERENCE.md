# Seed Corn: Reference

The research warrant for [Seed Corn](play_seed_corn.md). The title names the behaviour;
the play runs on the economics of managerial short-termism and the survey evidence on earnings
management, from Narayanan's and Stein's models of myopia to the CFO studies of real activities
sacrificed for the quarter, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging short-termism as a **systemic system**: when only the near term is measured and rewarded,
managers spend the far term to deliver it, so a firm consumes its own future to keep hitting numbers
that were never false, only short, and without a single fraud or fool. The play models the documented
mechanics, the present-tense measure that is all that pays, the future value stored as investment,
demand, and assets, the cut that books a saving by stopping the future, the demand pulled forward
across the period line, the one-time gain from selling the earning base, the deferred bill the current
sheet does not carry, and the longer measure that alone corrects it, as the load-bearing vectors of
the structure, not as a tale of greed. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's
governing law: you get what you measure, so a firm that measures and rewards the present alone gets
the present, bought with a future whose cost its measure was never built to show.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a fraud or a fool, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the
  scorer is doing plain accountability, the pruner's cut is applauded thrift, the harvester is closing
  deals, the stripper is unlocking value, and every number is real and reported true. The misfit is
  that a measure of the present alone rewards eating the future exactly as if it were grown.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into managers simply taking the long view on
  their own. Measuring the future can end the harvest, but only by trading a crisp, immediate number
  everyone understood for a slower, contestable one that defers the maker's reward to a horizon they
  may not be present to collect, so the reckoning is a fast clean payoff traded for a patient one, not
  a firm that grew patient by itself.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, from the myopia
  models to the CFO survey.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Seed Corn](piece_the_seed_corn.md)**: carries the stored future value (Laverty), investment, demand, and
  assets that can be eaten now or grown later.
- **[The Number](piece_the_number.md)**: carries the present-tense measure (Stein; Graham et al.), the current
  period that is all that is rewarded.
- **[The Cut](piece_the_cut.md)**: carries the sacrifice of real investment (Graham et al.), R&D and maintenance
  stopped and booked as a saving.
- **[The Pull-Forward](piece_the_pull_forward.md)**: carries demand borrowed across periods (practitioner), next
  quarter's sales pulled into this one.
- **[The One-Time Gain](piece_the_one_time_gain.md)**: carries the earning base sold (practitioner), a productive
  asset traded for a single sum worn as performance.
- **[The Deferred Bill](piece_the_deferred_bill.md)**: carries the future cost of a present gain (Stein), real, not
  yet due, and absent from the current sheet.
- **[The Bare Cupboard](piece_the_bare_cupboard.md)**: carries the depleted base the successor inherits (Narayanan),
  the winter after the seed is eaten.
- **[The Short-Termism](piece_the_short_termism.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Narayanan; Stein; Laverty), and
  its remedy in a longer measure (practitioner), the future brought onto the sheet that had only the present.
