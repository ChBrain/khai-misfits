# Play: The Bounce

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Bounce.
A result swings to an extreme, mostly on luck, and then does what extremes do and drifts back toward its true level ... and whoever acted at the bottom takes the credit for the rise, and whoever praised at the top takes the blame for the fall, so the wrong lesson is learned every single time, from data that is entirely real.

## Arc

The bend is this: react to an extreme and the mean will hand you a false lesson, dressed as proof.
It runs on a level and its noise.
Every performance has a true mean and a scatter of luck around it, so an unusually bad result is mostly a bad draw and an unusually good one mostly a good draw, and either way the next result will sit nearer the middle, all on its own, with nothing done at all.
But we do not act in the middle, we act at the extremes: we intervene when things are worst and celebrate when they are best. So the intervention at the trough is followed by a rise it did not cause, and the praise at the peak is followed by a fall it did not cause, and the eye that cannot see the counterfactual reads the sequence as cause.
From that, backwards: toughness works, because we got tough at the bottom and things improved; encouragement fails, because we praised at the top and things got worse. We learn to punish, distrust reward, hire the rescuer who merely timed the trough, and fire the one the slump landed on.
Judge the trend, not the extreme, and the bounce loses its witness: the mean is the story.

## Triggers

**[Extreme](plot_extreme.md)**
A performance swings well off its usual level, mostly on luck: a dreadful quarter, or a dazzling one. The Performer does the work, and the scoreboard shows an outlier that begs to be acted on. Closes when an extreme result stands on the board, and the true mean behind it is unseen.

**[React](plot_react.md)**
Cue: no one acts in the middle, everyone acts at the edge. The Boss comes down hard on the slump, the Axe fires the one it landed on, the Rescuer is hired at the bottom, and the peak is praised. Closes when the intervention lands exactly at the extreme, where reversion was already coming.

**[Bounce](plot_bounce.md)**
Cue: the extreme drifts back toward the mean, on its own, as extremes do. The rise after the trough and the fall after the peak arrive, and the intervention that happened to sit before them takes the credit or the blame. Closes when the reversion is read as the fruit of the action.

**[False Lesson](plot_false_lesson.md)**
Cue: the sequence is banked as cause. The Analyst concludes toughness works and praise backfires, and it hardens into policy, while the Doubter names the mean and is waved off. Closes when the backwards lesson is learned as proof ... or someone asks what would have happened anyway.

**[Control](plot_control.md)**
Cue: someone judges the trend instead of the extreme, and holds a group back to see what reversion does alone. The Designer runs a control, and the bounce is seen for what it is. Closes when the counterfactual is finally in view ... or the control is too slow and too cold, and the next trough is met with the same tough hand.

## Stakes

Whether anyone ever sees what would have happened anyway.
Every plot moves the same way: toward the extreme, which is where we act and where reversion is already waiting, and away from the mean, which is silent and does the real work. The production cannot make a counterfactual visible to an eye that only sees what did happen. It can only show that no one was fooled by a lie, that every number was real and every sequence honestly observed, and that the one fact that would have dissolved the lesson, what the result would have done untouched, is the one fact acting at the extreme guarantees no one will ever have.
