# Plot: False Lesson

## Taxonomy

[The Bounce](play_the_bounce.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The review](place_the_review.md), the sequence written up and the moral drawn.
The pattern has repeated enough to feel like law: act hard at the bottom and things rise, praise at the top and things fall, and someone is ready to bank what that plainly means.

## Action

[Entrenchment](process_entrenchment.md): [The Analyst](persona_the_analyst.md), holding [the learner's seat](position_learner.md), draws the causal rule from the credited reversions and hardens it into how the place is run, while [The Doubter](persona_the_doubter.md) at [the statistician's seat](position_statistician.md) points at the mean and is waved off.

## Stage

[The Analyst](persona_the_analyst.md) tables [Bank the Lesson](plan_bank_the_lesson.md): conclude that toughness works and praise backfires, so [the false lesson](piece_the_false_lesson.md) becomes policy, drawn straight from the data and backwards about the world.
[The Doubter](persona_the_doubter.md) tables [Name the Mean](plan_name_the_mean.md): say that the trough would have risen anyway, and is dismissed for doubting plain, repeated, hard-won experience, so [the regression](piece_the_regression.md) is mistaken for a management philosophy.

## Tension

The lesson is confirmed by all the evidence, and it is exactly wrong.
The analyst is not careless: the pattern is real, consistent, and reproduced every time it is tested, because regression will oblige for as long as anyone acts at extremes. And the doubter is right and unwelcome, because naming the mean asks a room full of experienced people to distrust the very thing they trust most, their own repeated observation, in favour of a rise no one can point to. So the harsh rule hardens, the unlucky are punished and the deserving mistrusted, and every future application will confirm it again.
