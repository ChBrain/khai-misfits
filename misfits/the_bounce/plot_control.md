# Plot: Control

## Taxonomy

[The Bounce](play_the_bounce.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The control](place_the_control.md), and someone who has stopped judging the last result and started judging the trend.
The question has changed: not did it improve after we acted, but what would it have done if we had not, and how could we ever see that.

## Action

[Control](process_control.md): [The Designer](persona_the_designer.md), holding [the experimenter's seat](position_experimenter.md), holds a group of troughs back untouched, weighs the base rate, and plots the trend across many results rather than the last one.

## Stage

[The Designer](persona_the_designer.md) tables [Run a Control](plan_run_a_control.md): leave some extremes unacted-on, so the untreated ones can be laid beside the treated, and [the missing counterfactual](piece_the_missing_counterfactual.md) is finally supplied.
Against it, [the regression](piece_the_regression.md) shows plain: the untouched troughs rise almost as much as the treated ones, and the intervention's true effect is revealed as small or none, the bounce unmasked as reversion.

## Tension

The truth comes into view, and the flattering certainty goes with it.
Building the counterfactual works, and it costs: it is slow, it means leaving some bad results alone to see what they do, it feels like negligence to sit on your hands at a trough, and it takes away the fast, vindicating story that acting at the extreme always supplied. It trades a warm, self-confirming philosophy of toughness for a cold reading that says most of what we were proud of was the mean coming home ... or the control is judged too slow and too heartless, the trend is ignored for the next vivid extreme, and the tough hand reaches out again.
