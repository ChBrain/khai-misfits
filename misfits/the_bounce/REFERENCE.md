# The Bounce: Reference

The research warrant for [The Bounce](play_the_bounce.md). The title names the reversion;
the play runs on the statistics of regression to the mean and the psychology of mistaking it for
cause, from Galton's discovery through Kahneman and Tversky's work on prediction to the
program-evaluation literature on regression artifacts, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging regression to the mean as a **systemic system**: extremes revert toward the mean on their
own, people act only at extremes, and the reversion is credited to the action, so a purely
statistical fact becomes a causal lesson learned backwards, without a single liar or fool. The play
models the documented mechanics, the true mean and the noise around it, the extreme that triggers the
reaction and guarantees the retreat, the bounce that owes nothing to any action, the credit that
misattributes it, the false lesson drawn in the direction of harshness, the missing counterfactual
that could have sized the effect, and the control that alone restores it, as the load-bearing vectors
of the structure, not as a tale of stupidity. The governing finding of the source literature is the
play's governing law: because we intervene at the extremes, where reversion is already coming, reward
is reliably followed by decline and punishment by improvement, so an honest observer learns, correctly
from the data and backwards about the world, that punishment works and reward does not.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any seat
  as a liar or a fool, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the boss acts
  where the need is greatest, the analyst reads a real and repeated pattern, the rescuer's recovery
  genuinely occurred, and every number is honest. The misfit is that acting at the extreme, where
  reversion is already coming, makes the mean's free work look like the fruit of the action.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into people simply reasoning better. The bounce
  is unmasked only by building the counterfactual the extreme destroys, holding some cases back
  untouched and judging the trend, which is slow, feels like negligence at a trough, and takes away the
  fast, vindicating story, so the reckoning is a flattering certainty traded for a cold reading, not an
  observer who saw through it unaided.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, from Galton's heights to
  the flight-instructor's landings.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Mean](piece_the_mean.md)**: carries the true underlying level (Galton), the centre every extreme reverts
  toward, present in every result and visible in none.
- **[The Noise](piece_the_noise.md)**: carries the luck component (Tversky & Kahneman), large in any single result
  and mistaken for signal.
- **[The Extreme](piece_the_extreme.md)**: carries the outlier (Galton; Tversky & Kahneman), the result made mostly of
  noise that triggers the reaction and guarantees the retreat.
- **[The Bounce](piece_the_bounce.md)**: carries regression to the mean in motion (Galton), the free reversion no
  action caused.
- **[The Credit](piece_the_credit.md)**: carries the misattribution (Kahneman & Tversky), a real reversion booked to
  the intervention that preceded it.
- **[The False Lesson](piece_the_false_lesson.md)**: carries the backwards causal rule (Kahneman & Tversky), punishment
  taught to help and reward to hurt, self-confirming with experience.
- **[The Missing Counterfactual](piece_the_missing_counterfactual.md)**: carries the unobserved untreated case (Campbell & Kenny),
  the comparison the extreme destroys and only a control restores.
- **[The Regression](piece_the_regression.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Galton; Kahneman & Tversky), and its
  remedy in the counterfactual (Campbell & Kenny), the fact that had to be measured to unmask the lesson.
