# Place: The Impasse

## Taxonomy

[No One in Charge](play_no_one_in_charge.md). The scene where the demand for an accountable answer meets the group's sincere denial that anyone holds power, and the remedy is refused.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A member asking who decided this and how to join the deciding, and a group answering, honestly, that no one is in charge.
Shown as a good-faith exchange going nowhere, a fair question met with a true reply that resolves nothing, the two talking past each other in perfect sincerity.

## Holds

Holds the deadlock, the collision of a real exclusion with a denied structure.
The Impasse is where the disclaimer does its work: the demand to name and check the power meets the group's belief that there is none to name, so there is no office to point the questioner at and no leader to hold, and the call to formalise runs into the wall of the ideal.

## Offers

Offers the group its clean conscience, and the questioner nothing to grip.
Here the answer that no one leads is both true and total, so the group is offered the reassurance that it has done no wrong, and the questioner is offered no lever, no name, and no door, only the truth that closes the case against them.

## Withheld

Withholds the one thing that would move it: an admission that a structure exists.
What the Impasse hides is that the deadlock breaks only if the group will name its own informal power, which it cannot do without conceding the hierarchy it denies, so the scene withholds the very acknowledgement the remedy depends on, and the standoff holds because to end it the group must give up believing itself open.
