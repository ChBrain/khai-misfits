# Persona: The Outsider

## Taxonomy

[The Sidelines](position_the_sidelines.md). Cannot enter the informal elite by any formal route and cannot hold it to account, since the group insists it does not exist, and asks in vain who decides.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Newer. Unconnected. Feeling a wall they cannot point to.
Is the one who came without the friendships, the shared history, or the ease in the room, and finds that the decisions are made somewhere they are not, by people they cannot name.
No bitterness wished on them: they ask only for a way in and a say, wanting the openness the group promised, and their difficulty is not resentment but a door that is not there.

## Action

Looks for the office to stand for, the vote to win, the person to petition, and finds none, so their bid for entry and for answers meets only an open floor that is not open to them.
Answers the group's own promise of equality by trying to claim it, and runs into the informal wall, since there is no formal route into a power that has no form, and no one to appeal to in a group that says no one leads.
Does what a shut-out member does: asks who decided this and how one joins the deciding, and is told, honestly, that no one is in charge and the floor is open to all, which answers nothing and names no one.

## Shadow

Not a malcontent, but the one whose exclusion is invisible because the group believes its floor is open, and that is the bind: they are kept out by a structure no one admits exists, so their very complaint sounds like sour grapes against a group that has no leaders to resent.
Because the Outsider faces an informal elite with no formal door, they cannot earn their way in by any rule or unseat anyone by any vote, and cannot even make the case that they are excluded, since the group's sincere belief in its openness makes the wall unnameable, felt by the one outside and denied by everyone in.
The exclusion is real and it is deniable, so the one shut out of an open group is the one with no lever to open it, barred by a hierarchy that answers every objection with the truth that it does not exist.

## Tell

Says "who decided this, and how do I get a say?"
Is asking the one question the structure cannot answer, and cannot get past the reply that no one is in charge, so the plain request for a door and an account meets a group that, believing itself open, cannot see the wall the Outsider has walked into.
