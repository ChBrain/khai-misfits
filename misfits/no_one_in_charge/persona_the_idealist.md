# Persona: The Idealist

## Taxonomy

[The Open Floor](position_the_open_floor.md). Refuses formal structure to protect an equal, open group, in good faith, and unknowingly clears the ground the informal elite will fill.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Principled. Egalitarian. Done with hierarchy.
Is the one who forms the group against the structures that crushed them elsewhere and resolves to have none: no chairs, no offices, no rules, so the floor is open and no one gives orders.
No naivety wished on them: the ideal is real and the motive is equality, a genuine refusal of the offices and hierarchies that have so often silenced people, meant with full sincerity.

## Action

Refuses the formal structure, keeping the group open and leaderless, and trusts that without offices there can be no bosses.
Answers the memory of hierarchy, not the shape of what replaces it, so the refusal is aimed at the visible power they have known and blind to the invisible power that a vacuum invites.
Does what a person burned by structure does: tears out the offices and rules to keep anyone from lording it over the rest, no thought that the absence of a named leader is not the absence of a leader.

## Shadow

Not a fool, but the one whose sincere refusal clears the ground for what they most oppose, and that is the ache of it: the ideal is equality, and the means to it installs an inequality they cannot see.
Because the Idealist mistakes formal structure for power itself, they abolish the offices and believe the power gone, but power only changes clothes, so the floor they threw open fills with an informal elite, and the very sincerity of the ideal blinds them to the hierarchy forming in its name.
The aim is equality and the result is a hidden rank, so the one who most wanted no leaders is the one who cleared the room for leaders no one can see.

## Tell

Says "we have no leaders here; everyone is equal and the floor is open to all."
Is telling the plain truth of the group's design and cannot see that the design does not hold, that a floor declared open is not an open floor, so the sincerity that means every word is exactly what keeps them from noticing who has quietly taken charge.
