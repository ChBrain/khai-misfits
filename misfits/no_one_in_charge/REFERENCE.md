# No One in Charge: Reference

The research warrant for [No One in Charge](play_no_one_in_charge.md).
The dynamic is the tyranny of structurelessness: Freeman's naming of it, the impossibility of a structureless group, the
oligarchy that shadows it, the informal domination it rests on, and the recurrence it shows, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the tyranny of structurelessness as a **refused-structure-goes-informal structure**: a group forms against
hierarchy and refuses formal structure to protect its openness, but because no group is truly structureless a structure
forms anyway, informal and unwritten, so an elite coalesces from friendship, confidence, time, and presence, makes the
real decisions where the meeting cannot follow, and, because the group has declared that no one leads, cannot be named,
questioned, or replaced. The play models the documented mechanics, the openness the refusal means to protect, the
informal sway the vacuum installs, the disclaimer of leadership that shields it, and the hidden hierarchy that no vote
convened and no rule governs, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a coup or a schemer. The
governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: refusing formal structure does not abolish power but drives
it into an informal, self-selected elite that is harder to check than a named hierarchy precisely because the group
denies it exists, so the openness meant for all becomes a closed room, and the only remedy is to formalise the
structure, at the cost of the openness it was adopted to protect.

It is distinct from the house's Vetocracy, where a surplus of formal veto points leaves a system able to block but
never act: there the trap is too much visible structure, many hands each able to say no, while here it is the refusal
of structure driving power off the record, the opposite motion, an absence of formal authority filled by an informal
one. It is distinct too from Pournelle's Iron Law, where a formal organisation is captured by those devoted to the
organisation over those devoted to its mission: there an organisation exists and is turned to its own maintenance,
while here no formal organisation exists at all, and the elite forms in the vacuum the refusal leaves, its power
informal from the start and denied rather than entrenched in office.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the informal elite as a
  clique of schemers or the group as dupes, because the source finding is precisely that no coup and no scheme is needed:
  the group refuses structure in sincere pursuit of equality, and the informal power forms from friendship, confidence,
  and presence, gathered by no vote and intended by no one. The insider only speaks up and shows up, and the group
  genuinely believes itself leaderless, so the misfit is that the refusal of formal structure installs an informal power
  that the sincere denial of leadership then shields from account, no ambition and no seizure required. The elite's sway
  is a fact of the vacuum, not a plot worked on the group, and the idealist who wanted the open floor is as blameless as
  the insider who filled it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into an openness that stays open by good faith. The one move that
  would make the power answerable is to formalise the structure, to build the offices, rules, and votes that name who
  decides and let them be checked, but to those who fled hierarchy that looks like rebuilding the thing they left, so it
  is refused, or resented, as a betrayal of the ideal, even though the democratic structuring the source proposes,
  delegated, rotated, and accountable, is meant to serve the equality structurelessness only promised rather than to
  restore the domination they fled. The reckoning is a structure named and checkable at the cost of the group's prized
  structurelessness, or that structurelessness kept at the cost of a power no one can reach, not a way to have an
  unstructured floor and an answerable leader at once, so the cure for a hidden hierarchy is the loss of the very
  structurelessness the group was built to protect.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the tyranny of structurelessness and its remedy
  of democratic structuring are Freeman's, the impossibility of a structureless group is Homans's, the tendency of even
  democratic bodies to oligarchy is Michels's, the informal domination of the articulate in consensus settings is
  Mansbridge's, and the recurrence across participatory movements is Polletta's, each carried as carefully as the finding
  itself: the misfit stages a sincere refusal of structure installing an unaccountable elite and credits that no one
  seized power and the denial of it was honestly meant.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Openness](piece_the_openness.md)**: carries the ideal the refusal means to protect (Freeman; Homans), the
  equal, unstructured floor that a structureless group cannot in fact sustain.
- **[The Sway](piece_the_sway.md)**: carries the informal power that fills the vacuum (Mansbridge), the
  influence gathered by friendship and confidence rather than by any vote.
- **[The Disclaimer](piece_the_disclaimer.md)**: carries the denial of leadership that shields the elite (Freeman), the
  sincere claim that no one is in charge which armours the ones who are.
- **[The Hidden Hierarchy](piece_the_hidden_hierarchy.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Freeman; Polletta), the
  informal, unaccountable structure that forms when the formal kind is refused and cannot be reached because it is
  denied.
