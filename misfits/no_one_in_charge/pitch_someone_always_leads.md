# Pitch: Someone Always Leads

## Taxonomy

[No One in Charge](play_no_one_in_charge.md). The single hook: refusing leaders does not abolish leadership, it only makes it informal, and informal power, denied by the group, cannot be held to account.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A group that prides itself on having no leaders, no offices, no one giving orders, open and equal by design.
The tenor is a sincere ideal: hierarchy has crushed too much, so the group refuses structure to keep the floor open to all, and means every word of it.

## Undertow

A structureless group is impossible, so the structure just goes unwritten.
Underneath, wherever people cooperate a structure forms, of friendship and confidence and presence, so refusing the formal kind installs an informal one, an elite gathered by no vote from who knows whom and who speaks well, making the real decisions where the meeting cannot see.

## Nerve

The denial is the armour: you cannot hold to account a leader who can truthfully say they hold no office.
The nerve is that the group's sincere belief in its own leaderlessness is exactly what shields the informal power, since the ones who lead were never named as leaders, never voted in, and cannot be voted out, their sway the safer for being honestly disowned.

## Echo

The only way to make the power answerable is to rebuild the structure the group formed to escape.
What lingers is that the cure is the founding wound reopened: to name and formalise the hierarchy is to trade the prized openness for accountability and, to the founders, to become the thing they fled, so the group must choose between an ideal it cannot check and a structure it did not want.
