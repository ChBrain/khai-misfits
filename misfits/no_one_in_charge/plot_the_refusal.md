# Plot: The Refusal

## Taxonomy

[No One in Charge](play_no_one_in_charge.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Meeting](place_the_meeting.md), a group forming against hierarchy with the offices still to be refused.
A room resolved on equality, remembering the structures that silenced people elsewhere and wanting none of them.

## Action

[Refusing](process_refusing.md): the group tears out chairs, offices, and rules to keep the floor open and no one in command.

## Stage

[The Idealist](persona_the_idealist.md), on [Refuse the Structure](plan_refuse_the_structure.md), refuses formal structure to protect [the openness](piece_the_openness.md), believing that where there is no named leader there can be no leader.
The refusal is sincere and the ideal is real, and clearing the offices clears, unseen, the ground an informal power will fill.

## Tension

Tearing out the structure that crushed you is the liberating act, and the liberating act removes the only tools that would keep the next power in view.
There is no folly: the Idealist means the equality, aims the refusal at the visible hierarchy they have suffered, and is right that offices can dominate. But structure is not the same as power, so removing the offices removes the means of naming power, not power itself, and the plot closes with the floor thrown open and the group believing itself equal, the sincere refusal having prepared a vacuum it cannot see and cannot govern.
