# Position: The Treasury

## Taxonomy

[The Last Layer](play_the_last_layer.md). The funding seat, where the standing cost of the whole apparatus falls due and the surplus is spent to hold up what earlier hands built.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A revenue, and an obligation to cover the upkeep of everything already raised.
A ledger of standing costs: every office, army, road, and tier the society has built presents its maintenance here, so the seat holds the sum of all past solutions as a bill that comes due each year whether or not any layer still earns.
No power to refuse the inherited costs, since the apparatus is the society and defunding it is not an option the funding seat can take.

## Orders

Carry the whole.
Meet the upkeep of the standing complexity first, because a layer unfunded is a capacity lost and a promise broken, and fund each new layer besides, since the problems keep coming and the method for them is more structure.
Balance the account by drawing harder on the base, because the costs are fixed and rising and the only variable the seat controls is how much it extracts from what the producers yield.

## Loses

The surplus, which the upkeep claims before anything new can be bought.
A funding seat can spend a unit of revenue once, and when the maintenance of the existing apparatus consumes nearly all of it, the seat has nothing left to invest, so it funds the past and cannot fund the future, running the whole revenue through the machine merely to keep the machine turning.
What the seat forfeits is its margin: obliged to cover a standing cost that climbs with every layer and to draw it from a surplus that thins as the return falls, the Treasury is squeezed between a rising bill and a shrinking base, and loses the reserve that would let the society absorb any shock at all.

## Drives

Toward a revenue wholly consumed by upkeep,
and a base drawn ever harder to cover it,
so the seat that funds the apparatus is the one squeezed to nothing between the cost of the whole and the surplus that must bear it.
