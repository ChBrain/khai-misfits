# Persona: The Successor

## Taxonomy

[The Treasury](position_the_treasury.md). Inherits the whole apparatus at the far end of the curve, funds its upkeep, and meets each new problem the only way the society knows, by adding more.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Dutiful. Stretched. Holding up what was handed down.
Is the one who takes charge of the apparatus already built, every office and army and work that earlier hands raised, and must keep it running, funding an upkeep that climbs while the surplus that once paid for it thins.
No decadence wished on them: they inherit real obligations and meet them responsibly, and adding another layer to a new problem is the same sound move the first administrator made, only made where the curve has already bent.

## Action

Funds the standing apparatus and, when a new problem comes, meets it by adding another layer, because that is the method the society has and the only one it knows.
Answers each difficulty as their predecessors did, but past the peak of the curve the answer costs more and returns less, so the surplus goes to maintenance and the new layer buys almost nothing, and still it is added, because doing nothing solves the problem not at all.
Does what a responsible successor does: holds the whole together, pays what it costs, and applies the proven method to each new problem, no option to refuse the method visible from where they sit, since to refuse it is to leave the problem unsolved and the apparatus is theirs to defend.

## Shadow

Not a wastrel, but the one who works the method where it has stopped paying, and that is the trap: the same act that made the founder great, solving a problem by adding complexity, makes the successor poorer, because the curve they stand on is not the curve the founder stood on.
Because the Successor inherits an apparatus whose easy returns are long spent, every layer they add meets a harder problem at a higher cost for a smaller yield, and the upkeep of all that came before must be paid whether it earns or not, so they run to stand still, spending nearly everything to hold a position that grows no stronger.
The duty is real and the method is proven, so the successor who does exactly what worked before is the one on whose watch the return goes negative, faithful to a strategy precisely as it turns against them.

## Tell

Says "we cannot stop paying for this, and the problem still needs solving."
Is right that the apparatus must be funded and the problem met, and cannot afford to see that the method itself has gone negative, that another layer now costs more than it saves, because the only alternative visible from the apex is to abandon what the society is made of.
