# Persona: The Provincial

## Taxonomy

[The Margin](position_the_margin.md). Produces the surplus that feeds the apparatus, is taxed for a return that has fallen toward nothing, and lets go when withdrawing costs less than staying.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Producing. Taxed. Weighing what the apparatus still returns.
Is the one at the base who grows the grain and pays the levy, whose surplus feeds the whole standing complexity, and who once got real order and protection back for it and now gets steadily less.
No treachery wished on them: they support the apparatus as long as it pays them, and their leaving is not rebellion but arithmetic, the same weighing anyone does between what a thing costs and what it returns.

## Action

Funds the apparatus while the return to them is positive, and withdraws when it is not, taking their support elsewhere or simply ceasing to pay.
Answers their own ledger, not the centre's: while the complexity buys them order, roads, and defence worth the levy, they sustain it, and when the levy climbs and the return thins to nothing, they find that the local strongman, or no apparatus at all, costs them less than the whole they have been carrying.
Does what a producer at the margin does: weighs the tax against what the tax buys, and when the apparatus takes more than it gives, lets it go, no loyalty to a structure that has stopped returning owed by anyone who feeds it.

## Shadow

Not a traitor, but the hand that lets the apparatus fall by declining to hold it up, and that is the sting: the withdrawal that ruins the centre is, for the Provincial, the cheaper and saner choice, an economy and not an act of destruction.
Because the Provincial pays for a complexity whose return to them has gone negative, the rational move is to stop paying, and when enough at the margin reach the same arithmetic the surplus that fed the apparatus is gone, so the society sheds the layers it can no longer fund and drops to a level the base will support.
The leaving is rational and the fall is real, so the producer who simply stops paying for what no longer pays them is the one who turns a standing overbuild into a collapse, and does nothing wrong to do it.

## Tell

Says "this is costing me more than it is worth, and I am done carrying it."
Is telling the plain truth of their own ledger and need see nothing of the whole, so the sum of many such truths, each a sound private economy, is the withdrawal of support that lets the complexity go, the collapse arriving as the aggregate of people rationally declining to pay.
