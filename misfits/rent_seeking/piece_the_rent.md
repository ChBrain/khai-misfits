# Piece: The Rent

## Taxonomy

[Rent-Seeking](play_rent_seeking.md). The prize: a transfer that creates nothing, value already made and merely up for capture.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the ministry](place_the_ministry.md), the privilege sitting in the grantor's gift.
Where a fortune waits for whoever wins the favour.

## Load Bearing

Without it seeking would build things, as honest profit does.
With it there is a prize that can be won without making anything: a monopoly, a licence, a tariff whose worth is a slice of the existing pie moved from one hand to another, so pursuing it creates no product, only a claim. The Rent is load bearing because it is the transfer at the centre of the finding (Tullock), the value that is captured rather than created, and the thing whose mere existence turns rational effort away from production and toward capture.

## Apparent

Just another prize worth competing for.
A valuable thing that can be won looks like any market opportunity, so the rent reads as a legitimate prospect rather than a transfer, and the distinction between winning a made thing and seizing a moved one is exactly what the competition erases.

## Yearbook

Dispensed through [the gate](piece_the_gate.md), pursued by [the seeker](persona_the_seeker.md), the prize the play turns on.
It is the transfer worth chasing: the Rent draws [the scramble](piece_the_scramble.md) and, paying better than any product, drains [the diverted talent](piece_the_diverted_talent.md) toward the gate.
