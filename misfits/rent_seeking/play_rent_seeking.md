# Play: Rent-Seeking

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Rent-Seeking.
Somewhere a privilege is dispensed, a licence, a tariff, a monopoly, a favour, worth a great deal and making nothing ... and because it is worth having, people compete to capture it, spending real effort and real money on the winning, so the lobbyists and the lawyers and the queues consume, in the fight over the transfer, up to the whole of what the transfer is worth, and a thing that was only ever a slice of the pie moved from one hand to another turns out to have shrunk the pie by the cost of the moving.

## Arc

The bend is this: chasing a fixed prize, not making a new one, burns the prize in the chase.
It runs on the difference between growing the pie and slicing it.
Profit honestly sought builds something: a better product, a lower cost, a thing that was not there before. A rent is different: it is a transfer, value already made and merely up for grabs, and seeking it builds nothing, only moves it. But the seeking is not free. To win a privilege worth a fortune, it is worth spending nearly a fortune, so the contestants pour real resources into lobbying, litigating, queuing, and courting the grantor, and those resources make nothing at all.
None of it is theft, and none of it is irrational. The seeker is right that the rent is worth chasing, the grantor is right that the favour is his to give, and the producer who watches capture out-earn creation is right to wonder why he still makes things. So the prize is dissipated in the competition to seize it, the ablest hands are drawn from building the pie to fighting over its slices, and each rent won teaches the next lobby to seek one, until the surest way to get rich is to capture a transfer and the society reorganises around the capture.
Shrink the rents, close the discretionary gate that dispenses privilege, and there is nothing to seek and the effort flows back to making things: but the grantor likes the gate and the seekers guard it, and some rents are the honest reward that calls forth invention, so telling the transfer from the earning is the hard, contested heart of the cure.

## Triggers

**[The Rent](plot_the_rent.md)**
A privilege sits in the grantor's gift, a licence or a tariff or a monopoly, worth a fortune and making nothing. The Grantor holds the gate that dispenses it, and the Seeker sees a prize plainly worth chasing. Closes when a valuable transfer exists that anyone may compete to capture.

**[The Scramble](plot_the_scramble.md)**
Cue: the rent is worth almost as much as it costs to win, so real resources are poured into winning it. The Seeker lobbies, litigates, and queues, and the Reckoner tallies that the effort spent chasing the transfer approaches the whole of the transfer, and makes nothing. Closes when the prize is dissipated in the contest to seize it.

**[The Diversion](plot_the_diversion.md)**
Cue: capture out-earns creation. The Producer, who builds things, watches the Convert quit building to chase rents where the returns are, and wonders why he still makes anything. Closes when the ablest hands are pulled from growing the pie to fighting over its slices.

**[The Rent Trap](plot_the_rent_trap.md)**
Cue: each rent won teaches the next lobby to seek one. The Seeker returns to the gate to win more privilege, and the Grantor, courted and paid, dispenses more, so the surest path to fortune becomes capture. Closes when the society has reorganised around seizing transfers rather than making value.

**[The Reform](plot_the_reform.md)**
Cue: someone moves to shrink the rents and close the discretionary gate, so there is nothing left to seek. The Reformer works to remove the privilege, and the Grantor and the seekers resist, while the honest question stands: which of these rents was the just reward that calls forth invention. Closes when the gate is narrowed and effort flows back to making things ... or the rent is defended, or the wrong one is cut, and the seeking resumes.

## Stakes

Whether a society spends itself making the pie larger, or fighting over how it is sliced.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the rent, which is worth capturing, and away from production, which builds the wealth the rent merely moves. The production cannot make a transfer worth seizing without making it worth spending to seize, so the prize calls forth a contest that consumes it, and the ablest are drawn to the contest because that is where the money is. It can only show that no seeker was a thief, that chasing a rent worth a fortune is rational and capture really does out-earn creation, and that the one thing that would end the waste, a gate too narrow to dispense privilege, is the very power the grantor keeps and the seekers guard, and is hard to close without cutting, by mistake, the honest reward that makes invention pay.
