# Plot: The Reform

## Taxonomy

[Rent-Seeking](play_rent_seeking.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The reform table](place_the_reform_table.md), where someone moves to shrink the rents and close the discretionary gate.
The remedy is not to shame the seekers but to remove the prize, so there is nothing left to chase.

## Action

[Reforming](process_reforming.md): [The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), holding [the reformer's seat](position_reformer.md), works to remove the licence, the tariff, the favour, or take the discretion out of granting, while [The Grantor](persona_the_grantor.md), holding [the grantor's seat](position_grantor.md), keeps the gate and the seekers guard their rents.

## Stage

[The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md) tables [Close the Gate](plan_close_the_gate.md), so [the rent-seeking](piece_the_rent_seeking.md) is answered by narrowing [the gate](piece_the_gate.md) until capture stops paying.
[The Grantor](persona_the_grantor.md) tables [Dispense the Favour](plan_dispense_the_favour.md) still, and the honest question stands unresolved: which of these rents was the just reward that calls forth invention.

## Tension

The one move that works must cut a power the whole room is invested in.
The grantor likes the discretion, the seekers have paid for their rents, and some of what sits on the table is the honest reward that makes invention pay, so the reformer must narrow the gate without cutting the incentive, against everyone who profits from leaving it open. The gate is narrowed and effort flows back to making things ... or the rent is defended, or the wrong prize cut and the honest return lost with it, and the seeking resumes.
