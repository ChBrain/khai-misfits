# Rent-Seeking: Reference

The research warrant for [Rent-Seeking](play_rent_seeking.md). The title is Krueger's term for
Tullock's finding; the play runs on the welfare economics of contested transfers, the theory of
rent dissipation, and the allocation-of-talent literature that follows, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging rent-seeking as a **systemic system**: a valuable privilege that creates nothing, a licence,
a tariff, a monopoly, a favour, draws real resources into the competition to capture it, so up to the
whole value of the transfer is dissipated in the seeking, and the ablest hands are pulled from making
the pie larger to fighting over its slices. The play models the documented mechanics, the
discretionary gate that dispenses privilege, the rent that is a transfer and not a creation, the
scramble of lobbying and litigation and queuing, the dissipation that consumes the prize in the
contest for it, the diversion of talent from production to capture, the rent trap by which each rent
won breeds the next, and the reform that alone shrinks the seeking at a contested cost, as the
load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of villainy. The governing finding of the source
literature is the play's governing law: where value can be obtained by capturing a transfer rather
than by creating wealth, rational actors spend real resources up to the value of the transfer to
capture it, and that spending is a pure social loss, directly unproductive. It is the general case of
the house's Regulatory Capture: not one agency come to serve the industry, but a whole economy's
effort and talent drawn toward seizing transfers instead of making value.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  seeker as a thief or the grantor as corrupt, because the source finding is precisely that the
  seeking is rational and lawful: a rent worth a fortune is worth nearly a fortune to win, the favour
  is the grantor's to give, and capture really does out-earn creation. The misfit is that a society of
  rational competitors for transfers spends itself deciding who gets the wealth instead of making more.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into simply abolishing every privilege. The cure
  is to shrink the discretionary gate, which the grantor keeps and the seekers guard, and which is
  hard to close without cutting, by mistake, the honest reward that makes invention pay, so the
  reckoning is a contested narrowing of power, not a costless sweep, and telling the transfer from the
  earning is its unfinished heart.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the welfare analysis, the dissipation
  result, and the allocation-of-talent account are theirs, and the honest difficulty of distinguishing
  a rent from a legitimate return is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Gate](piece_the_gate.md)**: carries the discretionary power to dispense privilege (Buchanan, Tollison
  & Tullock), the source that makes rents exist to be sought.
- **[The Rent](piece_the_rent.md)**: carries the transfer that creates nothing (Tullock), value already made and
  merely up for capture.
- **[The Scramble](piece_the_scramble.md)**: carries the competition to capture it (Krueger), the lobbying,
  litigating, and queuing spent on the winning.
- **[The Dissipation](piece_the_dissipation.md)**: carries the wasted resources (Tullock; Posner), up to the whole
  value of the transfer consumed in the contest for it.
- **[The Diverted Talent](piece_the_diverted_talent.md)**: carries the misallocation of ability (Baumol), the
  ablest hands pulled from production to capture.
- **[The Rent Trap](piece_the_rent_trap.md)**: carries the self-reinforcing society (Krueger), each rent won
  breeding the next until capture is the surest path to fortune.
- **[The Rent-Seeking](piece_the_rent_seeking.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Tullock; Krueger), effort spent
  seizing transfers instead of creating value, and its remedy in a narrowed gate at a contested cost.
