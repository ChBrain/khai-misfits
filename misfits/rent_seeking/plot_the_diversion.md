# Plot: The Diversion

## Taxonomy

[Rent-Seeking](play_rent_seeking.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The crossroads](place_the_crossroads.md), two roads from one point: the workshop and the gate.
An able person at the fork, reading the returns down each way, and capture out-earns creation.

## Action

[Diverting](process_diverting.md): [The Producer](persona_the_producer.md), holding [the producer's seat](position_producer.md), keeps building and is out-earned, while [The Convert](persona_the_convert.md), holding [the defector's seat](position_defector.md), quits building to chase rents where the returns are.

## Stage

[The Producer](persona_the_producer.md) tables [Grow the Pie](plan_grow_the_pie.md), and watches [the rent](piece_the_rent.md) pay better than any product he can make.
[The Convert](persona_the_convert.md) tables [Follow the Money](plan_follow_the_money.md), turning the talent that would have grown the pie toward the gate, so [the diverted talent](piece_the_diverted_talent.md) drains from making to capturing.

## Tension

The rules reward the slicing over the baking, so the ablest go to slice.
The convert is right that capture pays more, the producer is right that he is out-earned for building, and neither has erred. That is the deepest cost: not the fortune burned at the lobby but the wealth never made, the firms not founded and the technologies not built, because the talent that would have created them read the returns, correctly, and walked to the gate.
