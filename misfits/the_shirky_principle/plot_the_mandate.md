# Plot: The Mandate

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The charter](place_the_charter.md), a body founded to solve a real problem, its budget and posts and standing all pinned to that problem.
The mission sincere, the need genuine, the institution raised to meet it.

## Action

The founding: [The Steward](persona_the_steward.md), holding [the custodian's seat](position_custodian.md), takes up the mandate, while [The Salaried](persona_the_salaried.md) at [the beneficiary's seat](position_beneficiary.md) draw the posts the problem funds.

## Stage

[The Steward](persona_the_steward.md) tables [Hold the Mandate](plan_hold_the_mandate.md): keep the mission alive and the body funded, so [the mandate](piece_the_mandate.md) ties the institution to its problem.
[The Salaried](persona_the_salaried.md) table [Keep the Post](plan_keep_the_post.md), and [the livelihood](piece_the_livelihood.md) pins every wage to the problem lasting, while the charter, written to seek a victory, contains no clause for what to do when it arrives.

## Tension

The founding is entirely sound, and it lays the trap.
There is nothing wrong with raising a body to solve a real problem and funding it to do so: the mission is worthy and the people sincere. But the definition has no exit, so from the first day the institution's existence is bonded to the problem's, and the victory it was created to win is quietly the one event it cannot survive.
