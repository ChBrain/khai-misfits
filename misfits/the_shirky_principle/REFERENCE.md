# The Shirky Principle: Reference

The research warrant for [The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md). The title is
Clay Shirky's observation, named by Kevin Kelly; the play runs on the literature on
bureaucratic self-perpetuation, goal displacement, and interested blindness, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Shirky principle as a **systemic system**: an institution founded to solve a problem
comes to depend on the problem for its budget, jobs, and standing, so it preserves the problem it
was built to remove, solving it just enough to stay needed and never enough to end. The play models
the documented mechanics, the mandate that defines the body by its problem, the livelihood that
lasts only while the problem does, the interested blindness of those whose salary depends on not
seeing it, the goal displacement by which survival replaces mission, the half-cure that manages the
symptom, the perpetuation that widens the problem as it fades, and the outcome funding and sunset
that alone flip the incentive, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of
cynics. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: where solving a
problem is the source of an institution's resources, full success is the institution's own end, so
every incentive it creates points away from the cure. It is the provider's mirror of the house's
perverse-incentive plays: not a bounty that manufactures its target, but a solver that preserves its
problem.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a cynic or a fraud, because the source finding is precisely that none is needed: the
  mission is sincere, the work is real, and the interest in the problem's survival is structural and
  largely invisible to those who hold it (Sinclair). The misfit is that a body funded by a problem
  cannot want the problem gone, however much its people do.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into conscience closing the institution down.
  The incentive flips only when an outside hand funds the outcome and sunsets the mandate, so the
  reckoning is a body made to be paid for its own obsolescence, against everything it is organised to
  do, not a solver that chose to finish itself.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the principle, the goal-displacement
  account, and the bureaucratic-incentive models are theirs, and the condition the principle actually
  requires, an institution whose resources depend on the problem continuing, is carried as carefully
  as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Mandate](piece_the_mandate.md)**: carries the founding charter (Shirky / Kelly), the body defined
  by the problem it exists to solve.
- **[The Livelihood](piece_the_livelihood.md)**: carries interested blindness (Sinclair), the budget, jobs, and
  standing that last only while the problem does.
- **[The Half-Cure](piece_the_half_cure.md)**: carries the managed problem, solved to the level that keeps the
  solver needed and no further.
- **[The Displacement](piece_the_displacement.md)**: carries goal displacement (Merton), the institution's
  survival quietly replacing its mission as the true objective.
- **[The Perpetuation](piece_the_perpetuation.md)**: carries budget-maximising and bureaucratic growth (Niskanen;
  Parkinson), the problem kept alive by widening the mandate and finding new instances.
- **[The Obsolescence](piece_the_obsolescence.md)**: carries the institution's own end (Michels), the outcome full
  success would bring and the thing every incentive is arranged to avoid.
- **[The Shirky Principle](piece_the_shirky_principle.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Shirky / Kelly), a
  solver that preserves its problem, and its remedy in outcome funding and a sunset, held whole by nobody.
