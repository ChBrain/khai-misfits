# Plot: The Half-Cure

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The budget hearing](place_the_budget_hearing.md), where fully solving the problem would remove the reason to fund the solver.
A persisting problem is a renewed budget; a solved one is a closed line.

## Action

[Simmering](process_simmering.md): [The Maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md), holding [the operator's seat](position_operator.md), relieves the symptom that would force a real fix and files the cause as ongoing.

## Stage

[The Maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md) tables [Solve Just Enough](plan_solve_just_enough.md): take the fix to the level that keeps the pressure off and the franchise intact, so [the half-cure](piece_the_half_cure.md) holds the problem at a steady simmer.
The report that renews the mandate is managed, ongoing, well in hand, because that is the report the hearing pays for, and a solved problem is the one outcome it will not fund.

## Tension

The competence is real, and it is aimed at a simmer.
No one decides to keep the problem: the Maintainer genuinely handles it, and handling it well looks identical to solving it from inside a good quarter. But a cured problem is a job done and a job gone, so the skill bends, unchosen, toward control rather than completion, and the institution proves its worth precisely by never finishing the thing it was funded to finish.
