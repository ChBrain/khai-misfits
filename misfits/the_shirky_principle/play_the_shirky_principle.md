# Play: The Shirky Principle

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Shirky Principle.
A body is founded to solve a problem, and does, and is funded and staffed and honoured for solving it ... and everything it is now depends on the problem going on, so it solves the problem just enough to keep the pressure off and never enough to end it, because the day the problem ends, so does the body.

## Arc

The bend is this: the institution built to solve a problem comes to need the problem, so it preserves the thing it was made to remove.
It runs on a split between the mission and the livelihood, which start as one and slowly part.
At the founding they align: solve the problem, and the budget, the jobs, and the standing follow. But solving is not a state, it is a service, and the service is paid only while the problem lasts, so the fully solved problem is the institution's own end. And no one has to choose that end for it to be avoided: it is enough that every incentive points the other way, that a win shrinks the budget and a persisting problem renews it, that the people whose salaries depend on the problem sincerely cannot see the interest that depends on their not seeing it.
So the body solves to the level that keeps it needed and no further: it relieves the symptom that would force a real fix, widens the problem's definition as the old version fades, finds new instances, keeps the threat warm. The mission is never dropped, only quietly displaced by the survival that now wears its clothes, and the problem it was built to end becomes the problem it is built to keep.
Fund the outcome instead of the activity, and sunset the mandate on success, and the incentive flips: but that asks a body to be paid for making itself unnecessary, which is the one thing it is organised never to do.

## Triggers

**[The Mandate](plot_the_mandate.md)**
A body is founded to solve a real problem, and its budget, jobs, and standing are all pinned to that problem. The Steward takes up the mission, and the Salaried draw the posts it funds. Closes when everything the institution is depends on the problem it was made to end.

**[The Half-Cure](plot_the_half_cure.md)**
Cue: fully solving the problem would remove the reason to fund the solver. The Maintainer solves it just enough to relieve the pressure and keep the franchise, and files the rest as ongoing work. Closes when the problem is managed to a steady simmer, eased but never ended.

**[The Perpetuation](plot_the_perpetuation.md)**
Cue: the original problem starts, despite everything, to fade. The Expander widens the mandate, finds new instances, and redefines the threat so the work never runs out, and the mission quietly becomes the survival wearing its clothes. Closes when the problem is kept alive by the body built to remove it.

**[The Sunset](plot_the_sunset.md)**
Cue: someone funds the outcome instead of the activity and writes a sunset into the mandate. The Sunsetter makes success the thing that pays and obsolescence the thing that is planned for, and the body resists being organised to end itself. Closes when the incentive is flipped and the problem may actually be solved ... or the sunset is repealed as reckless, and the mandate renews.

## Stakes

Whether the problem is ever solved, or only ever sustained.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward the end of the problem, toward the continuation of the body that answers it. The production cannot make an institution pay for its own obsolescence. It can only show that no one was cynical and no one schemed, that the mission was sincere and the work was real, and that the one outcome that would have vindicated the founding, the problem actually ended, is the one outcome every incentive the founding created is arranged to avoid.
