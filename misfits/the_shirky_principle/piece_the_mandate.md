# Piece: The Mandate

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md). The founding charter: the body defined by the problem it exists to solve.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the charter](place_the_charter.md), in the founding document that names the problem as the reason to exist.
The bond written at the start, when mission and body are one.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no institution tied to a problem, and so no interest in the problem's survival.
With it the body's whole identity is the problem: its name, its funding, its reason to be, all pinned to the thing it was made to end. The Mandate is load bearing because it is the tie that turns solving into self-destruction (Shirky), the definition that makes a full cure the institution's own last act, so that everything downstream, the simmer, the expansion, the displacement, is the body protecting the mandate that is protecting the problem.

## Apparent

A worthy mission, clearly stated.
It looks like exactly what a founding should be: a real problem named, a body raised to solve it, a purpose everyone can believe in. Nothing about it announces the trap, because the trap is only that the definition has no exit.

## Yearbook

Held by [the steward](persona_the_steward.md), and never written with a clause for its own success.
It is the root of the misfit: the Mandate binds the body to the problem so tightly that [the obsolescence](piece_the_obsolescence.md) full success would bring becomes the thing the whole structure is arranged to avoid.
