# Piece: The Half-Cure

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md). The problem solved to the level that keeps the solver needed, and no further.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the budget hearing](place_the_budget_hearing.md), reported as a situation well in hand.
In the day-to-day handling that relieves the symptom and files the cause as ongoing.

## Load Bearing

Without it the body would either cure the problem or fail visibly, and either would force a reckoning.
With it there is a third state, permanent management: enough relief that no one demands a real fix, never enough resolution that the need ends. The Half-Cure is load bearing because it is how the principle operates without anyone choosing it: competence bent toward control rather than completion produces a problem held exactly at the level that maximises the solver's tenure, so the institution looks effective precisely by never finishing.

## Apparent

Good, steady management.
It looks like a problem responsibly handled: no crises, no scandals, a firm hand keeping things under control. That steadiness is the disguise, because a problem kept at a simmer and a problem being solved are indistinguishable from any single quarter.

## Yearbook

Practised by [the maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md) through [simmering](process_simmering.md), rewarded at [the budget hearing](place_the_budget_hearing.md).
It is the misfit in daily motion: the Half-Cure keeps the problem alive by relieving it, so the body stays needed and the cause stays unclosed, cycle after competent cycle.
