# Plot: The Sunset

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The sunset review](place_the_sunset_review.md), where an outside hand sees the body cannot want its own success.
The money is to be tied to the problem shrinking, and an ending written into the mandate.

## Action

[Sunsetting](process_sunsetting.md): [The Sunsetter](persona_the_sunsetter.md), holding [the reformer's seat](position_reformer.md), funds the outcome rather than the activity and writes a close-on-success clause into the charter.

## Stage

[The Sunsetter](persona_the_sunsetter.md) tables [Fund the Outcome](plan_fund_the_outcome.md): pay for the problem getting smaller and measure it from outside, so [the shirky principle](piece_the_shirky_principle.md) is answered by making [the obsolescence](piece_the_obsolescence.md) the thing that is funded and planned.
For once success pays and the interest in the problem's survival has no seat at the table, and for once the body could actually finish the thing it was founded to finish.

## Tension

The one move that works asks the body to be organised to end itself.
Everything inside the institution is arranged to renew the mandate, so the sunset reads as an attack on sincere people doing real work, and it is resented exactly where it bites. It holds only while the outside hand keeps re-imposing it ... or the clause is repealed as reckless, the money flows back to the activity, and the problem is preserved by its solver once more.
