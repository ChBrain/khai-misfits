# Piece: The Obsolescence

## Taxonomy

[The Shirky Principle](play_the_shirky_principle.md). The institution's own end, the outcome full success would bring.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At the vanishing point of the mission, where the problem is solved and the body is no longer needed.
The event named in no charter and dreaded in every budget.

## Load Bearing

Without it solving the problem would carry no cost to the solver, and the misfit would not exist.
With it success is self-destruction: the fully cured problem is the closed institution, the ended threat is the redundant staff, so the very outcome the body was founded to achieve is the one that would dissolve it. The Obsolescence is load bearing because it is the price of success (Michels), the thing the whole structure is arranged to avoid, and the reason a sincere, competent, well-meaning body cannot bring itself to win.

## Apparent

Nothing, because no one looks at it.
It goes unmentioned, unplanned, unnamed: the one future the body will not model, so it stays a blank the institution steers around without ever admitting it is there. Its invisibility is exactly what lets it govern every choice.

## Yearbook

Faced only by [the sunsetter](persona_the_sunsetter.md), and avoided by everyone else.
It is the gravity of the misfit: the Obsolescence is what [the perpetuation](piece_the_perpetuation.md) flees and what [the shirky principle](piece_the_shirky_principle.md) can be cured only by planning for on purpose.
