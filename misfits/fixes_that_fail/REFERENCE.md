# Fixes that Fail: Reference

The research warrant for [Fixes that Fail](play_fixes_that_fail.md). The title is Senge's systems
archetype; the play runs on the system-dynamics account of delayed feedback and policy resistance,
from Forrester through the archetype literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the fixes-that-fail archetype as a **systemic system**: a problem met by a fix that relieves
the symptom quickly and, through a longer and delayed loop, feeds the very problem it relieved, so
the trouble returns worse and is answered with more of the same fix, which relieves and worsens it
again, compounding under a remedy no one suspects. The play models the documented mechanics, the
symptom that demands action now, the fix that works at once, the relief that earns the fix its trust,
the delay that hides the second effect, the backfire by which the fix feeds the problem, the
escalation by which a worse return draws more fix, and the letting-go that alone breaks the loop at a
cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of stupidity. The governing finding
of the source literature is the play's governing law: a fix effective in the short term can have
delayed, unforeseen consequences that reinstate or worsen the problem, and because the relief is fast
and visible while the backfire is slow and diffuse, the rational response to the worsening is more of
the fix that is causing it. It is the sibling of the house's Shifting the Burden, and its distinct
engine: not a quick fix that atrophies the capacity for a fundamental one, but a single fix whose own
delayed side effect feeds the problem, so the cure is the disease.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  fixer as reckless, because the source finding is precisely that the fix works: the relief is real
  and immediate, the second effect is delayed and diffuse and never traced to the fix, and when the
  problem returns worse the natural reading is too little fix, not too much. The misfit is that a
  remedy that plainly helps is the thing feeding the trouble.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a clever tweak that keeps the fix and
  loses the backfire. The one move that works is to let the fix go and bear the symptom while the loop
  unwinds and the cause is faced, a worse-before-better that looks like negligence the whole time it
  is working, and the fix, which genuinely relieved, is right there the entire time. The reckoning is
  present pain taken for a late and unpromised gain, not a costless correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the archetype, the system-dynamics
  account, and the policy-resistance finding are theirs, and the condition the trap actually requires,
  a fast relief and a slow, hidden side effect on the same problem, is carried as carefully as the
  finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Symptom](piece_the_symptom.md)**: carries the problem that demands action now (Meadows), the pressure
  the fix is reached for to relieve.
- **[The Fix](piece_the_fix.md)**: carries the symptomatic intervention (Senge), the act that eases the symptom
  quickly and reliably.
- **[The Relief](piece_the_relief.md)**: carries the short-term success that reinforces the fix (Kim), the
  visible result that earns it trust and repetition.
- **[The Delay](piece_the_delay.md)**: carries the lag in the feedback loop (Forrester), the gap that hides the
  second effect and keeps the fix looking like pure remedy.
- **[The Backfire](piece_the_backfire.md)**: carries the unintended consequence (Senge; Forrester), the delayed
  effect by which the fix feeds the very problem it relieved.
- **[The Escalation](piece_the_escalation.md)**: carries the reinforcing response (Kim), more of the same fix
  applied as the worse return reads as too little fix.
- **[The Fix that Fails](piece_the_fix_that_fails.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Senge), the cure that is
  the disease, and its remedy in letting the fix go at the cost of a worse-before-better.
