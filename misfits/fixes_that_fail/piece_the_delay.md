# Piece: The Delay

## Taxonomy

[Fixes that Fail](play_fixes_that_fail.md). The lag: the gap between the fix and its second effect that hides the cause.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the morning after](place_the_morning_after.md), in the distance between act and harm.
Where the effect arrives too far from the fix for anyone to connect them.

## Load Bearing

Without it the backfire would follow the fix at once and be caught in the act.
With it the harm arrives late, severed in time from its cause, so no one lays the worsening at the fix's door, and the loop can run because its two halves never appear together. The Delay is load bearing because it is the feedback lag that makes the system counterintuitive (Forrester), the reason a fix's harm is never traced to the fix, and the exact gap in which a remedy gets to look like pure relief.

## Apparent

Just the passage of time.
Nothing about a delay announces that it is hiding a link, so the interval reads as ordinary and the later trouble as unrelated, and the separation of cause from effect passes for the simple fact that time has gone by.

## Yearbook

Working through [backfiring](process_backfiring.md), the gap that protects the fix.
It is the cover the loop needs: the Delay lets [the backfire](piece_the_backfire.md) go untraced, so the worse return is read as fresh trouble and not the fix's own.
