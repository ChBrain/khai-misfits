# Second Nature: Reference

The research warrant for [Second Nature](play_second_nature.md).
The dynamic is the curse of knowledge: Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber's finding, demonstrated by Newton,
theorised by Nickerson, and applied to prose by Pinker, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the curse of knowledge as a **knowledge-transfer structure**: a knower whose mastery of a thing has
become second nature, so that the sense of what is obvious is set by the knowledge and cannot be unlearned, the
knower cannot reconstruct the mind that lacks it and explains from inside the knowing, over the head of the
learner, and cannot perceive the gap, because perceiving it would require un-knowing the thing. The play models
the documented mechanics, the knowledge that resets obviousness and admits no way back, the systematic
overestimation of how much others share what one knows, the compressed shorthand and skipped steps transparent to
the knower and opaque to the novice, the invisibility of the gap to the one who has the knowledge, the failed
transfer and the blame misplaced on the learner, the second knower whose shared knowledge makes them ratify the
opacity rather than catch it, and the outside test against a real novice that alone recovers the view, at a cost
and never quite finished, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a condescending expert or
a slow student. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: better-informed agents cannot
ignore their own knowledge when judging what less-informed others know, so they systematically communicate as
though their knowledge were shared, and the gap widens with expertise.

It is distinct from the house's The Bus Factor, where knowledge lives tacitly in one person and is lost if they
leave: there the trouble is concentration and loss, knowledge held by too few and vulnerable to their departure,
here the knowledge is held and present and willingly offered but cannot be transferred, because the holder cannot
perceive the novice's ignorance, so the failure is not that the knowledge is scarce or hoarded but that the
knowing itself hides the gap it must cross. And it is distinct from the house's The Mirror, where an
organisation's communication structure is stamped onto the system it builds: there the shaping force is the shape
of the org, here it is a cognitive asymmetry within a single explainer, the knowledge that overwrites the
ignorance it would need to reach, no org chart required.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the knower as
  condescending or the learner as slow, because the source finding is precisely that the failure is cognitive and
  well-meant: the explanation is honest, the expert is genuinely trying to help, the thing really is obvious to
  them, the peer who ratifies it really does find it clear, and the learner really is attending in good faith.
  The miss is authored by no one and emerges from a knowing that has overwritten the ignorance it would need to
  reach, so the misfit is that an honest, helpful, well-reviewed explanation sails over the head of the very
  person it is for, and the one who most knows the thing is the one who least can hand it on.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that lets
  knowledge cross is to test the explanation on a real novice and rebuild from where they stumble, and it means
  admitting the obvious is not obvious, submitting the clear account to a stranger who will find it unclear, and
  rebuilding against the expert's own certainty and the peer's ratifying nod, and even then only approximating an
  ignorance that cannot be truly re-entered. The reckoning is a gap closed by humbling, effortful, outside
  testing, never quite finished, not a free correction, so the cure for a curse the knower cannot feel is the
  evidence of a mind they cannot inhabit.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the curse of knowledge is Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber's, the
  tapping demonstration is Newton's, the account of imputing one's knowledge to others is Nickerson's, the
  hindsight-bias cousin is Fischhoff's, and the application to writing and the testing remedy are Pinker's and
  Heath and Heath's, carried as carefully as the finding itself.
