# Play: Second Nature

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Second Nature.
Someone taps out a tune on the table, a song so plain in their head that the melody rings through every knock, and they are sure the listener will name it, and the listener hears only a run of dull taps and has no idea. That is the whole of it. The tapper cannot un-hear the tune, cannot make the taps go bare the way they land for the other, and so is genuinely astonished that anyone could miss something so obvious. And the expert is the tapper: the thing they know has sunk in so deep it has become second nature, automatic, transparent, the scaffolding long since kicked away, so when they explain it they explain from inside the knowing, skipping the steps that are now reflex and speaking the shorthand that is now plain, and it goes clean over the head of the one who does not know, because to them the steps are not reflex and the shorthand is not plain. And here is the cruel turn: the knower cannot even see that it happened. To feel the gap they would have to un-know the thing, to become again the person who did not understand, and that person is gone, so the explanation feels perfectly clear to the one giving it, and its failure feels like the dullness of the one receiving it. The novice, lost, thinks the fault is theirs, that everyone else must be following, and stays quiet. Another expert, reading it over, finds it perfectly clear too, because they also know, and waves it through. And the worst of it is that this grows with mastery: the deeper the expertise, the more has gone to reflex, the wider the gap and the less it can be felt, so the person who knows the most is the one least able to teach it, and knowledge fails to cross exactly where there is most of it to give. Nobody is careless and nobody is unkind. The explanation is honest and the expert is trying to help and the thing really is, to them, obvious. It is only that obvious is a thing that happens inside a head, and the head that knows can no longer visit the head that does not.

## Arc

The bend is this: knowing a thing reshapes what feels obvious and cannot be undone, so the knower cannot reconstruct the mind that lacks the knowledge and explains from inside it, over the head of the one who needs it, without ever being able to see that they have.
It runs on knowledge that cannot be unlearned.
Once a thing is known it changes the knower's sense of what is evident, and there is no way back to not knowing it: the steps become reflex, the jargon becomes transparent, the whole becomes second nature (Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber). So the knower's feeling of what is obvious is set by what they know, and they cannot restore the feeling they had before they knew, because the knowing has overwritten it.
And the gap is invisible from inside. To judge what the novice needs, the knower would have to simulate a mind without the knowledge, and they cannot, because their own knowledge intrudes and cannot be set aside (Nickerson; the hindsight cousin, Fischhoff). So they systematically overestimate how much others share what they know, and the gap between them is not merely unbridged but unperceived, a blind spot the knower has no instrument to detect.
And the explanation misses. Explaining from inside the knowing, the expert skips the now-automatic steps and speaks the now-transparent shorthand, so the account is clear to them and opaque to the learner, and the transfer fails (Newton's tappers; Pinker on the curse in prose). The learner, unable to follow and unable to see that the fault is the explanation's, takes it for their own dullness and stays quiet, and another knower, reading it over, finds it clear and ratifies it, so the miss is never caught.
And it worsens with mastery. The more expert the knower becomes, the more of the subject has gone to reflex and the wider and less perceptible the gap, so expertise and the ability to teach it pull apart, and the person who knows the most is the worst placed to hand it on, and knowledge fails to cross precisely where there is most to cross.
None of it is carelessness or contempt. The explanation is honest, the expert is genuinely trying to help, the thing really is obvious to them, and the other knower who ratifies it really does find it clear: no one talks down or holds back on purpose, and the failure emerges from a knowing that has overwritten the ignorance it would need to reach. What is meant to hand knowledge on speaks past the very person it is for.
Recover the novice's view by testing the explanation on someone who does not yet know, watch where they actually stumble, and rebuild from there, and the knowledge can be made to cross: but recovering that view means admitting the thing is not obvious and cannot be judged from inside, submitting the clear explanation to a stranger who will find it unclear, and rebuilding it against the expert's own certainty and the peer's ratifying nod, and even then only guessing at an ignorance that cannot be truly re-entered, so the escape is bought by the humbling, effortful work of testing against a mind one cannot inhabit, and is never finished, because the knowing that caused the gap is the knowing that cannot be given back.

## Triggers

**[The Telling](plot_the_telling.md)**
A knower explains a thing that has become second nature to them. The Knower gives the account from inside the knowing, skipping the reflex steps and speaking the transparent shorthand, sure it is plain. Closes when the explanation is given, clear to the one giving it and pitched over the head of the one who needs it.

**[The Blank](plot_the_blank.md)**
Cue: the account lands opaque. The Learner cannot follow, cannot see that the fault is the explanation's rather than their own, and takes the miss for their own dullness. Closes when the learner is lost and silent, blaming themselves and leaving the gap unspoken.

**[The Sign-Off](plot_the_sign_off.md)**
Cue: another knower reads it over. The Peer, sharing the knowledge, finds the explanation perfectly clear and waves it through, so the opacity is ratified by someone who also cannot see it. Closes when the explanation is approved by a second knower, its failure confirmed as clarity and never caught.

**[The Compounding](plot_the_compounding.md)**
Cue: mastery widens the gap. As the Knower grows more expert, more of the subject goes to reflex and the gap grows wider and less perceptible, so the ablest becomes the least able to teach. Closes when expertise and the power to hand it on have pulled apart, knowledge failing to cross where there is most of it.

**[The Check](plot_the_check.md)**
Cue: someone would recover the novice's view. The Tester would put the explanation before a real novice and rebuild from where they stumble, against the Knower's certainty it is obvious and the Peer's ratifying nod. Closes when the gap is found and the explanation remade ... or the test is waived as needless, the thing declared obvious once more, and the miss runs on.

## Stakes

Whether knowledge can be handed on, when the knowing that would hand it on is the knowing that hides the gap.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the clear explanation, which the knower is right to think plain and all is spoken from inside, and away from the tested one, which would find the gap and which asks the expert to doubt what feels obvious and submit it to a mind they cannot enter. The production cannot make the explanation reach from inside the knowing, because the feeling of obviousness is set by the knowledge and the gap is invisible to the one who has it, so the miss is the knowing working as knowing does, not a failure of care: the expert cannot un-know the thing to reach the one who has not learned it. It can only show that no one is careless or unkind, that the explanation is honest and the expert helpful and the thing truly obvious to them, and that the one move that lets knowledge cross, testing against a real novice and rebuilding from where they stumble, must be made by admitting the obvious is not, submitting the clear account to a stranger who will find it unclear, and working against one's own certainty and the peer's nod, at a cost and never finished, so the explanation is honest and the gap is real and the knowing that would give the knowledge is the knowing that cannot re-enter the mind without it.
