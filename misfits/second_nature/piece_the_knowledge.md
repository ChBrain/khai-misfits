# Piece: The Knowledge

## Taxonomy

[Second Nature](play_second_nature.md). The thing known, once learned unlearnable, that resets what feels obvious and cannot be given back, the source of the curse.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Sits in the knower, spoken from [the lectern](place_the_lectern.md), where it sets what feels plain.
Its absence is what [the back row](place_the_back_row.md) and [the trial](place_the_trial.md) hold, the ignorance it overwrote.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no expert and no gap, only two people who know the same amount.
With it the trap has its source: a thing learned so thoroughly it has become second nature, resetting the knower's sense of what is evident and admitting no way back to not knowing. The Knowledge is load bearing because it is the irreversible cause the whole curse runs on (Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber): the mastery that cannot be unlearned, so the piece carries the very thing whose possession sets the feeling of obviousness and whose absence in the learner the knower can no longer imagine, the asset that is also the blindfold.

## Apparent

An unqualified good, expertise, the very thing that makes the knower worth listening to.
Seen at the surface, the Knowledge looks like pure advantage, the command of a subject that qualifies one to teach it, and the truth that the same mastery that makes the account authoritative is what makes it unteachable, that knowing the thing resets what feels obvious and cannot be undone, that the asset is the source of the blind spot, stays hidden behind how plainly good it is to know.

## Yearbook

Held by [the knower](persona_the_knower.md) and spoken through [explaining](process_explaining.md), the mastery that cannot be unlearned.
It is the source of the trap: the Knowledge sets what feels obvious and speaks itself as [the shorthand](piece_the_shorthand.md), opening [the gap](piece_the_gap.md) it also hides behind [the blind spot](piece_the_blind_spot.md), the asset that becomes the curse of [second nature](piece_second_nature.md).
