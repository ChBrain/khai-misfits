# Plot: The Sign-Off

## Taxonomy

[Second Nature](play_second_nature.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Lectern](place_the_lectern.md), another knower asked to read the account over.
The explanation on the page, a qualified reviewer to check it, the same knowledge in the reviewer that made it plain to the writer.

## Action

[Ratifying](process_ratifying.md): [The Peer](persona_the_peer.md), holding [the peer's seat](position_peer.md), reads the account, finds it clear, and waves it through.

## Stage

[The Peer](persona_the_peer.md), on [Wave It Through](plan_wave_it_through.md), checks the explanation against their own understanding and finds it sound and plain, and approves it in good conscience.
The account reads clearly to them for the very reason it was written unclear, so the review meant to catch the gap confirms it, a second knower certifying the opacity as clarity.

## Tension

An honest, competent review is right to approve what reads clearly, and reading it from inside the knowing is what lets the miss survive the check.
There is nothing to fault: the peer is qualified and the account really does read plainly to them. But sharing the knowledge means sharing the blind spot, so the plot closes with the explanation ratified, its failure stamped as clarity by the one asked to catch it, and the gap now doubly unseen.
