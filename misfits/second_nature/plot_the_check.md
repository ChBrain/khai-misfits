# Plot: The Check

## Taxonomy

[Second Nature](play_second_nature.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Trial](place_the_trial.md), someone who would recover the novice's view.
The account approved and felt clear, a real novice to hand it to, the stumble there to be watched and the obvious there to be doubted.

## Action

[Testing](process_testing.md): [The Tester](persona_the_tester.md), holding [the tester's seat](position_tester.md), puts the explanation before a real novice and rebuilds from the stumble, against [The Knower](persona_the_knower.md) on [Spell It Out](plan_spell_it_out.md).

## Stage

[The Tester](persona_the_tester.md), on [Ask the Novice](plan_ask_the_novice.md), gives the account to someone who does not yet know, watches where they fall, takes their confusion as the data, and rebuilds from there.
[The Knower](persona_the_knower.md), on [Spell It Out](plan_spell_it_out.md), holds that the thing is obvious, and the peer's sign-off stands behind them, so the test must be won against the felt clarity of everyone who knows.

## Tension

Testing against a real novice is the one move that finds the gap, and it forgoes the comfort of the obvious.
There is nothing to fault: obviousness is felt from inside and must be checked from outside, and the tester only insists on it. But the account feels clear to every knower and the test will show it is not, so the plot closes one of two ways: the gap is found and the explanation remade, bought against the ease of the obvious and the knower's certainty ... or the test is waved off with "but it's obvious," and the miss runs on, ratified and unseen.
