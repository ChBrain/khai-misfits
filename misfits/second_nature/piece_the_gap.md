# Piece: The Gap

## Taxonomy

[Second Nature](play_second_nature.md). The real distance between what the knower knows and what the learner knows, the chasm the account must cross and the knower cannot perceive.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Runs between [the lectern](place_the_lectern.md) and [the back row](place_the_back_row.md), unmarked by the one and felt by the other.
Made visible only at [the trial](place_the_trial.md), where a novice's stumble shows its width.

## Load Bearing

Without it the account would land as given, and there would be nothing for the explanation to fall into.
With it the trap has its real terrain: the actual difference between two minds, the steps one has made reflex and the other has not, the terms one reads plainly and the other cannot, that the account must bridge and mostly does not. The Gap is load bearing because it is the true distance the whole curse is about (Nickerson): the space between the knower and the learner, so the piece carries the chasm the explanation sails over, real and wide and there whether or not anyone can see it, the thing the knowing opened and the knowing hides.

## Apparent

Nothing, an unseen space behind a clear account, easy to assume closed.
Seen at the surface, the Gap does not appear: the account sounds plain and the room seems to follow, and the truth that a real distance runs between the knower and the learner, that the explanation crossed almost none of it, that its width grows with the expert's mastery, stays hidden behind how confidently a clear-sounding account assumes its reader already stands on the far side.

## Yearbook

Opened by [the knowledge](piece_the_knowledge.md) and hidden by [the blind spot](piece_the_blind_spot.md), the distance between the two minds.
It is the terrain of the trap: the Gap is what [the shorthand](piece_the_shorthand.md) fails to cross and [the miss](piece_the_miss.md) falls into, the real width [testing](process_testing.md) alone reveals, the chasm the knower opened in learning and can no longer see across in [second nature](piece_second_nature.md).
