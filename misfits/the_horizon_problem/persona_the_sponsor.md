# Persona: The Sponsor

## Taxonomy

[Sponsor](position_sponsor.md). The one who asks for a plan to a horizon and, by the ask, sets the edge the instrument will reach to. The archetype of the reasonable demand that fixes the instrument's reach.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reasonable. Decisive. Clear about what is needed.
Is any sponsor commissioning a plan: the board asking for a five-year strategy, the client asking for a projection to the end of the contract, the office asking for a roadmap to the next release.
No carelessness in it: the Sponsor names a horizon that fits the decision, and asks for a plan that reaches it, because a decision needs a plan sized to the choice in front of it.

## Action

Asks for a plan to a horizon: plan the next five years, cost it to the end of the lease, project to the last quarter that matters to this decision.
Names the edge by naming the span, and takes the plan back complete to that edge, sound and sufficient for the choice at hand.
Does what a sponsor must: it sizes the plan to the decision, because a plan without a horizon is not a decision aid, and the horizon that fits the choice is the one to ask for.

## Shadow

Not careless, a reasonable demand that fixes the edge, and that is the whole difficulty: the ask that sizes the plan to the decision is the ask that draws the boundary, and the boundary excludes everything past it.
Because the horizon that fits the choice is finite and must be, the Sponsor sets an edge past which the instrument does not reach, and never weighs the beyond and sets it aside but simply asks for the span the decision needs, so the boundary is drawn by a reasonable ask and the future past it falls outside the plan without anyone deciding it should.
It asks for the span the decision needs, and the span the decision needs is exactly the edge past which the future is excluded.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "plan it to five years," and means the horizon the decision requires, not a judgement about year six.
Says it sizing the plan to the choice, and cannot see the ask as drawing a boundary, because the horizon feels like the scope of the decision and not the edge of a tool, so the beyond is left out by a reasonable request that never considered it as something to leave in.
