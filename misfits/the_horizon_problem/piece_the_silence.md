# Piece: The Silence

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The un-flagging: nothing in the process marks that the beyond was excluded, so the plan reads complete, the boundary judgement never brought back into view.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Carried through [the model](place_the_model.md), where the plan reckons its costs and says nothing of its edge.
Broken at [the overlook](place_the_overlook.md), where someone names what the horizon excluded.

## Load Bearing

Without it the plan would announce its own boundary and the exclusion would show, and the trap would flag itself.
With it the trap holds unseen: nothing in the plan names what the horizon left out, because the instrument does not represent its own boundary as a cost, so the boundary judgement that placed the edge is never examined as a judgement at all (Ulrich, on the unexamined boundary judgement, the framing whose margins are never brought back into view). The Silence is load bearing because it is what lets the plan read whole: not a lie about the beyond, which could be caught, but nothing said about it, and nothing said reads exactly like there being nothing to say, so the piece carries the un-flagging, the absence of any mark that a future was excluded, the plan sound and complete to its edge and quiet past it.

## Apparent

A complete and candid plan, on the surface: everything accounted for, nothing hidden.
Seen at the surface, the Silence is invisible, because the plan says nothing about the beyond and its saying nothing looks like completeness, so the plan reads candid and whole, and the truth that a future was excluded and never flagged, that the quiet past the edge is an omission the instrument cannot mark, stays hidden behind how sound a plan that names every cost it holds feels.
