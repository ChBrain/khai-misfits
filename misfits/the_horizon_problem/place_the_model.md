# Place: The Model

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The planning instrument itself, where every cost inside the edge is counted and weighed, the room in which the plan is built to its horizon and the beyond is never entered.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the plan is built, a model, a spreadsheet, a forecast, a roadmap, where the instrument reckons every cost within its reach.
The Planner working the instrument to its edge, the costs inside the span counted and balanced, the plan taking shape complete to the horizon it was asked to cover.

## Holds

The costed, and the edge that bounds it.
Here every cost the instrument can reach is entered and weighed, so the Model holds the whole account of the plan: everything inside the span reckoned, balanced, and constrained, and where the instrument stops, a boundary past which nothing is counted, so it is where the plan is complete to its horizon and where the beyond, carrying no number, exerts no pull, the account full to its edge and empty past it.

## Offers

A sound plan and a settled account.
Because the Model is where every reachable cost is counted, it offers a plan complete to its horizon, balancing and sufficient, so it hands out the confidence of a full account, and hands it out on the span inside the edge and not the years past it, the beyond left unentered beneath a plan that reads whole.

## Withheld

Any sight of the years past its edge.
The Model shows every cost it can reach but cannot show what lies past its reach, because the beyond carries no number and a structurally absent cost leaves no gap where it was, so it withholds the future past the horizon, letting a full account to the edge stand for a full account, and, all reckoned within and silent without, it withholds the beyond until the years run out and the successor arrives in them.
