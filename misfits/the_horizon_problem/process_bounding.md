# Process: Bounding

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The instrument drawing its edge and excluding the beyond, undeliberately, the boundary that falls where the tool stops and sweeps the future past it out of the account.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An instrument with a finite reach and an ask that fixes it.
At [the horizon](place_the_horizon.md), the edge falling where the instrument stops, drawn by the reach of the tool and the span the ask named.

## Direction

Toward the beyond excluded and the boundary unexamined.
Because the instrument has a finite reach, bounding draws a line where it stops, and everything past the line falls outside the plan's account, not weighed and dismissed but structurally absent, and because the boundary is a property of the tool and not a decision, the judgement that placed the edge is never examined as a judgement at all, the line taken for the natural end of the plan.

## Lever

The edge as a property of the instrument.
Because the boundary is drawn by the reach of the tool and not by anyone choosing where to stop, bounding runs not on any deliberation but on the plain fact that an instrument covers a finite span, so the beyond is swept outside the account undeliberately, excluded by a line no one placed on purpose and no one examined, the future past it absent from the plan without a decision to exclude it.

## Echo

A boundary drawn, and the beyond swept outside it.
The instrument has reached its limit and stopped, so what reads as the end of the plan is, in truth, the edge of the tool, the future past it excluded structurally and unflagged, the boundary judgement never brought back into view, the years past the horizon absent from the account because the tool could not reach them.
