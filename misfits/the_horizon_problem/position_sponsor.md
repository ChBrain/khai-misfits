# Position: Sponsor

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The seat that asks for a plan to a horizon and, by the ask, sets the edge the instrument reaches to, drawing the boundary without deciding it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A decision to make and a plan to size to it: the choice at hand and the horizon that fits it.
A reasonable ask: a span named to match the decision, plan the five years, cost it to the lease, project to the release.
No way to size a plan without naming a horizon, and no sign that the horizon named is the edge past which the future is excluded.

## Orders

Ask for the plan to the horizon the decision needs.
Name the span that fits the choice, commission a plan that reaches it, and take it back complete to that edge.
Size the plan to the decision, because a plan without a horizon does not aid a choice, and the fitting horizon is the one to ask for.

## Loses

The chance of asking for the beyond, exactly because the ask is sized to the decision and not to the tool.
Because the horizon that fits the choice is finite and must be, the seat draws an edge past which the instrument does not reach, and never weighs the beyond but simply asks for the span the decision needs, so loses the future past the edge to a reasonable request that never met it as something to leave in.
It gains a plan sized to the decision, and loses the years past the horizon to the ask that sized it.

## Drives

Toward the plan sized to the decision and the edge set by the ask,
the fitting horizon named and the boundary drawn without deliberation,
so the seat that asks for the plan fixes the instrument's reach, the future past it excluded by a reasonable request that never considered it.
