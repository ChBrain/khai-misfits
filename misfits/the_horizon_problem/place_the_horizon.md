# Place: The Horizon

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The edge itself, the line where the instrument stops and past which nothing is costed, the boundary the plan is built to and the beyond falls past.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the plan ends, the last year of the model, the end of the projection, the final quarter of the roadmap, where the instrument reaches its limit.
The line where the costed stops and the beyond begins, drawn where the instrument stops and the ask fixed it, everything inside it counted and everything past it absent.

## Holds

The boundary, and the exclusion it makes.
Here the instrument reaches its limit and stops, so the Horizon holds the line that divides the plan from what it cannot reach: on one side the costed, weighed and constrained, on the other the beyond, carrying no number, so it is where the exclusion happens, undeliberately and unflagged, the boundary judgement that placed the edge never examined as a judgement at all, the line taken for the natural end of the plan.

## Offers

A finished plan and a natural stopping point.
Because the Horizon is where the instrument stops, it offers the plan its completion, the sense of an account run to its proper end, so it hands out the closure of a plan finished, and hands it out as if the edge were the end of what matters and not the edge of a tool, the beyond past it excluded by a line that looks like a conclusion.

## Withheld

Any sign that the edge is a boundary and not an end.
The Horizon shows the plan reaching its limit but cannot show that the limit is the instrument's and not the world's, because the line looks like where the plan naturally stops, so it withholds that the beyond exists and was excluded, letting the edge of the tool stand for the end of what matters, and, all completion on one side and silence on the other, it withholds the future past it until that future arrives.
