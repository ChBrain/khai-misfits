# Process: Scouting

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The remedy: looking past the edge, extending the horizon and entering the beyond into the plan as a cost, at cost, so the future the instrument excluded appears before it lands.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

Someone willing to look past the edge before it arrives.
At [the overlook](place_the_overlook.md), the Scout extending the horizon, stressing the plan against the years past its reach, and naming the costs waiting in the beyond.

## Direction

Toward the beyond made visible and the future costed before it lands.
Because the beyond carries no number in the plan and the plan reads whole without it, scouting extends the horizon past the instrument's reach and enters the excluded years as a constraint, so the future the tool could not hold is made visible while it can still be weighed, the exclusion undone deliberately and the cost of the beyond carried before it arrives.

## Lever

The willingness to pay to look.
Because the beyond casts no shadow to justify the looking and the plan reads complete without it, scouting runs not on any signal from the plan but on the choice to spend present effort and present numbers on years the instrument was built to bracket off, so the future is made visible only by paying, continuously, to doubt a plan that reads whole and defend a line item for years nothing in the plan flags.

## Echo

The beyond entered, and the ease of a whole plan given up.
The Scout has looked past the edge and carried the excluded years as a cost, so what reads as reaching for the future is, in truth, the exclusion undone at cost, the beyond made visible before it lands and the settled soundness of a plan that stops at its edge spent to hold the vantage open.
