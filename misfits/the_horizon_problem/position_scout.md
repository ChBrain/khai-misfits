# Position: Scout

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The seat that would look past the edge and enter the beyond into the plan as a cost, at cost, the remedy that carries the future the instrument brackets off.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A vantage past the edge and a case to make: the years the instrument does not reach and the argument that the plan should carry them.
A real remedy: extend the horizon, stress the plan against the beyond, name what the instrument excludes and enter it as a constraint.
No shadow from the beyond to justify the cost of looking, and no gap in the plan to point at, because the plan reads complete without the years the Scout would add.

## Orders

Look past the edge and cost the beyond.
Extend the horizon, stress the plan against the years past its reach, and enter the excluded future into the account as a constraint before it arrives.
Report back the costs waiting past the edge, so the plan can weigh them while they can still be weighed.

## Loses

The ease of a plan that reads complete, exactly because carrying the beyond costs now and the beyond casts no shadow to warrant it.
Because the future past the edge has no number in the plan and the plan reads whole without it, the seat must spend present effort and present numbers to hold open an edge the instrument gives no reason to doubt, and defend a line item for years nothing in the plan flags, so loses the settled soundness of the plan to the cost of looking past its horizon.
It makes the beyond visible as a cost, and the beyond having no shadow of its own is exactly what makes the cost of looking hard to justify.

## Drives

Toward the beyond made visible and the future costed before it lands,
the horizon extended and the excluded years entered as a constraint,
so the seat that looks past the edge carries the future the instrument could not reach, by paying, continuously, to doubt a plan that reads complete and cost years the tool was built to bracket off.
