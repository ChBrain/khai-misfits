# Plot: The Overlook

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Overlook](place_the_overlook.md), someone willing to look past the edge before it arrives.
The Scout at the vantage, ready to extend the horizon and name the costs waiting in the beyond.

## Action

[Scouting](process_scouting.md): [The Scout](persona_the_scout.md), holding [the scout's seat](position_scout.md), looks past [the edge](piece_the_edge.md) and enters [the beyond](piece_the_beyond.md) into the plan as a cost.

## Stage

The [scout](persona_the_scout.md), on [Look Past the Edge](plan_look_past_the_edge.md), extends the horizon past [the instrument](piece_the_instrument.md), stresses the plan against the years past its reach, and carries the excluded future as a constraint against [the silence](piece_the_silence.md).
The beyond is made visible while it can still be weighed, the future seen before it lands as [the landing](piece_the_landing.md) ... against a plan that reads complete without it and an edge the instrument gives no reason to doubt.

## Tension

Looking past the edge catches the beyond before it lands, and the beyond casting no shadow is what makes the looking hard to justify.
There is a real remedy: the horizon can be extended and the future costed. But the beyond carries no shadow to warrant the cost and the plan reads whole without it, so the plot closes with the future made visible only by paying, continuously, to doubt a sound plan and cost years the instrument was built to bracket off ... or the overlook is waived and the beyond left for whoever arrives.
