# Process: Planning

## Taxonomy

[The Horizon Problem](play_the_horizon_problem.md). The Planner building the plan within the edge, the necessary work of reckoning every cost the instrument can reach, sound to its horizon and silent past it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An instrument taken up and a horizon to plan to.
At [the model](place_the_model.md), the Planner reckoning every cost inside the reach, weighing and balancing until the plan is complete to its edge.

## Direction

Toward the plan complete to its horizon and the beyond never entered.
Because a plan is built with an instrument and the instrument has a finite reach, planning counts every cost inside the edge and produces a plan sound to its horizon, and because the beyond carries no number in the model, planning never meets it as a cost to weigh, so the account is full to the edge and empty past it, the plan whole and the future past it unentered.

## Lever

The finite reach of the instrument.
Because the tool covers a finite span and a plan without an edge is not a plan, planning runs not on any short-sightedness but on the plain necessity of building within the reach of the instrument at hand, the beyond excluded not by judgement but by the boundary of the tool, so the plan is sound to its horizon and the years past it fall outside the account with no one deciding they should.

## Echo

An account full to the edge, and the beyond unentered.
The Planner has reckoned every cost the instrument can reach, so what reads as a complete plan is, in truth, a plan complete to its horizon and silent past it, the future the tool could not hold left uncosted because it was never in the account, the plan whole because a structurally absent cost leaves no gap where it was.
