# Pitch: Save the Day

## Taxonomy

[The Firefighter's Medal](play_the_firefighters_medal.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Heroic, admirable, cheered.
The plain worth of the one who charges into the crisis and puts it out, works through the night, saves the launch and the day, and earns the medal for it.
Nothing false in it: the save is real and hard, and honouring it is only right.

## Undertow

Under the heroism, the quiet prevention that stops fires from ever starting, earning nothing at all.
Because a fought fire can be seen and a spared one cannot, reward and effort flow to the visible rescue and away from the invisible prevention, so prevention starves, fires come faster, and the more heroic firefighting there is to reward, until the whole place is a permanent, decorated blaze.
The reward meant to honour competence starves the one kind of work that would end the crises.

## Nerve

The feeling of pinning the medal on the hero who saved the day, sure you are rewarding the best,
because the save was real and the rescue plain to see,
while down the hall the one who kept the fire from ever lighting gets nothing, and everyone learns where the glory goes.

## Echo

After the run: a place ablaze and decorated for it, everyone run off their feet and improving nothing,
no one cynical and no one idle, only real saves honoured and real emergencies answered and prevention quietly starved,
and a fenced capacity that would have ended the fires, refused because it meant letting a fire burn to protect against one that would never show, and pinning the medal on the person nothing happened to.
