# The Firefighter's Medal: Reference

The research warrant for [The Firefighter's Medal](play_the_firefighters_medal.md).
The dynamic is Repenning and Sterman's; the play runs on their firefighting-trap account of process improvement,
through Bohn's firefighting stages, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the firefighting trap as a **capability system**: an organisation with two kinds of work, prevention,
the improvement that stops crises from starting, and firefighting, the visible rescue that puts them out, in
which the save is provable and honoured while prevention's success is a non-event no one can point to, so
reward and effort flow to firefighting, prevention starves, capability erodes, fires come faster, and the
system tips from a self-sustaining improvement regime into a self-sustaining firefighting one. The play models
the documented mechanics, the vivid crisis and the unmistakable save, the reward that can measure the fire
fought but never the fire spared, the rational drain of effort from unrewarded prevention into rewarded
firefighting, the reinforcing loop in which less prevention means more fires and more fires means less
prevention, the tipping point past which firefighting becomes self-sustaining, and the fenced capacity and
counterfactual credit that alone reverse it, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a
tale of a lazy team or a cynical boss. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: because
prevention is unobservable and firefighting is rewarded, effort flows from the former to the latter, and the
resulting loss of capability is self-reinforcing, tipping the system into a firefighting regime that consumes
the very capacity needed to escape it. It is distinct from the house's Shifting the Burden, where reliance on
the symptomatic fix lets the fundamental-solution capacity atrophy from disuse: there the engine is dependence
and atrophy, here it is a reward-and-visibility asymmetry, the prevented crisis that earns no credit, plus a
bistable tipping dynamic. It is distinct from the house's The Shirky Principle, where an institution preserves
the problem it lives on: here no one needs the fires, everyone would rather they stopped. And it is distinct
from the slated Tyranny of the Urgent, where urgency's salience crowds out importance in one person's attention:
here the driver is the reward system and the erosion of collective capability, not the pull of the salient.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the firefighter as
  a glory-hound or the manager as cynical, because the source finding is precisely that each move is sound: the
  save is real and hard and worth honouring, the firefighter is genuinely good, rewarding the visible is the
  only fair thing when the prevented crisis cannot be seen, and triaging the urgent fire over the distant one is
  what any pressed manager must do. The blaze is authored by no one and emerges from the structure, a reward
  that can measure the fire fought but never the fire spared, so the misfit is that a system meaning to honour
  competence starves the prevention that would end its crises and decorates itself into a permanent fire.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that turns the
  loop back is to fence capacity for prevention even while fires burn and to credit the crisis that never came,
  and it means letting a present fire burn a little to protect against a future one, rewarding a non-event that
  cannot be proven, and withholding the medal from the visible hero to pin it on the person nothing happened to,
  against every instinct that runs toward the drama and the save. The reckoning is a fire ended by spending real
  capacity and real glory on crises that will now never light and can never be shown, not a free correction, so
  the cure for a system ablaze is the credit given to the quiet and the calm.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the firefighting trap, the reward asymmetry, and the capability
  tipping dynamic are Repenning and Sterman's, the firefighting stages are Bohn's, and the condition the trap
  actually requires, an unobservable prevention rewarded less than a visible rescue, feeding a self-reinforcing
  loss of the capability that would end the fires, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

  that demands an answer now.

- **[The Averted Fire](piece_the_averted_fire.md)**: carries the counterfactual (Repenning & Sterman), the
  crisis prevention stopped from ever starting, the non-event no one can point to or cheer.
- **[The Spiral](piece_the_spiral.md)**: carries the bistable tip (Repenning & Sterman, 2002), the reinforcing
  loop by which starved prevention breeds more fires and more fires starve prevention.

  capacity and counterfactual credit that reverse the loop, at the cost of a present fire and the visible glory.
- **[The Firefighter's Medal](piece_the_firefighters_medal.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Repenning &
  Sterman), a system that decorates the save, starves the prevention, and burns.
