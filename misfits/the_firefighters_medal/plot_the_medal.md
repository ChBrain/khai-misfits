# Plot: The Medal

## Taxonomy

[The Firefighter's Medal](play_the_firefighters_medal.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Podium](place_the_podium.md), the save retold and the reward to be given.
A fire fought that can be cheered, and a fire spared that cannot even be named.

## Action

[Rewarding](process_rewarding.md): [The Manager](persona_the_manager.md), holding [the manager's seat](position_manager.md), pins the credit on the visible save, while [The Preventer](persona_the_preventer.md), holding [the preventer's seat](position_preventer.md), sits with a quiet quarter and nothing to be called up for.

## Stage

[The Manager](persona_the_manager.md), still on [Fight the Fire](plan_fight_the_fire.md), honours the firefighter before everyone, crediting the result it can point to.
[The Preventer](persona_the_preventer.md), on [Head It Off](plan_head_it_off.md), whose quiet work months ago kept fires from starting, gets nothing, because no one can point to the disaster that never came.

## Tension

The fair reward is the one that cannot find the greater achievement.
There is nothing to fault: the save is real and the prevention is a non-event, and you cannot credit a fire that never lit. But the medal goes to the seen and passes the unseen, so the plot closes with the glory pinned on the rescue and withheld from the prevention, and everyone on the floor now taught, plainly, that the way to be valued is to be seen fighting a fire.
