# Plot: Net It Out

## Taxonomy

[Watermelon Reporting](play_watermelon_reporting.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The programme board](place_the_programme_board.md), a wall of tiles, one of them red.
The red status has climbed as far as it can on its own project, so the next move is not to raise it further but to set it among its neighbours, where one red among many greens is a rounding error.

## Action

[Aggregation](process_aggregation.md): [The Aggregator](persona_the_aggregator.md), holding [the programme manager's seat](position_programme_manager.md), reports the programme by the balance of its projects, and the single red becomes a managed issue inside a healthy whole.

## Stage

[The Aggregator](persona_the_aggregator.md) tables [Net It Out](plan_net_it_out.md): report the aggregate, cast the red as one issue among many, and keep the programme on track, so [the net green](piece_the_net_green.md) is the colour that reaches the level above.
The red is not rounded now, it is averaged: true at the rung the seat answers for, and blind to the project beneath it that is genuinely failing.

## Tension

The programme really is mostly green, and that is exactly the cover.
The Aggregator reports honestly at the level it is accountable for, and at that level a healthy balance of projects is the true picture, and a single red issue among many is genuinely a managed one. But netting is not fixing: the failing project is still failing, its red merely divided into a number too small to move the aggregate, so the higher the report climbs the more completely the one project that needed attention is dissolved into the health of the many that did not.
