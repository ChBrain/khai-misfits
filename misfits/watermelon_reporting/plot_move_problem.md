# Plot: Move Problem

## Taxonomy

[Watermelon Reporting](play_watermelon_reporting.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The workface](place_the_workface.md), one desk along, the red input arriving at the next task.
The task before this one ran late and handed its work over unfinished, and the seat receiving it has its own dates to protect and no wish to send the work back.

## Action

[Execution](process_execution.md): [The Neighbour](persona_the_neighbour.md), holding [the second doer's seat](position_second_doer.md), takes the hand-off as it comes, works around the gap, and keeps its own task moving.
The red does not clear, it relocates: booked against the upstream task, or the phase after this one, as [the external problem](piece_the_external_problem.md) that explains a green status here.

## Stage

[The Neighbour](persona_the_neighbour.md) tables [Take the Hand-off](plan_take_the_handoff.md): accept the input and protect downstream progress, so this seat reports green while the problem it inherited is logged as someone else's column.
[The colour travels](piece_the_colour_travels.md): the red steps from the task that made it to the task that received it, and at each hand-off the giver goes green and the taker owns a problem that is, honestly, upstream.

## Tension

Everyone is keeping the work moving, and the red never lands.
The Neighbour is right to protect its dates, right to work around a gap rather than idle, and right that the fault really was upstream, so nothing it books is false. But a problem that is always someone else's column is a problem that is nowhere on the board as red, and by moving with the work instead of stopping it, the red is carried the length of the critical path without ever being the thing this status has to show.
