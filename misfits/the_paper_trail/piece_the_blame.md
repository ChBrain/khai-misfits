# Piece: The Blame

## Taxonomy

[The Paper Trail](play_the_paper_trail.md). The incentive aimed at the process: failure met with who broke procedure, not what went wrong.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the tribunal](place_the_tribunal.md), where a bad outcome is judged.
Where the question asked is who deviated, and the clean file is the answer that acquits.

## Load Bearing

Without it none of the covering would pay, and the whole trail would never start.
With it the reward is moved off the outcome and onto the record: because the tribunal faults the undocumented judgement and spares the documented folly, being defensible, not being effective, becomes what protects the worker. The Blame is load bearing because it is the source of the bend (Tetlock: accountability to a process changes what people optimise): every cover, every ticked box, every spread sign-off is a rational answer to it, so the aim recedes not from laziness but from each hand correctly reading what the tribunal will punish.

## Apparent

Accountability.
A review that asks who followed the rules and faults the one who did not looks like an organisation taking failure seriously, so the blame reads as rigour, and its aim at the process rather than the outcome, at the deviation rather than the result, stays hidden behind the look of a standard enforced.

## Yearbook

Made by [the blamer](persona_the_blamer.md) through [blaming](process_blaming.md), tabled as [Punish the Deviation](plan_punish_the_deviation.md).
It is the incentive at the root of the trail: the Blame sets [the cover](piece_the_cover.md) paying, and when it meets the failure it aimed to prevent it dissolves into [the diffusion](piece_the_diffusion.md).
