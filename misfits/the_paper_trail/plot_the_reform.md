# Plot: The Reform

## Taxonomy

[The Paper Trail](play_the_paper_trail.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The reform](place_the_reform.md), someone moved to turn the incentive back toward the aim.
The proposal is to judge the honesty of the judgement and not the thickness of the file, to reward the honest failure over the documented folly, and to give people the safety to own a result.

## Action

[Reforming](process_reforming.md): [The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), holding [the reformer's seat](position_reformer.md), changes what is punished and moves to prune the defensive paper, while [The Diligent](persona_the_diligent.md), holding [the professional's seat](position_professional.md), stands to gain the room to work on judgement again.

## Stage

[The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md) tables [Change the Incentive](plan_change_the_incentive.md) and answers [the paper trail](piece_the_paper_trail.md) by turning [the blame](piece_the_blame.md) from the process to the honesty of the judgement.
[The Diligent](persona_the_diligent.md) tables [Use My Judgement](plan_use_my_judgement.md) and finds it might, at last, no longer read as a corner cut.

## Tension

The one real way out runs straight into two walls, and cannot be sure it has cleared either.
Changing what is punished is the only move that turns effort back to the aim, but the blame culture is entrenched and serves those who run it, and the hardest part is that some of the paper was a genuine safeguard: the reformer cannot tell the defensive file from the real check, since they look identical, so the pruning hand may tear out, with the ritual, the thing that was actually holding something up. It closes with the work turned back toward the outcome, or the culture holding, or a safeguard cut at the peril of a Chesterton's fence.
