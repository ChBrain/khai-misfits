# Process: Sorting

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The uneven allocation of cases across the groups, the lurking variable loading the subgroups differently and laying the setup for a reversal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A third variable correlated with both the grouping and the outcome.
At [the breakdown](place_the_breakdown.md), the world assigning cases to groups unevenly, the hard cases to one treatment, the many applicants to the selective departments, so the subgroups fill unalike.

## Direction

Toward groups that are not comparable and a total that will mislead.
Because the lurking variable is spread unevenly across the groups, sorting makes the subgroups differ in more than the thing being compared, so pooling them will average unlike things with unlike weights, and the setup is laid for the aggregate to point the opposite way from every part, without anyone arranging it.

## Lever

The ordinary way the world assigns cases.
Because sicker patients really are given the newer treatment and more applicants really do apply to the harder programmes, the uneven sorting is the world behaving as it does, not a bias introduced, so sorting runs on real structure, the confound assembled from no manipulation, only from cases falling into groups the way they actually fall.

## Echo

Groups that cannot be fairly pooled, and no sign of it in the total.
The cases have been sorted unevenly and the subgroups made unalike, so what reads, once summed, as one clean comparison is, in truth, a mixture of incomparable groups, the confounder's uneven hand hidden in a total that shows no trace of the sorting beneath it.
