# Piece: The Total

## Load Bearing

Without it there is only a set of subgroups, and no whole to reverse them and no authority to persuade.
With it the trap has its seductive pole: a single number built from every row, carrying the weight of the larger sample, that reads as the fuller and truer picture. The Total is load bearing because it is the aggregate the reversal turns on (Simpson): the honest pooled figure that can point the opposite way from every subgroup, so the piece carries the whole whose very completeness is what lets an uneven confounder flip it, the big number that feels the most right and can be the most wrong.

## Apparent

The truth, the plain and authoritative answer built from all the data there is.
Seen at the surface, the Total looks like the clearest view, a number too large and too complete to doubt, and the truth that it mixes unlike groups with unlike weights, that its completeness hides the sorting beneath it, that a whole built from incomparable parts can reverse them all, stays hidden behind how much a bigger sample feels like a better one.
