# Process: Adjudicating

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The choosing of the level to trust by a causal model, the reversal resolved through arrows drawn from outside the table, at the cost of an assumption it cannot check.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A causal question the counts cannot answer, and a modeller willing to draw it.
At [the diagram](place_the_diagram.md), the modeller setting the arrows between the variables, judging which is confounder and which is mediator, to decide which level licenses the decision.

## Direction

Toward the paradox resolved, if the arrows are right.
Because whether to adjust the grouping away or keep it turns on what caused it, adjudicating brings a causal structure to the table and reads the reversal through it, so it drives toward the level chosen and the decision grounded: but the structure is assumed, not measured, so the process resolves the paradox only by staking it on a cause the data can neither supply nor confirm.

## Lever

The truth that only causation can choose between the levels.
Because a table shows association and the choice of level is a causal one, drawing the cause is the one move that can settle the reversal, so adjudicating runs on the correct insight: yet the same move that resolves the paradox commits to arrows the numbers cannot check, so the process is right exactly when its guess at the cause is, and no more surely.

## Echo

A level chosen and a decision licensed, or the reversal reversed once more.
Either the causal structure is drawn correctly and the right level chosen, bought against an assumption the data cannot confirm, or the arrows are guessed wrong, the wrong variable adjusted, and the reversal reversed with the same confidence, the paradox settled only as well as the cause beneath it was known.
