# Plot: The Split

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Breakdown](place_the_breakdown.md), the same data read group by group.
The total's one clean answer on the table, the subgroups there to be read apart, the suspicion that the parts may not behave like the whole.

## Action

[Splitting](process_splitting.md): [The Splitter](persona_the_splitter.md), holding [the splitter's seat](position_splitter.md), separates the subgroups and finds the opposite trend in every one, equally honest.

## Stage

[The Splitter](persona_the_splitter.md), on [Split It Out](plan_split_it_out.md), breaks the table into its groups and reads the trend within each, and every single subgroup points the other way from the total.
Both readings stand, computed from the identical rows: the whole says one thing and each part says the opposite, and neither is a miscount.

## Tension

Disaggregating is the rigorous move, and reading every part correctly settles nothing about which level to trust.
There is nothing to fault: the strata are honestly computed and truly hold. But whether the grouping should be adjusted away or kept turns on a cause the data does not hold, so the plot closes with the parts and the whole openly at odds, the reversal laid bare and neither level able to prove it is the one to act on.
