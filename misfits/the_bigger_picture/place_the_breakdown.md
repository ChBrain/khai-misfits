# Place: The Breakdown

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The stratified view where the data is split by group, the subgroups laid out apart and the uneven sorting made plain.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the table is broken into its groups, each subgroup laid out on its own with its own trend, and the uneven counts across them there to be seen.
The splitter reading each stratum, the lurking variable's uneven load showing in the cell sizes, every group pointing the same way and against the total.

## Holds

The sorting and the splitting, and the parts the whole hid.
Here the data meets its groups, so the Breakdown holds the point where the strata speak apart: each subgroup read on its own and the uneven allocation shown, so it is where the confounder's fingerprints appear and every part points against the pooled total, the local trend plain where the aggregate blurred it.

## Offers

The true trend within each group, and the sight of how unevenly they were filled.
Because the Breakdown lays the subgroups out apart, it offers the splitter each stratum's honest reading and shows the uneven counts that loaded them, so it hands out the parts the total hid and the fingerprints of the confounder, and hands them out without the one thing that would say whether this grouping is the level to trust.

## Withheld

Any proof that the split is the right level to act on.
The Breakdown can show the true trend in every group and the uneven sorting between them, but it cannot say whether that grouping should be adjusted away or kept, so it withholds the causal fact that would license its level, leaving the strata true and mute on their standing, and, all parts and no cause, it withholds any sign that reading each group correctly can still be reading the wrong level of all.
