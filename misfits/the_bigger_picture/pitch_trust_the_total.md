# Pitch: Trust the Total

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Authoritative, reasonable, above the noise.
The plain good sense of stepping back to the whole, reading the big number built from all the data, and trusting the larger sample over the smaller slices.
Nothing false in it: the aggregate is honestly computed and really is built from every row, and trusting the fuller picture is a sound instinct.

## Undertow

Under the authority of the whole, a lurking variable that sorted the cases unevenly into the groups, so the total points the opposite way from every subgroup.
Because the groups are not alike and pooling mixes them with unlike weights, the aggregate can reverse the trend that holds in each part, and both readings are correct, and which should guide the decision turns on a cause the numbers do not carry.
The bigger picture that feels like rising to the truth is a mixture that can point the wrong way.

## Nerve

The feeling of stepping back to the whole and trusting the big honest number, sure you are seeing past the noise,
because the aggregate is larger and computed from all of it,
while every subgroup in the same data says the opposite, and the table cannot tell you which level to believe.

## Echo

After the run: a decision taken on the pooled total and against every subgroup, right or wrong with no way on the page to know,
no one miscounting and no one lying, only an honest aggregate and honest strata that disagree, both true of the level they describe,
and the one thing that would settle it, the causal structure that says which variable to adjust, brought in from outside or not at all, unprovable by the data it must interpret.
