# Position: Splitter

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The seat that separates the data by group and reads each stratum, where the honest parts point the opposite way from the whole and may still be the wrong level.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The groups laid out apart and the trend within each: the standing to disaggregate the table and read every subgroup on its own.
A clear case: the parts may not behave like the whole, and an average that hides what every group shows is hiding something.
No way to prove, from the numbers, that the grouping it split on is the level the decision belongs to.

## Orders

Split it out.
Break the table into its subgroups, read the trend in each, and refuse to let an average blur what every group plainly shows.
Let each stratum speak for itself, and hold the parts against the whole.

## Loses

The certainty that its level is the right one, exactly by reading the parts correctly.
Because whether the grouping should be adjusted away or kept turns on what caused it, the seat's honest strata guide the decision only if the grouping is on the causal path, and the data cannot say it is, so splitting correctly can be splitting on a variable that should have stayed pooled, breaking the truth into the wrong answer with full rigour.
It gains the true trend within each group, and loses any proof that the within-group trend is the one to act on.

## Drives

Toward the data split and each part read true,
bought against the proof that the parts are the right level,
so the seat that reads every group correctly may still read the wrong level of all.
