# Process: Splitting

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The disaggregating of the data by group, each stratum read on its own and shown to point against the total, true within its group and mute on its standing.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The suspicion that the parts may not behave like the whole.
At [the breakdown](place_the_breakdown.md), the splitter breaking the table into its subgroups and reading the trend within each, unwilling to let an average blur what every group shows.

## Direction

Toward the true local trend and the reversal laid bare.
Because the subgroups were loaded unevenly, reading them apart shows the within-group trend the aggregate hid, so splitting reveals that every group points the opposite way from the total, and lays the reversal bare, each stratum honest and the whole and the parts now openly at odds.

## Lever

The rigour of refusing to blur unlike groups.
Because the parts genuinely may not behave like the whole and an average really can hide what every group shows, disaggregating is the careful, rigorous move, so splitting runs on good practice, the wrong-level risk assembled from no error, only from reading correctly a grouping the data cannot confirm is the one to act on.

## Echo

Every part true and against the whole, and no proof of which to trust.
The strata have been read apart and each shown to point against the total, so what reads as the granular truth the aggregate concealed is, in truth, one of two correct readings, honest within each group and unable, from the numbers alone, to prove that its level is the one the decision belongs to.
