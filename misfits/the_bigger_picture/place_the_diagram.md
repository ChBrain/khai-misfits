# Place: The Diagram

## Taxonomy

[The Bigger Picture](play_the_bigger_picture.md). The causal graph where the arrows between the variables are drawn, the space off the table where the level to trust could be settled, or guessed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the variables are drawn with arrows between them, a graph of what is taken to cause what, standing off the table of counts.
The modeller setting the structure, marking one variable a confounder to adjust and another a mediator to leave, and reading the reversal through the arrows.

## Holds

The adjudicating, and the cause the table lacks.
Here the counts meet a claim about the world, so the Diagram holds the point where the level is chosen: the causal structure drawn and the confounder named, so it is where the reversal can be resolved, the arrows deciding what the numbers alone never could.

## Offers

A way to pick the level, and the cause the counting left out.
Because the Diagram carries what the table cannot, a model of what produced the data, it offers the modeller the ground to adjudicate the reversal and choose which level licenses the decision, so it hands out the resolution the numbers withheld, and hands it out resting on arrows that are assumed, not measured.

## Withheld

Any proof that the arrows are right.
The Diagram can settle which level to trust once the causal structure is drawn, but it cannot draw that structure from the data or confirm it against the table, so it withholds the certainty that the model is correct, leaving the resolution as sound as the guess beneath it, and, all cause and no check, it withholds any guarantee that the wrong arrows will not reverse the reversal with equal confidence.
