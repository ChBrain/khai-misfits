## Action

## Stage

Nothing is wrong: no mask under fire, no stake yet raised, only the standing structure of being seen doing what it always does, the front up because to be in the room is already to be in front of it.

## Tension

The front is up before anything is at stake, and that is the whole point: it is not a response to threat but the baseline of being seen.
There is nothing defensive here: no one is protecting a face under pressure, the account is honest and the room is at ease. But a self presented is a self this side of the curtain, so the plot closes with the performed self established as the room's baseline and not a reaction to anything, the back stage sealed by the plain fact of an audience, the trap running before any threat gives it a reason.
