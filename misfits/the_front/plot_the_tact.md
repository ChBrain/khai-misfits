# Plot: The Tact

## Taxonomy

[The Front](play_the_front.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Footlights](place_the_footlights.md), a front presented and a slip showing through it.
A flicker of the raw crossing a colleague's composure, a half-said doubt, a crack in the resolved account, and the room deciding, by reflex of manners, what to do with it.

## Action

[Face-Work](process_face_work.md): [The Room](persona_the_room.md), holding [the audience's seat](position_audience.md), smooths the slip, grants the face, and does not look behind the front, upholding jointly the performance it is shown.

## Stage

[The Room](persona_the_room.md), on [Uphold the Front](plan_uphold_the_front.md), meets the flicker of [the raw signal](piece_the_raw_signal.md) across [the curtain](piece_the_curtain.md) with courtesy, accepts [the version](piece_the_version.md) as competence, and returns the slip, unread, to the back stage.
A considerate room does not call a colleague's composure, so the raw that slipped across is met with tact and handed back, the front held up by everyone's agreement not to look behind it.

## Tension

The room's tact is real kindness, and the very kindness seals the back stage the raw was trying to cross.
There is nothing cynical: sparing a colleague the discomfort of being seen unresolved is what manners require. But the flicker it smooths away is the burning signal reaching for the room, so the plot closes with the slip returned and the face granted, the audience doing its considerate half of the front and the raw signal met with courtesy and kept, out of respect, behind the curtain.
