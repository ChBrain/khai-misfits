# Play: The Front

## Name

The Front.
Someone gives an update, and it is a good update: clear, resolved, the problem framed and the path named, the kind of account a competent person gives. And here is the thing about being in a room with others: there is a front stage, the version you present, and a back stage, the version before you presented it, and this is not a mask you put on when threatened, it is the standing structure of being seen at all, running before anything is at stake, because to be in front of anyone is already to be performing a self to them. So the person with something to say weighs it honestly, the way anyone would: the raw version, the tangle still being worked, the half-formed worry, the thing that is still burning, reads as noise, as not-yet-competent, as a person who has not done the work of thinking it through; and the processed version, the same matter resolved into a clean line, reads as competence, as a colleague who has it handled. So they process it before they show it, and this is not vanity, it is arithmetic: the resolved version is what lands, what the room rewards, what marks them as someone to trust with the next thing, while the raw version costs them standing for no gain they can see. And each person reasons the same, in good faith, and each is right about how their own signal will read, and so, one by one, what enters the room is the finished account and never the unfinished one, and the room, for its part, does its half: it meets each front with tact, smooths the small slips, grants each person the face they are claiming, because upholding the front on offer is what a considerate room does. And it holds, and it looks like candour, right up until the day the group needs the thing that was still burning, the doubt no one had resolved enough to say, the tangle someone was still inside, the raw signal that never got processed into anything presentable and so never got said at all, and the group discovers it has been sharing only what was already settled, moving only on what was already safe to show, and the one piece of information it most needed was the one piece that could not be made to read as competence in time. Nobody lied. Nobody performed a false self: each simply showed the resolved version because the resolved version is what a room rewards and the raw version is what a room reads as noise, and chose, as any person being watched would, the account that lands. It is only that a group whose members each rationally present the processed self is a group that shares what it has finished and starves for what it has not, and the front that every good colleague puts up, honestly, in good faith, is the front behind which the burning thing quietly stays.

## Arc

The bend is this: to be seen is already to perform, so each person edits the raw into the presentable before showing it, and a room that rewards the resolved and reads the raw as noise gets, in aggregate, only what is already finished and never the burning thing it most needs.
It runs on a split that is the baseline, not the exception.
Every exchange has a front stage and a back stage: the version presented and the version before presentation, and Goffman's claim is that this split is the universal structure of interaction and not a deviation from it, so the performed self is running before any threat appears and there is no unperformed moment behind it to recover (Goffman, on the presentation of self, the front and back regions as the standing architecture of social life). The performance is the condition of being seen, not a mask worn under fire, and this is what makes it a trap and not a fault, because there is no version of being in a room that is not already a version presented.
And legibility is rewarded, so the processed reads as competent and the raw as noise. In any group that prizes clear accounts, the resolved version, the tangle worked into a line, reads as a colleague who has done the thinking, while the raw version, the half-formed, the still-uncertain, reads as noise, as someone not yet on top of it, so self-presentation is motivated by how the signal will land and the incentive points, always, to showing the finished side (Leary & Kowalski, on impression management as a two-component motivation, the wish to make a given impression driving what is shown; Baumeister, on self-presentation as motivated cognition). The premium on the legible is what makes the edit rational.
And so editing becomes individually rational. For a person weighing what to show, the raw carries a real cost in standing and the processed carries a real gain, so they process the burning thing into the presentable before they show it, or, when it will not process in time, they hold it, and the choice to edit is not deceit but the honest reckoning of a self being read (Hochschild, on emotional labour, the managed presentation of feeling as the price of the role). No one performs a false self: each shows the resolved version because that is the version that lands.
And the room upholds every front it is shown. Being seen is a joint production: the audience meets each performance with tact, repairs the small slip, grants the face being claimed, because a considerate room does not call the bluff of a colleague's composure, so fronts are held up not by the performer alone but by everyone's collaboration in not looking behind them (Goffman, on face-work, the mutual tact by which participants sustain one another's line). The room's courtesy is the other half of the front, and it seals the back stage as surely as the performer does.
And the aggregate is a starved room. What each rationally shows is the finished account, so the group, summing those choices, holds only what is already resolved and never what is still burning, and moves only on the safe, settled signal, until the day it needs the doubt no one had processed enough to say and finds that signal was never in the room (the suppression running in every direction at once, upward and sideways and down and toward the self). The information the group most needs is precisely the information that cannot be made to read as competence, so it is precisely the information that never gets shown.
None of it is deceit or cowardice. Each shows the resolved version because a room rewards the resolved and reads the raw as noise, so no one lies and the burning thing stays back anyway. What makes a colleague valuable, that they give clear, resolved, legible accounts, is exactly what makes each of them edit away the unresolved thing the group cannot get any other way, and a self presented is a self whose back stage no one in the room can see.
Open the back stage, and the raw can enter: build the trust and pay the exposure that lets a person show the unfinished thing, make the room safe to be seen mid-thought and slow to read the raw as noise, and the burning signal can reach the group: but this means paying, in real standing and real exposure, to show the very thing a room is built to reward hiding, against an incentive that runs before any threat and never lets up, so the front comes down only by someone carrying, each time, the cost of being seen unresolved in a room that reads the resolved as competence.

Closes when the performed self is established as the baseline of the room and not a response to anything, the front up before any stake appears, the back stage sealed by the plain fact of being watched.

**[The Edit](plot_the_edit.md)**
Cue: The Burning holds something still unresolved, a tangle, a doubt, a thing still burning, and weighs whether to show it. Reckoning honestly that the raw will read as noise and the processed as competence, the Burning edits it into the presentable, or, when it will not process, holds it back. Closes when the raw has been worked down toward the showable and, in the working, thinned to what is already safe, the burning part left back because it could not be made to read as competent in time, the edit sound and the signal quietly lost in it.

The Room, considerate, does its half: it smooths the slip, grants the face, does not look behind the front, upholding jointly what the performer is presenting. Closes when the room's tact has sealed the back stage as surely as the performer did, the front held up by everyone's collaboration in not calling it, the raw signal met with courtesy and returned, unread, to behind the curtain.

**[The Starvation](plot_the_starvation.md)**

It reaches for that signal and finds only the finished accounts, the settled matter, the safe and resolved. Closes when the group discovers it has been sharing only what was already resolved and starving for what was still burning, the one piece of information it most needed never in the room, no one having lied and the front, honestly kept by each, the reason it is not there.

**[The Price](plot_the_price.md)**
Cue: the Host would open the back stage, make the room safe to be seen unresolved, invite the whole self and mean it. Through trust, through going first, through refusing to read the raw as noise, the Host works to let the burning signal enter. Closes when the front comes down for someone who paid, in real standing and real exposure, to be seen mid-thought ... or the invitation is judged too costly against a room that rewards the resolved, the whole-self opening itself performed as one more front, and the back stage left sealed, the burning thing left back.

## Stakes

Whether a group can get the signal it most needs, when the signal it most needs is the one that cannot be made to read as competence and every member is rationally showing the version that can.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the resolved version shown, which each person is right to show because the room rewards it, and away from the raw version said, which costs the one who says it standing for a gain the room cannot see. The production cannot make the private incentive match the group's need, because to be seen is already to perform and a room that reads the resolved as competence and the raw as noise makes each person's edit sound, so the burning thing stays back not from cowardice but from the plain arithmetic of a self being read: no one lies and every account is honest. It can only show that each showed the processed self because that is the self that lands, that the room's own tact seals the back stage the performer already sealed, that the suppression runs in every direction at once and starves the group of the doubt it most needs, and that the one move that lets the raw in, opening the back stage and making the room safe to be seen unresolved, must be made by someone paying, in real exposure, to show the very thing the room is built to reward hiding, so the group is candid and each is honest and the burning signal it was starving for is the one signal the front, kept in good faith by all, was built to keep back.
