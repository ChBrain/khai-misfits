# Piece: The Managed Heart

## Taxonomy

[The Front](play_the_front.md). The private cost of holding the front, the emotional labour of the sustained presentation, the price of the role that makes dropping the front an act of exposure and candour never free.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Paid across [the stand-up](place_the_stand_up.md) and beyond it, wherever the composed front must be sustained.
Weighed at [the all-hands](place_the_all_hands.md), where the cost of being seen unresolved is set against an invitation to be.

## Load Bearing

Without it the front would cost nothing to hold and nothing to drop, and there would be no price on the remedy.
With it the trap has its reckoning: the emotional labour of the sustained front, the managed presentation of composure and feeling as the price of the role, the real if unseen cost of keeping the version up over time (Hochschild, on the managed heart, emotional labour as the ongoing cost of a presented affect). The Managed Heart is load bearing because it is why there is no free hero: the cost that makes opening the back stage an exposure and not a choice, so the piece carries the reason candour is dear, that composure was never free and to be seen unresolved is to stop paying a cost the room had come to expect and to pay a different one instead.

## Apparent

An unbothered colleague, composed, handling it, no cost in sight.
Seen at the surface, the Managed Heart is invisible, because a sustained front looks exactly like ease, and the truth that the composure is labour, that holding the version up has a private price paid where no one sees it, that the calm the room reads as capacity is the cost that makes the whole self so rarely brought, stays hidden behind how effortless a held front looks.

## Yearbook

Paid by [the polished](persona_the_polished.md) and [the burning](persona_the_burning.md) through [surface acting](process_surface_acting.md), unlifted by [the host](persona_the_host.md) whose invitation cannot make it free.
It is the reckoning of the trap: the Managed Heart is the cost of holding [the version](piece_the_version.md) up across [the curtain](piece_the_curtain.md), the price [the premium](piece_the_premium.md) exacts, and the exposure that makes ending [the starvation](piece_the_starvation.md) a thing someone must pay for, not a free correction.
