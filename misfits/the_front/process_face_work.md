# Process: Face-Work

## Taxonomy

[The Front](play_the_front.md). The room's mutual tact by which each performance is jointly upheld, the courtesy that smooths the slip and grants the face and, in sparing the performer, seals the back stage the raw was trying to cross.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A front presented and a slip showing through it.
At the [stand-up](place_the_stand_up.md), the room meeting an account with courtesy, a flicker of raw showing across [the curtain](piece_the_curtain.md) and the room deciding, by reflex of manners, what to do with it.

## Direction

Toward the front upheld and the slip returned unread.
Because a considerate room does not call a colleague's composure, face-work accepts the resolved as competence and smooths the flicker of raw, so being seen is revealed as a joint production and not a solo one, the audience upholding the performance it is shown, and the raw signal that slipped across the curtain is met with tact and handed back, unheard, to the back stage it came from.

## Lever

The courtesy that a decent room owes a colleague.
Because sparing a person the discomfort of being seen unresolved is what manners require, face-work runs not on any wish to suppress but on plain respect, the raw signal returned by no unkindness, only by the tact that a good audience extends, so the back stage is sealed a second time, by the room's courtesy after the performer's edit, both in good faith.

## Echo

A slip smoothed and a face granted, the front held up by the room.
The audience has genuinely been considerate, so what reads as respect is, in truth, the other half of the front, the flicker of raw met with tact and returned, the performance upheld jointly and the back stage sealed by everyone's agreement not to look behind it.
