# The Dilbert Principle: Reference

The research warrant for [The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md).
The coinage is Adams's; the play runs on his observation set against the Peter Principle it inverts, through
the practitioner record of the kicked-upstairs exit and the cost-of-dismissal economics beneath it, all cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Dilbert Principle as an **organisational system**: a firm in which a worker overmatched by the job
damages the output, dismissal is costly and cruel, and the manager's humane, rational move is to promote the
worker up and off the critical path, so that across the whole organisation the ranks that lead accrete the
least able and competence is sorted out of the seats that most need it. The play models the documented
mechanics, the misplaced worker who means well and cannot do the work, the sack made dear by severance and law
and morale and the confrontation, the upward exit made cheap by sparing all of that, the leadership stratum
that fills one soft promotion at a time with those the work defeated, the self-locking inversion as a
leadership that never mastered the work promotes in its own image and shields its own kind, and the honest
reckoning that alone holds it off, at the cost the soft exit was taken to escape, as the load-bearing vectors
of the structure, not as a tale of a lazy worker or a scheming boss. The governing observation of the source is
the play's governing law: where removing the incapable is costlier than promoting them, the incapable are moved
to where they can do least damage to the work, which is up and out of it, so leadership fills with those least
able at the work it leads. It is distinct from the house's Rising to Unfit, which stages the Peter Principle:
there the competent are promoted for succeeding until they reach a level they cannot handle, the engine success
carried one rung too far; here the engine is the reverse, failure itself promoted to remove it, so competence
and rank part company by opposite motions. And it is distinct from the house's The Dead Sea Effect, where the
ablest leave of their own accord and the residue settles: there the stock degrades by the exit of the good,
here by the elevation of the overmatched, departure against promotion.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the overmatched
  worker as lazy or the promoting manager as scheming, because the source observation is precisely that each
  move is humane and rational: the worker means well and is simply misplaced, and the manager who lifts rather
  than fires spares a real cruelty, avoids a grievance and severance and a wounded team, and keeps the person's
  dignity. The inversion is authored by no one and emerges from the structure, a costly sack and a cheap upward
  exit taken by every manager at once, so the misfit is that a mercy to each worker and a relief to each
  manager fills the leadership, promotion by kind promotion, with those the work defeated.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that keeps
  competence and rank together is the honest reckoning, the hard conversation, the coaching to a fit or the
  managed exit, the rise rebuilt to reward the work, and it asks exactly the confrontation and grievance and
  admitted mistake the soft exit was taken to escape, and it must be pressed against a leadership that is
  itself the product of the trap and will not set the standard that would have stopped its own climb. The
  reckoning is an inversion held off against every cost the promotion was reached for to avoid, not a free
  correction, so the cure for a leadership that cannot lead is the cruelty and the confrontation the whole
  structure exists to spare.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the Dilbert Principle is Adams's, the Peter Principle it inverts
  is Peter and Hull's, the technocrat's inversion is Putt's, and the kicked-upstairs practice and the cost of
  dismissal are the practitioners' and the labour economists', and the condition the trap actually requires,
  removal made costlier than promotion so the incapable are moved up rather than out, is carried as carefully
  as the observation itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Poor Work](piece_the_poor_work.md)**: carries the output shortfall (Adams), the honest damage a
  misplaced worker does in a seat the job overmatches them in.

  dear, cruel, fraught dismissal the soft exit is taken to avoid.
- **[The Kick Upstairs](piece_the_kick_upstairs.md)**: carries the soft exit (HR practice), the cheap upward
  promotion that lifts the damage off the work and onto the ranks.
- **[The Inversion](piece_the_inversion.md)**: carries the competence-rank split (Putt; Adams), leadership
  accreting the overmatched so competence runs opposite to authority.
- **[The Hard Word](piece_the_hard_word.md)**: carries the remedy (practitioner), the honest reckoning that
  keeps competence in power, at the cost the promotion was reached for to escape.
- **[The Dilbert Principle](piece_the_dilbert_principle.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Adams), a kind
  exit for each that fills the leadership with those the work defeated.
