# Plot: The Reckoning

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Appraisal](place_the_appraisal.md), the ranks filling upside down and one actor moving to face it.
The honest tools at hand, the hard conversation and the managed exit and the rise rebuilt to reward the work.

## Action

[Facing](process_facing.md): [The Reckoner](persona_the_reckoner.md), holding [the reckoner's seat](position_reckoner.md), would name the shortfall and answer it honestly, against [The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), holding [the boss's seat](position_boss.md), whose soft exit spared the confrontation, and a Brass that will not back the standard.

## Stage

[The Reckoner](persona_the_reckoner.md), having tabled [Have It Out](plan_have_it_out.md), coaches the misplaced toward a fit or manages them honestly out and rebuilds the rise to reward the work, paying the cost the promotion dodged.
[The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), still on [Kick It Upstairs](plan_kick_it_upstairs.md), feels every reason the soft exit was kinder and cheaper, and the Brass above will not vote for the standard that would have stopped its own climb.

## Tension

The one move that keeps competence in power is the confrontation the whole trap was built to spare.
There is no easy remedy: the honest word plainly holds off the inversion, yet it costs the grievance, the severance, the wounded team, and the admitted mistake, and must be pressed against a leadership that is the trap's own product. So the play closes where it must, the shortfall faced and the ranks set right ... or the soft exit is taken once more, and the inversion deepens.
