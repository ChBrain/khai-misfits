# Process: Failing

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md). The honest shortfall of a worker the job overmatches, blameless in itself and the trigger the manager must answer.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A willing worker in a seat pitched past its reach.
At [the line](place_the_line.md), the Duffer trying in good faith and the output falling short, the manager watching the damage mount.

## Direction

Toward a shortfall plain enough to demand a decision.
The worker gives its best and the seat asks more, so failing drives the gap between effort and output into the open, until the incapacity cannot be left in place and the manager must choose what to do about it.

## Lever

The blamelessness of the shortfall.
Because the worker is not lazy but misplaced, trying honestly and simply overmatched, the failure earns sympathy rather than reproach, so failing runs on good faith, the very innocence of the shortfall making the honest sack feel cruel and the soft exit kind.

## Echo

A shortfall the manager cannot ignore and will not punish.
The output has suffered and the worker has plainly not managed, and the failure is so evidently no one's fault that dismissal feels harsh, so what reads as a problem to be solved kindly is, unseen, the first step toward lifting the overmatched up rather than out.
