# Piece: The Inversion

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md). The competence-rank split, leadership accreting the overmatched so competence runs opposite to authority.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Settles in [the heights](place_the_heights.md), where the accreted leadership governs the work it least understands.
Read against [the line](place_the_line.md), where the work is grasped best and ranked lowest, the two ends the Inversion holds apart.

## Load Bearing

Without it the soft exits are just scattered promotions, and no pattern binds them into harm.
With it the misfit has its shape: because up is where every manager sends the overmatched, the leadership fills with them, so competence and rank pull apart until the seats that most need mastery hold it least. The Inversion is load bearing because it is the outcome the whole structure produces (Putt; Adams): the sorting of ability downward as authority climbs, so the piece carries the split that makes a leadership least able exactly where it leads, the globally bad end no single kind promotion intends.

## Apparent

The natural order of things, the way hierarchies simply are, with direction at the top and doing at the bottom.
Seen at rest, the inversion looks like ordinary hierarchy, seniority above and labour below as everywhere, and the truth that this particular leadership is weakest at the very work it governs, that the arrangement was built by mercy and not by merit, stays hidden behind how much like a normal org chart an upside-down one appears.

## Yearbook

Locked by [the brass](persona_the_brass.md) through [entrenching](process_entrenching.md), the split each [kick upstairs](piece_the_kick_upstairs.md) deepens.
It is the wound of the trap: the Inversion is what the lifted workers sum to, the self-renewing gap between competence and rank that only [the hard word](piece_the_hard_word.md), keeping the overmatched off the ranks, can hold off.
