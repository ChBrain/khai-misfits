# Process: Elevating

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md). The soft upward exit, the kick upstairs that moves the overmatched off the work, humane for one and inverting in sum.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A shortfall that must be answered and a sack too dear to reach for.
At [the line](place_the_line.md), the manager weighing the cruel, costly dismissal against the kind, cheap promotion, and choosing the promotion.

## Direction

Toward the overmatched lifted up and off the critical path.
Rather than remove the worker, the manager moves it into a title where it can do less harm, so elevating drives the incapacity out of the productive seat and up toward [the heights](place_the_heights.md), the work spared and the person kept whole.

## Lever

The kindness and cheapness of the upward move.
Because promoting sideways-and-up avoids the grievance, the severance, the wounded team, and the admitted mistake, it is genuinely the humane and low-cost choice, so elevating runs on mercy and prudence together, each lift a manager doing right by the worker in front of it.

## Echo

A floor cleared and a rank quietly heavier.
The Line has its weakest hand removed and the worker keeps its dignity, and the seats above have taken on one more the work overmatched, so what reads as a problem solved kindly is, unseen, a contribution to the leadership filling with those the work defeated.
