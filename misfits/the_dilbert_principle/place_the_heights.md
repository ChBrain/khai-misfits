# Place: The Heights

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md). The management stratum the overmatched are lifted into, where competence no longer selects and the inversion locks.

## Shown

The floors above the work, the titles and the offices where course is set and the able below are judged.
The Duffer arriving among a stratum filled the same way, the Brass at its planning and its promoting, the work it governs a rumour from a distance.

## Holds

The elevating and the entrenching, the lifted worker received and the ranks renewing in their own image.
Here the soft exits from every Line converge, so the Heights hold the point where a mercy becomes a leadership: the overmatched accrete tier over tier, and a stratum built that way promotes as it was promoted, so it is where competence is sorted out of power and the inversion is made to last.

## Offers

A refuge for the overmatched and a throne over the work.
Because the Heights are where the harm is moved to be out of the work's way, they offer each lifted worker a safe berth and each manager a clean floor, so they hand out the destination that spares the Line its weakest, and hand the seats that direct and judge to those the work defeated.

## Withheld

Any pull back toward the work it governs.
The Heights cannot make the accreted leadership feel the mastery it lacks, so they withhold the corrective the work itself would apply, and, being above where ability is read, they withhold from the Brass the judgement of the Line, so the higher the seat the freer it floats from the work, and the less the standard that filled it can be seen from within it.
