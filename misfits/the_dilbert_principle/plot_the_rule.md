# Plot: The Rule

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Heights](place_the_heights.md), a leadership that did not master the work now directing it.
The Brass setting course, allocating the able below, and deciding who rises next.

## Action

[Entrenching](process_entrenching.md): [The Brass](persona_the_brass.md), holding [the brass's seat](position_brass.md), promotes in its own image and shields its own kind, while [The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), holding [the boss's seat](position_boss.md), sends up the next overmatched worker into ranks that will receive it gladly.

## Stage

[The Brass](persona_the_brass.md), still on [Promote in Kind](plan_promote_in_kind.md), judges leadership by its own reflection and raises those who look as it looks, so the inversion renews itself in its own likeness.
[The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), still on [Kick It Upstairs](plan_kick_it_upstairs.md), keeps reaching for the soft exit, feeding the very ranks that now set the standard for everyone below.

## Tension

The upside-down ranks are the ones who decide who rises, so they keep themselves upside down.
There is no cabal: a leadership that never mastered the work sincerely cannot know mastery, and promotes its own likeness in good faith. But it both receives the overmatched and controls the next promotion, so the plot closes with the inversion locked and self-renewing, the one standard that would unseat it the one thing the ranks will never raise.
