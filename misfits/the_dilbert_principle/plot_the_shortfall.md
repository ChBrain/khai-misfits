# Plot: The Shortfall

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Line](place_the_line.md), a worker overmatched by the job and the output paying for it.
The Duffer trying in good faith and falling short, the damage plain, the manager watching it mount.

## Action

[Failing](process_failing.md): [The Duffer](persona_the_duffer.md), holding [the duffer's seat](position_duffer.md), muddles honestly through work it cannot do well, while [The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), holding [the boss's seat](position_boss.md), watches the shortfall and knows it must act.

## Stage

[The Duffer](persona_the_duffer.md), on [Muddle Through](plan_muddle_through.md), gives its best to a seat that asks more than it holds, and the output suffers for it, blamelessly.
[The Boss](persona_the_boss.md), weighing what to do, sees a worker who plainly cannot manage the seat and just as plainly means no harm, and faces the choice of how to move it.

## Tension

The failure is real and no one's fault, and that is what makes it hard.
There is nothing to punish: the worker tries honestly and is simply overmatched, so the shortfall demands action while making the harsh action feel cruel. The plot closes with the incapacity too plain to leave in place and the manager at the fork, the honest sack on one side and some kinder way out on the other, the blamelessness already tilting the choice.
