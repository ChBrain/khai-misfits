# Pitch: Somewhere They Can Do Less Harm

## Taxonomy

[The Dilbert Principle](play_the_dilbert_principle.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Kind, pragmatic, sparing of a cruelty.
You cannot just sack someone who means well and cannot do the job, so you find them a place off the critical path where they can do less harm, a move up and to the side that keeps their dignity and clears the floor.
Nothing cruel in it: only a humane way out of a hard spot, for the person and the team alike.

## Undertow

Under the kindness, a leadership filling with those the work defeated.
Because dismissal is dear and the promotion is cheap, every manager reaches for the same soft exit, and the place they all move the weakest to is up, so the ranks that plan and judge and lead accrete the overmatched, one merciful promotion at a time, until competence runs downhill exactly as authority climbs.
The mercy to each worker is, in the sum, a leadership that cannot lead.

## Nerve

The feeling of doing the decent thing by someone struggling, lifting them somewhere safer rather than letting them go,
sure it is the kind and sensible call, because for this person and this seat it is,
while every manager makes the same call, and the seats above fill with the ones the work beat, the higher you look the less it is understood.

## Echo

After the run: an org chart weakest where it leads, the work best grasped at the bottom and least at the top,
no one lazy and no one scheming, only misplaced workers spared and humane managers reaching for the soft exit and each promotion a genuine mercy,
and an honest reckoning that would have kept competence in power, refused because it was the confrontation, the grievance, and the admitted mistake the whole kind exit was taken to escape.
