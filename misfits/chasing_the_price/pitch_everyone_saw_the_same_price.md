# Pitch: Everyone Saw the Same Price

## Taxonomy

[Chasing the Price](play_chasing_the_price.md). The single hook: every producer reads the same current price and answers it the same way, so their sound plans compound instead of cancelling, and the harvest overshoots the very price that called it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A producer deciding how much to make, and the price as the sensible guide.
The tenor is good business: the price is high, so plant a big crop; the price is low, so plant a small one, each producer answering the market as it stands, exactly as they should.

## Undertow

What is planted now is sold later, and everyone read the same number.
Underneath, the production lag divides the reading from the selling, and because every producer saw the same high price and planted the same big crop, the plans do not cancel into noise but compound into a flood, so the market answers a true signal in chorus and overshoots it together.

## Nerve

The crop arrives to spoil the very price that called it forth.
The nerve is that no one miscalculated: the price was real and the plan was correct at the moment of planting, but by harvest the shared response has moved the price, so the grower delivers into the glut their own sound decision helped make, punished not for an error but for answering the market truly at the same time as everyone else.

## Echo

The only way off the swing is to distrust the price you can see.
What lingers is that the cure is to plant to a forecast instead of the number in front of you, to plant little in a boom and much in a slump against the plain evidence of the market, so stilling the cycle means forgoing the truest signal it offers, and trusting that signal, as any sensible producer does, is exactly what keeps the market swinging.
