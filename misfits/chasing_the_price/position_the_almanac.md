# Position: The Almanac

## Taxonomy

[Chasing the Price](play_chasing_the_price.md). The forecasting seat, which plans on the price it expects rather than the price it sees, trading a legible signal for a prediction.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A forecast, and the discipline to plant against the day's price.
A read on the cycle: the seat holds a model of how the crowd's response to the current price will move the price by harvest, and plans output on that expected price rather than the one on offer.
One instrument only, since the seat's whole lever is the prediction, and a prediction, unlike the going price, cannot be pointed to or proven until the harvest that would confirm it has already come.

## Orders

Plant to the forecast.
Set output on the expected price, small when the current price is high and large when it is low, leaning against the swing rather than feeding it.
Distrust the going number, since the seat's method is precisely to treat the visible price as the sign of its own reversal and to answer the market the crowd will make, not the one it shows.

## Loses

The defensibility of the visible signal, which the seat gives up by design.
The Almanac forgoes the one number everyone can see and agree on, and stakes its output on a forecast that looks, in any single season, like a bet against plain evidence, so it can be right over the cycle and indefensible in the moment, unable to prove its read until it is too late to matter.
What the seat forfeits is standing in the present: acting on tomorrow's price in a market that trades on today's, it must plant against the crowd and bear the seasons when the crowd looks right, so the seat that alone could still the swing is the one whose only tool is a distrust of the signal everyone else, sensibly, obeys.

## Drives

Toward output aimed at a price not yet shown,
and a stand against the number in plain sight,
so the seat that would break the cycle is the one that must trade the market's clearest evidence for a forecast it cannot yet defend.
