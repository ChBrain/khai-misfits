# Persona: The Steady Hand

## Taxonomy

[The Almanac](position_the_almanac.md). Plants to the expected price rather than the current one, holding output level against the swing, and pays for the forecast in the seasons the crowd looks right.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Forecasting. Contrary by arithmetic. Distrusting the number in front of them.
Is the producer who plans not on the going price but on the price they expect at harvest, knowing that the crowd's response to today's price will have moved it, and so holds output near the level the market would bear in balance.
No cleverness wished on them: the forecast is not a gift but a discipline, a decision to plant against the visible signal because the signal, answered by all, is the thing that misleads.

## Action

Plants little when the price is high and much when it is low, against the temptation of the current number, aiming output at the expected price rather than the one on offer.
Answers the cycle rather than the moment, treating today's high price as the sign of tomorrow's glut and today's low price as the sign of tomorrow's dearth, so their planting leans against the swing instead of feeding it.
Does what a producer who has seen the pattern does: forecasts the price their peers' response will produce and plants to that, accepting that in any single season the crowd chasing the current price may look right and they may look wrong.

## Shadow

Not a seer, but the one whose correct forecast costs them in every season the crowd's mistiming happens to pay, and that is the bind: to still the swing they must plant against the plainest evidence, and evidence is persuasive.
Because the Steady Hand forgoes the current price for a predicted one, they plant small in a boom that, for a year, rewards the big planters, and large in a slump that, for a year, punishes them, so the discipline that would break the cycle looks in each moment like stubbornness against a market plainly saying otherwise.
The forecast is right over the cycle and wrong-looking in the season, so the one who would end the swing is the one who must bear looking foolish while it lasts, and, if too few join them, bear it without even damping the swing they planted against.

## Tell

Says "the price is high, which is exactly why I am planting less."
Is right that the high price signals the coming glut and sounds, to everyone chasing it, like someone throwing away a sure thing, so the sound forecast that would steady the market is the one that looks, in the moment it is made, like refusing free money.
