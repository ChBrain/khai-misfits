# Play: Chasing the Price

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Chasing the Price.
A grower must decide how much to plant, and the sensible guide is the price. This year the price is high, so the grower plants a large crop, as good business says they should. The trouble is the lag: what is planted now is sold only at harvest, months away, and the grower is not the only one reading the price. Every grower sees the same high number and every grower plants big, so the harvest arrives as a flood, far more than the market wants, and the price collapses. Stung, each grower plants little the next season, and the small harvest that follows meets a hungry market, so the price spikes again, which tempts everyone to plant big again, and the market swings from glut to dearth and back, year after year, on its own. No single plan is wrong: at the moment of planting, the price each grower reads is real and the response is correct. But the price they answer is the price that is passing, not the price they will get, and because they all answer the same signal at the same time, they overshoot it together, the crop always arriving to spoil the very price that called it forth. And the shape recurs wherever supply takes time to make and producers plan on the price in front of them: the hog raiser, the coffee grower, the property developer, the trained-up profession, each cohort committing on today's number and delivering into the market it will have changed. Nobody miscalculated. The grower answered a true price with a sound plan, and the boom and bust are the sum, the lag and the shared signal turning correct individual choices into a market that whipsaws itself.

## Arc

The bend is this: when production takes time and each producer plans output on the current price, the supply they commit arrives only later, so a high price calls forth a glut that crashes it and a low price a dearth that spikes it, and a single market oscillates boom to bust on its own, each plan sound on the price it saw (Ezekiel).
It runs on a signal read too soon.
The producer must commit output before it exists, and the guide to hand is the price today, so each plans on the current number in good faith, the correct response to the market as it stands (Ezekiel; Nerlove). The price is a true signal, and it is read at the moment of planting.
And the delay divides the reading from the selling.
What is committed now is delivered only after the production lag, the season, the herd, the years of training, so the plan made on today's price is realised against a price that has moved, the interval between decision and delivery the room the whole trap lives in (Ezekiel; Kaldor). The delay is the structural cause, and no single producer can plan around it.
And the shared signal is answered together.
Because every producer reads the same current price and responds the same way, the individual plans do not cancel but compound, so a high price draws a large supply from all at once and a low price a small one, the common reading synchronising the response into a market-wide swing (Nerlove). The price is answered in chorus, and the chorus is the overshoot.
And the delivery overshoots the mark.
When the committed supply arrives it meets a steady demand at a price the supply itself sets, so a big crop clears only by crashing the price and a small one by spiking it, the market overshooting equilibrium first one way and then the other, the height of the swing set by how sharply supply answers price (Kaldor; Hanau). The swing is endogenous, produced by the lag and the response, not by any shock from outside.
And the swing feeds itself.
Each overshoot becomes the next period's signal, so the crash that follows the glut tempts a small planting and the spike that follows the dearth a large one, and the market chases its own tail, boom calling bust and bust calling boom in a self-perpetuating cycle (Ezekiel; Hanau). The oscillation is standing, kept alive by the very soundness of each response to the price it sees.
None of it is a blunder. The producer reading a true price, committing a sound plan, and delivering into the market the plan helped move: each does the correct thing, and the whipsaw emerges from the sum, no miscalculation and no outside shock required, which is exactly why it repeats and why no single grower can step off it.
Plant to the expected price rather than the current one and the cycle is broken: but to do so is to ignore the real number in front of you and bet instead on a forecast, to plant little in a boom and much in a slump against the plain evidence of the market, so the one move that would still the swing forgoes the actual signal for a predicted one and, if others keep chasing the price, exposes the forecaster to their mistiming, and the reckoning is a producer who distrusts the price they can see, or a market that swings because everyone, sensibly, trusts it.

## Stakes

Whether a market can plan its supply on the price it can see, or is doomed to swing by the very soundness of everyone answering the same signal at once.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the self-feeding swing, which the Grower is right to plant big into a high price and the Buyer right to pay what the arriving supply clears at and each producer right to answer the number in front of them, and away from the steady output that would still the market by planting against the visible price. The production cannot make the boom and bust a blunder, because each plan is correct on the price it saw and the lag is a fact of making things that take time, so reading the price and planting to it is the sensible act: the grower doing good business and the buyer paying the going rate are each only answering the market truly. It can only show that a true signal read on a lag and answered in chorus overshoots itself, that the crop arrives to spoil the price that called it and the dearth to spike the price that discouraged it, and that the one move that would still the swing, planting to the forecast rather than the price, means distrusting the plainest evidence the market offers, so the cycle turns not by anyone's error but by the ordinary good sense of every producer who plants to the price in front of them.
