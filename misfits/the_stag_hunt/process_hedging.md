# Process: Hedging

## Taxonomy

[The Stag Hunt](play_the_stag_hunt.md). The hunters taking the safe hare against the doubt, and the stag coming apart as they do.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A doubt loose in the party and a sure hare within reach.
At [the hedgerow](place_the_hedgerow.md), one hunter peeling off to the catch that pays whatever the rest do, to be no one's fool.

## Direction

Toward the risk-dominant outcome, spreading hunter to hunter.
Once one may break, the safe move for each is to break first, so hedging drives the party off the stag, each peel making the next more sensible, until the line that needed everyone has too few to take the prize and all have turned to hares.

## Lever

The hare's safety against the stag's exposure.
Because the hare pays regardless and the stag only on all holding, the guarded response to any doubt is the hare, so hedging works on the plain prudence of each hunter, the same shield raised by all at once, no betrayal in a single move and a scattering in their sum.

## Echo

A field of hares and a stag walking free.
Each hunter has its sure smaller catch and none the great one, the line dissolved not by treachery but by every hunter guarding against the rest, so what protected each is what lost the party the prize they all preferred.
