# Place: The Hedgerow

## Taxonomy

[The Stag Hunt](play_the_stag_hunt.md). Where the hares are, catchable by a hunter alone, the safe fallback that needs no one else.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The grass and hedges where the hares sit, each within reach of a single hunter acting on its own.
A catch that asks nothing of the party: whoever turns aside to it takes it, whatever the others do at the thicket, and comes away with a sure smaller prize.

## Holds

The risk-dominant outcome, the prize that trust cannot cost.
Here the hare is taken alone, so the hedgerow holds the safe payoff each hunter can secure against any doubt, the move no one is ever the fool for making, so it is where a hunter shields itself from the others' flinching, and where the party, each shielding, ends up scattered off the stag.

## Offers

A sure catch to whoever doubts.
Because the hare pays whatever the rest choose, the hedgerow offers certainty to any hunter uneasy about the party, so it is always open, always safe, and always there to draw one more hunter off the line the moment the stag looks unsure.

## Withheld

The greater prize, and any reward for holding together.
The hedgerow cannot pay more than a hare, because that is all it holds, so it withholds the stag's worth from those who take its safety, and asking no coordination it withholds any gain from trust, giving each its small sure catch and the party its smaller sure day.
