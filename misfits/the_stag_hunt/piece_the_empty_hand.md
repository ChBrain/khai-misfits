# Piece: The Empty Hand

## Taxonomy

[The Stag Hunt](play_the_stag_hunt.md). The lone cooperator's loss, the nothing that waits for whoever holds for the stag while another peels away.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the thicket](place_the_thicket.md), where the hunter who stayed is left with nothing once the line breaks.
Weighed at [the hunt](place_the_hunt.md), where the mere prospect of it makes the sure hare the guarded move.

## Load Bearing

Without it the hedge would be groundless, and holding for the stag would carry no risk worth guarding against.
With it the caution is rational: the hunter who holds while another peels catches neither stag nor hare, so the worst case of loyalty is nothing at all. The Empty Hand is load bearing because it is what makes the hedge reasonable (Rousseau): the penalty for being the lone hold-out, not a smaller catch but an empty one, is exactly the risk each hunter shields against, so the piece carries the downside that turns prudence into the party's undoing.

## Apparent

Bad luck, a hunter who backed the wrong plan and came away with nothing.
Seen after the fact, the empty hand looks like the misfortune of the one who trusted too much, and the truth that the fear of it, felt by all in advance, is what scattered the party before anyone was ever left holding, stays hidden behind how plainly foolish the lone hold-out looks in hindsight.

## Yearbook

Suffered at [the thicket](place_the_thicket.md) when the line breaks, the price [the stag](persona_the_stag.md) exacts of the one who stayed.
It is the risk beneath the whole trap: the Empty Hand is what [the doubt](piece_the_doubt.md) points to, the outcome that makes [the hedge](piece_the_hedge.md) wise, and the fate [the assurance](piece_the_assurance.md) must make unthinkable before any hunter will hold.
