# Play: The Stag Hunt

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Stag Hunt.
Together you can take the stag, and the stag feeds everyone: it is the best prize on the field, and every hunter would rather have it ... but the stag needs all of you to hold your places, and off in the grass there are hares, and a hare you can catch alone. So each hunter, watching the others and wondering whether they will hold, thinks: if even one slips off after a hare, the stag is gone and I am left with nothing, so I had better take the sure hare myself. And because every hunter reasons the very same way, all of them take hares, and the stag that all of them wanted, and could have had together, walks off untaken.

## Arc

The bend is this: the best outcome is the one everyone prefers, and each is driven from it not by wanting less but by doubting the others will stay.
It runs on two ways the hunt can settle.
In one, all hold for the stag, and all do best: it is the prize each would choose if only the choosing were safe. In the other, all take hares, and all do worse, but no hunter can be caught out: the hare pays whatever the others do. Both are stable, because in each, no single hunter gains by breaking from the rest, so the game does not have one answer, it has two, and which one falls out is settled not by the payoffs alone but by what each believes the others will do.
And belief tips it the wrong way. The stag is worth more but hangs on everyone, so its value to me depends on my trust that you will hold, and yours on your trust in me. The hare is worth less but hangs on no one, so it needs no trust at all. Faced with any doubt, the safe move is the hare, because the worst that can happen with a hare is a smaller catch, while the worst that can happen holding for the stag is standing alone in the thicket with nothing. So each hedges against the others' flinching, and the hedging is the flinch, and the fear that someone will break the hunt is what breaks it.
None of it is greed or betrayal. Every hunter prefers the stag, no one is tempted to cheat a partner who stays, and taking the hare is not a trick but a shield: the failure is not that cooperation is against anyone's interest, it is that cooperation is safe only once each is sure of the rest, and no one is sure. This is what sets it apart from the traps where defecting simply pays better: here holding for the stag is the best answer to others holding, and only the doubt stands in the way.
Give the hunters assurance, a word each trusts, a hunt they have taken together before, a leader who moves first and visibly, a pledge that binds, and the trust that makes the stag safe can be built, and all can hold: but the assurance has to be genuinely believed, not merely spoken, one hunter breaking it strands every other, and it must be raised anew each time against the standing pull of the sure hare, so the peace of the shared hunt is real and never free, a thing built on trust and lost the moment the trust gives way.

## Triggers

**[The Stag](plot_the_stag.md)**
The prize is set: a stag that feeds everyone, worth more to each than any hare, if all hold their places to take it. The Hunter would go for it, and the Stag pays only if none breaks away. Closes when the stag is on offer and every hunter would rather have it.

**[The Doubt](plot_the_doubt.md)**
Cue: each hunter looks to the others and cannot be sure they will hold. The Partner wonders if the Hunter will stay, the Hunter wonders the same of the Partner, and the stag's worth, hanging on all, hangs now on a trust no one can confirm. Closes when the doubt is loose and the sure hare starts to look wise.

**[The Hedge](plot_the_hedge.md)**
Cue: unsure of the rest, one hunter takes the safe hare. The Partner peels off to the thing that pays whatever the others do, and once one may break, the safe move for each is to break first. Closes when the hedging spreads and the hunt for the stag comes apart.

**[The Empty Hand](plot_the_empty_hand.md)**
Cue: the stag needed all and did not get them. Whoever held for it stands in the thicket with nothing, the stag gone, while the harers have their smaller sure catch, so all settle for the worse outcome every one of them would rather not have. Closes when the stag is lost and the field has taken hares.

**[The Assurance](plot_the_assurance.md)**
Cue: someone moves to build the trust the stag needs. The Voucher would give a word each believes, a pledge, a lead taken first and in sight, so all can hold together, against the standing safety of the hare and the truth that one broken promise strands the rest. Closes when the assurance holds and the stag is taken ... or the doubt returns and the hunters scatter to hares again.

## Stakes

Whether hunters who all want the same prize can ever trust one another enough to take it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the hare, which each is safe to take and all are poorer for taking, and away from the stag, which all prefer and none dares hold for alone. The production cannot make the stag safe, because the stag hangs on everyone and the hare on no one, so any doubt about the others makes the sure hare the guarded move, and the guarding is the scattering. It can only show that no one was greedy or false, that every hunter wanted the stag and holding for it is the best reply to others holding, and that the one thing that would let them take it, an assurance each truly believes, must be built fresh against the pull of the sure thing and falls to a single broken nerve, so the better outcome is real and reachable and forever one doubt away from lost.
