# Piece: The Fork

## Taxonomy

[The Stag Hunt](play_the_stag_hunt.md). The two stable outcomes, the stag held by all and the hare taken alone, between which belief and not payoff decides.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the hunt](place_the_hunt.md), where both outcomes are still open and the party has not yet tipped.
Resolved at [the thicket](place_the_thicket.md) into the stag, or at [the hedgerow](place_the_hedgerow.md) into the hare.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no trap, only a single best move everyone would take.
With it the game has two answers: a cooperative equilibrium that pays most and a safe one that pays less, each stable because no lone hunter gains by breaking from it. The Fork is load bearing because it is the structure the whole finding rests on (Nash; Harsanyi and Selten): two equilibria mean the payoffs do not settle the outcome by themselves, so what the hunters believe about one another becomes the thing that decides, and the trap is that belief can settle them on the worse.

## Apparent

A simple choice between a big prize and a small one, with an obvious answer.
Seen plainly, the fork looks like it should resolve to the stag, since everyone prefers it, and the truth that both outcomes are stable and that doubt, not preference, selects between them, that the better prize can be the one not taken, stays hidden behind how clearly the stag is worth more.

## Yearbook

Faced by [the hunter](persona_the_hunter.md) and [the partner](persona_the_partner.md) at [the hunt](place_the_hunt.md), the choice with two settled answers.
It is why preferring the stag is not enough: the Fork is the pair of outcomes belief must choose between, the ground on which [the doubt](piece_the_doubt.md) tips the party toward [the hedge](piece_the_hedge.md), and the structure [the assurance](piece_the_assurance.md) works to settle on the better arm.
