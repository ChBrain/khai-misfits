# The Stag Hunt: Reference

The research warrant for [The Stag Hunt](play_the_stag_hunt.md).
The parable is Rousseau's; the play runs on the theory of the assurance game and equilibrium selection, from
Rousseau's telling through Skyrms's study and Harsanyi and Selten's risk-dominance, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the stag hunt as a **coordination system**: a game with two stable equilibria, one in which all
cooperate for a prize that pays most but requires everyone, and one in which all take a safe payoff that pays
less but depends on no one, so that although every player prefers the cooperative outcome, the risk that
another defects drives each to the safe one, and all settle for the worse. The play models the documented
mechanics, the payoff-dominant equilibrium every player would choose, the risk-dominant equilibrium safe
against any doubt, the strategic uncertainty about what the others will do, the hedge to the sure payoff that
is each player's guard and the group's undoing, the empty-handed loss of the lone cooperator that makes the
hedge rational, and the assurance that alone lets all coordinate on the better outcome, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of greed or betrayal. The governing finding of the source literature
is the play's governing law: when a game has a payoff-dominant and a risk-dominant equilibrium, and the safe
choice does not depend on trust while the rewarding one does, doubt about the others selects the inferior
equilibrium, so the failure is one of assurance and not of interest. It is distinct from the house's The Bank
Run, where a self-fulfilling panic and a first-come queue destroy a standing solvent institution: here nothing
is grabbed and nothing collapses, the better outcome is simply never formed. And it is distinct from the
house's the prisoner's-dilemma kin, The Commons and Social Loafing, where defection pays better whatever the
others do: here cooperation is the best reply to cooperation, and only the doubt, not the payoff, stands in
its way.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the hunter who
  takes the hare as greedy or false, because the source finding is precisely that each move is right: every
  hunter prefers the stag, no one is tempted to cheat a partner who stays, and taking the hare is a shield
  against being the one left with nothing, not a betrayal. The loss is authored by no error and emerges from
  the structure, a rewarding outcome that hangs on all and a safe one that hangs on none, so the misfit is
  that hunters who all want the same prize and could take it together are driven apart by nothing worse than
  doubt.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that lets all
  take the stag is an assurance each truly believes, a word, a shared history, a leader who moves first, a
  binding pledge, and it must be genuinely trusted rather than merely spoken, strands every other hunter the
  moment one breaks it, and must be raised anew each time against the standing pull of the sure hare. The
  reckoning is a better outcome held together by trust and lost to a single broken nerve, not a free
  correction, so the cure for a hunt scattered by doubt is a trust that is never owned, only kept.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the stag hunt, the assurance game, equilibrium
  selection, and risk-dominance are theirs, and the condition the trap actually requires, a payoff-dominant
  equilibrium that depends on all and a risk-dominant one that depends on none, is carried as carefully as the
  finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Fork](piece_the_fork.md)**: carries the two equilibria (Nash; Harsanyi and Selten), the stable
  cooperative outcome and the stable safe one, between which belief, not payoff alone, decides.
- **[The Hedge](piece_the_hedge.md)**: carries the risk-dominant choice (Harsanyi and Selten), the safe payoff
  taken under any doubt because it does not depend on the others.
- **[The Doubt](piece_the_doubt.md)**: carries the strategic uncertainty (Skyrms), the unconfirmable trust in
  the others on which the stag's whole worth depends.
- **[The Empty Hand](piece_the_empty_hand.md)**: carries the lone cooperator's loss (Rousseau), the nothing that
  waits for whoever holds for the stag while another peels away, the risk that makes the hedge rational.
- **[The Assurance](piece_the_assurance.md)**: carries the remedy (Sen; Skyrms), the believed commitment that
  makes the stag safe, built fresh each time and lost to one broken promise.
- **[The Stag Hunt](piece_the_stag_hunt.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Rousseau; Skyrms), a better
  outcome all prefer and none dares alone, and its remedy in a trust that is only ever kept, never owned.
