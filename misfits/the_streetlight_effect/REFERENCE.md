# The Streetlight Effect: Reference

The research warrant for [The Streetlight Effect](play_the_streetlight_effect.md). The title is
Freedman's name for the drunkard's search; the play runs on the account of cost-bound inquiry, from
Kaplan's parable through bounded rationality and the measurable-vs-important literature, all cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the streetlight effect as a **cognitive system**: a thing lost in the dark, a lit patch cheap
to search, and a cost of looking that steers the search to the light regardless of where the thing
is, so the search combs the easy ground thoroughly, reports its yield as the truth, and leaves the
answer in the unsearched dark. The play models the documented mechanics, the light that is cheap to
search and silent on where the thing is, the dark that holds the answer and refuses the cheap look,
the lost that waits where it fell, the easy search that flows to the light by the cost of looking, the
false find that takes the lit yield for the answer, and the thoroughness that makes a wrong-ground
search feel like progress, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of foolishness.
The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: a search under cost goes
where looking is cheapest, not where the answer is, so the ease of a place, which is unrelated to
whether the thing is there, comes to decide where the search looks and what it concludes. It is a
cousin of the house's Law of the Instrument, and its distinct engine: not a mastered tool reshaping
the problem to its nail, but a search steered to the easy ground by the cost of looking. It is prior,
too, to the house's Metric Fixation: the streetlight is about where the search looks, not yet about a
measure hardening into the target once it does.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  searcher as foolish, because the source finding is precisely that searching the light is rational:
  the dark genuinely cannot be searched cheaply, a search that can be run genuinely beats one that
  cannot, the diligence is real, and the lit patch is honestly easy to see. The misfit is that the
  ease which makes the light worth searching is unrelated to where the thing fell, so the very economy
  that steers the search is what walks it away from the answer.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that
  works is to search where the thing is, lighting the dark and instrumenting the unmeasured, and it is
  dear, may return nothing, since the dark can be genuinely barren, and cannot be told in advance from
  the case where the keys were near the lamp and the patient combing was right. The reckoning is a
  true, costly search that risks silence traded for a tractable one that gave a clean, misplaced
  result, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the drunkard's search, the streetlight
  name, bounded rationality, and the measurable-over-important critique are theirs, and the condition
  the effect actually requires, an answer in the costly dark and a cheapness in the light that is no
  evidence at all, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Light](piece_the_light.md)**: carries the well-lit ground of the parable (Kaplan), the region cheap to search
  and silent on where the thing is.
- **[The Dark](piece_the_dark.md)**: carries the costly region (Simon), the ground dear or impossible to search where
  the cost of looking keeps the answer.
- **[The Lost](piece_the_lost.md)**: carries the object of the search (Kaplan's keys), the answer located, all along, in
  the dark and not the light.
- **[The Easy Search](piece_the_easy_search.md)**: carries the cost-bound search (Simon), effort flowing to the lit patch
  because looking there is cheap, wherever the thing is.
- **[The False Find](piece_the_false_find.md)**: carries the misplaced result (Freedman; the McNamara fallacy), the lit
  yield or its emptiness reported as the truth.
- **[The Thoroughness](piece_the_thoroughness.md)**: carries the diligence mistaken for nearness, the careful combing of
  the wrong ground read as progress toward the answer.
- **[The Streetlight Effect](piece_the_streetlight_effect.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Kaplan; Freedman), the search
  steered to the easy ground by the cost of looking, and its remedy in lighting the dark at a cost.
