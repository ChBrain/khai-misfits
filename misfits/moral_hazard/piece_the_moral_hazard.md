# Piece: The Moral Hazard

## Taxonomy

[Moral Hazard](play_moral_hazard.md). The phenomenon whole: protection that buys the risk it covers, held whole by nobody.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Across the whole run, from [the underwriting desk](place_the_underwriting_desk.md) to [the claims table](place_the_claims_table.md).
And out beyond it, wherever a downside is guaranteed and a hand acts knowing it will be caught.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit is a scatter of parts: a cover, a hidden hand, an eased notch, a pooled bill.
With it they are one law: transfer a loss to a party who cannot see the care, and the care falls, so the act of protecting against a risk raises it. The Moral Hazard is load bearing because it is the whole read at once (Arrow; Pauly), and it scales, from a household policy to a bank too big to fail, where the surest downside protection dulls the largest risks and the pool that absorbs them is the public. It is the one shape the parts spell together.

## Apparent

Prudence, insurance, safety nets, bailouts, all working as designed.
It looks like a world doing the sensible thing everywhere: spreading risk, protecting the exposed, catching what falls. Nothing on the surface is a failure, and that is the whole of it, because the hazard is not a lapse from good protection but a property of it.

## Yearbook

Held whole by nobody: assembled from [the cover](piece_the_cover.md), [the free downside](piece_the_free_downside.md), and [the slipped care](piece_the_slipped_care.md), and answered only, and partly, by [the skin](piece_the_skin.md).
It is the misfit named: the safer the hand is made against a loss it cannot be seen to prevent, the less it prevents it.
