# Piece: The Free Downside

## Taxonomy

[Moral Hazard](play_moral_hazard.md). The loss made someone else's, so skipping care costs the actor nothing they still hold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In [the field](place_the_field.md), at the price the actor now sees on a moment of caution.
Care still costs. The loss it prevents does not, any more, land on the one deciding.

## Load Bearing

Without it easing the care would be a gamble with your own money, and the caution would hold.
With it the sum changes: precaution is a private cost whose only payoff, avoiding the loss, now accrues to the insurer, so the rational amount of care to buy falls. The Free Downside is load bearing because it is the incentive itself (Pauly), the reason the response is not a moral failing but arithmetic: at a downside of zero to the actor, the efficient private level of care is below the social one. It is the price that makes honest people ease off.

## Apparent

Peace of mind, working as sold.
It looks like the relief the policy promised: the fear is gone, so the hand relaxes. That relaxation is the product doing its job, which is exactly why no one experiences it as a cost to anyone, and why it is one.

## Yearbook

Felt by [the covered](persona_the_covered.md), the moment the loss stops being theirs to fear.
It is the engine under the ease: the Free Downside turns [the cover](piece_the_cover.md) into [the slipped care](piece_the_slipped_care.md), a rational hand at a shifted price.
