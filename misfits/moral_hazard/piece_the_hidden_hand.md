# Piece: The Hidden Hand

## Taxonomy

[Moral Hazard](play_moral_hazard.md). The care the insurer cannot see, and so cannot price or require.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In [the field](place_the_field.md), in every small choice about how carefully to act.
The effort that decides the risk, made where no policy can watch it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the insurer could simply write the care into the contract and price the premium on it.
With it the action is unobservable, so the one variable that now matters most, the precaution taken after the cover is in force, is the one the insurer can never confirm. The Hidden Hand is load bearing because it is the observability gap (Holmström): could the effort be seen, the contract could demand it and the hazard would close, and because it cannot, no contract restores the care the insured would have taken holding the whole risk. The blindness is structural, not negligence.

## Apparent

Ordinary privacy of work.
It looks like nothing more than the fact that a person does their job out of sight, as everyone does. There is no concealment and no lie: the hand simply acts where the watcher is not, and the effort, unlike the outcome, leaves no receipt.

## Yearbook

Sought by [the watcher](persona_the_watcher.md) through [monitoring](process_monitoring.md), and never quite caught.
It is why the cover cannot be written tight: the Hidden Hand keeps [the slipped care](piece_the_slipped_care.md) out of reach of the contract, so the loss can only be shared, never assigned.
