# Piece: The Cover

## Taxonomy

[Moral Hazard](play_moral_hazard.md). The risk written over to a party who will bear the loss the insured no longer fears.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the underwriting desk](place_the_underwriting_desk.md), in the moment the downside changes hands.
The policy signed, the premium paid, the loss that was one hand's now the pool's.

## Load Bearing

Without it the hand that acts also holds the loss, and buys care up to the point it stops paying.
With it the loss and the care come apart: the insured keeps the choice of how carefully to act and keeps none of the downside, so the whole misfit has room to form. The Cover is load bearing because it is the transfer itself (Arrow), the thing that moves the cost of a bad outcome away from the one who decides whether it happens. Every later step, the eased care, the pooled loss, the deductible, is a consequence of this one clean, honest handover.

## Apparent

A service, and a good one.
It looks like exactly what insurance should be: a risk too big for one hand spread across many, a party freed from a fear it could not carry alone. All of that is real, and none of it is the trick, because the trick is not in the cover being bad but in the cover working.

## Yearbook

Written by [the underwriter](persona_the_underwriter.md) through [underwriting](process_underwriting.md), signed by [the covered](persona_the_covered.md).
It is the hinge the play turns on: the Cover carries the risk away, and carries with it the reason [the slipped care](piece_the_slipped_care.md) was ever kept high.
