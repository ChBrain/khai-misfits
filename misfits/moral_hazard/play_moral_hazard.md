# Play: Moral Hazard

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Moral Hazard.
A risk is written over to someone who cannot watch how it is carried ... so the one who carries it, no longer holding the downside, carries it a little less carefully, and the loss, when it comes, comes home to the pool that insured it and not to the hand that let it fall.

## Arc

The bend is this: cover a risk, and the care taken against it falls.
It runs on a split between who bears the cost of a bad outcome and who takes the trouble to prevent it.
Before the cover, one hand holds both: the person exposed to the loss is the person who decides how carefully to act, so care is bought exactly up to the point where it stops paying for itself. The cover cuts that hand in two. Now the loss belongs to the insurer and the care still costs the insured, so every unit of precaution is a private expense whose benefit goes to someone else, and the level of care that is rational for the covered to buy drops below the level that was right when they held the whole risk.
No one defrauds. The insured is not reckless, only responding to a price: caution got cheaper to skip the moment its reward stopped being theirs. And the insurer cannot simply forbid the slack, because the one thing it would need to price it, the care actually taken, is the one thing it cannot see.
Put a little of the loss back on the hand that acts, a deductible, a share, a stake, and the care returns: but the protection returns with a hole in it, and the hole is the point, so the cover that was bought to carry the risk works only when it stops fully carrying it.

## Triggers

**[The Cover](plot_the_cover.md)**
A real risk is written over to a party who will bear the loss but cannot watch the care. The Underwriter takes it on and prices it on the average, and the Covered signs, protected. Closes when the downside belongs to the insurer and the care still belongs to the insured.

**[The Slack](plot_the_slack.md)**
Cue: the loss is no longer the actor's to fear. The Covered eases the precaution a notch, reasonably, because caution costs them and its reward is now the insurer's, and the Watcher cannot see the hand that eased. Closes when the care has quietly fallen and no one can prove it did.

**[The Claim](plot_the_claim.md)**
Cue: the risk that was covered arrives. The loss lands, and the Bearer pays it out of the pool, so the cost of one slackened hand is spread across every premium. Closes when the bad outcome is socialised and the actor who could have prevented it walks clear of the bill.

**[The Deductible](plot_the_deductible.md)**
Cue: someone puts a share of the loss back on the hand that acts. The Reformer sets a deductible, and the care returns because the downside is the actor's again. Closes when the stake restores the caution ... or the deductible is judged too harsh, the cover is made whole again, and the care slips back with it.

## Stakes

Whether care is ever paid for by the hand that takes it, or only ever by the pool that covers it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward prevention, toward protection, which is what the policy was bought for and is exactly what dulls the prevention. The production cannot make an insured hand fear a loss it no longer holds. It can only show that no one was reckless and no one cheated, that easing the care was the rational answer to a downside that had moved, and that the one thing that would have kept the care high, leaving a little of the loss on the hand that acts, is the very protection the cover was bought to take away.
