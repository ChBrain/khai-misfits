# Persona: The Redesigner

## Taxonomy

[Redesigner](position_redesigner.md). Would keep the gain by changing the felt danger rather than the real one, so the target is met without spending the margin.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Counterintuitive. Unwelcome. Willing to remove the comfort.
Is the reformer who has understood that a margin handed to a defended target will be spent, and that the only gain that stays is one that is never felt as slack.
No trick in it: makes the road feel as risky as it is, so the driver keeps the care of their own accord.

## Action

Changes what the driver feels rather than what the device does: strips the false comfort, sharpens the sense of risk, takes out the kerbs and the centre lines so the shared space reads as uncertain, invokes the thought of the spike in the steering wheel.
Sets the felt danger back up to the target from the other side, so the balance is met without re-taking real risk.
Does what only a redesign of the feeling can do: keeps the engineered gain by refusing the driver the sense that it is theirs to spend.

## Shadow

Not a safety's enemy, its keeper, and that is the whole difficulty: the move that saves the gain is to make people feel less safe, and that is the one thing the measure was sold to prevent.
Because a target of accepted risk will draw down any margin it can feel, the Redesigner can hold the gain only by denying the driver the comfort, sharpening the felt danger so the target is met without a spend, which means giving up the reassurance the measure promised and asking people to accept a road that feels worse in order to be better, and it cannot be carried past the point where real protection, for the crashes that still happen, is what is needed.
It keeps the gain by trading away the feeling of safety, and is thanked for neither.

## Tell

Says "if it feels safer, they will spend it: make it feel like what it is, and they will keep it."
Is right that the comfort is the leak, and knows the cost, that a safety kept by feeling unsafe is a hard thing to sell and a harder thing to hold.
