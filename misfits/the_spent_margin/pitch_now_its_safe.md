# Pitch: Now It's Safe

## Taxonomy

[The Spent Margin](play_the_spent_margin.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Improved, protected, proven.
The plain good of a safety measure that works: brakes that stop the car shorter, a belt that holds the body, a real margin won and measured, lives that the test says will be saved.
Nothing false in it: the device does exactly what it promises, and the gain on the track is as large as the engineer claims.

## Undertow

Under the proven gain, an agent who keeps a target of accepted risk and reads the new margin as slack to spend.
Because the felt danger has dropped below the balance they hold, the driver re-takes the risk to restore it, closing the distance and carrying more speed, until the margin is consumed and the crash rate is back where it began, the safety drawn off as haste.
The protection that was built to save lives is spent by the very confidence it earns.

## Nerve

The feeling of a road made safer and driven a little harder for it,
because a working device has handed a real buffer to someone who was already at their own comfortable edge of danger,
so the gain is felt as room to go faster, and the safety is enjoyed as speed rather than banked as survival.

## Echo

After the run: a device that works perfectly and a toll that has barely moved, the margin real on the track and gone on the road,
no one reckless and no one in error, only an engineer who built a true protection and a driver who kept the same balance of danger and reward they keep every day,
part of the risk not banked and not even kept by the driver but moved onto the pedestrian and the cyclist who were handed no margin, and the one move that would keep the gain forgone because it means making people feel less safe to make them safer.
