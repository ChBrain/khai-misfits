# Piece: The Transfer

## Taxonomy

[The Spent Margin](play_the_spent_margin.md). The share of the re-taken risk that lands outside the car, moved from the protected driver onto the uncovered at the verge.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Lands at [the road](place_the_road.md), where the harder driving meets the exposed.
Flows from the driver who spends the margin to the pedestrian and the cyclist who were fitted with none, a cost moved outward across the shared road.

## Load Bearing

Without it the spend would fall only on the one who chose it, and the offset would be the driver's own affair, a gain traded for speed by the person who holds both.
With it the trap reaches past the car: because the road is shared and the re-taken risk is a harder-driven road, part of the danger the device removed from inside is transferred to those outside, who received no margin and made no trade. The Transfer is load bearing because it is where the offset stops being a private balance and becomes a redistribution (Peltzman): the risk shifted onto non-occupants, so the piece carries the reason the measure can make some people less safe while making others feel more so, the cost of a spend borne by those who had no part in it.

## Apparent

Nothing at all, an unchanged road for the one at the verge, who sees no device fitted and no bargain struck.
Seen at the surface, the Transfer is invisible, the exposed simply crossing as before, and the truth that the road has grown more dangerous for them, that a safety fitted to the car is drawn in part from the exposure of those beside it, that a margin they never held is being spent partly at their cost, stays hidden behind how little, from the verge, appears to have changed.

## Yearbook
