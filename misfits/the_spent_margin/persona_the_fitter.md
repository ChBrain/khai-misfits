# Persona: The Fitter

## Taxonomy

[Bench](position_bench.md). Builds and proves the safety measure, holds behaviour fixed on the track, and expects the whole of the measured gain to reach the road.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Rigorous. Empirical. Sure of the number.
Is the engineer or regulator who designs the guard, tests it with the operator held constant, and reads off a margin that is real and repeatable.
No error in it: the device does stop the car shorter, and the gain on the track is exactly as large as measured.

## Action

Fits the brake, the belt, the guardrail, and proves it in the controlled test where behaviour does not vary.
Books the measured gain as the gain that will be delivered, and writes it into the case for the measure and the count of lives it will save.
Does what a sound engineer does: builds a real protection and proves it against a fixed baseline.

## Shadow

Not a fraud, a careful engineer, and that is the whole difficulty: the margin is real and the test is honest, but the test holds behaviour fixed and the road does not.
Because the number is measured with the operator constant, the Fitter books the whole margin as delivered safety, not seeing that a real buffer handed to an agent who keeps a target of accepted risk is a buffer that agent will read as slack and spend, so the gain proven on the bench is the gain before the driver draws it off, and the lives promised are counted against a behaviour that will not hold still.
It proves a true margin, and mistakes the proven margin for the kept one.

## Tell

Says "the device works: the stopping distance is shorter, the test is clean, so the road will be safer."
It is right about the device and wrong about the road, and cannot see that a margin proven with behaviour fixed is a margin the driver is free to spend.
