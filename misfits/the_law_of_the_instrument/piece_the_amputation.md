# Piece: The Amputation

## Taxonomy

[The Law of the Instrument](play_the_law_of_the_instrument.md). The cut that makes the fit: the part of the problem the tool cannot reach, trimmed off as noise.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the intake](place_the_intake.md), where the trimming is done.
Where the faces the instrument cannot strike are cut away to let the nail sit clean.

## Load Bearing

Without it the problem would not fit and the reshaping would fail, so the tool could not be used past its nail.
With it the fit is bought at a price paid out of sight: the amputation is the removal of everything the instrument cannot address, cut as noise so the strike can land square, and it is invisible because the tool that defines the problem cannot name what it cannot touch. The Amputation is load bearing because it is where the harm is set: it converts a mismatch into a clean-looking job by discarding the very parts that did not fit, which are often the parts that were the real problem, so the tidiness of the nail is exactly the measure of the damage.

## Apparent

Scoping, or cutting the noise.
Setting aside the parts that do not fit the tool looks like sensible focus, a problem trimmed to its essentials, so the amputation reads as discipline, and the truth that what was cut is not noise but the unfitting remainder, often the crux itself, stays hidden behind the reasonable look of narrowing the scope.
