# Process: Diagnosing

## Taxonomy

[The Law of the Instrument](play_the_law_of_the_instrument.md). The problem named before the tool is chosen, and the two costs of doing it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A recognition that the tool was chosen before the problem was read, and a hand willing to reverse the order.
At [the toolroom](place_the_toolroom.md), the diagnostician asking what the problem is and the generalist reaching across the kit for fit.

## Direction

Toward the problem met on its own terms.
The case is named whole before any instrument is lifted, the question of whether it is a nail at all is asked, and the tool is chosen to fit the answer, so the diagnosis precedes the reach and the problem chooses the instrument rather than the reverse.

## Lever

An order reversed against the pull of mastery.
Because only diagnosing first breaks the reshaping, the remedy must put the slow question before the fast reflex and fit before familiarity, which means setting down a mastered tool for a novice's grip, so the cure is right, humbling, and worse before better.

## Echo

The right tool on the real problem, or a master turned dabbler.
The case answered as itself when the fitted tool is wielded well enough, or the diagnosis beaten by a reflex that was correct, so the remedy ends either in the problem truly met or in a clean strike traded for a clumsy one on the right nail.
