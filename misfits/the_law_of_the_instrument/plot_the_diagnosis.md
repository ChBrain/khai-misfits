# Plot: The Diagnosis

## Taxonomy

[The Law of the Instrument](play_the_law_of_the_instrument.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The toolroom](place_the_toolroom.md), a move to name the problem before the tool is chosen.
The proposal is to ask what the case actually is, to reach for fit and not familiarity, and to pick from the whole wall rather than the one instrument the hand has mastered.

## Action

[Diagnosing](process_diagnosing.md): [The Diagnostician](persona_the_diagnostician.md), holding [the diagnostician's seat](position_diagnostician.md), names the problem whole before any tool is lifted, while [The Generalist](persona_the_generalist.md), holding [the generalist's seat](position_generalist.md), reaches across the kit for the tool that fits.

## Stage

[The Diagnostician](persona_the_diagnostician.md) tables [Name the Problem First](plan_name_the_problem_first.md) and answers [the law of the instrument](piece_the_law_of_the_instrument.md) by putting diagnosis before [the reflex](piece_the_reflex.md).
[The Generalist](persona_the_generalist.md) tables [Hold Many Tools](plan_hold_many_tools.md), fitting the problem better and, lacking [the mastery](piece_the_mastery.md), striking it worse.

## Tension

The only honest cure asks the master to set down its mastery, and cannot promise the trade was worth it.
Diagnosing first is the one move that breaks the reshaping, but it means putting a slow question before a fast, expert reflex, and reaching for fit means lifting a tool the hand can barely use. And the cost runs both ways: the generalist masters none of its kit, so it strikes the right nail clumsily, and sometimes the wielder's reflex tool was simply correct. It closes with the right tool meeting the real problem, or the reflex holding, or a clean strike traded for a dabbler's blow on a rightly chosen target.
