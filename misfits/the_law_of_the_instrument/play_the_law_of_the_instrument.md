# Play: The Law of the Instrument

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Law of the Instrument.
Give a hand a hammer and it grows expert with it, and the expertise is real, and earned. Then every problem that arrives is seen through the hammer: the part that can be struck becomes the problem, the part that cannot is trimmed away as noise, and the tool comes down, cleanly, on a nail it half made. To a hand that has mastered the hammer, the world is nails ... and the screw, and the seam, and the knot are all struck as though they were, and the ones that were never nails are the ones left broken.

## Arc

The bend is this: the tool a hand has mastered does not just answer the problem, it decides what the problem is.
It runs on competence turned to blindness.
Mastery is expensive and specific: to be excellent with one instrument is to have poured years into it, so the hand reaches for it first, sees the problem in its terms first, and is right to, because the tool genuinely solves the problems it fits.
But the reaching comes before the diagnosis, so the problem is reshaped to the tool rather than the tool chosen to the problem: the aspect the instrument can address is promoted to the whole of it, and the aspect it cannot is amputated to make the thing fit.
None of it is laziness. The wielder's skill is real and its reflex is the honest return on deep specialisation, the tool really is the best answer to everything shaped like its nail, and telling in advance whether a given problem is a nail is exactly the judgement the mastery crowds out. So the instrument is applied past its fit, the struck part looks solved, and the trimmed part festers where the tool could not reach.
Diagnose the problem before choosing the instrument, hold more than one tool, reach for fit over familiarity, and the right thing gets the right treatment: but that means setting down the tool you are a master of to pick up one you are a novice with, being worse before better, and it means bearing the symmetric error, that the generalist who holds many tools masters none, and that sometimes the hammer, honestly, was right.

## Triggers

**[The Reach](plot_the_reach.md)**
A problem arrives, and before it is diagnosed the Wielder reaches for the mastered tool, the Tool presenting itself as the answer to everything. The reflex is fast, expert, and earned, and it fires ahead of any question about what the problem actually is. Closes when the instrument is chosen before the problem is understood.

**[The Reshaping](plot_the_reshaping.md)**
Cue: the problem must be made to fit the tool. The Wielder promotes the aspect the instrument can strike to the whole of the problem, and the Nail takes the tool's shape, the case recast as exactly the job the hand is best at. Closes when the problem has become a nail and its other faces have gone out of view.

**[The Trimming](plot_the_trimming.md)**
Cue: the fit is not clean, and something must give. The Nail is fitted to the tool by cutting away the Residue, the part of the real problem the instrument cannot reach, trimmed off as noise so the strike can land square. Closes when the problem fits the tool because the parts that did not fit have been amputated.

**[The Miss](plot_the_miss.md)**
Cue: the tool comes down and the nail is struck clean. The struck part looks solved, and the Residue, left where the tool could not reach, festers and returns, exposing the instrument as apt but partial. Closes when the real problem is revealed unsolved behind a treatment that worked perfectly on the wrong target.

**[The Diagnosis](plot_the_diagnosis.md)**
Cue: someone moves to name the problem before choosing the tool. The Diagnostician would diagnose first and reach for fit, and the Generalist would hold many tools, against a Wielder asked to set down its mastery and the truth that the generalist masters none and the hammer is sometimes right. Closes when the right tool meets the real problem ... or the reflex holds, or a master is traded for a dabbler.

## Stakes

Whether a problem is ever met on its own terms, or only ever on the terms of the tool the hand happens to hold.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the instrument, which is mastered and reached for first, and away from the problem, which is reshaped to fit it before it is ever understood. The production cannot make the diagnosis come before the reflex, because the mastery that makes the tool worth reaching for is the same mastery that sees every problem as its nail. It can only show that no one is lazy, that the wielder's skill is real and its reach rational, that the tool truly is the best answer to everything shaped like its nail, and that the one move that meets the problem honestly, diagnosing before choosing and reaching for fit over familiarity, means being a novice again, worse before better, and running the symmetric risk that the many-tooled master nothing and the hammer, this time, was right.
