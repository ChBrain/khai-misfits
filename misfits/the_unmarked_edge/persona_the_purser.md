# Persona: The Purser

## Taxonomy

[The Blunt End](position_the_blunt_end.md). Presses for efficiency from a distance, trims the cost and the slack that seem to buy nothing, and reads a cheaper quarter with no incident as a saving cleanly made, pushing the operating point toward the edge it never sees.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Answerable for the numbers. Cutting what looks like waste. Rewarded by a leaner quarter.
Is the one at the blunt end, under a budget and a board and a competitor, whose job is to find the cost that can come out, and who looks at slack that has produced nothing visible and sees money sitting idle.
No greed wished on them: the pressure is real and legitimate, since a system that costs too much fails as surely as one that is unsafe, and trimming a margin that has bought no incident in living memory is exactly the economy the role exists to find.

## Action

Trims the budget, tightens the schedule, and cuts the slack that shows no return, then reads the quarter that comes in cheaper and no worse as the cut vindicated.
Sees the saving at once and the cost never, since the economy lands this quarter and the accident it risks, if it risks one, is distant, deniable, and someone else's to have.
Does what the blunt end does under competition: presses the operating point toward efficiency, banks each economy that pays and shows no harm, and moves the point toward the edge from a distance too great to feel the margin going.

## Shadow

The distance does its work on the feeling first: from where they sit the work is a report and not a place with people in it, so a cut lands as a solved problem, a clean and satisfying tightening, and never as a hand taken off something that was holding. They do not feel the slack going because they have never felt the slack, only ever the figure that stands for it, and a figure gives nothing up when it falls.
What moves them is the quiet pull of being good at it. The lean quarter arrives like praise, on time and hard to argue with, and it settles into a self-image they have earned and mean to keep: the prudent one, the steady hand on the money, the reason the whole thing can still afford to run. That picture has no place in it for the one who spends the margin, so the possibility never quite presents itself as a possibility, and the small disquiet that might have raised it, the sense of being rewarded a little too easily, is exactly the soft feeling a serious person learns to set aside.
The bind is interior and insulated: they must press for efficiency because the money is theirs to answer for, feel each cut as prudence and take real pride in it, and sit far enough from the work that the margin they remove was never something they could feel leaving, the distance that lets them cut without flinching the same distance that keeps the cost below the reach of feeling.

## Tell

Says "it came in under budget and nothing happened."
Is the whole economy in one line, since "nothing happened" is offered as the proof the cut was free, so the sentence that sounds like a clean result is the sound of the blunt end reading the absence of an accident as the absence of a cost, banking a margin spent as a margin saved because the bill for it has not yet come due.
