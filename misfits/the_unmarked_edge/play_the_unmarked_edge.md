# Play: The Unmarked Edge

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Unmarked Edge.
A safety-critical system works inside a space with three edges. It must not cost too much, or the organisation that runs it fails. It must not demand too much of the people, or they cannot sustain the work. And it must not be too unsafe, or it has an accident. The operating point, the way the work is actually done today, sits somewhere inside that space, and two steady pressures act on it. Management, under competition and a budget, presses for efficiency: do it cheaper, and the point moves away from the edge of economic failure. The people, with finite time and energy, press for less effort: do it easier, and the point moves away from the edge of unsustainable work. Both pressures are sound, and both push the point the same way, toward the third edge, the boundary of safe operation. And that boundary is not drawn anywhere. You cannot see it from inside ordinary work; you find it only by crossing it. So every step toward it is taken blind and rewarded: the shortcut saved effort, the tightened schedule saved money, and nothing went wrong, so the step looks like clear gain and the next one follows it. The system migrates toward the edge of safety not by any decision to be unsafe but by the sum of local moves, each of which paid and none of which, on its day, crossed the line, until one does, and the accident draws the boundary in hindsight, showing where the edge was the whole time. Nobody chose it. The manager pressed for efficiency because efficiency is the job, the worker adapted because the work had to be done with the time and effort there was, and each adaptation succeeded, so the drift was built of successes. The edge was unmarked, and it showed only in the crossing.

## Arc

The bend is this: the operating point of a system migrates, under the combined pressure of a cost gradient and an effort gradient, toward the boundary of safe operation, and because that boundary is unmarked and each step toward it is locally rewarded, the system drifts to the edge and across it with no decision to be unsafe ever taken (Rasmussen).
It runs on two pressures that push the same way.
Because management competes on cost and people conserve effort, the operating point is pushed by an efficiency gradient away from the edge of economic failure and by an effort gradient away from the edge of unsustainable work, and both gradients point toward the third boundary, so the point is driven toward the edge of safety by two forces neither of which is about safety at all (Rasmussen). The pressures are legitimate, and together they run downhill toward the edge.
And the boundary is not drawn.
Because the edge of safe operation is unmarked and cannot be seen from inside ordinary work, the system has no way to know how close it stands until it is across, so the margin is spent blind, each step into it indistinguishable, on the day, from a step still safely inside (Rasmussen; Dekker). The invisibility is the trap's floor, and it makes every move toward the edge look like every other.
And each step toward it is rewarded.
Because a shortcut that saves effort and a tightening that saves cost both pay at once, and neither causes an accident on the day it is taken, every move toward the edge returns a gain and no penalty, so the drift is built of local successes, each step reinforced by the good outcome it produced (Dekker). The reward is real, and it is what recruits the next step.
And the run of successes hides the edge.
Because nothing goes wrong along the way, the string of safe days is read as proof the system is safe, so the very absence of the accident that would mark the boundary is taken for evidence of room to spare, and the feedback that would halt the drift is exactly the accident that has not yet happened (Dekker). The quiet is mistaken for margin, and the silence that should warn instead reassures.
And the counter-pressure runs uphill.
Because holding the point back from an edge no one can see costs the very efficiency and effort the two gradients reward, keeping the margin is a continuous expense against a continuous pull, so the safety case is spent faster than it can be argued and the counter-gradient is always the uphill one against two that run down (Rasmussen). The defence is dear and thankless, paid against an accident that has not come.
And the edge shows only in the crossing.
Because the boundary is found by crossing it, the accident is the first unambiguous mark of where the edge was, drawing in hindsight the line no one could draw in advance, so the system learns the location of the boundary at the price of the accident that locates it (Dekker). The lesson is exact and too late, bought with the very crossing it would have prevented.
None of it is a fix. The manager presses for efficiency because efficiency is the mandate, the worker adapts because the job must be done with the time and effort there is, each adaptation succeeds and each economy pays, so pressing and adapting are only sound. Correct it, and the correction is dear: it means marking a boundary that shows itself only when crossed, spending real margin to hold the point back from an edge no one can prove is there, and sustaining that counter-pressure against two gradients that never rest, so the one move that would halt the drift is a continuous cost paid to avoid an accident that has not happened and looks, until it does, like effort and money spent on nothing. Short of it the point migrates under pressures each rational and each rewarded, to an edge marked only by the crossing, so the system drifts into failure not by anyone's recklessness but by the ordinary pull of doing the work cheaper and easier, every step of the way a success.

## Triggers

**[The Shortcut](plot_the_shortcut.md)**
A job to be done and less time or energy than it asks. The Hand finds a way to do it easier, dropping a step that has never mattered, and it works: the task is finished, the effort saved, and nothing goes wrong. Closes when the easier way becomes the way, the operating point moved a little toward the edge and the move rewarded by a good outcome, one adaptation banked as pure gain.

**[The Squeeze](plot_the_squeeze.md)**
Cue: a budget under pressure. The Purser trims the cost, tightens the schedule, and cuts the slack that seemed to buy nothing, and the numbers improve with no incident to show for the loss. Closes when the leaner way becomes the standard, the operating point pushed a little further toward the edge and the push confirmed by a quarter that came in cheaper and no worse, another economy banked.

**[The Slide](plot_the_slide.md)**
Cue: the adaptations and economies accumulate. The operating point migrates toward the boundary of safe operation, each step paying and none crossing, the run of safe days read as proof of room to spare. Closes when the system sits close to an edge it cannot see, its margin spent on a hundred rewarded moves, the drift invisible because every step of it succeeded.

**[The Crossing](plot_the_crossing.md)**
Cue: someone would hold the line. The Warden marks a boundary they cannot prove and presses back against the pull, and the margin they defend costs the efficiency the gradients reward, so the line is guessed and the guard is eroded, until the point crosses an edge no one had located and the accident draws it exactly. Closes when the boundary is marked at last by the crossing, the Warden's guessed line vindicated too late, or the counter-pressure never funded and the edge found the same way.

## Stakes

Whether a run of safe, efficient days is evidence the system has margin to spare, or is the sound of a working point drifting toward an edge no one can see until it is crossed.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the crossing, which the Purser is right to trim a cost that buys nothing visible, and the Hand right to do the job with the effort there is, and away from the counter-pressure that would hold the point back at the price of the efficiency the drift is rewarded for. The production cannot make the drift a recklessness, because each economy pays and each adaptation works and no accident marks the steps between, so trimming the cost and taking the shortcut and reading the safe record as safety are each only sensible acts. It can only show that a point pushed by a cost gradient and an effort gradient migrates toward the boundary they both point at, that the boundary is unmarked and its approach is rewarded and its silence mistaken for room, so a system tends to drift to the edge of safe operation on a run of successes and learn where the edge was only by crossing it, and that the one move which would halt the drift, marking the boundary and holding the point back, is a continuous cost against two gradients that never rest, so the failure arrives not by anyone's recklessness but by the ordinary good sense of everyone doing the work a little cheaper and a little easier, every step of the way a success.
