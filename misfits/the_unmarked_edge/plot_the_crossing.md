# Plot: The Crossing

## Taxonomy

[The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Verge](place_the_verge.md), someone who would hold the line.
A Warden who reads the drift and would mark the boundary, and a point already close to an edge no one has located.

## Action

[Marking](process_marking.md): the Warden fixes a line they cannot prove and presses back, the counter-pressure costing the efficiency the gradients reward and eroding against them.

## Stage

[The Warden](persona_the_warden.md) acts from [the Envelope](position_the_envelope.md) on [Mark the Edge](plan_mark_the_edge.md), guessing at [the unmarked boundary](piece_the_unmarked_boundary.md) and holding [the operating point](piece_the_operating_point.md) back, until the point crosses the real edge and the accident draws it exactly.
The line was guessed and the guard was thankless, and the edge showed itself only in the crossing.

## Tension

The one who would have prevented it could not point to the edge, and the accident that proves them right is the one they meant to stop.
There is no clean save available: the Warden reasoned to a boundary they could not mark, held a line the safe record argued against, and spent credibility on a risk they could not display, so their counter-pressure slowed the drift and did not halt it. The point crossed an edge no one had located, and the accident drew the line at last, exact and too late, vindicating the Warden in the only currency that could and at the price they existed to prevent. The plot closes with the boundary known at last by the crossing, the guessed line proved right by the disaster it warned of, or the counter-pressure never funded and the edge found the same way, the lesson bought with the crossing that taught it.
