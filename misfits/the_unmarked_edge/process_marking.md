# Process: Marking

## Taxonomy

[The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md). The attempt to fix the boundary and hold the point back, the counter-gradient the Warden presses against the pull, a case argued without a coordinate and eroded by every safe day.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Warden](persona_the_warden.md), at [the Verge](place_the_verge.md), reasoning to where the edge must be and pressing the point back from it.
Begins when someone reads the drift for what it is and tries to defend a margin the two gradients want spent.

## Direction

Turns an inference about the edge into a counter-pressure that mostly fails.
Because the boundary is unmarked and the Warden can only reason to it, marking tries to convert a case into a coordinate, giving the point a line to be held behind, but the line is guessed and the counter-pressure costs the very efficiency the gradients reward, so marking pushes uphill against two forces that run down and is spent faster than it is argued (Rasmussen; Dekker).

## Lever

An inference with no coordinate.
Because the edge leaves no sign until it is crossed, marking works only through reasoning, not evidence: the Warden argues that the boundary is near and cannot show it, so the lever is a case that must outbid a measured saving with an unprovable risk, contradicted by each safe day and lighter, on the record, than the economy it opposes.

## Echo

A guessed line, held for a while and overrun, proved only by the crossing.
The echo of marking is a counter-pressure that slows the drift and does not stop it, a margin defended on inference and spent anyway, so the Warden's line is a guess that the safe record argues against until the point crosses the real edge and the accident draws it, the marking vindicated exactly when it is too late to have mattered.
