# Process: Pressing

## Taxonomy

[The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md). The cost pressure applied from a distance, the cut that trims the slack and books the saving, the efficiency gradient made into a move of the operating point.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Purser](persona_the_purser.md), at [the Boardroom](place_the_boardroom.md), finding the cost that can come out.
Begins whenever a budget under pressure meets slack that has produced no visible return, and the slack is cut.

## Direction

Turns a demand for efficiency into a move toward the edge.
Because the system must not cost too much and slack shows no return the room can read, the Purser trims the margin and tightens the schedule, so pressing converts a legitimate economy, a routine trade of thoroughness for efficiency (Hollnagel), into a change in where the point sits, each cut pushing the operating point toward the boundary while booking a clean saving (Rasmussen).

## Lever

The saving the cut returns.
Because the economy lands this quarter with a clear signal and the risk it adds arrives, if at all, far off and on another account, pressing works through the lopsided reward of a cut that pays: the numbers the blunt end reads all improve, so the lever is the saving itself, applauded and uncharged, which confirms each cut and licenses the next, the margin removed appearing in no ledger the room holds.

## Echo

A leaner system, one cut nearer the edge and cheaper for it.
The echo of pressing is a margin gone and a quarter improved, the slack read as waste and removed with no incident to mourn it, so the operating point stands closer to the boundary and the record of the cut is a saving, the drift advanced by an economy that every legible measure calls sound.
