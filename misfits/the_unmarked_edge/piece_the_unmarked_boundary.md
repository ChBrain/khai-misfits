# Piece: The Unmarked Boundary

## Taxonomy

[The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md). The edge of safe operation that leaves no sign until it is crossed, the line the system cannot see from inside ordinary work, known only in hindsight by the accident that locates it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Lies at [the Verge](place_the_verge.md), unseen, the far side of the ground the point now rests on.
Flows as an edge without a mark, a boundary real in its consequence and absent in every sign, the one line in the space that is not drawn anywhere the system can read.

## Load Bearing

Without the Unmarked Boundary the drift would be safe: a visible edge would halt the point, since no one would step across a line they could see. It is the invisibility of the boundary that makes the migration a trap.
The Unmarked Boundary is load bearing because it is what turns a rewarded drift into a crossing: the edge is real, but it announces nothing, so the approach to it is indistinguishable from safe operation and the margin is spent blind (Rasmussen; Dekker). It is knowable only by being crossed, which means the one measurement that would locate it is the accident itself, so the system cannot price its own distance to the edge and reads its nearness as room. Every other piece depends on this one: the Pull could be resisted and the Quiet would not deceive if the boundary were marked, but because it is not, each step toward it looks like every other and the line is drawn only when it is too late.

## Apparent

Just open room, a system running well with no sign of danger.
Seen at the surface, the Unmarked Boundary is nothing at all, since an edge that leaves no mark looks exactly like clear space, and the truth that a line is there, that the point is near it, that the calm is the calm of ground that has not yet given way, stays hidden behind the plain fact that there is nothing to see.

## Yearbook

Unseen by [the Hand](persona_the_hand.md) who moves toward it, uncharged to [the Purser](persona_the_purser.md) who funds the approach, and named but not located by [the Warden](persona_the_warden.md) who would mark it.
It is the invisible line: the Unmarked Boundary is what [migrating](process_migrating.md) approaches and [marking](process_marking.md) cannot fix, the edge [the operating point](piece_the_operating_point.md) nears under [the pull](piece_the_pull.md) while [the quiet](piece_the_quiet.md) reads its silence as safety, drawn at last only by the crossing.
