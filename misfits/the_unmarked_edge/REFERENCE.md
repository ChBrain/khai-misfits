# The Unmarked Edge: Reference

The research warrant for [The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md).
The dynamic is Rasmussen's drift into failure: his migration model of the operating point under cost and effort
gradients, Dekker's account of drift as an incremental process visible only in hindsight, Snook's practical drift,
Cook and Woods's sharp-end and blunt-end, and Hollnagel's efficiency-thoroughness trade-off, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging Rasmussen's drift into failure as a **migration-to-an-unmarked-boundary structure**: a system's operating point,
the way the work is actually done, sits in a space bounded by economic failure, unsustainable workload, and unsafe
performance, and two steady gradients act on it. A cost gradient, the pressure for efficiency, pushes the point away from
the economic boundary; an effort gradient, the pull toward less work, pushes it away from the workload boundary; and both
point the same way, toward the boundary of safe operation. The play models the documented mechanics, the two aligned
gradients that sum rather than cancel, the operating point they migrate, the boundary of safe operation that is unmarked
and shows itself only when crossed, the run of safe outcomes that rewards each step and hides the edge, and the
counter-pressure that must argue for margin without a coordinate, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a
tale of a reckless operator or a negligent manager. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law:
under competitive and workload pressure the operating point migrates toward the boundary of acceptable performance, and
because that boundary is not marked and the migration is made of locally rational, locally rewarded adaptations, the
system drifts to the edge and across it with no decision to be unsafe. It is a tendency under pressure and not an iron
law, holding where the gradients are strong and the boundary unmarked and the feedback slow, and weakening where the
edge is instrumented and the margin defended, so the play stages the pull toward the edge, not a fate that every system
crosses.

It is distinct from the house's The New Normal, which stages the normalization of deviance, a risky act cognitively
reclassified as acceptable each time it passes without harm: there the movement is in perception, a shifting judgement of
what counts as acceptable, and the act itself is unchanged while the eye that reads it recalibrates. Here nothing need be
reclassified: the operating point migrates because two pressure gradients physically move the practice toward the
boundary, and the edge stays exactly where it was while the system slides toward it, so the engine is a gradient-driven
migration of the work and not a recalibration of the perception of it. It is distinct too from The Brittle Optimum, whose
engine is a leanness that breeds fragility once slack is optimised away, in that the load-bearing move here is the
continuous migration of the operating point toward an unmarked edge rather than the static fragility of a lean
configuration, and from The Spent Margin, whose risk compensation consumes a realised safety gain, in that no safety gain
is being spent here, the margin going to the pull of cost and effort with no dividend returned to be consumed.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the manager as negligent or
  the worker as reckless, because the source finding is precisely that no recklessness and no negligence is needed: the
  worker adapts because the job must be done with the time and effort there is and the adaptation succeeds (Snook), the
  manager trims because the system must not cost too much and the slack shows no return (Rasmussen; Hollnagel), and each
  move is rewarded and none causes an accident on its day (Dekker). So the misfit is that a point pushed by a cost
  gradient and an effort gradient migrates toward an unmarked boundary they both face, and the system drifts across it, no
  recklessness and no decision to be unsafe required, the adapting and the trimming each sound and the crossing the sum,
  the worker who takes the shortcut as reasonable as the manager who cuts the slack.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a system saved by one clear-eyed warden. The move that would
  halt the drift is to mark the boundary and hold the point back from it, but the boundary is unmarked and knowable only
  by crossing it (Rasmussen; Dekker), so the warden argues from inference with no coordinate, contradicted by every safe
  day and outbid by every real saving, and the counter-pressure they press costs the very efficiency the gradients
  reward, spent faster than it is argued. So the true reading arrives as a guessed line held uphill against two gradients
  that never rest, vindicated only by the accident it warned of, not a way to see the edge in time and be spared, the
  margin defended a continuous cost against a crossing that, prevented, leaves no proof it was ever near.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the migration model and the drift into failure
  are Rasmussen's, the account of drift as incremental and hindsight-only is Dekker's, the practical drift of practice
  from procedure is Snook's, the sharp-end and blunt-end are Cook and Woods's, and the efficiency-thoroughness trade-off
  is Hollnagel's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages a point migrating to an unmarked
  edge and credits that no one was reckless and the drift is the sum of good moves, holding it a tendency under pressure
  and not a law that fixes every system at the edge.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Operating Point](piece_the_operating_point.md)**: carries the migration model whole (Rasmussen), the live
  practice as a position in the bounded space, pushed by the two gradients toward the boundary of safe operation.
- **[The Pull](piece_the_pull.md)**: carries the aligned gradients (Rasmussen) and the efficiency-thoroughness trade-off
  that feeds them (Hollnagel), the cost and effort pressures summed into one slope toward the edge.
- **[The Unmarked Boundary](piece_the_unmarked_boundary.md)**: carries the unmarked edge (Rasmussen), the boundary of safe
  operation that leaves no sign until it is crossed and is drawn only in hindsight (Dekker).
- **[The Quiet](piece_the_quiet.md)**: carries the incremental drift (Dekker), the run of safe outcomes that rewards each
  step and is mistaken for margin, the missing feedback that would otherwise halt the point.
- **[The Sharp End](position_the_sharp_end.md)** and **[The Blunt End](position_the_blunt_end.md)**: carry the sharp-end
  and blunt-end structure (Cook and Woods), the practitioners who absorb the pressures the remote end sets.
