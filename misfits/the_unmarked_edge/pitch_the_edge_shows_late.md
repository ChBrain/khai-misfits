# Pitch: The Edge Shows Late

## Taxonomy

[The Unmarked Edge](play_the_unmarked_edge.md). The single hook: a cost gradient and an effort gradient both push a system's operating point toward the boundary of safe operation, and because that boundary is unmarked and each step toward it is rewarded, the system drifts to the edge on a run of successes and learns where it was only by crossing it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Work a little cheaper, work a little easier, and nothing goes wrong.
The tenor is sound operational practice: an economy that costs no incident is a saving, an adaptation that finishes the job with less effort is an improvement, so trimming the cost and easing the work are exactly what a well-run system should do.

## Undertow

Both economies push the same way, toward the edge of safe operation.
Underneath, the cost pressure and the effort pressure are two gradients pointing at one boundary, so every saving moves the operating point a little closer to the edge of safety, the point migrating under forces neither of which is about safety and both of which pay.

## Nerve

The edge is unmarked, so its approach looks like room to spare.
The nerve is that the boundary of safe operation cannot be seen from inside ordinary work, so the margin is spent blind and each step into it is rewarded like any other, the run of safe days read as proof of slack while the point sits closer to the edge than anyone can tell.

## Echo

The boundary is drawn only by the accident that crosses it.
What lingers is that the edge shows itself only in the crossing, so the counter-pressure that would hold the point back is a continuous cost against an accident that has not happened, and the system learns where the boundary was at the price of the crossing that locates it, the lesson exact and too late.
