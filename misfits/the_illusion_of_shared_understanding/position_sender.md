# Position: Sender

## Taxonomy

[The Illusion of Shared Understanding](play_the_illusion_of_shared_understanding.md). The seat that utters the form of words, where a meaning vivid in one head is felt as a message that carried.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A vivid particular meaning and a form of words to carry it: the intent clear in its own head and the sentence that names it.
A real clarity: the meaning is genuine and definite, so saying it in good faith is not vagueness.
No way to staple the meaning to the words, and no sight of the blank they leave, because the meaning feels too plain to need filling.

## Orders

Say it once.
Name the meaning in a clear form of words, send it to the room, and take the nod for understanding, because the meaning is plain and the words say it.
Trust the words to carry the meaning, since to the sender they plainly do.

## Loses

The meaning it meant to send, exactly by feeling it too plain to spell out.
Because the sender cannot picture their vivid meaning arriving as anything else, the seat does not ground it, so the room's nod is taken for agreement on the meaning when it is only agreement on the words, and the meaning that felt transparent leaves the room as several different readings, the sender's certainty added to a consensus that was never on the sense.
It gains a sentence said and a room that nods, and loses the shared meaning it was sure it sent.

## Drives

Toward the words sent and the meaning assumed landed,
the vividness inside felt as legibility outside,
so the seat that says it once sends a form of words, and takes the nod for the meaning it never grounded.
