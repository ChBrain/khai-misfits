# Position: Clarifier

## Taxonomy

[The Illusion of Shared Understanding](play_the_illusion_of_shared_understanding.md). The seat that would ground the meaning before acting, surfacing each reading in concrete terms, at the cost of looking like the one who did not follow.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A doubt about easy agreement and the standing to call for a read-back: the will to test the consensus and the authority to ask each to restate.
A real corrective: making the private readings public is the one thing that shows the agreement was only on the words.
No cheap way to do it, and no welcome for the one who slows a room that has plainly agreed.

## Orders

Say it back.
Have each receiver restate the instruction in their own concrete terms, surface the several readings, and ground the meaning rather than the assent, before anyone goes to the benches.
Check the meaning, and treat an easy consensus as suspect for coming easy.

## Loses

The goodwill of a room that has agreed, and the standing of one who keeps up.
Because the consensus is visible and grounding it means slowing a settled meeting, asking the question that confesses one did not follow, and spelling out what feels obvious, the seat must spend that social cost to catch the divergence, and the divergence it prevents leaves no trace while the delay it costs is plain, so the caught misunderstanding looks, after, like one that was never going to happen.
It gains the meaning grounded and the merge spared, and loses the ease and the standing that going along would have kept.

## Drives

Toward the reading surfaced and the meaning grounded,
at the social cost of doubting a consensus the room can feel,
so the seat that says it back catches the divergence in the room, and is read as the one who did not follow.
