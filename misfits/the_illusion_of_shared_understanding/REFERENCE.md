# The Illusion of Shared Understanding: Reference

The research warrant for [The Illusion of Shared Understanding](play_the_illusion_of_shared_understanding.md).
The dynamic is the illusion of transparency over an underdetermined message: Gilovich, Savitsky and Medvec's
illusion of transparency, Keysar's illusory transparency of intention, Clark's common ground and grounding, and
Nickerson on imputing one's own knowledge to others, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the illusion of shared understanding as a **filled-blank structure**: a form of words leaves a blank each
receiver must fill from the meaning nearest to hand, and because each filling feels not like a reading but like
the plain sense, each takes their private meaning for the shared one, the room agrees on the words and never on
the meaning, and one instruction executes in several faithful directions whose divergence shows only at the
merge. The play models the documented mechanics, the underdetermined message that names a meaning without carrying
it, the egocentric filling of the blank with the reading nearest to each context, the illusion of transparency by
which a meaning vivid to its holder is felt as shared and a sender feels their words landed as sent, the visible
assent that grounds the words and not the sense and raises the cost of the check, the divergent execution that
stays invisible until the pieces are joined, and the costly read-back that alone surfaces the readings in the
room, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of mishearing or inattention. The governing
finding of the sources is the play's governing law: understanding is not transmitted by an utterance but must be
grounded by mutual confirmation, and speakers and hearers systematically overestimate how well meaning is
conveyed, so a room can agree on a form of words while each holds a different meaning, sincerely certain the
meaning is shared.

It is distinct from the house's Second Nature, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: there the
engine is a dyadic knowledge gap, the expert who cannot un-know what they know and so cannot model the novice's
ignorance, pitching the explanation over their head; here there is no knowledge asymmetry required and the parties
may know exactly the same, the failure being that an underdetermined message is filled differently by each and
each filling is believed shared, a divergence of meaning and not a gap of knowledge. It is distinct from The Trip
to Abilene, where each privately disagrees but presumes the others agree and so goes along, a false consensus of
preference in which the true view is known and hidden: here nothing is hidden and each genuinely believes they
have understood, a false consensus of meaning and not of preference. And it is distinct from The Decision Travels,
where a correct decision crosses a chain of stations and none validates it against the body that must live with
it: there the decision arrives without its context and the loss is of validation across hand-offs, here the
decision arrives intact and is understood, differently, by everyone at once. (It is also distinct from the
just-staged Abstraction Ladder, whose loss is the checkable particular shed as a report climbs, a vertical
abstraction; the Illusion is a horizontal divergence of meaning over a message that did not move at all.)

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the receivers as bad
  listeners or the sender as vague, because the source finding is precisely that no one errs: the sentence is said
  in good faith with a definite meaning, each receiver fills the blank with a reasonable reading, the room is
  attentive, and every builder does faithfully what the words told them. The illusion is authored by no one and
  emerges from an underdetermined message filled from the meaning nearest each hand, so the misfit is that a form
  of words short enough to agree on is open enough to fill differently, and a reading that feels plain to its
  holder feels plain to every holder of a different one.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that grounds the
  meaning is to have each receiver say the instruction back in their own concrete terms, surface the reading and
  not just the assent, and check the meaning rather than the words, and it means slowing a meeting that has plainly
  agreed, asking the question that confesses one did not follow, spelling out what everyone feels is already
  obvious, and treating a real-seeming consensus as suspect for coming easy. The reckoning is a meaning grounded
  only by paying, continuously, the social cost of doubting a consensus the room can feel, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the illusion of transparency is Gilovich, Savitsky and Medvec's, the
  illusory transparency of intention and the egocentric resolution of an underdetermined message are Keysar's, the
  grounding of meaning by mutual confirmation is Clark and Brennan's, and the projection of one's own reading onto
  others is Nickerson's. The name is corrected from the batch's "The False Consensus", which collides with Ross's
  false-consensus effect, a different phenomenon; the honest boundary, that the message is heard the same and each
  reading is reasonable, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Form of Words](piece_the_form_of_words.md)**: carries the utterance that must be grounded (Clark), named
  meaning the sentence does not itself carry.
- **[The Blank](piece_the_blank.md)**: carries the underdetermination (Keysar), the space the words leave that
  each receiver must fill.
- **[The Meaning](piece_the_meaning.md)**: carries the egocentric reading (Keysar; Nickerson), the private
  resolution taken for the plain sense and believed shared.
- **[The Nod](piece_the_nod.md)**: carries grounding skipped (Clark & Brennan), the visible assent that ratifies
  the words and stands in for the confirmation it does not perform.
- **[The Divergence](piece_the_divergence.md)**: carries the divergent execution, one instruction built faithfully
  several ways, surfacing only at the merge.
- **[The Accord](piece_the_accord.md)**: carries the illusion of transparency whole (Gilovich, Savitsky &
  Medvec), the shared understanding sincerely believed over a meaning never held in common.
