# Place: The Read-Back

## Taxonomy

[The Illusion of Shared Understanding](play_the_illusion_of_shared_understanding.md). Where each receiver says the instruction back in their own concrete terms, the one arrangement in which the private readings are surfaced and the divergence caught before the benches.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the meaning is grounded before it is acted on, a read-back, a restatement round, each person saying in concrete terms what they will build.
The Clarifier asking each to say it back, and the several readings coming into the open where a single nod had stood.

## Holds

The grounding, and the divergence it brings to light.
Here each reading is made public before it is built, so the Read-Back holds the point where the meaning is tested, not the assent: each receiver restating the instruction in their own terms and the several fillings surfacing, so it is where the agreement on the words is finally checked against the meaning, the one arrangement in which the divergence is caught in the room rather than at the merge.

## Offers

A way to catch the divergence before the benches, and the meaning grounded and not just the words.
Because the Read-Back makes each private reading public, it offers the one thing the Meeting could not, the several meanings surfaced while they can still be reconciled, so it hands out the divergence caught early, and hands it out at the price of slowing a settled meeting and asking each to spell out what the room felt was already plain.

## Withheld

Any ease, or any proof that the catch was needed.
The Read-Back can surface the readings but cannot make the surfacing quick or make its worth show, because the divergence it prevents leaves no trace and the delay it costs is felt at once, so it withholds the standing of one who keeps the meeting moving, exacting the look of labouring the obvious, and, all grounded meaning and no visible payoff, it withholds the thanks for a merge that now never breaks, the caught misunderstanding seeming, after, like one that was never going to happen.
