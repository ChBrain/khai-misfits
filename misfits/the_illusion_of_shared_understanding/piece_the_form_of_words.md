# Piece: The Form of Words

## Taxonomy

[The Illusion of Shared Understanding](play_the_illusion_of_shared_understanding.md). The message uttered, the sentence that names the meaning without carrying it, clear enough to agree on and open enough to fill differently.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Said at [the meeting](place_the_meeting.md), where it is received and nodded to.
Restated at [the read-back](place_the_read_back.md), where each says back what they took it to mean.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no message and no blank, and no divergent readings to fill it.
With it the trap has its object: a form of words that names a meaning without stapling it on, so it is short enough to agree on and underdetermined enough that each must fill it (Clark, on the utterance as a coordination device that requires grounding to mean the same to both). The Form of Words is load bearing because it is the shared thing that is not a shared meaning: the sentence every receiver hears identically and reads differently, so the piece carries the message whose very usability, its brevity and clarity, is what leaves it open to be filled several ways.

## Apparent

A clear instruction, a plain sentence everyone heard the same.
Seen at the surface, the Form of Words looks like a shared meaning, the words identical in every ear and therefore, it seems, the same to every mind, and the truth that identical words are not an identical meaning, that the sentence names a meaning it does not carry, that being heard the same is not being understood the same, stays hidden behind how plainly a clear sentence seems to say one thing.

## Yearbook

Said by [the sender](persona_the_sender.md) through [sending](process_sending.md), filled by [the receiver](persona_the_receiver.md) through [filling](process_filling.md), the message all hear and each reads.
