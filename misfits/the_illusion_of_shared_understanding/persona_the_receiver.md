# Persona: The Receiver

## Taxonomy

[Receiver](position_receiver.md). The one who fills the blank the words leave with the meaning nearest to hand and takes that reading for the plain sense, believing everyone resolved it the same. The archetype of the several who diverge.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Attentive. Reasonable. Sure they understood.
Is any one of the several who hear the instruction, the builder, the team, the reader who takes the words and must act on them.
No inattention in it: the Receiver listens well and fills the words with a sensible meaning, its own work and worry supplying the reading nearest to hand.

## Action

Hears the form of words, fills the blank it leaves with the meaning closest to their own context, and takes that filling for what the words obviously say.
Reads "make it simple" as fewer features, or as a cleaner interface, or as less code, each certain their reading is the plain one.
Does what a receiver must: it fills the underdetermined message to use it, and takes the effortless filling for the shared meaning.

## Shadow

Not a bad listener, a faithful filler of a blank that must be filled, and that is the whole difficulty: the filling is necessary, and the meaning nearest to hand feels not like a reading but like the meaning.
Because the reading that came effortlessly feels like what the words simply say, the Receiver cannot see it as one resolution among several, so takes it for the obvious sense and assumes everyone else resolved it the same, then goes to their bench and builds it faithfully, one of several builders each executing a different reading of one sentence, sure to the last that they are on the same page as the rest.
It fills the blank sensibly, and the sense of its own filling is exactly what hides that others filled it otherwise.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "yes, understood," and means a different thing than the receiver beside them.
Says it having filled the blank with the meaning nearest to hand, and cannot tell their reading from the meaning, so hears its own gloss as the plain words and assumes the room heard the same.
