# Persona: The Sender

## Taxonomy

[Sender](position_sender.md). The one who utters the form of words, clear in their own head, and, feeling their meaning transparent, sends it as if it carried itself and believes it landed as sent.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Clear. Confident. Sure they were understood.
Is the one who gives the instruction, the leader, the author, the speaker who says the reasonable sentence, make it simple, tighten the scope, and moves on.
No vagueness intended: the Sender has a vivid, particular meaning behind the words and says the words in good faith, meaning exactly one thing.

## Action

Utters the form of words that names their meaning, and, feeling that meaning plain, sends it to the room and takes the nod for understanding.
Says the sentence once, clearly, and does not spell out the particular reading behind it, because to them it needs no spelling out.
Does what a sender does: it names the meaning in words and trusts the words to carry it, the vividness inside felt as legibility outside.

## Shadow

Not careless, a speaker whose meaning is genuinely vivid to them, and that is the whole difficulty: the vividness is real, and a meaning vivid to its holder feels transparent to everyone, though each holds a different one.
Because the Sender cannot picture their plain meaning arriving as anything else, they do not see the blank the words leave, so they take the room's nod as agreement on the meaning when it is only agreement on the words, and add their own certainty to the room's, the illusion of transparency sending them from the room as sure as everyone else that the page is shared.
It says the sentence in good faith, and the good faith is exactly what keeps it from seeing the sentence could be filled another way.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "you know what I mean," and believes it.
Says it with a meaning plain in their own head, and cannot see that plain-in-one-head is not the same as shared, the vividness inside mistaken for a message that carried.
