# Piece: The Unstable Surcharge

## Taxonomy

[The Calm You Pay For](play_the_calm_you_pay_for.md). The positive value the conserved total takes when the open loop carries right-half-plane poles, so a loop holding up something that would fall on its own pays out more amplification than it ever buys in calm.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Charged across [the spectrum](place_the_spectrum.md), where the total itself is tallied, and collected in [the far band](place_the_far_band.md), where the excess has nowhere to go but up.

## Load Bearing

Without it every loop, however hard its plant, would at worst break even, the calm it buys matched cleanly by the rise it pays, a fair trade whatever the difficulty.
With it the trade is turned against the loop by the very thing the loop was built to fix: the Unstable Surcharge is load bearing because for an open loop with right-half-plane poles the conserved integral is no longer zero but positive, set by the sum of those poles (Freudenberg and Looze), so the loop pays out more sensitivity above one than it ever wins below it. The piece carries the finding that instability does not just make control harder in the ordinary sense, it changes the arithmetic of the trade itself, charging a surcharge no amount of skill in [Shape the Sensitivity](plan_shape_the_sensitivity.md) can waive.

## Apparent

Ordinary hard tuning, a difficult plant that simply needs a stronger controller and more careful work.
Seen at the surface, the Unstable Surcharge looks like degree, as if an unstable plant were just a harder instance of the same fair trade every loop makes, and the truth that instability flips the sign of the ledger, that the total owed is no longer zero but a fixed positive number set by the poles themselves, stays hidden behind how similar a hard, well-tuned unstable loop looks to a hard, well-tuned stable one, right up until the bill for each is compared.
