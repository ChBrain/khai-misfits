# Pitch: Quiet Where It Matters

## Taxonomy

[The Calm You Pay For](play_the_calm_you_pay_for.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Smooth, rejected, well in hand.
The plain satisfaction of a loop that answers the disturbance where it lives: the sensitivity pushed down in the working band, the error small, the output quiet exactly where anyone would look.
Nothing false in it: the rejection is real, correctly placed where the trouble is, and the loop truly is calm in the band that matters.

## Undertow

Under the smoothness, the sensitivity forced above one out past the working band, amplifying the noise no one was watching and creeping toward the edge.
Because the integral of the log-sensitivity is conserved, the calm bought below one must be paid for by an equal rise above one somewhere else, the cost not removed but relocated to the frequencies no one inspected.
The rejection that makes the band that matters quiet is the same rejection that makes the far band sharp.

## Nerve

The confidence of the loop that rejects the disturbance beautifully, sure it is well designed because it is smooth where you looked,
because the sensitivity really is down where the trouble lives and the output really is quiet there,
while out past the band the same loop amplifies the noise and inches toward instability, the bill for the calm coming due where no one was watching.

## Echo

After the run: a loop calm in the band that mattered and peaky where it did not, no clumsiness and no mistake, only a conserved total balanced,
the rejection genuine and correctly placed and the rise its lawful counterpart, forced above one by the same theorem that let it fall below,
and, where the plant would have fallen on its own, more amplification paid out than calm ever bought.
