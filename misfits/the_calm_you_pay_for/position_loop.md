# Position: Loop

## Taxonomy

[The Calm You Pay For](play_the_calm_you_pay_for.md). The seat of the feedback that conserves the log-sensitivity integral, paying every calm forward as an equal rise, where the trade is made mandatory.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A conserved total and a shape that can be pushed: the standing to carry the sensitivity across frequency, and a fixed integral it must keep whatever the design does.
A clear case: the conservation is a theorem, so the total is not a policy but a law, and the seat holds it whether anyone wills it or not.
No power to change the sum, only to redistribute it, and where the plant is unstable the sum it must hold is positive, not zero.

## Orders

Conserve the sum.
Lower the sensitivity wherever it is pressed and raise it, by exactly as much, elsewhere, keeping the integral of the log fixed however the loop is shaped.
Pay every scrap of calm forward as an equal rise, and hold the total unmoved.

## Loses

Any freedom to grant calm without taking it back, and, where the plant is unstable, any hope of breaking even.
Because the integral is fixed, the seat cannot lower the sensitivity anywhere without raising it somewhere, so it gives up the possibility of net calm, holding a total it may reshape but never reduce; and with right-half-plane poles the fixed total is positive, so it must raise more than it lowers.
It surrenders the sum to the law, keeping only the freedom to say where, never how much.

## Drives

Toward every calm answered by an equal rise and the total held fixed,
the sensitivity moved and never spent,
so the seat that only conserves is the seat that makes the trade mandatory.
