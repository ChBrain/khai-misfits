# Process: Conserving

## Taxonomy

[The Calm You Pay For](play_the_calm_you_pay_for.md). The calm bought below one answered by a rise above one elsewhere, the integral held fixed by the Loop, the second half of the trade and the one the Designer does not choose.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The calm already bought below one in the working band.
At [the Working Band](place_the_working_band.md), once the sensitivity is pressed down there, conserving begins the instant that pressing registers on the rest of the axis.

## Direction

Toward a rise forced above one elsewhere, the integral held fixed.
Because the integral of the log-sensitivity is conserved across all frequencies, conserving drives an equal area up wherever the working band's area went down, so the direction is not chosen by the designer but assigned by the total: the sum unmoved, the shape displaced.

## Lever

The conservation theorem meeting the design.
Because the total cannot be lowered by any shaping of the loop, conserving runs on the theorem meeting whatever the designer presses, so the lever is the law itself: you cannot lower without raising, and the raising follows the lowering as its exact counterpart.

## Echo

A rise above one somewhere off band, the calm bound to an equal rise, the total unchanged.
The area pressed down in the working band has surfaced, unbidden, past it, so what reads as two separate facts, a calm here and a rise there, is one conserved total, positive rather than zero if the plant is unstable, and never less than what was bought.
