# Place: The Spectrum

## Taxonomy

[The Calm You Pay For](play_the_calm_you_pay_for.md). The whole frequency axis, the stage where the sensitivity is shaped and its integral held conserved, wide enough to show the working band's calm and the far band's rise as one bent line.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The whole sensitivity curve at once, one continuous line running from the lowest frequency to the highest, pressed below one in one stretch and lifted above one in another.
Nothing here is a snapshot: the working band's calm and the far band's rise are drawn in the same stroke, so what looks like two separate places is one shape, bent down on one side because it is bent up on the other.

## Holds

The working band and the far band, and the sum that binds them.
The Spectrum holds both the stretch where the disturbance lives and the stretch where the noise does, and holds too, uninspectable in either band alone, the conserved total that ties them: the integral of the log-sensitivity, fixed by the loop and indifferent to where its area falls.

## Offers

The freedom to reshape the curve, to move the sensitivity from one stretch to another.
Because the Spectrum is the whole axis and not one band of it, it offers the designer real room to choose where the calm is bought and where the rise is paid, so the curve can be pressed down here and let rise there, and there instead of here, the shape movable across the whole line.

## Withheld

Any net lowering of the curve as a whole.
The Spectrum will not hand over a sensitivity pushed below one everywhere, because the integral across it is fixed, so what it withholds is not this or that stretch of calm but the possibility of calm without a matching rise: the sensitivity can be moved around the axis, never removed from it.
