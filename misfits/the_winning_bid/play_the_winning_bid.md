# Play: The Winning Bid

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Winning Bid.
A field of bidders values the same prize and guesses at it differently, and the one who guesses highest wins ... so the very act of winning is proof you were the most optimistic in the room, and on a thing worth the same to everyone, the most optimistic is the most wrong.

## Arc

The bend is this: winning a common prize is bad news, because it selects the bidder who overestimated most.
It runs on a split between what a thing is worth and what each bidder guesses it is worth.
The prize has one true value nobody knows. Each bidder appraises it honestly and lands somewhere around the truth, some high, some low. Each bids near their own guess. The auction then does one thing: it hands the prize to the highest bid, which is the highest guess, which is, precisely because it is the highest, the one most likely above the truth. So the winner pays a price set by the rosiest estimate in the field, and the underbidders who lost were the ones the field protected.
Shade the bid below the estimate, bid as if winning already tells you that you were too high, and the curse lifts: the prize goes to whoever wins, and to win it at your honest guess is to learn, too late, that no one else would go so far.

## Triggers

**[The Estimate](plot_the_estimate.md)**
A prize of one unknown value goes up for sale, and every bidder appraises it honestly, landing somewhere around the truth. The Geologist works up a number. Closes when the field holds a spread of estimates scattered about a value none of them can see.

**[The Auction](plot_the_auction.md)**
Cue: bids are made near estimates, and the auction rewards the largest. The Bidder offers close to their number and the Auctioneer draws the field upward. Closes when the gavel falls to the highest bid, which is the highest guess.

**[The Win](plot_the_win.md)**
Cue: the prize is taken and the room celebrates. The Winner claims it and the Board approves, reading the victory as vindication. Closes when winning is booked as triumph, unaware the auction just selected the most optimistic estimate in the field.

**[The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md)**
Cue: the true value comes in, below the price paid. The Board writes it down and the Underbidders, who lost, turn out to have been spared. Closes when the loss is named as the winner's curse ... or blamed on bad luck and poor execution, and the next auction is entered the same way.

**[The Shade](plot_the_shade.md)**
Cue: someone bids as if winning already carries bad news. The Strategist shades the bid below the estimate to correct for the selection. Closes when bids are set for the curse, winning less often and profiting when they do ... or shading is judged too timid, and the firm bids to win again.

## Stakes

Whether a bidder ever prices the fact of winning, or only ever the prize.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the highest guess, which the auction rewards, and away from the true value, which no bid can see. The production cannot make a field of honest estimates agree, so the top of the spread wins every time, and the top of the spread is where the error lives. It can only show that no one bid dishonestly and no one appraised carelessly, that the winner was simply the most hopeful, and that the single thing that would have lifted the curse, treating a win as evidence you bid too high, is the correction a room that came to win is least ready to make.
