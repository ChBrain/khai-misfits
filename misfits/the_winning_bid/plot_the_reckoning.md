# Plot: The Reckoning

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The ledger](place_the_ledger.md), then [the boardroom](place_the_boardroom.md).
Quarters on, the true value comes in below the price paid, and the writedown has to be explained.

## Action

[Realizing](process_realizing.md): [The Board](persona_the_board.md), holding [the principal's seat](position_principal.md), books the loss, while [The Underbidder](persona_the_underbidder.md) at [the underbidder's seat](position_underbidder.md) turns out to have been spared.

## Stage

[The Board](persona_the_board.md) faces [Approve the Deal](plan_approve_the_deal.md) coming due, and [the overpay](piece_the_overpay.md) is recorded against the prize; [The Underbidder](persona_the_underbidder.md), who worked [Walk Away](plan_walk_away.md), kept the capital the winner spent.
The loss that names [the curse](piece_the_curse.md) sits on the books, and the lost auction is revealed as the lucky one.

## Tension

The truest finding is the one the winner can least afford to sign.
To name the loss the winner's curse is to admit the celebrated win was an overpayment baked in the moment the gavel fell, so the board reaches instead for the market, the managers, the environment, anything but the bid. And the underbidder's defeat, which stung in the room, is quietly revealed as the field's protection, felt by no one as the win it was.
