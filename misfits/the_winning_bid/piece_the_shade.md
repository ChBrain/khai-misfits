# Piece: The Shade

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md). The reversal: bidding below the estimate to account for winning being bad news.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the model](place_the_model.md), where the bid is pulled back below the honest estimate on purpose.
The discount applied for the field's size, the correction that says a win would mean you were highest, the discipline of bidding what the prize is worth given that you won it, not given your rosy read of it.

## Load Bearing

Without it every bid tracks its estimate and the winner always overpays.
With it the bidder folds the logic of the auction into the number itself: if winning proves I was the highest guess, I should bid as if I already know that, which means below my estimate, by more the larger the field. The Shade is load bearing because it is the one move that prices the fact of winning, turning a win from a curse into a deal.

## Apparent

Timidity. Leaving money on the table.
It looks like flinching: bidding under your own honest number, losing prizes you could have won, letting bolder rivals take the day. That reading is why it is so hard to hold, because the shade's reward is invisible, the losses it avoids and the prizes it declines, while every forgone win is a visible defeat in a room that came to win.

## Yearbook

Computed by [the strategist](persona_the_strategist.md) through [shading](process_shading.md), against [the bid](piece_the_bid.md).
It is the remedy the play holds: the Shade pulls the offer below [the estimate](piece_the_estimate.md), breaks [the curse](piece_the_curse.md), and wins less often but profitably ... at the cost of the prizes a bolder bid would have taken.
