# Plot: The Auction

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The auction floor](place_the_auction_floor.md), where the private estimates become public offers.
The bids are made near the numbers each firm worked up, and the room rewards the largest.

## Action

[Bidding](process_bidding.md): [The Bidder](persona_the_bidder.md), holding [the bidder's seat](position_bidder.md), offers close to the estimate.
Then the sale is worked by [The Auctioneer](persona_the_auctioneer.md) at [the seller's seat](position_seller.md), drawing the field upward.

## Stage

[The Bidder](persona_the_bidder.md) works [Bid to Win](plan_bid_to_win.md) and [The Auctioneer](persona_the_auctioneer.md) works [Court the Top Bid](plan_court_the_top_bid.md): [the bid](piece_the_bid.md) is set near the estimate, and the wider [the spread](piece_the_spread.md) the seller draws, the higher its top.
Bidding your honest number feels like the only way to compete.

## Tension

Everyone bids reasonably, and the reasonable bid is the trap.
Offering near your estimate is conviction, admired in the room, and it is precisely the behaviour that wins the prize on the days your estimate was highest. The seller, meanwhile, gains most from exactly what hurts the winner: a bigger, keener field, whose higher maximum is the sale's success and the winner's overpayment in one.
