# Plot: The Shade

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The model](place_the_model.md), and someone who has stopped bidding the estimate and started bidding the win.
The question has changed: not what is the prize worth, but what is it worth given that winning it means I was the highest.

## Action

[Shading](process_shading.md): [The Strategist](persona_the_strategist.md), holding [the bid strategist's seat](position_bid_strategist.md), pulls the bid below the honest estimate, deeper the larger the field.

## Stage

[The Strategist](persona_the_strategist.md) tables [Shade the Bid](plan_shade_the_bid.md): fold the logic of the auction into the number, so [the shade](piece_the_shade.md) prices the fact of winning and breaks [the curse](piece_the_curse.md).
The firm bids less, wins less, and when it wins, wins a deal instead of a diagnosis.

## Tension

The discipline is right, and it looks like losing.
Shading lifts the curse by declining most auctions and profiting on the few it takes, but its reward is invisible, the overpayments never made, while every forgone win is a visible defeat in a room that came to win. So the plot turns on whether the firm can hold a correction whose payoff cannot be shown ... or judges the shade too timid, bids the estimate again, and re-enters the curse it just escaped.
