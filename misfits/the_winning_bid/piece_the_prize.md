# Piece: The Prize

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md). The item of common but unknown value: worth the same to everyone, known to none.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the data room](place_the_data_room.md), a thing everyone is appraising and no one can measure.
The offshore tract, the company for sale, the contract up for tender, the free agent: a prize whose true worth is a single number that exists and is hidden, the same for every bidder who studies it.

## Load Bearing

Without a common value there is no shared truth for estimates to scatter around, and no curse.
With it there is one right answer nobody knows, so every bid is a guess at the same figure and the highest guess can be ranked against a real value it exceeds. The Prize is load bearing because it is what makes the winner's optimism an error rather than a preference: on a thing worth the same to all, being highest is being wrong.

## Apparent

An opportunity worth competing for.
It looks like a good asset that will reward whoever is sharp enough to win it, so the contest reads as a test of skill and nerve. That framing is the opening, because it turns a guess at a hidden number into a race, and a race is won by whoever guesses highest.

## Yearbook

Appraised by [the geologist](persona_the_geologist.md) through [appraising](process_appraising.md), and pursued by every bidder.
It is the object the whole play turns on: the Prize is what [the estimate](piece_the_estimate.md) reaches for and what [the overpay](piece_the_overpay.md) is measured against, the true value the winning bid overshoots.
