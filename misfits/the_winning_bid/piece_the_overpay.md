# Piece: The Overpay

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md). The realized overpayment: the price above the value that winning locked in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the ledger](place_the_ledger.md), where the true value comes in below the price paid.
The writedown, the impairment, the dry well, the contract that costs more to serve than it earns: the gap between what the winner paid and what the prize was worth, arriving quarters after the celebration.

## Load Bearing

Without it the curse is a theory and the winning bid a triumph.
With it there is a measured loss that no one chose and everyone can trace: the Overpay is the winner's optimism, priced and paid, the difference between the highest estimate and the truth it exceeded. It is load bearing because it is the harm the play exists to show, the cost that was baked into the win the moment the gavel fell.

## Apparent

Bad luck. A deal that went wrong.
It looks like misfortune after the fact: the market turned, the asset underperformed, execution slipped, anything but a loss that was structural at the moment of winning. That reading is the last defence of the next bid, because blaming luck protects the strategy that will overpay again.

## Yearbook

Booked by [the board](persona_the_board.md) through [realizing](process_realizing.md), against [the prize](piece_the_prize.md).
It is the reckoning of the play: the Overpay is what [the selection](piece_the_selection.md) guaranteed, the loss that names [the curse](piece_the_curse.md), or is blamed on luck so the winning bid can be made again.
