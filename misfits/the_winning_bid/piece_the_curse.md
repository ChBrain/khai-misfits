# Piece: The Curse

## Taxonomy

[The Winning Bid](play_the_winning_bid.md). The phenomenon whole: winning as adverse selection, and the shading that lifts it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the boardroom](place_the_boardroom.md), where the pattern across many auctions can finally be seen as one.
Not a single bad deal but the shape of all of them: the documented fact that in a common-value auction the winner is the bidder who most overestimated, so winning is itself evidence of an error, and the prize arrives already overpriced.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of unlucky deals and one cautious analyst.
With it there is a named law the production stands on: to win a common-value auction is bad news about your own estimate, because the auction selects the maximum of the field and the maximum is biased high, so the rational bidder must shade below their honest number. The Curse is load bearing because it holds both the mechanism and its remedy, that winning must be priced, not just the prize.

## Apparent

A paradox, or a losers' excuse.
It looks either like a clever puzzle that surely cannot bind real, sophisticated bidders, or like something the people who lost say to feel better about losing. That dismissal is the last disguise, because the same selection that makes the toy jar-of-coins auction lose money is the one operating in every acquisition and lease sale, and sophistication does not exempt a bid from being the highest.

## Yearbook

Assembled from [the prize](piece_the_prize.md) through [the overpay](piece_the_overpay.md), and answered by [the shade](piece_the_shade.md).
It is the law the play runs on: the Curse is why [the selection](piece_the_selection.md) turns honest estimates into a loss, and why the only counter is to bid as if winning already told you that you bid too high.
