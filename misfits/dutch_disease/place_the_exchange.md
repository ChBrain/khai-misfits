# Place: The Exchange

## Taxonomy

[Dutch Disease](play_dutch_disease.md). Where prices and the currency are set, the market the windfall's spending drives into a real appreciation.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The market where the windfall meets the country's goods, the price of homes and meals and services climbing as the new money chases them.
The currency strengthening in real terms as domestic costs rise, and the world's price for traded goods sitting flat and foreign, unmoved by any of it.

## Holds

The appreciating, and the squeeze it sets.
Here the spending of the boom meets the goods that cannot be imported, so the Exchange holds the point where enjoyed wealth becomes a strong currency: the extra demand lifts non-traded prices and wages, which is a real appreciation, so it is where the windfall's spending turns into the cost base that will price the factories out.

## Offers

The full worth of the windfall, in what it can buy at home.
Because the Exchange is where the new money bids for non-traded goods, it offers the country the enjoyment of its fortune, the richer life the boom pays for, so it hands out the windfall's domestic value, and hands it out as the very rise in prices that strengthens the currency against the traded sector.

## Withheld

A world price that rises with the country's costs.
The Exchange cannot lift the foreign price the factories must take, so it withholds any relief from the squeeze, letting domestic costs climb while the world's price holds, and, converting spending into appreciation, it withholds from the traded sector the one thing that would save it, a currency that did not rise.
