# Process: Hollowing

## Taxonomy

[Dutch Disease](play_dutch_disease.md). The contraction of the traded sector as the strong currency and the boom's costs crush the price-taker.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A real exchange rate risen above the world's, and a traded sector that must take the world's price.
At [the works](place_the_works.md), the factory selling abroad at a price it cannot lift while its wages and rents climb with the boom.

## Direction

Toward a competitive sector shrunk and shut.
The factory cannot pass the boom's inflation on to a world price it does not set, and it loses its workers to the fields and its buyers to cheaper rivals, so hollowing drives the traded sector down, undercutting it abroad and outbidding it at home until it contracts and closes.

## Lever

The price-taking that the world market imposes.
Because manufacturing must sell at the world's price and cannot raise it to cover the country's risen costs, the squeeze needs no error and no mismanagement, only a competitive sector meeting a strong currency, so hollowing runs on the market working exactly as it should against a sector that did nothing wrong.

## Echo

A traded base shrunk, its learning gone with it.
The factories have closed and their workers dispersed and their export earnings ended, and the productivity growth and skill they carried are gone where a resource cannot rebuild them, so what reads as an uncompetitive sector failing is, in truth, a genuine bonanza dismantling the base the country will need when it fades.
