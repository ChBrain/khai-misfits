# Piece: Dutch Disease

## Taxonomy

[Dutch Disease](play_dutch_disease.md). The phenomenon whole, a real bonanza that hollows the economy that receives it.

## Owner

## Place

Everywhere the production runs: from [the field](place_the_field.md) through [the exchange](place_the_exchange.md) to [the works](place_the_works.md), and against [the fund](place_the_fund.md) where it is resisted.
It is the whole arc, the windfall drawn and spent, the currency risen, the traded base hollowed out.

## Load Bearing

Without it the other pieces are parts without a name: a windfall, a strong currency, a lost sector, an offshore fund, and no account of how one becomes the next.
With it the misfit is whole: Dutch disease is the trap entire, a genuine gain that, spent as any windfall reasonably is, appreciates the currency and dismantles the traded base, the fortune and the hollowing shown to be one thing. It is load bearing because it is the structure the whole production exists to stage (Corden & Neary): the frame that binds the windfall, the real appreciation, the deindustrialisation, and the sovereign fund into a single bend, so the piece carries the phenomenon as a whole, a country left poorer for having struck it rich.

## Apparent

A boom, a stroke of luck, an economy on the rise, everything a windfall should be.
Seen at the surface, Dutch disease looks like unmixed good fortune, incomes up and the currency strong and the shops full, and the truth that the strong currency is pricing the factories out, that the base being lost is the one that would have carried the future, that a real bonanza is quietly hollowing the economy, stays hidden behind how prosperous a booming, spending country appears.
