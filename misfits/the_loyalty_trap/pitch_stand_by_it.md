# Pitch: Stand By It

## Taxonomy

[The Loyalty Trap](play_the_loyalty_trap.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Steadfast, devoted, admirable.
The plain virtue of standing by the firm or the party or the country you love when it slips, not bolting at the first fault and not making a scene, giving it the benefit of the doubt.
Nothing false in it: only loyalty, the good instinct to keep faith through a bad patch.

## Undertow

Under the devotion, that same loyalty stilling the only two signals a body has.
Because a declining organisation learns it is failing only from the members who leave and the members who complain, and loyalty holds both, the faithful neither walk nor speak, and the leadership, seeing a stable and quiet membership, reads it as content and sails on.
The devotion that should have been the body's strength is what muffles the alarm that could have saved it.

## Nerve

The feeling of keeping faith, staying and holding your tongue while the thing you love goes wrong,
sure loyalty is the right response, because in itself it is,
while your staying reads as satisfaction and your silence as consent, and the decline you would never abandon runs on unheard.

## Echo

After the run: a body declining in a silence its most loyal members are keeping for its sake,
no one treacherous and no one tyrannical, only the faithful staying and holding their tongues and slipping away at last without a word,
and a loyal opposition that would have made the devotion speak, refused because voicing the fault felt like disloyalty, and wounding what you love felt worse than watching it fail.
