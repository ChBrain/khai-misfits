# Piece: The Loyal Opposition

## Taxonomy

[The Loyalty Trap](play_the_loyalty_trap.md). The remedy, the voice and the credible exit-threat that restore the signal, at the cost of seeming disloyal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Raised on [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where the swallowed complaint is at last spoken.
Backed at [the door](place_the_door.md), by a readiness to leave real enough to give the voice weight.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play has no answer, and the decline runs to its end in a silence no devotion breaks.
With it the misfit has its costly cure: loyalty made to mean the loyal opposition rather than the loyal silence, a voice raised and an exit-threat held credibly, restoring both channels the faith had stilled. The Loyal Opposition is load bearing because it is the remedy that carries its own price (Hirschman): it works only by the faithful risking the look of disloyalty and spending the standing devotion earned, so the piece carries both the cure for the deafness and the cost of speaking against the grain of one's own faith.

## Apparent

Disloyalty, a member breaking ranks, the one voice that would not keep the faith the rest are keeping.
Seen from inside the silence, the loyal opposition looks like betrayal, the malcontent the faithful are right to distrust, and the truth that it is devotion at its most useful, that naming the fault is the loving act and nursing it the harmful one, that only a threatened exit gives the voice its weight, stays hidden behind how much like treachery breaking the loyal silence appears.

## Yearbook

Raised by [the dissenter](persona_the_dissenter.md) through [speaking](process_speaking.md), the voice and exit-threat that restore what [the faith](piece_the_faith.md) stilled.
It is the cure the play cannot make painless: the Loyal Opposition gives back [the voice](piece_the_voice.md) and a credible [exit](piece_the_exit.md) so [the decline](piece_the_decline.md) is at last heard, and cannot do so without the dissenter spending its standing and being taken, for a while, as the disloyal one.
