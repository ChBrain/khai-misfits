# Plot: The Screen

## Taxonomy

[The Proxy](play_the_proxy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Desk](place_the_desk.md), one post and a hundred applicants the screener cannot read.
A quality that cannot be cheaply seen, and a group average that can, the only cheap information there is.

## Action

[Screening](process_screening.md): the agent prices each applicant by the average of their group and discounts the return on a low-rated group's signal.

## Stage

[The Employer](persona_the_employer.md), on [Screen by the Group](plan_screen_by_the_group.md), reads [the prior](piece_the_prior.md) where it cannot read the individual and sets [the discount](piece_the_discount.md) on [the signal](piece_the_signal.md), the able and the weak of a low-rated group offered the same low rating.
It is the same actuarial move an insurer makes with a pool, and there is no distaste in it.

## Tension

Pricing the unknown by the known is the rational move, and it is the move that sets the trap.
There is nothing hateful: unable to see the individual, the agent uses the best cheap signal it has, and the group average is real. But the rating it sets discounts a group's return on the signal, so the plot closes with the individual priced by their group and the discount fixed, a proxy that reads as neutral statistics and has just cut the return whose withholding will prove it right.
