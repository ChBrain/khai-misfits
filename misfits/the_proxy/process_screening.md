# Process: Screening

## Taxonomy

[The Proxy](play_the_proxy.md). The agent pricing the unknown individual by the known group average, and discounting the return on a low-rated group's signal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Employer, with a post to fill and no cheap read on the individual.
At [the gate](place_the_gate.md), the applicant unreadable in the time there is and the group average readable, the screener pricing by the group because it is the best cheap signal it has.

## Direction

Toward the individual priced by the group and the discount set.
Because quality cannot be seen and the group average can, screening points the agent to price each applicant by their group, so the rating each is offered is the group's rating, and the return on any signal a low-rated applicant holds is discounted, the wedge set that the group's response will widen.

## Lever

The rationality of pricing the unknown by the known.
Because the group average is real information and the individual's quality is not cheaply available, screening runs on sound actuarial sense, the same the insurer uses on a pool, so the process turns on no distaste, only on an agent using the only cheap information there is exactly as reason recommends.

## Echo

An individual priced by their group, and a return discounted.
The agent has set each rating by the group and marked down the low-rated group's signal, so what leaves this process is a person priced by the company they keep and a discount on their effort, the first move of the loop, sound on the numbers and about to make the numbers.
