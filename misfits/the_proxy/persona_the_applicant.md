# Persona: The Applicant

## Taxonomy

[The Queue](position_the_queue.md). The member of the low-rated group, offered a discounted return on the signal, who rationally invests less and so lowers the average.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Rational. Weighing. Answering the return.
Is the member of a group the market rates low, deciding whether the costly signal of quality is worth acquiring.
No failing in it: only a person weighing an investment by the return it actually pays them, as anyone would.

## Action

Reckons the payoff on the signal, finds it discounted because their group is rated low, and invests to that lower return.
Acquires less of the credential, the training, the skill, because the market gives them less for it than it gives a member of a group rated high.
Does what a rational investor does: puts less into a thing that pays them less, answering the return the market sets, not the return it might have set.

## Shadow

Not a shirker, a rational investor, and that is the whole difficulty: investing less in a discounted signal is the sound response, and the sound response is what lowers the group's average to meet the rating.
Because the return on the signal is discounted for the low-rated group, the Applicant rightly invests less, and the group's real average quality falls, so the sound answer to a lowered return is the very move that confirms the rating that lowered it, each member's prudent choice summing to the average the Employer assumed.
It answers the return it is offered, and the answer makes the rating true.

## Tell

Says "why would I pay full price for a certificate the market pays me half for."
Is right that the market pays them half, and cannot see that the group answering that way is what keeps the market paying half, a rating confirmed by the response it provokes.
