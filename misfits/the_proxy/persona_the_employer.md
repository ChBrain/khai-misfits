# Persona: The Employer

## Taxonomy

[The Gate](position_the_gate.md). Prices the unknown individual by the known group average, the cheapest reliable signal it has, and holds the rating that discounts a group's return.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Rational. Unprejudiced. Actuarial.
Is the screener at the gate who cannot see each applicant's true quality and prices them by what it can see, the average of the group they come from.
No distaste in it: only an agent using the best cheap information it has under uncertainty, as an insurer prices a premium by the pool.

## Action

Reads the group average where it cannot read the individual, and sets the rating each applicant is offered by the group they belong to.
Discounts the return on any signal a low-rated applicant holds, because even with it they are still, in part, priced by their group.
Does what a rational screener does: prices the unknown by the known, and would judge the person on their merits if only their merits could be seen.

## Shadow

Not a bigot, an actuary, and that is the whole difficulty: pricing the individual by the group average is the rational move under uncertainty, and making it discounts a group's return until the group becomes what it was rated.
Because the Employer prices by the best cheap signal and that signal is the group, the low rating it sets, sound on the numbers before it, lowers the return the low-rated group gets on the signal, so the group invests less and its average falls to meet the rating, and the Employer's prior is confirmed by the very effort its rating discouraged, the belief manufacturing its own proof.
It prices by the numbers, and the pricing makes the numbers.

## Tell

Says "I am not prejudiced: I am playing the base rate, and the base rate is real."
Is right that the base rate is real, and cannot see that its own rating is what keeps the base rate low, a prior confirmed by the investment it discounts.
