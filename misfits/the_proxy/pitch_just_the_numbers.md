# Pitch: Just the Numbers

## Taxonomy

[The Proxy](play_the_proxy.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Reasonable, actuarial, unprejudiced.
The plain sense that pricing an unknown by its group average is not prejudice but statistics: an insurer sets a premium by the risk pool, an employer screens by the base rate, and using the best cheap information you have is simply rational under uncertainty.
Nothing hateful in it: the agent bears no distaste, forgoes no profit, and would judge the individual on their merits if only it could see them.

## Undertow

Under the actuarial calm, a rating that makes itself true.
Because the low rating discounts the return on a group's investment in the signal, the group rationally invests less, and its real average falls to meet the rating, the assumed average becoming the produced average and the prior confirmed by the very effort it discouraged.
The proxy that reads as neutral statistics is a self-confirming prior that manufactures the evidence that proves it, and prices the able member by a group they cannot signal free of.

## Nerve

The feeling of a judgement so rational it cannot be prejudice, a base rate applied without malice,
because no one at the gate feels a distaste and no one forgoes a profit, only prices the unknown by the known,
while underneath the rating lowers a group's investment until the group becomes what it was rated, and the good ones of it are doubted for the company they are counted in.

## Echo

After the run: a group priced low and, in time, made low, its average bent to meet the rating by the effort the rating discouraged,
no bigot in the story, only sound actuarial screening and a sound response to a discounted return,
and the one move that would break it, a mandate that lowers the bar the group is held to, able to feed the loop it meant to end unless it bites hard enough to make the signal pay, so the shortfall deepens where the help was meant to land, and the proxy holds without anyone ever meaning harm.
