# Persona: The Threshold

## Taxonomy

[Threshold](position_threshold.md). The critical density as a force and a line, the mark below which per-capita growth turns negative, invisible from above and irreversible from below, the attrition that carries the numbers down toward it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

A line, unmarked. A force, downward.
Is the critical density itself, the point in the numbers where growth crosses zero, and the standing pressure of attrition, mortality, harvest, hard seasons, that lowers the density toward and past that point.
No malice in it: the Threshold is a property of the population's own fitness curve and the ordinary losses any population takes, neither of which any member authored.

## Action

Lowers the numbers, and holds the line beneath them: attrition thins the ranks toward the critical density, and at the density the per-capita growth rate crosses from positive to negative.
Gives no sign of itself from above, and no way back from below, the same numbers that read as plenty above the line unable to sustain the population beneath it.
Does what a threshold does: it marks where the benefit can no longer carry the growth, and where it is passed the population falls.

## Shadow

Not a cliff anyone can see, a line without a marker and a pressure without a face, and that is the whole difficulty: the density where growth turns negative is real and fixed, and nothing in the headcount shows where it lies.
Because the Threshold is invisible from above, a population reads survivable at a number just over the line as at a number far above it, so the attrition that lowers the density crosses the line unremarked, and below it the population is not slowed but reversed, drawn toward extinction by a growth rate gone negative, the line that gave no warning giving no way back.
It is a line no count reveals, and being unrevealed is exactly what lets the numbers cross it before anyone acts.

## Tell

Is, in effect, "the number below which the same numbers no longer hold."
Is it silently, marked by nothing the headcount shows, so the population passes it reading survivable and learns of the line only from the far side, where growth has already turned.
