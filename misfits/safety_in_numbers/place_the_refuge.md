# Place: The Refuge

## Taxonomy

[Safety in Numbers](play_safety_in_numbers.md). Where the numbers are held above the line, the protected range, the reserve, the supplemented throng, the one arrangement in which the density is kept over the critical number before the attrition can carry it across.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the density is held above the line before it can fall to it, a reserve, a protected patch, a supplemented population, where the harvest is restrained and the numbers kept well over the threshold.
The Keeper holding the density up, the throng kept dense enough to confer its benefit, and the population kept off the edge where the growth would turn.

## Holds

The holding, and the safety it keeps.
Here the numbers are kept above the critical line before the thinning can reach it, so the Refuge holds the point where the safety is preserved, not restored: the density held over the threshold and the benefit held with it, so it is where the population is kept off the spiral, the one arrangement in which the safety in numbers is guarded against the fewness that would end it.

## Offers

A way to keep the density above the line, and the safety kept rather than lost.
Because the Refuge holds the numbers over the threshold before the crossing, it offers the one thing the Range could not once thinned, the safety preserved while it can still be preserved, so it hands out a population kept off the spiral, and hands it out at the price of spending on numbers that read ample and restraining a harvest that reads sustainable.

## Withheld

Any ease, or any proof that the holding was needed.
The Refuge can keep the density above the line but cannot make the spending look justified or make its worth show, because the spiral it prevents leaves no trace and the cost of holding is felt at once, so it withholds the standing of one who spends only when the count demands it, exacting the look of guarding against nothing, and, all preserved safety and no visible payoff, it withholds the thanks for a spiral that now never runs, the averted extinction seeming, after, like one that was never going to happen.
