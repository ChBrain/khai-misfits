# Safety in Numbers: Reference

The research warrant for [Safety in Numbers](play_safety_in_numbers.md).
The dynamic is positive density dependence and its reversal below a critical line: Allee's animal aggregations,
Courchamp, Berec and Gascoigne's mechanisms of the Allee effect, Stephens, Sutherland and Freckleton's
definition of the critical density, Dennis on the critical population size, and Gilpin and Soulé's extinction
vortex, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging safety in numbers as a **density-dependence structure**: individuals do better in larger groups, so
per-capita fitness rises with the density around them, and the safety a crowd confers is really the numbers' and
not any member's, so that a population thinned below a critical density does not merely grow more slowly but crosses
into negative per-capita growth, where each loss lowers the density, lower density lowers the benefit, and lower
benefit lowers the growth again, a self-reinforcing spiral that draws the population down faster the fewer of it
remain. The play models the documented mechanics, the positive relation of fitness to density through mate-finding,
cooperative defence and cooperative foraging, the per-capita benefit that rises with the numbers and falls with
them, the critical threshold below which growth turns negative that marks a strong Allee effect, the extinction
vortex in which the density dependence runs in reverse, and the deceptive headcount that reads survivable right up
to the crossing, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of any member's error. The governing
finding of the sources is the play's governing law: the trap is the density dependence itself, so that no
individual changes its behaviour or errs, each does exactly what a crowd rewards, and the safety that numbers
confer is genuine, which is precisely why losing the numbers loses the safety and the loss accelerates.

It is distinct from the house's commons dynamics, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: in a
commons a shared stock is drawn down by the users who draw on it, each taking a rational share and the sum
exhausting the resource, so the loss is an over-use of a common pool; here there is no shared stock and no
over-use at all, the numbers falling by attrition no member authored, and the loss is that cooperation's own
per-capita benefit vanishes as the numbers fall, a safety made of density and not a stock drawn down. It is
distinct, too, from the house's tipping and collapse dynamics keyed on an external stock or shock, a bank run
turning on a rush to withdraw against a common reserve, a limits dynamic turning on a resource ceiling or an
outside pressure: there the driver is external, a stock depleted or a shock struck, while here the driver is
internal, the per-capita fitness of the population falling with its own thinning, the same density dependence that
lifted the crowd drawing it down, so the collapse is authored by no run and no shock but by the population's own
numbers crossing the line where its fitness turns against it.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any member as
  reckless or any force as malign, because the source finding is precisely that no one errs: each member does
  exactly what a crowd rewards, finding its mate, diluting the predator, mobbing the intruder and foraging in
  company, and the attrition that thins the numbers is ordinary loss no member authored. The collapse is authored
  by no one and emerges from the density dependence itself, so the misfit is that the safety a crowd confers is
  genuinely the numbers', which is exactly why losing the numbers loses the safety and the loss accelerates below
  the line.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that keeps the
  safety is to hold the density above the critical line, protecting the range, restraining the harvest, and
  supplementing the throng before it thins to the threshold, and it means spending on a population that reads
  ample, restraining a harvest that reads sustainable, and guarding against a line no count reveals. The reckoning
  is a safety kept only by paying, continuously, the cost of holding a healthy-looking population above a threshold
  that reads survivable until it is past, not a free rescue.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; positive density dependence and the harm of under-crowding are Allee's,
  the mechanisms of mate-finding and cooperative defence and foraging are Courchamp, Berec and Gascoigne's, the
  definition of the Allee effect and its critical density are Stephens, Sutherland and Freckleton's, the critical
  population size and its extinction risk are Dennis's, and the extinction vortex is Gilpin and Soulé's. The honest
  boundary, that a weak Allee effect only slows growth without a threshold so that the vortex requires a strong one,
  and that the effect is bounded to species whose fitness depends on density, is carried as carefully as the
  finding itself.
