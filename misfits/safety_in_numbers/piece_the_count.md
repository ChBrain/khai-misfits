# Piece: The Count

## Taxonomy

[Safety in Numbers](play_safety_in_numbers.md). The apparent headcount, the figure that reads survivable right up to the crossing, the cue that gives no sign of the line beneath it and no warning that the same numbers cannot hold below it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Taken across [the range](place_the_range.md) and [the edge](place_the_edge.md), where the figure reads survivable at any density above the line.
Distrusted only at [the refuge](place_the_refuge.md), where the keeper treats an ample count as suspect.

## Load Bearing

Without it the danger would show in the numbers and the crossing could be seen coming, and someone would act before the line.
With it the trap is deceptive: the headcount that reads as a viable population and carries no mark of the threshold, so a number that was plenty yesterday reads plenty today and nothing in it warns that the line is near (the deceptive cue of a headcount that stays reassuring while the density approaches the critical number). The Count is load bearing because it is what hides the line: the figure that looks safe at the very density where safety fails, so the piece carries the reassuring number that reads survivable to the last figure before the turn, the cue that misleads not by error but by carrying no sign of the threshold at all.

## Apparent

A reliable measure, a healthy figure that means a healthy population.
Seen at the surface, the Count looks like exactly the signal it should be, a number that tracks the population's health, and the truth that it reads the same just above the line as far above it, that it gives no distance to the threshold, that a survivable count is not a safe count, stays hidden behind how much a healthy figure simply feels like proof of safety, right up until the crossing.
