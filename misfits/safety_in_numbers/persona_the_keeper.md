# Persona: The Keeper

## Taxonomy

[Keeper](position_keeper.md). The one who would hold the numbers above the line, protecting the range, restraining the attrition, and supplementing the throng before it thins to the critical density, at the cost of spending on a population that reads ample.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Watchful. Willing to spend early. Suspicious of a healthy count.
Is the conservator, the steward, the manager who guards the numbers, the one who keeps the density over the line where a headcount alone would not.
No alarmism in it: the Keeper does not doubt that the count reads survivable, only doubts that a survivable count is a safe distance from the line.

## Action

Protects the range, restrains the harvest, and supplements the throng, holding the density above the critical number before the attrition can carry it across.
Treats an ample headcount as suspect precisely because the threshold shows only after it is passed, and spends on the numbers while they still look fine.
Does what keeps the population off the spiral: it holds the density above the line, and holding it is the one thing that keeps the safety the numbers confer.

## Shadow

Not a free rescue, a costly and unthanked spending, and that is the whole difficulty: the holding that keeps the population above the line is exactly the outlay a healthy count makes hard to justify.
Because the numbers read ample right up to the line, the Keeper must spend on a population that looks fine, restrain a harvest that reads sustainable, and guard against a threshold no count reveals, so the density is held only against every signal that it need not be, the spiral it prevents leaving no trace and the cost of holding plain for all to see, the extinction averted looking, afterward, like one that was never going to happen.
It holds the numbers to keep the safety, and holding them is read as spending on a population that was doing fine.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "keep the numbers well above the line, before the count gives you reason to."
Says it over a population that reads survivable, and is heard, often, as spending too early, guarding against nothing, the one who will not trust a healthy count.
