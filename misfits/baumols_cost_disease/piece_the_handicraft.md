# Piece: The Handicraft

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md). The stagnant service: work whose hour is its product and cannot be sped up without loss.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the stage](place_the_stage.md), in the hour given whole.
Where the doing and the time are the same thing.

## Load Bearing

Without it there would be no service whose cost must climb, only goods that keep getting cheaper.
With it there is work where the labour is the product, so output per hour cannot rise without the service falling: a quartet cannot play faster, a nurse cannot halve the bedside hour, a teacher cannot double the class and teach the same. The Handicraft is load bearing because it is the stagnant-productivity sector at the root of the model (Baumol & Bowen), the work that holds its output per hour flat by its very nature, and the thing whose steady hour, priced against a faster world, becomes a runaway cost.

## Apparent

Just a service that ought to get more efficient like everything else.
Because most things do get faster, a service that does not looks like one that simply has not been modernised yet, so the handicraft reads as a laggard awaiting its productivity revolution, when its hour is the product and cannot be compressed at all.

## Yearbook

Given by [the server](persona_the_server.md) through [performing](process_performing.md), the fixed hour the play rests on.
It is the work that cannot be hurried: the Handicraft holds its output flat while [the common wage](piece_the_common_wage.md) lifts its pay, and that is where the rising cost begins.
