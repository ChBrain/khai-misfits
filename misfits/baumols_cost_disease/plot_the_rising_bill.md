# Plot: The Rising Bill

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The ledger](place_the_ledger.md), the price of the service climbing while the price of goods falls.
The bill grows year on year, and nothing in the service got worse to explain it.

## Action

[Pricing](process_pricing.md): [The Payer](persona_the_payer.md), holding [the payer's seat](position_payer.md), reads the widening gap as proof of waste, while [The Economist](persona_the_economist.md), holding [the economist's seat](position_economist.md), reads the same figure as plain arithmetic.

## Stage

[The Payer](persona_the_payer.md) tables [Blame the Bloat](plan_blame_the_bloat.md): set the falling goods against the rising service and demand a culprit, so [the rising cost](piece_the_rising_cost.md) is taken for [the misdiagnosis](piece_the_misdiagnosis.md).
[The Economist](persona_the_economist.md) tables [Name the Arithmetic](plan_name_the_arithmetic.md), and finds the true account, offering no one to blame, harder to hear than the hunt for a fault.

## Tension

The number is honest and reads exactly like a scandal.
The payer is right that costs rising against a falling tide usually mean mismanagement, and here they do not, so its reasonable instinct points at a culprit who does not exist. That is the misfit's hinge: a benign arithmetic wearing the face of bloat, an honest cost inviting the hunt for fault, and the true explanation losing to the false one for the simple reason that arithmetic makes no villain.
