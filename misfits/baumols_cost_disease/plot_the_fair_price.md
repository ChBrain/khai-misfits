# Plot: The Fair Price

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The budget](place_the_budget.md), where someone names the disease for what it is and moves to pay it.
The rising cost is set on the table not as a fault to cut but as a price to meet.

## Action

[Pricing](process_pricing.md): [The Economist](persona_the_economist.md), holding [the economist's seat](position_economist.md), shows the cost is the price of prosperity and not a fault, while [The Funder](persona_the_funder.md), holding [the funder's seat](position_funder.md), pays it out of the general growth that can afford it.

## Stage

[The Funder](persona_the_funder.md) tables [Fund It Anyway](plan_fund_it_anyway.md): pay the climbing price from the prosperity that raised it, so [baumol's cost disease](piece_baumols_cost_disease.md) is answered by meeting [the rising cost](piece_the_rising_cost.md) rather than cutting the care.
[The Economist](persona_the_economist.md) tables [Name the Arithmetic](plan_name_the_arithmetic.md), and adds the honest caveat that real waste still hides among the costs and must be found.

## Tension

The right answer is to spend more, forever, and it looks every year like losing.
The funder is right that the cost is affordable and that cutting it means cutting the care, but its remedy is an ever-larger share for things that never get cheaper, which reads as profligacy beside the cutter's tidy savings, and it must still tell the genuine bloat from the arithmetic or be accused of shielding both. The service is kept whole at its true price ... or the reflex to cut wins, and the care is thinned to save a cost that was never waste.
