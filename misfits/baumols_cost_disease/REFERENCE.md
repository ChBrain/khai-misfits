# Baumol's Cost Disease: Reference

The research warrant for [Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md). The title is Baumol
and Bowen's; the play runs on the macroeconomics of unbalanced growth, the arts and health cost
studies that follow, and the reframe that the disease is the price of prosperity, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging Baumol's cost disease as a **systemic system**: a service whose labour is its product, so its
output per hour cannot rise without the service becoming less, grows ever costlier because it shares a
labour market with sectors whose productivity does rise, so its wages must track theirs while its
output holds flat, and its cost per unit climbs without limit though nothing in it got worse. The play
models the documented mechanics, the stagnant handicraft, the progressive productive sector, the
common wage that links them, the relative cost that rises mechanically, the misdiagnosis that reads
the rise as waste, the false cure that raises output per hour by cutting the labour that was the
service, and the funding that alone keeps the service whole at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of
the structure, not as a tale of mismanagement. The governing finding of the source literature is the
play's governing law: where productivity growth is uneven and labour is mobile, the cost of the
stagnant-productivity services rises relentlessly relative to goods, not because they are inefficient
but because everything else got more efficient, and this rising cost is affordable out of the very
growth that causes it. The false cure is the house's Fixes that Fail worn as efficiency: forcing on a
service a productivity its work cannot bear relieves the cost by degrading the care.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  hospital as bloated or the nurse as overpaid, because the source finding is precisely that no waste
  is needed: the factory's progress is real, the nurse's wage is set fairly by a labour market she
  shares with it, and the rising cost is arithmetic, the price of a stagnant service measured against
  goods that keep getting cheaper. The misfit is that an honest rising cost looks exactly like
  inefficiency, and invites a cure that only degrades the service.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a clever efficiency that saves the cost and
  keeps the service. Where the labour is the product, more output per hour is a thinner service, so the
  real answer is to pay the rising price out of general prosperity, which asks an ever-larger share for
  care and teaching and art against a reflex to cut, and requires telling the genuine waste, which
  exists, apart from the honest costs it hides among. The reckoning is a price accepted, not a saving
  found.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the unbalanced-growth model, the arts and
  health studies, and the affordability reframe are theirs, and the honesty that not every rising cost
  is pure cost disease, that real bloat exists and must be distinguished, is carried as carefully as
  the finding itself.

- **[The Misdiagnosis](piece_the_misdiagnosis.md)**: carries the reading of arithmetic as waste (Baumol), the
  honest cost mistaken for bloat and a culprit sought.

  raised by cutting the labour that was the service.
- **[Baumol's Cost Disease](piece_baumols_cost_disease.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Baumol), the
  unfaster things dearer forever, and its remedy in paying the price out of the prosperity that causes it.
