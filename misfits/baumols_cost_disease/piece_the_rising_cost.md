# Piece: The Rising Cost

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md). The mechanical result: the service growing ever costlier relative to goods, though nothing in it got worse.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the ledger](place_the_ledger.md), the service line climbing as the goods line falls.
Where the price of care rises year on year.

## Load Bearing

Without it the disease would be invisible and there would be nothing to misread or cut.
With it the pay rises and the output does not, so cost per unit climbs without limit, and set against goods that keep getting cheaper the service looks like it is running away. The Rising Cost is load bearing because it is the relative-price rise the model predicts and the evidence confirms (Baumol; Nordhaus): a service dearer every year not because it changed but because everything around it got cheaper, the plain arithmetic that the whole misfit is fought over.

## Apparent

A cost out of control.
A price that only ever climbs looks like a system failing to contain itself, so the rising cost reads as crisis, when it is the orderly output of a fixed hour priced in a rising-wage economy, climbing exactly as the arithmetic says it must.

## Yearbook

Produced through [pricing](process_pricing.md), grown from [the common wage](piece_the_common_wage.md) over a flat output.
It is the figure everyone fights about: the Rising Cost is what [the misdiagnosis](piece_the_misdiagnosis.md) reads as bloat and what the funder must decide to pay.
