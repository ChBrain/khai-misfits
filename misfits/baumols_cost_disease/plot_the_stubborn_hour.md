# Plot: The Stubborn Hour

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The stage](place_the_stage.md), a quartet, a ward, a classroom, where the hour is the product and cannot be sped up.
The service given as it always was, at the only pace that keeps it whole.

## Action

[Performing](process_performing.md): [The Server](persona_the_server.md), holding [the server's seat](position_server.md), gives the same hour it always gave, while the rate set by [The Machinist](persona_the_machinist.md), holding [the machinist's seat](position_machinist.md), rises around it.

## Stage

[The Server](persona_the_server.md) tables [Give the Hour](plan_give_the_hour.md): keep the service whole at the pace it needs, so [the handicraft](piece_the_handicraft.md) holds its output per hour flat.
Yet [the common wage](piece_the_common_wage.md) means the server, able to earn the factory's rate, must be paid near it to stay, so its pay climbs while its output does not.

## Tension

The service is exactly as good as it ever was, and that is precisely the problem.
Nothing here got slower or worse: the hour is kept whole, the care undiminished, the craft intact. That is the trap loading: a fixed hour paid a rising wage, its cost per unit climbing for no fault of its own, faithful to the service and, for that fidelity, growing dearer against a world that learned to hurry everything else.
