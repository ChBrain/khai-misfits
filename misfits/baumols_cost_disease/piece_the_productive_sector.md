# Piece: The Productive Sector

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md). The progressive sector: where output per hour rises and goods grow cheaper.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the factory](place_the_factory.md), in the line that makes more each year.
Where progress turns into cheaper goods and higher pay.

## Load Bearing

Without it wages would not rise and the stagnant service would stay affordable.
With it there is a sector whose output per hour genuinely climbs, so its goods fall in price and its wages rise on real gains, and because those wages are set in a labour market the service shares, they become the rate the service must pay too. The Productive Sector is load bearing because it is the progressive half of the unbalanced-growth model (Baumol), the engine of general prosperity, and the innocent source of the rising wage that prices the stagnant service out.

## Apparent

Simply the economy getting better, with no bearing on the service at all.
Cheaper goods and higher pay look like unmixed good news, unconnected to the cost of care or teaching, so the productive sector reads as pure benefit, and its role in raising the service's cost stays hidden inside the very progress everyone celebrates.

It is the blameless engine: the Productive Sector sets, through [the common wage](piece_the_common_wage.md), the rate that makes [the handicraft](piece_the_handicraft.md) dear.
