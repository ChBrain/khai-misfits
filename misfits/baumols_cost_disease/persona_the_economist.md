# Persona: The Economist

## Taxonomy

[Economist](position_economist.md). The one who names the disease as arithmetic, not fault.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Cool, explanatory, two sectors and one wage in view.
Sees the progressive sector and the stagnant one sharing a labour market, and the cost that must follow.
Holds the unwelcome finding that the rising bill is not waste but the price of prosperity itself.

## Action

Names the mechanism, and refuses the culprit.
Shows the payer that goods fell because of real progress and the service rose because it could not share it, so the gap is arithmetic, not bloat.
Shows the cutter that more output per hour, here, is not a saving but a thinner service, and that the honest answer is to pay the price.

## Shadow

Being right buys nothing, because arithmetic makes a poor villain and a poorer headline.
Its diagnosis is correct and undramatic, set against a rising cost that feels like a scandal and a reflex that wants a fault to cut, so the true account persuades no one who needs a culprit, and it must add, honestly, that some real waste does hide among the honest costs and must still be found.
The clearest reader of the disease is the least able to make anyone accept its cure.

## Tell

"It's not bloat. It's the price of everything else getting cheaper."
The true diagnosis that offers no one to blame and nothing to cut.
