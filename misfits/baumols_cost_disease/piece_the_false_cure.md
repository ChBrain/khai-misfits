# Piece: The False Cure

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md). The degrading remedy: output per hour raised by cutting the labour that was the service.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the budget](place_the_budget.md), in the saving booked as the care thins.
Where the factory's cure is spent on the ward.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misdiagnosis would be a harmless error and the service would survive being misjudged.
With it the misreading acts: the one cure for a rising cost that worked on goods, more output per hour, is applied to work where the hour was the product, so raising units per worker does not cheapen the same service but thins it, and the cost falls only because the care does. The False Cure is load bearing because it is the house's Fixes that Fail worn as efficiency (Baumol): a remedy that genuinely lowers the cost while destroying the thing the cost was for, booked as a saving while the service quietly shrinks.

## Apparent

A hard-won efficiency.
Fewer labour-hours per patient or pupil looks like exactly the productivity gain the factory won, so the false cure reads as a success, and the fact that it bought the saving by cutting the service, not by doing the same for less, hides inside the tidy improvement of the numbers.

## Yearbook

It is the cure that hollows: the False Cure answers a cost that was never waste by removing the hour that was [the handicraft](piece_the_handicraft.md) itself.
