# Plot: The Squeeze

## Taxonomy

[Baumol's Cost Disease](play_baumols_cost_disease.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The budget](place_the_budget.md), the rising cost read as bloat and marked for cutting.
Someone brings the factory's cure to the ward: get more output per hour.

## Action

[Cutting](process_cutting.md): [The Cutter](persona_the_cutter.md), holding [the cutter's seat](position_cutter.md), raises the caseload, swells the class, speeds the shift, while [The Server](persona_the_server.md), holding [the server's seat](position_server.md), finds the hour it must give thinned to fit.

## Stage

[The Cutter](persona_the_cutter.md) tables [Trim the Service](plan_trim_the_service.md): more patients per nurse, more pupils per teacher, and book the saving, so [the false cure](piece_the_false_cure.md) answers [the misdiagnosis](piece_the_misdiagnosis.md) by removing labour.
But the labour was the service, so the cost falls only because the care does, the numbers improving as the thing behind them shrinks.

## Tension

The cure works on the ledger and hollows the thing it treats.
It is true that fewer labour-hours cost less, and here that is exactly the harm, because the hour was the care, so raising output per hour buys not the same service cheaper but a thinner one at the old price. The saving is real and booked, the degradation real and unbooked, and the factory's honest remedy, brought to work it does not fit, cuts the cost by quietly cutting the care.
