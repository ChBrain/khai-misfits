# Piece: The Delay

## Taxonomy

[Shifting the Burden](play_shifting_the_burden.md). The timing asymmetry: the lag that makes the fundamental fix lose every race to the quick one.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Between the reach and the reward, in the long gap the cure asks the system to wait.
The stretch of time where the fundamental fix costs and has not yet paid.

## Load Bearing

Without it the two fixes would compete on merit and the better one might win.
With it they never compete fairly: the quick fix pays now and the fundamental one pays much later, so against any live symptom and any real clock the fast answer wins by default, not by being better. The Delay is load bearing because it is the property that makes the choice a foregone conclusion (Forrester; Sterman), the reason a system full of sensible actors picks the worse long-run answer every single time: the good option is simply too slow to be felt in the window where the decision is made.

## Apparent

Just how long things take.
It looks like a neutral fact of the world, not a force: cures are slow, reliefs are fast, everyone knows that. But that plain fact is doing all the work, because it is what strips the fundamental fix of any chance the moment a clock is running.

## Yearbook

Weighed against [the fundamental fix](piece_the_fundamental_fix.md), and never against [the quick fix](piece_the_quick_fix.md).
It is the thumb on the scale: the Delay is why [the pressed](persona_the_pressed.md) reaches for relief every time, not from weakness but from a race the cure was never able to win.
