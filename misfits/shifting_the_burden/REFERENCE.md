# Shifting the Burden: Reference

The research warrant for [Shifting the Burden](play_shifting_the_burden.md). The title is
Senge's systems archetype; the play runs on the system-dynamics literature on symptomatic
versus fundamental solutions and the capability traps that follow, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the shifting-the-burden archetype as a **systemic system**: a symptom met by a fast
symptomatic solution that relieves it and, by relieving it, lets the slow fundamental capacity
waste, so reliance on the fix grows until the cause can no longer be reached. The play models the
documented mechanics, the symptom that demands relief now, the quick fix that acts fast and
addresses nothing, the fundamental fix that acts slowly on the cause, the delay that makes the
quick fix win, the side effect by which using the fix atrophies the fundamental capacity, the
dependence that deepens each cycle, and the weaning that alone shifts the burden back at a cost, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of laziness. The governing finding of the
source literature is the play's governing law: a symptomatic solution that eases the pressure for a
fundamental one erodes the capability to apply it, so each use of the fix makes the next use more
necessary, and the burden shifts from solving the problem to sustaining the relief.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  pressed hand as lazy or the reliever as a pusher, because the source finding is precisely that
  neither is needed: relieving a live symptom now is responsible, and the fundamental fix really is
  slower and its reward really is far. The misfit is that the reasonable relief is what wastes the
  capacity to cure, so a chain of sensible reliefs is an addiction.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into willpower arriving to cure the cause. The
  burden shifts back only by funding the slow capacity and enduring the symptom while it rebuilds, a
  withdrawal that hurts and shows no result for a long time, so the reckoning is relief given up for a
  cure not yet arrived, not a system that healed the moment it meant to.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the archetype, the system-dynamics
  account, and the capability-trap evidence are theirs, and the condition the structure actually
  requires, a symptomatic solution whose use erodes the fundamental capacity, is carried as carefully
  as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Symptom](piece_the_symptom.md)**: carries the problem pressure (Senge), the visible pain that
  demands an answer now and does not care which one.
- **[The Quick Fix](piece_the_quick_fix.md)**: carries the symptomatic solution (Senge), fast relief that
  eases the symptom and leaves the cause untouched.
- **[The Fundamental Fix](piece_the_fundamental_fix.md)**: carries the fundamental solution and the capacity
  to apply it (Senge; Repenning & Sterman), the slow cure that addresses the cause and must be built and kept.
- **[The Delay](piece_the_delay.md)**: carries the timing asymmetry (Forrester; Sterman), the lag that makes the
  fundamental fix lose every race to the quick one.
- **[The Atrophy](piece_the_atrophy.md)**: carries the side effect (Senge; Repenning & Sterman), the fundamental
  capacity wasting from disuse each cycle the quick fix is chosen instead.
- **[The Dependence](piece_the_dependence.md)**: carries the addiction structure (Meadows), reliance on the fix
  deepening as the capacity shrinks, until the relief is no longer a choice.
- **[The Shifting Burden](piece_the_shifting_burden.md)**: carries the archetype whole (Senge; Meadows), the load
  moving from curing the cause to sustaining the relief, and its remedy in weaning, held whole by nobody.
