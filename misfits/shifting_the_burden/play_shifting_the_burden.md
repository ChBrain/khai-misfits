# Play: Shifting the Burden

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Shifting the Burden.
A symptom flares, and there are two ways to answer it: the quick relief that works today, and the slow cure that works for good ... and the quick relief is chosen, again and again, because it is fast and the cure is far, until the muscle that would have cured it has wasted from disuse, and the relief that was a choice is the only thing left that works.

## Arc

The bend is this: relieve the symptom the easy way and you weaken the power to fix the cause, so the easy way becomes the only way.
It runs on two answers to one problem, and a clock that favours the wrong one.
The quick fix acts now: it takes the pain away, buys the quarter, ends the crisis, and asks nothing of a capacity no one has time to build. The fundamental fix acts later: it addresses the cause, but it is slow, and its reward arrives long after the symptom would have to be endured to get there.
So the pressed hand reaches for the relief, sensibly, every time. And here is the turn that makes it a trap rather than a mere shortcut: each time the relief is used, the fundamental capacity goes one more cycle unused, and unused capacity wastes. The symptom returns, met again by the fix, and the muscle that would have ended it is a little weaker, so next time the fix is a little more necessary, until it is wholly necessary, and the cause is untouchable behind the relief that keeps the symptom just bearable enough to never fix.
Endure the symptom long enough to rebuild the capacity, and the burden shifts back: but the withdrawal is real, the cure is slow, and the fix is right there.

## Triggers

**[The Relief](plot_the_relief.md)**
A symptom flares and demands an answer now. The Reliever brings the quick fix, the Pressed takes it gratefully, and the pain goes. Closes when the symptom is relieved and the cause is left exactly where it was.

**[The Erosion](plot_the_erosion.md)**
Cue: the symptom is handled, so the slow work of the cure has no urgency. The Builder's fundamental capacity goes another cycle unused and unfunded, and unused capacity wastes. Closes when the power to fix the cause is measurably weaker than before the fix was reached for.

**[The Dependence](plot_the_dependence.md)**
Cue: the symptom returns, and the capacity to cure it is too wasted to try. The Habitué reaches for the fix without a second thought, because the fix is now the only thing that still works. Closes when the relief is no longer a choice but a necessity, and the cause is untouchable behind it.

**[The Weaning](plot_the_weaning.md)**
Cue: someone sees that the fix is the reason the problem is permanent. The Weaner funds the fundamental capacity and endures the symptom long enough to rebuild it. Closes when the burden shifts back to the cure ... or the withdrawal is judged too painful, the fix is reached for once more, and the muscle wastes another notch.

## Stakes

Whether the cause is ever cured, or only ever relieved.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward the fundamental fix, which is slow and far, but toward the quick one, which is fast and here and quietly fatal to the power that would have made it unnecessary. The production cannot make a pressed hand endure a symptom it can end in a moment. It can only show that no one was lazy and every relief was reasonable, that the fix worked every single time it was used, and that the one thing that would have ended the problem, the slow capacity the fix let waste, is the very thing each easy relief made a little harder to rebuild.
