# Plot: The Clear Case

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Table](place_the_table.md), a dispute whose outcome both sides can call.
A case plain enough that each party reads it the same way, and the question of whether to spend the cost of proving what both already know.

## Action

[Filtering](process_filtering.md): the two sides settle on terms that price in the outcome they both foresee, and the case leaves the record without a verdict.

## Stage

[The Litigant](persona_the_litigant.md) settles the clear case at [the Table](place_the_table.md), on [Settle the Sure Ones](plan_settle_the_sure_ones.md), working [the sieve](piece_the_sieve.md) so the predictable dispute drops into [the settled](place_the_settled.md) as [the unlitigated](piece_the_unlitigated.md), never reaching a court.
The settlement is cheap and sensible, and it removes exactly the case whose known outcome would have shown the pattern.

## Tension

Settling the clear case is plain good sense, and plain good sense empties the record of the cases that would inform it.
There is no error: trying a case both sides can call would spend the trial cost to confirm a certainty, so settling is the only reasonable move. But the case removed is an informative one, its known outcome exactly what a reader would need, so the plot closes with the dispute resolved cheaply and the record quietly poorer, an informative case subtracted by a settlement no one could fault, the filter doing its work through nothing but reasonable economy.
