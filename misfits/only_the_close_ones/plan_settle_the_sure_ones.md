# Plan: Settle the Sure Ones

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md), inside [Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Settlement](position_the_settlement.md)

## Direction

Resolve the disputes both sides can call, on terms that price in the outcome they both foresee, since trying a case whose result is plain is spending the cost of trial to confirm a certainty already had (Landes). Go to trial only where the two sides disagree on where the case lands, each rating its own odds high enough to refuse the other's terms, because those are the only cases no settlement both will take. Accept that this fills the record with the toss-ups and empties it of the clear ones, so the very economy that saves the parties their trial costs is the selection that leaves the tried sample chosen on the outcome itself.

## Orders

([The Litigant](persona_the_litigant.md) settles the cases whose result both sides foresee and tries only the ones they cannot agree on, paying for a verdict solely when the verdict is in doubt.)

## Implementation

([The Table](place_the_table.md); through the [filtering](process_filtering.md) process; the Litigant works [the sieve](piece_the_sieve.md), settling the predictable into [the settled](place_the_settled.md) and passing the toss-ups to [the court](place_the_court.md), so [the docket](piece_the_docket.md) fills with only [the coin flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md) cases.)

## Targets

- [ ] the cases both sides can call are settled, not tried
- [ ] settlement terms price in the outcome both foresee
- [ ] only the genuine disagreements are passed to trial
- [ ] the filtering of the record by predictability is accepted as the cost of the economy
