# Plot: The Trial

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Court](place_the_court.md), a dispute neither side can call.
A case the two parties read differently, each rating its own chances high enough to fight, so no settlement both would accept exists.

## Action

[Clustering](process_clustering.md): the toss-up is tried because it could not settle, and its verdict lands either way, adding one more near-even case to the record.

## Stage

[The Litigant](persona_the_litigant.md) tries the case at [the Court](place_the_court.md) because no terms both sides would take exist, and the verdict enters [the docket](piece_the_docket.md) as one more [coin flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md), the pool of tried cases splitting close to even by its nature.
The trial is proper and the verdict sound, and the case is here only because its outcome was a real question.

## Tension

Trying the case is the only path left when the sides cannot agree, and the cases left are exactly the ones that make the record even.
There is nothing wrong in the trial: a genuine dispute deserves a verdict, and this one reached court precisely because no settlement could resolve it. But a case is tried only when its outcome is uncertain, and uncertain cases split near even, so the plot closes with a proper verdict added to a pool of proper verdicts that together read fifty-fifty, the record's even split composed entirely of cases that had to be toss-ups to be here at all.
