# Piece: The Docket

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The tried sample itself, selected by settlement and clustering near even, the whole phenomenon of which every other piece is a part: a record that reads for the world and shows only its own filter.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Fills at [the Court](place_the_court.md) with the cases that would not settle, read from [the Tally](position_the_tally.md), and corrected only from [the Settled](place_the_settled.md).
Flows as the body of decided cases, complete as a record of trials and selected as a sample of disputes, the shape of the whole trap in one list.

## Load Bearing

Without the Docket there is no misfit, only settlements and trials: it is the Docket that ties the sieve to the coin flip to the misread, the one record in which a selection on outcome becomes a near-even rate and a near-even rate becomes a false finding.
The Docket is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole, the tried cases as a sample selected by the predictability it removes, so its win rate is driven toward one half regardless of the merits and inference from it misleads (Priest and Klein). It carries the entire misfit: the sieve's selection is the Docket's composition, the coin flip's clustering is the Docket's rate, the tallyman's error is the Docket read straight, so the Docket is not one more piece beside the others but the shape they all compose, the record whose every property is another of the play's pieces at work. It is built of sound cases soundly decided, which is why it cannot simply be trusted: every row is a real verdict, and the flaw is not in any row but in which rows are there, so reading the Docket rightly is not checking the entries but correcting for the ones that never arrived.

## Apparent

Just the record, the body of decided cases anyone can count.
Seen at the surface, the Docket looks like the evidence itself, a complete and countable set of outcomes, and the truth that it is a residue and not a sample, that its completeness as a record of trials is exactly what disguises its selection, that its plainest reading is its falsest, stays hidden behind how much a full list of verdicts resembles a fair picture of the disputes.
