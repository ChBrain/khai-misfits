# Persona: The Tallyman

## Taxonomy

[The Tally](position_the_tally.md). Counts the decided cases and reads the win rate as the state of the world, and is misled because the record they count is the residue of a selection they cannot see in it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Empirical. Trusting the count. Reading outcomes for the pattern they should show.
Is the one who gathers the verdicts, tallies who won, and infers how a kind of claim tends to fare or which way the law leans, exactly as a body of outcomes ought to be read.
No sloppiness wished on them: the counting is careful and the arithmetic sound, and the trouble is not that they miscount but that the thing they count was selected before it reached them, so their honest measure is a measure of the wrong population.

## Action

Collects the decided cases and computes the win rate, treating the record as a sample of the disputes and the tally as evidence of the merits.
Finds the outcomes splitting close to even and concludes the claims are a wash, or the law has no tilt, or that neither side has the advantage, reading the number as a fact about the world.
Does what a careful empiricist does: lets the data speak, and is betrayed by it, because the data is not a draw from the disputes but what settlement left after removing every case whose outcome was known.

## Shadow

Not careless, but a person whose faith in the count is the very thing that undoes them, and that is the vertigo of it if it ever dawns: the habit of a lifetime, that clean data honestly counted yields truth, is exactly the habit that leads them wrong here, and no inner sense warns them, no felt doubt rises to be trusted, because the number sits as solid in them as any true one they have ever reached.
What gnaws, once they glimpse it, is that they cannot unfeel the confidence. The record still looks clean, the arithmetic still satisfies, and the wrongness lives somewhere they have no instinct for, so they are asked to mistrust a result that every trained reflex tells them to believe, and the mistrust will not come of itself, only as an effort turned against their own judgement.
The bind is interior and disorienting: careful counting is their whole discipline and their quiet pride, and here the discipline is the snare, so they must hold their best skill in suspicion, doubt the thing they are good at exactly when it feels most reliable, and carry a measure that is at once impeccable and empty, a craft they trust gone strange in their hands.

## Tell

Says "the numbers are right there; the win rate is fifty percent."
Is true and is the trap, because the numbers are right there and the win rate is fifty percent and both facts are about a selected sample, so the sentence that presents the record's plainest reading is the exact form the error takes, an honest count of a filtered record stated as a finding about the world.
