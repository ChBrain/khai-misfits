# Plan: Read the Verdicts

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Tally](position_the_tally.md), inside [Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Tally](position_the_tally.md)

## Direction

Gather the decided cases, compute the win rate, and infer the merits of the claims or the lean of the law from the tally, since a body of outcomes is evidence and a careful count should show the pattern (Priest and Klein, as the error the finding names). Treat the record as a sample of the disputes and let the data speak, refining the estimate with cleaner data and larger samples. Do not look past the record, since the record is clean, complete, and countable as a record of trials, and accept, only when it is shown, that the sample was selected on the outcome before it arrived, so the honest count measures the residue of a filter and not the world.

## Orders

([The Tallyman](persona_the_tallyman.md) counts the verdicts, finds the win rate near even, and concludes the claims are a wash or the law has no tilt, reading a property of the selected sample as a property of the disputes.)

## Implementation

([The Court](place_the_court.md); through the [tallying](process_tallying.md) process; the Tallyman counts [the docket](piece_the_docket.md) and reads [the coin flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md) clustering as the world's, blind to [the sieve](piece_the_sieve.md) that made it and to [the unlitigated](piece_the_unlitigated.md) it cannot see.)
