# Process: Filtering

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The removal of the predictable cases by settlement, the sieve that closes every dispute both sides can call and passes on only the ones they cannot.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Litigant](persona_the_litigant.md), at [the Table](place_the_table.md), settling the cases whose outcome both sides foresee.
Begins whenever two parties can read a dispute the same way, since a shared reading is a settlement waiting to be made, and the case closes before it is tried.

## Direction

Turns a population of disputes into a residue of toss-ups.
Because a case settles exactly when both sides can call it, filtering removes the predictable and retains the uncertain, so it converts the full run of disputes into the subset no one could agree on, the trial pool defined by exclusion of the clear (Landes; Priest and Klein).

## Lever

The cost of a verdict against the certainty of one.
Because trying a case whose end is known spends the trial cost for nothing, filtering works through the plain economy of settlement: there is no reason to try what can be foreseen, so the lever is the sound preference for agreement over a pointless verdict, the same good sense that saves the parties their costs removing the informative cases from the record.

## Echo

A record left holding only what could not be foreseen.
The echo of filtering is a trial pool emptied of the clear cases and filled with the toss-ups, so whatever is counted next is counted over a sample selected on the outcome, the sieve having done its work at the Table and left no trace on the verdicts that remain.
