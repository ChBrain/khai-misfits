# Process: Clustering

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The pull of the tried win rate toward one half, the near-even split a pool of toss-ups produces whatever the merits of the disputes it was drawn from.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Coin Flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md), at [the Court](place_the_court.md), as each tried case, being a genuine uncertainty, lands either way.
Begins once the trial pool is the residue of settlement, since a pool of cases whose outcomes were real questions splits close to even by its nature.

## Direction

Turns a selected sample into a near-even rate.
Because the tried cases are exactly the ones whose outcome neither side could call, their verdicts divide close to half, so clustering converts the selection into a win rate driven toward fifty percent by the composition of the sample rather than the tilt of the law (Priest and Klein).

## Lever

The uncertainty that defined the sample.
Because a case is tried only when its outcome is a genuine toss-up, the pool is built of near-even bets, so clustering works through the very property that selected the cases: they are here because they were uncertain, and uncertain cases split evenly, so the lever is the definition of the sample, the fifty-fifty a restatement of why these cases and not others were tried.

## Echo

A win rate at half that owes nothing to the merits.
The echo of clustering is a trial record that reads near even whatever the underlying law, so a lopsided world and a balanced one leave the same tally, the clustering a fact about the selected pool that the reader will mistake for a fact about the disputes, though it tends and does not fix, softened where the selection is imperfect (Kessler, Meites and Miller).
