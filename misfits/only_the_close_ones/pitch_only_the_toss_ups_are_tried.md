# Pitch: Only the Toss-Ups Are Tried

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The single hook: settlement removes the cases whose outcome is clear, so only the genuine toss-ups reach trial, and a docket of toss-ups clusters near even whatever the merits, telling the honest reader a fifty-fifty story about a world that was never even.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Read the record, count who wins, infer how the law leans.
The tenor is evidence handled soundly: outcomes are data, a pile of verdicts should show a pattern, so counting the decided cases and reading the win rate is exactly how one learns which way a kind of claim tends to go.

## Undertow

The record holds only what would not settle.
Underneath, the cases both sides could call have already left by settlement, priced on the outcome both foresaw, so the tried pool is the residue of the ones no settlement could resolve, the toss-ups kept and the clear cases gone before the counting begins.

## Nerve

A docket of toss-ups comes out near even, whatever the world was.
The nerve is that the trial win rate is driven toward one half by the selection and not by the merits, so a lopsided law and a balanced one leave the same fifty-fifty record, and the reader who counts it measures the sample's clustering and mistakes it for the world's.

## Echo

The only way to read the record rightly is to model the cases it never held.
What lingers is that correcting the inference means estimating a population you cannot see, since settlements are private and their terms unrecorded, so reading the trial record truly is rebuilding everything that settled away, and taking the fifty-fifty at face value, as any honest counter does, is exactly what the filter was built to make you do.
