# Piece: The Coin Flip

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The tried case as a genuine toss-up, and the near-even rate a pool of them produces, the fifty-fifty that is a property of the selection and not of the merits.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Lands at [the Court](place_the_court.md), where each toss-up is decided either way, and composes the near-even split of [the docket](piece_the_docket.md).
Flows as the uncertainty that reaches trial and the even division it yields, the coin flip that a case has to be to be tried at all.

## Load Bearing

Without the Coin Flip clustering near even the misread would have no hook: it is the fifty-fifty rate that the reader takes for the world, and the fifty-fifty is a fact about the selected pool.
The Coin Flip is load bearing because it is the finding the whole trap turns on, the tried win rate driven toward one half by the composition of the sample rather than the tilt of the law (Priest and Klein). Its being a property of the selection is the point: a case reaches trial only because its outcome was a real question, and questions with real answers on both sides split close to even, so the rate is a restatement of why these cases were tried, not a measure of who deserves to win, and it tends toward half rather than fixing there, softened where the parties misjudge or the stakes are lopsided (Kessler, Meites and Miller), a strong pull and not an iron rule.

## Apparent

Just the win rate, the plain fact of who prevails at trial.
Seen at the surface, the Coin Flip looks like a finding about the disputes, an even contest read straight off the record, and the truth that it is even because the sample was selected to be even, that the same rate would appear whatever the law, that it measures the toss-up nature of what is tried and not the balance of what is claimed, stays hidden behind how naturally a fifty-fifty record reads as a fifty-fifty world.
