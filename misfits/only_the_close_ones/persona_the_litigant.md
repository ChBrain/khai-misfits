# Persona: The Litigant

## Taxonomy

[The Settlement](position_the_settlement.md). A party to a dispute who settles the cases both sides can call and tries only the ones they cannot, so their sound choice of when to fight is the filter that fills the record with toss-ups.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Calculating. Weighing the cost of trial against the outcome foreseen. Fighting only what cannot be agreed.
Is the party who settles when both sides read a case the same way, because paying to try a case whose result is plain is spending for nothing, and goes to trial only when the two cannot agree on where it lands.
No litigiousness wished on them: the choice to settle or fight is not appetite but arithmetic, the sound weighing of trial's cost against a known result, and the trouble is not that they choose badly but that the sum of their choices is a record made of only the cases no one could call.

## Action

Settles the disputes whose outcome both sides foresee, on terms that price in the result, so the predictable cases leave before any verdict is entered.
Tries the disputes on which the two sides disagree, each rating its own odds high enough to refuse the other's terms, so the trials that happen are the genuine uncertainties.
Does what a rational party does: pays for a verdict only when the verdict is in doubt, and settles the rest, and so hands the record, without meaning to, exactly the cases whose outcome was a real question and none of the ones whose outcome was known.

## Shadow

Not a schemer, but a party whose every prudent settlement quietly empties the record of the cases that would have shown the pattern, and that is the unease when they glimpse it: nothing they did was anything but sensible, and the sum is a public record that cannot be read, each clear case they resolved a data point removed from a count someone else will misread.
What sits with them is that their prudence is invisible in its effect. They settled to save a cost, not to bias a record, and yet the record is biased by their settling, so they are the author of a distortion they never intended and cannot see in any single case, only in the aggregate they are not the one reading.
The bind is interior and diffuse: to stop filtering the record they would have to try the cases they can already call, spending to confirm the obvious purely so the count would be complete, which no party will do, so the very reasonableness that makes them settle is what guarantees the record is the residue of what they could not.

## Tell

Says "why would I pay to try a case I already know how it ends?"
Is unanswerable as a matter of their own interest and is the exact mechanism of the filter, because the cases they know how they end are the clear ones, and declining to try them is what leaves only the unknowable to be tried, so the sentence that states plain good sense states, in the same breath, why the trial record can hold only the toss-ups.
