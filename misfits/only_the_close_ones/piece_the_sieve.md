# Piece: The Sieve

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The settlement filter, which lets the predictable cases drop out and holds back only the uncertain, the mechanism that selects the trial pool on the outcome itself.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Sits at [the Table](place_the_table.md), draining the clear cases into [the settled](place_the_settled.md) and passing the toss-ups to [the court](place_the_court.md).
Flows as the sorting of disputes by predictability, the settlement that closes what can be foreseen and lets only what cannot proceed to a verdict.

## Load Bearing

Without the Sieve the trial record would be a sample of the disputes, and reading it would be sound: it is the settlement filter that makes the tried cases a selected residue rather than a draw.
The Sieve is load bearing because it is the selection itself, the sorting of cases by whether their outcome could be agreed, so the trial pool is defined by exclusion of the clear (Priest and Klein; Landes). It sorts on the very quantity the reader wants to measure: the outcome. Because a case settles when both sides foresee its result, the Sieve removes exactly the cases whose outcomes are known and keeps exactly the ones whose outcomes are in doubt, so the residue is not a random subset but the subset chosen on the thing being studied, and every distortion downstream, the near-even rate and the misread, follows from this one filter selecting the sample on its own outcome.

## Apparent

Just settlement, parties resolving what they can without a trial.
Seen at the surface, the Sieve looks like nothing but sensible dispute resolution, cases closed cheaply and cases tried when they must be, and the truth that it selects the record on the outcome, that it removes precisely the informative cases, that it builds a sample whose composition is chosen by the very variable under study, stays hidden behind how ordinary and reasonable each settlement is.

## Yearbook

Worked by [the Litigant](persona_the_litigant.md) settling what they can call, unseen by [the Tallyman](persona_the_tallyman.md) who counts only what it passes, and modelled by [the Realist](persona_the_realist.md) who must reconstruct what it removed.
It is the selection at the root: the Sieve is what [filtering](process_filtering.md) enacts, the filter that sends the clear cases to [the settled](place_the_settled.md) as [the unlitigated](piece_the_unlitigated.md) and leaves [the docket](piece_the_docket.md) holding only [the coin flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md) cases, selecting the sample on the outcome it hides.
