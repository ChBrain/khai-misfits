# Process: Tallying

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The reading of the trial record into a conclusion, the honest count that takes the win rate of a selected sample for a fact about the disputes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Tallyman](persona_the_tallyman.md), at [the Court](place_the_court.md), counting the verdicts and computing the rate.
Begins whenever a reader treats the decided cases as evidence and asks what the pattern of outcomes shows.

## Direction

Turns a selected record into a false finding.
Because the reader counts the tried cases as though they sampled the disputes, the near-even rate of the residue is read as the tilt of the world, so tallying converts an honest count into a wrong conclusion, the arithmetic sound and the population wrong (Priest and Klein).

## Lever

The soundness of the count.
Because the record is clean and complete and the arithmetic correct, tallying works through the reader's justified trust in careful measurement: every visible test of the data passes, so the lever is the very rigour of the count, which produces confidence in a number exactly because the flaw is not in the counting but in what was counted.

## Echo

A firm conclusion about a world the record never held.
The echo of tallying is a reader sure the claims are a wash or the law has no lean, their certainty grown with the care they took, the selection that shaped the record invisible to the count, so the more diligently the record is read the more firmly its filtered rate is believed.
