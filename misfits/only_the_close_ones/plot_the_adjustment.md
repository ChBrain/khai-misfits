# Plot: The Adjustment

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Settled](place_the_settled.md), someone who would read the record rightly.
A reader who treats the trial docket as a filtered residue and turns, before counting it, to ask what settled away.

## Action

[Adjusting](process_adjusting.md): the Realist models the settlement that removed the clear cases and corrects the win rate for it, estimating the disputes from the population rather than the residue.

## Stage

[The Realist](persona_the_realist.md) works from [the Settled](place_the_settled.md), on [Count the Settled](plan_count_the_settled.md), reconstructing [the unlitigated](piece_the_unlitigated.md) that [the sieve](piece_the_sieve.md) removed and discounting [the coin flip](piece_the_coin_flip.md) as the sample's, so [the docket](piece_the_docket.md) is read through the selection rather than at face value.
The correction is real, and it rests on the cases no one recorded.

## Tension

Reading the record rightly means modelling the very cases the record left out, so the true answer is the one built on the unseen.
The remedy is real: treat the tried cases as selected, estimate the settled population, correct the rate, and the inference can be repaired. But the settled disputes are private and unrecorded, so the correction rests hardest on the least observable data and can never be as firm as the plain count, and the plot closes with the choice laid bare: offer the honest range that models the missing cases and convinces fewer, or take the confident fifty-fifty at face value and be wrong with certainty, the true reading forever the less sure one because it rests on the cases the filter kept from ever being written down.
