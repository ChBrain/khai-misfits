# Persona: The Realist

## Taxonomy

[The Estimate](position_the_estimate.md). Reads the trial record knowing it is selected, and repairs the inference by modelling the settled cases that never appear, paying in the reconstruction of a population that cannot be observed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Distrusting the surface count. Reasoning about what is absent. Willing to estimate the unseen.
Is the one who takes the trial record as the residue of a filter rather than a sample of the world, and asks what settled away before asking what the verdicts show.
No genius wished on them: the correction is not a sharper reading of the record but a refusal to read it alone, a discipline of modelling the cases the record omits, undertaken knowing the omitted cases are exactly the ones no one wrote down.

## Action

Treats the tried cases as the ones that would not settle, and works backward from the settlement rate and the terms that do leak to the shape of the disputes that never reached a verdict.
Corrects the win rate for the selection, discounting the fifty-fifty as a property of the filter and estimating the merits from the population rather than the residue.
Does what reading a selected record rightly requires: builds a model of what did not get tried, accepts that the estimate rests on the unobserved, and trades the false certainty of the plain count for the honest uncertainty of a reconstruction.

## Shadow

Not an oracle, but someone who must build their answer on the cases they can least see, and that is the strain: the settled disputes are private, their terms unrecorded, so the correction rests hardest on the very data the world keeps hidden, and the confidence they can offer is bounded by a darkness they cannot light.
What wears on them is that their careful correction looks weaker than the error it fixes. The Tallyman offers a clean number and a firm conclusion; the Realist offers a range, an assumption, a caveat about the unobserved, so the true reading is the less persuasive one, and they are forever the person replacing a confident answer with an honest doubt that convinces fewer.
The bind is interior and thankless: to be right they must rest their estimate on what was never recorded, so they can never be as sure as the reader they correct, and the more faithfully they account for the missing cases the more provisional their answer must sound, the honesty of the reconstruction and its persuasiveness pulling against each other.

## Tell

Says "before I tell you what the record shows, tell me what settled."
Is the whole correction in one demand, since what settled is what the record cannot show and is exactly what the inference needs, so the sentence that sounds like a refusal to answer is the only path to a right answer, a reading of the trial record that begins by asking for everything the trial record left out.
