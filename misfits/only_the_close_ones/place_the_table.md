# Place: The Table

## Taxonomy

[Only the Close Ones](play_only_the_close_ones.md). The bargaining table, where the cases both sides can call are resolved without a verdict, the filter's mouth, where the predictable disputes leave the record before they are ever counted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Two parties reaching terms, a dispute closed without a trial.
Shown as sense: the sides that can see how a case would end agree on terms that reflect it, sparing themselves the cost of a verdict, the settlement looking like nothing but the reasonable resolution of a clear matter.

## Holds

Holds the resolving of every case whose outcome is foreseen.
The Table is where predictability is turned into agreement: because both sides read the clear case the same way, they meet on terms that price the outcome in, so the disputes with known ends are closed here, quietly and cheaply, and pass out of the record before any court sees them.

## Offers

Offers the parties a cheap resolution, and the record a silent subtraction.
Here the settlement is real and the saving genuine, so the Table offers the sound close of a case not worth trying, and in the same motion it offers the record a removal, taking the predictable case off the count so quietly that nothing marks its absence, the economy for the parties and the gap in the sample the same act.

## Withheld

Withholds that what leaves here is what the record most needs.
What the Table hides is that the cases it resolves are exactly the ones whose known outcomes would have shown the pattern, so the disputes settled away are the informative ones, removed precisely because they were clear, the Table draining from the record the cases a reader would most need to see and leaving only the ones that reveal nothing.
