# Prevalence-Induced Concept Change: Reference

The research warrant for [Prevalence-Induced Concept Change](play_prevalence_induced_concept_change.md).
The finding is Levari and colleagues'; the play runs on their experiments, through Haslam's concept-creep
account of the societal case and the adaptation-level psychophysics beneath the mechanism, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging prevalence-induced concept change as a **judgement system**: an assessor set to find some category of
case, the threat, the harm, the transgression, who judges each instance comparatively against the recent ones,
so that as the true cases genuinely dwindle the concept widens to fill the space they left, milder cases are
recategorised as the thing, the count never falls, and the effort chases a boundary that recedes as it is
approached. The play models the documented mechanics, the comparative eye that reads each case in the light of
recent company, the falling prevalence that is real progress, the line of the concept drifting outward by just
the amount the true cases fall, the formerly-fine case swept in in perfect good faith, the flat tally that hides
the win and drives the effort onto ever-milder ground, and the fixed, pre-declared standard that alone stops the
creep, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of bias or bad faith. The
governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: when the prevalence of a stimulus falls, the
concept of it expands, so people judging a shrinking category keep finding it at a steady rate by unknowingly
broadening what counts. It is distinct from the house's Metric Fixation, where a fixed measure is gamed until it
parts from the goal: there the number is chosen and worked, here nothing is gamed and the standard itself drifts
unbidden. It is distinct from the house's The Streetlight Effect, where the search goes where the light is
rather than where the answer is: there the looking is misplaced, here it is well placed and the category widens.
And it is distinct, most sharply, from the house's The New Normal, where the line of the acceptable slides
downward as deviations are tolerated: there the standard loosens as harm accumulates, here the concept widens as
harm recedes, the two drifting in opposite directions and driven by opposite forces.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the assessor as
  biased or the reformer as self-serving, because the source finding is precisely that each judgement is made in
  good faith: judging by recent context is how perception works and usually serves us well, a falling prevalence
  is genuine progress, and rooting out the thing is conscientious. The creep is authored by no one and emerges
  from the structure, a relative eye meeting a falling prevalence, so the misfit is that answering the problem is
  exactly what widens the concept of it, and the assessor feels no less strict as the line moves under it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that stops the
  creep is to fix the mark, to define the thing absolutely and in advance and count against a standard held
  though the company shifts, and it fights the way the eye works, forces the mild case to be called fine when
  that feels like complacency and denial, and forces the falling count to be read as victory when declaring
  victory looks like calling off the watch too soon and can defund the very effort that won. The reckoning is a
  line held still against the instinct of the vigilant, not a free correction, so the cure for a problem never
  seen to shrink is the willingness to say, against every reflex, that it has.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the prevalence-induced concept change finding is Levari and
  colleagues', the concept-creep account is Haslam's, and the relative-judgement mechanism is Helson's and
  Parducci's, and the condition the trap actually requires, a comparative judgement meeting a genuinely falling
  prevalence, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.
