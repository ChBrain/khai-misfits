# Play: Prevalence-Induced Concept Change

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Prevalence-Induced Concept Change.
You are the honest eye. Your task is to find the thing, the threat, the harm, the case that needs acting on, and you judge each one fairly, by the same standard, and act where it is warranted. And it works: the true cases grow fewer, the danger recedes, the harm is really answered ... and here the ground shifts under you without a tremor. Because you judge each new case not against a fixed rule but against the ones you have lately seen, and the ones you have lately seen are milder, because the bad ones are mostly gone. So the line of what counts as the thing drifts outward to fill the space the true cases left, and a case you would once have waved through as plainly fine you now flag as the thing, in perfect good faith, feeling no less strict than before. The rarer the real cases become, the wider your net grows to catch them, so the count never falls, the problem never reads as solved, and you press on, rooting out the thing in cases ever milder, sure the work is as needed as ever, because your own eye has quietly moved the line to keep it so.

## Arc

The bend is this: a fair eye judging by recent company will widen the concept as the true cases fall, so the more the problem is solved the less it is seen to be solved, and the effort chases a boundary that recedes as it is approached.
It runs on a judgement that is relative meeting a prevalence that falls.
Human judgement is comparative, not absolute: we read each case in the light of the recent ones, and this is efficient and usually right, the adaptation-level the eye works from. So when the task is to find the thing and the true cases are common, the eye is calibrated to them and the line sits where it should. But do the work well and the true cases dwindle, and now the recent company is milder, so the same comparative eye, feeling exactly as strict, sets its line further out.
And the line, once drifting, sweeps in the innocent. A case that is genuinely fine, that last year fell well outside the concept, now sits near the edge of a company grown mild, and the eye, judging relatively, calls it the thing. The concept has crept, and no one moved it: the assessor is as fair as ever, applying what feels like one unchanging standard, and yet the standard has widened by exactly the amount the true cases fell, so the tally the effort watches does not drop, and the reformer reads a flat count as a problem undefeated and drives on into milder and milder ground, the definition ballooning as the reality shrinks.
None of it is bias or bad faith. Judging by context is how perception works and mostly serves us well, a receding danger is real and good news, and the effort to root out the thing is conscientious: no one inflates the concept on purpose, and each judgement feels as strict as the last. The creep is authored by no one and emerges from the structure, a relative eye and a falling prevalence, so the misfit is that success itself moves the line, and the better the problem is answered the more of the world is called the problem.
Fix the mark, define the thing absolutely and in advance, count against a standard set before the cases are seen and hold it though the company shifts, and the creep can be stopped: but a fixed mark fights the way the eye works, effortful and unnatural, it forces you to call the mild case fine when calling it fine feels like complacency and denial, and it forces you to read the falling count as victory when declaring victory looks like abandoning the cause and can defund the very watch that won it, so holding the line still is bought against the instinct of the vigilant and the reflex to never say the danger has passed.

## Stakes

Whether a fair eye can answer a problem and still see that it has answered it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the creeping line, which each judgement is right to set and all are blinded by setting, and away from the fixed mark, which would let progress be seen and which fights the way the eye works and the instinct never to sound the all-clear. The production cannot make a relative eye register its own success, because the eye judges by recent company and success is exactly what mildens the company, so the line drifts out by just what the true cases fall and the count holds flat, the creep the eye working, not failing. It can only show that no one is biased or acting in bad faith, that judging by context is sound perception and a receding danger is real good news and rooting out the thing is conscientious, and that the one move that lets the win be seen, fixing the mark in advance, must be made against the vigilant's instinct and at the risk of looking like the one who called off the watch too soon, so the fairness is real and the blindness is real and the better the problem is answered the more of the world is called the problem.
