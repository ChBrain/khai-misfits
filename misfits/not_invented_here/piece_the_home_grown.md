# Piece: The Home-Grown

## Taxonomy

[Not-Invented-Here](play_not_invented_here.md). The internal version built in the import's place, worse or costlier, and owned.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the lab](place_the_lab.md), where it is built and loved.
The team's own rebuild of what was turned away, understood line by line.

## Load Bearing

Without it the rejection would leave a gap the group would have to feel.
With it the gap is filled and hidden: the home-grown is the internal version raised to meet the need the import would have met, at greater cost or lesser worth, and taken up with pride because it was made here. The Home-Grown is load bearing because it is where the loss is disguised (Katz and Allen): the real good of ownership, understanding and maintaining every line, is exactly what covers that the group has paid more for less, so the thing the team is proudest of is the standing measure of the better one it refused.

## Apparent

A triumph of self-reliance.
A capable version built in-house looks like a team that solves its own problems and owns its stack, so the home-grown reads as strength, and the truth that it cost more and does less than the thing at the door, that its pride is measuring a loss, stays hidden behind how genuinely good ownership is.
