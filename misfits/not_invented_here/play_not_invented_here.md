# Play: Not-Invented-Here

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Not-Invented-Here.
A good idea arrives from outside the group, better or cheaper than anything in-house, and it is turned away, not because it is worse but because it is theirs and not ours. The boundary that makes the team a team marks the outside idea as suspect, so the reflex fires: reject it, and build our own. The home-grown version is costlier, or weaker, and it is loved, because it is ours ... and the better idea is left at the door, the effort goes to reinventing it, and the group, letting less and less of the world in, quietly stops learning.

## Arc

The bend is this: the idea is judged by whose it is, not by whether it works, and outside is the wrong answer.
It runs on a boundary that trust needs.
A group coheres by marking an inside and an outside: it trusts what it made, understands what it owns, and is wary of what it did not build and cannot see the inside of. That boundary is real and valuable, the very thing that makes a close team effective.
But the same boundary that earns trust inward reflexively suspects outward, so an external idea is devalued for its origin before it is judged on its merit, and the group builds its own worse version to keep the origin in-house.
None of it is mere pride. The reflex is the shadow of real goods: cohesion that makes the team work, ownership that means you understand and can maintain what you run, and the true fact that an outside thing carries unknowns, lock-in, a fit you cannot verify, so some rejections are sound. And sometimes the import really is unfit, and the home-grown really is better.
Open the boundary, read the outside in, reward adoption and not only invention, and the better idea can win regardless of whose it is: but opening the boundary imports the very risk the reflex feared and erodes the cohesion and ownership that made the group strong, the one who can bridge inside and outside is rare, and no one can cleanly tell a reflex rejection from a sound judgement that the outside thing does not fit.

## Triggers

**[The Arrival](plot_the_arrival.md)**
A good idea arrives from outside, better or cheaper than anything in-house. The Import offers it across the boundary, and the Insider, reading its origin before its merit, turns it away for being theirs and not ours. Closes when the outside idea is rejected for where it came from, not for whether it works.

**[The Reinvention](plot_the_reinvention.md)**
Cue: the need the import would have met is still there. The Insider sets the group to build its own, and the Home-Grown rises, costlier or weaker than the thing turned away, and loved because it is ours. Closes when the effort has gone to reinventing what was offered, and the worse version is owned with pride.

**[The Staling](plot_the_staling.md)**
Cue: the group lets less and less of the world in. The Home-Grown ages without fresh outside knowledge, and the Beneficiary, out where the work is used, gets a product slipping behind what the world already has. Closes when the group's edge has dulled from insularity and the cost has reached the ones it serves.

**[The Guard](plot_the_guard.md)**
Cue: an outside idea really might carry risk. The Guardian rejects the import for its unknowns, its lock-in, its unverified fit, and is sometimes right, so the reflex and the sound judgement wear the same face. Closes when a rejection cannot be told, from the outside, as reflex or as reason.

**[The Bridge](plot_the_bridge.md)**
Cue: someone moves to let the world in. The Gatekeeper would read the outside and translate it inward, reward adoption as well as invention, against the Guardian's true warning that opening the boundary imports risk and erodes the cohesion and ownership that made the group strong. Closes when the better idea can win regardless of whose it is ... or the boundary holds, or the strength of the group is opened away with its walls.

## Stakes

Whether the best idea wins on its merit, or only ever the idea that was born inside.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the inside, which is trusted and owned, and away from the outside, which is suspect for being outside whatever its worth. The production cannot make a boundary that trusts inward stop suspecting outward, because the same edge that binds the group is the edge that turns the good idea away. It can only show that no one is merely proud, that cohesion and ownership are real goods and outside things carry real risk, that the reflex is their shadow and not a separate vice, and that the one move that lets the best idea win, opening the boundary and reading the world in, imports the danger the reflex feared, erodes the strength the boundary gave, and cannot cleanly tell a reflex rejection from a judgement that the outside thing truly did not fit.
