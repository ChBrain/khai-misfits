# Piece: The Staling

## Taxonomy

[Not-Invented-Here](play_not_invented_here.md). The group's knowledge dulling from insularity, its work aging against a world that moves on.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the lab](place_the_lab.md), where the edge dulls, and out in [the field](place_the_field.md), where it is felt.
In the slow gap between a group that stopped importing and a world that did not stop moving.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would end at a single refused idea, a one-time loss.
With it the loss compounds: the staling is the group's knowledge going stale as it lets less of the world in, so each shut door not only forgoes one better way but starves the group of the outside learning that keeps it sharp, and its output ages against rivals who kept importing. The Staling is load bearing because it is the finding that the reflex is cumulative (Katz and Allen on tenure and performance): the longer the boundary stays shut, the further behind the group falls, so insularity is not a stance but a slope.

## Apparent

The natural aging of any product.
A group's work slipping behind looks like the ordinary way things date, so the staling reads as time passing, and the truth that it is caused, that the falling-behind is the accumulated cost of a boundary that stopped letting the world in, stays hidden behind how normally all things grow old.

## Yearbook

Set in through [staling](process_staling.md), the slope [Ask for What Works](plan_ask_for_what_works.md) bears.
It is the compounding cost of the shut door: the Staling ages [the home-grown](piece_the_home_grown.md) and reaches the beneficiary in [the field](place_the_field.md), the reflex's price paid over years.
