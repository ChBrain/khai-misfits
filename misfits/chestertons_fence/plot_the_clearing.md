# Plot: The Clearing

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The review](place_the_review.md), where a reformer asks what the fence is for and no one can answer.
The barrier is on the agenda, its cost entered, its purpose nowhere to be found.

## Action

[Clearing](process_clearing.md): [The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), holding [the reformer's seat](position_reformer.md), lays out the plain cost and the absence of any use, while [The Keeper](persona_the_keeper.md), holding [the custodian's seat](position_custodian.md), can say only that it has always been there, and loses the room.

## Stage

[The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md) tables [Clear It Away](plan_clear_it_away.md): remove what cannot justify itself, so [the reformer's case](piece_the_reformers_case.md) is made soundly and carried.
[The Keeper](persona_the_keeper.md) tables [Leave It Standing](plan_leave_it_standing.md) and cannot answer it, because [the invisible benefit](piece_the_invisible_benefit.md) has nothing to enter into the record, and the fence goes to its hearing with everything for it a non-event.

## Tension

Every argument in the room is correct, and the correct decision is the disaster.
The reformer reasons soundly, the cost is real, the reason is genuinely missing, and the keeper genuinely cannot produce one. There is no error to catch and no villain to stop, only an honest weighing of a plain cost against an unprovable benefit, which can end only one way: the fence comes down, and everyone did their job.
