# Piece: The Recovered Reason

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The why found again: reconstructed from the harm too late, or dug out in time, or lost past finding.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the archive](place_the_archive.md), in the trail followed back to the purpose.
Where the reason is either recovered before the removal or explained after the damage.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would have no answer and no cost to its answer.
With it there is a way out and a price on it: the reason can be reconstructed, but the recovering is slow, often defeated by a record that was never kept, and most cheaply supplied by the very disaster it should have prevented. The Recovered Reason is load bearing because it holds both faces of Chesterton's remedy (Chesterton): the reason found in time, which saves the fence at the cost of delay, and the reason found too late, which explains the wreckage and saves nothing, and it is the same knowledge either way, differing only in whether it arrives before the axe or after.

## Apparent

Either vindication or hindsight.
Recovered in time it looks like needless caution rewarded; recovered too late it looks like an obvious truth everyone should have seen, and in both the ease of the reason after the fact hides how genuinely unavailable it was before.

## Yearbook

Sought by [the inquirer](persona_the_inquirer.md) and reconstructed by [the investigator](persona_the_investigator.md) through [recovering](process_recovering.md), dug back out of [the forgotten reason](piece_the_forgotten_reason.md).
It is the knowledge the fence needed to speak: the Recovered Reason is what answers [chesterton's fence](piece_chestertons_fence.md), when it comes at all, and whether it comes in time.
