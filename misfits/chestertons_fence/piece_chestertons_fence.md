# Piece: Chesterton's Fence

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The phenomenon whole: a safeguard removed for want of its reason, and the rule that answers it at a cost.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the fence line](place_the_fence_line.md), where the whole lesson stands.
And across the road, in every barrier weighed by its cost and not its purpose.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would be a heap of parts with no shape.
With it the parts become one thing: the safeguard, the forgotten reason, the invisible benefit, the reformer's sound case, and the returning harm are facets of a single rule and its violation, that a protection works by producing nothing, so it looks most useless exactly when it is most needed, and the only answer is to learn its reason before tearing it down. Chesterton's Fence is load bearing because it is the whole finding (Chesterton) with its whole price attached: it holds the misfit together, and it holds that the remedy is inquiry, slow and often futile and cutting both ways, not the fantasy of a reformer wise enough to see every hidden use.

## Apparent

Either mere nostalgia, or a licence to change nothing.
Seen from one side the rule looks like sentiment dressed as principle, an excuse to keep old things because they are old; seen from the other it looks like a mandate to freeze, when in truth it forbids neither reform nor removal, only removal in ignorance, which is the one thing that feels most like progress.

## Yearbook

Answered by [the inquirer](persona_the_inquirer.md) through [recovering](process_recovering.md), held whole by nobody.
It is the play in a single piece: Chesterton's Fence is what [the safeguard](piece_the_safeguard.md) becomes when its reason is lost, undone only when [the recovered reason](piece_the_recovered_reason.md) is dug out before the axe falls, at the price of the time and the fences that discipline keeps too long.
