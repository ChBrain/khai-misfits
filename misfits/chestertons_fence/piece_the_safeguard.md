# Piece: The Safeguard

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The object: the fence, the rule, the check that stands with its reason not written on its face.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the fence line](place_the_fence_line.md), upright and mended, no plaque to say why.
Where the barrier does its work in full view and unexplained.

## Load Bearing

Without it there would be no drama, only a hazard no one guarded against.
With it there is a protection in place whose value depends entirely on a reason kept somewhere else, so the whole play turns on the gap between the fence standing here and the why standing nowhere. The Safeguard is load bearing because it is the inherited arrangement at the centre of Chesterton's rule (Burke; Chesterton), the thing that carries real purpose in its function and none on its surface, and everything that follows is what happens to a protection that cannot speak for itself.

## Apparent

An old barrier, maybe useful, maybe not.
It looks like any inherited fixture, part obstruction and part furniture, so nothing about it announces that it is holding back a specific danger, and its steadiness reads as mere persistence rather than active work.

## Yearbook

Kept by [the keeper](persona_the_keeper.md) through [forgetting](process_forgetting.md), the barrier that outlives its story.
It is where the play begins: the Safeguard stands sound and mute, and [the forgotten reason](piece_the_forgotten_reason.md) is what leaves it undefended when the questions come.
