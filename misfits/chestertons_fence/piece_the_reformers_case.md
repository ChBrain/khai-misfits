# Piece: The Reformer's Case

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The sound argument: remove what carries a plain cost and no use anyone can name.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the review](place_the_review.md), laid out and carried on the visible evidence.
Where the cost is admissible and the defence is a non-event.

## Load Bearing

Without it the fence would fall to mere carelessness, and the misfit would be only a blunder.
With it the fence falls to good reasoning: the cost is real, the reason is genuinely missing, and no one can point to a harm that has not happened, so the case to remove is not a lapse but the correct conclusion from everything that can be seen. The Reformer's Case is load bearing because it is the rationalist conceit named honestly (Hayek): the demand that a thing justify itself on its face, applied to a safeguard whose justification is tacit, so that soundness itself does the damage, and there is no error to blame, only a method that cannot see a benefit shaped like an absence.

## Apparent

Simply good governance.
Questioning what things are for and removing what cannot answer is exactly how waste is cut and clutter cleared, so the case looks like diligence rather than danger, and its logic is genuinely the logic that should be applied to a fence that has truly died.
