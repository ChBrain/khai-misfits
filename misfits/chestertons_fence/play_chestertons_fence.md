# Play: Chesterton's Fence

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Chesterton's Fence.
A fence stands across a road, and no one can say why ... so a reformer, seeing no use in it and a plain cost to it, clears it away, and the thing the fence was built to hold back, unseen for so long precisely because the fence held it back, comes through the gap it left, and only then, too late, does everyone learn what the fence was for.

## Arc

The bend is this: a safeguard that works leaves no evidence that it is working.
It runs on a gap between a cost that shows and a benefit that cannot.
The fence has a plain price: it blocks the road, it wants mending, it offends the eye and the tidy mind. Its benefit is a harm that does not happen, a bull that does not get in, a fall that no one takes, and a non-event leaves no mark, so the ledger shows all cost and no gain. The reason it was raised is not written on it, and the ones who knew have gone, so the case to remove it is not reckless but sound: no one can name its use, and no one can, because its use is the disaster it has silently prevented for years.
So the fence comes down, cleanly and with good arguments, and the danger it stood against, which never left and only waited, walks back through. The reason is reconstructed at last from the wreckage, and it was a good reason all along.
Learn why the fence was built before you tear it down, and the harm need not return: but the learning is slow, the reason is often lost past recovery, and some fences truly have outlived their danger, so the discipline that saves you from the amputation is the same one that can leave you tending walls whose purpose is long dead.

## Triggers

**[The Fence](plot_the_fence.md)**
A fence stands across a road, its reason already gone from living memory. The Keeper maintains it because it has always been maintained, and the Bystander passes it every day, safe and unaware that it keeps him so. Closes when the fence stands, working, with no one left who can say what it is for.

**[The Clearing](plot_the_clearing.md)**
Cue: a reformer asks what the fence is for and no one can answer. The Reformer lays out the cost and the absence of any visible use, the Keeper can say only that it has always been there, and the case to remove it is unanswerable because the benefit is a harm that has not happened. Closes when the fence comes down on good arguments.

**[The Return](plot_the_return.md)**
Cue: the fence is gone and the road is open. The danger the fence stood against, which never left and only waited, comes back through the gap, the Bystander is struck by a harm he never knew was near, and the Investigator reconstructs from the damage the reason the fence was raised. Closes when the harm returns and the reason is understood, too late.

**[The Inquiry](plot_the_inquiry.md)**
Cue: another fence, another reformer, but this time the Inquirer refuses to let it fall until its reason is found. The reason is hunted through the archive, and either recovered and weighed, or lost past finding while the fence waits. Closes when the reason is learned before the fence is touched ... or the demand to learn it is judged mere obstruction, and the fence comes down unexamined all the same.

## Stakes

Whether a safeguard is ever weighed against the harm it prevents, or only against the cost it plainly shows.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the visible cost, which anyone can measure, and away from the invisible benefit, which leaves no mark to measure. The production cannot make a prevented disaster show up in the ledger, so a working fence looks like pure obstruction right up until it is gone. It can only show that no one was reckless, that the reformer reasoned soundly and the keeper simply could not say why, and that the one thing that would have saved the road, learning the fence's reason before tearing it down, is a discipline slow enough, and often futile enough, that the sound and modern thing is always to clear the fence away.
