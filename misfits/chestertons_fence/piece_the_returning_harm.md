# Piece: The Returning Harm

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The danger back: the disaster the fence held, walking through the gap it left.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the field](place_the_field.md), now open, where the threat arrives in full.
Where the ordinary safe ground becomes the scene of the very harm the fence made unimaginable.

## Load Bearing

Without it the removal would look like a clean gain forever, and the fence would be proved worthless.
With it the fence is proved priceless, too late: the danger that only ever waited returns at whatever scale it always could, and the damage is the exact measure of the benefit the ledger could never show. The Returning Harm is load bearing because it is the latent disaster released (Chesterton), the mirror of the house's New Normal failure: there the harm crept in as the line drifted, here it returns all at once through the gap, and both were always latent, waiting only for the safeguard to give way.

## Apparent

Bad luck, or a threat no one could have foreseen.
Struck out of a clear sky, the harm looks like misfortune unrelated to the fence, so even its arrival can be misread as accident rather than consequence, unless someone traces it back to the gap.

## Yearbook

Suffered by [the bystander](persona_the_bystander.md) through [returning](process_returning.md), let in by the fall of [the reformer's case](piece_the_reformers_case.md).
It is the proof that came too late: the Returning Harm is what finally makes [the invisible benefit](piece_the_invisible_benefit.md) visible, and what supplies [the recovered reason](piece_the_recovered_reason.md) at the price of the damage.
