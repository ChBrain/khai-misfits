# Piece: The Forgotten Reason

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The lost why: the purpose the fence was raised for, real once and now unrecorded.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the archive](place_the_archive.md), in the thin or missing trail of why.
Where the reason should have been kept, and largely was not.

## Load Bearing

Without it the keeper would simply state the fence's purpose and end the argument.
With it the purpose survives only as a practice and not as a record, so it is real, load-bearing, and unsayable, present in what is done and absent from anything anyone can produce on demand. The Forgotten Reason is load bearing because it is the tacit-knowledge core of the misfit (Polanyi): we know more than we can tell, so the fence's justification can be entirely genuine and entirely unavailable, which is exactly the condition that lets a sound reformer conclude there is no justification at all.

## Apparent

Evidence that there was never a reason.
The absence of a stated purpose reads as the absence of a purpose, so a reason merely unwritten looks identical to a reason that never existed, and the fence is convicted on the silence where its defence should be.

## Yearbook

Lost through [forgetting](process_forgetting.md), leaving [the safeguard](piece_the_safeguard.md) mute.
It is the missing defence: the Forgotten Reason is what makes [the reformer's case](piece_the_reformers_case.md) unanswerable, and what [the recovered reason](piece_the_recovered_reason.md) must be dug back out to supply.
