# Plot: The Return

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The field](place_the_field.md), the fence gone and the road open.
The danger the barrier stood against, which never left and only waited, finds nothing now between it and the ground the fence had kept.

## Action

[Returning](process_returning.md): [The Bystander](persona_the_bystander.md), holding [the beneficiary's seat](position_beneficiary.md), is struck by a harm he never knew was near, while [The Investigator](persona_the_investigator.md), holding [the investigator's seat](position_investigator.md), reads the damage back to the gap and the gap back to the fence.

## Stage

[The Bystander](persona_the_bystander.md) tables [Take It for Granted](plan_take_it_for_granted.md) to the last, so [the returning harm](piece_the_returning_harm.md) meets him full, uncredited and unforeseen.
[The Investigator](persona_the_investigator.md) tables [Reconstruct the Reason](plan_reconstruct_the_reason.md), and from the wreckage assembles [the recovered reason](piece_the_recovered_reason.md), whole and obvious now, and too late to matter.

## Tension

The reason for the fence is never so clear as the morning after it is torn down.
The harm proves the fence's worth exactly by the measure of the damage, and the understanding everyone lacked in the review is suddenly free, purchased with the disaster the fence existed to prevent. It is the New Normal's failure mirrored: there the harm crept in as the line drifted, here it returns all at once through the gap, and both were latent all along, waiting only for the safeguard to give way.
