# Piece: The Invisible Benefit

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md). The disaster averted: the fence's whole gain, delivered as a harm that does not happen.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the field](place_the_field.md), in the quiet where nothing goes wrong.
Where the fence's work shows up as an ordinary, uneventful day.

## Load Bearing

Without it the fence could prove its worth and would never be removed.
With it the fence's benefit is a non-event, a bull that does not enter, a fall no one takes, and a non-event leaves no mark, no record, nothing to enter on a ledger against the fence's plain cost. The Invisible Benefit is load bearing because it is the latent function made literal (Merton): a practice serving a purpose no one observes, so that removing it for failing its manifest use destroys the hidden one, and it is the exact reason a working safeguard looks most useless when it is most needed.

## Apparent

Proof that the fence does nothing.
The steady absence of trouble reads as evidence there was never trouble to prevent, so the better the fence works the more plainly pointless it seems, and its success is indistinguishable, from the outside, from having no effect at all.

## Yearbook

Enjoyed by [the bystander](persona_the_bystander.md), who credits it to nothing, grown out of [the safeguard](piece_the_safeguard.md) doing its silent work.
It is the benefit that cannot testify: the Invisible Benefit is what leaves [the reformer's case](piece_the_reformers_case.md) with nothing to weigh against, until [the returning harm](piece_the_returning_harm.md) makes it visible at last.
