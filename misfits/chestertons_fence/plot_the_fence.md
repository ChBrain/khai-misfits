# Plot: The Fence

## Taxonomy

[Chesterton's Fence](play_chestertons_fence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The fence line](place_the_fence_line.md), a barrier across the road with its reason already gone from living memory.
It stands mended and upright, kept by hands that inherited the keeping and not the why.

## Action

[Forgetting](process_forgetting.md): [The Keeper](persona_the_keeper.md), holding [the custodian's seat](position_custodian.md), maintains the fence because it has always been maintained, while [The Bystander](persona_the_bystander.md), holding [the beneficiary's seat](position_beneficiary.md), passes it daily, safe and unaware that it keeps him so.

## Stage

[The Keeper](persona_the_keeper.md) tables [Leave It Standing](plan_leave_it_standing.md): keep the fence up and trust that it matters, though [the safeguard](piece_the_safeguard.md) now stands mute, [the forgotten reason](piece_the_forgotten_reason.md) stripped from the practice.
[The Bystander](persona_the_bystander.md) tables [Take It for Granted](plan_take_it_for_granted.md), enjoying [the invisible benefit](piece_the_invisible_benefit.md) he credits to nothing, so the fence works perfectly and can prove nothing.

## Tension

The fence is doing its job, and that is exactly why no one can see the job.
Nothing is wrong here: the barrier holds, the field is quiet, the danger stays out of sight. That is the trap loading, a safeguard succeeding so completely that its success erases the memory of its purpose, standing sound and fully unexplained, waiting for the first person to ask what it is for and find no one who can say.
