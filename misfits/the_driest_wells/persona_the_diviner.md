# Persona: The Diviner

## Taxonomy

[The Divining](position_the_divining.md). Locates the well by the thirst rather than the reward, sinking it where the need is deepest against the pull of viability, and pays for the answered thirst with the ground that would have paid.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Directed by need. Digging against the slope. Willing to sink where it does not pay.
Is the one who reads the map of thirst rather than the map of coin, and sends the well to the hardpan, staffing the grim ground and holding it open against the reward that would draw it elsewhere.
No saintliness wished on them: the divining is not a gift for finding water but a discipline of choosing need over viability, undertaken in the knowledge that the well they sink will never turn the return the market would ask of it.

## Action

Sinks the well where the thirst is deepest, on ground the trade would never choose, and keeps it there by subsidy and direction against the slope that would carry it back to reward.
Forgoes the paying ground to staff the grieving one, sending scarce hands to the places that grind them and funding wells that will not return, so care is held to need rather than allowed to follow coin.
Does what setting the match right requires: pays, in reward foregone and effort spent uphill, for a provision aimed at thirst, and finds the pull of viability and the fuller reach of the well-watered always working to undo it.

## Shadow

Not a serenity, but the exhaustion of holding a thing uphill that wants to roll back, and that is the weight: the well sunk against the slope does not stay sunk by itself, so every gain must be re-won, the reward foregone re-forgone, the drift back to the paying ground resisted again and again with no end at which the holding can stop.
What gnaws is that the water they bring is invisible as an achievement. A thirst answered where none expected an answer leaves no monument, since the need it met was the need that read as low, so they spend themselves against the slope for a provision no ledger records as a gain, the absence of a crisis their only and unnoticed proof.
The bind is interior and tiring: to hold care over the thirst they must fight the plainest sense of everyone who sinks where it pays, so they are forever the one asking scarce hands to go where it grinds and scarce coin to fund what will not return, and the moment they tire the well drifts back down the slope, the correction undone by the same gravity it was always resisting.

## Tell

Says "put the well where the thirst is, not where the digging is easy."
Is the whole remedy and the whole cost in one line, since the thirst is on the hard ground and the easy digging is on the watered, so the sentence that states the right rule states, in the same breath, why no slope will follow it on its own, the correction sound and forever uphill, true and never self-sustaining.
