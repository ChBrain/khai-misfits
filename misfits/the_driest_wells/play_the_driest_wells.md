# Play: The Driest Wells

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Driest Wells.
Care is a well, and a well is sunk where the sinking pays and the ground gives way, where there are hands to work it and coin to keep it and firm earth to build on. That is only sense: a provider settles where they can practise and be sustained, where the conditions are good and the labour bears fruit. But the places where the ground is easy and the coin is ready are the places already watered, the healthy, the moneyed, the pleasant, and the places of deepest thirst, the poor and the sick and the hard, are the places where the digging is grim and the returns are thin, so the wells cluster where the thirst is shallow and stay unsunk where it runs deep. The match runs backwards: care is most available where it is least needed and scarcest where it is needed most, not because anyone aimed it there but because it flowed downhill along the slope of reward, and the slope of reward runs opposite to the slope of need. And where a well is shared, the ones with the longest reach and the loudest voice draw the most from it, so even a common source is drained first by those with the least thirst, the practised and the confident taking a fuller share of a fixed supply than the parched who most need it. Nobody withheld the water. The Welldigger sank where they could work, the well-watered drew what they could reach, and the thirst of the hardpan called in a currency the market does not hear. The wells are driest where the thirst is deepest, the sum of every sound decision about where care could be sustained, laid backwards over the map of where care was needed.

## Arc

The bend is this: the availability of good care varies inversely with the need for it, because provision follows the slope of reward and reward runs opposite to need, so care is most available where it is least needed, an inversion that runs more completely the more care is exposed to the market (Tudor Hart).
It runs on a supply that follows reward, not need.
Because care settles where it can be sustained, where there is coin and ease and firm ground, the provider sinks the well where the work is viable, which is the sound and necessary condition of practising at all, so supply gathers along the slope of reward (Tudor Hart). The choice is right for each provider, and it is a choice about where they can work, not about who most needs them.
And the slope of reward runs opposite to the slope of need.
Because the places rich in coin and ease are the places already well, and the places of deepest need are poor and hard and grim, following reward is following away from need, so the same gathering that is sound for each provider composes, across the whole, a provision laid backwards over the map of thirst (Tudor Hart; the social gradient, Marmot). The inversion is not anyone's aim but the shape the sum takes.
And a shared source is drawn first by the longest reach.
Because those with means and voice and knowledge navigate a common supply better than those without, even care held in common is drawn more fully by the advantaged, whose need is less, so the disadvantaged use less than their need would warrant of the very pool meant to serve all (Le Grand; the Black Report). The capture is not a theft but a difference in reach, and it widens the gap inside the shared well.
And exposure to the market runs the inversion to completion.
Because the slope of reward is steepest where care is bought and sold, the inverse match is sharpest under market forces and gentler where provision is shielded from them, so the law operates more completely the more care is exposed and less completely the more it is held to need (Tudor Hart). The market does not invent the inversion, it perfects it, and shelter from it is what holds the well over the thirst.
And the thirst calls in a currency the slope cannot hear.
Because need without means makes no pull on a supply that answers reward, the deepest thirst draws the fewest wells, its call real and unregistered, so the hardpan is served least precisely where it is parched most, the need that should command provision commanding none (Tudor Hart; Marmot). The silence is not indifference but a mismatch of currencies, need speaking in one and supply hearing another.
None of it is a wrong. The Welldigger sinking where they can be sustained, the well-watered drawing what their reach affords, the market pricing care as it prices anything: each is sound, and the inversion emerges from the sum, no malice and no neglect required. Sink the well where the thirst is, hold care to need against the slope of reward, and the match is set right (the deliberate direction, care shielded from the market and staffed to the hardpan): but that means forgoing the ground that pays for the ground that grieves, sending scarce hands to the grim places and subsidising the wells that will never turn a return, against the pull of viability and the fuller reach of the well-watered, so the one move that answers the deepest thirst is a standing effort uphill against reward, and the reckoning is a care that pools where it is least needed, or a hand that must dig where it does not pay.

## Stakes

Whether care can be held to need, or must pool where it is least needed by the plain slope of a reward that runs opposite to thirst.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the inversion, which the Welldigger is right to sink where they can be sustained and the well-watered right to draw what their reach affords and the market right to price care as it prices anything, and away from the deliberate direction that would answer the deepest thirst at the cost of the ground that pays. The production cannot make the inversion a wrong, because each provider's choice of where to practise is sound and a shared source is drawn by reach as any shared source is, so settling where the work is viable and drawing what one can reach are each only sensible acts. It can only show that a supply which follows reward follows away from need, that the wells therefore cluster where the thirst is shallow and thin where it runs deep, and that the one move which sets the match right, sinking the well where the thirst is against the slope of reward, means forgoing the ground that pays and digging where it does not, a standing effort uphill that the pull of viability and the fuller reach of the well-watered are always undoing, so the care pools where it is least needed not by anyone's malice but by the ordinary good sense of everyone who sinks a well where it can be kept.
