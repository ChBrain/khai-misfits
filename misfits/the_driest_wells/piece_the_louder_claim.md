# Piece: The Louder Claim

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The greater reach of the advantaged on a shared source, the means and voice and knowledge that draw a fuller share of a common well than the deeper thirst can.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Drawn at a common well, where [the Greenlands](place_the_greenlands.md) and [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md) share one source, and counted at [the Census](place_the_census.md) where the draw is read.
Flows as the reach that turns an equal entitlement into an unequal draw, the fuller pull of those who know how to pull.

## Load Bearing

Without the Louder Claim the inversion would end at the sinking of wells: it is the Louder Claim that carries the same backward slope inside a shared source, so that even care held for all is drawn more by those who need it less.
The Louder Claim is load bearing because it is the demand-side half of the inversion, the reach that lets the advantaged take a fuller share of a common well while the parched take less than their need would warrant (Le Grand; the Black Report). Its being reach and not rule is the point: no one is barred from the shared source, and it is drawn all the same by those with the means and voice and knowledge to draw it, so the capture is not a theft to be caught but a difference in reach that a common entitlement does nothing to close, the well open to all and taken by those who can take it.

## Apparent

Just people using what is offered them, making good use of a shared thing.
Seen at the surface, the Louder Claim looks like nothing but engagement, the well-served drawing on a source that is theirs to draw, and the truth that their fuller draw is the parched's thinner one, that a common well is emptied first by the least thirsty, that equal offer and unequal reach make an unequal share, stays hidden behind how reasonable a person drawing on their own entitlement looks.

## Yearbook

Drawn by the well-watered whose reach is longest, suffered by [the Parched](persona_the_parched.md) who take less than their need, and read or hidden at [the Census](place_the_census.md) by which currency it counts.
It is the demand-side of the backward map: the Louder Claim is what [crowding](process_crowding.md) works through, the reach that drains a shared well toward the least thirst, the fuller draw that leaves [the Parched](persona_the_parched.md) the thinner one even where the source was held in common.
