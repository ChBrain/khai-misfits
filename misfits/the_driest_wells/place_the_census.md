# Place: The Census

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The vantage where need and provision are counted together, the tally that could reveal the inversion, and that reads deep thirst as low demand when it counts wells drawn instead of need felt.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A tally of care given and care used, the ledger of who was served.
Shown as fairness measured: the numbers of wells and draws are set down plainly, the record of provision offered and taken, looking like nothing but an honest account of where care went.

## Holds

Holds the two maps that, laid together, show the match run backwards.
The Census is where the map of provision could be set over the map of need and the inversion seen, the wells counted against the thirst, so it holds the one view from which the whole slope is visible, the place the correction must read before it can be made.

## Offers

Offers the truth of the inversion, and the means to hide it.
Here the two maps can be compared, so the Census offers the plain sight of care pooling where it is least needed, and by the measure it chooses it offers, just as easily, concealment: count wells drawn and the hardpan reads as low demand, count thirst felt and the same hardpan reads as deepest need, the same ground served or failed depending on which currency the tally keeps.

## Withheld

Withholds which measure it is keeping.
What the Census hides is its own choice of currency: a count of care used reads unmet thirst as sufficiency, since the parched who cannot reach a well draw little and so appear to want little, so unless the tally measures need rather than use it will report the inversion as balance, the deepest thirst booked as low demand and the backward map read as a fair one.
