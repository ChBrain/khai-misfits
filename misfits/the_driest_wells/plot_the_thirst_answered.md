# Plot: The Thirst Answered

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md), someone willing to sink where it does not pay.
A reader of the map of thirst rather than the map of coin, moving to place a well on the ground the trade would never choose.

## Action

[Sinking](process_sinking.md): the Diviner puts the well where the thirst is deepest and holds it there by subsidy and direction, against the slope that would carry it back to reward.

## Stage

[The Diviner](persona_the_diviner.md) sinks the well on [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md), on [Sink Where the Thirst Is](plan_sink_where_the_thirst_is.md), shielding it from [the open market](piece_the_open_market.md) and staffing the grim ground, setting right the stretch of [the backward map](piece_the_backward_map.md) that [the gradient](piece_the_gradient.md) had laid wrong.
The water is real, and the well leans always toward the slope it was pulled from.

## Tension

Answering the deepest thirst means digging where it does not pay and holding, without end, what reward is always drawing back.
The remedy is real: place the well by need, shield it from the market, staff it against the slope, and the hardpan is served. But the well on unrewarding ground does not stay by itself, and the water it brings leaves no monument, since the crisis it prevents is the one that never comes, so the plot closes with the choice laid bare and no rest in it: hold the well over the thirst by a standing effort uphill, funding what will not return and sending hands where it grinds, or let the effort tire and watch the well drift back down the slope to the reward it came from, the match set right only for as long as someone spends to keep it so.
