# Process: Draining

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The flow of care toward reward and away from thirst, the gathering of provision along the slope so it pools where the ground pays and thins where it does not.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Welldigger](persona_the_welldigger.md), across [the Greenlands](place_the_greenlands.md) and [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md), sinking each well where it can be kept.
Begins whenever provision is free to settle by viability, the sum of many sound sitings drawing care toward the ground that rewards it.

## Direction

Turns a supply free to settle into a supply pooled at reward.
Because each well is sunk where the work will last, and lasting means coin and ease and firm ground, the provision gathers where reward is, so draining converts the freedom to place care into a flow that carries it toward the well-watered and away from the parched (Tudor Hart).

## Lever

The condition of a well that lasts.
Because a well survives only where it is rewarded, draining works through the plain requirement of viability: care cannot stay where it cannot be kept, so the lever is the same necessity that makes each siting sound, the flow toward reward being nothing but the sum of wells sunk where they will hold.

## Echo

Care pooled where the thirst is shallow, thin where it runs deep.
The echo of draining is a provision gathered on the paying ground and absent from the hardpan, so the map of care comes to run opposite to the map of need, the flow toward reward having laid, without anyone aiming it, a supply backwards over the thirst.
