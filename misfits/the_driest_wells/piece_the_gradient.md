# Piece: The Gradient

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The slope of reward that care runs down, sound for each provider to follow and running opposite to need, the tilt that carries provision toward the paying ground.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Runs from [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md) up to [the Greenlands](place_the_greenlands.md), the tilt care flows along, steepest where [the open market](piece_the_open_market.md) exposes it.
Flows as the pull of viability, the direction every well is drawn when it is sunk where it can be kept.

## Load Bearing

Without the Gradient there is no inversion, only wells placed at random: it is the slope of reward that gives the flow a direction, and the direction runs opposite to need.
The Gradient is load bearing because it is the tilt that makes each sound siting compose a backward map, the reward that keeps a well alive being found on the ground already watered, so following it is following away from thirst (Tudor Hart). Its soundness is the whole mechanism: no provider is wrong to follow reward, since reward is what sustains a well, so the Gradient is not a bias to be corrected in any single choice but the plain condition of viability, and because that condition sits on the well-watered ground, the sum of every provider following it lays care backwards over need, the slope right at every step and perverse in its total.

## Apparent

Just where care can be sustained, the sensible pull of viable ground.
Seen at the surface, the Gradient looks like nothing but good practice, provision gathering where it can last, and the truth that the ground which sustains a well is the ground that least needs one, that following the slope is following away from thirst, that the pull which keeps each well alive is the pull that misplaces them all, stays hidden behind how plainly a provider settling where they can work resembles care going where it should.

## Yearbook

Followed by [the Welldigger](persona_the_welldigger.md) as the condition of practising, fled by [the Diviner](persona_the_diviner.md) who digs against it, and unfelt by [the Parched](persona_the_parched.md) whose ground offers it nothing.
It is the tilt that lays the map backwards: the Gradient is what [draining](process_draining.md) flows along and [sinking](process_sinking.md) fights, the slope [the open market](piece_the_open_market.md) steepens and [the backward map](piece_the_backward_map.md) is the print of, the pull that carries every well toward reward and away from the thirst.
