# Persona: The Parched

## Taxonomy

[The Thirst](position_the_thirst.md). Stands on the hardpan with the deepest need and the fewest wells, whose thirst calls in a currency the slope of reward cannot hear, so their need draws the least of the care it most requires.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

In need, and unanswered. Thirstiest where the wells are fewest. Calling in a currency the market does not price.
Is the one whose need is greatest and whose provision is thinnest, not because they were refused but because care flowed toward reward and their ground offered none.
No fecklessness wished on them: the under-use of care is not indifference to their own thirst but the plain difficulty of reaching a well that was never sunk near them, the distance and the cost and the thin knowledge all standing between a real need and a supply that answers a different call.

## Action

Takes what little care reaches the hardpan, making do with the well that is far and the hours that are short, since the provision that answers reward does not answer them.
Uses less than their thirst would warrant, not for want of need but for want of a well within reach, so their real and heavy need reads, in the ledger of demand, as a low call.
Does what the underserved do: bears the thirst that no supply is coming for, stretches the thin water they can reach, and finds their deepest need counted as small because it is voiced in a currency the slope does not register.

## Shadow

Not resigned, but worn by a need that goes unregistered, and that is the ache: their thirst is real and heavy and makes no pull, so they are not refused so much as unheard, their deepest need reading as low demand precisely because they cannot reach the well that would answer it.
What wears on them is being counted as the author of their own thirst. The care that never came reads, from the comfortable distance of the well-watered, as care they did not seek, so the under-use forced by distance and cost and thin knowledge is mistaken for a lack of need, and they carry the quiet insult of having their unmet thirst named as low demand.
The bind is interior and mute: to be served they would need the pull that means and voice and nearness give, and those are the very things their thirst has left them without, so the deeper the need the fainter the call, and they cannot make their thirst heard in the one currency the slope answers, want of care and want of the means to summon it the same lack.

## Tell

Says "we manage; we do not ask for much."
Is the plain speech of a thirst that has learned not to expect a well, and is heard as a statement of low need, so the sentence that reports a need gone unanswered is taken as evidence there was little need, the parched counted as undemanding precisely because the demand they cannot voice is the demand no supply came for.
