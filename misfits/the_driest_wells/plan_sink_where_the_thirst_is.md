# Plan: Sink Where the Thirst Is

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Divining](position_the_divining.md), inside [The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Divining](position_the_divining.md)

## Direction

Read the map of thirst rather than the map of coin, and sink the well where the need is deepest, on ground the trade would never choose, holding care to need against the slope of reward (Tudor Hart; Starfield). Shield the well from the market that would draw it away, staff the grim ground and fund the well that will not return, since only provision aimed at need answers the deepest thirst. Hold it there against the pull of viability and the fuller reach of the well-watered, and accept that the correction is never done once but must be re-won for as long as it is to last, a standing tax in reward foregone and effort spent uphill, its whole achievement the invisible one of a thirst that did not go unanswered.

## Orders

([The Diviner](persona_the_diviner.md) sinks the well where the thirst is deepest, forgoes the paying ground to staff the grieving one, and holds the placement against a slope that is always drawing care back to reward.)

## Implementation

([The Census](place_the_census.md) reads the need; through the [sinking](process_sinking.md) process, against the [draining](process_draining.md) it resists; the Diviner shields the well from [the open market](piece_the_open_market.md) and places it by need, setting right the section of [the backward map](piece_the_backward_map.md) that reward had laid wrong.)

## Targets

- [ ] the well is placed by the depth of the thirst, not the reward of the ground
- [ ] the well is shielded from the market that would draw it back
- [ ] the paying ground foregone and the grim ground staffed are accepted as the cost
- [ ] the correction is held as a standing effort, re-won against the slope, never done once
