# Process: Sinking

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The placing of a well by need against the slope, the remedy that holds care over the thirst by subsidy and direction, and must be renewed against a reward always drawing it back.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Diviner](persona_the_diviner.md), at [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md), sinking the well where the thirst is deepest rather than where the ground pays.
Begins when someone reads the map of need and places care against the slope, holding it on ground the trade would never choose.

## Direction

Turns a supply that follows reward into one held to need.
Because a well can be sunk on unrewarding ground when subsidy and direction keep it there, placing care by thirst overrides the slope, so sinking converts a provision that would gather at reward into one aimed at need, the well held on the hardpan against the pull that would carry it to the greenlands (Tudor Hart; Starfield).

## Lever

The force that holds a well off its slope.
Because the correction runs against reward, sinking works only by spending, subsidy to keep a well that will not return and direction to send hands where they would not go, so the lever is a deliberate, funded resistance to the gradient, the well held over the thirst by exactly as much effort as the slope exerts to draw it away.

## Echo

A thirst answered, and a hold that can never be let go.
The echo of sinking is a well on the hardpan where none would have come, its water real and its achievement invisible, but the same slope it resists is always pulling, so the moment the subsidy tires or the direction lifts the well drifts back to reward, the correction never finished but only, for as long as it is held, sustained.
