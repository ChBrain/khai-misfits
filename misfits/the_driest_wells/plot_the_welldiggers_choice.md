# Plot: The Welldigger's Choice

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Greenlands](place_the_greenlands.md) and [the Hardpan](place_the_hardpan.md), a provider deciding where to sink.
A well to be placed, one ground firm and rewarding and one grim and thirsty, and the plain need to choose where a practice can hold.

## Action

[Draining](process_draining.md) at its source: the Welldigger weighs viability and sinks where the ground gives way, the drylands failing every test that keeps a well alive.

## Stage

[The Welldigger](persona_the_welldigger.md) sinks the well on the greenlands, on [Sink Where It Pays](plan_sink_where_it_pays.md), following [the gradient](piece_the_gradient.md) to the ground that will sustain the work, and the hardpan, thin of coin and hard of earth, goes undug.
The choice is right for the digger and away from the thirst, and there is no other choice that keeps the well alive.

## Tension

Sinking where the well will hold is the only responsible act, and the only responsible act is the one that leaves the thirst unanswered.
The digger cannot fault their reasoning: a well on the hardpan would fail, and a failed well serves no one, so viability is not a preference but the condition of practising at all. But the ground that sustains a well is the ground that least needs one, so the plot closes with the well soundly sunk and the deepest thirst still dry, the digger's prudence and the inversion the same act, care placed where it can last because it cannot last where it is wanted.
