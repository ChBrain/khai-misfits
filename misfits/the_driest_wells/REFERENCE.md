# The Driest Wells: Reference

The research warrant for [The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md).
The dynamic is the inverse care law: Tudor Hart's finding that good care varies inversely with need and more so under
market forces, Le Grand's capture of universal provision by the advantaged, the Black Report's and Marmot's social
gradient of need, and Starfield's case for provision distributed to need, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the inverse care law as a **supply-follows-reward-not-need structure**: care settles where it can be sustained,
where there is coin and ease and firm ground, but that ground is the ground already well, so following the slope of
reward is following away from need, and the wells gather where the thirst is shallow and thin where it runs deep. The
play models the documented mechanics, the reward gradient each provider soundly follows, the shared source the advantaged
draw more fully by their longer reach, the market exposure that steepens the slope, and the deliberate direction that
alone holds care to need, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a greedy doctor or a feckless
patient. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: the availability of good medical care tends to
vary inversely with the need of the population served, and this inverse care law operates more completely where care is
most exposed to market forces and less completely where that exposure is reduced, so provision is most available where it
is least needed by the plain slope of a reward that runs opposite to thirst.

It is distinct from the house's The Head Start, where an early advantage compounds over time so that success breeds
success along one axis: there the trap is temporal, a lead that widens by accumulation, while here the trap is a static
cross-axis inversion, two distributions, provision and need, laid over each other and running opposite at any given
moment, not a gap that grows with time but a map printed backwards. The advantaged reach on a shared source, the demand
side of this misfit, brushes the Matthew effect the Head Start stages, but the engine here is not compounding return, it
is a supply that answers reward while need speaks in a currency the slope cannot hear, so the deepest need draws the
least provision at once and not by any widening lead.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the provider as greedy or
  the underserved as feckless, because the source finding is precisely that no malice and no neglect is needed: the
  provider sinks the well where it can be sustained, which is the plain condition of practising at all, and following the
  ground that keeps a well alive is following away from the thirst without anyone aiming it there (Tudor Hart). The
  advantaged draw more of a shared source not by a rule that favours them but by a longer reach, and the underserved use
  less than their need would warrant because distance and cost and thin knowledge stand between them and a well, not
  because they want little (Le Grand; the Black Report). So the misfit is that a supply which follows reward follows away
  from need, and care pools where it is least required, no greed and no refusal required, the inversion the sum of sound
  sitings and unequal reach, and the doctor who settles where they can work is as reasonable as the patient who takes
  what reaches them.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a match set right by good will alone. The one move that
  answers the deepest thirst is to sink the well where the need is and hold it there against the slope, shielding it from
  the market and staffing the grim ground, but that means forgoing the ground that pays for the ground that grieves,
  subsidising wells that will never return and sending scarce hands where the work grinds them (Tudor Hart; Starfield).
  The correction is never finished, because the pull of viability and the fuller reach of the well-watered are always
  drawing care back down the slope, so the well over the thirst must be re-won for as long as it is to last, its water
  real and its achievement invisible, the crisis it prevents the one that never comes. The reckoning is a care that
  pools where it is least needed, or a standing effort uphill that tires and lets the wells drift back to reward, not a
  distribution that answers need and sustains itself.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the inverse care law and its sharpening under
  market forces are Tudor Hart's, the capture of universal provision by the advantaged is Le Grand's, the social gradient
  of health and need is the Black Report's and Marmot's, and the case for primary care distributed to need is
  Starfield's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages a supply that follows reward away from
  need and credits that no one was a villain and the backward map is the sum of sound choices.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Backward Map](piece_the_backward_map.md)**: carries the inverse care law whole (Tudor Hart), the availability of
  good care varying inversely with need, the pattern of provision laid backwards over the pattern of thirst.
- **[The Gradient](piece_the_gradient.md)**: carries the slope of reward (Tudor Hart), the pull toward viable ground that
  each provider soundly follows and that runs opposite to need.
- **[The Open Market](piece_the_open_market.md)**: carries the market-exposure clause (Tudor Hart), the dial that steepens
  the inversion where care is bought and sold and gentles it where care is shielded.
- **[The Louder Claim](piece_the_louder_claim.md)**: carries the capture of a shared source by the advantaged (Le Grand;
  the Black Report), the longer reach that draws a fuller share of a common well than the deeper thirst can.
