# Process: Crowding

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The drawing of a shared source first by the longest reach, so the well-watered take a fuller share of a common well than the parched whose need is greater.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The well-watered, at a well held in common, whose means and voice and knowledge give them the longer reach.
Begins wherever care is shared rather than sunk by ground, the advantaged drawing on a common source more readily than the disadvantaged can.

## Direction

Turns a common supply into an unequal draw.
Because those with reach navigate a shared well better than those without, the advantaged take more of it though their thirst is less, so crowding converts a source meant for all into one drained first by the least thirsty, the disadvantaged using less of it than their need would warrant (Le Grand; the Black Report).

## Lever

The difference in reach.
Because means and voice and knowledge turn a common entitlement into an actual draw, crowding works through the gap in who can reach: the same source is open to all and taken by those who know how to take it, so the lever is not a rule that favours the advantaged but a reach that does, the well equally offered and unequally drawn.

## Echo

A shared well drained by the least thirsty, the parched left with less.
The echo of crowding is a common source that reproduces the inversion inside itself, the well-watered drawing a fuller share and the parched a thinner one, so even care held for all runs the same way as care sunk by ground, toward the reach and away from the need.
