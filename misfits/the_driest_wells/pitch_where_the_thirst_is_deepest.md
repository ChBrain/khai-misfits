# Pitch: Where the Thirst Is Deepest

## Taxonomy

[The Driest Wells](play_the_driest_wells.md). The single hook: care follows the slope of reward, and reward runs opposite to need, so the well is sunk where the thirst is shallow and left unsunk where it runs deep, care most available exactly where it is least required.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Care as a resource that settles where it can be sustained.
The tenor is professional sense: a well is sunk where the ground is firm and the work is viable, where there is coin to keep it and hands to work it, exactly as any provision sensibly gathers where it can be maintained.

## Undertow

The ground that pays is the ground already watered.
Underneath, the places rich in coin and ease are the places already well, and the places of deepest thirst are poor and hard and grim, so following reward is following away from need, and the wells cluster where the thirst is shallow while the hardpan of deepest need goes undug.

## Nerve

The thirst calls in a currency the slope cannot hear.
The nerve is that need without means makes no pull on a supply that answers reward, so the deepest thirst draws the fewest wells, its call real and unregistered, and even a well held in common is drawn first by the longest reach, the well-watered taking a fuller share of a fixed source than the parched whose need is greater.

## Echo

The only way to answer the deepest thirst is to dig where it does not pay.
What lingers is that setting the match right means sinking the well against the slope, forgoing the ground that pays for the ground that grieves, staffing the grim places and subsidising the wells that will never return, a standing effort uphill that the pull of viability is always undoing, so the care pools where it is least needed unless someone holds it, at cost, to the thirst.
