# Persona: The Welldigger

## Taxonomy

[The Trade](position_the_trade.md). Sinks the well where the sinking pays and the ground gives way, the sound condition of practising at all, and so settles along the slope of reward, away from the hardpan that needs them most.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Practical. Settling where the work can be sustained. Choosing ground, not choosing against need.
Is the provider who sinks where there is coin to keep the well, firm earth to build on, and conditions they can bear, because a well that cannot be maintained serves no one.
No greed wished on them: the choice of viable ground is not a turning from thirst but the plain requirement of practising anywhere, since a provider who settles where the work destroys them is a provider soon gone, and the thin grim ground offers no way to stay.

## Action

Sinks the well where the ground is good, weighing coin and conditions and the chance of a practice that lasts, and finds the drylands failing every test that keeps a well alive.
Settles on the slope of reward not by aiming away from need but by aiming toward viability, so the same care that would answer the hardpan gathers instead where it can be sustained.
Does what a provider must: chooses ground they can work, builds where the well will hold, and leaves the parched undug not from refusal but because the place of deepest thirst is the place least able to keep a well, its need and its inhospitality the same fact.

## Shadow

Not callous, but pulled two ways by a conscience the ground will not let them follow, and that is the ache: they know the hardpan needs them more, and they cannot build a life or a lasting practice on ground that offers nothing to build on, so the pull of viability and the pull of need run opposite and only one is survivable.
What gnaws is that their soundest reasoning is what does the harm. Every consideration that keeps a well alive, coin, conditions, the chance to stay, points away from the thirst, so they cannot fault their own choice and cannot escape its sum, the care that gathers where they can work being care that gathers away from where it is wanted.
The bind is interior and unresolved: to serve the deepest thirst they would have to sink where they cannot last, and a well that fails helps no one, so the very responsibility that makes them sink where the well will hold is the responsibility that leaves the hardpan dry, their prudence and the inversion the same act seen from two sides.

## Tell

Says "I would love to work there; I just could not make it work there."
Is the whole slope in one sentence, the first half the conscience and the second the ground, so the words that mean to express a real willingness are the exact account of why the willingness never lands, the provider drawn to the thirst and held by the plain impossibility of keeping a well on ground that offers nothing to keep it with.
