# Persona: The Simplifier

## Taxonomy

[Simplifier](position_simplifier.md). The one who trims the regulator's repertoire for cost and legibility, folding many cases into one rule and coarsening the categories, each cut a real economy that reads as better governance.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Prudent. Cost-aware. An enemy of clutter.
Is the one who streamlines the controller, the manager who consolidates the rules, the architect who collapses the special cases, the auditor who wants a menu short enough to read.
No recklessness in it: the Simplifier is doing good and ordinary work, cutting waste and making the lever legible, and every cut is defensible on its own terms.

## Action

Looks at a wide repertoire, sees cost and clutter, and trims it: fewer responses, coarser categories, one rule where there were many, a menu short enough to audit.
Cuts what nothing is currently arriving to need, so each cut lands cleanly with nothing breaking and the console visibly tidier.
Does what a simplifier does: it lowers the count of distinct responses, and lowers it by exactly the economies that make the lever cheaper and more legible.

## Shadow

Not a vandal, a careful economiser whose every cut is locally right, and that is the whole difficulty: the trim that removes real cost also removes real variety, and the two cannot be separated by looking at the console.
Because a disturbance that is not arriving costs its response nothing to keep and everything to explain, cutting it reads as pure economy right up until it arrives, so the Simplifier lowers the repertoire toward the disturbance variety one defensible step at a time, seeing the saving plainly and the shortfall not at all, the crossing invisible because it shows only when the world does the trimmed thing.
It cuts clutter in good faith, and cutting clutter is exactly what cannot tell a needless response from a rarely needed one until the need comes.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "do we really need all these," and is usually right.
Says it of a repertoire whose extra states earn nothing while their disturbances stay away, and cannot see that the same states are the only thing standing between the outcome and the day those disturbances return.
