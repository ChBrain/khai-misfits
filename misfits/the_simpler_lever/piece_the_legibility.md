# Piece: The Legibility

## Taxonomy

[The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md). The apparent virtue of the simpler lever, the reading by which a shorter, coarser controller looks better governed, the deceptive metric that rises as the control it measures falls.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read at [the console](place_the_console.md), where the trimmed menu shows tidier and tighter.
Doubted, if ever, at [the model](place_the_model.md), where the count behind the tidiness is checked.

## Load Bearing

Without it a cut to the repertoire is just a loss of variety, and no one mistakes it for an improvement.
With it the trap is baited: legibility is the surface reading that a simpler controller is a better-governed one, a shorter menu easier to audit and a coarser category easier to read, so each cut that lowers the variety raises the very sign by which the controller is judged sound (Beer, on attenuation making a system legible to its controller, the reduced view that is easier to govern for holding less variety). The Legibility is load bearing because it is the deceptive virtue: the metric that moves the wrong way, reading a repertoire as better the smaller it gets, so the piece carries the reason the trimming feels like progress, the tidiness that stands in for control while draining it.

## Apparent

Good governance, a clean and auditable and well-run controller.
Seen at the surface, the Legibility looks like exactly the thing to want, a lever short enough to read and govern and defend, and the truth that it measures how readable the controller is and not how much of its world it can still hold, that it climbs as the repertoire is cut toward the field, stays hidden behind how much a legible controller looks like a well-controlled one, right up until the slip.

Read across [the console](place_the_console.md) as [the simplifier](persona_the_simplifier.md) trims through [simplifying](process_simplifying.md), doubted only by [the engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) at [the model](place_the_model.md).
