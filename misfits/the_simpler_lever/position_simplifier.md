# Position: Simplifier

## Taxonomy

[The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md). The seat that trims the repertoire for cost and legibility, where a cut that removes real cost removes real variety and the two cannot be told apart on the console.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A wide repertoire that is costly to run and the standing to cut it: a set of responses expensive to maintain and the authority to consolidate them.
A real economy: fewer responses are cheaper, coarser categories are more legible, and one rule for many cases is easier to govern.
No way to see the shortfall on the console, because a response earns nothing while its disturbance stays away.

## Orders

Trim the menu.
Fold many cases into one rule, coarsen the categories, cut the responses nothing is currently arriving to need, and make the lever cheaper and more legible.
Lower the count of distinct responses, since each cut is a defensible saving.

## Loses

The control the repertoire was carrying, exactly by the cut that read as pure economy.
Because a disturbance that is not arriving costs its response nothing to keep and everything to explain, the seat trims toward the disturbance variety one defensible step at a time, seeing the saving and not the shortfall, until the responses fall below the disturbances and the outcome the trimmed states were holding slips when their disturbances return.
It gains a tidier console and a tighter-looking governance, and loses the variety that was the only thing holding the outcome against the day the world does the trimmed thing.

## Drives

Toward the cost cut and the lever made legible,
the saving plain and the shortfall invisible,
so the seat that trims the menu lowers the repertoire toward the world, and the crossing shows only when a disturbance arrives that the cut removed the answer to.
