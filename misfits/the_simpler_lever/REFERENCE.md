# The Simpler Lever: Reference

The research warrant for [The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md).
The dynamic is the law of requisite variety: Ashby's finding that only variety can destroy variety, Conant and
Ashby's theorem that every good regulator must be a model of its system, and Beer's variety engineering of
amplification and attenuation, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the law of requisite variety as a **count of responses against disturbances**: a regulator holds an
outcome steady only while it commands at least as much variety as the environment it faces, at least one distinct
response for each distinct disturbance, because only variety can absorb variety. The play models the documented
mechanics: the repertoire of responses whose variety must be requisite to the disturbance variety, the field that
throws its full range indifferent to the controller, the simplification that is locally rational because a wider
repertoire is costly to run and harder to govern, the legibility by which a trimmed lever reads as better-governed
even as its variety falls, the shortfall the requisite-variety inequality opens when the responses drop below the
disturbances so that some pass uncontrolled, and the loss of control that follows though no component fails, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a broken part or an operator's mistake. The governing
finding of the sources is the play's governing law: regulation is a relation between two counts of states, and
where the regulator's variety falls below the environment's the outcome can no longer be held, the control lost by
a subtraction of responses and not by any failure. The remedy is the sources' own: match the variety, amplify the
regulator's repertoire or attenuate the incoming variety so the pair meets the law, and it is real and never free.

It is distinct from the house's abstraction and modelling misfits, and the two are close enough that the cut must
be exact: there a map or report sheds a checkable particular as detail is dropped, and the loss is one of
representational fidelity, the model no longer faithful to the terrain it stands for; here the loss is control
capacity, a shortfall of responses against disturbances, and what fails is regulation and not representation, the
outcome no longer held rather than a picture grown coarse. The regulator does model the disturbances it counters,
and the play uses that (Conant and Ashby), but the misfit's reckoning is the outcome slipping, not a map misread.
It is distinct, too, from the house's bottleneck and utilisation misfits about throughput and queues: there the
system is congested, work piling at a constraint or a server saturated by load; here nothing is congested and
nothing queues, the regulator simply has too few distinct states to match what the field throws, so the failure is
a missing response and not a full one, a matter of variety and not of capacity or wait.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the Simplifier as
  reckless or the Regulator as negligent, because the source finding is precisely that no one errs: the
  simplification is a real economy, cheaper to run and easier to govern, and the outcome does hold at the moment of
  every cut; the regulator runs exactly as designed and every response it keeps still works; the environment throws
  only the range it always could, with no malice and no aim at the gap. The loss is authored by no one and emerges
  from a count, the responses cut below the disturbances, so the misfit is that the variety that holds an outcome
  is the same variety that is costly and illegible, and a controller reads as better-governed the more it is
  trimmed toward the world.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that holds the
  outcome is to match the variety, amplify the repertoire so it answers each disturbance again or attenuate the
  incoming variety with a filter so the field throws fewer, and it means rebuilding the cost and clutter the
  simplification removed, adding a whole apparatus upstream, and accepting as complication what read, simplified, as
  good governance. The reckoning is an outcome held only by paying, continuously, to carry as much variety as the
  world can throw, the averted slip leaving no trace while the complication is felt at once, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the law of requisite variety and the finding that only variety can
  destroy variety are Ashby's, the theorem that every good regulator must be a model of its system is Conant and
  Ashby's, and variety engineering by amplification and attenuation is Stafford Beer's. The honest boundary, that
  the law is an inequality about counts of states and that simplification is safe where the environment's variety is
  low or a matching attenuator is paired with the simple regulator, is carried as carefully as the finding itself:
  Ashby's law is a necessary condition for regulation, not a recipe, meeting variety being required for control and
  not sufficient for good control.
