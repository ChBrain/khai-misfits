# Process: Simplifying

## Taxonomy

[The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md). The Simplifier cutting the repertoire for cost and legibility, the defensible economy that lowers the count of responses and cannot be told from the loss of control it carries.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A wide repertoire that is costly to run and hard to govern.
At [the console](place_the_console.md), the simplifier folding many cases into one rule, coarsening the categories, and cutting the responses nothing is currently arriving to need.

## Direction

Toward the menu shortened and the lever made legible.
Because a response earns nothing while its disturbance stays away, simplifying removes it as clutter and the console grows tidier with nothing breaking, so the count of distinct responses falls a defensible step at a time toward the disturbance variety, each cut a real saving and none of them showing the shortfall it opens.

## Lever

The invisibility of a shortfall not yet needed.
Because the disturbances that need the trimmed responses are not arriving at the moment of the cut, simplifying runs on real economy and not on carelessness, so the narrowing repertoire is assembled from sound decisions, each removing a cost that is plain and a variety that is not, the crossing hidden because it shows only when the world does the trimmed thing.

## Echo

A menu shortened, and a variety quietly lowered.
The simplifier has cut real cost and made the lever legible, so what reads as tighter governance is, in truth, the repertoire falling toward the field, the control undiminished on the surface and already thinner beneath, waiting on the disturbance the cut removed the answer to.
