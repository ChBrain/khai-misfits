## Taxonomy

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no regulator and no variety to meet the world with.
With it the trap has its lever: the repertoire is the count of distinct responses the regulator can make, and requisite variety is the finding that this count must be at least the disturbance variety for the outcome to be held, so the repertoire is the exact quantity a simplification lowers (Ashby, on requisite variety, the regulator needing as much variety as the disturbances it would counter). The Repertoire is load bearing because it is the thing that is cut: the variety that holds the outcome and the variety that is costly to run are the same variety, so the piece carries the set of responses whose every trim is at once a real economy and a lowering of the only count that keeps the outcome held.

## Apparent

Clutter, a bloated menu, a controller more complicated than it needs to be.
Seen at the surface, the wide Repertoire looks like waste, more responses than any tidy system should carry, and the truth that its width is set by the world it faces and not by any excess of design, that each extra state is the answer to a disturbance not currently arriving, stays hidden behind how much a wide repertoire looks like clutter when its disturbances are away.
