# Persona: The Regulator

## Taxonomy

[Regulator](position_regulator.md). The one who holds the outcome steady, meeting each disturbance the field throws with a response from its repertoire, so the essential variable stays within bounds.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Steady. Reliable. The controller that keeps the thing where it belongs.
Is the one who holds the outcome, the governor on the engine, the thermostat, the ops rota, the policy that answers each case, meeting disturbance with response and keeping the variable in bounds.
No heroics in it: the Regulator does what a regulator does, cancelling each disturbance with the matching state, and asks only for a repertoire wide enough to have an answer ready.

## Action

Reaches into its repertoire for the response that cancels the disturbance in front of it, and holds the outcome by having a distinct state for each distinct thing the world can do.
Runs exactly as designed, every response it keeps still working, and asks nothing of the disturbance but that it be one the repertoire can still answer.
Does what a regulator does: it matches responses to disturbances, and holds the outcome only so far as its variety reaches.

## Shadow

Not the author of its own limit, a controller that can only ever hold an outcome as wide as the repertoire it is given, and that is the whole difficulty: the Regulator cannot answer a disturbance for which no state remains, however faithfully it runs.
Because the trimmed responses were cut when nothing was arriving to need them, the Regulator meets the crossing without warning, reaching for a state that is no longer there and finding the disturbance passing through uncancelled, the control gone though it never faltered and every response it kept still works.
It runs perfectly to the last, and running perfectly is exactly what cannot conjure a state the repertoire no longer holds.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "I have a response for that," and does, until the day it does not.
Says it while the repertoire still covers the field, and cannot tell from the inside that a cut has dropped it below the world, because the missing response shows only when its disturbance arrives.
