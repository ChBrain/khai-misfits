# Piece: The Loss of Control

## Taxonomy

[The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md). The phenomenon whole, the outcome no longer held and its variety no longer bounded, regulation gone though no component failed and every response kept still works.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Suffered at [the console](place_the_console.md), where the outcome slips with the controller running as designed.
Averted, if ever, at [the model](place_the_model.md), where the variety is matched before the crossing.

## Load Bearing

Without it the shortfall is a number on a bench and nothing has actually slipped.
With it the trap is complete and authorless: the loss of control is the outcome breaking its bounds because a disturbance arrived with no matching response, the regulation gone by a shortfall of states and not by any breakage, so a controller that models fewer disturbances regulates fewer, and the ones it no longer models it can no longer hold (Conant and Ashby, on every good regulator being a model of its system, regulation failing exactly where the model does; Beer, on the system overwhelmed by a variety it cannot match). The Loss of Control is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole: not a fault but a floor breached, the outcome's variety no longer bounded because the regulator's no longer reaches it, so the piece carries the whole slip, the control lost silently at the cut and shown only when the world does the trimmed thing.

## Apparent

A sudden failure, a controller that unaccountably stopped holding, something that must have broken.
Seen at the surface, the Loss of Control looks like a breakage, a system that was working and then was not, and the truth that nothing broke, that every response still works and the regulator runs as designed and simply has no state for what arrived, that the control was given away at the cut and only expressed at the slip, stays hidden behind how much an outcome breaking its bounds looks like a fault rather than a shortfall of variety.
