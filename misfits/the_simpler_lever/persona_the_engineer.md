# Persona: The Engineer

## Taxonomy

[Engineer](position_engineer.md). The variety engineer who would match variety before the outcome slips, amplifying the repertoire or attenuating the disturbance until the pair meets the law, at a cost that reads as needless complication.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Deliberate. Willing to add cost. Suspicious of a lever that looks too simple for its world.
Is the one who counts the disturbances against the responses, the systems engineer who sizes the controller to its environment, the designer who builds the filter upstream.
No love of complexity in it: the Engineer does not want a wide repertoire for its own sake, only a repertoire wide enough, and knows the width is set by the world and not by taste.

## Action

Restores the requisite variety: amplifies the repertoire so it carries a response for each disturbance again, or attenuates the incoming variety with a filter that reduces the disturbances the regulator must meet, so the pair together commands enough.
Sizes the controller to the field it faces, treating the disturbance variety as the measure and the repertoire as the thing that must meet it.
Does what matches variety: it raises the regulator's count or lowers the disturbance's, until the law is satisfied and the outcome can be held again.

## Shadow

Not a free corrective, a costly rebuild of the very cost the simplification removed, and that is the whole difficulty: matching variety means paying back the clutter that was trimmed, and paying it reads as undoing good governance.
Because amplifying rebuilds the responses the Simplifier cut and attenuating adds a whole apparatus upstream and screens off disturbances the field used to let through, the Engineer's remedy costs continuously and shows no averted slip, so against a lever that looked better-governed for being simple the match reads as needless complication, the disturbance it would have caught leaving no trace of the outcome it would have moved.
It sizes the controller to the world in good faith, and sizing it to the world is exactly what a console trimmed for legibility cannot be made to look like.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "count the disturbances before you cut the responses."
Says it into a room that can see the cost of the wide repertoire and not the cost of the narrow one, and is heard, often, as adding complication to a lever everyone agreed was cleaner for being simple.
