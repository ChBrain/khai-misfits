# Plot: The Trim

## Taxonomy

[The Simpler Lever](play_the_simpler_lever.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Console](place_the_console.md), a wide repertoire costly to run and hard to govern.
The Simplifier seeing cost and clutter in the many responses and setting out to make the lever legible.

## Action

[Simplifying](process_simplifying.md): [The Simplifier](persona_the_simplifier.md), holding [the simplifier's seat](position_simplifier.md), folds many cases into one rule, coarsens the categories, and cuts the responses nothing is arriving to need.

## Stage

[The Simplifier](persona_the_simplifier.md), on [Trim the Menu](plan_trim_the_menu.md), lowers [the repertoire](piece_the_repertoire.md) a defensible step at a time, each cut read through [the legibility](piece_the_legibility.md) as a cleaner, better-governed lever.
The console grows tidier and nothing breaks, the [variety](piece_the_variety.md) quietly falling toward the field's with the saving plain and the shortfall unseen.

## Tension

Each cut is a real economy, cheaper to run and easier to govern, and each cut lowers the only count that holds the outcome.
There is nothing careless: the trimmed responses earn nothing while their disturbances stay away. But a cut that removes cost removes variety, and the two cannot be told apart on the console, so the plot closes with the menu shortened and the lever more legible, the repertoire fallen closer to the field and the crossing still invisible.
