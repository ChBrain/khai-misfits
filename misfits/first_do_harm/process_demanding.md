# Process: Demanding

## Taxonomy

[First Do Harm](play_first_do_harm.md). The pressure that something be done, the call to intervene that makes forbearance read as negligence and the cure the responsible move.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A real complaint and a claim that it be met.
At [the theatre](place_the_theatre.md) and around it, the petitioner pressing that a condition this real cannot be left alone.

## Direction

Toward intervention made the safe move.
Because a harm allowed is visible and a harm caused by a well-meant cure is hidden, demanding reads the forbearing healer as having failed and the treating one as having done their duty, so the pressure falls always toward the cure, and the remedy is given because not giving it looks like a dereliction, the demand to act sound and the something demanded the thing that wounds.

## Lever

The fairness, on its face, of calling for a response.
Because doing nothing about a genuine complaint is often wrong, demanding a cure is proper on its face, so the process runs not on panic but on a reasonable claim, the forced intervention assembled from no unreason, only from a fair demand to act that cannot see the hidden harm and gives no credit for the wound a stayed hand would spare.

## Echo

A cure demanded, and the stayed hand condemned.
The petitioner has rightly asked the complaint be met, so what reads as due concern is, in truth, the pressure that forces the cure that wounds, the visible complaint met and the hidden harm unweighed, the one move that would break the cascade made to look like negligence.
