# Persona: The Healer

## Taxonomy

[Healer](position_healer.md). The one who applies the genuine remedy in good faith to the presenting complaint, competent and caring, whose sound treatment reaches past the condition and wounds what it had left whole.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Competent. Caring. Meaning to help.
Is the one who administers the remedy, the physician, the reformer, the fixer, who treats the complaint that presents with a treatment that genuinely works.
No quackery in it: the Healer is skilled and sincere, the remedy is real, and the intent is nothing but to ease the thing that hurts.

## Action

Reads the complaint, applies the remedy that treats it, and eases the presenting condition competently and in good faith.
Does the demanded, responsible thing, treating what asks to be treated with a cure that works on it.
Does what a good healer does: it treats the complaint, and the treatment that eases the complaint reaches, doing exactly what it does, past it.

## Shadow

Not a quack, a competent and sincere healer whose remedy truly works, and that is the whole difficulty: the cure is real, and a cure real enough to treat the complaint is powerful enough to harm what the complaint had spared.
Because the remedy does exactly what it does, it reaches past the presenting condition and wounds a sound function the condition never touched, and because that wound arrives after the treatment and looks nothing like it, the Healer does not trace it to their own cure, so treats the wound as a fresh complaint with a second remedy that wounds again, each intervention competent and caring and none of them ever seen as the hand that harmed.
It treats the complaint in good faith, and treating it is what wounds the sound part the disease had left whole.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "the treatment worked, and now there is this new problem to address."
Says it having genuinely treated the complaint, and does not see, because the wound wears a fresh face, that the new problem is the shadow of the cure they gave.
