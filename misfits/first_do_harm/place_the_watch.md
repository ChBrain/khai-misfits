# Place: The Watch

## Taxonomy

[First Do Harm](play_first_do_harm.md). Where the hand is stayed, watchful waiting, the place a complaint is tolerated and weighed rather than treated, the one arrangement in which the cure's harm is not added.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place a complaint is watched and not yet treated, a room of watchful waiting, a review that weighs the cure against the condition before acting.
The Steward observing the complaint, tracing where a remedy would reach, and holding the hand where the treatment would cost more than the disease.

## Holds

The forbearance, and the sound part it spares.
Here the complaint is weighed rather than treated, so the Watch holds the point where the cascade is not fed: a condition tolerated because the cure would wound more than it eases, and a wound traced back to the remedy that would make it, so it is where the first rule is kept, the one arrangement in which the hand is stayed and the sound part the cure would reach past is left whole.

## Offers

A way to keep the first rule, and the sound part the cure would have wounded.
Because the Watch weighs the hidden harm against the visible complaint and forbears where the cure costs more, it offers the one thing the Theatre cannot, a complaint met without a new wound made, so it hands out the sound part spared and the cascade unfed, and hands it out at the price of a real complaint left standing and a healer read as having failed to act.

## Withheld

Any visible proof that the forbearance was right.
The Watch can spare the sound part but cannot show the wound it prevented, because a harm not caused leaves nothing to point to and the complaint it tolerates stays plainly there to be blamed, so it withholds the evidence that would vindicate the stayed hand, exacting the look of negligence continuously and returning only a wound no one sees, and, all kept rule and no visible reward, it withholds the thanks the treating healer is given, until restraint is read as failure and the cure is called for after all.
