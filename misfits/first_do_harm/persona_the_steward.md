# Persona: The Steward

## Taxonomy

[Steward](position_steward.md). The one who would stay the hand, trace the wound to the remedy, and tolerate the complaint rather than trade it for a new harm, at the price of being read as negligent for the harm not caused.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Patient. Watchful. Willing to be blamed for doing nothing.
Is the one who counsels restraint, the clinician of watchful waiting, the reformer who leaves the working thing alone, the one who asks whether the cure costs more than the complaint.
No fatalism in it: the Steward does not deny the complaint or refuse all treatment, only weighs the harm the cure will do against the harm it will ease.

## Action

Weighs the remedy's hidden harm against the complaint's visible one, traces the wound back to the cure that made it, and forbears where the treatment would cost more than the condition.
Lets a real complaint stand rather than trade it for a new wound, and breaks the cascade by not adding to it.
Does what keeps the first rule: it stays the hand, and staying the hand is the one move that stops the cure from wounding the sound.

## Shadow

Not a fatalist, a weigher of harms who will bear the blame for the one not caused, and that is the whole difficulty: forbearing is the move that breaks the cascade, and forbearing is exactly what reads as negligence.
Because a harm allowed is visible and a harm caused by a well-meant cure is hidden, the Steward who lets the complaint stand is seen to have failed while the healer who treats and wounds is thanked, so the restraint that alone keeps the first rule is exacted against the demand to act and rewarded rarely, the wound it prevents leaving no trace and the complaint it tolerates plain for all to blame.
It stays the hand to spare the sound, and staying it is read as the failure to do the thing that harms.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "this is better tolerated than treated, and the cure would cost more than the disease."
Says it knowing it will be heard as negligence, and cannot show the wound it prevented, because a harm not caused leaves nothing to point to.
