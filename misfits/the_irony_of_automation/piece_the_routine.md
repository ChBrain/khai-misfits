# Piece: The Routine

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The frequent, easy work automated, which was also the practice that kept the operator's skill.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where it is done, and where the machine takes it over from the hand that once did it.
Missed at [the simulator](place_the_simulator.md), where its rehearsal must be manufactured on purpose once the machine has it.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing to automate and no practice to lose, and the trap does not start.
With it the whole irony has its root: the routine is the obvious thing to mechanise, and it is also the operator's daily rehearsal, so automating it improves the system and ends the practice in the same act. The Routine is load bearing because it is the thing that played both parts (Bainbridge; Carr): the work the machine does better and the exercise that kept the human sharp, so the piece carries the fusion of task and training that makes an improvement into an erosion.

## Apparent

Mere drudgery, the dull repetitive part best handed to a machine.
Seen plainly, the routine looks like exactly what automation is for, boring work no one should have to do, and the truth that the same repetition was the practice keeping the operator's hand in, that removing it removes the skill, stays hidden behind how obviously worth automating the dull work appears.

## Yearbook

Taken by [the automation](persona_the_automation.md) through [automating](process_automating.md), the work that was also rehearsal.
It is the double loss: the Routine is what the machine improves and the operator stops practising, so its automation breeds [the rust](piece_the_rust.md) in the hand, and its rehearsal is what [the drill](piece_the_drill.md) must later supply by hand.
