# Position: Trainer

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The seat that keeps the operator's hand in against the handover, and pays for it in the automation's savings.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The knowledge that the operator will one day have to take over, and the means to keep them able.
The levers of the remedy: drills, simulator time, real spells at the controls, in-the-loop design.
A standing cost, and a readiness that can never be fully owned.

## Orders

Keep in practice.
Drill the skills the automation no longer exercises, hand control back before the crisis, and keep the human engaged.
Maintain the competence the machine was meant to make unnecessary.

## Loses

The efficiency argument, every day the machine does not fail.
Because practice costs the savings the automation was bought for, engages a human who is not strictly needed, and never quite matches the real rare case, the seat cannot prove its drills were worth the cost, so it is forever pressed to cut and can never close the gap the automation keeps reopening.
It buys back a readiness the machine keeps eroding, and holds it only while it keeps paying.

## Drives

Toward an operator kept ready at the cost of the automation's promise,
right in a way no calm day can show,
so the competence that catches the failure is the hardest cost to justify keeping.
