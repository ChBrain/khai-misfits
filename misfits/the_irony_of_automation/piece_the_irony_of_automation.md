# Piece: The Irony of Automation

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The phenomenon whole: a system improved into unreadiness, and its remedy in a competence paid for out of the efficiency it undercuts.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Born at [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where the routine is taken, and at [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), where the human erodes.
Sprung at [the handover](place_the_handover.md), and answered only at [the simulator](place_the_simulator.md).

## Load Bearing

Without it the other pieces are parts without the mechanism that binds them.
With it the whole trap stands in one object: a routine that was also rehearsal, a skill that rusts and an attention that drifts, a residue too hard to automate, a handover of the worst case to the least ready hand, and a drill that alone keeps the hand in. The Irony of Automation is load bearing because it is the structure entire (Bainbridge): not a failure of the human or the designer but the built-in consequence of a genuine improvement, correctable only by spending back part of the efficiency the automation promised.

## Apparent

A story of human error, or of a machine not yet automated enough.
Seen from outside, the irony looks like an operator who failed or a system that just needs the last cases automated too, and the truth that automating more only leaves a still harder residue to a still less practised hand, that the fault is structural and not personal, that the cure costs the automation's own savings, stays hidden behind how easily the blame lands on the human and how appealing more automation sounds.

## Yearbook

The trap entire, staged across the run.
The Irony of Automation is [the routine](piece_the_routine.md) taken until [the rust](piece_the_rust.md) and [the drift](piece_the_drift.md) set in, the [cold seat](piece_the_cold_seat.md) the next exception springs, and the unreadiness that only [the drill](piece_the_drill.md), paid for out of the efficiency it undercuts, can hold at bay.
