# Piece: The Cold Seat

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The worst case thrown to the least ready operator at the worst moment, the erosion come due.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the handover](place_the_handover.md), where control reverts and the rusty, drifted operator meets the hardest problem cold.
Foretold at [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), in every quiet shift that dulled the hand and the eye.

## Load Bearing

Without it the erosion would be a latent weakness that never came due, and the trap would stay theory.
With it the whole irony lands at once: the exception the automation could not handle arrives, control snaps back, and the operator, rusted and drifted, faces the single hardest task in the system with no warm-up. The Cold Seat is load bearing because it is the trap's outcome made real (Sarter and Woods): the mismatch of the worst case with the least ready hand, so the piece carries the crisis in which all the quiet unfitting is paid for in one moment.

## Apparent

Human error, an operator who froze or fumbled when it counted.
Seen from the wreckage, the cold seat looks like the failing of the person in the chair, and the truth that they were handed the hardest case by a machine that had spent months eroding their skill and attention, that anyone so unpractised and disengaged would fare no better, that the setup made the outcome, stays hidden behind how squarely the blame falls on the last hand on the controls.

## Yearbook

Delivered by [the exception](persona_the_exception.md) through [reverting](process_reverting.md), the crisis the calm was building toward.
It is the erosion come due: the Cold Seat is where [the rust](piece_the_rust.md) and [the drift](piece_the_drift.md) are paid for at once, the residue left by [the designer](persona_the_designer.md) meeting the hand the automation cooled, and the outcome that only [the drill](piece_the_drill.md) could have warmed against.
