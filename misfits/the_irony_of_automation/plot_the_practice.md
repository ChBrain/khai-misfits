# Plot: The Practice

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Simulator](place_the_simulator.md), the last cold handover still fresh and someone moving to keep the hand in.
A push to drill the skills the automation idled and rehearse the crisis before it is real.

## Action

[Training](process_training.md): [The Trainer](persona_the_trainer.md), holding [the trainer's seat](position_trainer.md), drills the operator on the manual task and the rare case, while [The Automation](persona_the_automation.md), holding [the automation's seat](position_automation.md), keeps idling the skill the moment each drill ends.

## Stage

[The Trainer](persona_the_trainer.md), having tabled [Keep in Practice](plan_keep_in_practice.md), puts the operator back through the manual work and the handover, and asks that control be handed back before the crisis, not in it.
[The Operator](persona_the_operator.md), leaving [Watch and Wait](plan_watch_and_wait.md) for the drill, sharpens again, while the cost of the practice, and the pull to trim it, mounts on every day the machine does not fail.

## Tension

The one thing that keeps the hand in is the thing efficiency always argues to cut.
There is no easy fix here: the drill plainly keeps the operator ready, yet it costs the very savings the automation was bought for, engages a human not strictly needed, and never quite matches the real rare case. So the play closes where it must, the operator kept in practice and ready for the handover ... or the calm returns, the drill is trimmed as waste, and the hand goes cold again against the next exception.
