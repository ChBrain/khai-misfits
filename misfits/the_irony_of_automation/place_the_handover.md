# Place: The Handover

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). Where control passes back to the operator when the exception hits, the worst case meeting the least ready hand.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The moment the automation reaches its limit and gives control back: an alarm, a disengagement, the system suddenly the human's again.
An operator jolted from monitoring into command, facing an abnormal state it did not see building, asked to fly by hand what the machine could no longer fly at all.

## Holds

The crisis and the mismatch at its heart.
Here the hardest problem in the system arrives at the same instant as the least prepared operator, so the handover holds the whole trap sprung: the residue too hard to automate delivered cold to a hand rusted and an attention drifted, the exact collision the calm had been building toward.

## Offers

Control, at the one moment the operator is least able to use it.
Because the handover returns the system to the human precisely when the automation cannot cope, it offers command in crisis to someone unpractised and out of the loop, so it is where the cost of all the quiet erosion comes due at once, the seat handed back too late to be ready for it.

## Withheld

The warm-up, the warning, and the time.
The handover cannot ease the operator in gently, because the exception is sudden and severe, so it withholds any run-up to the crisis, delivering the worst case with no rehearsal and no margin, the seat asked to be expert the instant it is handed the controls.
