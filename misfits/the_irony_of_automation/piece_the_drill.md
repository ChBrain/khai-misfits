# Piece: The Drill

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The deliberate practice and in-the-loop design that keep the hand in, at the cost of the automation's savings.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the simulator](place_the_simulator.md), where the skills the automation idled are exercised on purpose.
Set against [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), whose quiet efficiency argues every day to stop drilling.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play has no remedy, only an operator eroded to the handover every time.
With it the misfit is answered, and answered at a price: the practice the floor no longer provides, manufactured deliberately, plus a machine designed to keep its human engaged. The Drill is load bearing because it is the one correction the structure allows and the hardest to keep (Bainbridge): it costs the very efficiency the automation was bought for, engages a human not strictly needed, never fully matches the real rare case, and must be repeated because the automation keeps idling the skill, so the piece carries both the exit and its cost.

## Apparent

An indulgence, expensive practice for a failure that hardly ever comes.
Seen on the calm days, the drill looks like waste, drilling a skill the machine has made unnecessary and rehearsing a crisis that may never arrive, and the truth that it is the only thing keeping the operator able to catch what the machine drops, that its cost buys a readiness no quiet shift can show, stays hidden behind how plainly the automation runs fine without it.

## Yearbook

Run by [the trainer](persona_the_trainer.md) through [training](process_training.md), the practice kept up on purpose.
It is the exit and its cost: the Drill is what holds back [the rust](piece_the_rust.md) and the [drift](piece_the_drift.md), supplies the rehearsal that [the routine](piece_the_routine.md) no longer gives, and warms the hand against [the cold seat](piece_the_cold_seat.md), for exactly as long as its cost is paid.
