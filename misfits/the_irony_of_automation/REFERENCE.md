# The Irony of Automation: Reference

The research warrant for [The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md).
The name is Bainbridge's; the play runs on the human-factors account of automation, from her ironies through
Parasuraman and Riley's work on complacency and Sarter and Woods on the out-of-the-loop operator, all cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the ironies of automation as a **control system**: a system in which automating the routine work,
which is a real improvement, removes the practice that kept the operator's skill and the engagement that kept
the operator's attention, so that when the rare case the automation cannot handle finally arrives and control
reverts to the human, that human is rusty, out of the loop, and facing the hardest problem in the system with
no warm-up. The play models the documented mechanics, the routine that is both the work automated and the
rehearsal that kept the skill, the deskilling of a hand no longer practised, the vigilance decrement of a
monitor watching a system that almost never fails, the exception too hard or rare to automate that is left to
the human, the handover of the worst case to the least ready operator, and the practice and in-the-loop design
that alone keep the hand in, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of lazy
operators or careless designers. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law:
automating the easy, frequent tasks leaves the human the hard, rare ones while stripping away the practice and
attention needed to do them, so the more capable and reliable the automation, the less able the operator it
depends on in the moment it fails. It is distinct from the house's The Bus Factor, where knowledge is
concentrated in one person and lost when they leave: here the operators remain, but the tool itself has eroded
their skill and attention across the board. And it is distinct from the house's The Brittle Optimum, where
structural slack is deliberately trimmed: here nothing is knowingly cut, the competence fades as a side effect
of a genuine improvement, and the failure is specifically the human handover, not a structural shatter.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the operator as
  lazy or the designer as careless, because the source finding is precisely that each move is right:
  automating the routine is sound, the machine really is better at it, freeing the human from drudgery is a
  gain, no one can stay vigilant on a system that almost never fails, and no skill stays sharp with nothing to
  practise on. The unreadiness is authored by no error and emerges from the structure, a routine that was also
  the rehearsal and a residue too hard to automate, so the misfit is that a system improved exactly as it
  should be leaves its human worse at the one moment it needs them best.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that keeps the
  hand in is to drill the operator, hand control back before the crisis, and design the machine to keep its
  human engaged, and it costs the very efficiency the automation was bought for, engages a human who is not
  strictly needed, can never fully replace the practice the machine now does, and no drill quite matches the
  real rare case. The reckoning is a competence retained by deliberately spending some of the automation's
  savings, not a free correction, so the cure for a hand gone cold is to keep paying to warm it against a
  failure that may not come.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the ironies of automation, the deskilling and vigilance
  literatures, automation complacency, and the out-of-the-loop operator are theirs, and the condition the trap
  actually requires, automation of the routine that removes both the practice and the attention the residual
  task needs, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

  the least ready operator at the worst moment.

  design that keep the hand in, at the cost of the automation's savings.

  system improved into unreadiness, and its remedy in a competence paid for out of the efficiency it undercuts.
