# Process: Atrophying

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The operator's skill fading and attention drifting, both eroding as the machine runs well.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An operator reduced to monitoring a system that rarely fails.
At [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), the human with nothing to practise and almost nothing to track, watching the automation run itself.

## Direction

Toward a hand gone cold and an eye gone elsewhere.
Without the routine to exercise the skill, the hand loses its sharpness, and without failures to demand attention, the mind drifts out of the loop, so atrophying drives both erosions together, the operator growing less able and less engaged the longer and better the automation runs.

## Lever

The absence of practice and the absence of demand.
Because a skill decays with nothing to work it and vigilance decays with nothing to catch, the very smoothness of the automation is what erodes the operator, so atrophying works not by any failing of the human but by the reliability of the machine, which removes the two things that had kept them ready.

## Echo

An expert backup quietly turned rusty and distracted.
The operator is still in the chair and still called the backup, but the skill and the focus that made the title true have faded, so what looks like a system safely overseen is, unseen, a human being unfitted for the handover that will one day come.
