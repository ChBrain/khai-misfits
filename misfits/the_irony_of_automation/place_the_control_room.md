# Place: The Control Room

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). Where the operator sits and watches the machine run, the skill rusting and the attention drifting.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

An operator at a console, overseeing an automation that runs the routine without needing them.
Screens green, nothing to do, the hand idle and the mind wandering, a professional set to monitor a system that almost never asks anything of it.

## Holds

The making of the unready operator, hour by quiet hour.
Here the human does not practise and is barely required to watch, so the control room holds both erosions at once: the skill fading for lack of exercise and the attention drifting for lack of demand, the exact place where a capable operator is slowly turned into a rusty, disengaged one.

## Offers

Rest, and the appearance of a job well covered.
Because the machine runs smoothly and the operator is present, the control room offers the reassuring picture of a system overseen, so it hands out, shift by uneventful shift, the calm that is also the slow unfitting of the one who must step in when the calm breaks.

## Withheld

The practice and the demand that would keep the operator ready.
The control room cannot exercise a skill the machine now performs, nor hold an attention on a system that gives it nothing to track, so it withholds the very things that made the operator an expert, leaving them present but progressively less able the longer the watch runs quiet.
