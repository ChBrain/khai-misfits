# Place: The Floor

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). Where the work is done, taken over by the machine, and where the operator once practised by doing.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The operational work of the system, the routine that keeps it running, now performed by the automation.
Where a human hand once did the task and learned it in the doing, a machine now runs it faster and cleaner, and the human has stepped back to watch.

## Holds

The routine, and the double role it played.
Here the frequent work is done, so the floor holds both the task the automation improves and the rehearsal the operator loses, the one place where doing the job and staying able to do it were the same act, now split apart as the machine takes the doing and leaves the human none.

## Offers

Better, faster work, and relief from the drudgery.
Because the floor is where the routine runs, and the machine runs it well, it offers a genuine gain, fewer slips and less toil, so it hands the system a real improvement, the same improvement that quietly ends the operator's practice.

## Withheld

The doing that kept the operator's hand in.
The floor cannot let the human practise a task the machine now performs, because the work is the machine's, so it withholds the daily rehearsal that had kept the skill sharp, taking the drudgery and the competence away together.
