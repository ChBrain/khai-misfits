# Plot: The Drift

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Control Room](place_the_control_room.md), the operator set to monitor a system that almost never fails.
Screens green hour after hour, nothing to catch, the attention with nothing to hold it.

## Action

[Atrophying](process_atrophying.md): [The Operator](persona_the_operator.md), holding [the operator's seat](position_operator.md), watches a machine that rarely errs, while [The Automation](persona_the_automation.md), holding [the automation's seat](position_automation.md), runs so reliably it offers nothing to stay alert for.

## Stage

[The Operator](persona_the_operator.md), still on [Watch and Wait](plan_watch_and_wait.md), keeps the watch as asked, but a watch with nothing to catch cannot hold, so its trust grows and its tracking slips.
[The Automation](persona_the_automation.md), on [Take the Routine](plan_take_the_routine.md), gives no failures to sharpen the attention, so the operator settles out of the loop, no longer following the state closely enough to step in fast.

## Tension

The more reliable the machine, the less the human can watch it.
There is no negligence here: humans simply cannot stay vigilant on a system that hardly ever does anything wrong, and trusting a reliable machine is reasonable. But that very reliability erodes the attention it relies on, so the plot closes with the operator disengaged and out of the loop, present at the console and no longer truly tracking the system it is there to guard.
