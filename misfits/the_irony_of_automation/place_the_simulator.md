# Place: The Simulator

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). Where the operator's hand is kept in by drill, the skills the automation no longer exercises practised on purpose.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The operator put back through the manual task and the rare case, on the rig and in real spells at the controls, off the automation.
The skills the machine has taken exercised deliberately, the handover rehearsed before it is real, a hand kept warm against the day it will be needed cold.

## Holds

The remedy, and the whole price of it.
Here the practice the floor no longer provides is manufactured on purpose, so the simulator holds the one thing that keeps the operator ready, and holds with it everything that argues against it: the time and cost, the human engaged though not strictly needed, the drill that never quite matches the real rare case.

## Offers

A hand kept in, at the cost of the automation's savings.
Because the simulator drills the skills the machine has idled, it offers the operator a competence the automation keeps eroding, so it is where readiness is bought back, paid for in the very efficiency the automation was meant to deliver.

## Withheld

A readiness that keeps itself, and a match to the real thing.
The simulator cannot make the practice free or permanent, because the automation keeps idling the skill the moment the drill ends, and no rehearsal fully equals the true rare case, so it withholds any lasting or complete fix, offering only a readiness held for as long as the drilling is paid for and never quite equal to the crisis.
