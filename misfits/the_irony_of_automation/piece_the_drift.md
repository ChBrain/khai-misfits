# Piece: The Drift

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The vigilance decrement, the monitor's attention and engagement eroding on a system that rarely fails.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), where the watch grows slack on a machine that almost never errs.
Confronted at [the handover](place_the_handover.md), where the drifted operator must suddenly be fully in the loop.

## Load Bearing

Without it the operator would at least be watching closely and could step in fast, and the handover might hold.
With it the human is out of the loop: a person set to monitor a reliable system cannot hold their attention on it, so trust grows and tracking fades, and the operator no longer knows the state well enough to take over quickly. The Drift is load bearing because it is the second of the two ironies (Parasuraman and Riley): vigilance decays with nothing to catch, so the piece carries the disengagement that leaves the operator not just unskilled but unaware, slow to notice and slower to understand the failure when it comes.

## Apparent

An inattentive operator, someone who should simply have been paying closer attention.
Seen at the handover, the drift looks like a lapse of focus anyone could be faulted for, and the truth that humans cannot stay vigilant on a system that rarely fails, that the very reliability of the machine produces the inattention, that the disengagement was designed in, stays hidden behind how easily a distracted monitor invites the blame.

## Yearbook

Set in by [atrophying](process_atrophying.md), the attention fading with the demand gone.
It is the eye's half of the erosion: the Drift is what the smooth run of [the automation](persona_the_automation.md) breeds, the out-of-the-loop state that meets [the cold seat](piece_the_cold_seat.md) at the handover, and the disengagement that only being kept in the loop by [the drill](piece_the_drill.md) holds off.
