# Piece: The Rust

## Taxonomy

[The Irony of Automation](play_the_irony_of_automation.md). The deskilling, the operator's manual skill fading for want of the practice the automation took.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the control room](place_the_control_room.md), where the idle hand loses its sharpness shift by shift.
Held back only at [the simulator](place_the_simulator.md), where deliberate drill keeps the skill from fading.

## Load Bearing

Without it the operator would take over as capably as ever, and the handover would hold.
With it the human is diminished: a skill kept only by doing decays once the doing is the machine's, so the hand that could once fly the whole flight forgets. The Rust is load bearing because it is the first of the two ironies (Bainbridge): automating the routine removes the practice, and a skill without practice fades, so the piece carries the atrophy that leaves the operator, through no fault of their own, less able than the system assumes when it hands back control.

## Apparent

An out-of-practice operator, a lapse of individual diligence.
Seen at the handover, the rust looks like a human who should have kept their skills up, and the truth that the automation removed the only means of keeping them, that no one holds a skill with nothing to practise on, that the diminishment was built by the tool, stays hidden behind how naturally a rusty operator invites the blame.

## Yearbook

Set in by [atrophying](process_atrophying.md), the skill fading with the practice gone.
It is the hand's half of the erosion: the Rust is what the loss of [the routine](piece_the_routine.md) breeds, the unreadiness that meets [the cold seat](piece_the_cold_seat.md) at the handover, and the fading that only [the drill](piece_the_drill.md) holds back, at a cost.
