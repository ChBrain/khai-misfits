# Piece: The Excess

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The harm above the ceiling, free to the actor and uncompensated to the victim, the part of the harm that runs past everything the actor owns.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Born at [the scene](place_the_scene.md), where the harm is sized past the assets.
Left uncollected at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), where the judgment reaches the ceiling and the excess stands above it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the harm fits under the ceiling and the actor faces its whole cost, so the care and the recovery meet the harm and nothing goes free.
With it the trap has its stakes: the part of the harm above the actor's assets, real and counted at the scene, is free to the one who causes it because it will never pay, and lost to the one who bears it because no judgment can reach it (Shavell, on damages above the injurer's assets, the harm that cannot be internalised). The Excess is load bearing because it is the region the whole production abandons: the actor does not guard it, the court cannot collect it, the victim is not made whole for it, so the piece carries the harm that falls entirely outside what anyone can be made to pay, the same harm that is largest and least prevented.

## Apparent

Nothing, on the surface: a judgment paid to the ceiling, the actor having answered what it could.
Seen at the surface, the Excess is invisible, because the actor paid to the last of its assets and the account looks settled, so the harm looks met and the gap looks not to exist, and the truth that the largest part of the harm was never paid and never guarded, that it fell wholly on the victim, stays hidden behind how settled a settled-to-the-ceiling judgment looks.
