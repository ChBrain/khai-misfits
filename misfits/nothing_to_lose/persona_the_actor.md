# Persona: The Actor

## Taxonomy

[Actor](position_actor.md). The thinly-capitalised injurer whose potential harm exceeds its net worth, treating the harm above the ceiling as free, guarding only up to its assets, and structuring itself thin to keep the ceiling low. The archetype whose rational choice under-deters the largest harm.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Solvent. Rational. Fully liable in law.
Is any actor whose activity can cause more harm than it owns, the small hauler of hazardous loads, the under-capitalised operator, the shell subsidiary carrying the dangerous work.
No recklessness in it: the Actor answers its liability, keeps honest books, takes the care that pays, and uses limited liability as it was designed to be used.

## Action

Computes its precaution against the cost it will actually bear, guards up to the ceiling of its assets, and treats the harm above it as free, then holds fewer assets so the ceiling stays low.
Insures what it must and no more, spins the hazardous work into a shell, capitalises thin, because every asset it holds is an asset a judgment can reach.
Does what a rational actor must: it invests in care up to what it will pay and stops, and keeps low the worth that caps what it will pay.

## Shadow

Not a wrongdoer, a rational injurer facing a capped threat, and that is the whole difficulty: the discounting is rational and the thinness is legal, and the harm above the ceiling really is, to the actor, free.
Because it will never pay for the harm above its assets, the Actor cannot count that harm as a cost, so it under-guards against exactly the largest harms and structures itself to owe as little as possible when they land, each move privately optimal and lawful, one of many actors whose sensible choices sum to a standing under-deterrence of the biggest harms, and it does none of it in secret and none of it by fraud.
It guards up to what it is worth, and being worth little is exactly what lets the largest harm go unguarded.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "I have paid what I can," and has, to the last of its assets and no further.
Says it having guarded up to the ceiling and no higher and kept the ceiling low by design, and cannot see the shed care as a fault, because the harm it did not guard against is the harm it was never going to pay for.
