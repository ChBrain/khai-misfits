## Load Bearing

Without the ceiling the judgment collects the harm it awards, so the award and the payment are the same and the actor bears its whole cost.
With the ceiling the award and the payment part: the court imposes the full harm in good faith, and collection runs to the actor's assets and stops, so the whole judgment is real in law and the part above the ceiling is a number no one will pay (Summers, on the disappearing defendant, full liability meeting an empty pocket). The Judgment is load bearing because it is where the law's full threat is shown to be partial in fact, the award complete and the collection capped, so the piece carries the "judgment-proof" wall, the point where being liable for the whole harm and paying only the ceiling are revealed as the same event.

## Apparent

A full award, the actor held to the whole of the harm.
Seen at the surface, the Judgment looks like justice done, the court imposing the entire cost on the one who caused it, so the award looks like the harm met, and the truth that the sum above the ceiling will never be collected, that the actor priced its care against this same capped threat before it acted, stays hidden behind how complete a full award looks on the page.
