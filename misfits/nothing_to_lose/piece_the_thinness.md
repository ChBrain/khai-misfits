# Piece: The Thinness

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The deliberate thin capitalisation, shells and no insurance, held to keep the ceiling low, the lawful structuring that lowers the assets a judgment can reach.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Built at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), where the worth is kept low and the hazardous work spun off.
Undone at [the escrow](place_the_escrow.md), where a stake is required up front that the thinness can no longer avoid.

## Load Bearing

Without it the ceiling sits where the actor's real worth sets it, and a substantial actor faces a substantial threat.
With it the trap deepens by design: the actor, seeing that its exposure is capped at its assets, holds fewer assets, capitalising thin, spinning the hazardous activity into an under-capitalised subsidiary, carrying no insurance it is not forced to carry, because every asset held is one a judgment can reach (LoPucki, on judgment-proofing by asset structuring; Ringleb and Wiggins, on hazardous activity contracted out to small firms). The Thinness is load bearing because it is the second turn, the actor not merely facing a ceiling but lowering it, so the piece carries the lawful use of limited liability that widens the excess, a deliberate and valuable institution turned to keep the worth at risk small.

## Apparent

Prudent structuring, an actor using limited liability as everyone may.
Seen at the surface, the Thinness looks like ordinary enterprise, the entrepreneur risking only what they put in, the activity held in its own entity, so it looks like sound practice and not a device, and the truth that the same structure lowers the ceiling below the harm and widens the free excess, that it is chosen because it lowers the ceiling, stays hidden behind how ordinary a limited-liability structure looks.

## Yearbook
