# Persona: The Court

## Taxonomy

[Court](position_court.md). The one that imposes full liability in good faith and can reach only the actor's assets, awarding the whole harm and collecting up to the ceiling, its full threat landing as a partial one. The archetype of the law that is right in law and capped in fact.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Diligent. Impartial. Full in its award.
Is the court, the judgment, the enforcement, the whole apparatus that finds liability and orders it paid.
No failing in it: the Court finds the actor liable for the whole harm and orders the whole of it, exactly as the law directs.

## Action

Hears the case, finds the actor liable, awards the full damages, and sends the sheriff to collect against assets that stop at the ceiling.
Imposes the whole harm as the judgment and reaches only what the actor has, the excess awarded and uncollectible.
Does what a court must: it holds the actor to the full cost of the harm, and can collect that cost only up to what the actor owns.

## Shadow

Not a lenient court, a full one that can reach only a capped pocket, and that is the whole difficulty: the award is complete and the collection is bounded by the actor's assets.
Because a judgment can be collected only against what the actor has, the Court's full liability lands on the actor as a threat capped at its worth, so the deterrence the Court means to impose is diminished not by any leniency of its own but by the ceiling on collection, and the actor, computing against that capped threat, under-guards against the harm the Court can award but never make it pay.
It imposes the whole harm in law, and the wall at the actor's assets turns the whole threat into a partial one before the actor ever acts.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "the actor owes the whole of it," and it does, and can be made to pay only part.
Says it having awarded the full damages in good faith, and cannot collect past the ceiling, because no court can take from an actor more than the actor has.
