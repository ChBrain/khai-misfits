# Plot: The Discount

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Balance Sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), the ceiling capping what the Actor will pay.
The Actor computing its precaution against the cost it will actually bear and finding the harm above the ceiling costs it nothing.

## Action

[Discounting](process_discounting.md): the Actor, holding [the actor's seat](position_actor.md), prices its care against what it will pay, discounts the harm above the ceiling to zero, and invests in precaution only up to its assets.

## Stage

The actor, on [Risk What I Have](plan_risk_what_i_have.md), weighs its care at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md) against the cost it will bear, and because it will bear damages only up to [the assets](piece_the_assets.md), it buys [the precaution](piece_the_precaution.md) up to the ceiling and stops, treating [the excess](piece_the_excess.md) as free.
The care it sheds is the care against the largest harms, because those are the ones it will never pay for.

## Tension

Pricing care against the cost you will bear is rational, and the rationality is what sheds the guard on the biggest harms.
There is nothing to fault: the actor computes prudently and takes the care that pays. But the harm above the ceiling is free to it, so the plot closes with the actor guarded up to its worth and no further, under-deterred on exactly the largest harm by nothing but the cap on what it can lose.
