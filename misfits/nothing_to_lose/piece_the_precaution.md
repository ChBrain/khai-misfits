# Piece: The Precaution

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The care under-invested because the excess harm is free, the shed safety, the guard the actor does not buy against the harm it will never pay for.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Priced at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), where care is weighed against the cost the actor will bear.
Restored at [the escrow](place_the_escrow.md), where a stake up to the harm makes the excess something the actor can lose again.

## Load Bearing

Without the ceiling the actor prices its care against the whole harm, so it buys precaution up to the point where care costs more than the harm it prevents, and the safety is optimal.
With the ceiling the care is cut: the actor prices its precaution against the cost it will bear, which stops at its assets, so it buys care only up to the harm it can be charged for and sheds the guard against the harm above the ceiling, the very largest harms (Shavell, on suboptimal precaution by the judgment-proof injurer). The Precaution is load bearing because it is the safety that goes missing, the care not taken against exactly the biggest harms, so the piece carries the under-deterrence itself, the guard withheld not by carelessness but because the harm it would prevent is free.

## Apparent

A reasonable level of care, an actor guarding to the point that pays.
Seen at the surface, the Precaution looks adequate, the actor taking the care a prudent operator takes, so the safety looks sufficient and the gap looks not to exist, and the truth that the care stops exactly at the ceiling and the largest harms go unguarded, that the missing precaution is missing because the harm is free, stays hidden behind how prudent a care-to-the-ceiling looks.
