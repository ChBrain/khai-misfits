# Piece: The Assets

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The actor's net worth, which is the ceiling on liability, the total a judgment can reach and the line above which the harm is free.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Totted at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), where the worth is set and the ceiling drawn.
Reached at [the scene](place_the_scene.md) tried, where the judgment runs to the assets and stops at the total.

## Load Bearing

Without them there is no ceiling and the actor faces the full harm, so its care meets the whole cost and the deterrence holds.
With them the trap has its measure: the actor's worth is exactly the limit a judgment can collect, so the harm up to the assets is borne and the harm above them is free (Shavell, on the judgment-proof problem, deterrence achieved only up to the injurer's assets). The Assets are load bearing because they set the line the whole production turns on: the actor guards up to them, the court collects up to them, the victim recovers up to them, and everything above them, the largest harms, is what no one prevents and no one pays, so the piece carries the ceiling that caps care and recovery alike, a total the actor has every reason to keep low.

## Apparent

A solvent actor, worth what it is worth, able to answer a judgment.
Seen at the surface, the Assets look like solvency, the actor holding real value and paying what it owes, so the ceiling looks like health and not a limit, and the truth that this same total is the wall above which the harm goes free, that the actor keeps it low precisely because it is the wall, stays hidden behind how solvent a solvent balance sheet looks.

## Yearbook

Set at [the balance sheet](place_the_balance_sheet.md), reached by [the victim](persona_the_victim.md) through [suing](process_suing.md), the total a judgment can collect.
