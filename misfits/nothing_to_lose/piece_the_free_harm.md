## Load Bearing

Without it the pieces are separate facts: a ceiling, a shed precaution, a widened structure, an uncollectible award.
With it they are one phenomenon: liability deters only up to the injurer's assets, so an actor whose harm can exceed its worth treats the excess as free, under-guards against exactly the largest harms, and structures itself thin to keep the ceiling low, each choice private and rational and legal and the sum a systematic under-deterrence of the biggest harms with the victims of the excess uncompensated (Shavell, on the judgment-proof problem, the whole result). The Free Harm is the load-bearing whole because it is what the other pieces are pieces of: the assets are the ceiling, the excess is the harm above it, the thinness lowers it, the precaution is what the ceiling sheds, the judgment is where it shows, and this piece is the one dynamic they compose, the arithmetic that a threat you cannot collect does not deter, so the piece carries the trap entire, the largest harm the least prevented for the plain reason that it exceeds what anyone can be made to pay.

## Apparent

A run of solvent actors answering their liability, the law imposing full damages, limited liability working as designed.
Seen at the surface, the Free Harm is invisible, because each actor is rational and legal, each judgment full, each structure lawful, so nothing looks amiss and the failure looks not to exist, and the truth that the largest harms are systematically under-deterred and their victims systematically uncompensated, that this follows from arithmetic alone with nothing hidden and no one lying, stays hidden behind how lawful each separate part looks.
