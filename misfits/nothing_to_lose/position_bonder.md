# Position: Bonder

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The seat that sets the ceiling by hand, where capital, insurance, or a bond is required up front so the actor holds a stake up to the harm, at the cost of charging every actor for a harm most will never cause.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The power to require a stake up front and the harm to size it against: the mandate to set a floor and the scale of the harm the activity can cause.
A working remedy: capital, compulsory insurance, or a posted bond up to the harm makes the excess no longer free.
No way to charge only the actors who will cause the tail harm, because it cannot tell them apart in advance, so it must charge all of them.

## Orders

Bond the risk.
Require minimum capital, compulsory insurance, or a posted bond up to the harm the activity can cause, or pierce the veil when the thinness is contrived, so the actor holds a stake up to what it can destroy.
Raise the ceiling to meet the harm, because a stake up to the harm is what restores the care.

## Loses

The enterprise it chills and the capital it idles, exactly because it must charge every actor for a harm most will never cause.
Because it cannot pick out the judgment-proof in advance, the seat prices prudent limited liability out along with contrived thinness, forces capital to sit against a tail most will never trigger, and must draw by hand the line between lawful thinness and contrived thinness, so it loses in forgone enterprise and administered lines what it gains in restored deterrence.
It gains a ceiling raised to meet the harm, and loses the free ceiling the market set, paying for the care in the enterprise it prices out.

## Drives

Toward the stake required up front and the excess made no longer free,
the ceiling raised by hand to meet the harm and charged to every actor to raise it,
so the seat that restores the care buys it only with forgone enterprise and idled capital, the largest harm deterred at the cost of pricing the small and honest alongside the judgment-proof.
