# Place: The Balance Sheet

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The actor's assets, the ceiling on recoverable damages, the place where the whole judgment meets what the actor actually has and stops.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the actor's worth is totted up, its capital, its holdings, the assets a judgment can reach, and the line beneath which the shell holds the assets of a shell.
The Actor keeping the total low, the Court's enforcement running to it and no further, and the Victim reaching it to find the excess unpaid.

## Holds

The ceiling, and the excess it cuts off.
Here the actor's worth is set, so the Balance Sheet holds the point where liability is capped: a full judgment collected only up to the total on the page, so it is where the harm above the line becomes free to the actor and uncollectible to the victim, and where the actor's every asset is one it has reason to shed, because holding less means owing less when the harm lands.

## Offers

A bounded exposure and a collectible sum.
Because the Balance Sheet is where the assets are totted and the ceiling set, it offers the actor a cap on what it can lose and the court a sum it can collect, so it hands out the certainty of the ceiling, and hands it out as a limit the actor can lower by hand, the total kept low so the cap sits below the harm the activity can cause.

## Withheld

Any reach past the total on the page.
The Balance Sheet shows what the actor has but cannot show the harm above it paid, because a judgment collects only against assets and the assets stop at the ceiling, so it withholds the excess from the victim and the deterrence from the court, letting the whole harm be awarded and only the total be collected, and, all bounded worth and no reach beyond it, it withholds from the largest harm any pocket that could pay for it.
