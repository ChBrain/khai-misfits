# Persona: The Bonder

## Taxonomy

[Bonder](position_bonder.md). The reformer who sets the ceiling by hand, requiring capital, insurance, or a posted bond up front so the actor holds a stake up to the harm, at the cost of charging every actor for a harm most will never cause. The archetype of the remedy that raises the ceiling and prices for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Corrective. Deliberate. Costly.
Is the regulator, the legislature, the court that pierces the veil, the whole apparatus that would make the actor hold enough to pay.
No zeal in it: the Bonder does not accuse, it sets a floor, requiring the actor to post capital or carry insurance up to the scale of the harm the activity can cause.

## Action

Requires minimum capital, compulsory insurance, or a posted bond up front, up to the harm the activity can cause, or pierces the corporate veil when the thinness is contrived.
Makes the actor hold a stake up to what it can destroy, so the harm above the old ceiling stops being free and the care returns.
Does what a reformer must: it raises the ceiling by hand to meet the harm, and charges every actor to do it.

## Shadow

Not a free fix, a remedy that raises the ceiling and prices for it, and that is the whole difficulty: the bond that restores the care against the largest harm is charged to every actor, including the honest and the small who would never cause it.
Because the requirement cannot tell in advance which actor will cause the tail harm, the Bonder charges all of them, pricing prudent limited liability out along with contrived thinness, forcing capital to sit idle against a harm most will never cause, and drawing by hand the line between the thinness that is a lawful use of a valuable institution and the thinness that is contrived, a line the market never had to draw.
It restores the deterrence, and it does so only by paying, in forgone enterprise and administered lines, the cost of a ceiling the market set for free.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "hold enough to pay for what you can break," and means capital idled against a tail most will never trigger.
Says it having weighed the harm above the ceiling against the enterprise it will chill, and cannot make the remedy costless, because the only way to raise the ceiling is to charge for it.
