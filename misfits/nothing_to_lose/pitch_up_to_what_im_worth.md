# Pitch: Up to What I'm Worth

## Taxonomy

[Nothing to Lose](play_nothing_to_lose.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Solvent, liable, law-abiding.
The plain good sense of an actor that carries its liability, answers a judgment, pays what it can, and uses limited liability as everyone is meant to, taking enterprise risk without betting everything it owns.
Nothing false in it on its face: the actor is fully liable in law, its books are honest, its conduct is open, and limited liability is a deliberate and valuable institution.

## Undertow

Under the solvency, an asset ceiling: a judgment can be awarded in full but collected only up to what the actor has, so every pound of harm above its net worth is free to cause and impossible to recover.
Because the actor guards only up to what it will pay, it under-invests in care against exactly the largest harms and structures itself thin to keep the ceiling low, each choice rational and legal, the sum a standing under-deterrence of the biggest harms.
What makes the actor safe to lend to, that it can only lose what it holds, is what makes the harm above the line free.

## Nerve

The feeling of an actor answering its liability in good faith, paying what it can up to the last of its assets,
sure it has met the law, because it has, to the ceiling,
while the harm above the ceiling stands uncollected, the care against it shed as a cost never to be borne, and the victim of the excess left with a judgment no one will pay.

## Echo

After the run: a court's full award revealed at the sheriff's visit as a number no one will pay, the shell holding the assets of a shell,
no one having lied and no one concealing, only a payout capped below the harm and an actor rational to guard up to the ceiling and legal to keep it low,
and the one move that would have restored the care, setting the ceiling by hand with capital, insurance, or a bond up to the scale of the harm, forgone because it charges every actor for a harm most will never cause.
