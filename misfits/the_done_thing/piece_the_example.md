# Piece: The Example

## Taxonomy

[The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md). The mimetic pressure, the peer model copied under uncertainty, the shape a new organisation takes because it is the shape the credible others already have.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Set across [the field](place_the_field.md), where the peers' shape is the model on display.
Copied through [converging](process_converging.md), where each adoption becomes the example for the next.

## Load Bearing

Without it the form spreads only by force and expectation, and uncertainty finds no ready answer.
With it the trap has its softest and widest pressure: under uncertainty about what will work, an organisation copies the recognised shape of the credible peers, because doing what is done is the safe guess, so the form spreads by being modelled, each adoption a fresh example (DiMaggio and Powell, on mimetic isomorphism, imitation as a response to uncertainty). The Example is load bearing because it is the pressure that needs no enforcer: the field's own shape drawing the next adopter, so the piece carries the peer model that makes the recognised form the obvious answer, the convergence spreading by imitation with no one requiring or teaching it.

## Apparent

Learning from the best, following proven practice, the sensible study of what works in the field.
Seen at the surface, the Example looks like prudent benchmarking, an organisation wisely copying the shape of successful peers, and the truth that the copied shape is warranted only by its own spread, that no one tested whether it works, that imitation under uncertainty converges the field on a form no result ever justified, stays hidden behind how sensible copying the credible others looks.

## Yearbook
