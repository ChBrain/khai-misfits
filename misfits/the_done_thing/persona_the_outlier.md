# Persona: The Outlier

## Taxonomy

[Outlier](position_outlier.md). The organisation that would build the form its work needs and ask the field to grant legitimacy to the unfamiliar, at the cost of the standing the recognised form was the way to keep.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Independent. Fitted to its work. Illegible to the field.
Is the organisation shaped to its actual problem, the firm that dropped the layer it did not need, the body that built the odd structure its labour called for.
No contrarianism in it: the Outlier does not diverge to be different, only to fit, refusing to carry a shape built for recognition rather than for the work.

## Action

Builds the form its work needs, drops the recognised structure that does not fit, and asks the field to read the unfamiliar shape as proper.
Fits the organisation to its problem and stands, to the funder and the regulator and the recruit, as the one that looks unlike the field.
Does what fitting the work requires: it builds the shape the labour calls for, and the shape the labour calls for is the one the field does not recognise.

## Shadow

Not a free corrective, a costly and illegible divergence, and that is the whole difficulty: fitting the work means looking, to the field it depends on, like it does not belong, and looking like it does not belong costs the standing that is survival.
Because the recognised form is what the field funds and the fitting form is not, the Outlier must spend the legitimacy the done thing was the way to keep, ask the audience that grants standing to grant it to the unfamiliar, and carry the reading of amateur and unserious that the odd shape earns, so the fit that alone serves the work is bought against every pressure that made the recognised form safe, and is lost the moment the grant is lost.
It builds the form the work needs, and the building is the cost the trap is made to render unaffordable.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "we built what the work needs, not what the field expects."
Says it standing in a shape the field cannot read, and is heard, often, as amateur and unserious, a not-quite-real organisation that has not yet learned the done thing.
