# Piece: The Fit

## Taxonomy

[The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md). The form that would suit the organisation's actual problem, the fitting shape given up for the recognised one, the efficiency sacrificed to legitimacy and now the deviant, unfundable option.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Built, if ever, at [the workshop](place_the_workshop.md), where a shape is cut to the work.
Passed over at [the grant](place_the_grant.md), where the fitting form carries no legible warrant to back.

## Load Bearing

Without it the recognised form costs nothing, and there is no better shape being given up.
With it the trap has its cost: the shape that would actually fit the organisation's work, size, and people, sacrificed to the recognised form because the recognised form is what the field funds, so the labour is served worse to keep the standing (Tolbert and Zucker, on later adopters taking the form for legitimacy after any efficiency case has gone; Meyer and Rowan, on the decoupling of the legitimating structure from the work). The Fit is load bearing because it is what the recognised form displaces: the fitting shape now the deviant and unfundable one, so the piece carries the efficiency given up for standing, the form the work needed and the field will not read as proper.

## Apparent

An amateur set-up, an underbuilt or eccentric organisation, a body that has not yet got itself properly structured.
Seen at the surface, the Fit looks like the lesser, unfinished thing, an organisation missing the structures a real one has, and the truth that it is the shape actually suited to the work, that it was given up for a form built for recognition, that its deviance is the field's convergence and not its own failing, stays hidden behind how unserious a shape the field does not recognise looks.
