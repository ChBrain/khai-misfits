# Process: Converging

## Taxonomy

[The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md). The field homogenising as org after org adopts the recognised form, the emergent sum of every rational adoption, the sameness authored by no one and enforced by none.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A field of organisations each reading the others.
At [the field](place_the_field.md), the peers copying the recognised shape under uncertainty and being copied in turn, each adoption a model for the next.

## Direction

Toward the field made alike.
Because each organisation copies what the credible others do and each adoption makes the form more the norm, converging spreads the recognised shape until it is the mark of a proper organisation and the ones without it look strange, so the pressure to adopt climbs with every adopter, the field homogenising through the mandate that requires the form, the peers that model it, and the professions that expect it.

## Lever

The soundness, under uncertainty, of doing what is done.
Because no one knows what will work and copying the credible field is the safe guess, converging runs on a thousand reasonable decisions to match the field, so the sameness is assembled from no one's design, only from each organisation's rational adoption summing across the field into a shape no one chose and none enforces.

## Echo

A field made one shape, and no author to it.
Each organisation has matched the field as reason advised, so what reads as an industry standard emerging is, in truth, the convergence of every rational adoption, a mould built by all and designed by none, the recognised form now the field's whole shape and warranted only by its own spread.
