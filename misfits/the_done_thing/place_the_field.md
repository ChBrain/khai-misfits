# Place: The Field

## Taxonomy

[The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md). The organisational field, the set of like bodies that recognise one another, where the recognised form is displayed and read and the convergence happens.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the like organisations sit and watch each other, an industry, a sector, a profession, where each reads the others' shape and is read in turn.
The Peers already formed and the Adopter deciding what to become, the recognised form on display and the field taking the measure of who has it.

## Holds

The convergence, and the mutual recognition that drives it.
Here each organisation reads the others as the model of what a proper one looks like, so the Field holds the point where the shape becomes the norm: a set of bodies that grant one another legitimacy by resemblance, so it is where the recognised form spreads, each adoption making it more the mark and the ones without it stranger, the sameness assembling from every organisation reading every other.

## Offers

A legible answer to what a proper organisation looks like.
Because the Field is full of like bodies that have the recognised form, it offers any organisation a ready model, the safe shape under uncertainty, so it hands out the reassurance of doing what the credible others do, and hands it out with the convergence built in, the recognised form more obvious with each adopter and the fitting shape stranger, the answer its own reason for being the answer.

## Withheld

Any test of whether the shared shape works.
The Field shows what every organisation looks like but cannot show whether the recognised form fits any of their work, because the form is warranted by resemblance and not by result, so it withholds the evidence that would tell a fitting shape from a merely recognised one, letting the common form stand on its own spread, and, all mutual recognition and no proof, it withholds any sign that the sameness serves the labour, until the field is converged on a shape no one ever tested.
