# Piece: The Form

## Taxonomy

[The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md). The recognised organisational form itself, the structure and practice the field reads as proper, adopted for the legitimacy it confers rather than the work it does.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Displayed across [the field](place_the_field.md), where the recognised shape is read and copied.
Rewarded at [the grant](place_the_grant.md), where resources flow to the organisation that has it.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no recognised shape to adopt, and organisations are judged, however slowly, on their work.
With it the trap has its object: a structure adopted because the field reads it as the sign of a real organisation, a legitimating myth installed for standing whether or not it fits the labour (Meyer and Rowan, on formal structure as myth and ceremony, adopted to confer legitimacy and decoupled from the actual work). The Form is load bearing because it is the shape selected for recognition and not for fit: the office, the function, the framework that answers to the audience granting legitimacy, so the piece carries the recognised structure every organisation takes to look proper and the field converges upon, warranted by resemblance and not by result.

## Apparent

The mark of a real organisation, the structure a serious body simply has, the proper way to be set up.
Seen at the surface, the Form looks like competence made visible, the shape of an organisation that knows what it is doing, and the truth that it is adopted for the standing it confers and not the work it does, that it may fit no organisation's actual labour, that it is warranted by the field's recognition and not by any result, stays hidden behind how proper a recognised structure looks.
