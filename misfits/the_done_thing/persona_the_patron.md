# Persona: The Patron

## Taxonomy

[Patron](position_patron.md). The one who converts the field's legitimacy into resources, the funder, regulator, or acquirer who backs the recognised form because the recognised form is what it can read as proper.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Prudent. Accountable. Reading for the recognised.
Is whoever grants the standing, the grant reviewer, the regulator, the accreditor, the board of the acquirer, the recruit choosing where to train, the one whose recognition the form is adopted for.
No arbitrariness intended: the Patron backs the form it can read, because the recognised structure is the legible sign that an organisation is real, and it must place its resources on something it can judge.

## Action

Reviews the organisation, looks for the recognised form, and backs the one that has it with the grant, the licence, the deal, the recruit.
Rewards the legible shape it can judge as proper over the odd one it would have to investigate.
Does what a resource-granter under uncertainty does: it backs the recognised form, because the recognised form is the sign it was given to read.

## Shadow

Not capricious, a granter reading the only sign of properness it has, and that is the whole difficulty: the recognised form is the legible warrant of legitimacy, so backing it is prudent, and prudent backing of the form is exactly what pays organisations to adopt it.
Because the Patron must place resources on what it can judge and the recognised form is what it can judge, it backs the form, and by backing it makes the form pay, so every organisation is rewarded for adopting the recognised shape and the field converges on it, the Patron's prudence funding a sameness it did not intend and reads, when it sees it, as the whole field simply being proper.
It backs the recognised form for real prudence, and backing it is what makes the recognised form the price of standing.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "we fund real organisations, and we know one when we see the structure."
Says it backing the legible form in good faith, and cannot easily tell the organisation shaped to its work from the one shaped to be funded, only which one it can recognise.
