# The Done Thing: Reference

The research warrant for [The Done Thing](play_the_done_thing.md).
The dynamic is institutional isomorphism: DiMaggio and Powell's coercive, mimetic, and normative pressures, Meyer
and Rowan's formal structure as myth and ceremony, Tolbert and Zucker's legitimacy-over-efficiency adoption, and
Weber's iron cage of rationalised order, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging institutional isomorphism as a **legitimacy-and-convergence structure**: each organisation in a field
adopts the recognised form for the legitimacy the field grants it rather than the fit it has for the work, and
because every organisation reasons the same way, the field converges on one shape selected for standing and not
for fit, a cage built by everyone's rational adoption and no one's design, with the fitting form now the deviant
and unfundable one. The play models the documented mechanics, the recognised form adopted as a legitimating myth
decoupled from the work, the coercive pressure of mandate and dependency, the mimetic pressure of copying under
uncertainty, the normative pressure of professionalisation, the patron who backs the legible form because it must
place resources on what it can judge, the field-wide convergence that no organisation authors and none enforces,
the efficiency of a fitting shape sacrificed to legitimacy, and the costly divergence that alone opens the cage,
as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of cynical mimics and empty structures. The governing
finding of the sources is the play's governing law: organisations adopt structures that confer legitimacy in their
field, often independent of whether those structures make the work more efficient, so a field of organisations
each rationally seeking legitimacy homogenises into a common form, and the shape that would fit a given
organisation's labour becomes the deviant one it cannot afford.

It is distinct from the house's The Cargo Cult, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: there the
engine is the copying of one specific success's visible rituals in the hope of reproducing its result, a form
imitated for an outcome; here the engine is the field-wide adoption of a form for the legitimacy it confers, with
no single success being copied and no result expected of the form at all, only recognition, and driven by three
pressures of which imitation is merely one. The Cargo Cult copies a model to get what the model got; the Done
Thing adopts the recognised shape to be read as proper. It is distinct from Information Cascade, where each actor
copies the prior's choice to extract the information it seems to carry, a herding on private signals: here the
currency is legitimacy and not information, the form adopted to be recognised by the field rather than to learn
what others know. And it is distinct from the house's The Leadership Script, its nearest sibling in the
legitimacy-over-substance family: the Script runs within an organisation, selecting individuals on a spoken
register, while the Done Thing runs across a field, converging whole organisations on a structural form, one a
matter of who is promoted by the sound and the other of what shape every body takes to be funded.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the adopter as a
  cynical mimic or the patron as lazy, because the source finding is precisely that adoption is rational: the
  legitimacy the recognised form confers is real funding, credibility, and survival, the patron must place its
  resources on a sign it can read, and copying the credible field under uncertainty is the sound guess. The
  sameness is authored by no one and confirmed by all, so the misfit is that when each organisation adopts the
  legitimate form for its real legitimacy, a whole field converges on one shape selected for standing and not for
  fit, a cage of everyone's rational adoption and no one's design.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that keeps the fit
  is to build the form the work needs, fund the fit and not the familiar, and ask the field to grant legitimacy to
  the unfamiliar, and it means looking, to the funder and the regulator and the recruit, like an organisation that
  does not belong, spending the standing the recognised form was the way to keep, and asking the audience that
  grants legitimacy to grant it to a shape it cannot yet read. The reckoning is a fit kept only by paying,
  continuously, the legitimacy the done thing was the way to keep, against every pressure that made the recognised
  form safe, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the three pressures and the homogenising field are DiMaggio and
  Powell's, the formal structure as legitimating myth is Meyer and Rowan's, the legitimacy-over-efficiency
  adoption is Tolbert and Zucker's, and the iron cage of rationalised order is Weber's. The honest boundary, that
  the legitimacy the form confers is real and adoption is prudent, so the convergence is collective rationality and
  not folly, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.
