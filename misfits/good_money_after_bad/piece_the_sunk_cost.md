# Piece: The Sunk Cost

## Taxonomy

[Good Money After Bad](play_good_money_after_bad.md). The money and effort already spent, and not coming back.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In [the books](place_the_books.md), a figure that only ever climbs.
Every phase adds to it and no phase subtracts, because spent is spent: the total invested to date, sitting at the top of every review deck.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing to be loyal to, and each gate could weigh the project fresh.
With it there is a weight on the table that a sound decision would ignore and no human table can: the Sunk Cost is irrelevant to whether the next phase is worth funding, and decisive to whether anyone can bear to call it wasted. It carries no information about the future and all of the emotion about the past, and the emotion is what votes.

## Apparent

Commitment, made visible.
It looks like the measure of how serious the effort is, how much the organisation has believed in this. A big number looks like conviction. It is a big number and nothing else.

## Yearbook

Tallied by [the ledger](persona_the_ledger.md), who reads it out at every gate.
It is the most honest figure in the play and the most misused: true to the last cent, and pointed exactly the wrong way, because the one thing it cannot tell you is the only thing the gate is there to decide.
