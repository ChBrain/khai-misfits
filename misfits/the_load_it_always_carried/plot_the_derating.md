# Plot: The Derating

## Taxonomy

[The Load It Always Carried](play_the_load_it_always_carried.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Rig](place_the_rig.md), the part passing every check it is given, [the fatigue limit](piece_the_fatigue_limit.md) already set against a tally no check here can read.

## Action

[Derating](process_derating.md): [The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md), holding [the engineer's seat](position_engineer.md), would hold every cycle below the fatigue limit and retire the part on its count, spending weight and cost the checks call needless.

## Stage

[The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md), on [Derate to the Limit](plan_derate_to_the_limit.md), derates the load or thickens the section and pulls the sound-looking part from [the Service](place_the_service.md) on its cycles alone.
[The Inspector](persona_the_inspector.md), on [Pass the Check](plan_pass_the_check.md), measures the part honestly and passes it, every time, and asks what the reserve is for.

## Tension

Both are right: the checks never fail, and [the tally](piece_the_tally.md) is real. Nothing on any instrument settles it, only the choice to spend now against a sum no check can show. The plot closes ambivalently: the part derated and enduring, its reserve paid for in weight, cost, and good parts retired early ... or the checks win, the reserve spared as needless, and the part left to carry, once too often, the load it always carried.
