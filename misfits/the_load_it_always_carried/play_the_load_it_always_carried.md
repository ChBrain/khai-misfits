# Play: The Load It Always Carried

## Name

The Load It Always Carried.
The part is sound. Every load it takes sits below the yield strength, well inside its limits, and every check it is given it passes: measured, tested, certified, the stress nowhere near the number at which the metal gives. So it carries the load, and carries it again, and again, a shaft turning, an axle bearing, a wing spar flexing, each cycle a load it has borne a thousand times before and will bear a thousand times again, and nothing in any single instant of it is wrong. And yet each cycle, invisibly, spends something that does not come back: a tiny irreversible increment of damage deep in the metal, a dislocation moved, a microscopic step taken toward a crack, so small that no test can see it and no cycle undoes it, and the increments sum. They sum with no one counting, because there is nothing to count on any single-cycle check, the part passing every one, and the tally climbs in the dark toward a number, and when it reaches that number a crack nucleates, still too small to see, and begins to run. And now each cycle does visible work: the crack grows a little, and the longer it grows the faster it grows, until on some ordinary day, under the ordinary working load, the load it always carried, the remaining metal is too thin to hold and the part parts, suddenly and completely, with no overload to blame and no warning given. And the terrible thing is that nothing failed a test: the load was always within limits, the checks were always passed, the part was used exactly as it was meant to be, and it broke anyway, killed not by any cycle but by the number of them, by the mere count of a load that was safe every single time it was applied. No one overstressed it. No one was careless. The reserve that would have saved it was nowhere any instantaneous test could find, because the enemy was never the size of the load, it was the sum of its repetitions, and a sum is invisible to a check that only ever sees one.

## Arc

The bend is this: a load safe at every instant, applied within every limit and passing every check, destroys the part by the sum of its repetitions, no cycle at fault and the failure authored by the count.
It runs on damage that accumulates below the threshold of every check.
A part has a yield strength, and a load below it does no instantaneous harm, so a check that compares the load to the strength passes it, correctly, every time (Wöhler). But a cyclic load below yield is not harmless over time: each application spends an irreversible increment of the part's fatigue life, a fraction that no strength check reads because it is not a matter of the present stress but of the history of stresses, and the increments accumulate linearly toward a sum of one (Palmgren and Miner).
And the accumulation is invisible and one-way. No cycle undoes what an earlier cycle spent, and no single-cycle test can see what has been spent, because the damage lives in the count and not in the moment, so the part reads sound on every check while its remaining life falls, the tally climbing in the dark with nothing on any instrument to show it.
And once the sum arrives, the crack runs. When the accumulation reaches nucleation a crack forms, still below the threshold of sight, and thereafter each cycle advances it a determinate amount set by the stress at its tip, and because a longer crack concentrates more stress, it grows faster the longer it is, accelerating to a sudden final fracture (Paris and Erdogan).
And the fracture comes under the ordinary load. The part parts under a load it carried uneventfully for years, no overload and no warning, the failure surface alone recording the slow cycle-by-cycle growth and the final fast break, so the catastrophe wears the face of the routine, killed by the count of a load that was safe every time.
None of it is overstress or neglect. Every load was within limits, every check was correctly passed, the part was used exactly as designed: no one applied too much and no one missed a test, and the failure emerges from the structure, an irreversible per-cycle increment that no check reads summing to a crack and a fracture. What was certified sound on every instant is destroyed by the number of sound instants.
Hold every cycle below the fatigue limit, derate the load or overbuild the section or retire the part on a cycle count rather than an inspection, and the sum never reaches failure: but holding below the limit means carrying weight and cost that every single-cycle check calls needless, and retiring parts that pass every test they can be given, trusting a tally no instant reveals over a strength check that never fails, so the escape is bought by spending real reserve on a damage that cannot be seen and discarding what still looks perfectly sound.

## Triggers

**[The Check](plot_the_check.md)**

Closes when the part stands certified, every check passed and the load safe, and nothing anywhere reads a fault.

**[The Accrual](plot_the_accrual.md)**

Closes when the crack is established and accelerating toward the section that cannot hold, still invisible to the service it runs in.

**[The Parting](plot_the_parting.md)**
Cue: the crack has thinned the metal past holding. Under the ordinary working load, the load it always carried, the part parts, suddenly and completely, no overload and no warning, and the fracture surface alone records the slow growth and the final break. Closes when the part has failed under a routine load, the catastrophe wearing the face of the ordinary, no cycle to blame and the count the only cause.

**[The Derating](plot_the_derating.md)**
Cue: someone would read the count rather than the check. The Engineer would hold every cycle below the fatigue limit, derate the load or overbuild the section or retire the part on its cycles, against the Inspector's checks that all pass and call the reserve needless. Closes when the part is derated and endures ... or the checks win, the reserve is spared as unnecessary, and the part is left to carry the load it always carried, once too often.

## Stakes

Whether a part can be kept from failing, when every load is safe and every check is passed.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the sum, which climbs invisibly under loads each correctly certified, and away from any reserve, which no single-cycle check can justify because no single cycle is at fault. The production cannot make an instantaneous test see the tally, because the damage lives in the count and not in the moment, so the failure is the reasonable outcome of a part used exactly as designed, not any overstress or neglect: the part is not broken by a bad load, it is broken by the number of good ones. It can only show that no load was ever too large and every check was correctly passed and the part was used precisely within its limits, and that the one move that keeps it whole, holding every cycle below the fatigue limit and retiring on the count rather than the check, must be made by carrying weight and cost that every instantaneous test calls needless and discarding parts that pass every test they can be given, trusting a tally no instant reveals over a strength check that never fails, so the soundness is real and the checks are right and the part that was certified on every instant is destroyed by the sum of the instants.
