# Process: Derating

## Taxonomy

[The Load It Always Carried](play_the_load_it_always_carried.md). The one move that answers the count rather than the instant, argued mostly at [The Rig](place_the_rig.md) against a check that never once objects, the process that spends real reserve on a damage no test can show.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An Engineer who would read the tally instead of the check.
Where every strength test the part is given comes back passed, derating begins with someone willing to act on a count no instrument shows, choosing [Derate to the Limit](plan_derate_to_the_limit.md) over the certificate already in hand.

## Direction

Toward every cycle held below the fatigue limit, not merely below yield.
Because a load safe at every instant can still be lethal by repetition, derating moves the target from the single check to the whole run of cycles, so it runs toward a load lowered, a section thickened, or a part retired on its count, whichever holds the tally below the sum it would otherwise reach.

## Lever

A reserve that costs weight and money, met by checks that all say it is not needed.
Because every instantaneous test passes without the reserve and none can be shown that requires it, derating runs against its own evidence, spending margin that no single-cycle check will ever validate, so the lever is a wager on the count against a wall of passed checks, each one arguing the other way.

## Echo

A part heavier, costlier, and still sound when it is retired, or a part left as it was.
Where derating wins, the part endures past the load it would otherwise have carried once too often, at a cost every passing check called needless: where it loses, [The Passed Check](piece_the_passed_check.md) stands unchallenged, and the part is returned to carry, again, the load it always carried.
