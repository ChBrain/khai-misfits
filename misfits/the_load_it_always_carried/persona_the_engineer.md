# Persona: The Engineer

## Taxonomy

[Engineer](position_engineer.md). The one who would read the count rather than the check, holding every cycle below the fatigue limit and retiring the part on its cycles, at a cost the passed checks all call needless.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Cautious. Long-sighted. Willing to look wasteful.
Is the one who derates the load, overbuilds the section, and retires a part on its cycle count, spending reserve against a failure no test can yet show.
No timidity in it: only a designer who trusts the tally over the instant and pays for a margin the checks say is unnecessary.

## Action

Holds every cycle below the fatigue limit, on [Derate to the Limit](plan_derate_to_the_limit.md), derating the load or thickening the section so the sum never reaches failure.
Retires the sound-looking part on its count and carries the weight and cost of the reserve.
Does what a fatigue-wise designer does: spends real margin now against a crack that no inspection will find until it is too late.

## Shadow

Not a designer immune to the doubt, one who pays the cost and derates anyway, and that is the whole difficulty: the reserve is real weight and real money, and every single-cycle check says it was never needed.
Because the margin answers a damage no instant reveals and every test the part passes, the Engineer must justify weight, cost, and discarded good-looking parts against a failure that has not happened and cannot be shown, so holding the part whole is not a costless correction but a standing decision to spend against the invisible over the objection of every check that passes.
It buys the reserve the fatigue limit demands, and buys it at a price every passed test calls waste.

## Tell

Says "it passes today, and I am retiring it anyway: it has had its cycles."
Means it, and feels the waste the whole while, spending margin against a tally no instrument will ever show against a part that still looks, on every test, perfectly sound.
