# Piece: The Crack

## Taxonomy

[The Load It Always Carried](play_the_load_it_always_carried.md). The flaw that, once nucleated, grows a determinate amount every cycle and accelerates as it lengthens, the tally made physical.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Nucleates at [the Service](place_the_service.md), still too small to see, and runs there, cycle by cycle, until its face is read at [the Fracture Surface](place_the_fracture_surface.md).

## Load Bearing

Without it the sum arriving at one would be an abstraction with nothing to show for it, a number crossed with no consequence in the metal.
With it the abstraction becomes a physical advance: once nucleation is reached, the crack forms and every further cycle drives it forward a set amount, an advance fixed by the stress at its tip, and because a longer crack concentrates more stress at that tip, each advance is larger than the one before it. The Crack is load bearing because it is the propagation the part actually suffers (Paris and Erdogan): a determinate growth per cycle that compounds on its own length, so the piece carries the turn from invisible accumulation to visible, accelerating damage.

## Apparent

A hairline flaw, if it is seen at all, small and slow and seemingly stable, the kind of thing a routine inspection might miss for cycle after cycle.
Seen at the surface, the Crack looks stable because its early growth is small against the section it runs through, and the truth that its rate is climbing with its own length, that the same law governing its first imperceptible advance also governs its last and fastest one, stays hidden behind how gradual and how ordinary its opening cycles look.

## Yearbook

Nucleated when [the Tally](piece_the_tally.md) reaches one, through [Cracking](process_cracking.md), at [the Service](place_the_service.md).
It is the turn of the trap: the Crack advances a set amount each [Cycle](piece_the_cycle.md), the amount growing with the length already run, until the section it has thinned can no longer hold and it hands the story to [the Fracture](piece_the_fracture.md), its slow growth and its fast final run both recorded, after the fact, on [the Fracture Surface](place_the_fracture_surface.md).
