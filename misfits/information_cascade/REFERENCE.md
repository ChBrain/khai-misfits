# Information Cascade: Reference

The research warrant for [Information Cascade](play_information_cascade.md). The title is Bikhchandani,
Hirshleifer, and Welch's; the play runs on the theory of rational herding from actions, its
experimental confirmation, and the wisdom-of-crowds condition it violates, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the informational cascade as a **systemic system**: each actor holds a private signal and
observes only the actions of those before them, so once a run of concordant actions outweighs any one
private signal, each later actor rationally sets their own signal aside and copies, and their copy,
made on the crowd's information rather than their own, conveys no signal, so information stops
aggregating and the crowd locks onto whatever the first few happened to hold. The play models the
documented mechanics, the private signal, the public action that is the only visible evidence, the
inference that reads actions as signals, the tipping point where observed actions outweigh a private
hint, the cascade that self-sustains on the first few, the fragility by which it reverses on a breath,
and the aggregation that alone recovers the crowd's knowledge at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors
of the structure, not as a tale of conformity. The governing finding of the source literature is the
play's governing law: rational individuals inferring from others' actions will herd, discard their
own signals, and settle idiosyncratically and possibly wrongly, because a decision copied reveals
nothing and stops the pooling of what the crowd privately knows. It is the informational cousin of the
house's Path Dependence: not a payoff that rises with the crowd, but a rational reading of the crowd's
choices that keeps each member's own knowledge from ever being spoken.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  follower as a sheep, because the source finding is precisely that following is rational: actions are
  the only visible evidence, reading them as signals is correct, and a single private hint really is
  outweighed by a run of revealed ones, so deferring to the crowd is the right move for the individual.
  The misfit is that every correct deferral removes a signal, so a crowd of sound reasoners acts on
  almost none of what it collectively knows.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a brave individual thinking for themselves.
  A lone contrarian acting on one hint against the herd is usually wrong to, and breaking a cascade can
  swap a wrong herd for another; the move that works is structural, surfacing private signals before
  the actions can speak, and it costs, because independence is expensive and imitation is usually cheap
  and right. The reckoning is a mechanism built against a crowd each member would rather join, not
  courage winning free.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the cascade theory, the herd model, the
  laboratory evidence, and the wisdom-of-crowds condition are theirs, and the fact that rational
  imitation is often efficient is carried as carefully as the finding that it can lock a crowd wrong.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Private Signal](piece_the_private_signal.md)**: carries each actor's own noisy information (Bikhchandani,
  Hirshleifer & Welch), the hint that following will leave unspoken.
- **[The Public Action](piece_the_public_action.md)**: carries the observability of choices not signals (Anderson
  & Holt), the only evidence a later actor has of what others knew.
- **[The Inference](piece_the_inference.md)**: carries the rational reading of actions as signals (Banerjee), the
  correct update that makes following sensible.
- **[The Tipping Point](piece_the_tipping_point.md)**: carries the threshold where observed actions outweigh a
  private hint (Welch), after which the signal is set aside.
- **[The Cascade](piece_the_cascade.md)**: carries the self-sustaining herd (Bikhchandani, Hirshleifer & Welch;
  Banerjee), each copy revealing nothing so information stops aggregating.
- **[The Fragility](piece_the_fragility.md)**: carries the abrupt reversal (Bikhchandani, Hirshleifer & Welch,
  1998), a cascade resting on so little that new information flips it.
- **[The Information Cascade](piece_the_information_cascade.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Bikhchandani,
  Hirshleifer & Welch), the crowd blind to what it knows, and its remedy in surfaced signals at a cost.
