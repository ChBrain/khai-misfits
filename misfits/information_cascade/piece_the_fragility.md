# Piece: The Fragility

## Taxonomy

[Information Cascade](play_information_cascade.md). The abrupt reversal: a cascade resting on so little that new information flips it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the turn](place_the_turn.md), where a new signal meets the thin cascade.
Where one loud action can outweigh the whole run.

## Load Bearing

Without it the cascade would look as solid as it is large, and could not correct or collapse.
With it the crowd's size is revealed as no measure of its knowledge: because the herd rests on only the first few signals, a single new piece of public information, a bold contrarian or a fresh fact, can outweigh it and reverse the whole run at once. The Fragility is load bearing because it is the instability the theory insists on (Bikhchandani, Hirshleifer & Welch, 1998): cascades are easy to start, easy to shatter, and their sudden turns, fads collapsing, panics reversing, are not anomalies but the signature of a certainty founded on almost nothing.

## Apparent

A fickle, irrational crowd.
A herd that swings abruptly looks like mass caprice, so the reversal reads as the crowd losing its head, when each turn is the same rational reading of a new action as evidence, applied to a base always too thin to hold.

## Yearbook

Triggered by [the contrarian](persona_the_contrarian.md) through [cascading](process_cascading.md) run backward, exposing [the cascade](piece_the_cascade.md).
It is the thinness made visible: the Fragility shows the certainty always rested on little, and can swap a wrong herd for a right one, or the reverse.
