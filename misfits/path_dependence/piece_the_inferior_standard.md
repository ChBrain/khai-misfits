# Piece: The Inferior Standard

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md). The possibly worse winner: the option locked in over a better one, and the honest doubt whether it was worse at all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the rut](place_the_rut.md), the standard everyone uses and no one would design.
Where the better challenger stands stranded just outside the groove.

## Load Bearing

Without it lock-in would be harmless, merely a durable choice among equals.
With it lock-in has a cost: the entrenched option may be genuinely worse than the road not taken, so the market has settled on the inferior technology and made it permanent, and merit, having lost once to the base, cannot get a rematch. The Inferior Standard is load bearing because it is the claim that gives the misfit its sting (Arthur), that increasing returns can lock in the worse option, and it carries its own honest counter (Liebowitz & Margolis): the stranded-superior story is easier to tell than to prove, and the winner is sometimes fine, so the piece holds both the harm and the doubt about it.

## Apparent

Self-evidently the best, because it won.
Victory is read backward as proof of superiority, so the locked-in standard looks like the natural champion, and the burden falls, unfairly, on anyone claiming the loser was better to prove a counterfactual no market ever ran.

## Yearbook

Suffered by [the challenger](persona_the_challenger.md) through [locking](process_locking.md), kept in place by [the lock-in](piece_the_lock_in.md), doubted by [the chronicler](persona_the_chronicler.md).
It is the harm and its question mark: the Inferior Standard is what [the path dependence](piece_the_path_dependence.md) may leave behind, a worse option enthroned, or perhaps only a different one.
