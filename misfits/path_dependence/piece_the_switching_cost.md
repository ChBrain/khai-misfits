# Piece: The Switching Cost

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md). The barrier: the sunk investment and the need to move together that strand whoever moves alone.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the rut](place_the_rut.md), in the tools, the training, the files built around the standard.
Where leaving by yourself means losing all of it and the base besides.

## Load Bearing

Without it a user who preferred the better option could simply take it.
With it the switch is a loss for anyone who makes it alone: the sunk investment is forfeit and the mover is stranded from the base whose value he needed, so the gain of the better standard arrives only if everyone moves at once, and no one will be first. The Switching Cost is load bearing because it is the coordination barrier that makes lock-in a trap rather than a mere preference (Farrell & Saloner), the reason a crowd that would each rather switch cannot, and the exact obstacle only a sponsor can pay down.

## Apparent

A sensible reluctance to throw away what works.
Declining to junk a working investment for an uncertain move looks like prudence, so the switching cost reads as good sense rather than a trap, and the individual caution that keeps everyone stuck passes for nobody's fault.

## Yearbook

Borne by [the captive](persona_the_captive.md) through [locking](process_locking.md), the price that makes the rut a rut.
It is the toll on leaving: the Switching Cost produces [the lock-in](piece_the_lock_in.md) and is the very thing [the path dependence](piece_the_path_dependence.md) can be escaped only by paying together.
