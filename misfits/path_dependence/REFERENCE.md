# Path Dependence: Reference

The research warrant for [Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md). The title is the economists'
term; the play runs on David's history of QWERTY, Arthur's model of increasing returns and lock-in,
the network-effects and standards literature beneath them, and the critique that keeps the parable
honest, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging path dependence and lock-in as a **systemic system**: an option with an early lead, perhaps
won by a small event that had nothing to do with merit, is chosen by each adopter because others
chose it, so under increasing returns the lead compounds into a standard, the standard locks in, and
a better technology arriving later cannot dislodge it, because its value needs an installed base and
the base needs its value. The play models the documented mechanics, the small historical event, the
network externality and the learning curve that make value rise with adoption, the early lead that
compounds, the switching cost that strands anyone who moves alone, the lock-in held by the cost of
leaving rather than the choice to stay, the possibly inferior standard that results, and the
coordinated switch that alone escapes it, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale
of stupidity. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: with
increasing returns, small historical events can select which technology wins, and the winner need not
be the efficient one and can be locked in. It is the technology-adoption cousin of the house's Bank
Run: not a shared fear that selects the ruinous outcome, but a shared adoption that selects the
possibly worse standard, each outcome chosen by what everyone expects everyone else to do.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  adopter as a follower or the incumbent as a bully, because the source finding is precisely that the
  choices are rational: the value of a standard rises with its base, so betting on the base is the
  correct bet, and the incumbent need only be first and grow. The misfit is that a market of sound
  individual choices can lock the whole onto the option that was merely ahead, not best.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a better technology simply winning. The
  escape is a coordinated switch, which must be paid for, may be steered to the wrong winner, and is
  often not worth its cost, and the play carries the honest doubt with it: sometimes the standard the
  parable calls inferior was never truly the worse, so the reckoning is coordination bought at a
  price, or a lock-in that was efficient all along, not merit set free.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the QWERTY history, the increasing-returns
  model, and the network-effects account are theirs, and the critique that lock-in into a truly
  inferior standard is rarer than the parable suggests is carried as carefully as the parable itself.

Source to constraint, per element.

  self-reinforces into a standard.
- **[The Switching Cost](piece_the_switching_cost.md)**: carries the coordination barrier (Farrell & Saloner), the
  sunk investment and the need to move together that strand whoever moves alone.
- **[The Lock-In](piece_the_lock_in.md)**: carries the entrenched state (David; Arthur), the standard held by the
  cost of leaving rather than the choice to stay.
- **[The Inferior Standard](piece_the_inferior_standard.md)**: carries the possibly worse winner (Arthur), the
  option locked in over a better one, and the honest doubt whether it was worse at all (Liebowitz & Margolis).
- **[The Path Dependence](piece_the_path_dependence.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (David; Arthur), history
  selecting the standard, and its remedy in a coordinated switch at a cost.
