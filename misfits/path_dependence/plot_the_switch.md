# Plot: The Switch

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The table](place_the_table.md), where someone large enough moves to coordinate the jump the market cannot.
A sponsor with a mandate or a purse, and a bridge that might carry the whole base across at once.

## Action

[Coordinating](process_coordinating.md): [The Sponsor](persona_the_sponsor.md), holding [the sponsor's seat](position_sponsor.md), sets a date or ships a converter to move everyone together, while [The Chronicler](persona_the_chronicler.md), holding [the chronicler's seat](position_chronicler.md), asks whether the old standard was ever really the worse.

## Stage

[The Sponsor](persona_the_sponsor.md) tables [Move Them All at Once](plan_move_them_all_at_once.md), paying [the switching cost](piece_the_switching_cost.md) for the whole crowd so that [the path dependence](piece_the_path_dependence.md) can be escaped at last, and no one is stranded for going first.
[The Chronicler](persona_the_chronicler.md) tables [Read the History](plan_read_the_history.md), and holds the honest doubt beside the escape.

## Tension

The one move that frees the crowd must choose a destination, pay for the trip, and might be wrong to make it.
Only a sponsor can pay the switching cost for everyone, but paying it means picking the winner, which interest can steer and hindsight can fault, and the Chronicler's question stands unanswered: whether the locked-in standard was bad enough to be worth the leaving. The rut is escaped ... at a real price and a real risk, or the switch is judged to cost more than it saves, and the path holds, perhaps rightly.
