# Piece: The Learning Curve

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md). The other engine of the compounding: the option that grows cheaper and better by being made and used the most.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the crowd](place_the_crowd.md), in the falling cost and the accumulating skill.
Where the leader improves simply because it is the one being produced and practised.

## Load Bearing

Without it a rival could match the leader's quality and cost and compete on even ground.
With it the leader gets a real head start that widens on its own: made at greater scale and used by more hands, it becomes cheaper to build and better understood, so it improves by leading, and a latecomer must not only be better but overcome the leader's accumulated advantage from behind. The Learning Curve is load bearing because it is the learning-by-doing and scale return that reinforces lock-in (Arthur), the mechanism that lets an early lead turn into a genuine cost and quality edge, so the incumbent's dominance is not illusion but a moat the base itself digs.

## Apparent

Proof the leading standard is simply the better one.
Because the incumbent really is cheaper and more refined, it looks like the superior technology winning fairly, when its refinement is a product of its lead, not the cause of it.

## Yearbook

Turned by [the incumbent](persona_the_incumbent.md) through [compounding](process_compounding.md), the engine paired with [the network](piece_the_network.md).
It is the edge that leading builds: the Learning Curve widens [the early lead](piece_the_early_lead.md) into a gap a superior rival cannot close from behind.
