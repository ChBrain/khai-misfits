# Plot: The Challenger

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The crowd](place_the_crowd.md), a plainly better way arriving late to a world already standardised.
It wins every comparison on the merits, and finds the crowd gathered somewhere else.

## Action

[Compounding](process_compounding.md): [The Challenger](persona_the_challenger.md), holding [the challenger's seat](position_challenger.md), offers a superior standard and waits for a rational world to take it, while [The Adopter](persona_the_adopter.md), holding [the adopter's seat](position_adopter.md), still bets on the incumbent's base.

## Stage

[The Challenger](persona_the_challenger.md) tables [Win on the Merits](plan_win_on_the_merits.md), and finds [the network](piece_the_network.md) makes it worthless without adopters it cannot start, so [the inferior standard](piece_the_inferior_standard.md) holds against the better one.
[The Adopter](persona_the_adopter.md) tables [Bet on the Base](plan_bet_on_the_base.md), correctly, because a standard is worth its crowd and the challenger has none.

## Tension

The better option is here, and being better is exactly what does not matter.
The challenger wins every review and cannot win a single adopter, because its value waits on a base and the base waits on its value, and no one gains by switching to it alone. Merit was never the currency; the crowd was. The superior standard is not outcompeted but locked out, stranded by a market that rewards the shared choice over the good one, and would need everyone to move at once to reward it at all.
