# Piece: The Lock-In

## Taxonomy

[Path Dependence](play_path_dependence.md). The entrenched state: the standard held by the cost of leaving rather than the choice to stay.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the rut](place_the_rut.md), the groove worn too deep to climb out of alone.
Where the standard is fixed by everyone's investment and no one's decision.

## Load Bearing

Without it a bad standard would be dropped the moment enough people noticed.
With it the standard cannot be dropped, because dropping it requires a coordination no scattered market performs: each user's cost to leave keeps him in, and his staying keeps the next in, so the option is stable exactly because it is entrenched, not because it is good. The Lock-In is load bearing because it is the settled outcome the model and the history both name (David; Arthur), the point where the accident becomes irreversible by ordinary means, and the misfit's standing state, an option kept by inertia and not by merit.

## Apparent

A durable, proven standard the market clearly trusts.
Persistence reads as endorsement, so a standard that survives looks vindicated by its own longevity, when it may be held up by nothing but the collective cost of abandoning it.

## Yearbook

Held by [the captive](persona_the_captive.md) through [locking](process_locking.md), grown from [the early lead](piece_the_early_lead.md) and [the switching cost](piece_the_switching_cost.md).
It is the trap sprung: the Lock-In keeps [the inferior standard](piece_the_inferior_standard.md) in place, escapable only by the whole crowd moving at once.
