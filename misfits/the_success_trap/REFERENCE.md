# The Success Trap: Reference

The research warrant for [The Success Trap](play_the_success_trap.md). The vocabulary
is strategy and innovation practice; the mechanics the play runs on are grounded in
the disruption, resource-dependence, and organisational-learning literature cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the success trap as a **systemic system**: a firm in which every seat allocates
correctly by the yardstick that made the firm excellent, and the market is lost anyway
because that same yardstick points away from the bet that would have held it. The play
models the documented mechanics, sustaining versus disruptive innovation, the values
that disqualify low-margin bets, resource dependence on customers and capital, and the
exploitation that crowds out exploration, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure,
not as a tale of complacent managers. The governing finding of the source literature is
the play's governing law: the criteria that make a firm best at its current market are
the same criteria that make it rational to starve the market that will replace it, so
the failure is a property of good management, not a lapse from it.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to
  cast any seat as complacent or foolish, because the source finding is precisely that the
  firm is competent: each decision clears the yardstick, correctly. The misfit is that the
  yardstick that measures excellence points away from the seed.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into vision or virtue. The entrant
  is not smarter; it wins because it starts with values fit for the ground the incumbent is
  glad to lose, and the reckoning is the market inverting, not honesty or genius arriving.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed
  here, never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Best Customer](piece_the_best_customer.md)**: carries the voice-of-customer pull
  (Bower & Christensen), the best customers wanting more of the dimension that already pays
  and never the disruptive product.
- **[The Margin Hurdle](piece_the_margin_hurdle.md)**: carries the RPV values (Christensen &
  Raynor) and resource dependence (Pfeffer & Salancik), the criterion that starves the seed
  while working exactly as intended.
- **[The Overshoot](piece_the_overshoot.md)**: carries performance oversupply (Christensen),
  the sustaining trajectory exceeding what the mainstream can absorb and opening the low end.
- **[The Starved Seed](piece_the_starved_seed.md)**: carries exploration crowded out by
  exploitation (March), the disruptive bet defunded every cycle with the firm's eyes open.
- **[The Low-End Foothold](piece_the_low_end_foothold.md)**: carries the disruptive foothold
  (Christensen), the entrant taking the undesirable segment as a place to improve from.
- **[The Rational Retreat](piece_the_rational_retreat.md)**: carries asymmetric motivation and
  core rigidities (Leonard-Barton), the incumbent's disciplined flight upmarket reading as
  focus while it vacates the ground.
- **[The Climb](piece_the_climb.md)**: carries the entrant's sustaining-improvement trajectory
  crossing tiers (Christensen) and the reconfiguration the incumbent's screening cannot see
  (Henderson & Clark).
- **[The Success Trap](piece_the_success_trap.md)**: carries the success trap itself (Levinthal &
  March) and marketing myopia (Levitt), good management as the engine of failure, held whole by nobody.
