# Piece: The Rational Retreat

## Taxonomy

[The Success Trap](play_the_success_trap.md). The disciplined flight upmarket, vacating the ground below.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Up the margin, away from [the low end](place_the_low_end.md) and toward the richest customers.
Each time the entrant takes a tier, the incumbent does the sensible thing and cedes it, moving its mix to where the margin is fatter.

## Load Bearing

Without it the two climbs never cross, and the entrant would have to fight for every tier.
With it the incumbent hands the tiers over: pressed from below, it retreats up, and every retreat improves its numbers, because shedding the low-margin business raises the average. The firm flees into a smaller, richer corner and its dashboards reward it the whole way. The retreat is not surrender. It reads, at every step, as strategy.

## Apparent

Focus.
It looks like the firm concentrating on its most valuable customers, trimming the tail, improving its margins quarter over quarter. The margins really do improve. The firm really is more profitable, right up to the end.

## Yearbook

Ordered by [the allocator](persona_the_allocator.md), applauded by [the best customer](piece_the_best_customer.md).
It is the trap's cruelest move: the closer the entrant climbs, the better the incumbent's numbers look, because the retreat sheds exactly the business under attack. The firm is congratulated for discipline until the day there is no higher ground to retreat to.
