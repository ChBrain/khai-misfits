# Piece: The Margin Hurdle

## Taxonomy

[The Success Trap](play_the_success_trap.md). The yardstick that makes the firm excellent and starves the seed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the capital committee](place_the_capital_committee.md), applied to every bet that asks for money.
The criterion the firm judges itself by: margin, market size, certainty of return. The measure of a good decision, used exactly as intended.

## Load Bearing

Without it the firm cannot allocate, and any project is as fundable as any other.
With it the allocation is disciplined and the discipline is fatal: the disruptive bet is small, low-margin, and aimed at a market that does not yet exist, so it fails the hurdle honestly, every cycle, while the sustaining bet clears it every cycle. The yardstick is not broken. It is working, and its correct working is the trap.

## Apparent

Rigour.
It looks like the firm refusing to waste money, holding every project to the same fair standard. Nobody games it. The seed really does return less, today, than the roadmap.

## Yearbook

Held by [the hurdle](persona_the_hurdle.md), defended by [the allocator](persona_the_allocator.md).
It cannot see a market that has no revenue yet, because it was built to weigh revenue, and it rounds the future to the size of the present. The one criterion that would fund the seed, ground the firm might lose, is the one criterion the firm was disciplined not to use.
