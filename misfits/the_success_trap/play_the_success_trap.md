# Play: The Success Trap

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Success Trap.
A firm that does everything right, listens to its best customers, funds its best margins, keeps its discipline ... and is taken from below by the one market that discipline taught it to decline.

## Arc

The bend is this: the firm is never wrong, and it loses the market anyway.
It runs on two climbs that cross.
Upmarket, the incumbent climbs the margin: its best customers pull it toward more of what already works, its capital gate funds only what clears the hurdle, and every rational allocation walks it further into richer, narrower ground.
From below, the entrant climbs the performance: it takes the low customers the incumbent is glad to lose, ships good enough for cheap, and improves one tier at a time.
The two climbs cross at the mainstream, and on the day they cross the incumbent's discipline is the reason it is standing in the wrong place. The seed that would have held the ground was defunded every cycle, because it never cleared the hurdle that made the firm excellent.
Change the yardstick from margin defended to ground held and the seed is funded before the climb arrives: the criterion is the story.

## Triggers

**[Plan](plot_plan.md)**
The best customer asks for more along the dimension the firm already sells. The account lead carries it in, the roadmap turns upmarket, and the disruptive champion tables the small bet. Closes when the roadmap locks toward the margin and the seed is deferred to next cycle.

**[Do](plot_do.md)**
Cue: the capital committee applies the hurdle. The seed is starved, the sustaining line ships and overshoots what the mainstream can use, and the entrant takes the low ground nobody defended. Closes when the firm reports a strong quarter and the entrant is beneath notice.

**[Check](plot_check.md)**
Cue: the entrant's foothold improves a tier. The incumbent, doing everything right, retreats further upmarket into richer margin and is congratulated for the discipline. Closes when the core looks healthiest ... the quarter before the climb arrives.

**[The Inversion](plot_the_inversion.md)**
Cue: the climb crosses the mainstream threshold. The good-enough product is now enough, the payoff flips, and the ground the firm vacated is the whole market. Closes when the skin splits and the discipline reads as the disease ... or the quarter runs anyway.

## Stakes

Whether the firm ever funds the thing that would save it.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward the seed, toward the margin. The production cannot make the firm invest against its own discipline. It can only show that the discipline was the disease, and that the one bet that would have held the market was the one bet the yardstick was built to reject.
