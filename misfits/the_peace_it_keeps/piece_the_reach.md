## Load Bearing

Without the Reach the disease would be a lump to cut out: it is the systemic character of the trouble that makes it a sickness of the whole body and not a local overgrowth, the harm carried in the blood to everywhere the blood runs.
The Reach is load bearing because it makes the phrase system sickness literal, and truthfully so: a clonal expansion of the blood is not only a marrow disease but a blood-borne risk state, the mutant line and its inflammatory output tied to harm well past the marrow, raising the risk of vascular disease and of death by more than the cancer alone (Jaiswal and colleagues). It carries the truth that the body cannot localise what is made of its own circulating tissue, since blood goes everywhere and so does what is wrong with it. Held with its guardrail: clonal expansion is near-universal at depth in later life, and the clinically defined state highly prevalent rather than universal in all, most of it never becoming leukaemia, so the Reach stages a systemic risk and not a sentence, a whole-body state that most who carry a clone live with and do not die of.

## Apparent

Just a blood count, a number a little off.
Seen at the surface, the Reach hides inside the ordinary, a marrow finding or a mild anomaly in the blood, and the truth that a clone in the marrow is a state of the whole circulation, that what looks like a local or a merely numerical thing reaches into vessels and organs far from where it started, stays hidden behind how contained a blood test makes the trouble seem.
