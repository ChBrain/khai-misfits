# Position: The Advantage

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The seat of local fitness, which a cell comes to occupy when a heritable change lets it out-divide or outlast its neighbours, holding an edge that rewards winning and refers to nothing above it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The edge, and the heredity that keeps it.
The seat holds a heritable advantage in proliferating or in escaping death, and the certainty that every daughter inherits it, so what the position has is not a single win but a win that compounds down the generations of a line.
It holds the ground the body keeps open, since a renewing tissue leaves room for fresh cells, and the seat's edge turns that room into its own.

## Orders

Divide, hold the edge, take the ground.
Leave more copies than the cells around you, pass the advantage to every daughter, and fill the space the marrow makes, since the seat's whole measure is copies left and a cell that divides less is a cell overtaken.
Answer to nothing above, because a fitness advantage carries no reference to the whole and no instruction to stop, so the seat proceeds by the single rule that more is more.

## Loses

The ceiling on its own growth, which only the level above can supply.
The Advantage can win and cannot stop itself, because nothing in a fitness edge says enough: the seat holds a rule that rewards more copies without limit and contains no term for the good of the body, so it forfeits any bound of its own and can be checked only from outside, by the very suppression it escaped. What the position gives up is a stopping-point: it has no internal governor, only an appetite that scales, so once the outside check is slipped there is no inner one to meet it. It loses, too, any stake in the whole it grows in, since the seat is defined purely by the local contest and the body that hosts the contest is nowhere in its terms, so the ground it destroys is, to the seat, simply the ground it takes.

## Drives

Toward a line that fills the marrow and cannot halt itself,
and a winner with no stopping-point, bounded if at all only from above and never from within,
so the seat that rewards a cell for winning is the seat that cannot tell it when it has won too much, its edge a rule that runs until something outside it runs out.
