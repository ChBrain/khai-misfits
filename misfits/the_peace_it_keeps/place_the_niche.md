# Place: The Niche

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The supporting ground of the marrow, the stroma and its signals that bring blood cells to maturity, where the line does its harm not by eviction but by turning the soil against the cells it once fed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A nursery, the ground that raises blood to finish.
Shown at the surface is support working: the niche holding the developing cells and feeding them the signals that carry each to maturity, a soil whose business is to bring the marrow's young cells all the way to blood.

## Holds

Holds the signals that let normal cells finish, and the soil a line can turn.
The Niche is where maturation is granted or withheld, so it holds the cues a developing cell needs to complete, and holds them as a thing that can be remodelled: the same ground that nurses the blood can be reworked by an overgrowing line into a ground that starves it, the support and its subversion the same soil.

## Offers

Offers maturation to the cells it supports, and, once turned, the suppression of them.
Here the developing blood is brought to finish, so the Niche offers the last step of renewal; and offers, when a line remodels it, the opposite of that step: a soil that no longer lets the normal cells mature, so the place that raised the blood becomes the place that jams it, the same signals now missing or turned, feeding the line and famishing the rest.

## Withheld

Withholds a ground the line cannot turn, and maturation once the soil is poisoned.
What the Niche cannot offer is a soil immune to remodelling: the support it gives is given through signals a growing line can rework, so there is no version of the ground that nurses the blood and cannot be turned against it. Once the line has poisoned the soil the place withholds the very finishing it exists to grant, so the normal cells persist in it and cannot complete, present and unfinished, the ground still full and no longer nursing.
