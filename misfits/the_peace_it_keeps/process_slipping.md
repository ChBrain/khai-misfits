# Process: Slipping

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The lapse of the suppression that holds the cells down, a changed cell passing the layered checks that would have retired it, the peace giving at one cell and not by any betrayal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Body](persona_the_body.md), at [the Marrow](place_the_marrow.md), its own checks passing over a cell that carries a change.
Begins whenever a variant that should meet the order to specialise, to stop, or to die instead escapes it, and its daughters inherit the escape.

## Direction

Turns a held cell into an unheld one.
Because the suppression is layered and redundant but finite, slipping converts a cell that would have been retired into a cell that persists, one changed line passing the checks the peace relies on, so the process does not break the truce all at once but lets it give at a single point, the hold that catches almost everything missing this.

## Lever

The finitude of a redundancy that usually saves.
Because the checks are many and catch most lapses, they read as total, but many is not all, and over a lifetime of divisions the rare escape becomes expected: the lever is the gap in a defence that is strong and not perfect, so slipping works through no single failure but the cumulative certainty that layered odds, run long enough, are beaten once, the erosion of the peace by attrition and not by fault.

## Echo

A variant loose in the marrow, the peace given at one cell.
The echo of slipping is a changed line no longer held, its escape passed to every daughter, so the suppression that still governs the whole marrow no longer governs this, and the truce stands everywhere but the one place it gave, a single unheld cell now free to do what an advantage does.
