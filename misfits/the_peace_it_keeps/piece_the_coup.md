# Piece: The Coup

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The phenomenon whole: a lower order rising against the higher order that suppressed it, the body undone by one of its own cells, a seizure from within with no usurper from outside.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Falls in the body entire, staged from [the Marrow](place_the_marrow.md) outward.
Flows as the whole event the other pieces compose, the truce broken at one cell, the edge rewarded, the blood jammed, the reach spreading, gathered into a single shape: the whole brought down by a part of itself.

## Load Bearing

Without the Coup there is no misfit, only a body that works or a growth to remove: it is the Coup that ties the truce to the edge to the reach, the one event in which a member of the whole undoes the whole by doing what a member can.
The Coup is load bearing because it is the phenomenon entire and the seat of the misfit's one distinct vector, the resumption of cell-level selection under an organism that exists only as its suppressor, so the killer is not an invader and not a rival population but a component of its own victim, a lower level rising against the higher level built to hold it down (Nowell; Buss; Aktipis and colleagues). It carries the entire trap: the Truce is the peace it overturns, the Edge is the advantage it rises on, the Reach is how far it carries, so the Coup is not one more piece beside the others but the shape they compose. And it is where the play parts from a mere collapse: this is not a population degrading itself to extinction across generations but a single body undone by its own tissue and dying with it, the seizure confined to the one host, heritable to no next, the whole logic of it two levels deep. It is built of nothing but a member doing what any member could, each division correct and each lapsed check innocent, so it cannot simply be called off: there is no rebel to arrest and no border to close, only the body and the one cell of it that won.

## Apparent

Just an illness, a body that fell sick as bodies do.
Seen at the surface, the Coup looks like a misfortune that came upon the body, a disease it caught the way one catches things, and the truth that nothing came upon it, that the sickness rose from inside as a part of the body outgrowing the whole, that there was never an enemy at all but only the body's own cell winning, stays hidden behind how much a body brought down from within resembles a body struck from without.
