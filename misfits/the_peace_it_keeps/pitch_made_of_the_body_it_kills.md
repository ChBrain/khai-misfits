# Pitch: Made of the Body It Kills

## Taxonomy

The single hook of [The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A body renewing itself is the picture of health. The marrow makes fresh blood for a lifetime, each cell taking its place and doing its part, a commonwealth so well kept that we call it simply being alive. The strength of it is the peace: a trillion cells that could compete and do not, held in their places by the body's own quiet machinery.

## Undertow

The peace has no enforcer for its own. Because the body is a truce, a whole that exists only by holding its cells' competition down, a single cell that gains a heritable edge can resume the oldest fight inside it, and the body cannot cast the cell out, because the cell is the body: of the same kind as the tissue it destroys, made of the very thing it kills, so there is no invader to name and no border to hold. The killer is kin, and the weapon raised against it falls on the kin.

## Nerve

The trap's nerve is that nothing goes wrong. No cell rebels and no guard sleeps: the line spreads because a line that leaves more copies leaves more copies, and the checks miss it because layered checks miss one now and then, so the coup is built of nothing but biology doing exactly what it does. The whole is undone by a part of itself, one level rising against the level that suppressed it, and there is no fault anywhere to point at, only the truce and the single member who, doing what an advantage does, breaks it.

## Echo

What it leaves is a body failing of its own overgrown tissue, the enemy at no gate and the ruin all inside, and a remedy that cannot spare what it must spend, striking the renewal to strike the line because the two are one kind. Not a hopeless fight, but never a clean one: the peace, once broken from within, can be held to a standstill and sometimes truly won, but only at the price of the renewal the body is made of, because the harm and the making are the same cell.
