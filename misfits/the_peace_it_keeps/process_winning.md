# Process: Winning

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The resumed selection among the body's own cells, a line with a heritable edge out-dividing its neighbours, the oldest rule running one level down with no intent in it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Line](persona_the_line.md), at [the Marrow](place_the_marrow.md), leaving more copies than the cells around it.
Begins once a slipped variant holds an edge, since from there each division widens the line's share and the widening compounds.

## Direction

Turns a loose variant into an expanding clone.
Because a cell that leaves more copies leaves more copies, winning converts a single unheld line into a growing share of the marrow, the edge compounding down the generations of cells, so the process is nothing but selection resumed inside the body, the same variation-and-selection that built the living world now sorting the body's own cells against the body.

## Lever

The plain arithmetic of selection.
Because an advantage in dividing or in not dying means a larger next generation, winning works through a rule that needs no help and no aim: more begets more, so the lever is selection itself, blind and central, the cell fitting its niche and not choosing the harm, and it climbs by the same law whether the ground is a meadow or a marrow, indifferent to which it is running in.

## Echo

A clone climbing, the contest resumed one level down.
The echo of winning is a line grown from one cell to a power in the marrow, its share rising with every division and its edge intact in every daughter, so the competition the body had held shut is open again beneath it, a lower order sorting itself by advantage inside a higher order that no longer holds it.
