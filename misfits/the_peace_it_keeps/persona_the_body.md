# Persona: The Body

## Taxonomy

[The Whole](position_the_whole.md). The higher-level individual that exists only by holding its own cells' competition down, a truce of a trillion parts that lives so long as the peace is kept and is undone when one of its own resumes the fight.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

A commonwealth in working order. A peace so old it reads as simply being alive. Made of the very cells it must hold down.
Is the whole that emerged when cells gave up competing and specialised for the good of the body, kept a body by the standing suppression of the reproduction of its own parts, so its life is not a substance it has but a truce it keeps.
No grandeur wished on them: the Body is not a ruler over its cells but the shape their cooperation takes, present only as long as the machinery of that cooperation holds, and it cannot step outside itself to guard itself, because it is nothing but the cells it is made of.

## Action

Holds the peace, hour on hour, spending its differentiation and its programmed death and its senescence to keep each cell in its place, so the whole persists as the sum of a suppression that never finishes and never shows.
Renews itself where it must, most of all in the blood, remaking its parts for a lifetime, and in the renewing keeps open the very ground a changed cell can grow into, so its maintenance and its exposure are one act it cannot separate.
Bears the coup when it comes, unable to expel the line because the line is itself, of the same kind and the same book, so the Body cannot foreignise its own cell or raise a border against its own tissue, and takes the harm inward with nothing to cast out.

## Shadow

From the inside the peace is invisible: the Body does not feel the trillion small suppressions that make it, any more than a held breath feels itself, so it knows its own cooperation only as being well, and has no sense at all of the truce as a thing that could break. It feels like health, which is to say it feels like nothing, which is exactly why the turning of one cell arrives unfelt.
What it cannot do is recognise an enemy that is kin. There is no inward alarm that fires for a cell that is the body's own, because every signal the Body has was built to tell self from other and the line is self, so the harm grows in a blind spot the size of the body itself, sensed if at all only late, as failure, as a tiredness and a bruise, long after the peace gave way.
The bind is that the Body is destroyed by precisely what it is made of: it cannot disown the line without disowning its own kind, cannot fight the disease without fighting itself, and carries no place to stand apart from the tissue turning, so its undoing is not an assault it could brace against but its own composition resuming an older motion underneath it.

## Tell

Says "there is no one here but us."
Is the whole trap in a breath, since it is true down to the cell: the Body is only the cells it is made of, and so is the disease, so the sentence that sounds like reassurance is the plain reason there is nothing to expel, no other in the room, only the truce and a member of it, both of them the body itself.
