# Position: The Knife

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The remedy seat, which can strike dividing cells but cannot tell the clone's dividing from the renewal's, so it lowers the harm and the protection together, and buys a cure, where it comes, only as a trade.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The weapons that reach dividing cells, and the judgement of how far.
The seat holds the poison that kills what proliferates, the option to ablate and replace the whole blood system, and the standing to titrate them against the clock, reading the counts and the marrow to decide the dose.
A strike on all division and none on the clone alone, since the seat can lower what divides but cannot lower only the line, so what it holds acts on the marrow and the clone at once.

## Orders

Lower the line, and stop short of losing the body.
Cut the clone hard enough to win and not so hard that the emptied marrow or the infection takes the patient, titrating between two failures by the week, since the right dose cannot be known in advance and the line can return.
Win the trade rather than remove the harm, since the seat's only move spends the renewal to spend the clone and buys a remission at a price, never a clean excision.

## Loses

The selectivity between the clone and the renewal, which the seat's one kind of weapon cannot make.
The Knife can kill dividing cells and cannot spare the dividing marrow, because the clone and the renewal are the same kind of thing, cells that proliferate, so no dose reaches the one without reaching the other. What the position forfeits is a clean removal: there is no strike that lifts the line out and leaves the blood, since the property the weapon targets is the property the healthy tissue shares. And it forfeits completeness, since to cut the sensitive line hardest can clear the room around the resistant remnant and release it, so the seat's success can seed the return, and the more thorough the strike the more it can select for what survives it.

## Drives

Toward a remedy that is a trade held between two failures,
and a strike that cannot spare the renewal or finish the line, spending the marrow to lower the clone and sometimes freeing the resistant remnant to come back,
so the seat that alone can lower the harm is the seat that must spend the body's own blood to do it, its cure real but costly and its every dose paid on a knife-edge in the patient's own marrow.
