# The Peace It Keeps: Reference

The research warrant for [The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md).
The dynamic is the somatic clonal evolution of cancer, staged in the blood: Nowell's finding that a tumour is a Darwinian
process among the body's own cell lineages, Buss and Michod on the organism as an evolutionary transition that suppresses
cell-level selection, Aktipis and colleagues on cancer as the breakdown of multicellular cooperation, Jaiswal, Genovese
and Watson on the clonal haematopoiesis that renewal accrues with age, Greaves and Maley on clonal evolution and its
selection of resistance, and Miraki-Moud and colleagues on the marrow failing by a jammed maturation rather than an
eviction, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging somatic clonal evolution as a **truce-broken-from-within structure**: a body is a higher-level individual that
exists only by holding its own cells' competition down, and because the blood must be renewed for a lifetime the
suppression can never rest, so a single cell that gains a heritable edge resumes the ancient cell-level selection inside
the body, expands by the plain logic of advantage, and undoes the whole it is a part of, jamming the normal blood and
poisoning its ground rather than evicting it, until the body fails of its own overgrown tissue with nothing foreign in
it. The play models the documented mechanics, the truce that makes a body and the machinery that keeps it, the renewal
that keeps the tissue alive and the competition armed together, the edge that lets one line win by blind selection, and
the jamming by which the winning line starves the blood of function, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not
as a tale of a rogue cell that means harm or a guard that failed at its post. The governing finding is the play's
governing law: a cancer is the body's own cell, evolving inside the body by variation and selection, so the disease is a
lower-level evolutionary process resuming under a higher-level individual that was built to suppress it, the killer a
component of its victim. It is a tendency under stated conditions and not an iron rule, holding where the suppression
erodes and an advantaged line escapes the layered checks, and sparing where the checks hold or the change confers no
edge, so the play stages the coup a renewing body can be undone by, not a fate written into every cell.

The distinct load-bearing vector is the level asymmetry, and the play is pinned to it, because without it the structure
collapses onto the house's Fit to Fail. Fit to Fail stages evolutionary suicide, where individual selection installs a
trait that lowers the population's viability until it crosses a threshold and crashes: there the selected unit and the
doomed unit are the same kind of thing at one level, an individual degrading the population it belongs to, and the
lethal trait must reach fixation across a reproducing population. Here the selected unit is a cell and the doomed unit is
the organism, a higher-level individual of a different kind that exists only as the suppressor of the very cell-level
selection that destroys it, so the killer is not a member of a population it degrades but a component of the single body
it undoes, and the fight is per organism and heritable to no host generation, the clone dying with its host and fixing
across nothing. That two-level, suppressed-transition structure, a lower order rising against the higher order that held
it down, is the whole of the daylight, and it is where the play lives: not the renewal that grows the clone, which alone
reads as evolutionary suicide, and not the language of selfish cells and broken cooperation, which fits the commons and
the suicide alike, but the coup of a part against the whole it composes.

It is distinct too from the house's other neighbours. It is not The Commons, whose engine is rational agents depleting a
shared and reversible resource with a restraint lever they fail to pull: cell suppression is structural and not chosen,
there is no forum in which cells agree to limit, the clone kills by displacing tissue and not by drawing down a stock,
and a mutated line does not spontaneously re-cooperate. It is not In Its Own Defence, whose engine is a defence response
overshooting the threat and wrecking the host with its own weapons: here the immune surveillance is one of the
suppressors whose lapse permits the clone, so where immunity bears on this it is by under-reaction and not overshoot, and
the harm is the body's own building process resumed, not its own defence turned up. And it is not No Way Back Up, whose
engine is the drift-loss of the fittest class in an asexual population with no selection: this is the mirror, a fitter
class winning by positive selection and not a fitter class lost by chance, the clone gaining ground and not the lineage
shedding it.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a rogue cell that means
  harm or a guard that slept at its post, because the source finding is precisely that no intent and no malice is
  needed: the line expands because a line that leaves more copies leaves more copies, blind selection with the cell only
  fitting its niche (Nowell; Greaves and Maley), the suppression lapses by the cumulative erosion of layered and
  redundant checks and not by a single betrayal (Buss; Michod), and the clone is the body's own cell, of the same kind
  as the tissue it wrecks. So the misfit is that the truce which makes a body can be broken by one of its own members
  doing only what an advantage does, no rebel and no malice required, each division correct and each check innocent in
  its miss and the coup their sum. It is not, in most cases, without any external cause: ionising radiation, benzene,
  prior cytotoxic therapy, and inherited and age-related predispositions are established risks and are not denied, so
  the play holds that for most patients no specific trigger is found and the disease traces to the somatic mutation the
  body's own renewal accrues, not that a cause never exists.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a body saved by a clean strike. The one move that would lower
  the line is to kill dividing cells, but the body's own renewal is dividing cells, so cytotoxic therapy spends the
  marrow to spend the clone; the transplant that cures does so by ablating and replacing the whole blood system, its
  graft-versus-leukaemia effect inseparable from the graft-versus-host harm; and cutting the sensitive line can release
  the resistant remnant it was holding down, so eradication can breed the relapse (Greaves and Maley; Gatenby). Even the
  cleanest targeted case, imatinib in chronic myeloid leukaemia, is usually lifelong and defeated by resistance, with
  roughly half relapsing when the drug is withdrawn. The remedy is therefore a trade held between failures and never a
  free rescue, the renewal spent to spend the harm. It is a costly cure and not an absent one: some leukaemias are
  genuinely won, acute promyelocytic leukaemia cured in the large majority and childhood acute lymphoblastic leukaemia
  survived by most, so the play holds the remedy costly and not futile, the price of every cure and not the lack of any.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the finding that a cancer is a Darwinian process
  in the body's own cells is Nowell's, the organism as a suppressed evolutionary transition is Buss and Michod's, the
  cooperation-and-cheating framing is Aktipis and colleagues', the clonal haematopoiesis of renewal is Jaiswal,
  Genovese and Watson's, the branching clonal-evolution synthesis and therapy-as-selection are Greaves and Maley's, and
  the jammed-not-evicted marrow failure is Miraki-Moud and colleagues', each carried with its own guardrails: selection
  is central but not the sole author, drift and the niche matter too; clonal expansion is near-universal at depth but
  CHIP as clinically defined is highly prevalent, not universal in all; and the clone jams and poisons rather than
  evicts. The misfit stages a body undone by one of its own cells and credits that no cell was a villain and the body's
  suppression did its long work, holding it a tendency under stated conditions and not a law that fixes every renewing
  body at the harm.
