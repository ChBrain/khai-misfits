# Piece: The Truce

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). The suppression that makes a body, the standing subordination of each cell's own reproduction to the good of the whole, an achievement held every hour and never finished.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Kept throughout the body, held hardest at [the Marrow](place_the_marrow.md), where the renewing cells must be held down as they divide.
Flows as the peace that lets a trillion cells cooperate, the signals to specialise, to stop, to die, and the surveillance that clears the ones that miss them, running under everything the body does.

## Load Bearing

Without the Truce there is no body, only a colony of cells competing: it is the suppression of cell-level competition that makes the many into one, and the same suppression that a single member can slip.
The Truce is load bearing because it is the whole structure at its root, the organism understood as an evolutionary transition in which cell-level selection was subordinated to the whole and pressed down by machinery, not abolished but held (Buss; Michod). It is what makes a body possible, since without the peace the cells would sort themselves by advantage and no whole would stand; and it is exactly what the misfit turns on, because a suppression that must be kept continuously is a suppression that can lapse, and a whole that stands on holding its parts down is a whole a part can undo by slipping the hold. So the Truce carries the trap's foundation: the body is not a substance but a kept peace, alive only while the keeping lasts, and the disease is not an invasion of the peace but a member of it resuming the fight the peace exists to prevent.

## Apparent

Just being alive, health with no seam in it.
Seen at the surface, the Truce is invisible, since a peace kept well feels like nothing at all, only the ordinary fact of a working body, and the truth that this ease is a suppression held every hour, that the body is an achievement standing on the held-down competition of its own cells, that what reads as simply living is a truce that can break, stays hidden behind how completely a kept peace looks like no peace needed.
