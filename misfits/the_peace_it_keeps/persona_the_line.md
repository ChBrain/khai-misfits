# Persona: The Line

## Taxonomy

[The Advantage](position_the_advantage.md). The body's own cell-line that gained a heritable edge and, doing only what an advantage does, out-replicates its neighbours and spreads through the marrow it is made of, undoing the whole by the plain arithmetic of leaving more copies.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Multiplying without pause. Winning a fight it does not know it is in. Made of the body, and indifferent to it.
Is the descendant of one cell that changed, carrying a small heritable advantage in dividing or in not dying, and passing it to every daughter, so the line grows because each of its cells does what its edge lets it do.
No malice wished on them: the Line intends nothing and rebels against nothing, it only leaves more copies than its neighbours because that is what an advantage is, the same rule that fills a meadow with the faster grass, running here inside a body that is made of the grass.

## Action

Divides, and passes the edge on, so the advantage compounds down the generations of cells and the line's share of the marrow climbs with no step that is not simply a cell doing what it can.
Slips the checks that would end it, not by cunning but by holding, one changed cell escaping the order to specialise, to stop, to die, and every daughter inheriting the escape, so the line persists where a normal cell would have been retired.
Takes the ground the body renews, filling the space the marrow makes for fresh blood, so the very room kept open for renewal becomes the room the line grows into, its spread nothing but the winning of a competition the body had held shut.

## Shadow

From the inside there is no inside for the body: the Line is all forward, the flat and tireless pressure of one more division and one more, and nothing in it can register the whole it belongs to, because it is not the kind of thing that holds a whole in view. It does not feel itself as a self against the body; it does not feel the body at all.
What it cannot know is that everything it destroys is its own kin. The cells it starves are its cousins, built from the same book, and the ground it poisons is the ground it grew from, but there is no sense in the Line that carries kinship or debt, only the plain sufficiency of an edge that works. It wins the way water finds the low place, without meaning and without a stopping-point of its own.
The bind is that it is most itself exactly when it is most ruinous: to divide and to hold and to take the ground is to be a healthy cell in every particular but its address, so the Line is never more than a cell doing well, and the doing-well is the disease, with nothing in the cell that could ever tell it so.

## Tell

Says "I am only doing what any of you would."
Is the whole misfit in one line, since it is true: the Line is the body's own cell, no different in kind from the tissue it wrecks, doing only what an advantage does, so the sentence that sounds like a plea is the plain fact that there is no villain here, only a member of the truce doing what any member could and breaking it by succeeding.
