# Place: The Bedside

## Taxonomy

[The Peace It Keeps](play_the_peace_it_keeps.md). Where the remedy is faced, the counts read and the dose set, the one place in the play with a person deciding, holding a weapon that lowers the line only by spending the blood.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A place of care, the cure sought and often found.
Shown at the surface is medicine working: the patient attended, the disease named and measured, the treatment chosen and begun, a room whose whole purpose is to lift the harm and send the person home.

## Holds

Holds the trade the play turns on.
The Bedside is where the strike is set against the body it also strikes, so it holds the counts and the marrow and the dose together, the reading of how much clone and how much blood, the one place where the coupling of harm to protection is not endured but decided, weighed by a person by the week.

## Offers

Offers the strike and the remission, and the emptied marrow with them.
Here the line can be lowered, so the Bedside offers the fight and, in some diseases, the cure outright; and offers, through the same strike, the cost of it: the marrow spent to spend the clone, the infection let in through the emptied blood, the resistant remnant freed by too clean a cut, so the room that gives the remission gives the price in the same hand.

## Withheld

Withholds a clean cure, though not always a cure.
What the Bedside cannot offer is a dose that spares the blood: the weapon reaches all division and the renewal is division, so there is no strike that lifts the line and leaves the marrow whole. The place holds no clean setting, only a trade weighed well or badly, and yet it is not a place of no hope, since the trade is sometimes simply won: a leukaemia broken and gone, a life bought at the price and worth it, the cure real and never free.
