# Position: Restorer

## Taxonomy

[The Hollow Word](play_the_hollow_word.md). The seat that would re-anchor the word to a concrete referent, where each use is made to cash out in a specific practice, at the cost of pedantry and a broken shared language.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A word come loose and a ground to tie it to: the term the field spends freely and the concrete practice it is meant to point at.
A sound move: the re-anchoring is real, tying the word back to a thing a field actually does.
No cheap way to make it, because the word goes down easy and the field is happy to spend it, so the check reads as pedantry over a coin no one else questions.

## Orders

Re-anchor the word.
Stop the fluent use, ask what practice the term stands for, and refuse the coin until it buys something specific.
Cash it out, because a word trusted without a referent is a currency without content.

## Loses

The comfort of the shared language, exactly because re-anchoring means doubting a coin the field still accepts.
Because to ask what the word points at is to slow a fluent conversation and sound like a pedant over a term no one else questions, the seat pays continuously to be right, breaking the ease of the shared tongue to tie the word back to the ground, and loses its standing in the fluent room even as it recovers the referent.
It gains a word that cashes out, and loses the comfort of speaking a language no one stops to check.

## Drives

Toward the word re-anchored and the coin made to buy,
each use cashed out in a concrete practice and checked against the thing,
so the seat that would restore the word recovers the referent only by paying, each time, the cost of being the one who broke the fluent room.
