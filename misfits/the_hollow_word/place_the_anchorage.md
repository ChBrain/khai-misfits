# Place: The Anchorage

## Shown

The place the word is made to cash out, the review that asks what the term stands for, the definition pinned to a practice, the moment the coin is refused until it buys something specific.
The Restorer stopping the fluent use, asking what "ownership" points at now, tying the word back to a concrete thing a field actually does, and paying the cost of doubting a coin the field still accepts.

## Holds

The re-anchoring, and the cost it exacts.
Here the word is checked against the referent, so the Anchorage holds the point where the term is made to buy: a coin assayed against the Ground, so it is where the currency of the word is set against its content, and where the one who asks pays to be right, the re-anchoring real and the price continuous, the shared language broken exactly where the word is tied back to the thing.

## Offers

A word that cashes out, and the bill for it.
Because the Anchorage is where the term is checked against the referent, it offers a coin that buys something specific and a field that knows what it now says, the word re-tied to the ground and the drift caught in the speaking, so it hands out a recovered meaning, and hands it out at a price, the fluent room slowed and the asker made to sound like a pedant over a term no one else questions.

## Withheld

Any cheap version of the remedy.
The Anchorage shows the word re-anchored but cannot make the re-anchoring free, because to check the term against the thing is to doubt a coin the field still accepts and to break the ease of the shared tongue, so it withholds the costless correction, letting the referent be recovered only by continuous payment, and, all real remedy and no free one, it withholds the recovered meaning from any field unwilling to pay, each time, the social cost of assaying a word everyone is happy to spend.
