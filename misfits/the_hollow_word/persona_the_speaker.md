# Persona: The Speaker

## Taxonomy

[Speaker](position_speaker.md). The one who keeps using the word in good faith, meaning roughly what they inherited from the speakers before them. The archetype of the many whose sincere, repeated use keeps the currency alive.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Fluent. Sincere. Sure they mean it.
Is any one of the many who say the word, the manager, the engineer, the writer of the job post, the voice in the review that reaches for the term as common coin.
No carelessness in it: the Speaker knows the word, uses it as it is used around them, and means by it about what the field means, the sense nearest to hand supplying the content.

## Action

Says the word, meaning roughly what they inherited, and takes the term as if it still carries the practice it once named.
Says "ownership" or "agile" or "accountability" the way the room says them, certain the sense is plain and shared, because everyone uses the word the same and no one stops to ask.
Does what a speaker must: it uses the living word in the sense it received, and takes the inherited sense for the word's whole content.

## Shadow

Not a loose talker, a faithful user of a word worn smooth by use, and that is the whole difficulty: the use is sincere, and the sense nearest to hand feels not like a thinner inheritance but like the meaning.
Because the word goes down easy and everyone says it the same, the Speaker cannot see the sense as one worn thinner with every mouth it passed through, so takes it for the full content and assumes the field means the same thing by it, then says it again, one more sincere use keeping the coin passing over a referent that has changed or gone, sure to the last that the word still points where it always did.
It uses the word in good faith, and the good faith of its use is exactly what keeps the emptied coin in circulation.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "of course, we do ownership here," and means a thinner thing than the word once named.
Says it having inherited the sense from the room, and cannot tell the inherited sense from the word's full content, so hears its own use as the plain meaning and assumes the field hears the same.
