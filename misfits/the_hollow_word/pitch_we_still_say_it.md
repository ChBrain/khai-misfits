# Pitch: We Still Say It

## Taxonomy

[The Hollow Word](play_the_hollow_word.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Fluent, familiar, settled.
The plain good sense of a field that still says the word, easily, all of it nodding, the term worn smooth by use and taken as common coin, its meaning felt too obvious to spell out.
Nothing false in it on its face: each speaker uses the word in good faith, means roughly what they inherited, and the currency everyone accepts is genuinely accepted.

## Undertow

Under the fluency, a word whose referent has changed or gone while the term stays in circulation, the currency outrunning the content.
Because a word carries only the sense its speakers supply and heavy use across many mouths bleaches that sense away, the field keeps saying the word over an absent practice, each use sincere and the coin still passing, the gap between word and referent invisible because words are not checked against referents in the ordinary course of speaking.
What makes a word usable, that it is smooth and shared and needs no spelling out, is what lets its content drain unremarked.

## Nerve

The feeling of saying the word and being understood, the term plain and the field agreed,
sure the meaning is shared, because everyone still says it and no one asks,
while the practice the word once named has changed or gone, and each speaker means about what they inherited, which is a sense worn thinner with every mouth it passed through.

## Echo

After the run: an old word revealed to cover its opposite, the field saying "ownership" while it centralises and "agile" while it fixes the plan, each use sincere and none of them cashing out,
no one having hollowed the word and no one having lied, only a living term bleached by the very traffic that keeps it alive and a currency that was only ever an acceptance,
and the one move that would have re-anchored the meaning, cashing each use out in a concrete referent before trusting it, forgone because it breaks a shared language and reads as pedantry over a word everyone is happy to say.
