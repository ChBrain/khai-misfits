# Persona: The Field

## Taxonomy

[Field](position_field.md). The collective whose repeated use, across many speakers over time, bleaches the word's content away. The archetype of the many-as-one, the traffic that wears the coin, present in no single speaker and authored by none.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Many. Fluent. Nowhere in particular.
Is the discipline, the profession, the company, the whole population of speakers who say the word between them, none of whom is the field and all of whom compose it.
No agent in it: the Field intends nothing, empties nothing on purpose, and does only what many sincere speakers do together, saying the word again and again until the sense wears.

## Action

Circulates the word, mouth to mouth and year to year, and by the sheer traffic thins its sense and loosens its referent.
Passes the term down the line, each speaker a little further from the practice than the last, so the content drains not at any point but along the whole length of the passing.
Does what a field of speakers must: it keeps the useful word in use, and the use itself is what bleaches the content, no one meaning to wear the coin they keep spending.

## Shadow

Not a mob and not a mind, a distribution of sincere uses whose collective drift no single user can see, and that is the whole difficulty: the bleaching lives between the speakers and not in any of them.
Because each speaker means about what they inherited and each inheritance is a little thinner than the last, the loss is real and cumulative and authored by no one, a property of the language and not a decision of any mouth, so the Field cannot notice the draining any more than a river notices the bank it wears, and the word goes hollow inside the very currency that keeps it good.
It empties the word through nothing but faithful use, and the fact that no one is emptying it is exactly why no one catches it.

## Tell

Cannot be pointed at, only overheard: the word everywhere, the practice nowhere, the sense that "we all know what that means" true of the room and false of the referent.
Says nothing itself, because it has no mouth of its own, only the many mouths that say the word and, between them, wear it to a token no single one of them thinned.
