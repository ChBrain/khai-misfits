# Persona: The Restorer

## Taxonomy

[Restorer](position_restorer.md). The one who would re-anchor the word to a concrete referent, insisting each use cash out in a specific practice, at the cost of sounding like a pedant and breaking a shared language. The archetype of the reformer whose remedy is real and dear.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Exacting. Uneasy. Willing to be the pedant.
Is the one who notices the word has come loose, the editor, the sceptic, the voice that stops the fluent sentence to ask what exactly it points at now.
No zealotry in it: the Restorer does not want to ban the word, only to make it cash out, to tie the term back to a thing a field actually does.

## Action

Stops the fluent use to ask what practice the word stands for, and insists each use name a concrete referent before it is trusted.
Cashes "ownership" out into who decides what without asking, cashes "agile" out into which plan changed and when, refusing the token until it buys something specific.
Does what a restorer must: it re-anchors the word to the ground, and pays, each time, the cost of doubting a coin the field still accepts.

## Shadow

Not a purist and not a scold, a re-anchorer of a word the field is happy to spend as it is, and that is the whole difficulty: the remedy is real and the price is continuous.
Because the word goes down easy and everyone says it, to stop and ask what it points at is to slow a fluent conversation, to sound like a pedant over a term no one else questions, to break a shared language the field is comfortable speaking, so the Restorer is right and costly at once, re-anchoring the word only by treating a smooth old coin as suspect precisely because it goes down easy, against every signal that the field speaks the same tongue.
It ties the word back to the ground, and the tying is dear enough that the field mostly lets the coin keep passing.

## Tell

Asks, "when we say ownership, who decides what, exactly?" and is heard as splitting hairs over a word everyone understands.
Asks it because the coin has stopped buying anything definite, and cannot make the asking cheap, so re-anchors the word at the price of being the one who broke the fluent room.
