# Process: Bleaching

## Taxonomy

[The Hollow Word](play_the_hollow_word.md). The collective repeated use draining the word's content, undeliberately, the sense generalising and thinning as the term passes across many mouths over time.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A useful word in heavy circulation.
In [the currency](place_the_currency.md), the term passing hand to hand, each speaker meaning about what they inherited and the referent slipping a little further with each passing.

## Direction

Toward the content drained and the referent loosened.
Because a word carries only the sense its speakers supply and each supplies about what the last one did, the sheer traffic of the term across many mouths thins the sense and loosens its hold on the referent, the practice changing or going while the word stays, so the content drains not at any point but along the whole length of the passing, the loss cumulative and authored by no single mouth (Traugott and Dasher; Hopper and Traugott, on semantic bleaching, the loss of content through use).

## Lever

The traffic itself.
Because the word is worn by the very use that keeps it alive, and no speaker means to wear it, bleaching runs not on any speaker's error but on the standing property of a living word in constant use, the sense thinning like a coin worn smooth by handling, the loss real and cumulative and intended by none.

## Echo

A word worn to a token, and no one having worn it.
The field has kept the useful word in use, so what reads as a stable term is, in truth, a coin bleached of content by its own circulation, still passing at face value, the referent departed along the passing and the loss belonging to the language and not to any speaker.
