# Persona: The Neighbour

## Taxonomy

[The Address](position_the_address.md). Holds a mild preference about the local mix and moves only when they would be too small a minority, tolerant and lawful and the engine of the sort.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Tolerant. Settled. Content among the other kind.
Is the ordinary resident who would happily live on a mixed street and asks only not to be the last of their own on it, holding a modest preference that a wide range of mixes satisfies.
No bigotry wished on them: they seek no separation, hold no ill will, and would stay in a mixed block indefinitely so long as they are not reduced below the share at which they feel too few.

## Action

Reads the mix around them and stays while content, and moves only when their like-share drops below the line at which they would rather be elsewhere.
Answers their own comfort, not any wish about the whole: the move is a private reluctance to be too small a minority, made without reference to the map it helps compose, and every move is both an exit that thins one block and a search for another that fits.
Does what a tolerant resident does: lives where they are comfortable, leaves where they are not, and asks nothing of anyone, no preference for segregation required to relocate when the mix around them has crossed their line.

## Shadow

Not a bigot, but the unit whose mild wish is the whole engine, and that is the quiet of it: the preference is tolerant and the move is lawful, and the sum of tolerant moves is a segregated city.
Because the Neighbour will not stay below their comfort line, a block that drifts past it loses them, and their leaving lowers the like-share for the next resident, so a modest wish held by everyone propagates each departure into the next and composes, through the threshold inside it, into a sort no one holds.
The wish is mild and the move is free, so the tolerant resident who simply declines to be too few is the one whose blameless choice, summed across the city, draws a line they would never draw themselves.

## Tell

Says "I have nothing against anyone; I would just rather not be the only one of my kind on the street."
Is telling the plain truth and means exactly what they say, and cannot see that the truth of it is the trap, that a preference this modest, held by all and met by a mixed street, still sorts the city once a threshold is crossed, because the line lives inside the very tolerance that clears their name.
