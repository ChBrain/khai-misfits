# Plot: The Separation

## Taxonomy

[The Sort](play_the_sort.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Sorted City](place_the_sorted_city.md), the local tips composed into a map drawn in clean lines.
A whole city separated, quarter from quarter, the boundaries sharp and the blocks uniform, looking for all the world designed.

## Action

[Sorting](process_sorting.md): the tipped blocks compose across the map into a stark, stable segregation, and the Integrationist finds the only lever that would hold the mix is a constraint on every lawful move.

## Stage

[The Integrationist](persona_the_integrationist.md), on [Hold the Mix](plan_hold_the_mix.md), sees from [the overview](position_the_overview.md) the whole [tipping](piece_the_tipping.md) set, and reaches to keep the composition off [the threshold](piece_the_threshold.md), only to find that the moves that sorted it were each a mild and lawful exercise of [the preference](piece_the_preference.md).
The aim is decent and the sort is done, and the sole remedy left is force against people who did nothing wrong.

## Tension

Wanting to keep the city mixed is the decent wish, and the decent wish can be served only by constraining the blameless.
There is no villain to stop: the map was drawn by no one, each move that made it was tolerant and lawful, and no resident can be argued out of a preference a mixed street would have met, so the Integrationist has nothing to persuade and no gentle lever to pull. But the sort is the sum of free choices, so holding it off means ruling those choices, and the plot closes with the city separated and the only cure a standing limit on where tolerant people may live, the reckoning a mix held by force against everyone's small freedom or a separation reached by the free use of it, and, where the threshold bites, no third path in which free choice alone keeps the street mixed.
