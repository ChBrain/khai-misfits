# Pitch: No One Chose It

## Taxonomy

[The Sort](play_the_sort.md). The single hook: tolerant people, each wanting only not to be too small a minority, sort a whole city into a stark segregation none of them wanted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A mixed street of tolerant neighbours, no one preferring segregation, each content to live among the other kind so long as they are not the last of their own.
The tenor is a modest, lawful wish held by everyone: not a taste for separation, only a reluctance to be too small a minority, the kind of preference a mixed street satisfies for all at once.

## Undertow

Inside the mild wish is a threshold, and thresholds tip.
Underneath, each tolerant preference carries a hidden line, the share of like neighbours below which content turns to departure, so a single move can push a neighbour under their line, and their move push the next, the small wishes cascading past a tipping point no one felt themselves approach.

## Nerve

The macro-pattern is nothing like the micro-wish: mild preferences compose into an intolerant map.
The nerve is that no one is a bigot and no one chose the line, yet the line is drawn hard and clean, the segregated city an emergent sum of tolerant wishes each of which a mixed street would have met, so there is no cause to point to, only an aggregate that matches no one's preference.

## Echo

The only thing that keeps the street mixed is a constraint on the freedom that sorted it.
What lingers is that the remedy has a price no free society pays easily: holding the mix off the threshold means telling tolerant people where they may and may not move, so the mixed street survives only by force against the mild, lawful preference that, left free, sorts the city every time.
