# Play: The Sort

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Sort.
On a mixed street everyone is content, and no one wants segregation. Each resident holds only a mild preference: they would rather not be the last of their kind on the block, would rather not drop below, say, a third of the neighbours near them like themselves, a modest and tolerant wish that leaves plenty of room for a mixed street to satisfy everyone at once. But the wish has a threshold in it, and thresholds tip. One family moves, for reasons of their own, and the block they leave is now a little more one-sided; a neighbour who was just above their comfort line is now just below it, so they move too, and their leaving pushes the next neighbour under, and a mixed block that satisfied everyone a week ago empties of one kind entirely. The vacancies fill with the other, because a newcomer has the same mild preference and settles where they will not be too few, and so the block tips the rest of the way and sets. Repeat across a city and the map sorts itself: stark, near-total separation, produced by people none of whom wanted it, each acting on a preference that was tolerant, lawful, and mild. The macro-pattern is nothing like the micro-wish. And the shape recurs wherever a weak same-kind preference meets a threshold and the freedom to move: nobody is a bigot, nobody chose the line, and yet the line is drawn, hard and clean, by the sum of a thousand modest wishes no one of which drew it.

## Arc

The bend is this: when each agent holds a mild preference about the local mix and will move to satisfy it, the individually tolerant wishes aggregate, through a tipping threshold, into a stark segregation no one intended or preferred, the macro-pattern discontinuous in a smooth and modest change of taste (Schelling).
It runs on a preference that is mild and a threshold that is not.
Each resident wants only not to be too small a minority where they live, a tolerant wish satisfiable by a wide range of mixes, so no one holds a preference for segregation and a mixed street meets everyone's wish at once (Schelling; Clark). The preference is modest, and it is the whole of what anyone wants.
And the mild wish carries a hidden line.
Beneath the tolerance is a threshold, the share of like neighbours below which a resident becomes uncomfortable and moves, and though the threshold is generous it is a cliff, a point at which content turns to departure, so the smooth taste hides a sharp trigger (Grodzins; Granovetter). The threshold is the tip point, and everyone stands somewhere near one.
And one move moves the next.
A single departure, for any reason, lowers a block's like-share for those who remain, tipping a neighbour who was just above their line to just below it, so they move in turn and tip the next, each relocation both an exit that thins one block and an entry that fills another (Sakoda; Schelling). The move is the propagation, and it does not stop at one.
And the block tips and sets.
Once enough have gone the block crosses its threshold for the rest, the cascade running to completion as each departure unsettles another, and the vacancies fill with the other kind because a newcomer's identical mild preference settles them where they will not be too few, so the block empties of one kind and fills with the other and sets, sorted (Grodzins; Schelling). The tipping is discontinuous, a mixed block becoming a segregated one with no one having wanted the change.
And the map sorts itself.
Repeated across the city the local tips compose a global pattern, a stark and near-total separation that matches no one's preference and that no one designed, the aggregate of tolerant wishes producing an intolerant map (Schelling). The sort is emergent, the macro-behaviour nothing like the micro-motive that drove it.
None of it is bigotry. The resident who moves below their comfort line, the newcomer who settles where they fit, the family that leaves for reasons of its own: each acts on a mild and lawful preference, and the segregated city emerges from the sum, no bigot and no plan required, which is exactly why it is so hard to name a cause and harder to undo.
Hold the mix off the threshold and the sort is prevented: but to do so is to override the mild, lawful preference of each resident, to tell tolerant people where they may and may not move, so the one intervention that keeps the street mixed is a standing constraint on ordinary and blameless choice, and the reckoning is a mix held by force against everyone's small freedom or a separation reached by the free exercise of it, never a street that stays mixed because everyone, left alone, wished it so.

## Stakes

Whether a city can stay mixed by the free choice of tolerant people, or is sorted by the very thresholds hidden inside their mild and lawful preferences.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the sorted map, which the Neighbour is right to leave when they would be too few and the Newcomer right to settle where they fit and each resident right to act on a preference that is modest and lawful, and away from the mixed street, which no one's wish sustains once a threshold is crossed. The production cannot make the segregation a bigotry, because each preference is tolerant and each move is blameless, so leaving when too few is the reasonable act and settling where one fits the ordinary one: the residents doing no more than declining to be the last of their kind are only exercising a small and lawful freedom. It can only show that no one drew the line, that a thousand mild preferences, each satisfiable by a mixed street, compose through a tipping threshold into a separation none of them held, and that the one move that would keep the street mixed, holding the composition off the threshold, is a standing constraint on the free and blameless choice of where to live, so the map sorts not by anyone's malice but by the ordinary tolerance and ordinary freedom of everyone who moved to where they were content.
