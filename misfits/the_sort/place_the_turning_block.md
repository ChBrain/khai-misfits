# Place: The Turning Block

## Taxonomy

[The Sort](play_the_sort.md). The tipping scene, a block that has crossed a resident's threshold and begun the cascade, content turning to departure and back again.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A block in motion: one household gone, a neighbour packing, a vacancy filled by the other kind, the mix sliding as you watch.
Shown as sudden and self-driven, a street that was settled a week ago now emptying and refilling, the change arriving faster than any decision could explain.

## Holds

Holds the cascade, each departure lowering the like-share and unsettling the next resident in turn.
The Turning Block is where the threshold does its work: a first move pushes a neighbour below their line, whose move pushes the next, and the vacancies fill with the kind the block is tilting toward, so the composition runs one way and does not come back.

## Offers

Offers each mover a sound reason to go, and each arrival a genuine fit.
Here every single move is individually right, the leaver truly now too few and the newcomer truly more comfortable, so the block offers no one a wrong choice even as the choices compose into a rout.

## Withheld

Withholds the point of return, already behind it.
What the Turning Block hides is that its slide has no natural stopping place short of the sorted end: once the composition crosses the threshold there is no mix at which the departures halt and the balance re-forms, so the block that looks merely unsettled is in fact past the line from which it could have been held, on its way to set.
