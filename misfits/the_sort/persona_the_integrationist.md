# Persona: The Integrationist

## Taxonomy

[The Overview](position_the_overview.md). Wants the mixed street kept and finds it can be kept only by constraining the mild, lawful move of every resident, the remedy and its cost embodied.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Watching the whole. Committed to the mix. Reaching for a fix that does not exist for free.
Is the one who values the integrated street and sees, from the vantage of the whole map, the sort setting in, and who wants to hold the neighbourhood mixed against the drift.
No zealotry wished on them: the commitment is decent and the aim is good, and the difficulty is not their motive but the fact that the only lever on a self-sorting city is a constraint on the choices that sort it.

## Action

Would hold the mix, and reaches for the levers that might, only to find each one is a limit on where residents may move.
Answers the map, not a single block, seeing the sort compose from a height no resident occupies, and wanting to arrest it, but the moves that produce it are mild and lawful, so arresting them means quotas, controls, a hand laid on ordinary choice.
Does what a person who values integration does: names the drift, argues for the mix, and proposes to hold it, and discovers that holding it is not persuasion but constraint, the mild preferences too reasonable to argue away and too many to move.

## Shadow

Not a tyrant, but the one whose only working remedy is coercion of the blameless, and that is the bind: to keep the street mixed is to override the tolerant wish of every resident, so the defender of the mix must become the constrainer of freedom or watch the sort proceed.
Because the Integrationist sees the whole and holds no lever over the parts, they can prevent the sort only by holding the composition off the threshold, which no persuasion achieves because no resident is doing wrong, so the good aim runs into a cost it cannot avoid, a limit on lawful choice.
The aim is decent and the lever is coercive, so the one who most wants the mixed street is the one forced to choose between constraining innocent moves and letting the city sort, no third path on offer.

## Tell

Says "the street is worth keeping mixed; surely we can hold it together."
Is right that the mix is worth keeping and cannot make the holding free, because the only thing that would keep it is a rule against the moves that unmake it, so the decent wish for an integrated street meets the fact that its sole remedy lands on people who have done nothing wrong.
