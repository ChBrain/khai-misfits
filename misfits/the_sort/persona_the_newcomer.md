# Persona: The Newcomer

## Taxonomy

[The Vacancy](position_the_vacancy.md). Arrives and settles where they will not be too few, filling the openings a tipping block leaves and carrying it the rest of the way.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Arriving. Choosing. Looking for somewhere to fit.
Is the resident moving in from elsewhere, weighing the open homes and settling where they will not be too small a minority, holding the same mild preference as everyone already there.
No design wished on them: they seek only a place they are comfortable, intend nothing about the block's future, and would as readily have taken a mixed street had one offered them the fit they wanted.

## Action

Fills a vacancy where the mix suits them, choosing among the openings the place they will not be too few, and by settling there shifts that block's like-share toward their own kind.
Answers their own comfort as any resident does, but arrives precisely where the departures have left room, so the openings a tipping block offers are filled by those the tipping suits, and the entry pushes the block further the way it was already going.
Does what anyone looking for a home does: takes the open spot that fits them best, no wish about the neighbourhood's balance required to nudge that balance by the plain act of moving in.

## Shadow

Not a colonist, but the fill that sets the tip, and that is the sting: the vacancies a block sheds are filled by the kind the block is tipping toward, because that is the kind those openings now suit, so the sort completes itself through blameless arrivals.
Because the Newcomer settles where they will not be too few, they arrive where their own kind already predominates, and a block half-emptied of one kind draws newcomers of the other, whose entry raises the like-share further and draws the next, so the filling is as self-reinforcing as the leaving.
The choice is free and the fit is honest, so the arrival who simply takes the home that suits them is the one who sets the tipped block, completing a separation they did not intend by moving where they were welcome.

## Tell

Says "I looked for somewhere I would feel at home, and this is where I found it."
Is telling the truth and choosing exactly as anyone would, and cannot see that the home that felt right felt right because the block had already begun to tip, so the honest search for a fit lands them where they complete the sort, the openness of the vacancy indistinguishable, to them, from a welcome.
