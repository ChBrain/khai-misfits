# Place: The High Street

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md). Where spending meets income, the crossing at which one household's outlay becomes another's wage.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Tills and counters where the coin changes hands: a purchase here, a wage there, the same money passing from spender to earner and round again.
The takings rising and falling with the footfall, and, as the savers stay home, the tills coming up light and the earners who depend on them beginning to feel it.

## Holds

The circular flow made visible, the point where the withdrawal shows.
Here spending is plainly income, so the high street holds the link the household could not see: every coin not laid down is a wage not drawn, so it is where a general thrift stops being a private virtue and becomes a public shortfall, the crossing at which saved money registers as someone's lost livelihood.

## Offers

Each earner a living, exactly as large as the spending that reaches it.
Because the street pays out only what is spent into it, it offers full wages to a street full of spenders and thin ones to a street of savers, so it shows, in the plainest coin, that incomes here are the mirror of spending and fall the moment it does.

## Withheld

Any income the saving does not fund.
The high street cannot pay a wage from a coin kept at home, because it hands on only what crosses its counters, so it withholds the earnings the withheld spending would have been, and being the crossing and not the source it withholds any remedy of its own, only passing on the fall.
