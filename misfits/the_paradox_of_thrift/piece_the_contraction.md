# Piece: The Contraction

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md). The downward spiral, the fall in demand that lowers incomes that lower demand again, magnified by the multiplier.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the high street](place_the_high_street.md), where the trimmed wages become the next round's weaker demand.
Deepened at [the vault](place_the_vault.md), where the leaked saving drains the flow it never returns to.

## Load Bearing

Without it the withdrawal would be a one-off dip, and the economy would settle a little lower and stop.
With it the loss compounds: each fall in spending cuts incomes, which cut spending again, so the shortfall is multiplied through the flow rather than absorbed by it. The Contraction is load bearing because it is why a modest thrift makes a large slump (Kahn; Keynes; Fisher): the multiplier turns a single withdrawal into a spiral, each turn feeding the next, so the piece carries the self-feeding descent that makes the paradox costly and not merely curious.

## Apparent

A worsening economy, bad luck compounding, a downturn getting away from everyone.
Seen as it runs, the contraction looks like misfortune gathering pace, and the truth that it is the sum of sound economies, each firm and household rationally cutting to its fallen means, that the spiral is made entirely of prudent moves, stays hidden behind how much it feels like a slump happening to people rather than through them.
