# Plot: The Spiral

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The High Street](place_the_high_street.md), the vendor with a lighter till cutting to match.
Wages and hours and orders trimmed, and the households whose incomes fall now tightening in their turn.

## Action

[Contracting](process_contracting.md): [The Vendor](persona_the_vendor.md), holding [the vendor's seat](position_vendor.md), trims its costs to the takings, while [The Flow](persona_the_flow.md), holding [the flow's seat](position_flow.md), carries the fall onward as smaller incomes and a smaller demand.

## Stage

[The Vendor](persona_the_vendor.md), having tabled [Trim to Sales](plan_trim_to_sales.md), cuts the wages it can no longer meet, and those wages were spending too.
[The Flow](persona_the_flow.md), on [Carry the Coin](plan_carry_the_coin.md), passes the cut on as another household's fallen income, which tightens next, so the demand drops again and the circle turns downward once more.

## Tension

Every cut is sound, and the sum of them is a slump.
There is no recklessness to blame: each firm trims prudently to its fallen takings, each cut household saves from necessity. But the wages trimmed were the demand that fed the next till, so prudence multiplies into contraction, each careful economy lowering the income behind the next, and the plot closes with the flow shrinking on itself, pulled down not by any failure but by sound management everywhere at once.
