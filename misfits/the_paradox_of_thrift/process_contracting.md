# Process: Contracting

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md). The fall in demand lowering incomes that lower demand again, the circle turning downward.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

Spending withheld and a till come up short.
At [the high street](place_the_high_street.md), the vendor meeting the lighter takings and trimming wages, hours, and orders to fit.

## Direction

Toward incomes and demand falling together, round on round.
Because the wages the vendor cuts were someone's spending, each economy lowers another household's income, which lowers its spending, which lightens the next till, so the contracting drives the flow down in a spiral, each turn feeding the next by the multiplier of the last.

## Lever

The soundness of trimming to a smaller till.
Because each firm must match its costs to its fallen takings, the very prudence that keeps a business solvent removes the wages that were demand, so the contracting works not by any failure but by sound management repeated everywhere, each cut rational and the sum of them a shrinking whole.

## Echo

A flow smaller than before and still shrinking.
Incomes have fallen to meet the saving, more households now tighten from necessity, and the demand drops again, so what looks at each firm like keeping afloat is, in the aggregate, the economy pulling itself down by its own careful economies.
