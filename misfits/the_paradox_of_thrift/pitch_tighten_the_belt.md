# Pitch: Tighten the Belt

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Prudent, responsible, steady.
The plain good sense of spending less and saving more when hard times are coming, of building a store against the lean months.
Nothing rash in it: only the household virtue of living within its means and putting something by.

## Undertow

Under the prudence, a coin lifted out of the flow that feeds every wage.
Because one household's spending is another's income, the belt each tightens is a wage another does not earn, so a whole nation saving at once does not fill its buffers from a steady stream, it dries the stream.
The virtue is real for one and ruinous for all, and the same act is both.

## Nerve

The feeling of doing the responsible thing as the storm comes in,
sure that saving is the safe move, because for you it is,
while every neighbour saves too, and the incomes you were all saving from quietly fall away beneath you.

## Echo

After the run: a nation that tightened its belt into a leaner year, saving a bigger share of a smaller wage,
each household prudent and all of them poorer,
and a flow that would refill if only someone spent, wanted by no one to be the one who does.
