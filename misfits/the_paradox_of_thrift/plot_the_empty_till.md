# Plot: The Empty Till

## Taxonomy

[The Paradox of Thrift](play_the_paradox_of_thrift.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The High Street](place_the_high_street.md), the savers staying home and the tills coming up light.
The coin the saver kept never arriving at the counter, and the earner who lived on it beginning to feel the gap.

## Action

[Leaking](process_leaking.md): [The Vendor](persona_the_vendor.md), holding [the vendor's seat](position_vendor.md), takes less at the till, while [The Hoard](persona_the_hoard.md), holding [the hoard's seat](position_hoard.md), sits on the withheld coin idle, a leak from the flow and not a loan back.

## Stage

[The Vendor](persona_the_vendor.md), having tabled [Trim to Sales](plan_trim_to_sales.md), finds the takings short, the wage it was owed become a saver's buffer instead.
[The Saver](persona_the_saver.md), still on [Build the Buffer](plan_build_the_buffer.md), sees only its own store growing, and [The Hoard](persona_the_hoard.md), on [Hold the Cash](plan_hold_the_cash.md), keeps the coin safe and unlent, so it returns to no one as spending.

## Tension

One household's prudence is another's empty till, and neither did a thing wrong.
There is no fault in the ledger: the saver saved wisely, the coin is safe, the vendor simply took less. But the spending withheld was the vendor's income, and the saved coin, unborrowed, does not come back as anyone's spending, so the withdrawal is real and unreturned, and the plot closes with a buffer grown on one side and a wage missing on the other, the same coin on both.
