# Process: Fencing

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The guarding of an hour for the important against the urgent, the triage by worth that turns the trap back, at the cost of the one who holds it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A calendar allotted ahead and a seat that would triage by worth.
At [the diary](place_the_diary.md), the steward holding a block for the deadline-less work before the day begins, and reaching for what is worth the most rather than what falls due soonest.

## Direction

Toward the hour spent on what matters, if its cost can be borne.
Because the important gives no deadline to drive it and no closure to reward it, fencing must hold its hour by worth alone, against the pull of every loop that would close today, so it drives toward the worthy work done: but keeping the block means letting an urgent thing slip and pay its present cost, so the process spends the life well only by forgoing the visible relief the urgent would have paid.

## Lever

The truth that only a guarded hour reaches the important.
Because the urgent will fill every unfenced hour and the important will never once demand one, holding time in advance is the one move that gets the worthy work done, so fencing runs on a sound reading of the trap: yet the same move that spends the hour on worth shows no met deadline and no closed loop, so the process costs its owner exactly by aiming where nothing falls due.

## Echo

An hour spent on what matters, or a block reclaimed by the clock.
Either the fenced hour holds and the important is done, bought against the urgent let slip and the closure forgone, or the pressing tasks reclaim the block and the worthy work waits again, the triage by worth surrendered to the triage by clock.
