# Process: Deferring

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The important pushed to later, the deadline-less work sliding down the someday list each day, deferred by a hundred reasonable choices into never.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An important task with no deadline and an urgent one that has.
At [the backlog](place_the_backlog.md), the worthy work set aside because nothing forces it today, sliding toward a someday that recedes as it is approached.

## Direction

Toward the important postponed for ever.
Because the important carries no deadline and one more day never costs much, deferring pushes the worthy work to later each time the urgent presses, so it slides down the list day after day, the postponement always reasonable and the sum of the reasonable postponements a task deferred past the end of the working life.

## Lever

The small, true cost of waiting one more day.
Because the important genuinely can wait and waiting once genuinely costs little, each deferral is a sound and almost costless choice, so deferring runs on correct small decisions, the never-done assembled from a hundred one-more-days, no single one wrong and the whole of them fatal.

## Echo

A worthy task safe for later and never done.
The important has been postponed once more and its worth is intact and its someday intended, so what reads as a thing merely kept for a better time is, in truth, a thing deferred past every day it might have been done, patience read as safety until the years are gone.
