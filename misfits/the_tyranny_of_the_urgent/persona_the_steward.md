# Persona: The Steward

## Taxonomy

[Steward](position_steward.md). Would fence the hour for the important and triage by worth rather than clock, at the cost of an urgent thing let slip and the closure it would have brought.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Deliberate. Protective. Looking past the clock.
Is the one who guards a block of time for the deadline-less work and asks not what falls due soonest but what is worth the most.
No disdain for real deadlines in it: only the refusal to let urgency alone decide the hour.

## Action

Fences an hour for the important against the urgent, and triages the day by worth rather than by clock.
Acts on the deadline-less thing before it becomes a crisis, and lets an urgent task slip when the important is worth more.
Does what a good steward of time does: spends the hour where it is worth the most, not where it is demanded loudest.

## Shadow

Not a shirker of real work, a careful steward, and that is the whole difficulty: guarding the hour for the important is right, and it means letting an urgent thing slip and pay its present cost.
Because the important gives no deadline to drive the work and no closure to reward it, the Steward who fences its hour must work against the pull of every loop that would close today, spend on a payoff that never falls due, and bear the real cost of the urgent left undone, so the one move that spends the life well is the one with nothing pressing to justify it and no click of completion to show.
It would give the hour to what matters, and what matters is exactly what never asks for it.

## Tell

Says "the urgent can slip today: this hour is for the thing that is actually worth doing."
Says it, and is taken for the one who let a deadline pass, because the cost of the urgent is visible and the worth of the important is not.
