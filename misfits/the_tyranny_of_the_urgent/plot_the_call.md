# Plot: The Call

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Desk](place_the_desk.md), an urgent task breaking in with a deadline that falls due now.
The clock real, the cost of delay real, the small closable shape there to be finished, the hour tilting toward it.

## Action

[Pressing](process_pressing.md): [The Urgent](persona_the_urgent.md), holding [the urgent's seat](position_urgent.md), presses its deadline and demands instant action, and [The Doer](persona_the_doer.md) feels the pull.

## Stage

[The Urgent](persona_the_urgent.md), on [Demand It Now](plan_demand_it_now.md), puts a clock on the hour and a cost on delay, offering the quick relief of a loop that will shut.
[The Doer](persona_the_doer.md), on [Answer the Urgent](plan_answer_the_urgent.md), feels the claim of the thing that falls due now, the pressing task nearer to hand than the quiet worth of the important.

## Tension

The deadline is real and the pull toward it reasonable, and the pull is what tilts the hour by the clock rather than the worth.
There is nothing to fault: the task honestly must be done today. But the mind reads the deadline as importance, so the plot closes with the urgent claim live and demanding, the hour leaning toward the thing with the clock, whatever it is worth beside the work it will displace.
