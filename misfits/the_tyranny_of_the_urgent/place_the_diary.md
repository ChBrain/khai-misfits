# Place: The Diary

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). Where time is allotted and the hour could be fenced, the calendar on which the day is triaged by worth or surrendered to the clock.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the hours are laid out before they are spent, a calendar where a block could be held for the deadline-less work or left open for the urgent to fill.
The steward fencing an hour for the important, the urgent pressing to reclaim it, the day planned by worth or given over to whatever falls due.

## Holds

The fencing, and the triage it contests.
Here the hour meets the choice of what to spend it on, so the Diary holds the point where worth could beat the clock: a block that might be guarded for the important before the day begins, so it is where the deadline-less work could be given its time in advance, the planned hour the only hour the urgent has not already claimed.

## Offers

A block for the important, held before the urgent can take it.
Because the Diary allots the hours ahead of the day, it offers the steward the one lever against the stream: a time fenced for the worthy work before the pressing tasks arrive to fill it, so it hands out the chance to spend an hour by worth rather than by clock, and hands it out knowing the urgent will press all day to reclaim the block it holds.

## Withheld

Any deadline or closure to defend the fenced hour with.
The Diary can hold a block for the important, but it cannot lend that block the urgency or the quick relief the urgent tasks carry, so it withholds the very things that would make the fenced hour easy to keep, leaving it defended only by worth, and, all good plan and no pressing claim, it withholds any protection for the block beyond the steward's willingness to let the urgent slip.
