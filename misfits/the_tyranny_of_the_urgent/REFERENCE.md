# The Tyranny of the Urgent: Reference

The research warrant for [The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md).
The dynamic is the mere-urgency account of attention: Zhu, Yang, and Hsee's mere urgency effect, named by
Hummel and framed by the Eisenhower and Covey urgent-important distinction, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the tyranny of the urgent as an **attention-allocation structure**: a person facing a stream of tasks
whose urgency and importance are independent, in which the urgent task, carrying a deadline that falls due now, a
real cost if it slips, and a small closable shape, captures the hour, while the important task, worth far more but
carrying no deadline, is always deferrable and so deferred, so answering the urgent-now over the important-later
is a sound choice every single time and sums across a working life to the important never done. The play models
the documented mechanics, the independence of urgency and importance, the salient deadline that the mind reads as
worth, the intrinsic relief of completing a small urgent task, the deadline-less payoff that can always wait one
more day, the hundred defensible triages that each choose the pressing over the worthy, the accrual of the
deferrals into a life of answered trivia, and the fenced hour and worth-first triage that alone reverse it, at a
cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a lazy or disorganised worker. The governing
finding of the source is the play's governing law: people are drawn to complete urgent tasks over more rewarding
non-urgent ones merely because the urgent carry a sense of expiration, so that urgency captures effort even when
the deferred task is worth more, and a life of sound daily choices is spent on the wrong things.

It is distinct from the house's The Firefighter's Medal, where a reward-and-visibility asymmetry across a team
sends effort to the dramatic save and starves prevention until collective capability erodes and the system tips:
there the driver is an external reward system and the erosion of a shared capacity, and the trap needs other
people to credit the save, here the driver is the intrinsic salience of urgency within a single actor's
attention, no reward system and no capability erosion required, and the important is deferred not because it is
unrewarded by others but because it carries no deadline of its own. And it is distinct from a simple story of
procrastination, because the person is not idle but busy and productive: the mere urgency effect is the choice of
the unimportant urgent over the important non-urgent while working hard the whole time, not the avoidance of work
altogether.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the doer as lazy or
  disorganised, because the source finding is precisely that each move is sound: every urgent task is real and
  has a real deadline and a real cost, answering it is genuinely productive, and deferring the important one more
  day is always individually correct, since it truly can wait and waiting once truly costs little. The waste is
  authored by no one and emerges from the structure, an urgency that captures the hour and an importance that
  carries no clock, so the misfit is that a busy, diligent worker making a hundred sound daily choices spends the
  life on what fell due and never on what mattered.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that spends the
  life well is to fence an hour for the important against the urgent and triage by worth rather than clock, and it
  means letting an urgent thing slip and pay its real, present cost, working on something with no deadline to
  drive it and no closure to reward it, and trusting a payoff that never falls due over a demand that does. The
  reckoning is an hour spent on what matters, bought against the urgent let slip and the relief forgone, not a
  free correction, so the cure for a life of answered trivia is the discomfort of the worthy work that never once
  asks to be done.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the mere urgency effect is Zhu, Yang, and Hsee's, the name and the
  phenomenology are Hummel's, and the urgent-important framing and the neglect of the important-not-urgent
  quadrant are the Eisenhower matrix's and Covey's, and the condition the trap actually requires, urgency and
  importance pulling apart while only the urgent carries a deadline and a closure, is carried as carefully as the
  finding itself.

  Hsee; Hummel; Covey), urgency capturing the hour against importance by the mere fact of falling due.
