# Plot: The Fence

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Diary](place_the_diary.md), someone who would guard the hour for the important.
The calendar allotted ahead, a block there to be held for the deadline-less work, the urgent pressing to reclaim it.

## Action

[Fencing](process_fencing.md): [The Steward](persona_the_steward.md), holding [the steward's seat](position_steward.md), fences the hour for the important and triages by worth, against [The Urgent](persona_the_urgent.md) on [Demand It Now](plan_demand_it_now.md).

## Stage

[The Steward](persona_the_steward.md), on [Fence the Hour](plan_fence_the_hour.md), holds a block for the worthy work before the day begins and lets an urgent task slip to keep it, spending the hour by worth rather than clock.
[The Urgent](persona_the_urgent.md), on [Demand It Now](plan_demand_it_now.md), presses its deadline against the fenced block, its real cost and quick closure pulling to reclaim the hour.

## Tension

Guarding the hour for the important is the one move that reaches it, and it means letting an urgent thing slip and pay its present cost.
There is nothing to fault: the important will never be done unless its time is guarded, and the steward only guards it. But the fenced hour has no deadline to drive it and no closure to reward it, so the plot closes one of two ways: the block holds and the important is done, bought against the urgent let slip ... or the pressing tasks reclaim the hour, the loop closes today once more, and the important waits another day.
