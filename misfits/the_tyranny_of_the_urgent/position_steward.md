# Position: Steward

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The seat that would fence the hour for the important and triage by worth, at the cost of an urgent thing let slip and the closure it would have brought.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A view past the clock and a hard case: the sight that urgency is not worth and the argument that the hour should go where it counts most.
A clear case: a life is made of the important, and the important will never be done unless its time is guarded from the urgent.
No deadline to drive the fenced work and no closure to reward it, and no cover from the charge of letting an urgent thing slip.

## Orders

Fence the hour.
Guard a block of time for the deadline-less work, triage the day by worth rather than clock, and act on the important before it becomes a crisis.
Let an urgent task slip when the important is worth more, and spend the hour where it counts.

## Loses

The met deadline and the closed loop, exactly by spending the hour on what is worth the most.
Because the important gives no deadline to drive the work and no closure to reward it, the seat that fences its hour must work against the pull of every loop that would close today, bear the real present cost of the urgent let slip, and trust a payoff that never falls due, so it cannot spend the life well without forgoing the visible relief the urgent would have paid.
It buys an hour spent on what matters against the cost of the urgent left undone, and is taken, for a while, as the one who let a deadline pass.

## Drives

Toward the hour fenced and the important done,
bought against the urgent let slip and the closure forgone,
so the one seat that would spend the life on what matters must let the pressing thing slip to do it.
