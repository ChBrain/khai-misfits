# Piece: The Deadline

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The urgent's salient claim, the clock that falls due now and demands instant action, the thing that commands the hour whatever the task is worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Falls due at [the desk](place_the_desk.md), where the pressing tasks arrive and demand the hour.
Pressed against [the diary](place_the_diary.md), where it would reclaim the block fenced for the important.

## Load Bearing

Without it the urgent has no hold, and every task would wait its turn by worth alone.
With it the trap has its driver: a clock on the task, a real cost if it slips, a claim that falls due now, so the hour tilts toward it. The Deadline is load bearing because it is the salient claim that captures attention (Zhu, Yang, and Hsee): the clock the mind reads as importance, so the piece carries the urgency that stands in for worth, the visible pole of the trap against which the deadline-less important can make no claim at all.

## Apparent

The mark of what matters, the plain signal that a task with a clock is a task to do now.
Seen at the surface, the Deadline looks like importance, a claim that the task before it is the one that counts, and the truth that urgency and worth pull apart, that a clock measures only when a thing is due and not how much it is worth, that the smallest deadline outranks the largest patient payoff, stays hidden behind how naturally a thing that must be done now feels like a thing that must be done.
