# Piece: The Closure

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The relief of finishing the urgent, the click of a small loop shut, the intrinsic reward that makes answering the pressing task feel good.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Felt at [the desk](place_the_desk.md), where each urgent task closes with a satisfying click.
Absent from [the backlog](place_the_backlog.md), where the important, never finished, gives no such relief.

## Load Bearing

Without it the urgent would win on its deadline alone, and doing it would bring no pleasure to pull toward.
With it the urgent is rewarded from the inside: a small, clear, closable shape that ends with the relief of a loop shut, so answering it feels good in a way the open-ended important never can. The Closure is load bearing because it is the intrinsic pull of the urgent (Zhu, Yang, and Hsee): the completion the small task pays, so the piece carries the relief that makes the pressing thing not just demanded but wanted, the reward for spending the hour on what falls due and closes.

## Apparent

Progress, the plain good feeling of getting things done and clearing the desk.
Seen at the surface, the Closure looks like accomplishment, the honest satisfaction of a finished task, and the truth that it rewards finishing and not worth, that the important gives no such click and so cannot compete for the feeling, that a day of satisfying closures can be a day of nothing that mattered, stays hidden behind how genuinely good it feels to close a loop.

## Yearbook

Paid by [answering](process_answering.md) and offered by [the deadline](piece_the_deadline.md), the relief that rewards the urgent.
It is the pull of the trap: the Closure makes the urgent wanted as well as demanded, the click the small task pays and [the payoff](piece_the_payoff.md) of the important never does, drawing the hour toward what closes and away from what matters in the [tyranny of the urgent](piece_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md).
