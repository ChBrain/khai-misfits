# Process: Pressing

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of the Urgent](play_the_tyranny_of_the_urgent.md). The urgent task asserting its deadline, putting a clock on the hour and a cost on delay, the salience that claims attention whatever the task is worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A task with a deadline and a cost if it slips.
At [the desk](place_the_desk.md), the urgent claim falling due, the phone ringing and the form due today, demanding instant action.

## Direction

Toward the hour claimed by whatever falls due soonest.
Because a deadline puts a real cost on delay and offers a small closable shape, pressing makes the urgent task feel like a claim that must be met now, so it tilts the hour toward the thing with the clock, and the mind reads the deadline as importance, letting the smallest pressing task command attention the largest patient one cannot.

## Lever

The reality of the deadline and the cost of delay.
Because the clock is genuine and the task honestly must be done today, pressing runs on real time-bound claims, not manufactured ones, so the urgency that captures the hour is true urgency, and the trap needs no trick, only a real deadline standing in for a worth it does not have.

## Echo

An hour tilted toward the pressing thing.
The deadline has claimed the attention and the urgent task feels like the one that matters, so what reads as the mind attending to what is important is, in truth, the mind attending to what falls due, urgency worn as though it were worth.
