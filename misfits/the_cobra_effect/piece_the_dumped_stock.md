# Piece: The Dumped Stock

## Taxonomy

[The Cobra Effect](play_the_cobra_effect.md). The farmed stock turned loose when the reward ends, leaving the problem worse than the start.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Released onto [the street](place_the_street.md), the moment the bounty is pulled.
The breeding sheds emptied: cobras worth money yesterday and nothing today, let go rather than fed, straight into the city they were bred beside.

## Load Bearing

Without it the cancellation would simply end the programme and cut the losses.
With it the cancellation is the worst moment of all, because the Dumped Stock is what the manufactured supply becomes when its price hits zero: an inventory of the exact harm the programme fought, now valueless, and cheaper to release than to keep. So the fix does not just fail, it leaves a residue larger than the original problem, and the day the incentive is corrected is the day the city is most overrun, because everything bred for the reward is set free at once.

## Apparent

The end of a failed scheme.
Pulling the bounty looks like the responsible correction, the moment sanity returns. It is the correction, and it is also the trigger: stopping the reward strands the supply the reward created, and the supply has to go somewhere.

## Yearbook

Loosed by [the breeder](persona_the_breeder.md) the hour the price dies, inherited by [the snakebit](persona_the_snakebit.md).
It is the sting in the tail of the play: the programme is judged by whether it left the problem better or worse, and it left it worse, because the reckoning that ended the farm also emptied it into the street, and the surplus outlives the scheme that made it.
