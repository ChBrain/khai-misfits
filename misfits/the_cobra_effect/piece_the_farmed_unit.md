# Piece: The Farmed Unit

## Taxonomy

[The Cobra Effect](play_the_cobra_effect.md). The target produced on purpose, to be removed for the reward.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On [the farm](place_the_farm.md), bred, raised, and killed on schedule.
The cobra born to be a bounty: reared not as a pest but as a crop, its whole existence a route from the reward to a pocket.

## Load Bearing

Without it the bounty only draws down the wild supply and might even work.
With it the bounty grows the supply, because the Farmed Unit is cheaper to make than to hunt: rearing a cobra to claim its bounty costs less effort than tracking a wild one, so a rational earner breeds rather than hunts, and every unit farmed is a unit that would not have existed but for the reward. It is indistinguishable at the counter from a wild one, identical proof, identical payout, which is precisely why it works: the till cannot tell a pest from a product.

## Apparent

A dead pest, same as any other.
At [the bounty office](place_the_bounty_office.md) it looks like exactly what the programme wanted: one fewer cobra, proof in hand. It is one fewer cobra and two more in the breeding shed, and nothing on the proof says so.

## Yearbook

Raised by [the breeder](persona_the_breeder.md), who is not cheating but farming, responding to the price as offered.
It is the perverse output at the heart of the play: the reward for removing the thing becomes a reason to make the thing, so [the manufactured supply](piece_the_manufactured_supply.md) grows in the exact shape of the incentive, one bred cobra per bounty, forever, as long as making beats hunting.
