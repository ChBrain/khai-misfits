# Plot: Plan

## Taxonomy

[The Cobra Effect](play_the_cobra_effect.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The podium](place_the_podium.md), a real problem named and a fair reward announced.
There are too many cobras in the city, and the harm is genuine and hard to count, so a price is set on the one thing that can be counted: a dead one, brought in as proof.

## Action

[Incentivising](process_incentivising.md): [The Well-Meaner](persona_the_well_meaner.md), holding [the sponsor's seat](position_sponsor.md), sets a bounty per unit removed, in good faith, to draw the supply down.

## Stage

[The Well-Meaner](persona_the_well_meaner.md) tables [Set the Bounty](plan_set_the_bounty.md): put a fair price on a dead cobra, and [the bounty](piece_the_bounty.md) goes live, a price on a token that stands in for the goal.
Behind it sits [the real problem](piece_the_real_problem.md): the cobras in the street, wanted gone, and impossible to pay for directly, so the token is paid for instead.

## Tension

Nothing here is careless.
The problem is real, paying for outcomes is sound, and a per-unit reward is the obvious clean design. But the goal cannot be counted and the token can, and worse, the token can be made, so the price meant to draw the supply down is, to anyone who can breed the thing, an open offer to supply it. The trap is set the moment the reward is aimed at the token instead of the outcome.
