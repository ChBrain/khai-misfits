# Play: The Cobra Effect

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Cobra Effect.
A bounty offered to rid the city of cobras, paid per dead snake, working exactly as designed ... except that the surest way to earn it is to breed cobras and kill them, so the reward set to end the snakes becomes the reason there are more.

## Arc

The bend is this: the reward does precisely what it pays for, and the problem gets worse.
It runs on two lines that the same reward drives apart.
One line is the metric: dead units handed in, bounties paid, a number that climbs and reads as the problem being solved.
The other line is the problem itself, and it climbs too, because a reward paid per unit removed is a price offered per unit produced: the fastest way to earn is not to hunt the wild ones but to breed and kill your own, so the bounty does not draw down the supply, it manufactures it.
Nobody cheats. The sponsor means to solve a real problem, the farmer responds rationally to the price on offer, the office pays honestly on proof it cannot question. So the reward creates the very thing it rewards removing, the two lines rise together, and when the farming is finally seen and the bounty pulled, the manufactured stock, now worthless, is turned loose, and the problem is left worse than before anyone tried to fix it.
Reward the outcome you want, not the token you can count, and the farm has nothing to sell: the reward is the story.

## Triggers

**[Bounty](plot_bounty.md)**
A real problem is named, and a bounty is set per unit removed, in good faith, and the office stands ready to pay on proof. The Well-Meaner sets the price, the Teller readies the till. Closes when the reward per unit is live.

**[Manufactured Supply](plot_manufactured_supply.md)**
Cue: the reward meets the market. Breeding the unit and killing it for the bounty beats hunting the wild one, so the supply is manufactured and the payouts soar. Closes when the reward is a farm and the metric is booming.

**[Backfire](plot_backfire.md)**
Cue: the booming payouts are reported as the problem being solved. The Noticer follows the supply and finds the farm, payments up and the real problem up with them. Closes when it is clear the bounty is breeding what it pays to remove.

**[Release](plot_release.md)**
Cue: the farming is undeniable, and the bounty is pulled. The manufactured stock, now worthless, is turned loose, and the problem is left worse than before. Closes when the cobra effect is named ... or a new bounty is set on the new surplus.

## Stakes

Whether the reward ever buys the outcome instead of the token.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward fewer of the thing, toward more of it, because the price is on the token and the token can be made. The production cannot make a per-unit reward stop manufacturing units. It can only show that no one cheated, that the sponsor meant well and the farmer was rational and the office paid honestly, and that the one design that would have worked, reward the outcome you cannot fake instead of the token you can count, was the one never chosen, because the token was the thing that was easy to see.
