# The Survivors: Reference

The research warrant for [The Survivors](play_the_survivors.md). The title names the visible sample;
the play runs on the statistics of selection bias, from Bacon's silent evidence through Wald's
wartime analysis to the survivorship correction in modern performance studies, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging survivorship bias as a **systemic system**: a lesson read off the cases that survived, sound
on its sample and backwards on the world, drawn without a single lie or blunder, because the very
outcome under study is the filter that assembled the evidence, so the failures that would overturn
the conclusion are precisely the ones absent from the data. The play models the documented mechanics,
the sample cut by survival, the vivid damage on the survivors, the clean spots that mark the fatal
hits, the uncounted failures who did the same and lost, the hidden denominator that turns a long shot
into a method, and the recovery of the absent that alone corrects the inference, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of carelessness. The governing finding of the source
literature is the play's governing law: when a sample is conditioned on survival, any trait the
survivors share is confounded with surviving, so honest analysis of the present points away from the
truth, and only reasoning about the missing turns it back.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any seat
  as a liar or a fool, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the survivor is
  honestly present, the failure is honestly gone, the analyst reads the sample faithfully, and the
  lesson is drawn by sound arithmetic. The misfit is that a sample chosen by the outcome makes honest
  analysis point exactly the wrong way.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the data simply speaking for itself. The
  inference turns over only when someone hunts down the absent, counts the drowned, and argues against
  the vivid evidence in the room, spending the flattering lesson the survivors told, so the reckoning
  is a clean story traded for a true one built on cases no one can point to.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and Wald's wartime
  analysis is credited beside Bacon's older question and the finance literature that formalised it.
