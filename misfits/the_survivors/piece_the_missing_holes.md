# Piece: The Missing Holes

## Taxonomy

[The Survivors](play_the_survivors.md). The clean spots on the survivors, where the lost were struck.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), in the negative space of the damage chart.
The places the survivors are unmarked: the engines with no bullet holes, the cockpit that came home clean. Not the absence of hits in the world, only the absence of hits among the planes that lived.

## Load Bearing

Without it the chart says armour the damage and the wrong steel goes on.
With it the chart turns over and says the opposite, because the Missing Holes are the survivorship insight itself: a spot clean on every returned plane is not a spot that never gets hit, it is a spot where a hit does not come home. The gaps in the survivors' damage map the fatal geography, so the armour belongs where the Holes are not, and reading the blank areas as safe is exactly the error that plates the strong parts and leaves the lethal ones bare.

## Apparent

Untouched, and so unimportant.
It looks like the safe part, the region the enemy never seems to reach, the trait the winners happen not to share. That is the trap sprung in reverse: the emptiest part of the survivors' chart is the deadliest part of the aircraft, and it looks like the one place not worth a thought.

## Yearbook

Seen at last by [the statistician](persona_the_statistician.md), who armours where there are no holes.
It is the turned-over truth: only by reasoning past [the holes](piece_the_holes.md) to the Missing Holes, and past the survivors to [the drowned](piece_the_drowned.md), does the map point where the armour saves a plane instead of wasting weight on one already proven able to take the hit.
