# Plot: The Drawing Board

## Taxonomy

[The Survivors](play_the_survivors.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), and someone who has stopped studying the planes in the hangar.
The lesson is drawn and about to be cast in steel, and one person asks the other question: not where were these planes hit, but where were the ones that never came back.

## Action

[Recovery](process_recovery.md): [The Statistician](persona_the_statistician.md), holding [the corrector's seat](position_corrector.md), reasons from the planes that are absent, and reads the survivors' clean spots as the map of the fatal hits.

## Stage

[The Statistician](persona_the_statistician.md) tables [Armor the Gaps](plan_armor_the_gaps.md): put the failures back under the line, see that [the missing holes](piece_the_missing_holes.md) mark where a hit does not come home, and armour where the survivors show no damage at all.
[The survivorship bias](piece_the_survivorship_bias.md) is named at last: a lesson sound on its sample and backwards on the world, turned over only by counting the drowned the hangar was built to exclude.

## Tension

The armour goes on the right place, and the clean story is gone.
Reasoning from the absent works, and it costs: the flattering lesson the survivors told is overturned, the confident recipe is downgraded to a lucky escape with a hidden denominator, and the analyst who found the real answer looks, for a while, like the one arguing against the evidence in the room. It trades a vivid, teachable, wrong conclusion for a true one built on planes no one can point to ... or the survivors are too present and too persuasive to doubt, the drowned stay uncounted, and the steel is bolted over the bullet holes while the engines fly bare.
