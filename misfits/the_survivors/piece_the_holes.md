# Piece: The Holes

## Taxonomy

[The Survivors](play_the_survivors.md). The damage on the survivors, the pattern you can see.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the hangar](place_the_hangar.md), mapped across the returned planes, wings and fuselage and tail.
The visible marks the survivors carry: the bullet holes on the bombers that landed, the scars the winners show off, the near-misses the successful recount. Real damage, plainly there, easy to chart.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no pattern to read and no lesson to draw, right or wrong.
With it there is a vivid pattern that begs to be acted on, and begs wrong, because the Holes mark where a plane was hit and lived. They cluster on the survivors exactly where a hit is survivable, so the fullest concentration of holes is the safest place to be shot, and the naive eye reads the densest damage as the part most in need of armour. The Holes are honest about where survivors were struck and silent about where the dead were.

## Apparent

The obvious place to reinforce.
It looks like a heat map of danger: here is where the planes get hit, so here is where to add the steel. That reading is immediate and almost irresistible, because the Holes are the loudest thing in the hangar and the planes they are missing from are at the bottom of the sea.

## Yearbook

Charted by [the reader](persona_the_reader.md), turned into orders by [the storyteller](persona_the_storyteller.md).
It is the visible half of the truth: the Holes show where survivors can be hit, and only by turning them over into [the missing holes](piece_the_missing_holes.md) does the map point at the armour that would actually save a plane.
