# The Straight Rows: Reference

The research warrant for [The Straight Rows](play_the_straight_rows.md).
The dynamic is legibility and the loss of mētis: Scott's account of the state's drive to make a domain readable, the
Greek concept of mētis it clears, the dispersed local knowledge no centre can hold, the urban case and its defence,
and the scientific-forestry case that shows the grid thriving a generation and dying, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging legibility as a **remake-the-territory-to-read-it structure**: a centre can act only on what it can see, so
to tax, conscript, plan, or manage a domain it reduces the tangle to a legible grid, a map, a table, a standard unit
it can hold from a distance, and because that grid can record only what it can measure it clears away the tacit,
local, practical knowledge, the mētis, that actually ran the system and no survey could hold, so the domain is
remade into the thin image the centre can read and loses the function the mētis carried. The play models the
documented mechanics, the centre that must see to act, the grid pressed down onto the ground, the mētis cleared
because it cannot be recorded, the first yield that vindicates the method, and the later collapse of a system run on
only what the grid can see, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a tyrant. The governing
finding of the source is the play's governing law: where a centre imposes a legible order to govern a domain, the
simplification that lets it see the place is the same act that clears the working knowledge beneath it, so the map,
pressed down, remakes the territory into its own thin image and then cannot keep it alive.

It is distinct from the house's The Abstraction Ladder, where a message sheds its specifics undeliberately as it
climbs a reporting chain, the detail lost passively on the way up: here the loss is not a report thinning as it
rises but the territory itself remade, the grid pressed down onto the ground and the mētis actively cleared, a map
that does not merely lose the detail but destroys it. It is distinct from The Simpler Lever (Ashby's requisite variety), where a regulator's
response repertoire is provably too small to match the variety it must absorb: here the centre does not simply fail
to match the domain's variety but remakes the domain to fit its own thin repertoire, razing the mētis rather than
falling short of it, the mismatch resolved by destroying the richer side. And it is distinct from Metric Fixation,
where a target is set and then gamed until it corrupts what it measures: here no number is gamed and no target set,
the legible grid genuinely governs and delivers at first, and what is lost is not a measure corrupted by those it
rates but a tacit knowledge cleared by the very act of making the domain readable.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the planner or the
  state as a tyrant, because the source finding is precisely that no tyranny is needed: a centre cannot tax, conscript,
  plan, or feed a domain it cannot read, so it makes the place legible because that is the only way to act on it at
  all, and the grid it draws is a real and useful tool that governs and, at first, delivers. The ruin is authored by
  no malice and emerges from a legible order met by a domain whose working knowledge that order cannot hold, so the
  misfit is that a centre meaning to improve a place, and reducing it to what it can see because it must, remakes the
  domain into a thin image it can read and cannot keep alive, no cruelty and no despot required. Scott's own darkest
  cases, the forced collectivisation of Soviet agriculture and the ujamaa villagisation of Tanzania, did need an
  authoritarian state and a prostrate society to be driven through, coercion doing the work legibility alone could
  not: this misfit stages the benign end of his spectrum, the scientific forest and the high-modernist city, where
  the mechanism runs to ruin with no coercion at all, and credits that his worst cases added a force it does not
  model.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the centre governing by the mētis it cleared. The one move
  that could spare the domain is to hold the legibility below the point where it displaces the working knowledge, to
  leave room for the mixed stand and the customary practice the grid cannot record, but a centre that must act from a
  distance can trust only what it can read, and the high-modern confidence that its grid is the whole of the knowledge
  is the very confidence that clears the rest, so the restraint runs against the drive that imposed the grid. The
  reckoning is a domain kept only by a centre that forbears to see all of it, not an order that preserves the life
  beneath it once the map is pressed down, so the cure for a hollowing legibility is a limit the will to govern
  resists.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the account of legibility, high modernism, and
  the loss of mētis is Scott's, the concept of mētis is Detienne and Vernant's, the argument that local knowledge is
  dispersed and cannot be centralised is Hayek's, the defence of the living city's unplanned order is Jacobs's, and
  the scientific-forestry case of the legible monoculture that thrived and died is documented by Lowood, each carried
  as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages the legible grid that clears the knowledge it cannot read and
  credits that the destruction needs no tyrant, only a centre that must see to act.
