# Process: Simplifying

## Taxonomy

[The Straight Rows](play_the_straight_rows.md). The centre reducing the domain to a legible grid, keeping the countable and dropping the rest.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Planner, with a domain to govern and only a distance to govern it across.
At [the survey](place_the_survey.md), the tangle is drawn as rows and squares and figures, and at [the forest](place_the_forest.md) the wild wood is replanted to match, the map pressed onto the ground.

## Direction

Toward a domain remade to be read.
Because a centre cannot act on what it cannot hold in a table, simplifying points the domain at legibility: the forest becomes even-aged rows, the tenure becomes owned squares, the quarter becomes a plan, so what could not be seen is made into what can, and the place is reduced to a grid the centre can manage from a distance.

## Lever

The precondition of governing at all.
Because a state must be able to read its domain to tax, conscript, plan, and feed it, simplifying runs on no vanity but a real need, each reduction the honest cost of acting from the centre, so no despot is required to impose the grid, only a centre that must see to govern and can see only what it has made legible.

## Echo

A legible grid pressed onto living ground.
What leaves this process is a domain remade into the thin image a map can hold, the countable kept and the particular dropped, so the centre can at last read and run the place, and the reduction that made it governable has already set aside the knowledge the next processes will find missing.
