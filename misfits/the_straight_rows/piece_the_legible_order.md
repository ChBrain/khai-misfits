# Piece: The Legible Order

## Taxonomy

[The Straight Rows](play_the_straight_rows.md). The phenomenon whole, the grid pressed down onto the ground that remakes the territory into a thin image it can read and cannot keep alive.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Runs across [the survey](place_the_survey.md), [the forest](place_the_forest.md), and [the village](place_the_village.md) as the whole arc, the map drawn, pressed down, and left standing over the ground it cleared.
Flows through the entire domain, from the tangle reduced to the ruin that follows, the trap seen entire.

## Load Bearing

Without it there are only parts: a map, a cleared knowledge, a first harvest, a dying stand, with no name for the order that binds them.
With it the parts become one legible engine: the grid is imposed to govern, the mētis is cleared because the grid cannot record it, the first yield vindicates the method, and the domain, run on only what the grid can see, gives out. The Legible Order is load bearing because it is the phenomenon entire (Scott): the map pressed down onto the ground that remakes the territory into its own thin image and then cannot keep it alive, so the piece carries the trap whole, a rational, improving, governing order that hollows the place in the act of learning to read it, and needs no tyrant to do it.

## Apparent

Just rational improvement, a domain brought to order, planned, standardised, and at last well run.
Seen at the surface, the Legible Order looks like progress, method replacing muddle and a centre managing what was once a tangle, and the truth that the order is a reduction that cleared the working knowledge, that the improvement thrives one rotation and dies the next, that the well-run domain is being hollowed by the very grid that runs it, stays hidden behind how much a legible order looks like a governed one.
