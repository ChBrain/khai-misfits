# Persona: The Planner

## Taxonomy

[The Centre](position_the_centre.md). Governs a domain it can act on only from a distance, so it makes the place legible to see it, and clears the knowledge the survey cannot hold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Rational. Improving. Bringing order to a tangle.
Is the one who must run the forest, the city, or the province from a centre, and cannot manage what cannot be read, so it surveys, standardises, and plans until the domain can be seen.
No cruelty wished on them: they mean to improve the place, and they are not wrong that a state must be able to read its domain to tax it, feed it, and defend it.

## Action

Imposes the legible grid: replants the forest in countable rows, flattens the customary tenure into owned squares, straightens the crooked quarter into a diagrammable plan.
Answers the precondition of governing at all, since a centre cannot act on what it cannot hold in a table, so it remakes the domain into what it can read.
Does what a centre must under distance: reduces the tangle to a grid it can manage, and trusts the map because the map is the only part of the place it can actually see.

## Shadow

Not a tyrant, but the one whose need to see clears what it cannot read, and that is the quiet of it: the grid is a real tool and it razes a real knowledge.
Because the Planner can record only what it can measure, the legible order it imposes keeps the rows and clears the mētis, the mixed stand and the customary rotation and the street's unplanned life, so the domain is remade into the thin image the map can hold, and the Planner, meaning only to govern, has cleared the working knowledge that made the place worth governing.
Every simplification is reasonable, and the sum of reasonable simplifications is a domain that can be read and cannot live.

## Tell

Says "we cannot manage what we cannot measure: make it legible and then it can be run properly."
Is right that the domain must be read to be governed and cannot see that reading is not knowing, so the knowledge the survey leaves out is the knowledge the plantation will miss.
