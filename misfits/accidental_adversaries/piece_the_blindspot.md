# Piece: The Blindspot

## Taxonomy

[Accidental Adversaries](play_accidental_adversaries.md). The boundary where the harm crosses unseen: neither party can see its effect on the other's side.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the boundary](place_the_boundary.md), which each party sees only its own side of.
In the seam where the cost of a move lands out of the mover's view.

## Load Bearing

Without it the harm would be seen and could be stopped or explained.
With it the harm is dealt and received in the dark: the blindspot is the seam that shows each party only its own half, so the cost a move imposes is invisible to the one who imposes it and unexplained to the one who bears it. The Blindspot is load bearing because it is why no one can stop the loop from inside (Ross on attribution): the mover cannot see the harm to correct it, and the wounded cannot see the cause to forgive it, so injury is dealt without knowledge and received without understanding, and read, inevitably, as intent.

## Apparent

Just the normal limit of any one point of view.
Not seeing the other party's inner workings looks like the ordinary condition of separate parties, so the blindspot reads as unremarkable, and the truth that this exact limit is what turns an accident into an attack, hiding the cause from the one who most needs it, stays hidden behind how natural it is to see only one's own side.

## Yearbook

Opened through [undermining](process_undermining.md), the seam [the local move](piece_the_local_move.md) crosses.
It is why the harm is misread: the Blindspot hides the cause of the injury from the wounded, so an accident is taken for [the misattribution](piece_the_misattribution.md), and the whole loop stays invisible to everyone standing in it.
