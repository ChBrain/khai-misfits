# Place: The Appeal

## Taxonomy

[The One in Front of You](play_the_one_in_front_of_you.md). Where the identified victim is presented and the giving happens, the charity appeal, the bedside, the courtroom, the named face brought before the giver.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The photograph, the name, the story: a child with brown hair who needs an operation, a person the giver can hold in mind and picture whole.
The wallets opening, the appeal swamped, the sum found almost overnight, because here is a real person in real need and the giver's eye has somewhere to rest.

## Holds

The identified victim's face and story, and the feeling they draw.
Here the Named stands before the Giver in full, a name and a photograph and a story that closes the distance between them, so the Appeal holds the one condition sympathy needs to be live: a person the giver can picture, near enough and whole enough to feel for, and holding that is all it does.

## Offers

The chance to help the one you can see, freely and warmly given.
Because the Appeal presents a real person in real need, it offers the giver a gift that costs nothing to want to make, the checkbook out before the case is even reasoned through, a good heart meeting a plain need and answering it well, the offer clean and the giving genuine.

## Withheld

Any sight of the statistical many, and so any claim on the giver's feeling.
The Appeal is built to frame one face, lit, named, storied, and a frame that holds one face has no room left for a thousand, so the thousand are not weighed here and passed over, they are simply nowhere in the picture, the giver never asked about them because the Appeal never shows them, and what it withholds it withholds by omission, not by refusal.
