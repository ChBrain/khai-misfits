# Piece: The Number

## Taxonomy

[The One in Front of You](play_the_one_in_front_of_you.md). The statistic object, the count, the estimate of preventable deaths, the cost-per-life figure the statistical many are reduced to.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Stands across [The Field](place_the_field.md), the anonymous many present only as a count.
Weighed at [The Table](place_the_table.md), where the figure is set against the face and found to move nothing on its own.

## Load Bearing

Without it, the countless would have no shape at all, not even the shape of a statistic, and there would be nothing left for an agency to weigh or a play to count. With it, the many are a figure: an estimate of preventable deaths, a cost-per-life, a tally standing where a name would stand for the identified. The Number is load bearing because it is the form a statistical life takes (Schelling): the distinction between an identified life and a statistical one is the distinction between a face and a figure, and the piece carries the figure, the abstraction the countless are left as once no single one of them can be picked out to feel.

## Apparent

A fact, a plain and useful fact, the sort of figure a report is right to state.
Seen at the surface, the Number looks like information: a count that tells the giver how much good is at stake, more informative even than a single face since it is the whole of the need in one figure. And the truth that the completeness is what empties it, that the more the figure states the less the heart can hold, that a thousand written down moves nobody the way one name does, stays hidden behind how reasonable it seems that a larger number should matter more.
