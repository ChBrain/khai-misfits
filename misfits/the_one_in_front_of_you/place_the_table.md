# Place: The Table

## Taxonomy

[The One in Front of You](play_the_one_in_front_of_you.md). The actuary's domain, where statistical lives are counted and allocation is weighed by the number, the policy office, the cost-effectiveness spreadsheet.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Columns of figures: a cost-per-life, an estimate of preventable deaths, a budget divided by a count.
No face anywhere on the page, the Actuary at the Table weighing one programme against another by the number each would save, the weighing even because nothing on the sheet asks to be pictured.

## Holds

The count, the statistical lives, and the rule that allocates by them.
Here the Countless finally has a place that can hold it, a column instead of a face, so the Table holds what the Appeal cannot: the thousand entered as a figure the instrument can weigh, taken whole, none of them named and none of them needing to be.

## Offers

A fair weighing the feeling cannot do, the many funded by the number.
Because the Table sets every statistical life beside every other at the same rate, it offers what sympathy structurally cannot: an allocation that does not shrink as the count it is counting grows, so the same total help, however faceless the population behind it, buys the same total good, and it can be spent that way only here.

## Withheld

Any face, any warmth, and so any pull on the giver directly.
The Table can weigh the count but it cannot make anyone feel it, a column of figures moving no one the way a photograph does at a glance, so what it withholds is the very thing the Appeal supplies, and the two never meet: the number is only ever funded here, by an instrument, never by a giver moved.
