# Piece: The Numbing

## Taxonomy

[The One in Front of You](play_the_one_in_front_of_you.md). The flattening of feeling as the number rises, a single victim moving more than a group and the mass moving least of all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Falls across [The Field](place_the_field.md), where the Countless stands and the feeling that should meet its size does not.

## Load Bearing

Without it, a larger need would draw a larger feeling, the thousand moving more than the one, and the trap would not exist. With it, feeling falls as the count rises: a single victim moves more than a group, a group moves more than a crowd, and the countless, the largest need of all, move the least. The Numbing is load bearing because it is the mechanism by which scale defeats itself (Slovic; Kogut and Ritov): the singularity by which one victim draws more than many, so the piece carries the collapse, the more who suffer the less that is felt, and without it the statistical many would only be unseen, not actively drained of the feeling their size should command.

## Apparent

A limit of attention, a forgivable smallness of heart before a large number, the sort of thing any mind would show before a crowd it cannot truly picture.
Seen at the surface, the Numbing looks like a ceiling: feeling that simply runs out past a certain size. And the truth that it is not a ceiling but a curve running the wrong way, that feeling does not plateau but falls as the count climbs, that the mind gives less to a thousand than to ten and less to ten than to one, stays hidden behind how reasonable a limit on attention sounds.

## Yearbook

Felt at [The Field](place_the_field.md) by [The Giver](persona_the_giver.md), where [The Number](piece_the_number.md) grows and [The Sympathy](piece_the_sympathy.md) shrinks against it.
It is the piece that turns scale against itself: the Numbing is why [The Countless](persona_the_countless.md) draws least when its need is greatest, the flattening [Neglecting](process_neglecting.md) performs and that sums, ruling by ruling, into [The Shortfall](piece_the_shortfall.md).
