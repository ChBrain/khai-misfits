# The One in Front of You: Reference

The research warrant for **The One in Front of You** (play and company not yet staged;
this warrant is drafted first, to prove the science and the distinction before authoring).
The dynamic is the identifiable-victim account of allocation: Schelling's identified and statistical
lives, Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic's sympathy and callousness, and Slovic's psychic numbing, all
cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the identifiable victim effect as an **allocation structure**: a giver, a donor or a court or a
health system, who responds to the one named, pictured, present victim with real and proportionate
compassion, and directs resources to that person, while an equal or larger statistical, anonymous,
future population, drawing no such feeling, goes underfunded. The governing law is not that the giver is
callous or the choice ill-meant, but that **sympathy answers the identified individual in view and
falls silent before the statistical many, so aid flows to the one who can be seen and away from the more
who cannot**, each single act of compassion sound and the sum a systematic under-serving of the unseen.
The play models the documented mechanics: the sharp gap between what is spent to save an identified life
and an equal statistical one (Schelling), the concentration of giving on a single identified victim even
where dispersing it would help more (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic), the collapse of feeling as the
number rises and the singularity by which one victim draws more than many (Slovic), and the perverse
cost of the correction, that deliberating on the discrepancy lowers giving to the identified without
raising it to the statistical, an overall drop in caring (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic), as the
load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a giver who does not care.

It is distinct from the house's Not Worth My While, where a measure that saves many offers each person
so little that each rationally declines and the shared benefit is lost: there the failure is supply-side,
a diffuse benefit too thin per head to move its own beneficiaries, here it is demand-side, a giver
allocating by identifiability, drawn to concentrate aid on the one in view rather than disperse it to the
many, the pull toward the visible individual and not the thinness of a per-head share. It is distinct
from The Availability Cascade, where a risk snowballs through media and mutual reinforcement into public
alarm: there the driver is a self-feeding social process over time, here it is a static, single-decision
affective bias, the identified victim before the giver now, needing no cascade to command the feeling.
The house stages the donation-and-allocation core, the sound compassion for the one in view summing to
the neglect of the unseen many, and refuses any reading in which the giver is uncaring.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a giver who
  does not care, because the source finding it stages is the opposite: the response to the identified
  victim is genuine, proportionate compassion for a real person in need (Schelling; Small, Loewenstein,
  and Slovic), and the neglect of the statistical many is not coldness but the plain silence of feeling
  before an abstraction. The under-serving is authored by no one and emerges from the structure, a
  sympathy that answers the seen and cannot answer the unseen, so the misfit is that a giver moved by
  real compassion, giving well to the one in front of them, leaves the larger unseen population unhelped,
  not by choosing against it but by never feeling it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The obvious cure, to
  reason past the feeling and value the statistical life equally, does not work as hoped: deliberating on
  the discrepancy dampens the sympathy that drove the giving to the identified without kindling any for
  the statistical, so caring falls overall (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic). The reckoning is that the
  feeling which misallocates is the same feeling that motivates any giving at all, so correcting the aim
  risks cooling the heart, and the escape must be built in the allocation, not in asking the giver to
  feel the number.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the identified and statistical lives are
  Schelling's, the donation mechanism and the perverse effect of deliberation are Small's, Loewenstein's,
  and Slovic's, the psychic numbing is Slovic's and the singularity Kogut's and Ritov's, and the
  reference-group account is Jenni's and Loewenstein's, and the condition the trap requires, a sympathy
  answering the identified and silent before the statistical, is carried as carefully as the finding.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element. The play and its company are not yet authored; these are the
proposed load-bearing pieces the warrant commits the staging to carry, one per vector, no filler.

- **The Face**: carries the identified victim (Schelling; Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic), the named,
  pictured, present person whose need the giver can see and feel.
- **The Number**: carries the statistical victims (Schelling), the anonymous, future, countable many a
  policy would save, who draw no feeling because they have no face.
- **The Sympathy**: carries the affect (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic), the compassion the Face evokes,
  real and proportionate, the motive force of every gift and the thing the Number cannot summon.
- **The Numbing**: carries the flattening (Slovic; Kogut and Ritov), feeling falling as the number rises,
  so a single victim moves more than a group and the mass moves least.
- **The Shortfall**: carries the accrued sum (Schelling), the statistical many left underfunded gift by
  gift and ruling by ruling, the cost that lives only in the aggregate no giver sees.
- **The Deliberation**: carries the perverse cure (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic), analytic thought
  dampening the sympathy for the Face without raising any for the Number, so caring falls overall.
