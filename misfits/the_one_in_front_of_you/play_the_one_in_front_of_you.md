# Play: The One in Front of You

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The One in Front of You.
A child needs an operation, and there is a name, and a photograph, a face with brown hair and a story, and the money comes: strangers reach for their wallets, the appeal is swamped, the sum needed appears almost overnight, because here is a real person in real need and the heart answers, as it should. And down the road, or years ahead, a programme that would prevent a barely perceptible increase in preventable deaths, a clinic, a safety rule, a vaccination drive, goes underfunded, and no appeal is swamped for it, because it has no face: it is a number, a statistical many, an anonymous future population who will die if it is not funded and live if it is, and no one can picture them, and so no one weeps for them, and the money does not come. And the two are not weighed against each other by anyone, and no one chooses the child over the thousand: the giver answering the face is not being asked about the thousand, and could not feel the thousand if they were, because a thousand has no face and feeling has nothing to fasten on. And that is the whole difficulty: the compassion for the one in front of you is real and good and exactly what a decent heart should do, and the same compassion, meeting a number, goes quiet, so the gifts pile on the identified and the statistical go short, ruling by ruling, appeal by appeal, and the sum across a society is that resources flow to the few who can be seen and away from the many who cannot, and no one meant it, and no one is cold, and the reckoning is only visible from far above, in an aggregate no giver ever sees. And the obvious fix does not fix it: tell people the number, ask them to reason past the face and value the statistical life the same, and they do not give more to the thousand, they give less to the child, because thinking about the discrepancy cools the very feeling that made them give at all, so the heart that misallocates is the heart that gives, and you cannot correct its aim by asking it to stop feeling the one it can see.

## Arc

The bend is this: sympathy answers the identified individual in view and falls silent before the statistical many, so aid flows to the one who can be seen and away from the more who cannot, each act of compassion sound and the sum a systematic neglect of the unseen.
It runs on a feeling that needs a face.
There is a difference between an identified life, a specific person needing help now, and a statistical life, a nameless death a policy would prevent, and people will spend far more to save the first (Schelling). The gap is not in the worth of the lives but in the response: the identified draws feeling, the statistical draws none.
And the feeling is the motive. Compassion for a named, pictured, present victim is real and proportionate, and it is what moves the giver to give, so gifts concentrate on the single identified victim even where dispersing them would help more, the heart fastening on the one it can see (Small, Loewenstein, and Slovic).
And the feeling flattens with number. As the count rises the feeling does not rise with it but falls, so a single victim moves more than a group and the mass moves least of all, the more who suffer the less that is felt, the statistical many the hardest of all to feel because they are most numerous and least faced (Slovic).
And the sum is a shortfall. Gift by gift and ruling by ruling the identified are funded and the statistical are not, so across a society resources flow to the few in view and the larger unseen population goes underfunded, a cost that lives only in the aggregate and that no giver, answering their one face, ever chose or saw.
None of it is coldness. The response to the identified victim is genuine compassion for a real person, and the neglect of the statistical is not cruelty but the plain silence of feeling before an abstraction: no one weighs the child against the thousand and picks the child, and the shortfall emerges from a sympathy that answers the seen and cannot answer the unseen. What is most humane in the giver is what misallocates the aid.
Build the correction into the allocation, pool the giving, let an agency weigh the statistical lives the feeling cannot, and the many can be funded: but do not try to fix it by asking the giver to reason past the face, because deliberating on the discrepancy dampens the sympathy for the identified without kindling any for the statistical, so caring falls overall, and the escape is bought only by moving the choice off the feeling giver and onto a colder instrument, never by asking the warm heart to feel a number it cannot.

## Stakes

Whether the unseen many can be helped, when only the one in view can be felt.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the face, which draws real compassion and is funded, and away from the number, which draws none and goes short. The production cannot make the giver feel the statistical many, because feeling needs a face and a count has none, so the neglect of the unseen is not the giver's coldness but the plain silence of sympathy before an abstraction: no one weighs the child against the thousand, and the shortfall is the sum of a hundred warm and proper gifts to the one in front of each giver. It can only show that the compassion for the identified is genuine and right, that the statistical many go underfunded not by anyone's choosing but by having no face to summon feeling, and that the one move that funds the many, building the correction into the allocation and moving the choice onto a colder instrument, cannot be made by asking the warm heart to reason past the face, since deliberating on the discrepancy cools the sympathy for the seen without kindling any for the unseen, so the humanity that answers the one in front of you is the same humanity that leaves the thousand in the dark.
