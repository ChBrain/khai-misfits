## Load Bearing

Without it, no giver's feeling has anything to fasten on: every victim is as blank as a statistic, and the play collapses into a field of numbers no one gives to. With it, the identified life has a name, a photograph, a story, a surface the eye can hold and the heart can answer. The Face is load bearing because it is what makes an identified life identified at all (Schelling; Jenni and Loewenstein): the picturable particulars that let compassion land on a person rather than pass over an abstraction, so the piece carries the very picturability the feeling requires before it can move at all, the visible pole against which the faceless many can make no claim.

## Apparent

A detail, a nicety of the appeal, the sort of thing a fundraiser adds to make a case read well.
Seen at the surface, the Face looks like decoration: a name and a photograph attached to a need that would be just as real without them, and the truth that the decoration is the mechanism, that the same need stripped of its face draws no gift at all, that the picture is not illustrating the sympathy but manufacturing the only handle it has, stays hidden behind how natural it feels to want to know who you are helping.
