# Play: Sick Enough to Spread

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Sick Enough to Spread.
A germ is not trying to hurt anyone. It is only a lineage among lineages, and the ones that leave the most copies in the most new hosts are the ones that persist, so a germ is selected for spread and for nothing else, least of all for malice. But a germ spreads by multiplying inside a host, and the same multiplying that fills the host with copies to pass on is what wears the host down, so the harm is not a separate cruelty but the price of the spread, bought with the very act that sends the germ onward. Then the harm cannot fall to nothing, because a germ that multiplied too little to make the host sick would also multiply too little to leave for the next host, and it cannot climb without limit either, because a host killed too fast stops carrying and stops passing the germ on. Between those, selection finds the harm that spreads best: not the gentlest, which spreads too weakly, and not the most lethal, which burns its hosts before they carry it far, but the level in between where the spread is greatest, which is sicker than the host would ever choose and can still be very sick indeed. And there the germ settles and stays, locked to a harm that is worse than it need be for anyone, worse for the host who bears it and no better for the germ's own long run, because a lineage that held back would simply be outspread by one that did not, so no germ can choose the gentler road and keep the host. Change how it travels, though, by cleaning the water or killing the vector or keeping the sick apart, so that only a host still up and about can pass it on, and the same selection that fixed the harm high pulls it back down, milder strains now spreading best, the disease evolving kinder not by anyone's mercy but because the road that rewarded harm was closed. So the sickness is the price of the spread, set by no one and paid by the host, as high as it pays and no higher, and lowered only by changing the world the germ moves through and never by asking the germ to be kind.

## Arc

The bend is this: a parasite is selected only to maximise its spread, and because the replication that spreads it is the same replication that harms the host, harm is the price of transmission, so selection settles virulence not at zero but at the level that spreads best, a durable equilibrium sicker than the host would choose and no better for the parasite's own long run, and lowered only by changing the transmission ecology, never by any parasite choosing restraint (Anderson and May).
It runs on a spread that must be paid for in harm.
Because a parasite transmits by multiplying inside its host, and multiplying is what damages the host, the transmission a lineage is selected for is coupled to the harm it does, so a germ cannot spread well without harming and cannot stop harming without spreading less, the two bound in the one act and neither free of the other (Anderson and May). The coupling is physical, and it is why harm cannot simply fall away.
And the harm has a best amount, not a least.
Because transmission rises with the parasite's replication but the host, once killed, stops transmitting, fitness peaks at an intermediate virulence: too gentle spreads too little, too lethal ends the host too fast, and in between lies the level that maximises the reproduction number and so the spread, higher than the host would choose and, taken as the technical rate of host death, not always mild (Anderson and May). The optimum is real, and it is set by what spreads, not by what spares.
And selection settles the germ there and holds it.
Because a strain that harms less than the optimum spreads less and is outcompeted, and a strain that harms more burns its hosts and is outcompeted too, the lineage is winnowed to the transmission optimum and stays, a durable equilibrium and not a crash, worse than an achievable gentler state for the host and no gain to the parasite's long run, since restraint cannot be held against a rival that will not hold it (Anderson and May; Frank). The settling is stable, and it is why the harm persists rather than passing.
And where it settles depends on the road.
Because the optimum is fixed by how the parasite travels, a route that needs a healthy mobile host punishes lethality and pulls virulence down, while a route that carries the germ from a bedridden or dying host, by water, by vector, by the hands of attendants, relaxes that check and lets virulence rise, so the same trade-off yields a gentle disease on one road and a deadly one on another (Ewald). The road sets the level, and it is the lever the germ itself does not hold.
And the harm can be pared back, but only from outside.
Because closing the routes that reward lethality shifts the optimum down, cleaning the water or cutting the vector or parting the sick from the well selects for milder strains without touching the germ directly, and over years a disease can evolve kinder, as the myxoma virus loosed on Australia's rabbits fell within a few seasons from killing almost all it infected to killing fewer and later, because the slower-killing strains spread better by their longer-lasting sores (Ewald; Anderson and May). The remedy is real, and it is a change to the world and not a plea to the parasite.
None of it is a fix. The parasite multiplies because that is how it spreads, the multiplying harms because that is what it is, selection settles the harm where it spreads best because a gentler lineage is simply outspread, so each part is only fitness doing what fitness does, and the harm the host bears emerges from the sum, no cruelty and no design required. Undo it, and the undoing is dear: it means changing how the disease travels, the water and the vectors and the keeping of the sick, slow and partial and never a lever on the germ itself, and even then the harm falls only to the new road's optimum and not to none. Short of that the germ sits at the harm that pays, as sick as spreads best and sicker than anyone would choose, a bad bargain struck by no one and paid by the host, held in place not by malice but by the plain fact that the strain which harms exactly enough to spread the most is the strain that inherits the world.

## Stakes

Whether the harm a disease does is a cruelty in the germ or a price in its spread, the host wasted not by any malice but by the plain working of a parasite selected to travel and paying its fare in the only currency it has.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the set point, which the Guest is not wrong to reach, since a lineage that spread less would simply be replaced, and the harm right on the way, since the spread cannot be had without it, and away from the gentleness that would spare the host, which selection outcompetes wherever the road rewards harm. The production cannot make the disease a villain, because nothing in it means harm: the germ multiplies to spread and harms in the multiplying, the fittest strain is the one that pays best, so multiplying and spreading and being winnowed to the optimum are each only fitness at work. It can only show that a parasite selected purely to spread settles at the harm that spreads best, so the host tends to be made sicker than anyone would choose and can be wasted at a level no better for the germ's own long run, and that the harm is not fixed by the germ's nature alone but by the road it travels, pared back only by changing that road, the water and the vectors and the keeping of the sick, slow and partial and never a lever on the germ. It is a tendency and not a law, contested where the trade-off is unmeasured and reversed where the germ rides its host's own survival, so the disease need not escalate and an escalated one can be selected back down, but where spread is bought with harm the germ sits at the harm that pays, and the host carries the difference between the sickness it would choose and the sickness that spreads.
