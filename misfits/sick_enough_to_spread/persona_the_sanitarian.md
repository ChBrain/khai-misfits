# Persona: The Sanitarian

## Taxonomy

[The Cordon](position_the_cordon.md). Would make the disease gentler, and finds the germ cannot be asked to spare the host, only the road it travels can be changed, so the remedy is to clean the water, cut the vector, part the sick from the well, slow and partial and never a cure in the germ.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reaching to lower the harm. Learning the lever is the road, not the germ. Changing the world and waiting.
Is the one who would reduce what the disease does, and who comes to see that no appeal to the parasite will work, since the germ is only as harmful as its spread rewards, so the way to a gentler disease runs through the way it travels.
No miracle wished on them: the Sanitarian holds no cure for the germ and works instead on the water and the vector and the keeping of the sick, so their remedy is oblique and their victory slow, a selection pressure applied to the road and not a medicine given to the disease.

## Action

Changes the transmission ecology, cleaning the water so a waterborne germ can no longer spread from a bedridden host, cutting the vector so a mosquito no longer carries it from the dying, parting the sick from the well so only a mobile host passes it on.
Waits on selection to do the rest, since a road that now needs a healthy host punishes lethality, and over seasons the milder strains outspread the deadlier ones and the disease evolves kinder, as the myxoma virus did once its rabbits were left to it.
Reckons the cost and the limit, knowing the change is to infrastructure and takes years and is never complete, and that the harm falls only to the new road's optimum and not to none, a disease made milder and not made harmless.

## Shadow

The strange thing the Sanitarian carries is a healer's helplessness before the patient that is the disease: there is no dose to give the germ, no word that reaches it, because the thing has no ear and no will to appeal to, only a level set by its spread, so the whole craft of medicine as a thing done to a sickness is useless here and the work moves elsewhere, to pipes and screens and wards.
What sits oddly is the indirection of it: to lessen a harm by touching everything except the harm, to change the water and the vectors and wait for the disease to become, on its own and for its own reasons, a little less cruel, so the Sanitarian never faces the germ and never defeats it, only rearranges the world until the germ finds gentleness pays.
The bind is a mercy that must be built and cannot be granted: the Sanitarian cannot spare the host by any act aimed at the host or the germ, only by the long, costly, partial work of closing the roads that rewarded harm, and must live with a remedy that is slow where the suffering is now, oblique where the instinct is to treat, and bounded by the new road's own optimum, a kindness engineered into the world and never simply given.

## Tell

Says "I cannot make the germ kind, only close the road that paid it to be cruel."
Is the whole remedy in one line, since it names both the limit and the lever: no appeal lowers the germ's harm, because the harm is set by its spread, but a road closed shifts what its spread rewards, so the sentence that sounds like defeat is the plain shape of the only cure there is, a change to the world the disease moves through and never a change of heart in the disease.
