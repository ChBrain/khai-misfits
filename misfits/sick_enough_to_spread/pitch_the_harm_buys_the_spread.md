# Pitch: The Harm Buys the Spread

## Taxonomy

The single hook of [Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A germ that wanted to survive would surely learn to spare its host, since a dead host carries nothing onward. The old wisdom was exactly this: given time, parasites evolve toward harmlessness, a truce of mutual survival, the well-adapted disease a mild one. Health and long life for the host look like the parasite's own best interest.

## Undertow

The harm is what buys the spread. Because a parasite travels by multiplying inside a host, and the multiplying that fills the host with copies to pass on is the same multiplying that wears the host down, the germ cannot spread without harming: transmission is paid for in damage, and a strain that would not pay simply does not spread. So selection does not seek the gentle germ. It seeks the one that harms exactly enough to spread the most, which is neither the mildest nor the most lethal but the amount in between that pays, higher than the host would ever choose, and it settles the germ there and holds it.

## Nerve

The trap's nerve is that nothing is cruel. The germ has no aim and strikes no bargain in malice: it multiplies to spread, harms in the multiplying, and the fittest strain is the one whose harm buys the most passage, so the sickness is a price and not a purpose. And the germ cannot choose to pay less, because a lineage that held its harm down would be outspread by one that did not, so even a level worse for the germ's own long run is locked in, kept there not by anyone's will but by the plain fact that the strain which spreads most inherits the world.

## Echo

What it leaves is a host wasted to the degree the spread requires, a fare paid for a passage it never booked, and no gentleness to be asked of the germ. The harm is loosened only from outside: change how the disease travels, clean the water, cut the vector, keep the sick apart, so only a host still up and about can pass it on, and the same selection that fixed the harm high pulls it back toward mild, the disease evolving kinder not by mercy but because the road that rewarded harm was closed, and even then no gentler than the new road pays.
