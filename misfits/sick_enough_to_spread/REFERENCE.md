# Sick Enough to Spread: Reference

The research warrant for [Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md).
The dynamic is the evolution of virulence: Anderson and May's finding that virulence is coupled to transmission and
selection settles it at an intermediate optimum, Ewald on the transmission mode that shifts where the optimum sits, Frank
on the within-host competition that can push it higher, Levin and Bull on the short-sighted virulence that aids no
transmission, and Ebert and Bull with Alizon and colleagues on the trade-off model's contested standing, all cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the evolution of virulence as a **harm-is-the-price-of-spread structure**: a parasite is selected only to
maximise its transmission, and because the replication that spreads it through a host is the same replication that
damages the host, harm is coupled to spread, so selection does not drive virulence to zero but settles it at the level
that spreads best, an intermediate optimum that is sicker than the host would choose and no better for the parasite's own
long run, and holds there as a durable equilibrium because a lineage that harmed less would spread less and be
outcompeted. The play models the documented mechanics, the coupling of transmission to harm through replication, the
intermediate optimum where the reproduction number peaks, the host consumed to the degree the spread requires, and the
transmission ecology that moves the optimum, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a cruel germ
or a designed plague. The governing finding is the play's governing law: virulence is an evolved trait coupled to
transmission, so selection settles it at an intermediate, transmission-optimal level rather than minimising it, the harm
the price of the spread and set by what spreads and not by what spares. It is a tendency under stated conditions and not
an iron rule: the coupling and its optimum hold where the trade-off is present and are contested where it is unmeasured,
and the optimum falls where the parasite rides its host's own survival, so the play stages the harm a parasite selected
to spread tends to settle at, not a fate of every disease, and it holds the technical sense of virulence, the rate at
which the parasite kills its host, distinct from lay nastiness.

The distinct load-bearing vector is that harm settles at a **durable, suboptimal interior equilibrium** set by a **real,
mechanical** coupling of transmission to harm in a **separate self-interested lineage**, and the play is pinned to it.
It is distinct from Fit to Fail, whose engine is evolutionary suicide, a trait selected up until the population crosses a
viability threshold and crashes: there the endpoint is collapse, terminal and system-level, while here the endpoint is a
stable interior optimum that merely settles worse than an achievable gentler state and persists, a durable bad bargain
and not a self-termination, so the play keeps the parasite's long-run cost as short-sighted persistence and never a
slow-motion extinction. It is distinct from The Commons, whose engine is the depletion of a shared, reversible stock with
a restraint lever unpulled: here there is no stock and no lever, because higher transmission physically requires more
harm, so a restraining parasite simply transmits less, and the coupling curve, not a drawdown, is the engine, with the
within-host competition of coinfecting strains kept a secondary amplifier that shifts the optimum and never the
generative mechanism, the harm arising in a single-strain infection with no competition at all. It is distinct, most
pointedly, from The False Trade-Off, whose trade-off is illusory, a spurious correlation manufactured by conditioning on
a collider: there the remedy is to see clearly and the trade-off dissolves, while here the trade-off is real in the
biophysics, so seeing it clearly changes nothing and the harm is inescapable without a real cost, the two the exact foils
of an artefactual against a mechanical coupling. It is distinct from The Peace It Keeps, whose harmer is a cell that is
part of the very body it destroys, a truce broken from within: here the parasite is a separate organism and the host its
resource and vehicle, never a cooperative whole it belongs to, the harm a byproduct of the parasite's own transmission
and not a defection from a shared body. And it is distinct from In Its Own Defence, whose harm is the host's own immune
response overshooting: here the harm is the parasite's replication, the selective engine on the parasite's fitness and
not on the host's regulation, offence by the germ and not the host wounding itself.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a cruel germ or a designed
  plague, because the source finding is precisely that no malice and no design is needed: the parasite multiplies to
  spread and harms in the multiplying, so the harm is the price of the transmission and not an aim (Anderson and May),
  the fittest strain is the one that harms exactly enough to spread most, so the level is set by what spreads and not by
  any intent (Anderson and May), and a gentler lineage is simply outspread, so restraint cannot be held even where it
  would serve the germ's own long run (Frank). So the misfit is that a parasite selected purely to travel settles at the
  harm that spreads best, sicker than the host would choose and no better for the parasite's future, no cruelty and no
  plan required. The play holds virulence in its technical sense, the parasite-induced rate of host death, and not lay
  nastiness, and stages the harm as offence by the germ and not the host's own response, keeping clear of the
  immunopathology that in real disease often does the damage.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a disease made gentle by a plea to the germ. The one move that
  lowers virulence is to change how the parasite travels, cleaning the water, cutting the vector, parting the sick from
  the well, so that only a mobile host still passes it on and lethality is punished (Ewald), or to wait on the slow,
  partial coevolution that pared the myxoma virus from killing almost all its rabbits to killing fewer and later within a
  few seasons, the milder strains spreading better by their longer-lasting sores (Anderson and May; Ewald). Both are real
  costs, infrastructure and vector control and time, and not a lever anyone forgot to pull, and even then the harm falls
  only to the new road's optimum and not to none. The remedy is therefore a change to the world the germ moves through
  and never a cure in the germ, and it is real and not absent, since a parasite bound to its host's survival, one passed
  from parent to offspring, is selected toward mildness, so the play holds the harm loosenable and not fixed, the price
  of a road and lowered by closing it.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the transmission-coupled intermediate optimum is
  Anderson and May's, the transmission-mode shift is Ewald's, the within-host competition is Frank's, the short-sighted
  residual virulence is Levin and Bull's, and the contest over the model's generality is Ebert and Bull's with Alizon and
  colleagues', each carried with its guardrails: the optimum is intermediate and not maximal, and intermediate is not the
  same as mild; the trade-off is a tendency where the coupling holds and is often assumed rather than measured, so it is
  not staged as universal physics; the optimum is ecology-dependent and moves with the road; within-host competition and
  short-sighted evolution are secondary and residual and not the master mechanism; and vertical transmission and
  coevolution genuinely lower virulence, so the old view that parasites evolve benign is not simply wrong but not general.
  The misfit stages a host wasted at the harm that spreads best and credits that no germ was a villain and the level was
  set by fitness, holding it a tendency under stated conditions and not a law that fixes every disease at the harmful.
