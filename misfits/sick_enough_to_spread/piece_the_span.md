# Piece: The Span

## Taxonomy

[Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md). The infectious period, the time the host lasts and carries the germ, which a harsher parasite shortens, the countervailing arm that keeps the harm from climbing without limit.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Runs in [the Reservoir](place_the_reservoir.md) and out across [the Outbreak](place_the_outbreak.md), the length of the host's carrying.
Flows as the window in which a host, still alive, still passes the germ on, long under a gentle strain and cut short under a deadly one.

## Load Bearing

Without the Span the harm would rise without end: it is the shortening of the host's carrying by a harsher germ that punishes lethality, the cost that turns spread down again past a point and makes the optimum intermediate rather than maximal.
The Span is load bearing because it is the second arm of the trade-off, the reason too much harm spreads less (Anderson and May). A parasite that kills fast empties its host of the time to carry it, so beyond a certain harm each further increment of virulence buys less transmission than it loses in shortened carrying, and the fitness peak falls between. It is what keeps the germ from simply maximising harm, and so what makes the trap a settling at an intermediate level and not a race to the deadliest, the balance between the spread that harm buys and the carrying that harm cuts, decided on this arm.

## Apparent

Just how long someone is ill before they recover or die, a clinical detail.
Seen at the surface, the Span looks like a fact about a course of illness, the days of an infection, and the truth that it is the very thing that bounds the harm, that a germ is held back from maximal lethality only because killing fast cuts the carrying that spreads it, that the host's survival matters to the germ exactly and only as time to be transmitted from, stays hidden behind how ordinary the length of an illness seems.

## Yearbook

Cut by [the Guest](persona_the_guest.md) when it harms hard, kept by [the Carrier](persona_the_carrier.md) when it survives, and lengthened by [the Sanitarian](persona_the_sanitarian.md) only where the road comes to need the host alive.
It is the countervailing arm: the Span is what [winnowing](process_winnowing.md) weighs against the spread, the carrying [consuming](process_consuming.md) shortens, the term on [the curve](piece_the_curve.md) that bends the harm back down and sets [the set point](piece_the_set_point.md) in the middle and not at the maximum.
