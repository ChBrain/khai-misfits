# Place: The Route

## Taxonomy

[Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md). The road the germ travels from host to host, whose character sets where the harm settles: a road needing a mobile host punishing lethality, a road that carries from the failing letting it rise.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The way a disease gets about, the plain matter of how it is caught.
Shown at the surface is transmission as logistics: a germ passing by touch, by cough, by water, by the bite of a mosquito, by the hands of those who tend the sick, the ordinary channels through which one host's infection becomes another's.

## Holds

Holds the road, and the level of harm the road allows.
The Route is where it is fixed whether the germ needs its host well to travel or can spread from a host already failing, so it holds not just the channel but the check on virulence that channel sets: a road that needs a mobile host makes lethality a dead end, while a road by water or vector or attendant carries the germ from the bedridden, so the same disease is held to mildness on one road and let toward deadliness on another.

## Offers

Offers the germ its passage, and the host whatever mercy the passage's shape allows.
Here the parasite is carried onward, so the Route offers the spread its channel; and offers, through the channel's character, the level of the harm: where the road needs the host up and about, the germ is selected gentle and the host is spared as a byproduct of what the germ requires; where the road spreads from a failing host, that mercy is withdrawn and the harm is let rise, so the road that carries the germ also sets what the carrying costs.

## Withheld

Withholds any harm the road does not set, and any road that is nothing but a channel.
What the Route cannot offer is a transmission with no bearing on virulence, because the way a germ travels is the very thing that decides whether killing its host is a cost or no cost to it, so there is no neutral channel, only roads that punish lethality and roads that reward it. The place that is the germ's passage is never merely its passage: it is the lever that sets the harm, held by whoever can open or close it and by no appeal to the germ, so to change the harm is to change the road and to leave the road unchanged is to leave the harm where the road has put it.
