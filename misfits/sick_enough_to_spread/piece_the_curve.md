# Piece: The Curve

## Taxonomy

[Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md). The trade-off itself, the coupling by which transmission rises with the parasite's replication and so with the harm, the relation that makes spread cost the host and cannot be undone.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Runs through [the Reservoir](place_the_reservoir.md), where the replication that spreads is the replication that harms.
Flows as the bond between how far a strain spreads and how much it costs the host, rising together because both are the one multiplying, a line no strain sits off.

## Load Bearing

Without the Curve the harm could fall to nothing: it is the coupling of transmission to virulence that makes spread cost the host, the relation without which a germ could spread freely and spare its host both.
The Curve is load bearing because it is the trade-off at the root, the finding that a parasite's transmission and its harm are bound because both grow with its replication (Anderson and May). It is what forbids the gentle germ, since a strain cannot raise its spread without raising its harm, and it is what shapes every level downstream, because the harm that spreads best is a point on this line and nowhere else. So the Curve carries the trap's first premise: the spread is real and so is its price, bound in the one act, so the disease is not a cruelty added to the germ but a cost built into how the germ travels, and the whole of the harm begins in this coupling, which lets no transmission be had for free.

## Apparent

Just how sick a bug happens to make you, a fact about a particular germ.
Seen at the surface, the Curve looks like a mere property of a disease, this one severe and that one mild, and the truth that the severity is not a fixed trait but a coupling, that the harm rises with the spread by the same replication and cannot be prised off it, that a germ is severe because severity is what its spread costs, stays hidden behind how much a coupled price resembles a simple attribute.
