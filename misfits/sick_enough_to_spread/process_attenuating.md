# Process: Attenuating

## Taxonomy

[Sick Enough to Spread](play_sick_enough_to_spread.md). The pulling of virulence back down when the road is changed, selection favouring milder strains once lethality no longer spreads, the counter-force that is real, slow, partial, and never a lever on the germ itself.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Sanitarian](persona_the_sanitarian.md), at [the Route](place_the_route.md), closing the roads that let the germ spread from a failing host.
Begins when the transmission ecology is changed so that only a mobile host still passes the germ on, or when a parasite comes to depend on its host's own survival.

## Direction

Turns a road that rewarded harm into one that rewards mildness.
Because the optimum is set by what spread rewards, attenuating shifts it by changing the road: close the routes that carry the germ from the failing, and lethality becomes a dead end, so selection now favours the strains that keep their hosts up and about, and over the generations the disease evolves kinder, the same sorting that fixed the harm high now pulling it down.

## Lever

The optimum's dependence on the road.
Because virulence settles wherever the transmission rewards it, attenuating works not on the germ but on the world it travels through: the lever is the road, changed so that a mobile host is needed and a lethal strain outspread, so the harm falls by selection and not by treatment, as it fell in the myxoma virus once its slower-killing strains spread better from their longer-lasting sores, and as it falls in a parasite passed to its host's offspring, whose fitness is bound to the host's survival.

## Echo

A disease made milder, slowly and partially and only to the new road's optimum.
The echo of attenuating is a harm pared back by a changed ecology, the germ no gentler by any mercy but because gentleness now spreads best, so the disease settles lower and stays there, still at the level the new road rewards and not at none, a real remission bought with infrastructure and time and never a cure worked in the germ.
