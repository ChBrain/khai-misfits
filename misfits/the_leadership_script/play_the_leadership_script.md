# Play: The Leadership Script

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Leadership Script.
There is a way that leadership sounds, and everyone in the building knows it: the level gaze and the strategic frame, the vision stated in the round confident register, the vocabulary of alignment and ownership and the bold call, the ability to hold a room and name a direction and make the uncertain sound handled. And it is not nothing. Some of what the register carries is real: articulacy is part of leading, a person who can frame a choice and align a room is doing a piece of the job, and the sound is not always empty. So when a chair falls open and two people stand for it, the one who speaks the register and the one who does the work, the panel hears leadership in the first and reaches for it, and reaching for it is not folly: the register is legible, it reads in an hour, and it correlates, loosely, with something the role wants, while the substance of the second candidate is buried in work that takes a month to read and a specialist to judge. So the fluent rises, and the doer is thanked and kept where the doing is needed, and no one has done wrong: the panel selected on the best signal it could hear in the time it had, and the signal was the sound. And here the loop closes. Because now the risen leader speaks the register, and the next panel, looking for what leadership sounds like, hears it confirmed in the ones already up there, so the register is not just a signal of leadership, it is becoming the definition of it, the mark by which the thing is known. And the aspirants below, who are not fools either, hear exactly what rises, and they learn the register, coach it, rehearse it, cast themselves to the mould, because to speak it is to be heard as leadership and to speak plainly is to be heard as staff. So the sound spreads and the ear hardens, and speaking the Script comes to feel like the natural voice of anyone fit to lead, no one having imposed it and everyone confirming it, until the organisation cannot easily tell, at the top, the register from the thing it stood for, and the work goes on down on the Floor, carried by the plain hands whose substance the register never learned to hear, load-bearing and unpromoted. Nobody rigged this. The panel read the best signal it had, the aspirant learned what was rewarded, the fluent may be able as well as audible, and articulacy really is a part of the job. It is only that a register legible enough to select on, selected on round after round, becomes the identity of the thing it was only ever a loose sign of, so the house comes to hear leadership as a sound and to promote the sound, and the competence beneath, never the thing selected for, is left to be hoped for.

## Arc

The bend is this: a register legible enough to be selected on, selected on round after round, becomes the mark of leadership it was only a loose sign of, so the house promotes the sound and the substance beneath goes unheard.
It runs on a sound that really does carry part of the job.
There is a way leadership sounds, and it is not empty: articulacy, the framing of a choice, the holding of a room are real parts of leading, so a register that carries them is a true partial signal (Fairclough, on the order of discourse, the way a dominant way of speaking comes to seem the natural one). This is what makes it a trap rather than a fault, because selecting on the sound is not foolish: the register is legible in an hour, reads across a table, and points, loosely, at something the role wants.
And the sound is legible where the substance is not. The register can be read in a meeting; the work takes a month and a specialist to judge, so the panel, choosing under real constraint, reaches for the signal it can actually hear, and the fluent candidate reads as leadership while the doer's competence stays buried in work no panel has time to open (Khurana, on selection driven by the charismatic image over the demonstrable record). The choice is rational, and it is a choice of the sound.
And the selection makes the sign the definition. Once the fluent are the risen, the next panel hears leadership confirmed in the ones already up, so the register stops being a signal of leadership and starts being the mark of it, and the aspirants below, hearing exactly what rises, learn the register, coach it, and cast themselves to the mould, because to speak it is to be heard as a leader and to speak plainly is to be heard as staff (Bourdieu, on linguistic capital, the legitimate tongue that buys standing; Meindl, on the romance of leadership, the thing read into a performance). The loop closes and the ear hardens.
And the work goes on unheard beneath it. The substance the register never captured is still carried, down on the Floor by the plain hands who do it and are kept where the doing is needed, load-bearing and unpromoted, so the organisation runs on a competence it does not select for and cannot, at the top, easily tell from the sound it does (Alvesson and Sveningsson, on the extra-ordinarisation of the mundane, leadership talked into being over the ordinary work). The register rises and the work stays down.
None of it is a rig. The panel read the best signal it could hear in the time it had, the aspirant learned what the house rewarded, the fluent leader may be able as well as audible, and articulacy is a real part of the job, so no one imposes the Script and everyone confirms it: it is naturalised, the sound of anyone fit to lead, authored by no one. What makes the register worth hearing, that it carries a true part of leadership and reads fast, is exactly what lets it stand in for the whole and be selected until it is the whole.
Read the work and not the register, promote on the demonstrated substance and hear the plain hand, and the sound can be put back in its place as a part and not the mark: but this means judging what takes a month and a specialist over what reads in an hour, distrusting a signal that is genuinely part-right, telling the aspirants who learned the register that the register is not the thing after the house spent years teaching them it was, and hearing leadership in a voice the ear has been trained to file as staff, against every incentive to reach again for the legible sound, so the substance is heard only by paying, continuously, the cost of reading the work the register was the cheap way not to.

## Triggers

**[The Audition](plot_the_audition.md)**
A chair falls open and the Fluent speaks the register at the Review. The Panel, hearing articulacy and vision and the confident frame, hears leadership, and the register reads as the mark of it in the time the Panel has. Closes when the sound is taken, in the room, as the sign of the thing, a true part of the job standing in for the whole.

**[The Rise](plot_the_rise.md)**
Cue: two stand for the chair, the fluent and the doer. The Panel selects the legible signal it can hear over the substance it cannot, and the Fluent rises while the Plain Hand is thanked and kept where the doing is needed. Closes when the register has been selected over the work, rationally, under the real constraint of what reads in an hour against what takes a month.

**[The Casting](plot_the_casting.md)**
Cue: the aspirants below hear exactly what rises. They learn the register, coach and rehearse it, and cast themselves to the mould, because to speak it is to be heard as a leader and to speak plainly is to be heard as staff. Closes when the Script is the identity aspirants form themselves to, the loop closed and the ear hardened, the sign becoming the definition.

**[The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md)**
Cue: the house looks at its top and cannot easily tell the register from the thing it stood for. Selection has run on the sound so long that the correlation between the sound and the substance has thinned, and no panel can now read, in the risen, which had the work beneath the words. Closes when the organisation has lost the means to tell sound from substance at the level it selects, no one having faked and the signal having simply been optimised.

**[The Doubt](plot_the_doubt.md)**
Cue: someone would read the work and not the register. The Reader would promote on demonstrated substance and hear the plain hand, against the legibility of the sound, the fluency of everyone invested, and an ear trained to file plain speech as staff. Closes when the work is read at real cost and the substance is heard ... or the read is waived as slow and unfair, the legible sound reached for again, and the Script kept.

## Stakes

Whether a house can hear the work over the sound of leadership, when the sound is legible and part-true and the work is buried and slow.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the register selected, which the Panel is right to reach for and the aspirants right to learn, and away from the substance heard, which would ask the house to judge what takes a month over what reads in an hour and to hear leadership in a voice it has trained itself to file as staff. The production cannot make the sound be the substance, because a register legible enough to select on really does carry a true part of the job and really is the cheap way to read a room, so the Script rising is selection working as selection must under constraint, not a rig: no one imposes the register and everyone confirms it. It can only show that the panel read the best signal it had, that the aspirant learned what the house rewarded, that the fluent may be able as well as audible, and that the one move that puts the sound back in its place, reading the work and promoting the substance and hearing the plain hand, must be made by paying, continuously, the cost of judging what the register was the cheap way not to judge, against every incentive to reach again for the legible sound, so the ear is honest and the choice is rational and the leadership the house selected for is the sound of the thing and not always the thing.
