# Persona: The Panel

## Taxonomy

[Panel](position_panel.md). The one who selects for the chair, reading the register as the signal of leadership because the register is legible in the time it has and the substance is not.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Discerning. Pressed for time. Reaching for the legible.
Is whoever fills the chair, the promotion committee, the hiring manager, the board, the room that decides who leads and must decide it on what it can read.
No prejudice intended: the Panel reads the best signal it can hear in an hour, and the register really does point, loosely, at part of the job.

## Action

Hears the candidates, reads the register as the mark of leadership, and reaches for the fluent over the plain.
Selects the sound it can judge across a table over the substance it would need a month and a specialist to open.
Does what a selector under constraint does: it chooses on the legible signal, and the legible signal is the sound.

## Shadow

Not biased in bad faith, a chooser reading the only signal it has time to read, and that is the whole difficulty: the register is legible and the work is not, so the rational choice under constraint is the choice of the sound.
Because the Panel must decide in the time it has and the register reads in that time while the work does not, it selects the fluent, and by selecting the fluent it makes the fluent the risen, so the next Panel hears leadership confirmed in the ones already up and reaches for the register again, the choice sound at each step and the sum a top selected for the sound, the Panel never able, at the moment of choosing, to read the substance it would have preferred.
It reads the best signal it can hear, and the best signal it can hear is exactly the one the register was optimised to send.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "we know a leader when we hear one."
Says it having heard the register and reached for it in good faith, and cannot, in the hour it has, tell the candidate who has the work beneath the words from the one who has only the words.
