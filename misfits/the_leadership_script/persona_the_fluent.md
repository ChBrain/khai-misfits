# Persona: The Fluent

## Taxonomy

[Fluent](position_fluent.md). The one who speaks the register natively and rises on the sound, becoming, once risen, the living proof by which the next panel knows what leadership sounds like.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Articulate. Assured. Fluent in the register.
Is the candidate who sounds like a leader, who frames the choice, holds the room, and states the direction in the confident vocabulary the house hears as leadership.
No fraud in it necessarily: the Fluent may be able as well as audible, and the register really does carry a real part of the job, offered in good faith.

## Action

Speaks the Script, frames and aligns and states the bold direction, and is heard as leadership in the time a panel has to listen.
Reads, across a table, as the thing the role wants, where a doer's substance would take a month and a specialist to judge.
Does what the register rewards: it sounds the part, and sounding the part is what the house has learned to promote.

## Shadow

Not a faker, a genuine speaker of a register that is genuinely part of the job, and that is the whole difficulty: the sound is legible and part-true, and legible-and-part-true is exactly what gets selected over the whole.
Because the Fluent reads fast and the work reads slow, the panel reaches for the sound, and once risen the Fluent becomes the confirmation the next panel hears, the register turning from a signal of leadership into the mark of it, so the fluency that was only ever a loose sign of competence is selected until it stands as the definition, and the Fluent need not have less substance than the doer, only more of the sound that is actually read.
It speaks the part it was rewarded to speak, and being heard as the part is what leaves the work below unheard.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "here is the vision, here is the call," in the register that reads as leadership.
Says it the same whether the substance is beneath the words or not, and cannot be told, across a table, from the one who has the work and not the sound.
