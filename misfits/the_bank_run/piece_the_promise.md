# Piece: The Promise

## Taxonomy

[The Bank Run](play_the_bank_run.md). The contract: full payment, on demand, first come first served, sound and unkeepable if all ask at once.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the window](place_the_window.md), made good one saver at a time.
Where the promise is honoured exactly as written, in the order people arrive.

## Load Bearing

Without it the saver would bear his own liquidity risk and there would be nothing to run to.
With it every saver holds a claim to be paid in full the instant he asks, so the bank owes everyone at once what it can only pay a few at a time, and the promise is genuine for whoever comes early and hollow for whoever comes late. The Promise is load bearing because it is the demandable-debt contract the run turns on (Bryant; Diamond & Dybvig): a pledge a solvent bank can keep any ordinary day and no bank can keep on the day it is doubted, so the run is a failure to honour a promise that was never keepable in full.

## Apparent

A guarantee of safety.
Money payable in full on demand reads as the very definition of a safe place to keep it, so the promise looks like protection, when it is the instrument that turns a shared fear into a race to the window.

## Yearbook

Honoured by [the banker](persona_the_banker.md), grown out of [the maturity mismatch](piece_the_maturity_mismatch.md) it cannot outrun.
It is the pledge that cannot bend: the Promise is what [the queue](piece_the_queue.md) tests to destruction, real for the front of the line and empty for the end.
