# Play: The Bank Run

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Bank Run.
A bank takes in savings payable on demand and lends them out long, which is the useful thing a bank is for ... and one day a whisper says it may not be able to pay, and though the loans are good and the bank is sound, each saver reasons that if the others line up he must be paid before the cash runs out, so he lines up too, and the lining up is what empties the till, and the bank that could have paid everyone in time cannot pay everyone at once, and the fear that it would fail is precisely what makes it.

## Arc

The bend is this: the belief that the bank will fail is what makes it fail.
It runs on a promise a sound bank still cannot keep.
The deposits are payable in full, on demand, first come first served, and the money is lent out in loans that cannot be called back in a day. So long as most savers leave their money in, the few who need theirs are paid and all is well: that is one settled state, and nothing in the books rules out another. Let enough savers believe the others will run, and running first becomes the sensible move for every one of them, because the last in line gets nothing, and a solvent bank forced to sell good loans at a loss to pay the queue is made insolvent by the paying.
None of it is folly. The saver who runs is right that if the others run he must run first; the one who trusts the bank and holds his place is wiped out for being right about the loans and wrong about the crowd; and the fear needs no true weakness to feed on, so it can jump to the sound bank next door and fell it too.
Stand a credible promise behind the deposits, a guarantee that pays whatever comes, and the reason to run first is gone and the good state holds: but the same backstop that stops the run also stands behind every risk the bank now knows it can take, so the calm is bought with the house's moral hazard.

## Triggers

**[The Mismatch](plot_the_mismatch.md)**
A bank takes savings payable on demand and lends them out in loans that cannot be called in a day. The Banker does the useful work of turning short money into long, and the Trusting leaves his savings in, paid on demand whenever he asks. Closes when the promise is in place, sound and unkeepable if everyone asks at once.

**[The Whisper](plot_the_whisper.md)**
Cue: a doubt appears, a rumour, a headline, a neighbour's worry, that the bank may not be able to pay. The Whisperer carries it, and the Depositor begins to reckon not with the loans but with the crowd. Closes when enough savers believe the others will run that running becomes each one's sensible move.

**[The Run](plot_the_run.md)**
Cue: the line forms at the window and the money is paid first come first served. The Depositor runs because the others are running, the Trusting holds his place and is wiped out, and the Banker sells good loans at a loss to pay the queue and is made insolvent by paying it. Closes when the fear has emptied the till of a bank that was sound.

**[The Contagion](plot_the_contagion.md)**
Cue: the panic looks for the next bank, and does not check its books first. The Neighbour, never doubted and entirely solvent, insists it is different, and is run all the same. Closes when a sound bank falls to a fear it did nothing to earn, proving the run was never about the loans.

**[The Backstop](plot_the_backstop.md)**
Cue: someone stands a credible promise behind the deposits, a guarantee that pays whatever comes. The Backstop removes the reason to run first, and the good state holds without a coin changing hands. Closes when the run is stopped by a promise ... at the price of standing behind every risk the bank now knows it can take.

## Stakes

Whether a bank is ever judged by its loans, or only ever by the length of the line outside it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward the books, which are sound, but toward the crowd, which is all that matters once it moves. The production cannot make a promise to pay everyone on demand survive everyone asking at once, so a solvent bank is only ever one shared fear away from the queue that empties it. It can only show that no saver was foolish, that running first is right when the others run and trusting is punished when they do, and that the one thing that stops the run, a guarantee credible enough that no one needs to be first, works by underwriting the very risk-taking it makes safe, so the panic is answered with the house's moral hazard.
