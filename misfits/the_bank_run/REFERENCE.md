# The Bank Run: Reference

The research warrant for [The Bank Run](play_the_bank_run.md). The title names the event; the play
runs on Diamond and Dybvig's model of banking and runs, Merton's self-fulfilling prophecy, and the
lender-of-last-resort and deposit-insurance answers to panic, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the bank run as a **systemic system**: a solvent bank that turns demand deposits into
illiquid loans is destroyed not by its books but by the belief that it will be destroyed, because
the deposits are payable in full on demand and first come first served, so once enough savers expect
the others to withdraw, withdrawing first is each one's rational move, and the withdrawals themselves
make the failure real. The play models the documented mechanics, the maturity mismatch that is the
bank's whole purpose and whole weakness, the promise a sound bank still cannot keep, the two
equilibria that the same fundamentals allow, the first-come-first-served queue that makes running
first rational, the self-fulfilling belief that validates itself, the collapse of a solvent bank into
insolvency by fire sale, the contagion to sound banks, and the backstop that alone restores the good
state, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of panic-as-stupidity. The
governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: a demand-deposit bank has a
good equilibrium and a run equilibrium, and nothing in its solvency picks between them, so a run is a
self-fulfilling coordination failure that a credible guarantee can prevent. The guarantee's price is
the house's own Moral Hazard: the backstop that ends the run also stands behind the risk the bank
now knows it can take.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  running saver as a fool or a coward, because the source finding is precisely that the run is
  rational: in a queue paid first come first served, if the others withdraw the last in line gets
  nothing, so withdrawing first is each saver's best reply to a run he expects. The misfit is that
  every saver reasoning soundly about the crowd empties a bank that was sound about its loans.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into confidence restored by a brave speech. No
  reassurance holds while the queue can still form, because the fear is self-justifying; the one move
  that works is structural, a guarantee credible enough that no one needs to be first, and it costs,
  because the same backstop that removes the run underwrites the risk that made the bank fragile. The
  panic is stopped by a promise, at the price of moral hazard.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the model, the self-fulfilling-prophecy
  account, and the lender-of-last-resort doctrine are theirs, and the condition a run actually
  requires, a demand-deposit contract and a coordinating fear, is carried as carefully as the finding
  itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Maturity Mismatch](piece_the_maturity_mismatch.md)**: carries maturity transformation (Diamond & Dybvig),
  short demand deposits funding long illiquid loans, the bank's purpose and its fragility in one.
- **[The Promise](piece_the_promise.md)**: carries the demandable-debt contract (Bryant; Diamond & Dybvig), full
  payment on demand, first come first served, sound and unkeepable if all ask at once.
- **[The Two Equilibria](piece_the_two_equilibria.md)**: carries the multiple-equilibrium result (Diamond & Dybvig),
  a good state and a run state that the same solvency allows.
- **[The Queue](piece_the_queue.md)**: carries the sequential-service constraint (Diamond & Dybvig), the
  first-come-first-served rule that makes running first each saver's rational move.
- **[The Self-Fulfilling Prophecy](piece_the_self_fulfilling_prophecy.md)**: carries the self-fulfilling belief
  (Merton), the fear that makes itself true by being acted upon.
- **[The Collapse](piece_the_collapse.md)**: carries the fire-sale insolvency (Diamond & Dybvig; Bernanke), a
  solvent bank made insolvent by liquidating good loans to pay the queue.
- **[The Contagion](piece_the_contagion.md)**: carries the spread of panic (Bernanke), the run jumping to sound
  banks it has no reason to doubt.
- **[The Backstop](piece_the_backstop.md)**: carries the remedy and its price (Bagehot; Diamond & Dybvig), the
  credible guarantee that removes the reason to run, at the cost of the house's moral hazard.
