# Piece: The Backstop

## Taxonomy

[The Bank Run](play_the_bank_run.md). The remedy: a credible guarantee that removes the reason to run, at the cost of the house's moral hazard.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the guarantee](place_the_guarantee.md), the promise stood behind every deposit.
And across the whole system, wherever a backstop makes being first not matter.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would have no answer, only the hope that no one ever whispers.
With it there is a way out and a price on it: a guarantee bigger than the panic removes the fear of being last, so the run equilibrium is closed and the good one holds without a coin paid, and yet the same promise that stops the saver watching stops the banker fearing, and stands behind whatever risk is taken behind it. The Backstop is load bearing because it holds both halves of the answer (Bagehot; Diamond & Dybvig): deposit insurance and the lender of last resort really do stop runs, and they do it by underwriting the fragility, so the panic is cured with the house's Moral Hazard.

## Apparent

A costless rescue, or a reckless subsidy.
Seen from one side it looks like something for nothing, a run stopped by a promise never called; seen from the other, a licence for banks to gamble on the public purse, and both miss that it is one thing, a fear removed and a discipline removed with it.

## Yearbook

Wielded by [the backstop](persona_the_backstop.md) through [backstopping](process_backstopping.md), the shutter on [the two equilibria](piece_the_two_equilibria.md).
It is the play's answer and its bill: the Backstop closes [the self-fulfilling prophecy](piece_the_self_fulfilling_prophecy.md), and pays for the calm by standing behind the risk that made the run possible.
