# Process: Trimming

## Taxonomy

[The Brittle Optimum](play_the_brittle_optimum.md). The idle slack cut slice by slice for a real, immediate saving, the give thinned without harm in calm.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A margin sitting idle and a system judged on the ordinary day.
At [the margin](place_the_margin.md), the optimiser finding the spare, the buffer, the extra time that earns nothing, and paring it away.

## Direction

Toward a leaner, cheaper system.
Each cut removes a slice of slack and returns its cost as a saving, and because the slack did nothing that day, nothing breaks, so trimming drives the system down toward the optimum on a run of cuts each of which is a true gain and none of which shows a loss.

## Lever

An immediate saving against a cost that has not come due.
Because the gain from cutting is booked at once and the fragility it buys waits on a shock, the optimiser is rewarded for every trim and charged for none, so trimming runs on the plain asymmetry of a certain present saving over an invisible future risk, cut after cut.

## Echo

A slice of give gone, and the couplings a notch tighter.
The system is leaner and the numbers better, and the capacity that would have absorbed a shock is that much smaller, so what reads at the margin as pure discipline is, unseen, the thinning of the give the shatter will need.
