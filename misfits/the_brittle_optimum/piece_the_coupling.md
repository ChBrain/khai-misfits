# Piece: The Coupling

## Taxonomy

[The Brittle Optimum](play_the_brittle_optimum.md). The tightness that grows as slack is removed, so a failure in one part runs straight to the next with nothing to damp it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Tightened at [the works](place_the_works.md), where each cut leaves the parts leaning closer on one another.
Discharged at [the break](place_the_break.md), where the failure it carries runs the whole system down.

## Load Bearing

Without it a failure would stay local and the shatter would not spread.
With it the parts depend on each other so closely that a jolt in one is felt at once by the rest: slack is not only spare, it is the looseness that lets one part fail without the next feeling it, and cutting it couples the system tight. The Coupling is load bearing because it is why the trap ends in collapse and not a stumble (Perrow): the same optimisation that raised efficiency raised the coupling, so the piece carries the structural cost of the give removed, the tightness that turns a local failure into a total one.

## Apparent

Neat integration, a system whose parts work in close concert.
Seen in calm, the coupling looks like the elegance of a well-fitted machine, everything meshing, nothing loose, and the truth that the same tightness that makes it efficient is what will carry one failure to all, that the loose joint was a safety feature, stays hidden behind how well the close-knit system runs.

## Yearbook

Grown through [tightening](process_tightening.md), the closeness that removes each part's cover.
It is the give's absence made structure: the Coupling is what [trimming](process_trimming.md) leaves as it removes [the slack](piece_the_slack.md), the tightness [the efficiency](piece_the_efficiency.md) is bought with, and the path along which [the shatter](piece_the_shatter.md) runs when the shock lands.
