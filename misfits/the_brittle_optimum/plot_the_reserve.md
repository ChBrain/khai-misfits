# Plot: The Reserve

## Taxonomy

[The Brittle Optimum](play_the_brittle_optimum.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Backup](place_the_backup.md), the last shatter still fresh and someone moving to hold slack against the next.
A push to keep the spare and the buffer and the margin as insurance, against every calm-day argument to cut them.

## Action

[Reserving](process_reserving.md): [The Reserver](persona_the_reserver.md), holding [the reserver's seat](position_reserver.md), keeps the give on purpose, while [The Optimizer](persona_the_optimizer.md), holding [the optimizer's seat](position_optimizer.md), presses that it costs every calm day and proves nothing.

## Stage

[The Reserver](persona_the_reserver.md), having tabled [Keep the Reserve](plan_keep_the_reserve.md), holds the slack so the system can flex, carrying the standing cost through the calm.
[The Optimizer](persona_the_optimizer.md), still on [Trim the Fat](plan_trim_the_fat.md), points to the leaner rival and the unmarked calm, and warns that a reserve too large is only bloat, so the give is kept under constant pressure to cut.

## Tension

The one thing that lets the system flex is the thing the calm always argues to remove.
There is no easy answer here: the reserve plainly saves the system, yet it costs on every ordinary day, underperforms the leaner rival, cannot prove the shatter it prevents, and pressed too far becomes waste of its own. So the play closes where it must, the slack kept and the system able to flex ... or the calm returns, the numbers win, and the give is trimmed again toward the optimum that breaks.
