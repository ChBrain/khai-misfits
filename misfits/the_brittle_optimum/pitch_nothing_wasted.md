# Pitch: Nothing Wasted

## Taxonomy

[The Brittle Optimum](play_the_brittle_optimum.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Lean, disciplined, well-run.
The satisfaction of a system with the waste cut out of it, every part earning its keep and nothing sitting idle.
Nothing careless in it: only the steady discipline of trimming what does nothing and running tight.

## Undertow

Under the discipline, the give that would have caught the shock, cut away as idle.
Because slack does nothing on an ordinary day, each trim is a real saving and nothing breaks, so the cutting is rewarded and repeated, and the same efficiency tightens the coupling until the system has no flex left in it.
The waste that was removed was the insurance, and it is missed only on the one day it would have mattered.

## Nerve

The feeling of cutting the last idle margin and watching the numbers improve,
sure it is only good discipline, because nothing broke,
while the give that would have absorbed the next shock is exactly what was trimmed to earn the gain.

## Echo

After the run: a lean, admired system shattered by a jolt a looser one would have shrugged off,
every cut a true saving and their sum a fragility no one saw until the break,
and a reserve that would have held it, cut long ago because, on every ordinary day, it looked like waste.
