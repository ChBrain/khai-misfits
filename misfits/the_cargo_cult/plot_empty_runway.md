# Plot: Empty Runway

## Taxonomy

[The Cargo Cult](play_the_cargo_cult.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The checklist](place_the_checklist.md), out and ready, and no cargo on the strip.
The season has passed and nothing has landed, so it is time to review, and the review is of the form: how faithfully was the model followed, how cleanly was the ceremony kept.

## Action

[Conformance](process_conformance.md): [The Inspector](persona_the_inspector.md), holding [the auditor's seat](position_auditor.md), scores the team's fidelity to the model, ticks the ritual against the manual, and passes it.
Alongside, [The Doubter](persona_the_doubter.md) at [the skeptic's seat](position_skeptic.md) asks the other question: where are the planes.

## Stage

[The Inspector](persona_the_inspector.md) tables [Check the Form](plan_check_the_form.md): audit the ceremony against the model and find it faithful, so [the ritual](piece_the_ritual.md) passes cleanly while the empty result goes unmeasured.
[The Doubter](persona_the_doubter.md) tables [Ask Where the Planes Are](plan_ask_where_the_planes_are.md): point at [the empty runway](piece_the_empty_runway.md) and ask what the ceremony is actually producing, and is set aside as off-book, negative, not a team player.

## Tension

The audit passes and the runway is empty, and both facts stand.
The inspector is diligent and the checklist is real, but it measures fidelity to the form, not the arrival of cargo, so a perfect score and a failed outcome sit side by side without contradiction. And the doubter is right and unwelcome, because the question that would break the cult, does this actually work, is the one question the form is not built to hear, so the doubt is filed, the ceremony is certified, and the strip stays bare with a clean report on top of it.
