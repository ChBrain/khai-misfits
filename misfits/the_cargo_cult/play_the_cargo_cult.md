# Play: The Cargo Cult

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Cargo Cult.
A team that saw a thing work somewhere else and built its exact likeness: the same ceremonies, the same artifacts, the same words in the same order, faithful to the last detail ... and waited on the swept runway for a cargo that never came, because the runway was never what made the planes land.

## Arc

The bend is this: they copied everything that could be seen, and missed the only thing that mattered.
It runs on a split between form and substance.
The form is the visible shape of a success: the standup, the framework, the org chart, the ritual a winning team was seen to keep, all of it copyable, all of it safe to be seen copying.
The substance is the invisible thing that made the form work: the judgement, the trust, the hard-won fit to a particular problem, none of it on the surface, none of it copied, because it could not be seen to be copied.
Under uncertainty, copying a proven model is the sensible bet, and the copy buys something real, the look of legitimacy, the credit for doing it properly, and that credit arrives whether or not the cargo does, so the ritual is rewarded for its fidelity and never audited for its result.
Strip the ceremony back to the mechanism and ask what actually makes cargo come, and function can return: but the runway is swept, the tower is built, the headphones are carved, and everyone is doing it exactly right.

## Triggers

**[Plan](plot_plan.md)**
A team under pressure to succeed, unsure what causes success, looks to a model that worked. The Copyist adopts its form, and the Patron rewards the resemblance. Closes when the form is chosen over the substance, because the form is what can be seen and paid for.

**[Do](plot_do.md)**
Cue: the form is built to the letter. The Ritualist performs the ceremony faithfully, and the Screen keeps it on display while the real work is decoupled behind it. Closes when the ritual runs perfectly and produces nothing.

**[Check](plot_check.md)**
Cue: no cargo lands, but the audit is of the form. The Inspector scores fidelity to the model and passes it, and the Doubter asks where the planes are and is set aside as off-book. Closes when the form passes its audit while the function fails.

**[The Workshop](plot_the_workshop.md)**
Cue: someone stops asking whether the ritual is faithful and asks what actually makes cargo come. The Rebuilder strips the ceremony back to the mechanism and rebuilds function from cause. Closes when function returns at the cost of the ritual's easy legitimacy ... or the ceremony is too comfortable to give up, and the runway stays empty.

## Stakes

Whether anyone asks what actually made the planes land before the season ends.
Every plot moves the same way: toward the form, which can be seen, copied, displayed, and rewarded, and away from the substance, which cannot. The production cannot make legitimacy depend on results, because the whole point of the form is that it pays before the results are in. It can only show that no one was foolish, that copying a proven model under uncertainty was the sensible bet and the ceremony bought real credit, and that the one thing that would have brought the cargo, the invisible cause the form left behind, was the one thing a faithful copy could never carry.
