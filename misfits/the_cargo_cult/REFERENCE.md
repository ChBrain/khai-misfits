# The Cargo Cult: Reference

The research warrant for [The Cargo Cult](play_the_cargo_cult.md). The title is Feynman's phrase;
the play runs on the organizational literature on institutional imitation, the ceremony that
substitutes for function, and the decoupling that keeps the two apart, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging cargo-cult imitation as a **systemic system**: a faithful reproduction of the visible form
of a success that does not work, without a single foolish or dishonest act, because the thing that
made the original succeed was never on the surface to copy, and copying a proven form under
uncertainty buys real legitimacy whether or not it buys results. The play models the documented
mechanics, the form copied and the substance left behind, the proven model imitated for safety, the
ceremony kept as an end in itself, the decoupling of the shown from the done, the legitimacy the form
collects on sight, and the costly return to cause that alone restores function, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of stupidity. The governing finding of the source literature
is the play's governing law: organizations adopt the outward forms of legitimate models to secure
acceptance, and those forms can be kept, displayed, and rewarded while wholly decoupled from the work
they were meant to do, so fidelity to the form and failure of the function survive side by side.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a fool or a fake, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: copying a
  proven model under uncertainty is a rational bet, the ceremony is kept sincerely, the decoupling is
  ordinary pragmatism, and the legitimacy the form earns is genuine. The misfit is that a perfect copy
  of a success can be everything the success was, except successful.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into fidelity finally paying off. Function returns
  only when someone tears the ceremony back to its mechanism and rebuilds from cause, spending the easy
  legitimacy the ritual bought and taking on the risk of a practice that must earn its keep by working,
  so the reckoning is a comfort traded for a result, not a copy that came good.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and Feynman's
  image is credited beside the sociology it illustrates.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Form](piece_the_form.md)**: carries the visible, copyable shape of a success (Feynman; DiMaggio & Powell),
  everything an outsider can observe and reproduce.
- **[The Substance](piece_the_substance.md)**: carries the invisible causal mechanism (Feynman), the reason the
  model worked, and the part a copy cannot take.
- **[The Model](piece_the_model.md)**: carries the legitimate exemplar imitated under uncertainty (DiMaggio & Powell),
  proof the form can work and no guide to why.
- **[The Ritual](piece_the_ritual.md)**: carries ceremony kept for its own sake (Meyer & Rowan), the form performed
  as if performance were the work.
- **[The Decoupling](piece_the_decoupling.md)**: carries the decoupling of structure from activity (Meyer & Rowan),
  the shown and the done running on separate tracks.
- **[The Legitimacy](piece_the_legitimacy.md)**: carries the acceptance the form secures (Meyer & Rowan; DiMaggio & Powell),
  a real return paid on resemblance before any result.
- **[The Empty Runway](piece_the_empty_runway.md)**: carries the faithful copy that produces nothing (Feynman),
  the failure that accuses no one because the ceremony passed.
- **[The Cargo Cult](piece_the_cargo_cult.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Feynman; DiMaggio & Powell), and its
  remedy in a costly return to cause (Meyer & Rowan), the ritual that had to be rebuilt to work.
