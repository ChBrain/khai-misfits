# Piece: The Decoupling

## Taxonomy

[The Cargo Cult](play_the_cargo_cult.md). The gap between the ceremony shown and the work done.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Between [the window](place_the_window.md) and [the back room](place_the_back_room.md), where display and work quietly part.
The space that opens once the ritual is on show: the ceremony kept up front for the audience, the actual work, or its absence, going on out of sight behind it, the two no longer needing to match.

## Load Bearing

Without it the empty ritual would collide with reality and someone would have to stop it.
With it the collision never comes, because the Decoupling lets the form and the work run on separate tracks: the ceremony satisfies the watchers while the real business is done some other way, or not done at all, and neither track disturbs the other. It is what makes an unworkable ritual survivable, the join loosened so the show can stay flawless while nothing behind it depends on the show.

## Apparent

Pragmatism.
It looks like sensible flexing, keeping the required ceremony for those who need to see it and getting the real work done however it actually gets done. Everyone means well: the display is maintained out of respect for the audience, and the shortcut behind it out of respect for the deadline.

## Yearbook

Worked by [the screen](persona_the_screen.md) under [keep up appearances](plan_keep_up_appearances.md), unnoticed by [the inspector](persona_the_inspector.md) who audits only the front.
It is what protects [the ritual](piece_the_ritual.md) from its own emptiness: by parting the shown from the done, the Decoupling lets [the legitimacy](piece_the_legitimacy.md) be collected on the ceremony alone, so the form is never made to answer for the cargo.
