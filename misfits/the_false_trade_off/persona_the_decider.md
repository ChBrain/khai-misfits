# Persona: The Decider

## Taxonomy

[The Call](position_the_call.md). Acts on the trade-off as a fact of the world and spends the compensations the gate had been admitting.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Decisive. Evidence-led. Acting on the finding.
Is the one who turns the analysis into a policy: the head of admissions, the hiring lead, the editor setting a rule, taking the reported trade-off and building the next selection on it.
No recklessness wished on them: acting on a clear, peer-reviewed finding is exactly what a responsible decider should do, and the finding said the one quality costs the other.

## Action

Acts on the trade-off: screens on the one quality because it seems to cost the other, dropping the second criterion as redundant or worse, retuning the gate to the law the analysis found.
Answers the evidence as any decider should, letting a measured relationship guide the rule, since a trade-off in the data is a reason to stop paying for both.
Does what turning findings into action demands: builds the policy the analysis implied, and meets an outcome that does not improve and often falls.

## Shadow

Not rash, but spending the very range the trade-off was made of, and that is the sting: the qualities were independent all along, and the gate's only trick was to admit the ones who compensated.
Because the Decider screens on the one quality believing it buys the other cheap, the new selection stops admitting the compensating units the old gate quietly let through, so the trade-off that was an artefact of the wide gate is destroyed by narrowing it, and the outcome disappoints for a relationship that was never in the world, the acted-on ghost taking real value with it.
The decision is sound on the finding and the finding was a shadow, so the responsible act on a false trade-off is the one that turns the artefact into a loss.

## Tell

Says "the data are clear that the two trade off, so we stop selecting for both and screen on the one that matters."
Is right to act on the evidence and wrong about the world, so the diligence of the decision is exactly what converts a harmless artefact into a policy that throws the compensations away.
