# Play: The False Trade-Off

## Estate

## Name

The False Trade-Off.
Why are the good-looking ones so often dull, the strong candidates so often poor writers, the brilliant so rarely diligent? Look at how the set was chosen. You only dated people above a bar on looks and charm together; the admissions office only took students above a bar on scores and essays together; the journal only ran papers strong on novelty and rigour together. In every case the gate was set on the sum of two qualities that, in the world at large, have nothing to do with each other. Now watch what the gate does to the ones it lets through. A plain-looking person cleared your bar only by being unusually charming; a low scorer got in only on a stellar essay. So among the chosen, the one quality buys its way past a shortfall in the other, and the two, independent everywhere, pull apart precisely here. The analyst who studies only the admitted finds looks and charm anti-correlated, scores and essays opposed, and reads off a trade-off: as if beauty cost wit, as if talent spent diligence. It is not there. In the whole population the two are unrelated, and the negative correlation is a shadow cast by the selection, an association manufactured by conditioning on the gate both qualities helped to pass. Nobody faked the data and nobody chose badly: taking the best on the sum is the sensible rule, and reading a correlation off your sample is what an analyst does. But the gate is a common effect of two independent causes, and conditioning on a common effect ties its causes together in the sample though nothing ties them in the world, so the trade-off is real in the data, false in the population, and the one who believes it and acts, screening on the one quality because it seems to cost the other, throws away the very compensations the gate was letting in.

## Arc

The bend is this: when a gate admits on the sum of two independent qualities, the admitted trade one quality off against the other to clear it, so a spurious association appears among the chosen that is absent in the world, and the analyst who reads the sample straight infers a trade-off that is not there, a false finding manufactured by no error of method and no bad data, only by conditioning on a common effect.
It runs on two causes that are truly unrelated.
In the population the two qualities are independent: looks tell you nothing about charm, scores nothing about essays, one is not bought with the other, and a fair look at everyone would find no association at all (Berkson). The independence is the ground truth the sample will hide.
And the gate is set on their sum.
The selection admits whatever clears a bar on the two qualities together, the best applicants, the dated, the published, chosen on the combination because selecting on total merit is the sensible thing (Berkson; Sackett). The gate is a common effect: both qualities are arrows into it.
And conditioning opens the path.
Because entry required the sum to clear the bar, a unit low on one quality is in the sample only if it was high on the other, so within the admitted the two are forced apart, an association induced between causes that share nothing but the effect they were selected on (Pearl; Hernán, Hernández-Díaz and Robins). The collider is conditioned on, and a spurious link appears.
And the analyst reads the trade-off.
Studying the sample straight, the analyst finds the two qualities negatively correlated and infers a relationship, that beauty costs wit, that talent spends diligence, a trade-off read cleanly off good data by sound method (Elwert and Winship). The finding is real in the sample and false in the world, and nothing in the analysis reveals the difference.
And the decision spends the compensation.
Believing the trade-off, the decider acts, screening on the one quality because it seems to cost the other, and the outcome disappoints, because the qualities were independent all along and the gate had been quietly admitting the ones who compensated (Sackett). The false trade-off, acted on, throws away the very range it was an artefact of.
None of it is malice or error. The gate selecting on the sum, the analyst reading the sample, the decider acting on the finding: each is doing the sound thing, and the false trade-off emerges from the sum of sound moves, no faked data and no fallacy of logic, which is exactly why it survives peer review and fools the careful.
Reason about the selection that built the sample and the trade-off dissolves: but the units the gate excluded are invisible, a correlation in hand needs no defending, and the collider is a common effect and reads exactly like a common cause one ought to control for, so the reckoning is a finding undone only by picturing the sample you cannot see, not a pattern that announces itself as an artefact, and the mirror of it, controlling for a collider as though it were a confounder, is the same move that manufactures the ghost.

## Stakes

Whether a field can tell a relationship in the world from a shadow its own selection cast, or is always fooled by the sample it kept into reading a trade-off that vanishes the moment it looks at everyone.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the false trade-off, which the selector is right to induce by choosing on the sum, the analyst right to read off the sample, and the decider right to act on as a finding, and away from the true independence, which is visible only in the population the selection removed from view. The production cannot make the analyst see the whole world, because the excluded units are gone from the data and the sample is all there is to read, so selecting on the sum is the sound rule and reading the correlation the sound analysis: the gate taking the best on the combination and the analyst trusting clean data are each only doing the sensible thing. It can only show that no one erred, that a sensible selection met by a sound reading manufactures a trade-off the world does not hold and spends real value when believed, and that the one move that would break it, reasoning about the sample the gate built rather than the sample in hand, is the hardest move of all because the missing units are invisible and a collider reads exactly like the confounder one is trained to control for, so the ghost holds not by anyone's fault but by the sound work of everyone who meets it.
