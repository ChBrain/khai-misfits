# Plot: The Policy

## Taxonomy

[The False Trade-Off](play_the_false_trade_off.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The World](place_the_world.md), the trade-off believed and a rule about to be built on it.
A decider with a clear finding, and the power to retune the gate to what the analysis found.

## Action

[Acting](process_acting.md): the gate is narrowed to screen on the one quality believed to cost the other, and the new rule meets the population.

## Stage

[The Decider](persona_the_decider.md), on [Act on the Finding](plan_act_on_the_finding.md), screens on one of [the traits](piece_the_traits.md) believing [the trade-off](piece_the_trade_off.md), and stops admitting the compensating units the wide gate let through, against [the independence](piece_the_independence.md) the world actually holds, so the outcome comes in flat or worse.
The decision is sound on the finding, and the finding was a shadow.

## Tension

Acting on a clear, peer-reviewed finding is exactly what a responsible decider should do, and doing it is what turns a harmless artefact into a loss.
There is no rashness: a measured trade-off is a reason to stop paying for both qualities, and the decider builds the rule the evidence implied. But the qualities were independent all along, so narrowing the gate spends the very range it was an artefact of, and the plot closes with a real cost paid for a relationship the world never held, the ghost made expensive at last by the diligence that believed it.
