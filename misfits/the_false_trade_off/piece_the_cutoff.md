# Piece: The Cutoff

## Taxonomy

[The False Trade-Off](play_the_false_trade_off.md). The selection threshold on the sum of the two qualities, the condition on the common effect.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Set at [the intake](place_the_intake.md), where the bar on the two qualities together decides who is kept.
Flows from the selector's rule onto every unit, the line that a surplus in one quality can carry a shortfall in the other across.

## Load Bearing

Without it the two qualities would never be tied, and the sample would be everyone, and no association could be induced at all.
With it the gate becomes a common effect and the sample is conditioned on it: because entry demands the sum clear the bar, the Cutoff is what lets one quality compensate for the other and so forces them apart among the kept. The Cutoff is load bearing because it is the act of conditioning that opens the collider (Berkson; Sackett): the threshold on the pair that turns a fair selection into a manufactured correlation, so the piece carries the move that builds the artefact, the sensible bar whose only fault is that both qualities help to pass it.

## Apparent

Just a fair standard, the sensible line below which a unit is not good enough overall.
Seen at the surface, the Cutoff looks like plain quality control, a threshold on combined merit that any selector would set, and the truth that setting it on the sum is exactly what conditions the sample on a common effect, that the fair bar is the collider being switched on, stays hidden behind how obviously right it is to take the best overall.

## Yearbook

Set by [the Selector](persona_the_selector.md) and, once believed, narrowed by [the Decider](persona_the_decider.md), the threshold the whole artefact hangs on.
It is the switch of the trap: the Cutoff is set on [the traits](piece_the_traits.md), it is what [gating](process_gating.md) applies, and it is the conditioning that makes [the collider](piece_the_collider.md) a collider, the fair line whose setting is the manufacture of the trade-off.
