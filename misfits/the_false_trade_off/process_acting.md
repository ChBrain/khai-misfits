# Process: Acting

## Taxonomy

[The False Trade-Off](play_the_false_trade_off.md). The decision built on the trade-off, narrowing the gate and spending the compensations it had admitted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Decider, with a measured trade-off and the power to build a rule on it.
At [the world](place_the_world.md), the gate is retuned to screen on the one quality believed to cost the other, and the new rule is set loose on the population.

## Direction

Toward a rule built on the artefact, and a loss.
Because the finding reads as evidence, acting points the policy at the reported trade-off: the decider screens on one quality and drops the other as redundant, and since the qualities were independent all along, narrowing the gate stops admitting the compensating units the wide gate quietly let through, so the outcome comes in flat or worse for a relationship the world never held.

## Lever

The evidence a decider is bound to use.
Because acting on a clear, peer-reviewed finding is exactly what responsibility demands, acting runs on the finding's own authority, so no rashness is needed to reach the loss, only the sound duty to let measured evidence guide a rule, and the trade-off that was harmless as a curiosity becomes costly the moment it is built into a selection.

## Echo

A narrowed gate, and the range it used to hold spent.
What leaves this process is a policy that screens on one quality and loses the compensations of the other, a real cost paid for a shadow, so the trade-off that lived only in the sample is destroyed by acting on it and takes the value of the wide gate with it, the ghost finally made expensive by the diligence that believed it.
