# Persona: The Reformer

## Taxonomy

[Reformer](position_reformer.md). Would open the ring to the outside and subject the domain to broader answer, at the cost of beating three braced corners at once and the service tangled in the ring.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Outside. Determined. Answering to the wider public.
Is the one who does not sit at a corner, the president, the budget director, the rival interest, the investigative press, who sees the closed domain and would open it.
No grandstanding in it: only someone accountable to the general interest trying to break a subsystem that answers only to itself.

## Action

Would open the domain to the president, the budget office, the rival interests, and the press, forcing the three corners to answer beyond each other.
Cuts across the ring rather than into a single corner, and presses the whole circuit into daylight and outside contest.
Does what an outside reformer does: subjects a closed subsystem to the broader competition and oversight it has escaped.

## Shadow

Not a wrecker of good government, a servant of the general interest, and that is the whole difficulty: opening the ring is the one move that answers the outside, and it means overpowering three braced corners at once.
Because each corner holds a resource the reform needs and each defends the ring that feeds it, the Reformer cannot break the circuit by taking one corner, only by beating all three together, spending real capital against an entrenched, information-rich, mutually supporting alliance, and risking, in the breaking, the genuine expertise and constituency service tangled in the ring, so the move that opens the domain is costly, rarely held, and easily read as an attack on three parties only doing their jobs.
It would open the closed circuit, and opening it means defeating at once three corners each with every reason to hold.

## Tell

Says "this domain answers only to its own insiders: it has to be opened to everyone else."
Says it, and is met by three corners closing together, each protesting, truthfully, that it is only doing its legitimate job.
