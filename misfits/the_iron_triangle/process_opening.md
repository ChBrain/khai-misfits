# Process: Opening

## Taxonomy

[The Iron Triangle](play_the_iron_triangle.md). The dragging of the domain into daylight and outside contest, the move that breaks the ring, at the cost of beating three braced corners at once.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An outsider with a mandate to the general interest and the levers to force the domain open.
At [the open floor](place_the_open_floor.md), the reformer subjecting the domain to the president, the budget office, the rivals, and the press, forcing the three corners to answer beyond each other.

## Direction

Toward the circuit broken, if all three corners can be beaten together.
Because each corner holds a resource the reform needs and each defends the ring, opening cannot break the circuit by taking one corner, only by overpowering all three at once, so it drives toward the domain answering the outside: but it means spending real capital against an entrenched, mutually supporting alliance and risking the genuine expertise and service tangled in the ring, so the process opens the domain only by defeating three braced parties together.

## Lever

The claim of the outside that the domain must answer beyond its corners.
Because a subsystem that serves only its insiders owes an answer to the public it governs, the reformer's push to open it is a legitimate call on behalf of the general interest, so opening runs on a sound democratic claim: yet the same move that opens the domain must beat three corners each doing its legitimate job, so it is right and costly at once, and easily read as an attack on the innocent.

## Echo

A domain opened to the outside, or a ring that closes again the moment the pressure lifts.
Either the three corners are beaten together and the domain is subjected to the wider public, bought against real capital and the service the ring also carried, or the reformer is spent, the corners close ranks once more, and the triangle stands, iron as before.
