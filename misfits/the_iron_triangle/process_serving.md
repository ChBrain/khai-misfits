# Process: Serving

## Taxonomy

[The Iron Triangle](play_the_iron_triangle.md). Each corner pursuing its own legitimate mandate, the re-election, the mission, the members, the rational individual work that the exchange is built from.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

Three corners, each with a proper role and a goal it cannot reach alone.
At [the hearing room](place_the_hearing_room.md), the committee seeking re-election, the agency defending its budget and mission, the interest advocating for its members, each doing the job the role defines.

## Direction

Toward each corner pursuing its mandate and needing the other two.
Because the committee needs support it cannot vote itself, the agency needs a budget it cannot appropriate itself, and the interest needs policy it cannot make itself, serving sets each corner to a goal it can reach only through the others, so the legitimate pursuit of each role points it, of itself, toward the two corners that hold what it lacks.

## Lever

The legitimacy of each role played well.
Because seeking re-election, defending a mission, and advocating for members are each the proper work of the role, every corner's pursuit of its goal is sound and lawful, so serving runs on legitimate representation, the ring's first cause assembled from no wrongdoing, only from three roles each doing exactly what it is meant to do.

## Echo

Three corners each rightly served and each dependent on the others.
Each has pursued its proper goal and found it reachable only through the other two, so what reads as three parties doing their separate legitimate jobs is, in truth, three corners already leaning on each other, the mandates that answer three constituencies pointing each toward the trade that will close the ring.
