# Pitch: Everyone Wins

## Taxonomy

[The Iron Triangle](play_the_iron_triangle.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Responsive, representative, expert.
The plain good of three corners each served: a committee delivering for its district, an agency defending a programme it believes in, a lobby speaking for the members who pay it, each getting what it legitimately wants.
Nothing false in it: every corner is playing its proper role, and each is right to want what it wants.

## Undertow

Under the mutual benefit, a closed ring in which each corner supplies the other two, governing its policy domain in their shared interest and shutting the outside out.
Because each holds a resource the others need and rationally trades it, the three exchanges lock into a circuit that runs the domain from the inside, so the win-win-win is paid for by the president, the rival, and the public who fund it and hold no corner.
The responsive representation that serves three constituencies becomes a subsystem that has captured its own slice of government.

## Nerve

The feeling of three parties each getting what they need and calling it good government,
because every corner is only doing its legitimate job and everyone at the table is served,
while the general interest, seated at no corner, pays the bill it never voted and cannot get into the room.

## Echo

After the run: a policy domain run by its insiders, a ring that outlasts elections because no single corner built it and all three hold it,
no one corrupt and no one conspiring, only a legislator seeking re-election, an administrator defending a mission, and a lobbyist advocating for members,
and the one move that would open it, subjecting the domain to the outside, forgone because it means beating three braced corners at once and risking the real service tangled in the ring.
