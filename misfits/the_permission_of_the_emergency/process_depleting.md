# Process: Depleting

## Taxonomy

[The Permission of the Emergency](play_the_permission_of_the_emergency.md). The years' worth of hours found for disasters and none kept for the self, summed at [the Ledger](place_the_ledger.md) as [The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md) closes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The licensed hours found and the unlicensed hours refused, the same trade struck again the next day.
At [the Ledger](place_the_ledger.md), a column of crises answered in full and a column, beside it, of ordinary rest that never once cleared the Warden.

## Direction

Toward a self drawn down across the years.
Because each day's refusal is small and each day's licensed stop is real, depleting moves without any single alarming step: it is the sum of a hundred reasonable withholdings, day added to day until the total, read only at the Ledger, is a life spent on other people's disasters and almost never on its own repair.

## Lever

The ordinary refill never taken meeting the crises that are the only stillness ever reached.
Because the soft, undemanding hour that restores depleted attention is exactly the unlicensed hour the Warden refuses, and the only stop this person reliably wins is the one a disaster forces, depleting runs on a refill that arrives solely through the wrong door: the rest that would reload attention is refused for want of a licence, and the rest that is licensed reloads nothing, being spent already on the damage that earned it.

## Echo

Attention never reloaded, a self run down not for want of time but for want of leave.
Every crisis was met and every bath was skipped, and neither choice looks, on its own day, like the cause of anything, so what is left at the end is not a story of neglect but a Ledger no single day wrote: hours enough were always found, they were only ever found for the wrong thing.
