# Place: The Ledger

## Taxonomy

[The Permission of the Emergency](play_the_permission_of_the_emergency.md). Where the years are summed and the shape of the trade finally shows: the site of [The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md), the accounting no single day was ever asked to keep.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Column after column of hours found, each one dated to a burst pipe or a fall or a fire, and beside them, page after page, nothing: no bath entered, no book finished, no afternoon spent on the self and kept.
Read across a week the tally looks like coping, a life meeting what came at it; read across the years it reads like something else entirely.

## Holds

The tally, and the record of the years it sums.
Here every hour the Held ever spent is entered once, so the Ledger holds what no single day holds on its own: the full count of leave granted to disasters and the full count, alongside it, of leave never asked for and never given, the two columns kept faithfully against each other.

## Offers

The sight of what the time was actually spent on.
Because the Ledger sums rather than samples, it offers what the memory of any one week cannot: the pattern underneath the coping, hours found instantly for everything that broke and for nothing that merely mattered, a self's whole allotment of stillness spent almost entirely on other people's crises.

## Withheld

Any single day proving the loss.
No day in the Ledger shows the depletion, because each day, taken alone, was a reasonable response to what that day required: the loss is withheld from the diligent person until the pages are read together, and only in the sum, never in the instance, does the self drawn down finally come into view.
