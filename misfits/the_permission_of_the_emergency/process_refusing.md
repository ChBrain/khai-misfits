# Process: Refusing

## Taxonomy

[The Permission of the Emergency](play_the_permission_of_the_emergency.md). The ordinary hour turned away at the door, the bath declined not for want of time but for want of a reason, run by [the Warden](persona_the_warden.md) on [Refuse the Leave](plan_refuse_the_leave.md) each time it is offered.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An ordinary hour of rest standing open, and nothing behind it that needs defending.
At [the Bathroom](place_the_bathroom.md), the tub run and the book at hand and no claim on the Held that the hour would be answering.

## Direction

Toward the unlicensed hour set aside, once more.
Because rest that is its own end supplies no justification and the Warden grants leave only against one, refusing turns the bath away every time it is proposed, so the hour is not spent, it is postponed to a moment that never itself arrives, the same refusal made afresh each day it is offered.

## Lever

The aversion to unjustified stillness meeting the plain wish to rest.
Because dreading a stop that carries no reason is a real and reasonable disposition, and the bath carries none, refusing runs on a sound instinct applied correctly every time: it is never a want of desire for rest, it is a correct reading that this rest, here, has nothing to show for itself, so the wish loses honestly, and loses again tomorrow.

## Echo

The bath unrun, and the self telling itself there was no time.
The hour was there and the water would have been warm, but what is left afterward is not the memory of a refusal, it is the account that there was simply nothing free, so the Held repeats a story that was never quite true, and the story, repeated, is what lets the refusal happen again without ever being named.
