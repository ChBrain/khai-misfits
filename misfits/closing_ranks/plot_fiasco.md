# Plot: Fiasco

## Taxonomy

[Closing Ranks](play_closing_ranks.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The fiasco](place_the_fiasco.md), the decision met by the world, and the world unimpressed.
The plan the room agreed on so warmly has failed in the open, in roughly the way the unspoken worry said it might, and the team that made it is still, for now, entirely intact.

## Action

[Reckoning](process_reckoning.md): [The Odd One](persona_the_odd_one.md), whose doubt was swallowed and then frozen out, watches it come true, while [The Loyalist](persona_the_loyalist.md) at [the insider's seat](position_insider.md) and the rest of the united room ask how a team this good got it this wrong.

## Stage

[The Loyalist](persona_the_loyalist.md) looks for the mistake and cannot find a culprit, because [the groupthink](piece_the_groupthink.md) leaves none: every seat was loyal, the leader was decisive, the mood was high, and the plan still failed.
[The Odd One](persona_the_odd_one.md) knows where [the swallowed doubt](piece_the_swallowed_doubt.md) went, and knows too that raising it would have cost more on the day than it saved, so even now the lesson is hard to bank.

## Tension

The team is still close, and its judgement was still wrong.
There is no scandal to point at, no negligence, no bad actor: the decision failed not because the group was careless but because it was cohesive, and asked, honestly, how it erred, the only true answer is that it agreed too well. That answer is nearly useless to a warm room, because the fix, to have fought with itself at the moment it was happiest, is the one thing the team's best quality would never have let it do ... or no one draws the lesson, the failure is filed as bad luck, and the next huddle is just as warm.
