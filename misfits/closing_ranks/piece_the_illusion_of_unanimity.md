# Piece: The Illusion of Unanimity

## Taxonomy

[Closing Ranks](play_closing_ranks.md). Silence read as agreement, so the doubts never meet.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the whiteboard](place_the_whiteboard.md), in the nods around a single plan with no counter-plan beside it.
The look of a room that all agrees: no objection voiced, no hand raised against, every face composed and confident, and one option on the board because no one drew another.

## Load Bearing

Without it each member knows others share the worry and the doubts can find each other.
With it each member believes they are alone in doubting, because the Illusion of Unanimity feeds on itself: every swallowed worry presents as one more contented face, and every contented face makes the next member surer that the worry is theirs alone and best kept in. The silence is not agreement, it is a room full of people each mistaking the others' silence for the agreement they are too unsure to break, and the mistake compounds until the unspoken majority is invisible even to itself.

## Apparent

Consensus.
It looks like the real thing, a team that has genuinely converged, because from any seat the evidence is overwhelming: everyone else seems fine with it. That is exactly what makes it an illusion rather than a lie, since no one is faking agreement, they are each reading a false unanimity off everyone else's identical restraint.

## Yearbook

Built, seat by seat, out of every [swallowed doubt](piece_the_swallowed_doubt.md), and never questioned by [the cheerleader](persona_the_cheerleader.md) who takes it for a win.
It is the play's central deception: the Illusion of Unanimity is what lets [the cohesion](piece_the_cohesion.md) masquerade as a verdict, so a room that merely agreed to stay warm looks like a room that agreed because it was right.
