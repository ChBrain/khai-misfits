# Plot: Devil's Advocate

## Taxonomy

[Closing Ranks](play_closing_ranks.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The open door](place_the_open_door.md), held open this time on purpose, and a doubter seated in the room by design.
Someone has decided the plan should be tested before the world tests it, and has built the test into the meeting rather than hoping a brave soul volunteers.

## Action

[Dissent](process_dissent.md): [The Advocate](persona_the_advocate.md), holding [the devil's advocate's seat](position_devils_advocate.md), argues the other side by assignment, while [The Steer](persona_the_steer.md) keeps a preference back so the room forms a view before it hears the chief's.

## Stage

[The Advocate](persona_the_advocate.md) tables [Argue the Other Side](plan_argue_the_other_side.md): make the strongest case against the favoured plan because it is the job, not because it is disloyal, so the objection lands as a role rather than a betrayal and [the groupthink](piece_the_groupthink.md) has something in the room it cannot freeze out.
The doubt is now safe to raise, being someone's assigned task, and the plan is made to survive it before it is allowed to ship.

## Tension

The decision comes out stronger, and the room comes out colder.
Building dissent in works: a doubt no one would pay to raise is raised for free by a person paid to raise it, the outside voice is invited rather than intercepted, and the leader's held tongue lets the team think for itself. None of it is free either, because it spends the very thing that made the team a joy: the meeting is slower, the warmth is thinner, and arguing on purpose with people you like is a discipline that has to be renewed against the constant pull back toward easy agreement ... or the advocate is thanked for a good performance, ignored on the substance, and quietly not asked to the next one.
