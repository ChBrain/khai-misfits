# Closing Ranks: Reference

The research warrant for [Closing Ranks](play_closing_ranks.md). The title names the mechanism;
the play runs on the social psychology of groupthink, from Asch's conformity work through Janis's
account of decision fiascos to the remedies of structured dissent, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging groupthink as a **systemic system**: a cohesive group's drive for agreement overriding its
appraisal of the facts, so a close, capable team gets a decision badly wrong without a single coward
or fool, because the very bond that makes it strong makes dissent a cost paid by the doubter alone.
The play models the documented mechanics, cohesion pricing disagreement, the leader's early steer,
self-censorship of private doubt, the illusion of unanimity built from that silence, collective
rationalisation and a sense of invulnerability, self-appointed mindguards keeping dissent and outside
views away, and the structured dissent that alone corrects it, as the load-bearing vectors of the
structure, not as a tale of weakness. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's
governing law: when a group prizes its own cohesion, the drive to concur suppresses the realistic
appraisal of alternatives, so agreement is reached not because the group is right but because
agreeing is what keeps the group.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a coward or a fool, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the
  cohesion is a real virtue, the leader's steer is ordinary decisiveness, the swallowed doubt is
  courtesy plus honest arithmetic, and the mindguard acts out of loyalty. The misfit is that the very
  qualities of a great team are what make it unable to hear the one voice that would save it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the group simply coming to its senses.
  Structured dissent can test the plan, but only by building in the friction the cohesion was built to
  avoid, spending the warmth and speed the team enjoyed, and only while someone keeps renewing it
  against the constant pull back to easy agreement, so the reckoning is a comfort traded for a check,
  not a team that grew wise on its own.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and the empirical
  caveats around the model are respected, not smoothed over.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Cohesion](piece_the_cohesion.md)**: carries the cohesive group (Janis), the bond that makes the team
  strong and turns dissent into a betrayal.
- **[The Steer](piece_the_steer.md)**: carries directive leadership (Janis), the leader's early preference that
  the group forms around before weighing alternatives.
- **[The Illusion of Unanimity](piece_the_illusion_of_unanimity.md)**: carries the illusion of unanimity (Janis; Asch),
  silence read as agreement so the private doubts never meet.
- **[The Swallowed Doubt](piece_the_swallowed_doubt.md)**: carries self-censorship (Janis; Asch), the worry held back
  to keep the peace, by exactly the members whose doubts were worth hearing.
- **[The Mindguard](piece_the_mindguard.md)**: carries the self-appointed mindguard (Janis), keeping dissent and
  outside information away from the group out of loyalty.
- **[The Invulnerability](piece_the_invulnerability.md)**: carries collective rationalisation and the illusion of
  invulnerability (Janis), warnings explained away on shared certainty.
- **[The Frozen-Out Dissent](piece_the_frozen_out_dissent.md)**: carries direct pressure on dissenters (Janis), the
  soft social penalty that repositions the doubter and teaches the watchers to stay quiet.
- **[The Groupthink](piece_the_groupthink.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Whyte; Janis; 't Hart), and its remedy
  in structured dissent at a cost (practitioner), the cohesion made to tolerate the voice it least wants.
