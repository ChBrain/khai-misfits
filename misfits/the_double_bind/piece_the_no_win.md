# Piece: The No-Win

## Taxonomy

[The Double Bind](play_the_double_bind.md). The trap sprung, every response breaking one injunction, damned for coming and damned for holding back, no answer available that is not wrong.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Springs in [the room](place_the_room.md), where the bound one answers and the answer is wrong.
Could be escaped only at [the threshold](place_the_threshold.md), by naming the bind rather than answering it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the message could be met one way or another, and the bind would have a solution somewhere.
With it the trap is complete: every response the bound one can give breaks one demand or the other, come close and meet the flinch, hold back and refuse the call, so there is no answer that is not wrong. The No-Win is load bearing because it is the trap sprung (Bateson and colleagues): the state in which no available response satisfies both injunctions, so the piece carries the impossibility itself, the reason the failure is the message's and not the answerer's, the checkmate with no move that is not into a worse square.

## Apparent

Incompetence, the plain look of someone who keeps getting a simple thing wrong.
Seen at the surface, the No-Win looks like the bound one's failure, a person who cannot manage what others manage easily, and the truth that no answer was available, that the wrongness is built into the message and not the responder, that the checkmate was set before the first move, stays hidden behind how much a run of wrong answers looks like the fault of the one who gave them.

## Yearbook

Sprung on [the bound](persona_the_bound.md) through [straining](process_straining.md), the state where no answer is right.
It is the trap sprung: the No-Win is what [the double message](piece_the_double_message.md) does to whoever answers it, held shut by [the bond](piece_the_bond.md) and [the gag](piece_the_gag.md), the impossibility that hardens into [the pattern](piece_the_pattern.md), the checkmate at the centre of the [double bind](piece_the_double_bind.md).
