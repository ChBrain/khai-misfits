# Piece: The Bond

## Taxonomy

[The Double Bind](play_the_double_bind.md). The intense relationship of survival value that cannot be left, the field that holds the bound one in reach of the message and shuts the door out.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Holds through [the room](place_the_room.md), the field of survival value the message passes in.
Its door could be opened only at [the threshold](place_the_threshold.md), where leaving becomes possible at a cost.

## Load Bearing

Without it the bound one could simply walk away, and the impossible message would be a thing to leave, not a trap.
With it the field is sealed: a bond one depends on for survival, a parent, a partner, a world, that cannot be left without a loss as real as the bind, so the bound one is held where the message keeps arriving. The Bond is load bearing because it is the field the trap requires (Bateson and colleagues): the relationship of survival value and the injunction against escaping it, so the piece carries the inescapability that turns an impossible message into a trap, the reason the bound one must stay and answer rather than leave.

## Apparent

Love, the plain preciousness of a relationship that matters more than anything.
Seen at the surface, the Bond looks like the best thing there is, a closeness worth everything, and the truth that its very preciousness is what shuts the door, that a bond of survival value is one that cannot be left, that the love is exactly what holds the bound one in reach of the message, stays hidden behind how good and how necessary the relationship plainly is.

## Yearbook

Held in [the room](place_the_room.md) and kept shut by [sealing](process_sealing.md), the field of survival value.
It is the field of the trap: the Bond holds the Bound in reach of [the double message](piece_the_double_message.md) and shuts the door [the no-win](piece_the_no_win.md) would send them through, the preciousness that seals the field and the cost [naming](process_naming.md) must pay to open it, the ground the whole [double bind](piece_the_double_bind.md) stands on.
