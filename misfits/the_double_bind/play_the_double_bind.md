# Play: The Double Bind

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Double Bind.
Someone you cannot leave, and would not want to, says: come here, be close to me. And as you come, they stiffen, and their voice cools, and the closeness they asked for is met with a flinch, so the same breath that called you in pushes you back. And you cannot do both, you cannot be close and keep your distance at once, and whichever you choose is wrong: come near and you meet the flinch, hold back and you have refused the call. And you would say something, you would ask what is happening here, why the words invite what the body forbids, but there is a rule against that too, unspoken and iron: to name it is to accuse, to say "you are sending me two things at once" is to be told you are imagining it, or being difficult, or that you do not love them after all. So you cannot obey the message, and you cannot question the message, and you cannot walk away from the person sending it, and every road out of the room is a door that has been nailed shut. And here is the cruelty that has no one cruel in it: the one who binds you is very often not scheming at all, is themselves anxious, or frightened of the closeness they crave, or caught in the very same pattern from their own history, so they do not know they are asking the impossible, they only feel the need and its terror at once and send both down the same wire. And you, receiving both, learn after long enough to stop expecting messages to mean one thing, to read every warmth for the rejection folded inside it, until the bind is not something done to you any more but the shape the whole world takes, a way of hearing in which no answer was ever going to be the right one, and the room you could never leave is now the only room you know how to be in.

## Arc

The bend is this: a message can carry two demands that contradict at different levels, and if the one who receives it cannot obey both, cannot name the contradiction, and cannot leave, then every response breaks a rule and none can be right, a trap sprung by no cruelty but by a demand that means two opposite things at once.
It runs on two injunctions at different logical levels.
A message has a content and a frame: what it says, and what it says about how to take what it says. When the content commands one thing and the frame commands its opposite, come close and keep away, be spontaneous on demand, want what I tell you to want, the two cannot both be met, because obeying the content violates the frame and obeying the frame violates the content (Bateson, Jackson, Haley, and Weakland). The contradiction is not loud: it is split across levels, so it binds without ever showing itself as a plain "do X and do not X."
And the field cannot be left. The relationship is one of survival value, a parent, a partner, a world one depends on, and an injunction, spoken or not, forbids escaping it: you cannot simply walk away from the one who binds you, because leaving carries a punishment as real as staying (Bateson and colleagues). So the bound one is held in the field where the impossible message keeps arriving.
And the contradiction cannot be named. A further rule forbids metacommunication, forbids saying "these two demands conflict," because to name the bind is to accuse the binder, and the accusation is met with denial or punishment, so the one move that would dissolve the trap, pointing at it, is the one move the trap forbids (Watzlawick, Beavin, and Jackson). Held in the field, unable to obey both and unable to name the split, the bound one can only answer, and every answer is wrong.
And the bind teaches itself. Under the message often enough, the bound one stops expecting coherence, learns to hear the rejection inside every warmth, and comes to perceive the world in double-bind terms, so the pattern no longer needs the full apparatus to keep running: it has become a way of hearing, self-sustaining, the trap grown into the person.
None of it need be malice. The binder is very often anxious, or frightened of what they ask for, or caught in the same pattern from their own past, and does not know they send two things at once: no one designs the bind, and it emerges from a need and its terror travelling down one wire, a message that cannot be obeyed meeting a field that cannot be left and a mouth that cannot name it.
Name the bind from outside the rule that forbids naming it, or make the field one that can, after all, be left, and the trap can be dissolved: but naming it means breaking the very injunction the bind is sealed with and risking the accusation and the punishment that guard it, and leaving means paying the real cost the field was held together with, the loss of a bond of survival value, against every pull to keep the peace and stay, so the escape is bought by saying the forbidden thing to the one who forbade it, or by walking out of the only room one knows.

## Triggers

**[The Message](plot_the_message.md)**
A message arrives carrying two demands that clash at different levels. The Binder, out of need and not malice, sends come-close and keep-away down one wire, and the Bound receives both at once. Closes when the double message is live and its two demands are both in force, neither able to be met without breaking the other.

**[The Strain](plot_the_strain.md)**
Cue: both demands cannot be obeyed. The Bound tries to answer, comes near and meets the flinch, holds back and refuses the call, every response breaking one injunction or the other. Closes when the Bound has answered and the answer is wrong, damned for coming and damned for holding back.

**[The Seal](plot_the_seal.md)**
Cue: the one way out is forbidden. The Bound would name the contradiction, but the Gag forbids it and the Bond forbids leaving, and the Bystander, under the same rule, says nothing. Closes when the contradiction stays unnamed and the field unleft, the trap sealed with the bound one still inside it.

**[The Pattern](plot_the_pattern.md)**
Cue: the message comes again, and again. Under the bind often enough, the Bound stops expecting coherence and learns to hear the rejection inside every warmth, until the pattern is a way of hearing and needs no further binding to run. Closes when the trap has grown into the person, self-sustaining, the room the only one they know.

**[The Naming](plot_the_naming.md)**
Cue: someone would say the forbidden thing. The Namer would name the bind from outside the rule that seals it, or open a door in the field, against the Gag that forbids the naming and the Bond that forbids the leaving. Closes when the bind is named and dissolved ... or the naming is met with denial once more, the peace kept once more, and the bound one answers the next impossible message.

## Stakes

Whether a bond that cannot be left can send a message that cannot be obeyed, and be survived.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the answered message, which the Bound is compelled to try and cannot get right, and away from the named bind, which would dissolve the trap and which the field forbids the one move of. The production cannot make the answer come out right, because the two demands contradict across levels and meeting either breaks the other, so the no-win is the structure of the message and not any failure of the one who receives it: no answer was available. It can only show that no one need be cruel, that the binder is often frightened or caught in the same pattern and sends both demands without knowing it, and that the one move that frees the bound one, naming the contradiction or leaving the field, must be made by breaking the very injunction the bind is sealed with and paying the loss of a bond of survival value, against every pull to keep the peace and stay, so the demand is impossible and the field is inescapable and the freeing word is the one the trap forbids.
