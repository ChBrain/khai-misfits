# The Empty Seat: Reference

The research warrant for [The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md).
The dynamic is automation bias: the overtrust of Parasuraman and Riley, the commission-and-omission errors of
Skitka, Mosier, and Burdick, and the complacency review of Parasuraman and Manzey, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging automation bias as an **oversight-hollowing structure**: a human kept in the loop to catch the rare
failure of an automated aid, working with an aid reliable enough that deferring to it is the calibrated response,
so the trust the reliability earns erodes the active monitoring and independent judgement the human was posted to
supply, and at the rare moment the aid is wrong the operator follows it against their own signal or misses what it
did not flag, and the safeguard is present in the seat and absent in the judgement. The play models the documented
mechanics, the genuine high reliability that makes deference rational, the growth of trust into complacency as a
system always right teaches it is always right, the error of commission in which the operator follows a wrong
output over their own contradicting evidence, the error of omission in which the un-monitoring operator misses an
unflagged failure, the throughput incentive that prices vigilance as a drag and deference as productivity, and
the forced independent judgement and cross-check that alone keep the backstop live, at a cost, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a lazy or sleeping operator. The governing finding of the source is the
play's governing law: reliance on a reliable automated aid produces overtrust and complacency, so operators
accept its outputs rather than evaluate them and fail to catch its errors, the safeguard of a human in the loop
undermined by the very reliability that made the safeguard seem sufficient.

It is distinct from the house's The Irony of Automation, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact:
there the engine is skill atrophy, automating the routine deskills and disengages the operator so that when the
rare hard case comes the hand is too rusty to handle it, a loss of capability; here the engine is overtrust, the
operator keeps the capability but does not apply it, deferring to and following the aid and ceasing to check it, a
failure of vigilance and judgement rather than of skill. The Irony's operator cannot do it any more; the Empty
Seat's operator can, but trusts the machine over their own read. And it is distinct from the house's Alarm
Fatigue, where a flood of false-positive alarms desensitises the responder so they ignore the one true alarm,
an undertrust bred by noise: here the miscalibration runs the other way, an overtrust bred by reliability, the
operator following the aid too much rather than heeding it too little.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the operator as
  lazy or asleep or the aid as treacherous, because the source finding is precisely that the reliance is rational:
  the aid is genuinely right far more often than the operator, overriding it is usually the error, hand-checking
  it is the waste it was installed to spare, and the vigilance the trust erodes is almost always spent for
  nothing. The empty seat is authored by no one and emerges from a reliability that makes deference correct, so
  the misfit is that a well-calibrated operator of a reliable aid becomes a poor guard against it, the safeguard
  hollowed by the very dependability it was posted to watch.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that keeps the
  backstop live is to force independent judgement before the output is accepted, cross-check the aid rather than
  confirm it, and design the work so vigilance is exercised and rewarded, and it means spending attention that is
  almost always wasted, distrusting a system that is almost always right, slowing the throughput the aid was
  installed to speed, and holding a scrutiny the reliability constantly lulls. The reckoning is a guard kept only
  by paying, continuously, for a vigilance whose worth shows once in a hundred times and never on the day it is
  spent, not a free correction, so the cure for a hollowed backstop is the wasted-seeming check that alone can
  fill the seat.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the overtrust-and-misuse frame is Parasuraman and Riley's, the
  commission and omission errors are Skitka, Mosier, and Burdick's, the complacency-and-reliability integration is
  Parasuraman and Manzey's, and the finding that the bias resists both expertise and warning, so that design and
  not exhortation keeps the human in the loop, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.
