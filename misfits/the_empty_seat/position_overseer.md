# Position: Overseer

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The seat that deployed the aid to speed the work and measures the operator on throughput, pricing vigilance as a drag and deference as productivity.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The aid deployed and the metrics of the work: the standing to install the system for speed and to measure the operator on how much they get through.
A clear case: the aid was bought to ease the workload and raise the output, and that is the value it should be held to.
No number on the seat that catches the once-in-a-hundred failure, and no line on the scorecard for the vigilance that is almost always wasted.

## Orders

Count the throughput.
Measure the operator on volume and speed, reward the one who keeps the work moving, and treat the one who slows to hand-check a reliable aid as a drag.
Hold the work to the productivity the aid was bought to give.

## Loses

Any measure of the guard the seat was for, exactly by measuring the value the aid was bought for.
Because the aid is right almost every time, the checking the seat penalises really is almost always wasted, so measuring throughput is a fair reading of ordinary value, and yet it teaches the operator that deference is productivity and scrutiny is drag, pricing out the vigilance that alone catches the rare failure, the incentive locking in the complacency the reliability already breeds.
It gains the speed and volume it bought the aid for, and loses the backstop it cannot see on any scorecard.

## Drives

Toward the throughput counted and the deference rewarded,
bought against the vigilance it prices out,
so the seat that would speed the work rewards the emptying of the seat that guards it.
