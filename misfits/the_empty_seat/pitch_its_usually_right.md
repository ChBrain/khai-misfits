# Pitch: It's Usually Right

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Calm, efficient, sensible.
The plain good sense of trusting an aid that is right almost every time, letting the reliable machine carry the work and easing the load it was installed to ease.
Nothing false in it: the aid really is right far more often than the operator would be, so deferring to it is calibration, not laziness.

## Undertow

Under the calm, the reliability that makes trust correct eroding the vigilance the human was kept in the loop to supply.
Because watching a thing that is always right teaches that it is always right, active judgement goes slack, so at the rare moment the aid is wrong the operator follows it past their own signal or misses what it did not flag, and the backstop is empty exactly when it is needed.
The trust that makes a good operator of the aid makes a poor guard against it.

## Nerve

The feeling of waving the output through because the output is almost always right,
sure it is only sense, because overriding a reliable system is usually the mistake,
while the one time it is wrong the earned trust follows it, and the seat kept for that time holds a person and not a judgement.

## Echo

After the run: a safeguard present in the seat and absent in the judgement, the rare failure passed uncaught,
no one asleep and no one negligent, only a trust that was earned and a reliance that was right and a watch that was almost always wasted,
and the one move that would keep the backstop live, forcing independent judgement and cross-checking the aid, forgone because it spends attention that shows its worth once in a hundred times and never on the day it is spent.
