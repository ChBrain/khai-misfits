# Position: Skeptic

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The seat that would keep the human truly in the loop, forcing independent judgement and cross-checking the aid, at the cost of attention almost always wasted and throughput slowed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A knowledge of how the seat empties and a way to hold it open: the standing to design the work so a judgement must be formed before the output is accepted.
A clear case: a backstop that only confirms the aid is no backstop, so the human must evaluate and not merely approve.
No way to make the vigilance pay on an ordinary day, and no cover from the charge of distrusting a system that is almost always right.

## Orders

Check it yourself.
Force independent judgement before the aid's output is taken, cross-check the aid against other evidence rather than confirming it, and build the work so the operator must read the case before seeing the machine's answer.
Keep the seat holding a judgement, not just a body.

## Loses

The ease and the speed of letting the good machine work, exactly by keeping the guard the machine needs.
Because the aid is right almost every time, the independent judgement the seat forces is, in the great majority of cases, effort thrown away, so it slows the throughput and distrusts a system that has earned trust, and the move that keeps the backstop live looks, on every ordinary day, like paranoia and waste, paying off once in a hundred times and never on the day it is spent.
It buys a live backstop against the vigilance it must keep spending, and is taken, most days, for the one who slows a system that is almost always right.

## Drives

Toward the judgement kept live and the rare failure caught,
bought against attention almost always wasted and the throughput slowed,
so the one seat that would keep the backstop must pay, every day, for a guard whose worth shows once in a hundred.
