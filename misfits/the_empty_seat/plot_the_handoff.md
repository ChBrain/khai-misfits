# Plot: The Handoff

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Console](place_the_console.md), a human set over a reliable automated aid.
The Aid doing the work well, right almost every time, the operator learning that overriding it is usually the error and hand-checking it a waste.

## Action

[Deferring](process_deferring.md): [The Operator](persona_the_operator.md), holding [the operator's seat](position_operator.md), learns to trust the aid and begins to defer, while [The Aid](persona_the_aid.md) delivers its confident output.

## Stage

[The Aid](persona_the_aid.md), on [Give the Answer](plan_give_the_answer.md), does the work and delivers its recommendation plainly, right time after time.
[The Operator](persona_the_operator.md), on [Follow the Aid](plan_follow_the_aid.md), finds that overriding is usually wrong and re-checking is wasted, and learns, correctly, to defer.

## Tension

Trusting a reliable aid is calibration, not laziness, and the calibration is what will hollow the seat.
There is nothing to fault: the aid really is right far more often than any override, so deferring is the sound response to its record. But the trust that is correct will teach the operator to accept rather than evaluate, so the plot closes with the deferring begun and earned, a calibration to a real base rate that has not yet become complacency but has started down toward it.
