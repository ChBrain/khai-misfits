# Process: Rewarding

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The measuring of the work on throughput, the incentive that prices deference as productivity and vigilance as a drag, locking in the complacency.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

An aid deployed for speed and a metric of how much is got through.
At [the scorecard](place_the_scorecard.md), the overseer counting the operator's volume, the fast deferring hand rising and the slow checking one marked as a bottleneck.

## Direction

Toward deference paid and vigilance charged.
Because the metric counts speed and volume and the aid is right almost every time, rewarding pays the operator who defers and keeps the queue moving and penalises the one who slows to check, so it prices the vigilance out as wasted time, and the incentive teaches what the reliability already suggested, that trusting the aid is productivity and scrutinising it is drag.

## Lever

The fairness of measuring the value the aid was bought for.
Because the aid was installed for throughput and the checking really is almost always wasted, measuring the operator on speed is a fair reading of ordinary value, so rewarding runs on sound management, the emptied seat assembled from no recklessness, only from an incentive that counts what shows and cannot count the failure a spent vigilance would have caught.

## Echo

A board that rewards the emptying of the seat it depends on.
The throughput has been counted and the deference paid, so what reads as fair reward for productivity is, in truth, a price put on the guard the seat was for, the metric prizing the trust that empties the seat and charging the vigilance that would keep it full.
