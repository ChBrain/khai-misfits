# Persona: The Overseer

## Taxonomy

[Overseer](position_overseer.md). Deployed the aid to speed the work and measures the operator on throughput, so vigilance reads as a drag and deference as productivity, pricing the backstop out.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Practical. Efficiency-minded. Watching the numbers.
Is the one who installed the aid to ease the workload and speed the output, and who measures the operator on how much they get through.
No recklessness in it: only a manager rightly wanting the productivity the reliable aid was bought to deliver.

## Action

Counts the throughput, rewards the operator who keeps the work moving, and treats the one who slows to hand-check as a drag on a system that is almost always right.
Takes the aid's reliability as licence to price vigilance as wasted time, since checking a good machine is, almost always, effort spent for nothing.
Does what an efficiency-minded manager does: it rewards speed and volume and the trust that produces them.

## Shadow

Not a cost-cutter courting disaster, a rational manager, and that is the whole difficulty: rewarding throughput on a reliable aid is sensible, and it prices out the vigilance the seat was for.
Because the aid is right almost every time, the checking the Overseer penalises really is almost always wasted, so measuring the operator on speed is a fair reading of ordinary value, and yet it teaches the operator that deference is productivity and scrutiny is drag, locking in the very complacency that empties the seat, the incentive confirming what the reliability already suggested.
It rewards the productivity the aid was for, and rewarding it prices out the guard against the aid.

## Tell

Says "we bought the system to go faster, not to double-check it by hand."
Bought it for speed, truly, and cannot see that the doubling-check it grudges is the one thing that keeps the seat from being empty.
