# Process: Coasting

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The operator's active monitoring lapsing under a reliable aid, the complacency that at the rare failure becomes the error of omission.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A system that is almost always right and a watch that need almost never act.
At [the console](place_the_console.md), the operator watching an aid that keeps being right, the vigilance finding nothing to do and quietly slackening.

## Direction

Toward the watch gone slack and the unflagged failure missed.
Because monitoring a thing that is always right teaches that it is always right, coasting lets the operator's active attention lapse and their judgement, unexercised, go slack, so they come to accept outputs rather than evaluate them, and when the aid fails silently, flagging nothing, the not-flagging reads as all-clear and the failure passes unseen, the omission the mirror of the trust.

## Lever

The truth that a reliable aid gives the watch almost nothing to do.
Because the aid handles the routine well and is right almost every time, there is genuinely little for the vigilance to catch, so attention lapses not from negligence but from a watch kept over a thing that almost never needs it, the complacency assembled from a reliability that starves the very scrutiny it was to be checked by.

## Echo

A watch that has gone quiet, and a silent failure it does not see.
The operator's attention has slackened as the aid's record taught it to, so what reads as a calm, well-run station is, in truth, a scrutiny dissolved by dependability, the failure the aid did not flag passing an operator who has learned that no flag means all-clear.
