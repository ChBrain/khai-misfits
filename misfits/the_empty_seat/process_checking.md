# Process: Checking

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The forcing of independent judgement and the cross-checking of the aid, the move that keeps the backstop live, at the cost of attention almost always wasted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A knowledge of how the seat empties and a will to hold it open.
At [the cross-check](place_the_cross_check.md), the skeptic building the work so a read is formed before the output is seen, or a second source set against the machine's answer.

## Direction

Toward the seat kept holding a judgement, if the cost can be paid.
Because a backstop that only confirms the aid is no backstop, checking forces the operator to evaluate rather than approve and weighs the aid against other evidence, so it keeps the vigilance exercised and the rare failure catchable: but the aid is right almost every time, so the independent read is almost always confirmed and the cost almost always wasted, and the process holds the seat live only by paying, daily, for a guard whose worth shows once in a hundred.

## Lever

The truth that only an exercised judgement can catch the rare wrong.
Because vigilance eased away cannot catch what it no longer watches for, keeping the human genuinely in the loop is the one move that keeps the backstop real, so checking runs on a sound reading of the trap: yet the same move spends attention that the aid's reliability makes almost always redundant, so it is right and costly at once, and looks, on every ordinary day, like paranoia.

## Echo

A backstop kept live, or a check waived as waste.
Either the independent judgement is forced and the aid cross-checked, and the rare failure is caught, bought against the daily cost of a vigilance almost always confirmed, or the check is dismissed as slowing a system that is almost always right, the good machine let work, and the seat left empty for the day it fails.
