# Persona: The Operator

## Taxonomy

[Operator](position_operator.md). The human kept in the loop to catch the aid's rare failure, who learns correctly to defer, and at the rare moment follows the aid or misses its failure.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Competent. Trusting. Working smoothly with the aid.
Is the person over the automation, the pilot, the analyst, the dispatcher, who has learned that the aid is reliable and works with it rather than against it.
No idleness in it: only a capable operator calibrated to a system that is right almost every time.

## Action

Defers to the aid's output, follows its recommendation, and lets it carry the work it does well.
Stops hand-checking a system that is almost always right, because overriding it is usually the error and re-doing it is the waste it was meant to spare.
Does what a rational operator of a reliable aid does: it trusts the thing that has earned trust.

## Shadow

Not asleep at the wheel, a well-calibrated operator, and that is the whole difficulty: the deferring is the correct response to the aid's record, and the correctness is what hollows the seat.
Because trusting a reliable aid is rational and watching a thing that is always right teaches it is always right, the Operator's judgement goes slack from disuse, so at the rare moment the aid is wrong they follow it past their own instruments, the aid having earned the benefit of every doubt, or miss what it did not flag, the not-flagging having come to mean all-clear, and the very trust that made them a good operator makes them a poor guard.
It relies on the aid because relying is right, and relying is what empties the seat when the aid is wrong.

## Tell

Says "it's almost always right: why would I override it?"
Would not, and should not, ninety-nine times, and cannot feel that the hundredth is the one the whole seat was for.
