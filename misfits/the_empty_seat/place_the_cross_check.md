# Place: The Cross-Check

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). Where the human is kept truly in the loop, the independent read and the second source against which the aid is checked rather than confirmed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place a judgement is formed apart from the aid, a procedure where the operator reads the case first, or a second instrument set against the machine's answer.
The Skeptic building the work so the output must be evaluated, not just approved, the operator's own read held up beside the aid's.

## Holds

The checking, and the live judgement it keeps.
Here the aid meets an independent read, so the Cross-Check holds the point where the backstop stays real: a judgement formed before the output is accepted and a second source the aid is weighed against, so it is where the human evaluates rather than confirms, the one arrangement in which the seat holds a judgement and not merely a body.

## Offers

A way to keep the seat live, and the second look the aid was to be checked by.
Because the Cross-Check forces an independent read and a second source, it offers the one thing that catches the aid's rare wrong output, the human judgement exercised rather than eased away, so it hands out the live backstop the seat was for, and hands it out at the price of the attention and the time it takes to form a read the aid has almost always already gotten right.

## Withheld

Any day-to-day proof that the check was worth it.
The Cross-Check can keep the vigilance live, but it cannot make its value show on an ordinary day, because the failure it prevents leaves no trace and the reads it forces are almost always confirmed, so it withholds the visible payoff, exacting the cost of the check continuously and returning it once in a hundred times, and, all live guard and no daily reward, it withholds any sign, until the rare case, that the seat it kept full was ever needed.
