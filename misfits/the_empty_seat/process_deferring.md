# Process: Deferring

## Taxonomy

[The Empty Seat](play_the_empty_seat.md). The operator accepting the aid's output over their own judgement, the calibrated trust that at the rare wrong becomes the error of commission.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A reliable aid and a human posted to accept or override it.
At [the console](place_the_console.md), the operator meeting an output that is right almost every time and learning, correctly, to take it.

## Direction

Toward the aid's output accepted, right or wrong.
Because the aid is right far more often than any override, deferring accepts its recommendation and sets aside the operator's own read, so the trust is calibrated to a real base rate, and the same deference that is correct ninety-nine times follows the aid the hundredth, past the operator's own contradicting signal, the benefit of the doubt going always to the thing usually right.

## Lever

The genuine reliability of the aid.
Because the aid really is right more often than the human, and overriding it really is usually the error, deferring is the sound response to its record, so the process runs on calibration, not complacency, the commission error assembled from no laziness, only from a trust that the aid earned every day but the day it was wrong.

## Echo

An output accepted, and one time in a hundred the wrong one.
The operator has deferred as the record warranted, so what reads as smooth, sensible reliance is, in truth, a trust that cannot tell the rare wrong output from the many right, following the aid the one time following it was the error.
