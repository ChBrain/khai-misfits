# Plan: Hold the Margin

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the engineer's seat](position_engineer.md), inside [Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [Engineer](position_engineer.md)

## Direction

Set the operating point far below the critical threshold and keep it there: derate the system, add cooling, fit a vent, monitor the drift, because distance from the point is the whole of the safety and it must be bought. Do it knowing the margin buys time and not immunity, that an exponential source will always eventually out-climb a linear sink, and that the point keeps coming down as the system ages however well the margin is held.

## Orders

([The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) derates the operating point below [the Critical Point](piece_the_critical_point.md), paying in capacity, and fights [the Drift](piece_the_drift.md) that keeps narrowing the margin.)

## Implementation

([The Diagram](place_the_diagram.md) is read for the threshold; the operating point on [the Rating](place_the_rating.md) is set far below it, at a cost in capacity; [Drifting](process_drifting.md) narrows the margin anyway, so the derating buys time against [the Drift](piece_the_drift.md) and never abolishes the asymmetry, and past the point only containment of [the Runaway](piece_the_runaway.md) is left.)

## Targets

- [ ] the operating point is set far below the critical threshold, and the distance is paid for in capacity
- [ ] cooling is added, a vent is fitted, and the drift is monitored across the system's life
- [ ] the margin is understood to buy time and never immunity, the asymmetry never abolished
- [ ] the point keeps drifting closer as the system ages, and past it only the containment of a runaway remains
