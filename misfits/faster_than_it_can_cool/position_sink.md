# Position: Sink

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The seat of the heat-loss path, shedding warmth at a rate linear in temperature, winning the race below the point and outrun the instant the curve catches its line.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A cooling law and the standing to run it: the heat it removes rising in plain proportion to how much hotter the system is than its surroundings, steady and reliable.
A faithful case: the linear loss never weakens and never stops, it carries heat away exactly as heat crosses to the surroundings, so the cooling is sound and working the whole time.
No power to bend its line, only to raise it in proportion, and so the seat cannot keep pace with a generation that climbs exponentially once the gap between them has closed.

## Orders

Shed the heat.
Carry warmth away at the rate the temperature difference sets, more when the system is hotter, because that is what a heat sink does and it does it faithfully.
Hold the balance while the loss outpaces the generation, and go on shedding at the same linear best when it no longer does, because the seat can only ever climb its line and never bend it to match the curve.

## Loses

The race, at the point, without ever having faltered.
Because the seat's loss rises only linearly while the generation rises exponentially, it outpaces the source for the whole ordinary range and is outpaced the instant the curve catches its line, so what it surrenders is not its function, which never fails, but the lead, which a straight line cannot hold against a bending curve.
It keeps cooling at full strength and loses anyway, outrun rather than broken, its faithful proportion simply left behind.

## Drives

Toward a cooling working at its best and no longer enough,
the loss climbing its steady line while the generation bends away above it,
so the seat that never once stopped shedding heat is outpaced at the point and cannot catch the runaway it was holding off the whole life before.
