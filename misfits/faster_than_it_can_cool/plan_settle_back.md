# Plan: Settle Back

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the cell's seat](position_cell.md), inside [Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [Cell](position_cell.md)

## Direction

Rest at the operating point, and when a small warming comes, let the loss grow to meet it and return to rest, because below the critical point the cooling rises faster than the heating and any warming is erased. Do nothing but sit and self-correct, since that is the whole of what a stable point can do, and go on doing it until a warming comes that the loss no longer outpaces.

## Orders

([The Cell](persona_the_cell.md) settles every small warming back to the operating point, holding [the Balance](piece_the_balance.md) while [the Sink](persona_the_sink.md) sheds heat faster than [the Reaction](persona_the_reaction.md) makes it.)

## Implementation

([The Vessel](place_the_vessel.md) holds the operating point; the balance of [Shedding](process_shedding.md) over [Generating](process_generating.md) returns each warming to rest; the self-correction holds [the Balance](piece_the_balance.md) until [Drifting](process_drifting.md) narrows the margin to [the Critical Point](piece_the_critical_point.md), past which the settling reverses.)

## Targets

- [ ] the operating point is held, and every small warming is settled back to rest
- [ ] the loss is left to outpace the generation, so the correction needs nothing but the two laws
- [ ] every instantaneous check is passed, because the stability is genuine the whole way
- [ ] the settling holds until the margin closes, then a warming comes that cannot be settled, and there is no other move
