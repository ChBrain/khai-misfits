# Plot: The Tangency

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

At [The Diagram](place_the_diagram.md), the curves are read as they finally meet: years of [Drifting](process_drifting.md) behind them, the loss line and the generation curve no longer standing apart.

## Action

[The Loss](piece_the_loss.md) stops crossing [the Generation](piece_the_generation.md) and only touches it, at one temperature, tangent. This is [the Critical Point](piece_the_critical_point.md): the stable operating point that held [the Balance](piece_the_balance.md) and its unstable neighbour above it merge at that single temperature and vanish together, a saddle-node collapsing to nothing. At the instant of contact the sign of the net feedback flips.

## Stage

Below the tangency, for the whole of the system's life, a rise in temperature made the loss grow faster than the generation, and the rise was erased. At the tangency itself there is exactly one point of contact, not a smear, because a straight line meets a bending curve at a single temperature or not at all. This is not read from a distance or inferred from a trend: it is the moment the two curves, drawn on the same axes, share one point and one slope.

## Tension

One degree below the point, a warming still settles: the old feedback, the old self-correction, is still there. One degree above the same point, the identical warming amplifies: the sign has flipped, not because anything failed but because the curve has finally out-climbed the line, and now the system sits above the temperature where anything settles it and below nothing that will catch it. Nothing has happened yet. Everything about what happens next has just been decided.
