# Faster Than It Can Cool: Reference

The research warrant for **Faster Than It Can Cool** (play and company not yet staged;
this warrant is drafted first, to prove the science and the distinction before authoring).
The dynamic is the thermal-runaway account of catastrophe: Semenov's thermal explosion theory and
Frank-Kamenetskii's distributed extension, with the lithium-ion cell as the modern electrochemical
instance, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging thermal runaway as a **sign-flipping-feedback structure**: a single system whose heat
generation rises exponentially with temperature while its heat loss rises only linearly, so that at
low temperature a small rise in temperature is met by a loss that grows faster than the generation and
erases it, the operating point genuinely stable and self-correcting, but as ordinary conditions drift,
a cooling path fouling, an ambient creeping up, a cell ageing, a normal load settling in, the generation
curve rises until it just touches the loss line at a single critical point, past which generation exceeds
loss at every temperature, the feedback that was restoring becomes amplifying, and the temperature runs
away to destruction with no equilibrium left to fall back to. The governing law is not that the system
was defective or that something struck it hard, but that **because heat generation is exponential in
temperature and heat loss only linear, an operating point that is genuinely, provably stable for the
whole of a system's life sits a fixed distance from a critical threshold that ordinary drift alone can
reach, and the instant that threshold is crossed the stabilising feedback reverses its sign and the
system runs away, needing no shock and no fault, only the crossing**. The play models the documented
mechanics: the exponential heat source and the linear heat sink whose balance sets a critical condition
at the tangency of the two curves, stable below it and explosive above (Semenov), the distributed form
with conduction included and a critical parameter beyond which no steady temperature profile can exist
at all (Frank-Kamenetskii), and the electrochemical instance in which a cell self-heats above an onset
temperature and, once ordinary ageing has lowered its margin and a normal charge tips it past the
triggering point, decomposes exothermically faster than it can shed the heat and runs away (Feng and
colleagues), as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a defective part or a
careless operator.

It is distinct from the house's The Unmarked Edge, where a system migrates gradually across an unmarked
boundary along a cost or effort gradient, each step a small worsening pulled by an incentive slope and
the harm accruing smoothly once the line is behind it: there the driver is a gradient an agent is drawn
down and the crossing is soft, here there is no gradient, no agent, and no incentive, the crossing is a
hard physical bifurcation at a fixed and in principle computable point, and past it the system does not
sit a little worse, it diverges, the stable state not degraded but gone. It is distinct from The Load It
Always Carried, where invisible damage accumulates cycle by cycle until a crack nucleates and runs, the
failure the sum of a hidden tally: there something is quietly accumulating the whole time, here nothing
accumulates and nothing wears, the system is fully sound and undamaged right up to the critical point,
and what changes at the point is not a count reaching its limit but the sign of a feedback flipping, so
it is stable a degree below and runs away a degree above with no damage done in between. And it is
distinct from Held Under, the house's metastable failure, where a transient tips the system into a
second stable state that a feedback loop then sustains, a bad equilibrium it sticks in and cannot leave:
there the system is captured by a second stable state, here past the critical point there is no
equilibrium at all, stable or otherwise, the system does not settle into a failing state but accelerates
out of every state toward destruction, a loss of stability rather than a capture by a second one.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a
  defective part or a careless operator, because the source finding it stages is precisely that each
  part obeys correct physics: the reaction generates heat by the ordinary temperature dependence of its
  rate, the housing sheds heat by ordinary cooling, the operating point is genuinely stable and passes
  every instantaneous check right up to the threshold, and the drift that carries it there is ordinary
  ageing or ordinary use, not abuse (Semenov; Feng and colleagues). No slack was cut and no shock was
  struck, so this is not The Brittle Optimum's removed buffer meeting a blow: the exact load carried
  safely a thousand times becomes fatal because the critical point drifted down onto it, not because
  anything was taken away or anything hit it. The runaway is authored by no one and emerges from the
  structure, the exponential heat source set against the linear heat sink, so the misfit is that a
  sound system, doing nothing wrong and struck by nothing, is carried by its own ordinary ageing across
  a line past which the very feedback that kept it safe turns and destroys it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The moves that hold
  it off, derating the system to sit further below the point, adding cooling, venting, a thermal fuse,
  keeping a margin, all cost real capacity, energy, mass, or performance, and none of them abolishes the
  asymmetry that makes the trap: an exponential source will always, eventually, outrun a linear sink, so
  the best a design can do is hold the operating point below a critical point that ageing keeps lowering
  toward it, and the margin that buys the safety is exactly the capability given up to buy it (Semenov;
  Frank-Kamenetskii). Past the point there is no correction at all, only the containment of a runaway
  already underway, so the reckoning is a permanent tax of margin against a threshold that never stops
  drifting closer, not a fix that ends the danger.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the thermal explosion theory of an exponential
  source against a linear sink and its critical tangency is Semenov's, the distributed form and the
  precise critical parameter are Frank-Kamenetskii's, and the characteristic temperatures and the
  ageing of the margin in a real cell are Feng and colleagues', and the condition the trap actually
  requires, a provably stable operating point a fixed distance from a threshold that ordinary drift can
  reach, past which the feedback reverses sign, is carried as carefully as the finding itself. The scope
  is held to thermal, chemical, electrochemical, and electrical systems with a genuine exponential
  self-heating law, and not stretched into a metaphor for social or many-agent runaways it does not
  govern.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element. The play and its company are not yet authored; these are the
proposed load-bearing pieces the warrant commits the staging to carry, one per vector, no filler.

- **The Generation**: carries the exponential heat source (Semenov; Feng and colleagues), the heat the
  reaction makes rising ever faster with temperature, the curve that will always eventually outrun the
  line.
- **The Loss**: carries the linear heat sink (Semenov), the heat the system sheds to its surroundings
  rising only in proportion to how much hotter it is, the straight line the generation curve climbs past.
- **The Balance**: carries the stable operating point (Semenov), where loss holds generation and a small
  rise in temperature is erased because loss grows faster than generation there, the point that passes
  every instantaneous check and looks perfectly safe because, until the threshold, it is.
- **The Drift**: carries the ordinary, unforced change (Feng and colleagues), the ageing, the fouling
  cooling path, the creeping ambient, the settled-in normal load that carries the balance toward the
  critical point over a life, with no shock and no abuse anywhere in it.
- **The Critical Point**: carries the threshold (Semenov; Frank-Kamenetskii), the tangency where the
  loss line just touches the generation curve and the stable point and its neighbour merge and vanish,
  the fixed bifurcation at which the stabilising feedback reverses its sign.
- **The Runaway**: carries the terminal divergence (Semenov; Frank-Kamenetskii; Feng and colleagues),
  generation now exceeding loss at every temperature and the feedback amplifying, the temperature
  accelerating to destruction with no equilibrium left anywhere below it to return to.
