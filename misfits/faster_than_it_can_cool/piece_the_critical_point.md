# Piece: The Critical Point

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The threshold itself: the temperature where the loss line just touches the generation curve, the stable point and its unstable neighbour merging and vanishing, and the sign of the feedback flipping from restoring to amplifying.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read on [The Diagram](place_the_diagram.md), the single temperature at which the two curves stop crossing and only touch, a hard and sharply defined threshold set by the shapes of the curves, which ordinary ageing carries down toward the operating temperature as the curves themselves shift over the system's life.

## Load Bearing

It is load bearing as the hinge of the whole play, the exact condition under which the stability proven at the balance ceases to exist: at the tangency of the loss line and the generation curve the two intersection points, one stable, one unstable, meet and annihilate, a saddle-node bifurcation, and beyond that condition no steady temperature satisfies the balance at all, only runaway (Semenov). The distributed form of the same result, accounting for heat conduction through the material rather than treating it as perfectly stirred, sharpens this into a dimensionless critical parameter: below it a steady temperature profile exists somewhere in the body, above it none does, anywhere, and the transition between the two is exact rather than gradual (Frank-Kamenetskii). What must be held precisely: the threshold is a hard, sharply defined bifurcation set by the shapes of the two curves, not a soft or fuzzy edge and not created by any shock, and it is determinate at every moment, computable from the curves as they stand. What ordinary ageing does is bring it down: as the curves shift, a resistance rising or a cooling path fouling, the tangency descends over the system's life toward the operating temperature, so the margin closes not because the operating point climbs to a fixed threshold but because the threshold itself is carried down to meet it.

## Apparent

Reads as nothing, most of a system's life: an abstract temperature on a diagram, far above where anything ever runs, easy to compute once and file away as though it would stay there. It gives no warning as it is approached because it descends silently, no alarm in the curves as they slowly shift, so there is no single reading that announces how far the threshold has already come down toward the operating temperature.
