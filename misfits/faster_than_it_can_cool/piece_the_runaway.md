# Piece: The Runaway

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The terminal divergence: past the critical point, generation exceeds loss at every temperature, the feedback amplifying instead of restoring, temperature accelerating to destruction with no equilibrium left anywhere to catch it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Occurs inside [The Vessel](place_the_vessel.md), the same enclosure that held the stable balance for the whole of the system's life, now with nothing above it to settle into.

## Load Bearing

It is load bearing as the far side of the tangency, the state that exists once the critical condition is crossed rather than a worse version of the state before it: beyond the point where the loss line last touches the generation curve, the curve sits above the line at every temperature reachable from there, so there is no intersection left to define a new, higher balance, and the system's temperature climbs without bound in the model, in reality until the material itself fails (Semenov). The distributed treatment confirms this is a genuine absence of steady states, not merely a large but finite one, above the critical parameter no stationary temperature profile solves the heat balance anywhere in the body (Frank-Kamenetskii). In the lithium-ion cell this stage is named and staged in temperature: self-heating that began at the onset accelerates through a triggering temperature into the peak temperature of the runaway itself, venting, fire, or fracture the physical form the divergence takes in that chemistry (Feng, Ouyang, and colleagues). What must be held exactly: the loss has not failed, it is still shedding heat at its full linear rate for whatever temperature it is given, it is only that the generation, still following its own correct law, is by then rising faster than any linear rate can match, at every point along the way, so this is a loss of stability entire, not a slide into a different, still-findable rest.

## Apparent

Reads, for the first seconds, as an ordinary temperature reading climbing, the kind of number that has always come back down before. It gives almost no warning of its own kind, because the ordinary corrective feedback that made every prior rise self-limiting is exactly what has just gone missing, and by the time the rate itself is visibly unusual there is no longer anywhere for it to settle.
