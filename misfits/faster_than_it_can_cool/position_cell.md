# Position: Cell

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The seat of the system that makes heat and sheds it, genuinely stable while its loss outpaces its generation and carried by ordinary drift to the point past which it cannot pull itself back.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A stable operating point and the standing to hold it: the self-correcting steadiness that erases any small warming, real and provable, passing every instantaneous check.
A clean case: the stability is genuine, not luck and not a thin margin, so running at the operating point is sound and the checks that pass are right to pass.
No power of its own to change the two laws or to stop the drift, only to sit at the point and settle the warmings, and so the seat cannot save itself once the margin that made the settling possible has been spent by time.

## Orders

Settle back.
Sit at the operating point, and when a small warming comes, let the loss grow to meet it and return to rest, because below the point that is exactly what happens and there is nothing else to do.
Then settle the next warming, and the next, until the margin has closed and a warming comes that the loss no longer outpaces, and there is no settling it.

## Loses

The power to correct its own warming, and with it everything, at a single sharp point.
Because the seat's stability was only ever the loss outpacing the generation, and ordinary drift narrows the gap until the loss no longer can, it keeps its self-correction right up to the tangency and loses it entirely a degree past, its steadying feedback reversing sign with no warning it could give.
It surrenders its stability not to a shock but to time, at a point it was carried to, and once past it there is no equilibrium anywhere above to fall back on.

## Drives

Toward a runaway from a temperature that was merely warm,
the operating point steady its whole life and then, past the threshold, climbing without bound,
so the seat that corrected every warming faithfully is destroyed by the first one it cannot, at a point ordinary drift alone delivered it to.
