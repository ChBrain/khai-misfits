# Place: The Diagram

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The analytic surface where the two curves are drawn, host to [The Derating](plot_the_derating.md) and [The Tangency](plot_the_tangency.md), where [the Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) reads the threshold before setting the margin.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The exponential generation curve and the linear loss line, drawn together on the same axes: the gap between them across the whole ordinary range, and, at one temperature, the point where the line stops crossing the curve and only touches it. The tangency is not inferred here, it is visible.

## Holds

[The Generation](piece_the_generation.md), drawn as the curve; [the Loss](piece_the_loss.md), drawn as the line; wherever they still cross, [the Balance](piece_the_balance.md) between them. At the single point where the line no longer crosses but only touches the curve, [the Critical Point](piece_the_critical_point.md), a hard threshold computable from the curves as they stand today.

## Offers

A reading, in advance, of where the threshold sits and how far the operating point is being kept from it: the Diagram is what lets [the Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) derate against a known distance instead of a guessed one, and set a margin that means something.

## Withheld

Motion. The Diagram is a still picture of where the curve and the line stand today; it does not show, and cannot show, the [Drifting](process_drifting.md) already carrying the point down toward the operating temperature tomorrow. A diagram read at any single sitting is correct, and already going stale the moment it is read.
