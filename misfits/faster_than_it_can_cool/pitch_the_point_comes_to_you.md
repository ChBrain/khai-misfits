# Pitch: The Point Comes to You

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Physical, exact, unhurried.
A system running cool and stable, its operating point self-correcting, its margin wide, two curves on a diagram that have not yet touched.
Nothing false in it: the stability is genuine and provable, the cooling works, the checks pass, and the system is sound in every part.

## Undertow

Under the calm, an asymmetry that decides the ending before it begins.
Because generation rises exponentially with temperature and loss only linearly, the safe operating point sits a fixed distance from a critical threshold that no shock is needed to reach, only ordinary ageing: a resistance creeping up, a cooling path fouling, an ambient climbing, spending by time the margin a blow never touched.
The threshold does not wait to be struck. It is a hard, computable point, no surprise and no soft edge, and the system's own slow drift brings it down across the years to meet the temperature it has run at all along.

## Nerve

The feeling of a system that is stable in every check right up to the instant it is not, where one degree below the point a warming still settles it and one degree above the same warming runs it away,
because at the tangency the sign of the feedback flips, and the very thing that steadied the system becomes the thing that destroys it,
so nothing announces the end: the system is genuinely safe the whole way to a line it crosses without a tremor.

## Echo

After the run: a runaway to venting or fire or fracture, in seconds, from a temperature that an hour before was merely warm,
no fault in the reaction that obeyed its rate law and none in the cooling that worked at its linear best and was simply outpaced,
and a margin that could be held far below the point but never past the asymmetry, a permanent tax of capacity paid against a threshold that never once stopped drifting closer.
