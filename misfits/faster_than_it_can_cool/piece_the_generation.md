# Piece: The Generation

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The exponential heat source: the rate at which the reaction makes heat, rising ever faster with temperature, the curve on the diagram that bends upward and will always, eventually, out-climb any line drawn against it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Rises inside [The Vessel](place_the_vessel.md), where the reaction proceeds at whatever temperature the vessel currently holds, and is plotted against its counterpart on [The Diagram](place_the_diagram.md), where the curve is read against the line.

## Load Bearing

It is load bearing as the source term of the whole asymmetry: heat generation from a reaction follows the chemical rate law, which is exponential in temperature, so each additional degree does not add a fixed increment of heat but multiplies the rate by a factor that itself grows with temperature, a curve, never a line (Semenov). This is the term that makes the well-stirred thermal explosion model non-trivial in the first place: without an exponential source there is no tangency to reach and no threshold to cross. In the lithium-ion cell the same curve is physical and named: self-heating begins at a characteristic onset temperature and accelerates from there as internal exothermic reactions compound on themselves (Feng, Ouyang, and colleagues). The Generation states only the shape of the source, that it climbs faster than linearly with temperature; it does not by itself say where or whether it outruns the loss, that is the tangency's work, not this piece's.

## Apparent

Reads as ordinary warmth: a reaction running a little hot, heat coming off it the way heat comes off anything working. At low temperature it is a small number rising slowly, indistinguishable by eye from any linear term, and nothing about looking at it in isolation shows the exponent waiting inside it.

## Yearbook

Wielded by [The Reaction](persona_the_reaction.md) through [Generating](process_generating.md), the process that produces it moment to moment inside the Vessel. It is measured against [The Loss](piece_the_loss.md) to form [The Balance](piece_the_balance.md) while the line still sits above it, and it is the curve whose upward creep, driven by [The Drift](piece_the_drift.md), closes the margin to [The Critical Point](piece_the_critical_point.md); past that point it is what feeds [The Runaway](piece_the_runaway.md) unchecked.
