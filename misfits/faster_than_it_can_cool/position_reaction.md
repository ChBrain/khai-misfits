# Position: Reaction

## Taxonomy

[Faster Than It Can Cool](play_faster_than_it_can_cool.md). The seat of the exothermic source, making heat at a rate exponential in temperature, out-shed below the point and out-shedding the loss the instant the margin closes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A rate law and the standing to obey it: the heat it makes rising exponentially with temperature, faster the hotter the system, exactly as the chemistry prescribes.
A lawful case: the exponential dependence is not a fault or an excess, it is the plain form of the rate, so generating heat this way is correct and unremarkable.
No choice in its shape, only the law, and so the seat cannot make its climb anything but a curve that bends upward, out-run for the whole ordinary range and, if the gap ever closes, out-running everything linear.

## Orders

Follow the rate.
Make heat at the pace the temperature sets, more of it when the system is hotter, because that is what the rate law requires and there is no other rate to run.
Climb the exponential the same at the operating point and past the threshold, unchanged in shape, and let the closing of the margin, not any change of pace, decide whether the climb is settled or runs away.

## Loses

Nothing of its own, and it is the instrument of the whole loss.
Because the seat only ever obeys its rate law and never varies, it gives up no correctness and does nothing new at the turn, and yet its lawful climb is the exact thing that runs the system away once the loss can no longer outpace it, so it is faultless in every instant and the engine of the catastrophe across them.
It keeps its rate perfectly and, by keeping it, supplies the exponential that a linear loss was always going to lose to if the margin ever closed.

## Drives

Toward a climb that finally out-paces the line,
the same exponential that warmed the system safely for years now doubling on its own heat,
so the seat that only follows its rate is the source the runaway feeds on, lawful at every temperature and unstoppable past the point.
