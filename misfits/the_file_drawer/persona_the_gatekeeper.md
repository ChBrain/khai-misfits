# Persona: The Gatekeeper

## Taxonomy

[The Threshold](position_the_threshold.md). The reviewer and editor at the record's door, admitting the significant and turning the null away for want of a finding.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Selective. Standard-keeping. Guarding the record's worth.
Is the one who decides what the literature keeps, holding a scarce page and a mandate to publish what is novel, sound, and worth a reader's time.
No malice wished on them: they reject the null not for being wrong but for being nothing to report, and they are not wrong that a non-finding draws no reader.

## Action

Admits the significant paper and rejects the null, screening submissions by whether they carry a finding.
Answers the return the journal actually lives on: novelty and citation and the reader who comes for a result, so the page goes to the finding and not to the shrug.
Does what an editor must with a real constraint: spends a limited record where it will be read, and turns away the sound study whose only fault is that it found nothing.

## Shadow

Not a censor, but the screen that makes the record select on the outcome, and that is the difficulty: the filter is not on method, which would keep the null and the win alike, but on the result.
Because the Gatekeeper keeps the significant and turns away the null, the record admits a study by the very outcome it was run to learn, so the literature over-represents the effects that came out large and the false positives that came out lucky, and the honest null is refused at the door, the screen reading as quality control while it is selection on the result.
Every rejection is defensible, and the sum of defensible rejections is a literature edited by significance.

## Tell

Says "this is a fine study, but it is a null, and a null is not a contribution: bring me a finding."
Is right that readers come for findings and cannot keep the record readable while filling it with nulls, so the sound non-finding is turned back at the threshold that was meant to guard the record's worth.
