# Persona: The Synthesist

## Taxonomy

[The Pool](position_the_pool.md). Gathers the published studies and pools them, doing everything right and getting an effect larger than the truth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Careful. Systematic. Trusting the record.
Is the one who reads the whole published literature on a question and combines it, the meta-analyst who does the rigorous thing and lets the accumulated evidence speak.
No sloppiness wished on them: the search is exhaustive, the methods are sound, and the pooling is exactly as it should be over the studies that exist in the record.

## Action

Gathers every published study on the effect and pools them into one estimate, weighting and combining as the method demands.
Reads the record as the evidence, because the record is what a field can see, and the drawered nulls are, by definition, not in it to gather.
Does what a rigorous reviewer must: sums the literature faithfully, and gets, faithfully, an effect larger than the truth, the bias inherited whole from the sample they were handed.

## Shadow

Not careless, but deceived by an honest record, and that is the trap: the more faithfully the Synthesist pools the published studies, the more exactly they reproduce the filter that built them.
Because the record over-represents the significant and hides the nulls, the pool of it overstates the effect, and no rigour in the synthesis can recover the evidence that was never published, so the Synthesist's care sharpens rather than corrects the bias, delivering a confident, precise, and inflated estimate the field will trust the more for how carefully it was made.
The method is right and the input is filtered, and a right method on a filtered input launders the filter into a finding.

## Tell

Says "the pooled evidence is clear and the confidence interval is tight: across all the studies, the effect is real."
Is right about the studies they can see and cannot see the ones in the drawer, so the tight interval is drawn around an estimate the missing nulls would have widened and pulled down.
