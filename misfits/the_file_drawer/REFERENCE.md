# The File Drawer: Reference

The research warrant for [The File Drawer](play_the_file_drawer.md).
The dynamic is publication bias: Sterling's finding that the record keeps the significant, Rosenthal's file-drawer
problem and the nulls that fill it, the aggregate overstatement it manufactures, the medical evidence and the case
for registration, and the p-hacking cousin it is set apart from, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging publication bias as a **selection-on-outcome filter over the record**: a study is kept for the literature
not by how well it was done, which would keep the significant and the null alike, but by whether its result came out
significant, so the significant enter the record and the nulls file into the drawer, and the sample everyone reads
and pools is filtered on the very outcome it is read to learn, over-representing the true effects and the lucky false
positives and missing every null that would pull the estimate down. The play models the documented mechanics, the
outcome filter that keeps a result by its significance and not its method, the null consigned to the drawer for want
of a citation or a career, the biased record that remains, the inflation that appears only when the record is pooled,
and the failed replication that would correct it but is a non-finding the same filter drawers, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a fraud. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law:
where the record selects results by significance rather than soundness, the published literature is a biased sample
of the work that was done and overstates the effects it holds, a false confidence sustained by no fabrication and
corrected by no replication, because the replication that fails drawers too.

It is distinct from the house's The Survivors, though both belong to one statistical family, selection on an
outcome, and the cut is in how the removal works: survivorship bias reasons from the units that persisted, the
planes that returned, mistaking a sample thinned by a passive attrition for the whole, the unit dropped by a hazard
indifferent to any finding, whereas publication bias keeps every study alive and sound and filters them by the
result, an active selection by rational gatekeepers on the outcome and not the finder, a chosen editorial and career
screen and not a blind loss, which is why a registry can keep the record honest going forward, logging a study
before its outcome is known so a later silence is conspicuous. It is distinct too from Metric Fixation, where a target is
set and then gamed until it corrupts what it measures: here no target is set and no one games a number, the filter
falls on outcome-significance after the fact and each study is honestly done. And it is distinct from The Streetlight
Effect, where the search runs where the light is easy: here the science is done across the whole field, the bias
living not in where anyone looked but in which honest results the record kept, and from The Availability Cascade,
whose belief gains felt truth by repetition, in that the engine is a selection filter on the record and not a
snowball of voiced opinion, though the hardening of a false positive that no null can enter to correct rhymes with
it.

## Origin

The phenomenon in theory, and the research spine beneath it.

| Source                          | Key Work / Event                                                                                                                                                           | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Theodore Sterling**           | "Publication Decisions and Their Possible Effects on Inferences Drawn from Tests of Significance: Or Vice Versa" (_Journal of the American Statistical Association_, 1959) | **The spine and the governing law**: journals publish the significant result and turn away the null, so the published record is selected on the outcome and inferences drawn from it are biased, the effect overstated by the tests that never printed. The play's governing law.                                                                                                            |
| **Robert Rosenthal**            | "The File Drawer Problem and Tolerance for Null Results" (_Psychological Bulletin_, 1979)                                                                                  | **The name and the image**: the drawer of unpublished nulls behind every published finding, and the fail-safe N, the count of filed nulls that would be needed to overturn a result, which Rosenthal read as reassurance where a finding survived a large one, the reassurance itself the tool's later-faulted weakness, since it assumes the drawer's nulls average to nothing. The Drawer. |
| **John Ioannidis**              | "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False" (_PLoS Medicine_, 2005)                                                                                                   | **The aggregate consequence**: on a probabilistic argument, selection for the significant, joined to analytic flexibility and low power, means the published literature systematically overstates, so that most claimed findings, he argues, will not hold, the distortion living in the pooled record and not in any one study. The Inflation.                                              |
| **Kay Dickersin**               | "The Existence of Publication Bias and Risk Factors for Its Occurrence" (_JAMA_, 1990)                                                                                     | **The evidence and the remedy**: the documented tilt of the medical literature toward positive results, harm to patients and to meta-analysis, and the case for prospective trial registration to capture the drawer before the outcome is known. The Registry.                                                                                                                              |
| **Simmons, Nelson & Simonsohn** | "False-Positive Psychology: Undisclosed Flexibility in Data Collection and Analysis Allows Presenting Anything as Significant" (_Psychological Science_, 2011)             | **The cousin, set apart**: the naming and demonstration of researcher degrees of freedom, the undisclosed flexibility within a single study that can present anything as significant, distinguished from publication bias in that it works the analysis before submission where the file drawer filters honest results after the fact. The No-Hack.                                          |

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the author, the
  reviewer, or the editor as a fraud, because the source finding is precisely that no fraud is needed: the author who
  writes up a significant result and files a null is spending effort where it earns a citation and a career, the
  reviewer who prefers the novel is screening for what a reader will read, and the editor who spends a scarce page on
  a finding rather than a non-finding is allocating a real constraint. The overstatement is authored by no fabrication
  and emerges from a filter met by reasonable responses, so the misfit is that a field of honest scientists, each
  keeping the result its reader will read, builds a record that overstates what the field knows, no faked data and no
  lie required.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the science correcting itself once enough eyes have read
  it. The one move that could clear it is to pre-register the study and publish the null whatever it shows, but a
  registered null still earns no citation and no career, and registration is slow and unrewarded, so the same
  incentives that filled the drawer run against emptying it, and worse, the replication that is science's own
  correction is itself a null when it fails, drawered by the same filter, so the false positive stands uncorrected.
  The reckoning is a record cleared only by a discipline that pays no one to keep it, not a literature that self-
  corrects through a screen that keeps only the positive, so the cure for a filtered record is a filter the record's
  own rewards discourage.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the finding that the record selects the
  significant and biases its inferences is Sterling's, the file-drawer problem and the fail-safe N are Rosenthal's,
  the aggregate overstatement of the published literature is Ioannidis's, the medical evidence and the case for
  registration are Dickersin's, and the researcher-degrees-of-freedom cousin it is set apart from is Simmons, Nelson
  and Simonsohn's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages the outcome-filtered record and
  credits that publication bias needs no fraud and is not, in itself, the p-hacking it is so easily confused with.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Significant](piece_the_significant.md)**: carries the result that clears the threshold (Sterling), the
  finding the record keeps because it came out significant and reads as worth reporting.
- **[The Null](piece_the_null.md)**: carries the non-finding filed away (Rosenthal), the sound study whose result was
  not significant and so earns no publication, drawered and unseen.
- **[The Record](piece_the_record.md)**: carries the biased sample (Sterling), the published literature that keeps the
  significant and turns away the null, read as the field's knowledge.
- **[The Inflation](piece_the_inflation.md)**: carries the pooled overstatement (Rosenthal; Ioannidis), the effect
  larger than the truth that appears only when the filtered record is summed.
- **[The File Drawer](piece_the_file_drawer.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Sterling; Rosenthal), the record
  selected on the outcome and the failures filed unseen, a false confidence sustained by no fraud.
