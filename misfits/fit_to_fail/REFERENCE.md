# Fit to Fail: Reference

The research warrant for [Fit to Fail](play_fit_to_fail.md).
The dynamic is evolutionary suicide: a trait that raises an individual's relative fitness lowers the
population's viability, spreads by a one-way ratchet, is reinstalled by selection each generation, and carries
the population across a viability threshold into collapse. The spine is Rankin and Lopez-Sepulcre's synthesis,
with Matsuda and Abrams's foraging-effort model, Gyllenberg and Parvinen's fixation conditions, and Webb's
classification of Darwinian extinction, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging evolutionary suicide as a **fit-to-fail structure**: a heritable trait genuinely raises the carrier's
relative fitness while lowering the whole population's viability, and because it wins locally those without it
are out-competed and must adopt it too, so it fixes by a one-way ratchet, selection reinstalls it each
generation blind to the whole, and the viability sinks until it crosses a threshold and the population crashes,
driven to extinction by selection itself. The play models the documented mechanics, the trait whose advantage is
real and relative, the edge banked individual against individual and never against the survival of all of them,
the ratchet by which the trait can enter the population but not leave because to lack it is to lose and to drop
it is to lose, the selection that favours the fitter each generation with no sight of the level of the whole, the
viability of the population lowered by the very move each carrier is rewarded for, and the threshold whose
crossing turns decline into extinction, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of greed or a
defector taking more than its share. The governing finding of the sources is the play's governing law: an agent
maximising individual relative fitness can drive its own population extinct, and does so with no error and no
cheat, because selection sees the margin between individuals and never the viability of the whole, so the fittest
move for the one can be the fatal move for all, and the remedy is restraint imposed from outside or selection
lifted above the individual, real but costly.

It is distinct from the house's The Commons, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: there the
engine is a within-generation depletion of a shared stock, reversible the moment restraint returns, a loss that
each agent could stop by holding back and that abates as soon as they do; here the engine is a multi-generation
ratchet in which selection keeps reinstalling the self-terminating trait, so there is no restraint lever any
single agent can pull and no holding back that survives, the trait fixed past reversal and re-chosen every
generation. It is distinct from The Arms Race, where the loss is wasteful positional escalation, spending to
stand still, resources burned to hold a position that never improves: here the population does not merely burn
resources to hold position, it crosses a viability threshold into collapse, the escalation not costly but
terminal, ending in extinction rather than in a standoff maintained at expense.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the competitor as
  greedy or the population as reckless, because the source finding is precisely that no one defects: the trait
  genuinely raises the carrier's relative fitness, taking it is the fit thing to do, and every individual that
  takes it is rewarded exactly as selection promises. The ruin is authored by no one and emerges from a genuine
  advantage spreading by a ratchet and reinstalled by selection blind to the whole, so the misfit is that the
  fittest move for the individual is the fatal move for the population, and any agent maximising individual
  relative fitness produces it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that holds the
  line is to impose a limit no individual would choose or to lift selection above the individual to the group, so
  the survival of the whole is favoured over the margin of the one, and it means overriding a genuine advantage,
  holding each individual back from a move it would be rewarded for, and paying that cost continuously against
  selection that reinstalls the trait every generation. The reckoning is a viability held only by paying, without
  end, the cost of forbidding the winning move, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the synthesis of evolutionary suicide is Rankin and
  Lopez-Sepulcre's, the foraging-effort model crossing to prey extinction is Matsuda and Abrams's, the necessary
  and sufficient conditions for the trait to fix and drive its population extinct are Gyllenberg and Parvinen's,
  and the classification of Darwinian extinction across the viability threshold is Webb's. The honest boundary,
  that the advantage is real and selection is not an error and there is no defector, is carried as carefully as
  the finding itself.

  return where decline becomes extinction.

  population extinguished by its own selection, self-inflicted and villain-less.
