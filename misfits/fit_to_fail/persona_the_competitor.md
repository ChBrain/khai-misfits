# Persona: The Competitor

## Taxonomy

[Competitor](position_competitor.md). The one who takes the trait that raises its relative fitness and out-competes the rivals that do not carry it. The archetype of the individual who does the fit thing and is rewarded for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Fit. Rewarded. Doing plainly well.
Is any one individual under selection, the forager who presses harder, the firm that grows larger, the boat that fishes longer, the one who takes a little more of the shared resource and comes out ahead.
No greed in it and no cheating: the Competitor does the fit thing, and the fit thing genuinely wins, its relative fitness really higher than the rival's.

## Action

Takes the trait that raises its relative fitness, presses harder than the individual beside it, and out-competes the ones without it, leaving more offspring and winning more of the contested stock.
Forages a little harder, or grows a little larger, or draws a little more of the shared resource, each move rewarded against its rivals.
Does what a fit individual must: it takes the edge that pays, and is paid for it, exactly as selection promises.

## Shadow

Not a cheat, a faithful taker of a genuine advantage, and that is the whole difficulty: the trait really raises its fitness, and what raises the individual's fitness lowers the population's viability.
Because the advantage is relative, measured against its rivals and not against the survival of all of them, the Competitor cannot see that the same pressing that lifts it above its neighbours draws down the whole they depend on, so it takes the edge and banks the reward and thins the viability, one of a population each doing the fit thing, sure to the last that the move that pays is the move that is right.
It takes the advantage that genuinely wins, and the winning is exactly what draws the population down.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "this pays, so I take it," and is right that it pays.
Says it having taken the trait that out-competes its rivals, and cannot see its gain as the population's loss, so reads the reward as vindication and presses on, sure the fit move is the right one.
