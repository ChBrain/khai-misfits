# Process: Selecting

## Taxonomy

[Fit to Fail](play_fit_to_fail.md). Selection reinstalling the trait each generation, favouring the fitter without error and without aim, blind to the population's viability.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A difference in relative fitness, generation on generation.
At [the range](place_the_range.md) across the generations, the higher relative fitness favoured and the winning trait re-chosen each time it is tested.

## Direction

Toward the fitter favoured and the trait reinstalled.
Because selection favours the higher relative fitness, selecting keeps the carriers and drops the non-carriers each generation, and because it sees the margin between individuals and never the level of the whole, it re-chooses the self-terminating trait every generation, breeding the population fitter individual against individual and less viable whole against its threshold, the viability sinking under the very rule that rewards each carrier.

## Lever

The blindness of the rule to the whole.
Because selection ranks only the margin between individuals and cannot weigh the viability of the population, selecting runs not on any malice or aim but on the plain consistency of favouring the fitter, the descent assembled from no error, only from the rule re-choosing the winning move each generation with no sight of the floor.

## Echo

A trait reinstalled, and the viability sunk one generation.
Selection has favoured the fitter faithfully, so what reads as adaptation is, in truth, the population bred one generation nearer the threshold, the winning move re-chosen and the level lowered, the rule stopping at nothing before the floor because it can see nothing there.
