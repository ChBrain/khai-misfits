# Plot: The Advantage

## Taxonomy

[Fit to Fail](play_fit_to_fail.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Range](place_the_range.md), a move that pays presenting itself.
A competitor reaching for the trait that raises its relative fitness above the rivals beside it.

## Action

[Competing](process_competing.md): [The Competitor](persona_the_competitor.md), holding [the competitor's seat](position_competitor.md), takes the trait that raises its relative fitness and out-competes the rivals without it.

## Stage

The [competitor](persona_the_competitor.md), on [Take the Edge](plan_take_the_edge.md), takes [the trait](piece_the_trait.md) on [the range](place_the_range.md) and banks [the edge](piece_the_edge.md) against its rivals, one pressing harder, one growing larger, one drawing more of the shared stock.
Each takes the winning move not as a gamble but as the fit thing, the reward real and banked, and cannot see [the viability](piece_the_viability.md) drawn down a shade beneath the margin it won.

## Tension

Taking a genuine advantage against a rival is the fit thing, and the fitness is what draws the whole down.
There is nothing to fault: the trait really raises the carrier's fitness and the reward is really banked. But the advantage is relative, measured against rivals and not against the survival of all of them, so the plot closes with the competitor ahead and rewarded, the winning move made and the population's viability already a shade lower, the gain visible and the cost unseen.
