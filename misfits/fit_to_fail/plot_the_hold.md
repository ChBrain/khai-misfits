# Plot: The Hold

## Taxonomy

[Fit to Fail](play_fit_to_fail.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Refuge](place_the_refuge.md), someone who would restrain the trait before it fixes.
A view of the whole and a lever above the individual, a limit that could be imposed or selection that could be lifted to the group.

## Action

[Restraining](process_restraining.md): [The Restrainer](persona_the_restrainer.md), holding [the restrainer's seat](position_restrainer.md), imposes a limit from outside or selects above the individual, holding each back from a move that genuinely wins.

## Stage

The [restrainer](persona_the_restrainer.md), on [Hold the Line](plan_hold_the_line.md), sets the limit on [the trait](piece_the_trait.md) before it turns [the ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md), favouring the survival of the whole over the margin of the one and holding [the viability](piece_the_viability.md) above [the threshold](piece_the_threshold.md).
It forbids the winning move at the level where it wins, against every individual rewarded for taking it and against selection that reinstalls it, the line holding only as long as the cost is paid.

## Tension

Holding back a move that genuinely wins keeps the whole, and the keeping costs without end.
There is nothing free in it: the move restrained is a real advantage, so forbidding it overrides a genuine win at the level where it is fit. The remedy is real, restraint from outside or selection above the individual genuinely holds the trait back, but the plot closes with the viability kept only by paying, continuously, the cost of forbidding the winning move to every individual that would be rewarded for it ... or the restraint refused as too costly, the advantage taken, and the descent left to run to the crash.
