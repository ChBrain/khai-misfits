# Persona: The Rival

## Taxonomy

[Rival](position_rival.md). The one who lacks the trait, is out-competed by the carriers, and must adopt it too or be gone, so spreads it. The archetype of the individual the ratchet forces to match.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Behind. Pressed. Made to keep up.
Is any individual without the winning trait, the forager who held back, the firm that stayed small, the boat that fished less, the one who did not press and is losing to the one who did.
No recklessness in it: the Rival would rather not take the trait, and takes it only because holding back means being out-competed to nothing.

## Action

Loses to the carriers, then takes the trait too, matching the move that beat it so as not to be gone, and by matching it carries it further.
Adopts the harder foraging, the larger size, the greater draw, not to get ahead but to keep up with those already ahead.
Does what an out-competed individual must: it matches the winning move to survive, and the matching is how the trait spreads.

## Shadow

Not a follower by appetite, an individual out-competed into matching, and that is the whole difficulty: taking the trait is forced, and the forcing is exactly what fixes it in the population.
Because the individual that keeps the trait out-competes the one that drops it, the Rival cannot afford to hold back, so it adopts the trait to survive and by adopting it removes the last holdouts, turning the ratchet one more notch, one of a population each pressed to match, sure that it had no choice and right that it had none.
It takes the trait to keep from being gone, and the keeping-up is what leaves no one who does not carry it.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "I have to, or I am out," and is right that it has to.
Says it having been out-competed by the carriers, and cannot hold back without losing, so matches the winning move and spreads it, sure that keeping up was the only survivable thing to do.
