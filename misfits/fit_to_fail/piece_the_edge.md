# Piece: The Edge

## Taxonomy

[Fit to Fail](play_fit_to_fail.md). The relative-fitness advantage the trait confers, the margin banked against a rival, measured individual against individual and never against the whole.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Won at [the range](place_the_range.md), where fitness is measured against rivals.
Reinstalled across the generations, where selection re-chooses the margin each time.

## Load Bearing

Without it the trait confers nothing to select, and there is no reason it wins or spreads.
With it the trap has its reward: the advantage the trait gives its carrier over the individual beside it, real and banked, the thing selection sees and favours (Rankin and Lopez-Sepulcre, on evolutionary suicide, adaptation at the individual level, the relative gain that drives the population's decline). The Edge is load bearing because it is relative: it is measured against rivals and not against the survival of all of them, so it can be genuine and rewarded every generation while the level it is banked over sinks, the piece carrying the margin that is everything to selection and blind to the whole.

## Apparent

A win, plainly, the reward of the fit move, the carrier ahead of the one beside it.
Seen at the surface, the Edge looks like exactly what it is at the individual level, a real advantage banked, and the truth that the margin is measured against a rival and not against the population, that it can be won every generation while the viability it draws on sinks, stays hidden behind how plainly the margin pays.
