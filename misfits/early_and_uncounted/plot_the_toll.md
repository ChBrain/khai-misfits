# Plot: The Toll

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Long Run](place_the_long_run.md), the whole span considered at once, not one trait but many.
A genome and a lineage, carrying gene after gene that has struck the identical early-favoured, late-uncounted trade.

## Action

[Accruing](process_accruing.md): the sum of every trait's early weight and thin count, across a genome and across generations, stands as a single compounding pattern of decline with age.

## Stage

No single persona stages this beat alone: [the Trait](persona_the_trait.md), repeated gene after gene, [the Cohort](persona_the_cohort.md), thinned the same way each time, and [the Count](persona_the_count.md), applying the one rule it has always applied, together compose [the Toll](piece_the_toll.md) across [the Long Run](place_the_long_run.md).
The toll stands revealed exactly as its parts were struck, correct at every step and total only in the sum.

## Tension

The pattern is real, and nothing inside the mechanism can undo it.
There is no error anywhere to find: every early effect was rewarded honestly, every cohort thinned for its own reasons, every late effect weighed by the one fair rule the Count has ever had, so the plot cannot resolve into a fix. The one thing that would change the sum, a cohort surviving further into years it currently loses to unrelated hazards, is not a lever any persona here holds, so the plot closes with the toll named for what it is, the accrued residue of many individually correct countings, and with no party inside the mechanism, not the Trait, not the Cohort, not the Count, in any position to reach for the one change that would alter it.
