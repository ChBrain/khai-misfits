# Process: Accruing

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md). The summing, across many genes and many generations, of many individually favoured, individually uncounted trades into the single visible pattern of decline with age.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

No single persona, but the whole cast repeated, gene after gene, across [the Long Run](place_the_long_run.md).
Begins wherever a second trait, and a third, and every one after it, strikes the same early-favoured, late-uncounted trade the first one did.

## Direction

Turns many separate, correct countings into one visible decline.
Because each gene's early effect is favoured on its own terms and each gene's late effect is discounted on its own terms, accruing converts a great many individually sound trades into a single compounding pattern, no one trade responsible for more than its own small share (Williams; Rose and Charlesworth).

## Lever

Repetition across a genome and across evolutionary time.
Because the same asymmetry applies to every gene with two age-specific, opposed effects, accruing works through sheer number: the more such genes a genome carries and the longer the process runs, the more visible the summed decline becomes, no single trade needing to be large for the total to be.

## Echo

A body's whole senescence, legible only as a sum.
The echo of accruing is decline with age itself, weaker, slower, more brittle, less fertile, standing as the accumulated residue of every trade the Long Run has struck, each one correct, none of them alone sufficient to explain the whole.
