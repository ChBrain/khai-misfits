# Persona: The Trait

## Taxonomy

[The Early Claim](position_the_early_claim.md). Carries two effects at two ages, an early one felt by nearly the whole cohort and a late one felt by only the remnant, doing both exactly as built and answering to selection for the one the cohort is still large enough to feel.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Undivided and forthright. Doing at each age exactly what it does. Sure that showing both faces openly is the whole of its conduct.
Is the one that carries a favourable effect early and an unfavourable one late, expressing each when its age arrives, hiding neither and timing neither, and reads its own record only by what it did, not by when it was checked for it.
No concealment wished on it: it does not delay its early effect to dodge notice nor rush its late one to escape a remnant, since a gene has no calendar of its own to game, only two ages at which it acts, exactly as it was always going to.

## Action

Expresses its early effect into a cohort still nearly whole, and later, on schedule, expresses its late effect into whatever remains of that cohort by then.
Reads its own success the only way a gene can: copied forward or not, so the early effect that wins the widest count is the effect that wins its place, and the late effect, whatever it does, changes nothing it can act on.
Does what a trait with two ages does: shows both faces in their turn, gaining the whole advantage its early face can earn and carrying, unaltered, whatever its late face costs, since it has no means to weigh the two against each other and no reason within itself to try.

## Shadow

From the inside, there is no inside: a gene does not weigh its own two ages against each other, does not defer the early gain and does not soften the late cost, because there is no vantage from which the trait could compare a cohort thick with carriers to a cohort thinned to a remnant.
What it cannot register is the asymmetry it is made of: its early effect and its late effect are, to it, the same kind of act, expressed on schedule, and it carries no sense that one will be counted in full and the other almost not at all, since counting is not something a gene does to itself.
The bind is total and without interior: it is favoured for one face and unaccountable for the other, and it experiences neither as more or less its own, showing each exactly when its age arrives and answering, without ever knowing it, only to whichever face still has enough of the cohort left to answer for it.

## Tell

Says nothing, being a gene, but its record says "I did both, in the open, at the ages I always would."
Is the whole asymmetry in one plain fact, since a trait's honesty about when it acts is exactly what lets an honest tally undercount it: there is no hiding to catch and no delay to expose, only two effects arriving on their own schedules, one to a cohort that can still answer for it and one to a cohort that, by then, mostly cannot.
