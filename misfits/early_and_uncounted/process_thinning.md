# Process: Thinning

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md). The narrowing of a cohort with age, by predation, accident, and disease, that leaves only a remnant present by the time a trait's late effect arrives.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Cohort](persona_the_cohort.md), across every age from [the Thick Years](place_the_thick_years.md) onward, losing members to hazards unrelated to any one trait.
Begins at birth and continues without pause across the whole span of a generation's life.

## Direction

Turns a whole generation into a remnant, one loss at a time.
Because predation, accident, and disease remove individuals steadily and for reasons of their own, thinning converts a cohort's early width into its later scarcity, so that by any given later age only a fraction of the original generation remains (Medawar).

## Lever

The ordinary hazards of being alive, indifferent to any particular gene.
Because these hazards act on the cohort regardless of what any one trait does, thinning works through nothing but the accumulated toll of unrelated risk, so a remnant's thinness owes nothing to the trait it happens to carry and everything to the span of time and danger it has already survived.

## Echo

A cohort narrowed into the Thin Years before any late effect ever shows itself.
The echo of thinning is a remnant already in place by the time a trait's late face arrives, so the scarcity that will discount that late effect was set in motion long before the effect ever had a chance to matter.
