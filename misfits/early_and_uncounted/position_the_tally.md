# Position: The Tally

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md). The seat that weighs every effect a trait shows by how many carriers are present to have it, holding one procedure for every age and unable to weigh a cost by anything but the numbers left to bear it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The whole procedure of weighing an effect by its reach, applied identically at every age.
The seat holds the single rule that decides whether a trait spreads: weigh what an effect does by how many of the cohort are alive to feel it, and apply that rule without exception, whatever age the effect belongs to.
Command of the rule and none of the population it is applied to, since the seat can only weigh who is handed to it and has no instrument to enlarge a remnant into the cohort it once was.

## Orders

Weigh every effect by its reach, and weigh it the same way every time.
Take an early effect against a nearly whole cohort and return its full weight, since a gain felt by nearly everyone is a gain the rule must register in full, so the seat rewards the width it is given without adjustment.
Take a late effect against a thinned remnant and return its thin weight, the identical rule now returning almost nothing, so the seat is not softer or harder on a late cost, only handed fewer carriers to weigh it against.

## Loses

Any way to weigh a cost by its severity alone, apart from how many are left to carry it.
The Tally can weigh reach with perfect consistency and cannot weigh harm on its own terms, because the seat's only instrument is numbers present, so a catastrophic cost to a handful of survivors and a mild cost to the same handful register, to the seat, as nearly the same small number. What the position forfeits is any separate register for severity: the rule that correctly rewards a wide early gain is the only rule the seat has for a narrow late cost too, and it cannot be told to weigh the second kind differently.

## Drives

Toward a tally that is exactly fair at every age and exactly blind, by the same fairness, to whatever a thinned cohort carries,
so the seat that most honestly rewards a trait's early reach is the seat that most honestly discounts its late cost, one procedure producing both, never bent and never negligent, only ever as wide, or as thin, as the cohort it is given.
