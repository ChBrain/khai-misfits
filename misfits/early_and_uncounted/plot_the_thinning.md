# Plot: The Thinning

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Thick Years](place_the_thick_years.md) giving way, across time, to [the Thin Years](place_the_thin_years.md).
A cohort that began whole, worn down age by age by hazards that have nothing to do with any trait it carries.

## Action

[Thinning](process_thinning.md): predation, accident, and disease remove individuals from the Cohort steadily across its span, regardless of the Trait, until only a remnant remains.

## Stage

[The Cohort](persona_the_cohort.md) acts from [the Remnant](position_the_remnant.md) on [Thin Regardless](plan_thin_regardless.md), losing members to hazards entirely its own until it stands as the remnant that later ages will meet.
The thinning proceeds exactly as ordinary life proceeds, no trait involved in the losing.

## Tension

The thinning is blameless, and the blamelessness is what sets the trap.
There is no error: the Cohort loses members for reasons that predate and outlast any one gene, and a population thinning with age is nothing but the ordinary course of being alive. But this same unrelated, blameless thinning is exactly what will leave the Trait's late face too few carriers to be weighed against, so the plot closes with a cohort honestly worn down to a remnant, its thinness owed to nothing the Trait did, and yet its thinness the very thing the Trait's late cost will hide behind.
