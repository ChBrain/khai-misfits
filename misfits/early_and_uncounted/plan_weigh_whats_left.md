# Plan: Weigh What's Left

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [The Tally](position_the_tally.md), inside [Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Tally](position_the_tally.md)

## Direction

Weigh every effect by how many carriers are present to have it, and apply that one rule without exception at every age, since a fair tally has no other procedure to offer (Hamilton). Reward an early effect in full when it reaches a cohort still nearly whole, and return a thin weight for a late effect met by a thinned remnant, the identical rule producing both without ever softening for the first or hardening for the second. Never ask why a cohort has thinned by the time a late effect arrives, since the seat has no instrument for that question, only for weighing whoever is actually there.

## Orders

([The Count](persona_the_count.md) tallies the Trait's early effect against the Thick Years and returns its full weight, then tallies the same Trait's late effect against the Thin Years and returns almost nothing, the one procedure unchanged between them.)

## Implementation

([The Thick Years](place_the_thick_years.md) and [the Thin Years](place_the_thin_years.md) both; through the [weighing](process_weighing.md) process, which applies one rule at every age; The Count returns [the early weight](piece_the_early_weight.md) in full and [the thin count](piece_the_thin_count.md) in kind, the difference between them owed entirely to the cohort each age hands over.)

## Targets

- [ ] every effect is weighed by the number of carriers present to have it
- [ ] the identical procedure is applied at every age, without exception
- [ ] a wide early gain returns a heavy weight and a narrow late cost returns a thin one
- [ ] the tally never adjusts for why a cohort has thinned, only for the fact that it has
