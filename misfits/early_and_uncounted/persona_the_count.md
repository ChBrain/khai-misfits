# Persona: The Count

## Taxonomy

[The Tally](position_the_tally.md). Weighs every trait's effect by how many carriers are present to have it, in full while the cohort is thick and thin once the cohort has narrowed, the same rule producing both.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Exact and impartial. Weighing only what is in front of it. Sure that counting by numbers present is simply what a fair tally does.
Is the one that takes each effect a trait shows and weighs it by how many of the cohort are alive to feel it, at whatever age the effect arrives, applying the identical procedure without exception every time it is asked.
No favour is shown and none withheld: the Count does not choose to look away from a late cost, since it has no procedure for looking away, only a procedure for weighing what is present, applied exactly the same at every age it is given something to weigh.

## Action

Tallies an early effect against a cohort still nearly whole and returns a heavy weight, since a gain felt by nearly everyone is a gain the tally must register in full.
Tallies a late effect against a cohort already thinned to a remnant and returns a thin weight, the identical procedure now returning almost nothing, because almost no one is left for the procedure to weigh it against.
Does what a fair tally does at every age: weighs by who is there, favours a trait exactly to the extent its effect reaches the cohort, and never asks whether the cohort it is given was thinned by the trait or by something else entirely.

## Shadow

From the inside, there is no other way to weigh: it has never had a procedure for caring about a cost's severity apart from how many carriers are present to bear it, so it cannot register that the same fairness which correctly rewards a widely felt gain is, by the same motion, discounting a severe cost simply because so few are left to feel it.
What it cannot register is that its own consistency is the mechanism of the undercount: it applies one rule at every age without wavering, and that very evenness, never bending for an early effect and never bending for a late one, is what lets a late cost of any severity pass for next to nothing once the remnant it falls on is thin enough.
The bind is procedural and blameless: it has no rule but weighing who is there, no memory of what the cohort used to be, and no way to ask why so few remain, so it tallies the remnant exactly as it would tally anyone, and the undercount it produces is the fairness, unaltered, meeting a population that has, for its own reasons, thinned.

## Tell

Says "I weigh what's there to weigh. I was never given a way to count who isn't."
Is the whole mechanism in one plain fact, since the Count's honesty about its own single procedure is exactly why the late cost goes uncounted: there is no bias to catch and no favour to expose, only one rule, applied without exception, meeting a cohort that has, by the time the rule reaches it, almost nothing left to weigh.
