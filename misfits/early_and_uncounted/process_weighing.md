# Process: Weighing

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md). The tallying of an effect by how many carriers are present to have it, the one rule that returns a heavy weight against a whole cohort and a thin weight against a remnant.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Count](persona_the_count.md), at whatever age an effect is shown, whether [the Thick Years](place_the_thick_years.md) or [the Thin Years](place_the_thin_years.md).
Begins whenever a trait's effect, early or late, meets the cohort present to feel it.

## Direction

Turns a cohort's size into an effect's weight.
Because the tally has only one procedure, weigh by who is there, weighing converts the width or thinness of whatever population it is given directly into the strength of the reward or the size of the discount, the same rule producing opposite outcomes from opposite cohorts (Hamilton).

## Lever

The single, unbending rule of counting reach.
Because the procedure never varies with the age it is applied to, weighing works through pure consistency: a wide cohort returns a heavy weight, a thin one returns a light weight, and the rule itself never asks whether the thinness was caused by the trait, by the hazards it survived, or by nothing more than time passing.

## Echo

An early effect rewarded to the full width of its cohort, a late effect discounted to the thinness of its remnant.
The echo of weighing is the entire asymmetry itself, the same fair procedure meeting two different cohorts and returning two very different verdicts, neither verdict a departure from the rule.
