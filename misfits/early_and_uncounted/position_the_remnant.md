# Position: The Remnant

## Taxonomy

[Early and Uncounted](play_early_and_uncounted.md). The seat a cohort occupies once age and hazard have thinned it, holding whatever a trait's late face brings and offering the tally too few carriers to weigh it against.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

Whatever is left of the cohort by a given later age, and nothing more to offer the tally than that.
The seat holds the survivors, thinned by predation, accident, and disease across every age that came before, so whatever effect a trait shows now is shown into exactly this remnant, and the remnant is all the tally will ever have to weigh it against.
Command of nothing but its own size, since the seat did not choose to be thin and cannot present itself as anything wider than the survivors actually present, no matter how severe the effect that lands on it.

## Orders

Carry whatever the late face brings, in whatever numbers remain.
Receive the trait's late effect exactly as it arrives, since the seat has no capacity to summon back the individuals already lost to unrelated hazards, so it bears the cost in the only numbers it has left to bear it in.
Offer itself to the tally honestly, since the seat has no other size to offer than its true one, so a thin remnant reports itself as thin, and the tally weighs the late effect against exactly as few carriers as are actually there.

## Loses

Any weight its cost might have carried had the cohort still been whole.
The Remnant cannot recover the width an early age would have offered the same effect, because the individuals who would have supplied that width are already gone to causes the trait never caused, so whatever severity the late effect carries lands on too few to move the tally, however real the harm to each of them. What the position forfeits is scale: the same cost, met by a whole cohort, would register; met by a remnant, it barely does, and the seat has no way to be anything but as thin as it has become.

## Drives

Toward a cost that is real to every member of the remnant and negligible to the tally that weighs the remnant as a whole,
so the seat that bears the latest, and often the heaviest, effects of a trait is the seat least able to make the tally answer for them, its own thinness the very thing that keeps the reckoning light.
