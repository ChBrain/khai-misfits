# Persona: The Sceptic

## Taxonomy

[The Review](position_the_review.md). Shrinks the estimates toward the prior before choosing, correcting the curse at the price of the boldest bet.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Doubting. Bayesian. Trusting the dazzling number least.
Is the one who will not take the top estimate at face value, knowing that the highest of many noisy readings is the one most likely lifted by error, and who pulls each estimate back toward the prior by how noisy it is before ranking them.
No cynicism wished on them: the doubt is not distrust of the analyst, whose numbers are honest, but respect for what a maximum does to honest numbers, a correction owed to the arithmetic and not to any suspicion of fraud.

## Action

Shrinks each estimate toward the prior mean in proportion to its noise, then chooses, so the flattered top is pulled back hardest and the ranking de-biased.
Answers not the raw reading but the reading adjusted for its reliability, discounting a dazzling projection built on thin evidence more than a modest one built on solid, so the option that wins after shrinkage is the one whose worth survives the discount.
Does what a Bayesian does: treats the estimate as evidence to be weighed against a prior rather than a value to be taken whole, and accepts that the corrected choice will often not be the one that scored highest before.

## Shadow

Not a defeatist, but the one for whom the discipline of doubt hardens into a temperament, and that is the private cost: the Sceptic has trained themselves never to be dazzled, and a mind that shrinks every bright number learns, in time, to distrust its own enthusiasm as a reflex.
The habit does not stay at the ledger. Having watched too many top readings flatter and fade, they come to meet any bold estimate, their own included, with the instinct to discount it first, so the very scepticism that spares them the letdown also dulls the capacity to be moved by a genuinely good bet.
What wears on them is not being wrong but being right in a way that never thrills: the one who always shrinks the dazzling number books the sound, modest winner and watches from a distance the bold prizes they talked themselves out of, carrying the quiet suspicion that a life of correct discounts is its own kind of loss.

## Tell

Says "the highest estimate is the one I trust least; discount it toward the average before you choose."
Is right that the maximum is flattered and cannot make the fix free, because shrinking the dazzling number back is exactly what forgoes the dazzling bet, so the sound scepticism that spares the letdown is the same scepticism that declines the boldest apparent prize.
