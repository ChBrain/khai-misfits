# Place: The Outcome

## Taxonomy

[The Best on Paper](play_the_best_on_paper.md). The realisation, where the chosen option's true value comes in and falls below the estimate that selected it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The winner delivering its actual worth, and the number that chose it looking too high in hindsight.
Shown as a disappointment that feels like bad luck, a pick that promised more than it paid, the gap between projection and result plain once the result is in.

## Holds

Holds the regression, the return of the flattered estimate's value toward the truth it left.
The Outcome is where the selected extreme comes back to earth: the chosen option's realisation regresses toward its true worth, so the value lands below the estimate that topped the list, a postdecision surprise owed to the maximum and not to any error in the reading.

## Offers

Offers, in hindsight, the proof that the choice was flattered, and the lesson the shrink would have taught in advance.
Here the shortfall is legible at last, the realised value showing how much of the winning estimate was noise, so the Outcome offers the evidence that a discount toward the prior would have priced the option right before the fact.

## Withheld

Withholds any culprit, because there is none.
What the Outcome hides is that its disappointment names no error to blame: the analyst was unbiased, the decider was rational, and the value still fell short, so the search for a mistake behind the letdown turns up nothing but the arithmetic of having taken the maximum of noisy numbers.
