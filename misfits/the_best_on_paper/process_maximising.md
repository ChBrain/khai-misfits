# Process: Maximising

## Taxonomy

[The Best on Paper](play_the_best_on_paper.md). The selection, taking the highest estimate, which conditions on the largest upward error along with the largest worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Selector](persona_the_selector.md), at [the Pick](place_the_pick.md), ranking the estimates and taking the top.
Begins when the honest slate is ordered and the highest number chosen, the optimal rule applied to unbiased valuations.

## Direction

Turns a fair slate into a flattered winner.
Taking the maximum is not a neutral read of the list but a selection on error as much as on worth, because the option that tops the ranking is the one whose truth and whose lucky error summed largest, so maximising quietly conditions on upward noise and lifts the winner's estimate above its truth in the act of choosing it.

## Lever

The correlation between topping the list and having erred high.
Because an option is likeliest to rank first when its error ran up, maximising selects for upward noise without meaning to, its lever the plain fact that the largest of many noisy readings is disproportionately the one the noise most flattered.

## Echo

A winner whose estimate stands above its worth because it won.
The echo of maximising is an overstatement created by the choice itself: the selected option's number is biased high not from any mis-measurement but from the selection, so the decider books a value the world will not pay and has no sign, in the estimate alone, that the maximum has flattered it.
