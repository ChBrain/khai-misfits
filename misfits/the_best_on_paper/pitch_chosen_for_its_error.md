# Pitch: Chosen for Its Error

## Taxonomy

[The Best on Paper](play_the_best_on_paper.md). The single hook: pick the highest of many honest estimates and you pick, in part, the largest error, so the option that scored best is the one its noise most flattered.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A decider with a slate of options and an honest estimate for each, picking the one that scored highest.
The tenor is optimisation done right: the valuations are unbiased, the ranking is fair, and taking the top of the list is exactly what a rational chooser should do.

## Undertow

Taking the maximum takes the error with it.
Underneath, the option that tops a ranked list is the one whose true worth and whose lucky upward error summed highest, so selecting the best estimate quietly selects for the estimate that erred upward, and the winner is flattered by the very act of winning.

## Nerve

The winner's number is too high because it won, not because anyone measured wrong.
The nerve is that no estimate was biased and no one lied: the overstatement is created by the selection itself, invisible in any valuation seen alone and real only for the option the maximum picked out, so the disappointment is a property of choosing, not a mistake in the reading.

## Echo

The realised value regresses toward the truth the estimate left, and the best on paper, on average, falls short in fact.
What lingers is that the cure is to distrust your own best number: shrink the estimates toward the prior and the choice de-biases, but the boldest projection is pulled back hardest, so correcting the curse means passing on the very option that looked best, and taking the top of the list means, on average, being let down.
