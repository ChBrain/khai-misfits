# Persona: The Selector

## Taxonomy

[The Ranking](position_the_ranking.md). Takes the option with the highest estimate, the rational rule that, in taking the maximum, takes the largest upward error with it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Decisive. Rational. Taking the best on offer.
Is the one who ranks the estimates and chooses the top, doing the plainly correct thing with a slate of honest numbers: pick the option that scored highest.
No greed or haste wished on them: taking the highest estimate is the textbook optimal rule given unbiased valuations, and the decider follows it exactly as they should, choosing the best that the reading shows.

## Action

Ranks the options by their estimates and picks the highest, committing to the top of the list as the optimal choice.
Answers the numbers as given, treating each estimate as the honest guess it is and selecting the largest, which is precisely what a rational chooser does when the valuations are unbiased and the aim is the most worth.
Does what an optimiser does: orders the slate, takes the top, and books the winner's estimate as the value to expect, no reason visible to distrust the very number that made this option best.

## Shadow

Not reckless, but the one whose correct rule injects the bias, and that is the trap: taking the maximum is not a neutral read of the list but a selection on error, so the act of choosing the best estimate is the act that flatters it.
Because the Selector takes the highest, they take the option whose true worth and whose lucky error summed largest, so the winner is disproportionately the one the noise lifted, and its estimate stands above its truth not despite the choice but because of it, a bias the rule creates in the moment it is applied.
The rule is optimal and the outcome is overstated, so the decider who correctly takes the best on paper is the one who conditions on the largest error and books a number the world will not pay.

## Tell

Says "this one scored highest, so this is the one to take."
Is following the right rule on honest numbers and cannot see that the rule is not innocent of the errors it ranks, that selecting the maximum quietly selects the upward noise, so the sound decision to take the top of the list is the same decision that overstates whatever sits there.
