# Position: The Ranking

## Taxonomy

[The Best on Paper](play_the_best_on_paper.md). The ordering seat, where the estimates are lined up and the highest taken, the seat that sees rank and not the noise behind it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A slate of estimates, and one rule for them.
An ordering: the seat lines the options up by their numbers and reads off the top, treating each estimate as a value to be compared with the rest.
No sight of the error, since the seat is handed point estimates and not their reliability, so it sees which number is largest and nothing of how much of that number is worth and how much is noise.

## Orders

Take the top.
Rank the options by their estimates and choose the highest, since with unbiased valuations the largest is the optimal pick and the seat's rule is to take it.
Book the winner's estimate as the value to expect, because the seat has no other number for the choice than the one that made it the choice.

## Loses

The distinction between worth and luck, which the seat cannot draw.
A ranking sees only order, so it cannot tell an option that sits at the top because it is truly best from one that sits there because its error ran high, and it treats both alike, taking the maximum as if the maximum were merit alone.
What the seat forfeits is any defence against the selection effect: built to compare estimates and blind to their noise, the Ranking cannot help conditioning on upward error when it takes the top, so the seat whose rule is optimal is the seat that, lacking the noise, turns the honest spread of estimates into a flattered winner.

## Drives

Toward the highest number taken whole,
and no way to subtract the luck inside it,
so the seat that orders the slate and takes the top is the one that books an overstatement it has no instrument to see.
