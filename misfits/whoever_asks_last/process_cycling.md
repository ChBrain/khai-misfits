# Process: Cycling

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). The summing of sincere rankings into an intransitive majority, a ring in which A beats B beats C beats A, the emergence of a group with no coherent top from members who each have one.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Voter](persona_the_voter.md) collectively, at [the Division](place_the_division.md), the honest ballots adding pair by pair.
Begins once the rankings are counted and the options are weighed against each other two at a time.

## Direction

Turns sound individual orderings into an unsound collective one.
Because pairwise majorities over three or more options need not be transitive, cycling converts a room of consistent voters into a group whose majority preference loops, so the process makes the aggregate incoherent out of parts that are each coherent, the ring appearing in the sum though it is in no member's ballot, most surely where the choice spans more than one dimension and no centre holds.

## Lever

The failure of majority to carry transitivity up.
Because a majority prefers A to B, and a majority B to C, without a majority for A over C, cycling works through the plain fact that individual transitivity does not aggregate: the lever is the gap between rational parts and rational whole, no majority miscounted and none dishonest, so the loop is not a fault introduced anywhere but a property the adding-up has whenever the preferences are spread enough to close the ring.

## Echo

A group preference that runs in a circle, with no head to crown.
The echo of cycling is a set of honest majorities that chain into a loop, so the room holds a verdict for every pair and no verdict over all, the collective ranking senseless as an ordering though sound in each link, a decision demanded of a group that, taken whole, prefers nothing to everything.
