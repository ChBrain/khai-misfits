# Position: The Method

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). The rule-making seat, which would aggregate the members' rankings into one fair and decisive group choice, and finds that for three or more options no rule keeps all its fairness conditions at once.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The fairness conditions, and the freedom to write any rule.
The seat holds the conditions a fair aggregation should meet, that it work for every possible set of preferences, honour a choice all prefer, judge each pair on that pair alone, and let no single voter dictate, and the liberty to design any rule it likes toward them.
It holds the group's hope that method can settle what the raw vote cannot, a rule that turns honest rankings into a choice truly the group's.

## Orders

Build a rule general, faithful, independent, and shared.
Design a method defined for all preferences, faithful to unanimity, independent across pairs, and ruled by no one voter, since a rule that drops any of these is not the fair aggregation the seat exists to supply.
Turn the members' honest orderings into a single group ordering, because the seat's charge is coherence with fairness and not coherence at any price.

## Loses

All four conditions at once, which no rule holds for three or more options.
The Method can keep any three of its conditions and cannot keep the fourth, because Arrow's theorem shows the four are jointly unsatisfiable once there are three alternatives, so the seat forfeits one fairness with every rule it writes: a method faithful, independent, and general must be a dictatorship, and a non-dictatorship that is faithful and general must break independence. What the position forfeits is the fair-and-decisive rule as such, not through any flaw in its drafting but because the object does not exist to be drafted. It loses even the escapes for free: restricting the domain to one axis regains a winner but surrenders the unrestricted preferences, and admitting intensity regains coherence but surrenders the independence, so the seat cannot buy the coherence back without spending a condition it was charged to keep.

## Drives

Toward a rule that must break one of its own promises,
and a coherence available only at a fairness surrendered, never both together for three or more options,
so the seat that would let the group choose fairly for itself is the seat that proves it cannot, its every rule a fairness kept by dropping another and no draft that keeps them all.
