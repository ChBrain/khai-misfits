# Place: The Division

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). Where the room votes and the majorities are taken, pair by pair, the floor on which honest rankings become counts and the counts reveal a ring with no head.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A fair count in progress, the plainest picture of a group deciding.
Shown at the surface is democracy working: the members casting honest ballots, each pair of options weighed by an equal majority, the votes tallied cleanly and the winner of each contest beyond dispute, a room settling its choice the fair way.

## Holds

Holds the majorities, and the ring they can form.
The Division is where each pair is put and carried, so it holds a true majority for A over B and for B over C and, in the same honest room, for C over A, keeping every pairwise verdict sound and the loop they compose invisible until the pairs are set beside each other, the fairness of each contest and the senselessness of their whole living in the same place.

## Offers

Offers a fair verdict in every pair, and no winner over all.
Here each contest is decided honestly, so the Division offers a clean majority wherever two options meet; and offers, through the same clean majorities, no option that beats all the rest, since the pairwise winners chain into a cycle, so the floor that settles every pair cannot settle the whole, a place of decisive contests and no decisive result.

## Withheld

Withholds the group's own choice, which the counts do not contain.
What the Division cannot offer is an option the room prefers to all others, because the honest majorities cycle and a cycle has no top, so no reading of the votes taken here names a winner the group can be said to want. The floor that weighs every pair fairly holds no fair answer to the whole question, only a set of contests whose verdicts loop, so the choice must be settled somewhere the votes are not, by the order they are taken in and not by the counts themselves.
