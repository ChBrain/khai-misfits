# Piece: The Impossibility

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). Arrow's proof that no aggregation rule is at once general, faithful, independent, and shared, the closed door that turns the trap from a fault to be fixed into a limit to be lived with.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Stands over the whole choice, met wherever [the Method](position_the_method.md) drafts a rule.
Flows as a wall reached from every direction, each fair rule arriving at it by a different face and none passing through, the shape of what cannot be built.

## Load Bearing

Without the Impossibility the cycle is a problem: it is Arrow's theorem that makes it a permanent one, the proof that the coherence the group lacks cannot be handed back by any fair rule and so must be paid for or done without.
The Impossibility is load bearing because it is the misfit's floor, the reason there is no hero: for three or more options no method satisfies unrestricted domain, unanimity, independence of irrelevant alternatives, and non-dictatorship together, so every rule that would let the group choose fairly for itself must break one of these, and the only rules that keep generality, faithfulness, and independence are dictatorships (Arrow). It carries the whole no-exit of the trap, since it forecloses not a particular fix but the class of them: the escapes remain, restricting the domain to a single axis or admitting intensity or seating a decider, but each spends a fairness, so this piece is what makes the remedy a trade and never a cure, the coherence available only at a price the vote was meant not to pay.

## Apparent

Just a hard problem, a fair rule not yet found.
Seen at the surface, the Impossibility looks like an open question, a better voting method waiting on a cleverer designer, and the truth that it is not unsolved but proven unsolvable, that the fair and decisive rule is not late but ruled out, that no further craft will reach it, stays hidden behind how much a closed door resembles one not yet opened.
