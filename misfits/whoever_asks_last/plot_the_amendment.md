# Plot: The Amendment

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Division](place_the_division.md), someone who would make the rule fair.
A decision left to its order, and a fair-minded member sure a better method can be built.

## Action

[Amending](process_amending.md): the Framer drafts a rule that is general, faithful, independent, and ruled by no one voter, tests it, watches it break one condition, and amends again, finding the fault moves from rule to rule and never leaves.

## Stage

[The Framer](persona_the_framer.md) acts from [the Method](position_the_method.md) on [Build a Fair Rule](plan_build_a_fair_rule.md), meeting [the impossibility](piece_the_impossibility.md) at every draft, and turning at last to the escapes only to see each buy coherence with a fairness spent.
The rule improves in every respect but the one it needs, and the one it needs cannot be had.

## Tension

The fair and decisive rule is not late, it is ruled out, and no further care reaches what does not exist.
There is no adversary: nothing blocks the Framer but a proof, that for three or more options no rule keeps all four fairnesses at once, so the wall is logic and gives nothing to push against. And the escapes are real but priced, a single axis that forbids the honest rankings off it, an intensity that drops the plain ranked vote, a decider that takes the last word, so the plot closes with the group holding a decision made by its order and no fair rule to unseat it, the reformer left to choose which fairness to surrender, the remedy a trade and never the cure, and [the made majority](piece_the_made_majority.md) standing because nothing lawful can take it down.
