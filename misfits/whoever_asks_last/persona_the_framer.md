# Persona: The Framer

## Taxonomy

[The Method](position_the_method.md). Would build a rule that turns the members' honest rankings into a choice that is truly the group's, fair and decisive at once, and finds that for three or more options no such rule exists, each one they draft breaking a promise it had to keep.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Fair-minded and methodical. Sure a good enough rule can be built. Reaching for the method that would settle it right.
Is the one who takes the paradox as a problem to be solved, believing that with enough care a voting rule can be written that is general, faithful to what all prefer, independent, and ruled by no one voter.
No cynicism wished on them: the Framer wants the fair rule sincerely and looks for it in good faith, so their defeat is not a failure of will or of craft but a wall that no craft gets past.

## Action

Drafts a rule and tests it against the fairness it must keep, and watches it honour almost all and break one, a Borda count that lets an irrelevant option tip the pair, a runoff that punishes a candidate for gaining support.
Tries the next rule and the next, each satisfying a different three of the four conditions and failing the last, the failure moving from rule to rule but never leaving.
Turns at last to the escapes, narrowing what voters may prefer or seating a decider, and sees the coherence come back only as the fairness it was bought with goes.

## Shadow

What deepens in the Framer is the particular dismay of a fair-minded person meeting a limit that is not anyone's fault: there is no adversary here, no interest blocking the fair rule, only a proof that it cannot be written, and a wall of logic gives nothing to push against and no one to persuade.
What wears is the slow turn from craft to grief. Each failed rule feels at first like a draft to be improved, and only after enough of them does it land that the improving has no destination, that the fair and decisive rule they keep almost reaching is not almost there but nowhere, forbidden by the same arithmetic in every attempt.
The bind is having to keep choosing anyway: the group still needs a rule, so the Framer must pick one of the flawed ones and live with the promise it breaks, knowing the choice of which fairness to surrender is now theirs, and that no amount of further care will hand them back the rule that keeps them all.

## Tell

Says "every fair rule I write breaks one of its own promises."
Is the whole impossibility in one line, since it is exactly what Arrow proved and exactly what the Framer keeps finding, so the sentence that sounds like a confession of failure is the plain report of a limit no drafter escapes, the fair rule not missed by their hand but ruled out for every hand.
