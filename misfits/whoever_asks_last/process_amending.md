# Process: Amending

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). The reaching for a fair aggregation rule and the amending of it when it fails, each revision keeping most of the fairness and breaking one, the search that Arrow's theorem closes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Framer](persona_the_framer.md), across [the Division](place_the_division.md) and [the Grid](place_the_grid.md), drafting a rule to turn the rankings into a fair group choice.
Begins when someone tries to mend the vote with method rather than leave the outcome to its order.

## Direction

Turns a search for the fair rule into a tour of the price of every rule.
Because no method for three or more options keeps unrestricted domain, unanimity, independence, and non-dictatorship at once, amending converts each draft into a demonstration of what it must give up, so the process does not converge on the fair rule but maps the ways of failing to reach it, every revision faithful to more and short by one, the fault moving from rule to rule and never leaving.

## Lever

The joint unsatisfiability of the fairness conditions.
Because the four conditions cannot all hold together, amending works through an impossibility and not a difficulty: the lever is Arrow's theorem, which makes every rule a choice of which fairness to drop, so the drafter's care improves the draft in every respect but the one that cannot be had, and the escapes, restricting the preferences or admitting intensity or seating a decider, buy coherence only by spending a condition, no revision reaching a rule that pays nothing.

## Echo

A stack of near-fair rules, each breaking a promise, and no rule that keeps them all.
The echo of amending is a set of methods each honouring three conditions and failing the fourth, and a reformer left to pick which failure to live with, so the group gains no rule that lets it choose fairly for itself and keeps instead a decision made by its order, the fair-and-decisive rule shown not missed but ruled out, the coherence buyable only at a fairness surrendered.
