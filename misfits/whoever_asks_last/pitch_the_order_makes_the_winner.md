# Pitch: The Order Makes the Winner

## Taxonomy

The single hook of [Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A fair vote is the picture of a group deciding for itself. Everyone ranks the options honestly, the majority carries, and the winner is the will of the room. Nothing is more legitimate than a decision the members made themselves, each with an equal say and no one overruled.

## Undertow

The room can want nothing in particular. Because sincere, consistent rankings can add up to a majority that cycles, a majority for A over B and B over C and C over A, there is no option the group prefers to all the rest, so there is no will of the room to read. A winner must still be found, and the options come a pair at a time, so the order of the pairings decides which survives, and whoever set that order chose the outcome. The same honest votes return a different winner for every running order, and the difference is not in the room but in the pen that wrote the agenda.

## Nerve

The trap's nerve is that nothing is rigged. No voter lies, no voter is fooled, and the one who sets the order is not cheating but doing a job that has to be done, since a decision cannot be taken all at once. The arbitrariness is in the honest preferences themselves, a ring with no head, and the sequence is only what fills the gap the group left. So the outcome is authored by no one's will and is still binding on everyone, a decision real and legitimate in every step and answerable to no coherent choice, because there was none to answer to.

## Echo

What it leaves is a decision made by its order and not its members, and no fair rule to take it back: Arrow's proof that for three or more options no method can be at once general, faithful, independent, and shared, so the coherence the group lacks can be bought only by narrowing what people may want, or by seating a decider, or by dropping the plainness of a straight ranked vote. Put the choice on one line and a median holds and the ring dissolves, but open a second issue and the centre goes, and the room is back to deciding whatever the last question makes it decide.
