# Place: The Order Paper

## Taxonomy

[Whoever Asks Last](play_whoever_asks_last.md). Where the sequence of the business is set, the running order of the pairings, an administrative document that looks like housekeeping and is where the outcome is really decided.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A schedule, the dull necessary order of a meeting's business.
Shown at the surface is administration: a list of what will be put to the vote and in what order, the sort of procedural housekeeping no one attends to, a neutral timetable so the room can take its questions one at a time.

## Holds

Holds the sequence, and the winner hidden in it.
The Order Paper is where it is fixed which options meet first and which the survivor faces next, so it holds the running order of the contests and, folded invisibly into that order, the result: because the cycling majorities return a different survivor for every sequence, the paper that sets the order is the paper that sets the winner, the decision carried in a document that reads as mere schedule.

## Offers

Offers a way to take the choice a pair at a time, and the outcome with it.
Here the room is given an order in which to decide, so the Order Paper offers the only means by which a cyclic vote can conclude at all, a sequence that yields a single survivor; and offers, through that same sequence, the survivor itself, since which option is left standing depends entirely on the order, so the paper that lets the vote finish is the paper that chooses how it finishes.

## Withheld

Withholds a neutral order, and any sequence that leaves the choice to the room.
What the Order Paper cannot offer is an ordering that does not decide, because when the majority cycles every sequence is as fair as every other and each names a different winner, so there is no neutral timetable to be had, only a choice of orders that are choices of outcomes. The place that looks like housekeeping holds no housekeeping that is not also a verdict, so the room that trusts the schedule as procedure has handed the decision to whoever wrote the schedule.
