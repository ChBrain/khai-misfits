# Piece: The Orphan

## Taxonomy

[Everybody's Business](play_everybodys_business.md). The accumulated whole: a decision seen by all, owned by none.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

The gap between the willing and the done.
On one side a room of capable people, each ready to help and each sure it is handled. On the other, an item that no hand ever closed. The Orphan is the standing distance between the willingness and the act.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of reasonable deferrals and one thing that slipped.
With it those are one object: a duty visible to everyone, plausibly anyone's, and therefore no one's, held whole by nobody, because the seat that raised it is not the seat that owns it, and no seat was ever made to own it. It is the proverb made flesh, what is everybody's business is nobody's, and it fails not for want of hands but for the surplus of them, each capable one a reason the others need not act.

## Apparent

A shared and supported thing.
From every seat the item is fine: raised, discussed, socialised, with the whole room behind it. The trap is invisible from within because support and ownership look identical from the outside, and the room is full of the first and empty of the second.

## Yearbook

The room built it in good faith: [the open item](piece_the_open_item.md) raised, [the someone-else](piece_the_someone_else.md) assumed, [the blank box](piece_the_blank_box.md) left open, [the many hands](piece_the_many_hands.md) all over it.
[The holder](persona_the_holder.md) was the first to stop passing and put a single name against it, too late to prevent and just in time to prove the point: one owner would always have been enough. Nobody had a name for the item, because a thing that is everybody's never needed one, until it did.
