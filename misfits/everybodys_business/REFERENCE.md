# Everybody's Business: Reference

The research warrant for [Everybody's Business](play_everybodys_business.md). The title is
the old proverb, what is everybody's business is nobody's business; the mechanics the play
runs on are grounded in the social-psychology and organisational literature on diffusion of
responsibility cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging diffusion of responsibility as a **systemic system**: a group in which every member
is willing and the open duty is dropped anyway, because responsibility divides by the number
who could hold it, so the more capable people who can see a thing, the less of the duty to act
falls on any one of them. The play models the documented mechanics, diffusion of responsibility
in the presence of others, the falling rate of intervention as group size grows, the willing kept
from acting by the cost of overstepping an unassigned remit, the problem of many hands under shared
contribution, and the assignment structure that leaves no single owner, as the load-bearing vectors
of the structure, not as a tale of shirkers. The governing finding
of the source literature is the play's governing law: bystander intervention decreases as the
number of bystanders increases, so the duty runs one direction, thinning with every added seat,
and the room best able to act is the room least likely to.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast
  any seat as a shirker, because the source finding is precisely that each is willing and each
  deferral is reasonable: do not overstep a colleague, do not duplicate effort, and with many
  capable people present, assume one of them has it. The misfit is that everyone is reasonable at
  once, about each other.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a conscientious volunteer stepping up
  in time. The item is owned only once it fails and someone is left holding it, or a newcomer
  takes it up not knowing it was nobody's, so the reckoning is where the passing stops, not virtue
  arriving to prevent the drop.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Open Item](piece_the_open_item.md)**: carries the un-owned duty, the task or risk visible to
  a group and assigned to no one.
- **[The Someone-Else](piece_the_someone_else.md)**: carries diffusion of responsibility (Darley &
  Latané), the individual and reasonable inference that another will hold it.
- **[The Blank Box](piece_the_blank_box.md)**: carries the responsibility-assignment failure, the RACI
  with no single Accountable, diffusion written into the chart.
- **[The Unraised Hand](piece_the_unraised_hand.md)**: carries the bystander non-response (Latané &
  Darley), the group asked to volunteer and declining in unison.
- **[The Overstep](piece_the_overstep.md)**: carries evaluation apprehension (Fischer et al.; the
  "do not overstep a colleague" norm), the willing kept from an unowned duty because acting on it
  would exceed their remit and be judged as presumption, distinct from assuming another holds it.
- **[The Many Hands](piece_the_many_hands.md)**: carries the problem of many hands (Thompson), a thing
  everyone touched and no one is answerable for.
- **[The Thinning](piece_the_thinning.md)**: carries the group-size effect (Latané, Williams & Harkins;
  Ringelmann), the share of duty per person falling as the number who could act rises.
- **[The Hot Potato](piece_the_hot_potato.md)**: carries diffusion in motion, the item kept in transit
  by referral so it never has to land.
- **[The Orphan](piece_the_orphan.md)**: carries the proverb whole (Walton), a duty everyone's and so
  nobody's, held whole by nobody.
