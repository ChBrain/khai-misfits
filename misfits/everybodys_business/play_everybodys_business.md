# Play: Everybody's Business

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Everybody's Business.
A decision that everyone in the room can see, that anyone in the room could take, dropped by all of them at once ... because what is everybody's business is nobody's, and the more hands that could catch it, the surer each is that another will.

## Arc

The bend is this: everyone is willing to help, and no one owns it.
It runs on two lines that should rise together and instead cross.
One line is the visibility: the more people who see the open thing, the more of them could act.
The other line is the ownership, and it falls as the first rises, because responsibility divides by the number who hold it, so the more people who could own the thing, the smaller each one's share of the duty to, until every share is too thin to move a hand.
A single person alone with the thing would simply do it: there is no one to defer to. Put ten capable people around it and it is safe, because each reads the other nine as reason enough to assume it is handled, and each deferral is evidence to the next that it is someone else's, and it sits at the seam between remits where it is plausibly anyone's and clearly no one's.
Give it one name on one desk and it is done by Tuesday: the ownership is the story.

## Triggers

**[Blank Box](plot_blank_box.md)**
An open item is raised in the meeting. It sits at the seam between two remits, and on the RACI its Accountable box is blank, or shared. The Flag raises it, the Sidestep passes it to the more obvious owner. Closes when the item is on the table with no name against it.

**[Thinning](plot_thinning.md)**
Cue: the item travels the circle of plausible owners. The department head assumes the specialist has it, the specialist waits for a mandate, and each deferral thins the next one's share. Closes when responsibility has divided so far that no share is enough to act on.

**[Parking Lot](plot_parking_lot.md)**
Cue: the room is asked who owns it, and no hand goes up. Each capable seat reads the others as reason to assume it is handled, and the many hands make it nobody's. Closes when the item is officially with the working group and actually with no one.

**[Left Holding](plot_left_holding.md)**
Cue: the un-owned thing fails, and in the aftermath someone is left holding it, or a newcomer takes it up not knowing it was not theirs. Closes when the orphan is named ... or it is re-parked, and drops again.

## Stakes

Whether the open thing ever lands on a desk.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward an owner, toward the group. The production cannot make responsibility survive division: the more it is shared, the less of it there is to hold. It can only show that everyone was willing, that no one was wrong to assume another had it, and that the one move that would have saved it, put your own name against it, was the one move a room full of capable people gives to each other and none of them takes.
