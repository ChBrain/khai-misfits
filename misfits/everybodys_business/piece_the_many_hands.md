# Piece: The Many Hands

## Taxonomy

[Everybody's Business](play_everybodys_business.md). So many touched it that no one is answerable for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Across the whole process, in every set of initials on the trail.
The item that passed through a dozen inboxes, each adding a comment, a caveat, a forward, and none adding a decision.

## Load Bearing

Without it a failure has an author and the lesson has an address.
With it the failure has none: the Many Hands is the problem of accountability under shared contribution, where a thing everyone touched is a thing no one made, so when it goes wrong there is no single seat to point to, and therefore no single seat that felt, in advance, the weight of pointing. It runs forward as diffusion runs: the more hands on the item, the less each hand carries, until the load per hand is nothing and the item, handled by all, is owned by none.

## Apparent

Collaboration, and thoroughness.
It looks like an item that got proper attention: many eyes, many inputs, well socialised. All those hands look like care. What they actually distribute is not care but liability, thinned to nothing across the crowd.

## Yearbook

Left by everyone who touched it, claimed by [the holder](persona_the_holder.md) only when it lands.
It is why the reckoning finds no villain: the trail is full of reasonable contributions and empty of a decision, so the search for who is responsible returns everyone, which returns no one, and the only name that ever attaches is whoever happens to be standing there when it breaks.
