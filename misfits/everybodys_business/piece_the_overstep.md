# Piece: The Overstep

## Taxonomy

[Everybody's Business](play_everybodys_business.md). The barrier that acting unbidden is a transgression, so the willing wait to be authorised.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At the edge of every remit, in the hands nearest the thing and least sure they may touch it.
The held breath of the capable: I could do this, and doing it means reaching past my brief onto another's patch, claiming a call that was never handed to me ... so I wait to be told it is mine.

## Load Bearing

Without it the nearest expert would simply act, mandate or no.
With it acting is not neutral: on a shared item everyone's authority is partial, so to move is to exceed your charge and risk grabbing what was not yours to take, and the seat closest to the work is the one with the most patch to overstep. The Overstep is evaluation apprehension made structural: the willing defer not because they assume another holds it, [the someone-else](piece_the_someone_else.md) is that, but because holding it themselves would be presumption, and presumption is judged. It is the distance between "surely someone has this" and "it is not mine to take."

## Apparent

Propriety. Respect for the remit.
It looks like exactly the discipline a good organisation teaches: stay in your lane, do not grab, do not make a call above your grade, wait for the owner to own it. Every one of those is a virtue, and together, over a thing with no owner, they are a room of capable people each too well-mannered to reach.

## Yearbook

Held most tightly by [the expert](persona_the_expert.md), the hands nearest the thing and surest they may not.
It is the gate [the blank box](piece_the_blank_box.md) leaves locked: where no seat is named Accountable, no seat is authorised either, so the duty is not only unowned but unclaimable, and the mandate that would free a willing hand is the one thing a room with no owner cannot issue.
