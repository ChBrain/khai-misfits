# Piece: The Unraised Hand

## Taxonomy

[Everybody's Business](play_everybodys_business.md). The moment the room is asked who owns it, and nobody volunteers.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In [the meeting](place_the_meeting.md), in the pause after so, who's taking this.
The half-second of shared silence, every seat waiting a beat to see whose hand goes up, and every seat reading the same beat as a reason to keep its own down.

## Load Bearing

Without it someone claims the item and the play ends in a name.
With it the claiming never happens, because volunteering is a move each seat is waiting for another to make: the Unraised Hand is the diffusion made visible for one instant, a whole room that agrees the thing matters declining, in unison, to be the one who owns it. Nobody says no. Everyone simply waits, and the wait is answer enough, and the chair, reading the silence as no strong view rather than no owner, moves the item on.

## Apparent

Consensus that it can wait.
It looks like a room without urgency on this one, no one fussed, easy to defer. The calm reads as slack. It is the opposite of slack: it is ten people each certain that if it were truly urgent, one of the others would have raised a hand.

## Yearbook

Held by every seat, and moved past by the chair before it becomes awkward.
It is the pivot of the play: the one instant when a name could still be spoken and is not, so the item leaves the meeting exactly as it arrived, agreed to matter and assigned to no one, on its way to [the parking lot](place_the_parking_lot.md).
