# Play: The Only Basket

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Only Basket.
For most people the house is not one investment among many, it is the investment: the largest thing they own, the store of their savings, the security they will retire on, and unlike a share in a company it cannot be diversified, cannot be spread across a hundred small stakes so that no single blow can hurt too much. It is all in one basket, and the basket sits on one street. So when a development is proposed nearby, an apartment block, a denser subdivision, anything that might, rightly or wrongly, crowd the schools or the roads or simply change the character that the value rests on, the owner does the prudent thing: they go to the town meeting, and they object, and they vote to restrict it, because they cannot afford a gamble with the one asset they cannot hedge. And this is not malice, and it is not contempt for newcomers: it is exactly what a careful person protecting their family's security should do, and the ballot is the only instrument they have to do it. And every owner in the town reasons the same way, and votes the same way, and the town down-zones, and so does the next town, and the next, each meeting full of sincere people protecting sound investments, and the sum, across a whole metro, is that almost nowhere can enough housing be built, and the price of a home climbs out of reach, and the region is starved of the very thing all those houses are made of. And the terrible turn is that no one voted for the shortage: each town voted only on its own streets, each owner only on their own basket, and the people who bear the cost, the young family, the worker, the newcomer who would have moved in and could not, were never in any of those meetings, because you do not get a vote in the town you cannot yet afford to live in. So the shortage is authored by no one and suffered by those with no voice in it, the sum of a thousand prudent defences of a thousand undiversifiable baskets, each one reasonable and all of them together a wall.

## Arc

The bend is this: each owner rationally uses the local ballot to insure the one asset they cannot spread their risk across, and the sum of these sound defences is a housing supply choked below what the region needs, the cost falling on newcomers who were never at the ballot.
It runs on a home that cannot be diversified.
For most owners the house is the largest, most concentrated share of their wealth, and unlike a stock it cannot be diversified, so a threat to its value is a threat they cannot hedge, and defending it is rational in a way a diversified investor's caution would not be (Fischel). The stake is real and the exposure total.
And the ballot is the instrument. Local government responds to the homeowners who vote in it, and zoning is the tool through which they insure the home's value, restricting the density and development nearby that might depress it, so the owner protects the basket the only way open to them, at the town meeting (Fischel).
And each restriction is locally sound. Down-zoning a parcel, capping density, controlling growth: each is a reasonable defence of the values around it, decided by the people those values belong to, and none, on its own, is unreasonable or ill-meant.
And the restrictions sum to a wall. Town by town across a metro the sound defences accumulate into a constraint on housing supply that is not the cost of building but the cost of permission, choking supply and driving prices above the cost of construction wherever the regulation binds (Glaeser and Gyourko), a regional shortage that no jurisdiction chose.
And the cost falls across the boundary. The people who bear the shortage, the priced-out newcomer, the young family, the worker, have no vote in the towns whose restrictions produce it, because the franchise stops at the municipal line and they live, or would live, on the other side of it, so the ones who pay are precisely the ones with no voice in the decision.
None of it is malice. The home really is undiversifiable and defending it really is prudent, the ballot really is the owner's only instrument, and each restriction really is a sound local choice: no one votes for a regional shortage, and it emerges from the sum of sincere defences of sincere stakes. What is most responsible in the owner is what walls the region off.
Ease the shortage, upzone, allow building by right, move the land-use decision to a regional or state table where the excluded newcomers count, and the housing can be built: but every such move strips the incumbent owner of the local control that is their only means of insuring an undiversifiable asset, and asks them to bear a real risk to the largest thing they own for the sake of people who do not vote in their town, so the escape is bought by exposing the one basket the restriction was there to protect.

## Triggers

**[The Threat](plot_the_threat.md)**
A development is proposed nearby that might depress values, and the Homeowner mobilises. The Builder brings a plan for denser housing, and the owner, whose undiversifiable stake is at risk, prepares to defend it at the meeting, prudently and in good faith. Closes when the threat to the value is live and the owner is roused to protect the one basket they cannot hedge.

**[The Vote](plot_the_vote.md)**
Cue: the meeting is called. The Homeowners fill the hearing and vote the restriction, down-zoning or capping the density, and the Builder's plan is blocked, the defence sound and the room sincere, and the Newcomer who would live there is nowhere in it. Closes when the restriction is enacted, the value protected and one more limit on the books, decided by those it belongs to.

**[The Shut Door](plot_the_shut_door.md)**
Cue: the restriction binds. The Newcomer, the young family or worker who would have moved in, finds the housing not built and the price out of reach, priced out by a decision made in a meeting they could not vote in. Closes when a would-be resident has been shut out, bearing a cost settled across a boundary they had no voice inside.

**[The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md)**
Cue: the restrictions sum across the metro. Town by town the sound defences accumulate, supply chokes below what the region needs, and prices climb above the cost of building wherever the zoning binds. Closes when a whole region is short of housing, the shortage a sum no jurisdiction chose and no owner voted for as a goal, its cost borne by those with no vote in it.

**[The Preemption](plot_the_preemption.md)**
Cue: the State would raise the table. Seeing the regional shortage, the State would preempt local zoning, upzone by right, and move the land-use decision to where the excluded newcomers count, against the Homeowner's loss of the local control that insures their asset. Closes when the decision is raised and the housing can be built ... or local control holds, the owner keeps the only instrument that guards their basket, and the wall stands.

## Stakes

Whether a region can house its people, when each owner is right to defend the one asset they cannot hedge.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the restriction, each a sound defence of an undiversifiable stake by the people it belongs to, and away from the supply, choked town by town below what the region needs. The production cannot make any single owner or town the culprit, because each defence is prudent and sincere and none votes for the shortage: the wall is the sum of a thousand reasonable protections of a thousand only baskets, and the cost falls on newcomers who were never in the room. It can only show that the home truly cannot be diversified and defending it truly is prudent, that the ballot is the owner's only instrument and each restriction a sound local choice, and that the one move that houses the region, raising the decision to a table where the excluded count, must be made by stripping the incumbent of the local control that insures their largest asset and asking them to bear a real risk to it for people who do not vote in their town, so the prudence is real and the defences are sincere and the region is walled off by the sum of a thousand owners each rightly guarding the only basket they have.
