# Piece: The Restriction

## Taxonomy

[The Only Basket](play_the_only_basket.md). The act itself, the down-zoning or density cap cast at the hearing, each one locally sound and none of them, alone, a wall.

## Place

Enacted at [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md), where the vote becomes a rule on the books.

## Load Bearing

Without it the ballot would be cast and nothing would change, the threat named but never actually kept out, and the value would remain as exposed as before the meeting.
With it the vote has a result: a parcel down-zoned, a density cap set, a growth control adopted, the development that threatened the value blocked or shrunk or slowed, decided by the people whose values it protects. The Restriction is load bearing because it is the individual, locally reasonable act by which the ballot becomes a rule that defends the value nearby (Fischel): the piece that carries the outcome itself, one town's ordinary and sincere defence, indistinguishable, taken alone, from any other exercise of local government doing exactly what it is for.

## Apparent

A single, sensible zoning rule, no more remarkable than a parking ordinance, sized to one parcel and reasoned by one room.
Seen at the surface, the Restriction looks like nothing at all, an unremarkable line in a town's code, and the truth that it is one of thousands of identical, separately reasonable acts across a whole metro, that its harm exists only in the company of every other town's version of itself, stays hidden behind how sound and small each single restriction looks on its own.
