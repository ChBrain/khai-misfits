# The Only Basket: Reference

The research warrant for **The Only Basket** (play and company not yet staged;
this warrant is drafted first, to prove the science and the distinction before authoring).
The dynamic is the homevoter account of land use: Fischel's homevoter hypothesis and zoning as home-value
insurance, with Glaeser and Gyourko on the supply and price effects, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the homevoter hypothesis as a **portfolio-defence structure**: a homeowner whose largest asset,
the house, cannot be diversified, and who therefore votes at the local level to restrict the nearby
development that might depress its value, a prudent, sincere defence of an undiversifiable stake that
sums across a metro into a regional housing shortage no one voted for as a goal. The governing law is
not that homeowners are selfish or hostile to newcomers, but that **each incumbent owner rationally uses
the local ballot to insure the one asset they cannot spread their risk across, and the sum of these sound
defences is a housing supply choked below what the region needs**, the cost falling on newcomers who
were never at the ballot. The play models the documented mechanics: the home as an undiversifiable,
dominant share of the owner's wealth, which makes defending its value rational in a way a diversified
shareholder's caution is not (Fischel), the local zoning ballot as the instrument through which owners
insure that value by restricting density and growth (Fischel), the accumulation of many locally-sound
restrictions into a metro-wide constraint on housing supply that raises prices and excludes (Glaeser and
Gyourko), and the incidence, the would-be residents who bear the shortage having no vote in the
jurisdictions that produce it, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of
homeowners driving newcomers out from malice.

It is distinct from the house's The Sort, where individuals with mild preferences relocate until a
neighbourhood tips into segregation: there the actor is a mobile chooser sorting by preference, and the
lever is the move, here the actor is the stationary incumbent owner and the lever is the zoning ballot,
the restriction defending an asset rather than a sorting emerging from mobility. It is distinct from The
Lowest Bar, where a mobile base plays jurisdictions against each other and each shaves its standards to
win it: there the mobile party is courted and the jurisdictions compete downward, here the base is
stationary and it is the incumbents who exclude, the newcomer the one shut out rather than the prize
fought over. And it is distinct from The Tyranny of Small Decisions, where uncoordinated choices sum to
an aggregate no one willed: that is the family the misfit belongs to, but its distinct vector is the
asset-insurance motive of an undiversifiable home and the disenfranchisement of the newcomers who bear
the cost, a sincere portfolio defence by the enfranchised falling on the unenfranchised, not merely an
unmanaged sum. The house stages the sincere equity-protection core and refuses any reading of the
homeowner as a NIMBY acting from malice.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  homeowner as a NIMBY acting from malice or contempt for newcomers, because the source finding it stages
  is the prudent case: the home is the owner's largest asset and cannot be diversified, so insuring its
  value against nearby change is a rational, sincere defence no diversified investor would need to make
  (Fischel), and the ballot is the only instrument they have to make it. The shortage is authored by no
  one and emerges from the structure, many locally-sound restrictions summing to a supply choked below
  the region's need (Glaeser and Gyourko), so the misfit is that homeowners each defending an
  undiversifiable stake, sincerely and by the rules, produce a housing shortage no one of them ever voted
  for as a goal.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The moves that ease the
  shortage, upzoning, by-right development, moving the land-use decision to a regional or state level
  where the excluded newcomers count, do not come free: each takes from the incumbent owner the local
  control that is their only means of insuring an undiversifiable asset, and asks them to bear a risk to
  their largest holding for the sake of people who do not vote in their town. The reckoning is supply
  bought against the security of the owner's one basket, a shortage eased by exposing the home value the
  restriction was there to protect, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the homevoter hypothesis and zoning-as-insurance
  are Fischel's, and the supply and price effects of land-use restriction are Glaeser's and Gyourko's,
  and the condition the trap actually requires, an undiversifiable home defended through a local ballot
  whose cost falls on the unenfranchised, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element. The play and its company are not yet authored; these are the
proposed load-bearing pieces the warrant commits the staging to carry, one per vector, no filler.

- **The Only Basket**: carries the undiversifiable home (Fischel), the owner's largest asset and dominant
  share of their wealth, which makes defending its value rational where a diversified investor's would not
  be.
- **The Ballot**: carries the local franchise (Fischel), the zoning vote and plebiscite through which the
  owner insures the value of the home, and which the newcomer does not hold.
- **The Restriction**: carries the individual act (Fischel), the down-zoning, density cap, or growth
  control that defends the value nearby, each one locally reasonable.
- **The Newcomer**: carries the incidence (the disenfranchised), the would-be resident who bears the
  shortage and has no vote in the jurisdiction that imposes it, the externality across the boundary.
- **The Shortage**: carries the accrued sum (Glaeser and Gyourko), the metro-wide housing supply choked
  below need and the prices raised, the cost that lives only in the aggregate no town chose.
- **The Higher Table**: carries the escape and its price (Fischel), moving the land-use decision to a
  regional or state level where the newcomers count, bought by stripping the incumbent of the local
  control that insures their one basket.
