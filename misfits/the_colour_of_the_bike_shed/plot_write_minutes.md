# Plot: Write Minutes

## Taxonomy

[The Colour of the Bike Shed](play_the_colour_of_the_bike_shed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The minutes](place_the_minutes.md), open for writing, the meeting just closed.
Both items were on the agenda and both were dealt with, so both go into the record the same way: item raised, item resolved, meeting adjourned.

## Action

[Reckoning](process_reckoning.md) in reverse: [The Facilitator](persona_the_facilitator.md), holding [the chair's seat](position_chair.md), writes up the meeting item by item, each line reporting that the matter was considered and closed.
The paint and the reactor are set down as peers, exactly as [the flat agenda](piece_the_flat_agenda.md) listed them.

## Stage

[The Facilitator](persona_the_facilitator.md) tables [Run the Agenda](plan_run_the_agenda.md) to the page: record every item as handled, and [the drift](piece_the_drift.md) is preserved and hidden at once, an hour of care and a passing nod written up in the same flat grammar.
The record shows full engagement on everything and the distribution of that engagement on nothing, so the one number that would expose the meeting, minutes spent against stakes carried, is the one number the minutes do not keep.

## Tension

An honest record of a meeting that went wrong looks exactly like a record of one that went right.
The minutes are accurate, every line is true, the chair has invented nothing, and that is the trouble: a faithful log of items raised and resolved cannot show that the room poured its hour into the trivial and waved the vital through, because the ledger records that things were discussed and never how much or to what end. So the drift is entered into the permanent record as good order, and the only trace of the misallocation is the one the format is built not to keep.
