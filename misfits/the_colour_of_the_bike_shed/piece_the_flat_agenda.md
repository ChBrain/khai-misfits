# Piece: The Flat Agenda

## Taxonomy

[The Colour of the Bike Shed](play_the_colour_of_the_bike_shed.md). Equal slots for unequal stakes, the format that lets it happen.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On [the agenda](place_the_agenda.md), the reactor and the shed as two lines of the same size.
Item four and item five, one under the other, each an entry with a heading and no weighting, as if a decade and an afternoon were the same unit.

## Load Bearing

Without it the stakes are visible and the room might spend by them.
With it the stakes are erased, because a list treats its items as peers: the Flat Agenda strips the consequence off every line and leaves only the topic, so the room arrives to find the reactor and the shed presented as equals, and fills each slot by appetite rather than importance. It is the structural enabler, the neutral-looking format that quietly hands the meeting to whichever item the room most wants to discuss.

## Apparent

Order, fairness, good chairing.
It looks like organisation itself: numbered items, allotted in turn, everything getting its place. The fairness is the flaw, because giving unequal things equal footing is not neutral, it is a thumb on the scale toward the small.

## Yearbook

Drawn up by [the facilitator](persona_the_facilitator.md), in the name of running a tidy meeting.
It is the stage the whole play needs: level the agenda and you invite the room to allocate by [the comprehension gap](piece_the_comprehension_gap.md), because once the stakes are off the page, the only thing left to choose by is what each seat can hold.
