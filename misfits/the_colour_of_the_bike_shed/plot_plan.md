# Plot: The Flat Agenda

## Taxonomy

[The Colour of the Bike Shed](play_the_colour_of_the_bike_shed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The agenda](place_the_agenda.md), drawn up the night before, tidy and numbered.
The reactor and the bike shed sit one under the other, equal lines. At [the table](place_the_table.md) both are about to be tabled, level, as if the stakes were the same.

## Action

[Agenda-setting](process_agenda_setting.md): [The Facilitator](persona_the_facilitator.md), holding [the chair's seat](position_chair.md), lists the items in order and allots each its turn.
[The Specialist](persona_the_specialist.md) at [the reactor owner's seat](position_reactor_owner.md) tables the reactor hoping for a hard look, and [The Handyman](persona_the_handyman.md) at [the shed owner's seat](position_shed_owner.md) tables the shed expecting five minutes.

## Stage

[The Facilitator](persona_the_facilitator.md) tables [Run the Agenda](plan_run_the_agenda.md): keep a tidy order, and draws [the flat agenda](piece_the_flat_agenda.md), unequal stakes on equal lines.
[The Specialist](persona_the_specialist.md) tables [Table the Reactor](plan_table_the_reactor.md): bring [the reactor](piece_the_reactor.md) for scrutiny.
[The Handyman](persona_the_handyman.md) tables [Table the Shed](plan_table_the_shed.md): settle [the bike shed](piece_the_bike_shed.md) quickly.

## Tension

Nothing here is careless.
A tidy agenda is good chairing, bringing the big decision to the room is right, and the shed genuinely is a five-minute item. But the stakes have been stripped off the page and the two items now sit as peers, so the room arrives free to spend its hour on whichever it would rather hold, and that choice is already made by what it can hold.
