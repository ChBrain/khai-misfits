# Plot: Table Reactor

## Taxonomy

[The Colour of the Bike Shed](play_the_colour_of_the_bike_shed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The table](place_the_table.md), the reactor up, and the room quietly relieved to move it along.
The item is too large for the hour and too hard for the seats, so rather than open it, the meeting agrees to hand it somewhere else and get on.

## Action

[Deferral](process_deferral.md): [The Deferrer](persona_the_deferrer.md), holding [the abstainer's seat](position_abstainer.md), proposes the reactor go to a sub-group of experts, and the room, glad of a clean exit, agrees.
[The Specialist](persona_the_specialist.md) at [the reactor owner's seat](position_reactor_owner.md) lets it pass, since a smaller room sounds like a harder look.

## Stage

[The Deferrer](persona_the_deferrer.md) tables [Defer to the Experts](plan_defer_to_the_experts.md): send [the reactor](piece_the_reactor.md) to a workstream that will meet later, so the full room never has to hold it at all.
The sub-group is named, minuted, and owned by no one who was in the room, and the deferral becomes a workstream: avoidance, formalised, with a slot on a future agenda that keeps receding.

## Tension

Sending a hard item to the experts is exactly what a sensible room does.
The reactor is genuinely too big for a general meeting, and a focused sub-group genuinely is the right venue, and no one is dodging, they are routing. But the routing is also the avoidance the room wanted, because a thing handed to a smaller room later is a thing this room does not have to face now, and the workstream that was meant to give it a harder look is the same device that lets it drift, unheld, past every meeting that could have caught it.
