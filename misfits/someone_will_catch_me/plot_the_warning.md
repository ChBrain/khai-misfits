# Plot: The Warning

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Hammock](place_the_hammock.md), the Samaritan resolved that this cannot go on.
A helper who has seen the footing go unbuilt and means to change it, drawing a line and telling the recipient that next time the help will not come.

## Action

[Softening](process_softening.md) foreseen: the warning is uttered in the calm and priced at once as a bluff, because both sides know it will dissolve when the real need arrives.

## Stage

[The Samaritan](persona_the_samaritan.md) delivers [the empty threat](piece_the_empty_threat.md) from [the Hammock](place_the_hammock.md), meaning every word, and [the Sheltered](persona_the_sheltered.md), reading the ex-post incentive, discounts it entirely, since [the soft heart](piece_the_soft_heart.md) will open the hand when the brink comes whatever was said.
The words are firm and change nothing, because the deeds are foreknown to be soft.

## Tension

The Samaritan means the threat sincerely, and its sincerity is worthless, because sincerity is not credibility.
There is no bluff intended: the resolve is genuine, and the Samaritan truly wishes to hold to it. But a threat is credible only if it will be kept when the moment comes, and this one will not, because the compassion that made it will unmake it at the brink, so the plot closes with the warning heard, understood, and rationally ignored, the recipient right to discount it and the Samaritan unable to make firm words bind a soft heart, the condition that would break the cycle exposed as one the helper cannot impose on themselves.
