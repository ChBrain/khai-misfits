# Place: The Hammock

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The comfortable reliant state under the guarantee, where the recipient rests on a rescue they can count on and the footing goes quietly unbuilt.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Ease and security, a life arranged sensibly around a sure thing.
Shown as prudence rewarded: the risks that are truly theirs are covered, the ones the rescue carries are left to it, and the arrangement looks like nothing but a reasonable person making good use of a guarantee.

## Holds

Holds the slack that a covered risk permits, the provision not made because it need not be.
The Hammock is where the reliance settles into a way of living: the recipient rests the weight they would otherwise have had to carry onto the rescue that has agreed to carry it, so the ease is real and earned by good sense, the footing left unbuilt not from laziness but because the ground beneath it is spoken for.

## Offers

Offers a genuine comfort now, and a capability quietly forgone.
Here the security is real and the economy sound, so the Hammock offers the true rest of not bearing a weight another has taken, and in the same ease it offers the slow forgoing of the strength that only carrying one's own weight builds, the comfort and the enfeeblement the same repose.

## Withheld

Withholds that the rest is also a diminishment.
What the Hammock hides is that the weight not carried is a strength not built, so the recipient cannot feel, from inside the ease, that their own footing is thinning, the comfort concealing that a life arranged around a catch is a life growing less able to stand without one, the slack that feels like prudence today the incapacity that will need the rescue tomorrow.
