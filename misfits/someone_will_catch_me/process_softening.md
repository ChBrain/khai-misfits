# Process: Softening

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The dissolving of the threat to withhold, the firm resolve made beforehand undone by the compassion that arrives with the need, so the words stay hard and the deeds go soft.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Empty Threat](piece_the_empty_threat.md), at [the Brink](place_the_brink.md), where a resolve to refuse meets a suffering it cannot survive.
Begins the moment the hypothetical becomes actual, when the person the threat was aimed at is really in need and really in front of the one who made it.

## Direction

Turns a firm intention into an open hand.
Because a resolve to withhold, sincere in the calm, cannot hold against the sight of real suffering, the intention gives way to the rescue when the moment comes, so softening converts every ex-ante threat into an ex-post help, the firmness dissolving exactly when it would have to bite (Kydland and Prescott; Schelling).

## Lever

The gap between resolving and doing.
Because the resolve is formed at a distance from the suffering and tested in its presence, softening works through the difference between the two moments: what is easy to intend when the need is hypothetical is impossible to hold when it is real, so the lever is the plain fact that compassion is stronger in the moment than resolve is across it.

## Echo

A warning discredited, and a recipient who reads it right.
The echo of softening is a threat both sides now know to be empty, so the recipient discounts every future warning as the bluff it is, the repeated dissolving of the resolve teaching that the words are noise and the certain help the only fact worth planning on.
