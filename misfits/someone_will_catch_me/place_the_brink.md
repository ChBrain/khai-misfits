# Place: The Brink

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The edge of real need, where the fall is imminent and the rescue is decided in the moment, the place the Samaritan cannot look on and refuse.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A person about to fall, and a helper who can stop it.
Shown as a clear call: the need is genuine and immediate, the means to relieve it are at hand, so stepping in reads as nothing but the obvious decency of not letting someone come to harm you could prevent.

## Holds

Holds the ex-post moment, where a suffering in front of you overrides any plan made about it.
The Brink is where the decision is really made, not in the calm beforehand but in the presence of actual need, so whatever was resolved about conditions and lessons, here there is only a person in trouble and a hand that can help, and the hand, being good, opens.

## Offers

Offers a real chance to relieve a real harm, and the setting in which every threat to withhold dies.
Here the suffering is true and the relief within reach, so the Brink offers the Samaritan the plainest possible reason to help, and by making the need present and immediate it offers the exact conditions under which no prior resolve to refuse can survive, the moment that converts every firm intention into one more rescue.

## Withheld

Withholds that the fall was made likelier by the certainty of the catch.
What the Brink hides is its own history: the person arrives at the edge less prepared than they would have been unaided, because the rescue that is about to save them is the reliability they planned around, so the immediacy that makes helping obvious conceals that the need was shaped, in part, by the very hand now reaching for it.
