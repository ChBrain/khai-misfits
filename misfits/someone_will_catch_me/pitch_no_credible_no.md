# Pitch: No Credible No

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The single hook: a benefactor who cannot credibly refuse makes the rescue certain, so the recipient rationally builds less of their own footing, and the reliability of the help manufactures the dependence it means to relieve.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Someone in real need, and a good person who steps in.
The tenor is decency: the suffering is genuine and the means are to hand, so the help is given, exactly as a person who cannot stand by another's trouble should give it, the rescue right and the relief real.

## Undertow

The good person cannot commit to not helping, and everyone knows it.
Underneath, the compassion is certain ex post, so the recipient, reading a guarantee they can count on, provisions less against a risk another has agreed to carry, under-saving and under-bracing not from vice but from the plain sense of not building a footing they will not need.

## Nerve

The threat to withhold is a bluff both sides see through.
The nerve is that the benefactor would dearly like to say next time you are on your own, and cannot make it stick, because when next time comes and the need is real they will help again, so the words are firm and the deeds stay soft, the warning discounted the moment it is understood.

## Echo

The kindness that would never let them fall is the kindness under which they never learn to stand.
What lingers is that the only cure is a credible no, a binding of oneself against one's own compassion to let the recipient bear a fall they could be spared, so freeing them to stand means inflicting the very harm the help exists to prevent, and giving the help, as any good heart does, is what keeps the need alive.
