# Persona: The Hard Hand

## Taxonomy

[The Vow](position_the_vow.md). The one who binds themselves to withhold, letting the recipient meet the brink alone to restore the reason to build their own footing, and bearing the sight of a suffering they could have relieved.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Bound by choice. Withholding on purpose. Willing to let a fall happen to teach a standing.
Is the one who has seen that a certain rescue makes the footing go unbuilt, and so ties their own hands against the ex-post pull to help, so that the threat to withhold becomes real and the recipient provisions again.
No cruelty wished on them: the withholding is not coldness but a deliberate binding against their own compassion, undertaken in the knowledge that the present suffering it allows is the price of the future capacity it restores.

## Action

Commits, in advance and bindingly, to not stepping in, and holds to it when the brink comes, letting the recipient face the fall the Samaritan would always have caught.
Bears the immediate, visible harm of a suffering they could end, refusing the rescue not because they do not feel it but because relieving it once more would unmake the very lesson the refusal is for.
Does what a hard love does: trades a present, certain, nameable harm to the person for a future, uncertain, unprovable capacity in them, and is called cruel for it by everyone who sees only the fall and not the standing it is meant to buy.

## Shadow

Not a hardness of heart, but the anguish of feeling every bit of the suffering and choosing it anyway, and that is the weight: they are not cold, they are bound, and the binding is against a compassion they have in full, so each withheld rescue is a wound they take as much as give.
What gnaws is the doubt that the lesson will take. The fall is certain and the standing is not, so they inflict a sure harm for an unsure good, and cannot know, while they watch the person go down, whether they are teaching them to stand or merely letting them break, the whole justification resting on a future they cannot see and might be wrong about.
The bind is interior and lonely: to hold the vow they must silence the part of themselves that is right to want to help, so they are at war with their own decency, and if the lesson fails they will have been cruel for nothing, and if it succeeds no one will ever see the fall they spared the person later by refusing to spare them now.

## Tell

Says "I am not helping you, and it is because I love you, not because I don't."
Is the exact inversion the trap requires and the hardest thing to be believed, because every visible sign, the withheld hand, the unrelieved suffering, says the opposite, so the sentence that states the whole logic of the cure is the one that sounds most like an excuse for cruelty, the love provable only by the fall it allows and never by the standing no one will trace back to it.
