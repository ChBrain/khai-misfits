# Persona: The Sheltered

## Taxonomy

[The Reliance](position_the_reliance.md). The recipient who reads the rescue as certain and rationally builds less of their own footing, under-provisioning against a risk another has agreed to carry, not from sloth but from the plain sense of a guarantee.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Rational. Reading the guarantee. Provisioning against the risks that are actually theirs to bear.
Is the one who knows the catch will come, and so does not lay by against a fall someone else has undertaken to break, arranging their affairs around the help as sensibly as anyone arranges around a sure thing.
No fraud wished on them: the under-provision is not a con but the correct response to a reliable rescue, exactly what a reasonable person does when a net hangs where they were going to stand.

## Action

Builds less footing than they would if the ground were the only floor, under-saving and under-bracing and keeping less guard, because the rescue is certain and provisioning against a covered risk is waste.
Discounts the warning that next time will be different, because they can read the ex-post incentive as well as anyone and know the hand will open when the need is real, whatever was said before.
Does what a rational recipient does: treats the guarantee as the guarantee it is, plans around the help rather than against it, and arrives at each brink a little less prepared, the reliance sound at every step and the maker of the need it meets.

## Shadow

Not a shirker, but someone slowly hollowed by a comfort they did not choose and cannot refuse alone, and that is the ache: they can feel their own footing thinning, half-know they would stand straighter if the net were gone, and cannot rationally build against a rescue that will come regardless.
What wears on them is the loss of a capacity they never decided to give up. Each covered risk is a muscle unbuilt, and though no single choice to lean was wrong, the sum is a person less able than they might have been, dependent by increments they can see in hindsight and could not have refused in the moment without absorbing a fall no reasonable person absorbs by choice.
The bind is interior and quietly diminishing: to build their own footing while the rescue stands is to pay for a strength they will not need, so the rational thing and the enfeebling thing are the same thing, and they cannot want the net gone, because in any single storm they are glad of it, only sense, over time, that its always being there is why they never grew tall enough to do without it.

## Tell

Says "why would I prepare for something they will never actually let happen to me?"
Is exactly right and is the sound of the trap closing, because the rescue really will come and preparing against it really is waste, so the sentence that reads as sensible planning is the precise mechanism of the under-provision, a rational mind arranging itself around a guarantee and calling the result, correctly, good sense.
