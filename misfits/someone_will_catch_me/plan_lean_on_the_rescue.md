# Plan: Lean on the Rescue

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Reliance](position_the_reliance.md), inside [Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Reliance](position_the_reliance.md)

## Direction

Read the rescue as the guarantee it is, and provision only against the risks it does not cover, since building a footing the catch will make unnecessary is paying for a strength you will not use (Kornai; Bruce and Waldman). Do not lay by against a fall another has undertaken to break, and discount the warning that next time will be different, because the helper's ex-post incentive is legible and the hand will open when the need is real. Accept that this is sound economy and that its sum is a self-sufficiency slowly surrendered, each covered risk a footing left unbuilt, the reliance right in every instance and the maker, over cycles, of the need it meets.

## Orders

([The Sheltered](persona_the_sheltered.md) builds less of their own footing because the catch is certain, under-saving and under-bracing against covered risks, and discounts the empty threat as the noise it is.)

## Implementation

([The Hammock](place_the_hammock.md); through the [relying](process_relying.md) process; the Sheltered leans on [the rescue](piece_the_rescue.md) and lets [the unbuilt](piece_the_unbuilt.md) go unbuilt, reading [the empty threat](piece_the_empty_threat.md) correctly and settling into what [the soft heart](piece_the_soft_heart.md) reliably provides.)

## Targets

- [ ] provision is made only against the risks the rescue does not cover
- [ ] no footing is built against a fall another has agreed to break
- [ ] the threat to withhold is discounted as the bluff it is
- [ ] the surrender of self-sufficiency is accepted as the sum of sound choices
