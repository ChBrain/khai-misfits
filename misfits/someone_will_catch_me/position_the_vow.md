# Position: The Vow

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The binding seat, which ties its own hands against the ex-post pull to help so the threat to withhold becomes real, and pays for the credibility with the suffering it must then allow.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A rule to withhold, and a binding that makes it stick.
The seat holds a precommitment against its own future compassion, a hand tied in advance so that when the brink comes it cannot open, giving the threat to withhold the credibility a mere warning lacks.
Foregone discretion as its instrument, since the seat works only by removing its own freedom to relieve, buying a binding no at the price of the option to say yes.

## Orders

Bind yourself before the moment, not in it.
Make the commitment to withhold in advance and in a form you cannot revise when the suffering is in front of you, since the whole power of the seat is that it has taken the ex-post choice off the table.
Hold the line when the brink comes, since a vow abandoned at the first real need is no vow, and the credibility the seat exists to create dies the instant it is seen to bend.

## Loses

The freedom to relieve present suffering, which the seat trades away to make its refusal believed.
The Vow can restore the recipient's footing only by binding itself against the very act that defines a helper, so it forgoes, by design, the power to end a suffering it could end, holding to a no while a real need goes unmet in front of it. What the seat forfeits is the thing aid exists for: it must let a present, certain, relievable harm stand in order to keep a future, diffuse, unprovable incentive alive, so its whole discipline is the refusal of the good it is most moved to do, and because the harm it allows is visible and the capacity it builds is not, the seat is defensible only in a future it cannot show and looks, in every present moment, exactly like the cruelty it is trying not to be.

## Drives

Toward a credible no bought by a foregone yes,
and a present suffering allowed to stand for a future standing,
so the seat that alone can free the recipient is the seat that must refuse the help it exists to give, its credibility purchased with the very compassion it binds.
