# Plot: The Cry for Help

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Brink](place_the_brink.md), a person in real and immediate need.
Someone about to fall, a genuine suffering within reach of relief, and a helper who has the means and cannot look away.

## Action

[Rescuing](process_rescuing.md): the Samaritan steps in and relieves the suffering, the aid the correct response to a present harm and delivered whatever was resolved before.

## Stage

[The Samaritan](persona_the_samaritan.md) meets the need at [the Brink](place_the_brink.md), on [Help Regardless](plan_help_regardless.md), delivering [the rescue](piece_the_rescue.md) that ends the present harm, unable to stand by and right not to.
The relief is real and the gratitude earned, and nothing in the moment looks like anything but good.

## Tension

Relieving the suffering is the plainly decent thing, and the plainly decent thing sets the trap.
There is no error in the rescue: the need is real, the means are to hand, and helping is what a good person does. But by helping, and by being the kind who always will, the Samaritan confirms a guarantee, so the plot closes with the suffering relieved and the pattern set, the catch shown to be certain and its certainty, unseen, already teaching the recipient that the next fall need not be prepared for, the good deed correct in itself and the first turn of the trap.
