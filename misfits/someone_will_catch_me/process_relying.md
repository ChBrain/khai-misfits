# Process: Relying

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md). The recipient's rational under-provision, the building of less footing because the rescue is certain, the sound economy that leaves the need to recur.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Sheltered](persona_the_sheltered.md), at [the Hammock](place_the_hammock.md), reading the guaranteed rescue and provisioning only against the risks it does not cover.
Begins once the catch is known to be certain, when the recipient can see that a fall another will break is not a fall they must prepare for.

## Direction

Turns a certain rescue into an under-built footing.
Because provisioning against a covered risk is waste, the recipient builds only what the uncovered risks require and lets the rest go, so relying converts a guarantee into a rational reduction of one's own preparation, the footing left unbuilt because the ground beneath it is spoken for (Kornai; Bruce and Waldman).

## Lever

The soundness of not paying for what you will not need.
Because a strength you will never use is a cost with no return, relying works through plain economy: there is no reason to bear the expense of a footing the rescue makes unnecessary, so the lever is the same good sense that governs any prudent arrangement around a sure thing, which is exactly why the under-provision feels like nothing but wisdom.

## Echo

A footing unbuilt, and a need made likelier to return.
The echo of relying is a recipient less prepared than they would have been unaided, so the next brink finds them nearer the edge, the sound economy of leaning on a certain catch quietly shaping the recurrence of the very need the catch exists to meet.
