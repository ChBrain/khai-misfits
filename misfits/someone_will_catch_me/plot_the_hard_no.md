# Plot: The Hard No

## Taxonomy

[Someone Will Catch Me](play_someone_will_catch_me.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Cold Shoulder](place_the_cold_shoulder.md), someone willing to bind against their own heart.
A helper who has understood that only a credible refusal frees the recipient to stand, and who ties their hands in advance so the no cannot be taken back when the brink comes.

## Action

[Vowing](process_vowing.md): the Hard Hand precommits and holds, letting the real need go unmet so the recipient meets the fall and, with it, the reason to build.

## Stage

[The Hard Hand](persona_the_hard_hand.md) binds [the soft heart](piece_the_soft_heart.md) with a vow and refuses [the rescue](piece_the_rescue.md) at [the Cold Shoulder](place_the_cold_shoulder.md), on [Withhold the Hand](plan_withhold_the_hand.md), bearing the sight of a suffering they could relieve so [the unbuilt](piece_the_unbuilt.md) is built at last.
They are called cruel by everyone who sees the fall, and cannot show the standing it is meant to buy.

## Tension

Freeing the recipient to stand means inflicting the very harm the help exists to prevent, and there is no version of it that is both kind and effective.
The remedy is real: a credible no returns the incentive to build a footing, and only a binding vow makes the no credible. But the binding works by allowing a present, certain, relievable suffering to stand, and by requiring the helper to war against their own compassion, and the capacity it buys is future and unprovable, so the plot closes with the choice laid bare and no gentle way through it: bear the fall and the charge of cruelty for a standing no one will trace back to the refusal, or let the heart win, open the hand, and keep the one you love dependent, the cure indistinguishable in the moment from the coldness it is not.
