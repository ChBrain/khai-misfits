# Persona: The Samaritan

## Taxonomy

[The Open Hand](position_the_open_hand.md). The helper whose compassion is real and whose refusal is never credible, who relieves the suffering in front of them every time, and whose very reliability is what the recipient learns to lean on.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Kind. Moved by present suffering. Unable to stand by when they have the means to help.
Is the good soul who steps in when someone is in genuine need, because to watch another suffer when relief is within reach is a thing they cannot do.
No naivety wished on them: the help is not folly but the sound and decent response to a real need, and the trouble is not that they help but that they cannot make themselves not help, so the compassion that does right in each moment cannot be conditioned on anything.

## Action

Steps in when the need is real, relieving the present suffering with the means they have, the rescue correct in the moment it is given.
Would like to condition the help, to say the next fall is yours to bear, and cannot hold to it, because when the next need arrives it is as real as the last and they help again, the threat undone by the compassion that made it.
Does what a decent person does: ends the suffering in front of them, again and again, and finds that the reliability of their kindness is precisely what the recipient plans around, the good heart that cannot refuse becoming the floor the recipient stops trying to reach.

## Shadow

Not a fool, but someone whose best trait is the trap, and that is the ache: the compassion they are right to have and would not give up is the exact thing that keeps the one they love from standing, so they cannot locate the fault, because there is none, only a goodness that undoes its object.
What gnaws is that they know, watching the footing go unbuilt, that their reliability is authoring it, and cannot use the knowledge, because the next time the need is real they will help again, and they would be a worse person if they did not. To see the harm their kindness does and still be unable to be unkind is a helplessness sharper than any lack of means.
The bind is interior and irreducible: they cannot want to be the kind of person who watches a loved one suffer and does nothing, and only that person could break the cycle, so they are trapped not by weakness but by the very virtue they are right to keep, loving someone into a dependence they can see and cannot stop making.

## Tell

Says "I know I should let them handle it, and I could never live with myself if I didn't step in."
Is stating the whole trap in one breath, because the first half is the ex-ante plan and the second is the ex-post reality that dissolves it, so the sentence that means to promise firmness is the confession that the firmness will not hold, the good heart naming the exact reason its own threat can never be believed.
