# Piece: The Pooling Price

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md). One price for good and bad alike, set at the average.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the pool](place_the_pool.md), where the whole market is priced as one.
The single number a buyer will pay for a unit they cannot judge: not the worth of this thing, which is unknown, but the worth of an average thing, which is all they can reason about.

## Load Bearing

Without it each unit fetches its own worth and the good and the bad are paid apart.
With it the good and the bad are paid the same, and being paid the same is the whole injury: the Pooling Price is the market's rational response to hidden quality, one price for the pool, and it is a discount to the cherry and a premium to the lemon at exactly the same moment. It is fair in the only way a blind market can be, and unfair in the way that matters, because it pays for the average and the average is not what anyone is holding.

## Apparent

The market rate.
It looks like the fair going price, what these things sell for, quoted with a straight face. It is fair for an average unit. It is a robbery of every unit better than average and a gift to every unit worse, and the market cannot tell which is which to correct it.

## Yearbook

Quoted by [the averager](persona_the_averager.md), who prices the pool because the units cannot be priced.
It is the hinge the play swings on: a fair price for the average is an unfair price for the good, so [the cherry](piece_the_cherry.md) will not take it and [the lemon](piece_the_lemon.md) will, and the pool the price was averaged from begins, that instant, to change.
