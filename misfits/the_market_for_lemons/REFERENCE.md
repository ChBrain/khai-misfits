# The Market for Lemons: Reference

The research warrant for [The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md). The title
is Akerlof's; the play runs on the information-economics literature on asymmetric information,
adverse selection, and its remedies cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging adverse selection as a **systemic system**: a market that destroys its own quality
without a single unfair act, because buyers cannot observe quality and so can pay only for the
average, and a single average price drives the good out and keeps the bad in. The play models
the documented mechanics, quality hidden on one side of the deal, a pooling price for unlike
units, the good withdrawing rather than sell below worth, the market unravelling round by round,
and the costly signalling or certification that alone can restore it, as the load-bearing vectors
of the structure, not as a tale of fraud. The governing finding of the source literature is the
play's governing law: when quality is unobservable to buyers, price reflects the average, which
overpays the bad and underpays the good, so the good exit and the average falls, driving out the
next best, and the market runs one direction, down, toward the worst it contains.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a fraud, because the source finding is precisely that no fraud is needed: the good seller
  hides nothing, the bad one need not lie, the buyer who cannot verify quality is right to discount,
  and the good seller is right to withdraw. The misfit is that a fair price for the average is a
  robbery of the good.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the market healing itself. Certification
  and signalling can restore trade, but only from outside the naive deal and only at a deadweight
  cost paid on every trade, so the reckoning is a market taxed to see what a seller always knew for
  free, not a clean recovery.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Hidden Quality](piece_the_hidden_quality.md)**: carries asymmetric information (Akerlof), quality
  known to the seller and unobservable to the buyer.
- **[The Pooling Price](piece_the_pooling_price.md)**: carries the pooling equilibrium (Akerlof), one price
  for good and bad because the units cannot be told apart.
- **[The Cherry](piece_the_cherry.md)**: carries the good driven out, the above-average unit that withdraws
  rather than sell for the average.
- **[The Discount](piece_the_discount.md)**: carries the buyer's rational markdown for unverifiable quality,
  the caution that falls hardest on the good.
- **[The Lemon](piece_the_lemon.md)**: carries the bad retained, the below-average unit overpaid by the
  pooling price and glad to stay.
- **[The Adverse Selection](piece_the_adverse_selection.md)**: carries adverse selection itself (Akerlof),
  the pool sorting toward the worst with no sorter.
- **[The Unraveling](piece_the_unraveling.md)**: carries the unravelling dynamic (Wilson), price and quality
  spiralling down together round by round.
- **[The Lemons Market](piece_the_lemons_market.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Akerlof), and its
  remedy at a cost (Spence; Rothschild & Stiglitz), a market that must buy back its own trust.
