# Plot: Plan

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The lot](place_the_lot.md), a good unit and a bad one for sale side by side.
Each is at [the listing](place_the_listing.md), described in the same words, and only the seller of each knows which is which. The buyer cannot tell them apart.

## Action

[Listing](process_listing.md): [The Honest](persona_the_honest.md), holding [the good seller's seat](position_good_seller.md), lists a genuinely good unit and asks a fair price for it.
[Pooling](process_pooling.md): [The Averager](persona_the_averager.md) at [the price-setter's seat](position_price_setter.md), unable to price a unit that cannot be judged, prices the pool at the average.

## Stage

[The Honest](persona_the_honest.md) tables [Ask a Fair Price](plan_ask_a_fair_price.md): name what the good thing is worth, over [the hidden quality](piece_the_hidden_quality.md) the buyer cannot see.
[The Averager](persona_the_averager.md) tables [Split the Difference](plan_split_the_difference.md): set one number for good and bad alike, and [the pooling price](piece_the_pooling_price.md) forms, fair to the average and to nothing actually on the lot.

## Tension

Nothing here is dishonest.
The good seller is hiding nothing, the bad seller need not lie, and the buyer who cannot verify quality is right not to pay top price for a coin flip. But a single price for unlike things is a discount to the good and a premium to the bad in the same breath, and the moment it is quoted, the pool it averaged begins to change.
