# Piece: The Adverse Selection

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md). The pool selecting itself toward the bad, the good priced out and the bad priced in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Across [the pool](place_the_pool.md), in who chooses to stay and who chooses to go.
Not a sorting anyone does on purpose: just the sum of each seller's own decision, the good declining the average and the bad accepting it, leaving a pool that is worse than the one before.

## Load Bearing

Without it the market keeps a steady mix and the price finds a level.
With it the mix curdles, because the Adverse Selection is the standing fact that a single price sorts by worth in exactly the wrong direction: the price the market can offer is acceptable only to those worth less than it, so the market retains what it overpays and loses what it underpays. It is selection with no selector, an outcome no one chose emerging from choices everyone made well, and it points one way, toward the bad, because the good have somewhere better to be and the bad do not.

## Apparent

A market finding its level.
It looks like ordinary sorting, buyers and sellers settling into a price, the market doing what markets do. What it is settling toward is the bottom, because the level a blind price finds is the level of the worst, not the average, once the better have been given a reason to leave.

## Yearbook

Enacted by no one and produced by [the honest](persona_the_honest.md) leaving and [the passer](persona_the_passer.md) staying.
It is the mechanism the play is named around: the good are not defrauded, they are selected out by a fair price that suits them least, so the market ends up adversely selected, holding the units it wanted to avoid and having driven off the ones it wanted to keep.
