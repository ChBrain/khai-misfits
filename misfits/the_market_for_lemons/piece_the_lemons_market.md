# Piece: The Lemons Market

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md). The accumulated whole: a market emptied of its good by a fair price.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

The gap between what the market could have traded and what it does.
On one side, all the good units that exist, held now by people who would not sell them here. On the other, a lot of lemons at a lemon price. The Lemons Market is the standing distance between the goods and the market that drove them off.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of fair deals and a soft patch in prices.
With it those are one object: a market that destroyed its own quality without a single unfair act, held whole by nobody, because the buyer who discounted never met the seller who withdrew, and no seat was answerable for the pool as a whole. It is Akerlof's result made flesh: information on one side only, a fair price on the other, and between them a market that unravels to its worst, not because anyone cheated but because no one could see.

## Apparent

A cheap market.
From any single trade it looks fine, even good: low prices, willing sellers, fair deals all round. The trap is invisible from within because nothing failed in any transaction, only in the sum, where the best goods are all missing and no one can point to the trade that lost them.

## Yearbook

The market built it in good faith: [the hidden quality](piece_the_hidden_quality.md) unseen, [the pooling price](piece_the_pooling_price.md) set, [the cherry](piece_the_cherry.md) withdrawn, [the discount](piece_the_discount.md) deepened.
[The stamp](persona_the_stamp.md) can rebuild a thinner market by making quality visible, at a cost everyone now pays for the fog, or no signal is trusted and the lot stands empty. Nobody had a name for what the market had become ... until the day an honest seller could not get a fair price at any of it.
