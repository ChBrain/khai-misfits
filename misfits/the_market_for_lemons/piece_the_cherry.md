# Piece: The Cherry

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md). The above-average unit, worth more than it can prove.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Leaving by [the exit](place_the_exit.md), listed and then withdrawn.
The genuinely good thing: the well-kept car, the solid codebase, the strong hire, held by someone who knows its worth and cannot make the buyer see it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the market is only lemons and there was never anything to lose.
With it there is something good on offer and a reason it cannot stay, because the Cherry is worth more than the pooling price and its owner is not a fool: offered the average for an above-average thing, the rational move is to withdraw, keep it, sell it elsewhere, hold out for a market that can see. So the good does not get cheated into selling low, it declines to sell at all, and its leaving is the most reasonable act in the play and the most damaging.

## Apparent

A seller being difficult.
It looks like someone overvaluing their thing, refusing a fair offer, walking away from a market rate. From the buyer's blind side it is stubbornness. From the seller's it is simple arithmetic: the offer is below worth, so there is no deal.

## Yearbook

Owned by [the honest](persona_the_honest.md), who asks a fair price for a good thing and is offered an average one.
It is the piece that makes the market bleed quality: every Cherry that withdraws lowers the average of what remains, so [the adverse selection](piece_the_adverse_selection.md) is not the good being tricked out of the market but priced out of it, one reasonable refusal at a time.
