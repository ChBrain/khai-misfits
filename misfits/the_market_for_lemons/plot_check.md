# Plot: Check

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The pool](place_the_pool.md), worse this round than last, because the good have gone.
The average has dropped, so the buyer discounts again, so the next-best sellers face the same insult the first did. At [the inspection](place_the_inspection.md), the buyer tries to tell good from bad and cannot.

## Action

[Unraveling](process_unraveling.md): [The Squint](persona_the_squint.md), holding [the appraiser's seat](position_appraiser.md), kicks the tyres and still cannot reliably read the hidden quality, so the discount stands.
Alongside, [The Voucher](persona_the_voucher.md) at [the signaller's seat](position_signaller.md) offers a costly warranty to prove a unit is good, and is only half believed.

## Stage

[The Squint](persona_the_squint.md) tables [Kick the Tyres](plan_kick_the_tyres.md): try to verify by inspection, and fails, because [the lemon](piece_the_lemon.md) looks like everything else on the lot.
[The Voucher](persona_the_voucher.md) tables [Prove It](plan_prove_it.md): back the unit with a guarantee, but a promise a lemon could also make is not trusted, so [the adverse selection](piece_the_adverse_selection.md) grinds on, the good priced out round after round.

## Tension

Everyone is still acting well, and the pool keeps rotting.
The appraiser is diligent, the warranty is sincere, the discount is fair, and none of it stops the slide, because inspection cannot see what is hidden and a signal a liar could copy proves nothing. So the market unravels a tier at a time, each round shedding its best remaining unit, until what is left on the lot is a lemon at a lemon price.
