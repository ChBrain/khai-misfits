# Plot: Stamp

## Taxonomy

[The Market for Lemons](play_the_market_for_lemons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The lot](place_the_lot.md), unravelled, mostly lemons, the good long gone.
The market on its own has reached its floor, so the only way back is to make quality visible from outside the deal: a trusted stamp that a buyer will believe when a seller's word they will not.

## Action

[Certification](process_certification.md): [The Stamp](persona_the_stamp.md), holding [the certifier's seat](position_certifier.md), imposes a third-party check that a lemon cannot pass and a cherry can, so quality can finally be seen at the point of sale.

## Stage

[The Stamp](persona_the_stamp.md) tables [Stamp It](plan_stamp_it.md): certify the good so buyers will pay for it, and the good can return, at a price that now carries the cost of the certificate.
[The unraveling](piece_the_unraveling.md) halts, and [the lemons market](piece_the_lemons_market.md) is named at last: a market that had to buy back the trust it lost, paying a deadweight toll on every trade to see what a seller always knew for free.

## Tension

Nobody was cheated, and the fix is a tax on the fog.
The certification works, quality is visible, the good come back, and none of it is free: every trade now pays for the stamp that stands in for the trust the market could not keep, so the market is repaired at a permanent cost it did not need before the asymmetry drove the good away ... or no stamp is trusted, the fog holds, and the lot stays empty of everything but lemons.
