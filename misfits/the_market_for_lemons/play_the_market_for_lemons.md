# Play: The Market for Lemons

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Market for Lemons.
A marketplace where the seller knows what a thing is worth and the buyer cannot tell, so every good and every bad one fetches the same middling price ... and the good, refusing to sell for the average, leave, until the average is all that is left, and then it leaves too.

## Arc

The bend is this: everyone pays a fair price, and the good disappear.
It runs on two lines that pull each other down.
One line is the price a buyer will offer, which can only be for the average, because the buyer cannot tell a good unit from a bad one and will not pay top money for a coin flip.
The other line is the quality on offer, and it falls as the price does, because a single average price is generous to the bad and an insult to the good, so the good withdraw rather than sell for less than they are worth, and the bad stay, delighted.
Every round the good leave, the pool that remains is worse, so the fair average drops, so the next-best leave, and the market unravels, price and quality spiralling down together, until what trades is only lemons, and a buyer who assumes a lemon will not pay even for that.
Make the quality visible and the good can name their price and stay: the average is the story.

## Triggers

**[Pooling Price](plot_pooling_price.md)**
A good unit and a bad one come to market, and only the seller knows which is which. The buyer cannot verify, so a single price forms for the pool, at the average. The Honest lists a cherry, the Averager sets one price for all. Closes when good and bad are priced the same.

**[Cherry Withdraws](plot_cherry_withdraws.md)**
Cue: the buyer discounts for the doubt. The good seller, offered the average for an above-average unit, withdraws rather than be robbed, and the lemon seller stays, glad of the price. Closes when the good exit and the pool tilts toward lemons.

**[Adverse Selection](plot_adverse_selection.md)**
Cue: the good are gone, so the pool is worse, so the price falls again. The appraiser cannot reliably tell good from bad, and a signaller's costly warranty is only half believed. Closes when the pool is mostly lemons and the price is a lemon price.

**[Stamp](plot_stamp.md)**
Cue: someone imposes a trusted third-party certification, making quality visible at last. The good can return, at a price that now includes the cost of proving they are good. Closes when the lemons market is named ... or no signal is trusted, and the market empties.

## Stakes

Whether quality is ever made visible before the good give up.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward the good, toward the average. The production cannot make a buyer pay for a quality they cannot see. It can only show that no one was cheated, that the buyer discounted fairly and the good left rationally and the bad stayed sensibly, and that the one thing that would have saved the market, a way to tell good from bad, was the one thing the market, on its own, could not supply.
