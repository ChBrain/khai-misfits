# The New Normal: Reference

The research warrant for [The New Normal](play_the_new_normal.md). The title names the
outcome; the play runs on Vaughan's normalization of deviance and the safety-science
literature on drift, from the Challenger analysis through the studies of how accidents
incubate, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the normalization of deviance as a **systemic system**: a signal that crosses a
design limit is tolerated once because nothing breaks, then reclassified as acceptable, so
each later signal is judged against the last success rather than the original spec, and the
boundary of acceptable drifts outward until the deviation that was never allowed is routine
and finally fails. The play models the documented mechanics, the anomaly against the spec,
the success that reinforces it, the reclassification into experience, the baseline that
shifts, the failure that was always latent, and the reset that alone restores the line, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of recklessness. The governing
finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: risk is redefined by its own
survivals, so a standard erodes not by a decision to lower it but by a run of successes that
quietly move the line.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as reckless, because the source finding is precisely that the people are careful and the calls
  are data-driven: each reclassification rests on real evidence, the evidence that it flew, and
  proceeding on an acceptable risk is the responsible-looking move. The misfit is that the evidence is
  only ever the survivals, so the standard erodes by success and not by choice.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a lone catch. No single alarm stops the
  drift, because each signal really did survive; the one move that works is structural, an outside
  eye that measures against the spec and resets the line, and it costs the schedule the drift was
  buying. The system is not saved by vigilance alone; the baseline is reset, at a price.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the mechanism, the incubation account,
  and the drift model are theirs, and the condition normalization actually requires, a signal that
  survives and is judged by its survival, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

- **[The Baseline](piece_the_baseline.md)**: carries the drifted boundary (Rasmussen; Vaughan), the new normal
  each later signal is judged against instead of the spec.
- **[The Failure](piece_the_failure.md)**: carries the latent catastrophe released (Turner), the day the
  normalized deviation does what it always could.
- **[The Reset](piece_the_reset.md)**: carries the remedy (Feynman), the outside eye measuring against the
  spec and restoring the line, at the cost of the schedule.
- **[The Normalization](piece_the_normalization.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Vaughan; Dekker), the
  standard eroded by its own survivals, and its remedy in a reset baseline.
