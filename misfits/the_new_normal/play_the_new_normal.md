# Play: The New Normal

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The New Normal.
An anomaly appears that was never supposed to, and nothing bad happens ... so it is rated acceptable, and the next one is measured against it and not against the spec, and success by success the line of what is normal creeps out to meet the thing that will finally fail.

## Arc

The bend is this: tolerate a deviation once and it becomes the standard the next one is judged against.
It runs on a split between the original limit and the last thing that worked.
A signal crosses the line the design drew, and the system flies anyway, and the flight is a success. So the signal is reclassified: not a failure, within experience, acceptable. The next signal is larger, and it is measured against the last success rather than the spec, and it flies too. The boundary of acceptable is redrawn to wherever the evidence last stopped, and the evidence is only ever the times it did not break yet.
Each decision is data-driven and locally sound, and the baseline drifts the whole time, until the deviation that was never allowed is routine and one day does what it always could. Reset the line to the spec, treat the signal as a signal, and the drift stops: what you accept as normal once is what you will measure everything against.

## Triggers

**[The Signal](plot_the_signal.md)**
An anomaly appears that the design said should not: erosion past the line, a reading out of spec. The Engineer flags it and asks to stop. Closes when the concern is on the table, against a schedule that wants to proceed.

**[The Reclassification](plot_the_reclassification.md)**
Cue: the system flew anyway and nothing broke. The Analyst rates the anomaly within experience and the Manager proceeds. Closes when the signal is booked acceptable and the success is banked as proof.

**[The Drift](plot_the_drift.md)**
Cue: the next signal is larger, and it is measured against the last success, not the spec. The Operator runs it again and the Veteran vouches that it always holds. Closes when the boundary of acceptable has crept so far that the original limit is a forgotten number.

**[The Failure](plot_the_failure.md)**
Cue: the normalized deviation finally does what it always could. The Investigator reconstructs the drift and finds no single wrong call, only a line that moved. Closes when the failure is traced to the normalization ... or blamed on one bad part, and the baseline holds where it drifted to.

**[The Reset](plot_the_reset.md)**
Cue: someone treats the signal as a signal again and measures it against the spec, not the last success. The Outsider resets the baseline and halts the schedule to do it. Closes when the line is restored to the design limit ... or the reset is judged too costly and the drift resumes.

## Stakes

Whether a limit is ever measured against its spec, or only ever against the last time it held.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the most recent success, which each new signal is judged against, and away from the original line, which no one has looked at in years. The production cannot make a system fail on a schedule, so every deviation looks survivable right up to the one that is not. It can only show that every call was data-driven and locally sound, that no one chose to court disaster, and that the single thing that would have held the line, treating a signal as a signal and measuring it against the spec, is the discipline that each quiet success made easier to drop.
