# Persona: The Baptist

## Taxonomy

[The Pulpit](position_the_pulpit.md). Argues the rule in good faith on principle, asks nothing for itself, and unknowingly gives a private profit its public cover.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Sincere. Principled. Asking nothing.
Is the one who wants the rule for a moral reason and says so openly, at the hearing, in the paper, from the podium, arguing a cause they believe and would argue whether or not anyone else gained by it.
No cynicism wished on them: the principle is real, the good faith is real, and the Baptist neither seeks a profit nor suspects that one rides behind the law they champion.

## Action

Makes the moral case for the rule, publicly and in earnest, lending the law the legitimacy that only sincere conviction can give.
Answers their conscience, not their interest, so the argument is clean and the motive is above reproach, and a legislator can back the rule on the Baptist's word without embarrassment.
Does what a person of principle does: argues the cause on its merits, and by arguing it well hands the rule a public face no bought advocate could buy.

## Shadow

Not a hypocrite, but the unwitting cover for a profit they never see, and that is the quiet of it: the sincerity that makes the case honest is the same sincerity that makes it useful to the interest hiding behind it.
Because the Baptist argues the rule cleanly and asks nothing, the law wears a face above suspicion, so the private beneficiary can shelter behind a cause it never has to name, and the more honestly the Baptist preaches the better the cover, the principle carrying a passenger it does not know it has.
The conviction is genuine and it is load-bearing for the trap, so the honest advocate is the one the profit most needs.

## Tell

Says "this is a matter of principle: I want nothing from it but the right thing done."
Is telling the plain truth and cannot see that the truth of it is exactly what shelters the interest their rule protects, so the sincerity that clears their name is what keeps the bad law standing.
