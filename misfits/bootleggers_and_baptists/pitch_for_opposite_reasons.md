# Pitch: For Opposite Reasons

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Civic, principled, above-board.
The plain sense that the rule has a good case and a real constituency: the moralists argue it sincerely in the open, the law answers a genuine concern, and a regulation with honest believers behind it is a regulation with legitimacy, exactly what a public debate is supposed to produce.
Nothing underhanded in it: the principle is meant, the hearing is public, and the case for the rule is made in good faith by people who ask nothing for themselves.

## Undertow

Under the good-faith case, a second backer who never speaks.
Because the same rule that honours the principle also shelters a private profit, a quiet interest gains from the law and spends to keep it, riding hidden inside a moral case it never has to join, so the rule carries two constituencies who want it for reasons that never touch and a legislator who would repeal it faces conviction and cash at once.
The law that reads as a principle upheld is a principle with a passenger, its public face sincere and its staying power bought, immovable not for being good but for being defended from two sides that never had to meet.

## Nerve

The feeling of a rule too principled to question, a cause argued so sincerely it must be pursued for its own sake,
because the believers are honest and the hearing is open and no one at the podium is asking for a cent,
while underneath a private interest banks the same law it never names, and the sincerity of the one backer becomes the cover for the profit of the other.

## Echo

After the run: a rule that serves a hidden interest kept in force by the honesty of the people who back it for better reasons, a bad law made permanent by a coalition no one convened,
no conspiracy in the story and no cynic, only a sincere principle and a rational profit converging on the same statute,
and the one move that would break it, naming the profit behind the principle, refused because it calls the honest believer a front and the whole coalition closes ranks, so the rule holds and the two keep it from opposite sides without ever having to agree.
