# Plot: The Overlap

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

Between [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md) and [the market](place_the_market.md), one rule wanted by a principle and a profit for reasons that never touch.
A sincere case and a quiet earning that happen to need the same law.

## Action

[Covering](process_covering.md): the two constituencies converge on the same rule without meeting, and the moral case becomes the interest's shelter.

## Stage

[The principle](piece_the_principle.md) argues [the rule](piece_the_rule.md) in public and [the profit](piece_the_profit.md) funds it in private, so [the cover](piece_the_cover.md) stretches from one to the other and [the coalition](piece_the_coalition.md) forms though no one convened it, the moralist and the interest each served by the same law and neither needing the other.
No arrangement is made, and none is needed: wanting the same thing is enough.

## Tension

The principle and the profit want the same rule, and wanting the same rule is all it takes to ally them.
There is no meeting: neither side coordinates with or acknowledges the other, and each pursues its own reason in good faith or plain interest. But one law serves both, so the plot closes with a coalition formed by no one, the profit riding hidden inside the principle and the principle none the wiser, an alliance that is real precisely because it was never arranged.
