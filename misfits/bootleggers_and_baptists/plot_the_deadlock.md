# Plot: The Deadlock

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Statute](place_the_statute.md), a legislator weighing whether the rule stands or falls.
A room where principle presses from the podium and profit from the back room, and a public too diffuse to press at all.

## Action

[Entrenching](process_entrenching.md): repeal would cost votes and resources at once, so the responsive legislator keeps the rule.

## Stage

[The Legislator](persona_the_legislator.md), on [Keep the Rule](plan_keep_the_rule.md), reads the room and keeps [the rule](piece_the_rule.md) because [the coalition](piece_the_coalition.md) defends it from both sides, the honest accounting overridden by a balance of pressure the two constituencies have stacked.
The read is sound and takes no bribe, and it keeps a law its own cost-benefit would fail.

## Tension

Keeping a rule defended by principle and money and barely opposed is the sound political read, and the sound read makes the bad law permanent.
There is no corruption: the legislator holds no view of their own and answers the pressures the room presents, which is what a representative does. But the room was stacked by a coalition no one convened, so the plot closes with a rule kept for its constituencies and not its merits, immovable because attacking it offends the honest and threatens the interested at once, and the one winning move against it, naming the profit behind the principle, is the one that insults the sincere along with the sheltered.
