# Piece: The Coalition

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The phenomenon whole, the tacit two-sided alliance no one convened that holds the bad rule immovable.

## Place

Runs across [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md), [the market](place_the_market.md), and [the statute](place_the_statute.md) as the whole structure, the principle speaking, the profit paying, and the rule kept.
Flows through the entire play, from the two constituencies wanting one rule to the immovable law they leave behind, the alliance seen entire.

## Load Bearing

Without it there are only parts: a rule, a principle, a profit, a cover, with no name for the force that binds them into a durable law.
With it the parts become one alliance: a sincere principle and a quiet profit want the same rule for reasons that never touch, so the moral case shelters the interest and the interest funds the case, and together they defend the law from opposite flanks against a public too diffuse to press. The Coalition is load bearing because it is the phenomenon entire (Yandle; Becker): the two-sided alliance formed by no one, convened by no meeting, that makes a rule no honest accounting would keep politically immovable, so the piece carries the trap whole, a bad law held in place by conviction and cash at once and dissolvable by neither alone.

## Apparent

Just a rule with support, a law that stands because people are for it and few against.
Seen at the surface, the Coalition looks like ordinary democratic weight, a regulation kept because it has a constituency, and the truth that the constituency is two interests that never met, that a principle and a profit converged on one law without arrangement, that a bad rule is made permanent by an alliance no one formed, stays hidden behind how much a defended law looks like a wanted one.
