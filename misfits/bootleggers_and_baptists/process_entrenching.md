# Process: Entrenching

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The rule made immovable, the legislator reading a room where principle and profit defend the same law at once.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Legislator, reading the balance of pressure the coalition has stacked.
At [the statute](place_the_statute.md), the seat weighs repeal and finds the rule defended by the moralists' votes and the profiteers' resources together, and opposed only by a public too diffuse to press.

## Direction

Toward a durable bad rule.
Because principle and profit push the same law from both flanks, entrenching points the outcome at permanence: any move to repeal meets outrage and a war chest at once, so the responsive legislator keeps a rule its own cost-benefit would fail, and the law outlasts every honest accounting because touching it is doubly expensive and leaving it costs nothing.

## Lever

A repeal that is doubly costly, against an opposition too thin to organise.
Because the coalition defends the rule with conviction and cash while its cost falls diffusely on a public that cannot mobilise, entrenching runs on the plain political arithmetic that a rule cheap to keep and costly to touch is a rule kept, so no corruption is needed to make the law permanent, only a legislator answering the pressures a stacked room presents.

## Echo

A bad law made immovable, held by a coalition no one convened.
What leaves this process is a rule that endures on its constituencies and not on its merits, kept by a legislator who took no bribe and read the room correctly, so the sum of a sincere principle, a quiet profit, and a responsive seat is a law whose costs exceed its benefits and which the ordinary workings of politics will not repeal.
