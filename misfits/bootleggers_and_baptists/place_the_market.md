## Holds

Here the rule's real effect lands, so the Market holds the profit half of the coalition: the law that honours a principle also shelters a private earning, concentrated enough to be worth organising and paying to keep, so the Market is where the rule pays out and where the interest that banks it finds its reason to spend.

## Offers

A protected earning, and a reason to defend the law.
Because the rule shapes the trade in the interest's favour, the Market offers the Bootlegger a gain the law creates and the concentrated stake to fund its defence, handing out real profit, and in the same motion it offers that profit a place to stay hidden, since an earning banked in the market makes no speech at the hearing and needs the principle to argue for the rule it lives on.

## Withheld

Its own visibility, and any account of who the rule really pays.
The Market can hand the interest a protected gain, but it withholds that gain from the public view, so the profit the rule produces is felt as a price and never traced to the law that set it, and it withholds the tally that would connect the moral rule to the private income, so the earning stays quiet and the principle keeps the credit, the profit banked where no hearing looks.
