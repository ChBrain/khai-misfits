## Load Bearing

Without a private gain there is no muscle, and the rule would have only a sincere voice and no purse, easily repealed once the principle tired.
With it the rule has a resourced defender: the Profit is large and particular enough that the interest organises and pays to keep the law where the diffuse public will not. The Profit is load bearing because it is the muscle that makes the rule durable (Stigler; Olson): the concentrated earning that funds the defence, so the piece carries the interested half of the coalition, the quiet income that buys the law its staying power and never has to argue for it in its own name.

## Apparent

Just a price, or a market's shape, a cost felt by the public and traced to no particular law.
Seen at the surface, the Profit looks like the ordinary working of a market, a higher price or a thinner competition, and the truth that a rule created it, that a quiet interest banks it and spends it to keep the law, that the cost the public pays is the income the interest defends, stays hidden behind how naturally a price reads as a fact of the market rather than the product of a rule.
