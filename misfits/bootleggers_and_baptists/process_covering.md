# Process: Covering

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The principle and the profit converging on one rule without meeting, the moral case sheltering the private interest.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The same rule, wanted by the principle and the profit for reasons that never touch.
Between [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md) and [the market](place_the_market.md), the sincere case and the quiet earning land on one law, the moralist and the interest each served by it and neither aware of, or needing, the other.

## Direction

Toward a profit sheltered behind a principle.
Because one rule serves both, covering points the two constituencies at the same statute: the principle argues it in public and the profit funds it in private, so the moral case, made for its own reasons, becomes the interest's shelter, and the coalition forms as an overlap of aims rather than a meeting of parties, no arrangement made and none needed.

## Lever

One rule that pays two ways.
Because the law honours a conviction and shelters an earning at once, covering runs on coincidence of interest and not on collusion, so the two need neither coordinate nor acknowledge each other, the principle carrying the profit simply by wanting the same thing, and the cover free precisely because it was never bought.

## Echo

A coalition no one convened, and a rule with a sincere face over a private gain.
What leaves this process is a law backed from opposite sides that never met, the profit riding hidden inside the principle and the principle none the wiser, so the rule now carries both constituencies as one force, ready for the seat that will find it too defended to move.
