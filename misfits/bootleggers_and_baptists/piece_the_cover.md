# Piece: The Cover

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The legitimacy the principle lends the profit, the moral face that lets a private interest shelter behind a cause it never joins.

## Owner

## Place

Cast at [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md), where the sincere case is made, and used at [the market](place_the_market.md), where the interest banks behind it.
Flows from the principle to the profit as a borrowed respectability, the public face of the one becoming the shelter of the other.

## Load Bearing

Without it the profit would stand exposed, an interest arguing for a rule that pays it and losing on the merits to the public it costs.
With it the interest is safe: the Cover is the legitimacy the sincere case confers, so a private gain that could never win its own argument rides hidden inside a moral one that already has. The Cover is load bearing because it is what a naked interest cannot buy and a sincere principle gives for free (Yandle and Smith): the respectability of an honest cause extended, unknowingly, over a private profit, so the piece carries the shelter of the trap, the moral face that turns an indefensible interest into an unassailable rule.

## Apparent

Just legitimacy, the ordinary respect a sincerely argued rule deserves.
Seen at the surface, the Cover looks like nothing but earned credibility, a law that is respectable because its case was honest, and the truth that the same respectability shelters a private interest, that a sincere principle can extend its good name over a profit it never sees, that legitimacy meant for a cause becomes cover for an income, stays hidden behind how rightly an honest case is trusted.
