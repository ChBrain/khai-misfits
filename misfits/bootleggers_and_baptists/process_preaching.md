# Process: Preaching

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The moral case argued in good faith, giving the rule a public face and its legitimacy.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Baptist, with a principle and the standing to argue it.
At [the hearing](place_the_hearing.md), the case for the rule is made in earnest and in the open, the moralist asking nothing and arguing the cause on its merits.

## Direction

Toward a rule with a sincere public face.
Because the case is honest and made openly, preaching points the rule at legitimacy: a law argued in good faith by people who seek no gain reads as the will of honest citizens, so a legislator can back it without embarrassment and a public can endorse it without suspicion, the sincerity of the advocacy becoming the rule's credential.

## Lever

Sincerity, which no bought advocate can match.
Because the Baptist means the principle and asks nothing, preaching runs on a legitimacy that only genuine conviction confers, so the rule gains a public face precisely because its champion is disinterested, and the more honestly the case is argued the more unimpeachable the law appears, the good faith of the seat doing what money never could.

## Echo

A rule with a legitimacy it did not earn on the ledger, and a cover it did not know it cast.
What leaves this process is a law wearing an honest public face, argued so cleanly that its quiet beneficiary can shelter behind the cause, so the sincere case is handed on as the rule's credential and, unknowingly, as the profit's cover, the principle carrying a passenger into the next process.
