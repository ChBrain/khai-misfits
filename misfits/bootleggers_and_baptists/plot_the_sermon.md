# Plot: The Sermon

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Hearing](place_the_hearing.md), a rule on the table with a moral case to be made.
A Baptist who wants the rule for a principle and will argue it in the open, asking nothing.

## Action

[Preaching](process_preaching.md): the moral case is argued in good faith, and the rule gains a sincere public face.

## Stage

[The Baptist](persona_the_baptist.md), on [Preach the Ban](plan_preach_the_ban.md), argues [the rule](piece_the_rule.md) on [the principle](piece_the_principle.md), and the sincerity of the case lends the law [the cover](piece_the_cover.md) a private interest will shelter behind.
The conviction is genuine and asks for nothing, and that is exactly what makes it useful.

## Tension

Arguing a sincere principle in the open is the honest act, and the honesty is what casts the cover.
There is no cynicism: the Baptist means the cause and seeks no gain, and a sincere case argued cleanly deserves the legitimacy it earns. But the same sincerity makes the rule respectable enough for a profit to hide behind, so the plot closes with the law given an honest public face and, unknowingly, a shelter, the better the sermon the better the cover, and no hypocrisy anywhere in it.
