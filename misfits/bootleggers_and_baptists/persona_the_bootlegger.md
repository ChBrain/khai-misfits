# Persona: The Bootlegger

## Taxonomy

[The Back Room](position_the_back_room.md). Profits quietly from the rule and spends to keep it, never arguing for it in its own name and never meeting the Baptist.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Quiet. Interested. Out of the light.
Is the one the rule pays: the incumbent the licence shields, the grower the tariff enriches, the black-market seller the ban hands a monopoly, gaining from a law they need never praise aloud.
No malice wished on them: they do not manufacture the moral case or deceive the Baptist, they simply back a rule that helps them, as any interest backs its own advantage.

## Action

Gains from the rule and funds its defence, spending quietly to keep the law that protects their earning, and letting the moral case do the arguing in public.
Answers their interest, which the rule serves, so they organise and pay where a diffuse public will not, the concentrated beneficiary who shows up because the stakes for them are large and particular.
Does what a rational interest does: defends the advantage the law hands it, and does so from the back room, since a profit has no case it can make in its own name and needs none while the principle speaks.

## Shadow

Not a schemer, but a passenger the principle never sees, and that is the trap: the profit needs no plot, only a moral case already being argued for its own reasons that it can quietly ride.
Because the Bootlegger gains from the same rule the Baptist preaches, and need neither coordinate with the moralist nor be acknowledged by them, the interest shelters behind a conviction it never has to share, its money defending a law its name never touches, so the quiet profit is real and hidden and requires no deception to hide, the public case supplied sincerely by someone else.
The gain is rational and the cover is free, so the interest that would be exposed on its own is safe behind a principle it did not buy.

## Tell

Says nothing in public, and in private "the law suits me, and I will help it stay."
Is honest enough about their interest where it cannot be heard, and never has to lie in the open, because the Baptist's sincere case is already saying, for free, everything the profit needs said.
