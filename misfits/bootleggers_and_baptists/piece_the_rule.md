## Load Bearing

Without a rule that serves two masters at once there is no coalition, only a moralist and an interest with nothing between them.
With it the two have a single object: the Rule honours a moral concern and, in the same stroke, hands a private interest a protected gain, so it is the point on which principle and profit converge without meeting. The Rule is load bearing because it is the one law both sides back (Yandle): the Sabbath ban that honours the Sabbath and shelters the bootlegger, so the piece carries the shared object of the trap, the regulation whose two effects give it two constituencies who want it for reasons that never touch.

## Apparent

Just a principled law, a rule with a moral case and a public constituency behind it.
Seen at the surface, the Rule looks like the outcome of an honest debate, a regulation people wanted for good reasons, and the truth that the same law hands a quiet interest a protected gain, that its second effect is what buys its permanence, that a principled rule can be a private earning wearing a public face, stays hidden behind how much a law with sincere backers looks like a law worth keeping.
