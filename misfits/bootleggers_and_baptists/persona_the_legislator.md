# Persona: The Legislator

## Taxonomy

[The Chamber](position_the_chamber.md). Keeps the rule because it carries both a moral constituency and a resourced one, so repeal is doubly costly.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Responsive. Vote-counting. Weighing the room.
Is the one who must decide whether the rule stands or falls, and reads the constituencies for and against it: the sincere believers who want it kept and the quiet money that wants the same.
No corruption wished on them: they need take no bribe and hold no view of their own, only respond to the pressures a rule attracts, which is what a representative is meant to do.

## Action

Keeps the rule, because moving it would cost votes to the moralists and resources to the profiteers, and keeping it costs neither.
Answers the balance of pressure, which favours a law defended by principle and money together against a diffuse public that feels the cost too thinly to organise, so the sound political read is to leave the rule where it is.
Does what a responsive legislator does: counts the constituencies, finds the rule doubly defended and barely opposed, and keeps it, no accounting of its actual costs required to make the call.

## Shadow

Not corrupt, but the seat where the coalition's two pressures become one immovable law, and that is the difficulty: keeping the rule is the rational response to a room the coalition has stacked from both sides.
Because principle pushes from the podium and profit pushes from the back room, the Legislator faces outrage and a war chest at once for any move to repeal, so the rational course is to keep a rule its own cost-benefit would fail, and the representative who merely answers the pressures in the room becomes the mechanism by which a bad law is made permanent.
The response is sound and the rule is bad, so the legislator who takes no bribe still keeps the law the bribe would have bought.

## Tell

Says "there is a real constituency for this rule, and no appetite to reopen it."
Is right about the room and cannot see that the room was stacked by a coalition no one convened, so the honest read of the pressures is exactly what holds the bad law in place.
