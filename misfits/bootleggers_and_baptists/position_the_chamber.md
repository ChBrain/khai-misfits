# Position: The Chamber

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The lawmaking seat, where the rule is kept because principle and profit defend it at once and the diffuse public barely opposes it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A rule to keep or repeal, and a room of pressures to read.
A representative's job: weigh who wants the law kept and who wants it gone, and answer the balance, the sincere believers and the quiet money on one side and a public that feels the cost too thinly to organise on the other.
No need of a bribe or a view of its own, since the seat has only to respond to the pressures a rule attracts.

## Orders

Keep the rule.
Leave the law where it is, because repeal would cost votes to the moralists and resources to the profiteers while keeping it costs neither, and answer the balance of pressure the room presents.
Count the constituencies, since a rule defended by principle and money and barely opposed is a rule a responsive seat keeps.

## Loses

The honest accounting, which the balance of pressure overrides.
Because principle pushes from the podium and profit from the back room, the Chamber meets outrage and a war chest at once for any move to repeal, so the rational course is to keep a rule its own cost-benefit would fail, and the seat that merely answers the room becomes the mechanism that makes a bad law permanent.
It reads the pressures soundly and loses the public interest, the representative that takes no bribe still keeping the law the bribe would have bought.

## Drives

Toward a rule kept for its constituencies,
and a cost no one weighs,
so the seat that responds to the room is the one the coalition's two pressures make into an immovable law.
