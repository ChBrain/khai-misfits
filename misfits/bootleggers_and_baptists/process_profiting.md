# Process: Profiting

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The private gain banked from the rule, the quiet muscle that funds its defence and never speaks for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Bootlegger, with a protected gain and a concentrated stake to defend it.
At [the market](place_the_market.md), the rule's real effect lands as an earning, the monopoly or the margin the law hands the interest, quietly collected.

## Direction

Toward a rule with a funded, invisible defence.
Because the gain is large and particular, profiting points the interest at organising: the concentrated beneficiary spends to keep the law that pays it while the diffuse public will not, so the rule acquires a resourced defender, and because a profit has no case fit for a podium, the interest funds and never argues, its muscle supplied and its face withheld.

## Lever

A concentrated stake against a diffuse cost.
Because the earning is worth much to the few and the rule's cost is spread thin across the many, profiting runs on the asymmetry that makes the interest show up where the public stays home, so no deception is needed to buy the rule its defence, only a benefit sharp enough to be worth defending and a cost too diffuse to be worth fighting.

## Echo

A rule with quiet money behind it, defended by an interest that never appears.
What leaves this process is a law with a resourced defender who makes no public case, the profit banked and the muscle ready, so the rule now has both a sincere voice and a silent purse, the interest's spending waiting to converge with the principle's speaking on the same statute.
