# Position: The Pulpit

## Taxonomy

[Bootleggers and Baptists](play_bootleggers_and_baptists.md). The moral seat, where the rule is argued in good faith on principle and given the public face that only sincerity can lend.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A principle, and the standing to argue it in the open.
A clean motive: the seat wants the rule for a moral reason and asks nothing for itself, so its case is above suspicion and its word can be taken at face value.
No knowledge of the passenger, since the Pulpit argues the cause on its merits and neither seeks nor sees the profit that the same rule shelters.

## Orders

Preach the ban.
Make the moral case for the rule, publicly and in earnest, and lend the law the legitimacy that a bought advocate never could.
Argue the principle on its merits, since the sincerity of the case is exactly what gives the rule its public face.

## Loses

Not its integrity, which stays whole, but its title to what that integrity buys.
A seat of moral authority puts legitimacy into the public record, and legitimacy once spoken is a thing anyone may draw on, so the Pulpit cannot meter who spends the credit its sincerity earns.
What it forfeits is ownership of its own good name: the respectability the seat builds is a resource it cannot keep to its cause, drawn down by an interest it never licensed and cannot see, so the seat gives away, in the plain act of speaking, exactly what it never meant to lend.

## Drives

Toward a rule with a sincere public face,
and a profit safe behind it,
so the seat that argues in good faith is the seat the interest most needs, its honesty the coalition's cover.
