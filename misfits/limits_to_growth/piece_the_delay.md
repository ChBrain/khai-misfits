# Piece: The Delay

## Taxonomy

[Limits to Growth](play_limits_to_growth.md). The lag between the engine pushed and the limit's pushback felt, the gap that hides the cause.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the dashboard](place_the_dashboard.md), where the pushback has not yet arrived in the numbers.
Between the drawing-down at the bottleneck and the stall that shows up later.

## Load Bearing

Without it the limit would be caught the moment it bound, and the reflex would never fire.
With it the cause and its effect come apart in time: the delay is the lag in the balancing loop, so the leak lands well after the load that opened it, and by then the drawing-down is out of sight and the stall reads as a dip. The Delay is load bearing because it is what makes the misreading honest (Forrester on delayed feedback): it keeps the limit out of the data while the decision is made, so the base rate stays intact, the reflex stays rational, and the wrong move is chosen by sound reasoning on a picture the lag has made obsolete.

## Apparent

A quiet interval where nothing is wrong.
A gap in which the growth still climbs and the pushback has not landed looks like proof the engine is fine, so the delay reads as calm, and its real work, carrying the limit's answer far enough from its cause that no one connects them, stays hidden inside the lag itself.

## Yearbook

Working through [misreading](process_misreading.md), the gap [Call It a Dip](plan_call_it_a_dip.md) reads across.
It is the lag that hides the cause: the Delay keeps [the limit](piece_the_limit.md) out of sight and turns [the stall](piece_the_stall.md) into a dip, so [the engine](piece_the_engine.md) is driven harder on a system already changed.
