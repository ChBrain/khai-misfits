# Limits to Growth: Reference

The research warrant for [Limits to Growth](play_limits_to_growth.md). The title is Senge's systems
archetype; the play runs on the system-dynamics account of a reinforcing loop meeting a balancing
loop through a delay, from Forrester and Meadows through the constraint literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the limits-to-growth archetype as a **systemic system**: a reinforcing engine that builds
real growth, a finite capacity it draws on, and a delay that keeps the two apart in time, so that
when the balancing loop binds and the growth stalls, the stall is read as a shortfall of the engine
and answered with more engine, which the bound limit turns into a widening leak rather than more
growth. The play models the documented mechanics, the reinforcing loop that made the success and
taught no other lever, the balancing loop of a finite capacity that governs the throughput once it
binds, the delay that hides the second loop from the first, the reflex to drive the engine harder,
the leak by which won growth is handed back, and the stall that fires the reflex, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of stupidity. The governing finding of the source literature
is the play's governing law: growth generated by a reinforcing process will slow or stop as a
balancing process reaches a limit, and the leverage lies not in pushing the engine harder but in
identifying and easing the limiting factor, so the intuitive response, more of what worked, is the
one that cannot work. It is a sibling of the house's Fixes that Fail and Shifting the Burden, and its
distinct engine: not a fix whose side effect feeds the problem, and not a symptomatic relief that
atrophies the fundamental, but a growth lever that stays right until a limit binds and stays reflex
long after, aimed at a factor it does not control.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  driver as foolish, because the source finding is precisely that driving the engine is rational: the
  engine genuinely made every past win, the slowdowns it drove through genuinely recovered, the delay
  genuinely keeps the limit out of the data, and the limit is genuinely nobody's fault. The misfit is
  that the move built on all that truth is exactly the move that cannot work once the binding factor
  has shifted to the limit.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless pivot. The one move that frees
  the growth is to work the limit rather than the engine, which means telling a winning team to leave
  the lever that made it and feed a thing not visibly broken, or to ease the engine before a limit
  that has not shown itself, since the same delay that fools the driver denies the restrainer any
  proof of which wall is real. The leverage is correct and unpersuasive, and easing early carries the
  symmetric error of a growth forgone to a ceiling that never came.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the archetype, the system-dynamics
  account, the law of the minimum, and the theory of constraints are theirs, and the condition the
  trap actually requires, a reinforcing engine and a balancing limit separated by a delay, is carried
  as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Engine](piece_the_engine.md)**: carries the reinforcing loop (Forrester; Senge), the growth process that
  made the success and taught the team its only lever.
- **[The Limit](piece_the_limit.md)**: carries the balancing loop and the binding factor (Liebig; Goldratt), the
  finite capacity that governs the throughput once the load crosses it.
- **[The Delay](piece_the_delay.md)**: carries the lag in the feedback loop (Forrester; Meadows), the gap that hides
  the limit from the data while the decision is made.
- **[The Harder Push](piece_the_harder_push.md)**: carries the intuitive response (Senge), more of the reinforcing
  process applied when the balancing loop is what binds.
- **[The Leak](piece_the_leak.md)**: carries the balancing loop's output (Meadows on overshoot), the won growth
  handed back through the strained limit as the load rises.
- **[The Stall](piece_the_stall.md)**: carries the slowing of growth (Senge; Meadows), the net going flat as the
  leak rises to meet the intake, the symptom that fires the reflex.
- **[Limits to Growth](piece_limits_to_growth.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Senge), the reinforcing engine
  checked by a balancing limit through a delay, and its remedy in the limit at the cost of a lever the
  success never taught.
