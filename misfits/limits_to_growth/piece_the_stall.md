# Piece: The Stall

## Taxonomy

[Limits to Growth](play_limits_to_growth.md). The net flattening: intake and leak drawn level, the growth curve going flat.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the dashboard](place_the_dashboard.md), where the flat line shows and is named.
The visible sum of a full engine and an open leak.

## Load Bearing

Without it there would be no signal to misread and no trigger for the reflex.
With it the misfit has its turning point: the stall is the net going flat as the leak rises to meet the intake, the one thing everyone can see, and it is precisely what fires the harder push. The Stall is load bearing because it is the symptom that hides its own cause: it appears exactly where a dip would, so it is read as a dip, and the very flatness that should point to a binding limit points instead, through the record, to a call for more engine, making the visible sign the engine of the wrong response.

## Apparent

A temporary dip.
A flat stretch that looks like every flat stretch the engine drove through before reads as a passing slowdown, so the stall reads as nothing new, and the truth that this one is intake meeting an open leak, a limit binding and not an engine tiring, stays hidden behind the familiar shape of the curve.

## Yearbook

Shown through [misreading](process_misreading.md), the flat line [Call It a Dip](plan_call_it_a_dip.md) names as a dip.
It is the leak surfaced as a number: the Stall is [the leak](piece_the_leak.md) risen to meet the intake, misread past [the delay](piece_the_delay.md) as a shortfall of [the engine](piece_the_engine.md).
