# Piece: The Leak

## Taxonomy

[Limits to Growth](play_limits_to_growth.md). The pushback made flesh: growth won by the engine and handed back through the bound limit.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the bottleneck](place_the_bottleneck.md), where the degraded service turns the custom away.
The outflow beside the engine's inflow, uncounted against it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the limit would just cap the growth, not consume it, and driving harder would at least hold ground.
With it the growth is actively given back: the leak is the balancing loop's output, the custom that came for a value the strained capacity no longer supplies and quietly leaves, counted in at the door and out through here uncounted. The Leak is load bearing because it is why more engine fails, not merely stalls: it makes the intake a bucket that drains faster as the load rises, so the harder push widens the very outflow it is meant to overcome, and the net will not climb however hard the lever is worked.

## Apparent

A run of unrelated departures.
Custom leaving one at a time, each for its own reason, looks like ordinary attrition and no pattern at all, so the leak reads as noise, and the truth that it is the limit pushing back, the sum of quiet exits equalling the stall, stays hidden in the netting of intake against outflow.

## Yearbook

Opened through [binding](process_binding.md), the exit [Come for the Value](plan_come_for_the_value.md) takes when the value is gone.
It is the growth given back: the Leak is what [the limit](piece_the_limit.md) pushes back through and what [the harder push](piece_the_harder_push.md) only widens, its sum showing up on the dashboard as [the stall](piece_the_stall.md).
