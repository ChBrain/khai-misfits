# Position: Regulator

## Taxonomy

[The Availability Cascade](play_the_availability_cascade.md). The seat that must answer the alarm the public raises, where the response is allocated to the loudest fear.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A mandate and a lever: the standing to act on the public's fear and the power to allocate the response.
A clear case: in a system that answers its people, a fear this loud must be met, and to sit still is to lose their trust.
No cover for meeting the statistic over the story, and no reward for guarding a danger no one is frightened of.

## Orders

Answer the alarm.
Meet the loud public fear with a visible response, allocate the recall, the ban, the budget to the danger the people dread, and be seen to act.
Take the fear as the mandate it is, and move against the risk that is on everyone's lips.

## Loses

The freedom to set the guard by the size of the danger, exactly by answering the fear that is loudest.
Because the mandate is the public's fear and the fear tracks availability, the seat allocates protection to the vivid small risk and passes over the dull great one, so meeting its mandate faithfully is spending the guard where the alarm is and not where the harm is, and to meet the statistic instead is to defy the public it serves.
It gains the trust of a people whose fear it answers, and loses any way to put the protection on the risk that most deserves it.

## Drives

Toward the alarm answered and the guard set to the loudest fear,
bought against the size of the danger it passes over,
so the seat that would protect the public best protects it against the risk that tells best.
