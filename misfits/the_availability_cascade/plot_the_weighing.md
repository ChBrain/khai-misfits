# Plot: The Weighing

## Taxonomy

[The Availability Cascade](play_the_availability_cascade.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Chamber](place_the_chamber.md), someone who would weigh the magnitude against the story.
The cold number in hand, the vivid case on the table, the public certain that a danger so easy to picture must be common.

## Action

[Weighing](process_weighing.md): [The Assessor](persona_the_assessor.md), holding [the assessor's seat](position_assessor.md), holds the base rate up against the vivid case and asks that the risk be met by its size, against [The Regulator](persona_the_regulator.md) on [Answer the Alarm](plan_answer_the_alarm.md).

## Stage

[The Assessor](persona_the_assessor.md), on [Weigh the Risk](plan_weigh_the_risk.md), sets the cold number beside the story, names the salient risk small and the dull one vast, and asks that the guard follow the harm and not the fear.
[The Regulator](persona_the_regulator.md), on [Answer the Alarm](plan_answer_the_alarm.md), holds to the mandate the frightened public gave it, and the public hears the weighing as a shrug at a danger it can plainly picture.

## Tension

Sizing the danger by its harm is right, and doing it against a public alarm reads as waving the danger away.
There is nothing to fault: the base rate is the truest measure, and the assessor only insists on it. But the number is bloodless beside the case, so the plot closes one of two ways: the risk is weighed by its size and the alarm settles, bought against the assessor's standing ... or the cold number is taken for callousness and waved off, and the cascade climbs on.
