# Pitch: Take It Up

## Taxonomy

[The Availability Cascade](play_the_availability_cascade.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Alert, concerned, responsible.
The plain sense of a public taking up a real danger: a vivid case that everyone is talking about, a fear that everyone seems to share, and the reasonable move of believing what everyone believes and adding a voice to the warning.
Nothing false in it: the case is true, and taking a shared alarm as a sign of a real risk is how a careful person reasons.

## Undertow

Under the concern, the alarm feeding on its own repetition rather than on the evidence.
Because a risk easy to recall feels common, and each voice makes it easier to recall, and to doubt a public danger costs more than to echo it, the concern swells past what the facts support, and the response, set to answer a fear this loud, spends its protection on the vivid small risk while the dull great one waits unfeared.
The reasoning meant to weigh a danger sets the guard by how well the danger tells rather than how much it harms.

## Nerve

The feeling of taking up a fear the whole world seems to share, sure you are being careful,
because the story is vivid and everyone is alarmed and to shrug it off would be careless,
while the cold number that would size the risk goes unheard, and the one who holds it up is taken for the one who waved a danger away.

## Echo

After the run: a guard set to the vivid and not the vast, the small dramatic risk fenced round while the great dull one waits,
no one panicked and no one lying, only a true story retold until it felt common, a crowd reasoning from a shared alarm, and a loud fear answered as a responsive system must,
and a cold number that would have sized the risk, refused because it meant telling frightened people their fear was out of proportion and standing as the one who dismissed a danger everyone could picture.
