# The Availability Cascade: Reference

The research warrant for [The Availability Cascade](play_the_availability_cascade.md).
The dynamic is Kuran and Sunstein's; the play runs on their availability-cascade account of risk perception and regulation,
resting on the availability heuristic of Tversky and Kahneman, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the availability cascade as a **risk-perception system**: a public forming its sense of how dangerous a
thing is, in which a vivid instance is repeated until its ease of recall is read as frequency, each person updates
on the shared alarm and echoes it rather than pay the cost of lone dissent, availability entrepreneurs amplify the
story for their own ends, and risk regulation is allocated by how salient a danger is rather than how large, so
the vivid small risk is fenced round while the dull great one waits unattended. The play models the documented
mechanics, the vivid case and its availability, the availability heuristic that reads ease of recall as
probability, the informational cascade in which reasonable people converge on a belief because everyone else
appears to hold it, the reputational cascade in which the safe move is to echo the alarm rather than voice the
doubt, the availability entrepreneurs who inflate the fear by repetition, the response set to salience not size,
and the insulated assessment of magnitude that alone reverses it, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the
structure, not as a tale of a hysterical mob or a cynical demagogue. The governing finding of the source is the
play's governing law: an expressed perception of risk triggers a self-reinforcing chain, informational and
reputational, that makes the perception ever more available and so ever more believed, until it drives risk
regulation out of proportion to the underlying danger.

It is distinct from the house's Information Cascade, where each actor, seeing others' choices, rationally
discards a private signal and copies the herd on a discrete action: there the engine is observed action
overriding private information, here it is a repeated belief whose availability inflates a felt probability, plus
a reputational cost to dissent and amplifiers who profit from the fear, running not on a one-off choice but on a
risk perception that drives policy. It is distinct from the house's The Filter Bubble, where a personalised feed
narrows what one sees: here the story is public and shared, amplified in the open, and the fear grows by being
seen by everyone at once rather than by each being shown a different thing. And it is distinct from the house's
The Trip to Abilene, where a small group agrees its way into a choice no member privately wants through mutual
misreading: here the alarm becomes genuinely and increasingly held as availability climbs, at the scale of a
society, and its cost is a real misallocation of protection, not a single unwanted outing.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the public as a
  hysterical mob or the amplifier as a cynical demagogue, because the source finding is precisely that each move
  is sound: the story is true, taking a shared alarm as evidence and updating toward it is rational, echoing the
  consensus rather than paying the cost of lone dissent is prudent, amplifying a risk one sincerely fears is
  honest, and answering a loud public fear is what a responsive government must do. The inflated alarm is
  authored by no one and emerges from the structure, a repeated story whose availability is read as frequency, so
  the misfit is that a public reasoning carefully from a true case sets its guard by how vivid a danger is rather
  than how large, and spends its protection on the small risk that tells best.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that brings the
  alarm back to the evidence is to weigh the magnitude against the salience and insulate the assessment from the
  vividness of the story, and it means telling frightened people their fear is out of proportion, answering less
  to the loudest voice, and crediting a bloodless statistic over a danger everyone can picture, against every
  pull toward the vivid. The reckoning is an alarm settled by spending real standing and real trust to say a true
  story is rarer than it feels, and by standing as the one who waved off a danger, not a free correction, so the
  cure for a fear out of proportion is the credit given to the cold number no one wants to hear.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the availability cascade, the informational and reputational chain,
  and the salience-driven regulation are Kuran and Sunstein's, the availability heuristic is Tversky and
  Kahneman's, and the condition the trap actually requires, a vivid risk whose repeated availability is read as
  frequency, feeding a self-reinforcing alarm that sets the response to salience rather than size, is carried as
  carefully as the finding itself.
