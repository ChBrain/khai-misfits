# Process: Bedding In

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The settling of the raised level into the standing baseline, the crisis measure becoming routine, the money staying where it landed after the emergency that placed it has gone.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Outlay](piece_the_outlay.md), at [the Plateau](place_the_plateau.md), continuing past the crisis that raised it.
Begins when the emergency passes and the spending, instead of draining back, keeps flowing, the programme now serving a settled purpose it did not serve before.

## Direction

Turns a temporary measure into a permanent one.
Because money that lands in a budget tends to stick there as spending rather than pass back to taxpayers as a tax cut, the crisis outlay is not returned when the crisis ends but stays in the ordinary budget, so bedding in converts an emergency response into a standing commitment, the temporary naturalised into the baseline, the placed money sticking where it landed as the flypaper finding has it (Hines and Thaler).

## Lever

The stickiness of a placed commitment.
Because a programme, once funded, builds the routines and expectations and services that depend on it, the money does not flow back out the way it flowed in, so bedding in works through the plain fact that spending is easier to start than to stop: the outlay has taken root by the time the crisis lifts, and rooted spending stays.

## Echo

A crisis measure become the way things are done.
The echo of bedding in is a commitment no longer felt as extraordinary, its origin in an emergency forgotten and its continuation unquestioned, so the level that would have looked like a crisis crest before now reads as the ordinary floor, the mark settled into the baseline and taken for the ground.
