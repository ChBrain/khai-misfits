# Plot: The Drawdown

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Plateau](place_the_plateau.md), the crisis passing and the emergency over.
The war ended, the slump lifted, the disaster receded, and the reason the spending rose no longer present, the moment the level might have fallen back.

## Action

[Bedding In](process_bedding_in.md): the water recedes but the spending does not, the programme now serving a settled purpose and a people it did not serve before, the temporary measure rooting into the baseline.

## Stage

[The Provider](persona_the_provider.md)'s outlay continues past its crisis at [the Plateau](place_the_plateau.md), and [the outlay](piece_the_outlay.md) that met the emergency stays as a standing line, the level settling above the old floor and [the sunset](piece_the_sunset.md) that would have ended it arriving unenforced.
The crisis is gone and its spending is not, and nothing about the continuing looks like a decision at all.

## Tension

The crisis ends but the commitment does not, and no one has to choose to keep it: it simply stays.
There is no moment of profligacy: the water recedes on its own, but the spending has taken root, built the routines and services that depend on it, so it does not drain away with the emergency that placed it. The sunset comes due and is not enforced, because by now the outlay serves a real purpose and ending it would take from real people, so the plot closes with the level settled above the old line, the mark set, the temporary become permanent not by a fresh decision to keep it but by the absence of any bearable decision to end it.
