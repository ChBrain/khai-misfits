# Place: The Surge

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The crisis, where the level lifts, an emergency that loosens what calm would refuse and lets the spending crest to a height the ordinary years would never reach.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

An emergency and a rising answer, the spending lifting to meet a real and present need.
Shown as duty done: the crisis is genuine, the response is proportionate, and funding it to the hilt looks like nothing but a prudent body meeting the moment as it must.

## Holds

Holds the loosening that a crisis grants, the permission to spend that calm withholds.
The Surge is where the level crests: an emergency makes bearable what would not be borne otherwise, taxes tolerated and programmes stood up that answer the moment exactly, so the spending rises fast and far, the height of the crest set by the size of the need the crisis presents (Peacock and Wiseman).

## Offers

Offers a true reason to raise the spending, and the seed of a floor that will not fall.
Here the need is real and the response correct, so the Surge offers sound and present duty, and in the same rise it lays the commitment that will outlive the crisis, the crest that answers the emergency becoming the mark the receding water leaves.

## Withheld

Withholds that the water will not go all the way back down.
What the Surge hides is that the level it lifts is not on loan: the programmes it funds will gather constituencies, the measures it calls temporary will bed in, so the rise that feels like a bounded response to a bounded crisis is the first stroke of a permanent gain, the crest concealing the mark it is about to set.
