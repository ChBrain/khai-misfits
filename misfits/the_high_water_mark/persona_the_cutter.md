# Persona: The Cutter

## Taxonomy

[The Knife](position_the_knife.md). Moves to lower the floor when the crisis that raised it is long over, and bears the concentrated fury of those who depend on it for a diffuse saving no one will thank them for, right in the aggregate and hated in the particular.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reforming. Sound on the sum, alone on the particular. Willing to be the one who ends it.
Is the hand that would bring the spending back down to where the passed crisis no longer justifies it, and knows the arithmetic is on their side and the people are not.
No zeal wished on them: the cut is not a war on the needy but an attempt to lower a floor no present emergency holds up, undertaken in the knowledge that being right about the total buys nothing against the ones who will lose.

## Action

Moves against the standing commitment when its crisis is over, proposing to let the level fall back toward the line the calm years would bear, the saving real and spread across everyone who funds it.
Meets, the moment they move, the full and focused resistance of the constituency the commitment has gathered, and finds no answering force on their own side, since the many who would save a little have no reason to fight and the few who would lose everything have every reason.
Does what a reformer does against an entrenched outlay: trades a concentrated, immediate, nameable harm to identifiable people for a diffuse, deferred, unnoticed gain to the mass, and loses the trade more often than not, the asymmetry that raised the floor now the asymmetry that defends it.

## Shadow

Not a cruelty, but the private cost of being cast as the villain for doing the sum right, and that is the weight they carry alone: the faces of the people a cut would hurt are specific and known to them, and the mass who would gain are no one they will ever meet or be thanked by.
What gnaws is the doubt the role breeds in them. To be met with anger, season after season, for a good they cannot show anyone is to begin to wonder whether a rightness no one can feel is worth the harm everyone can, and the wondering does not resolve, because their own conscience keeps returning a verdict their heart will not sign. They must harden to do the thing at all, and then they distrust the hardening.
The bind is interior and corrosive: they have to become the kind of person who can look at a named loss and choose it anyway, and that is not a comfortable person to be, so each cut they carry leaves them a little colder, a little more sure they were right and a little less sure it was worth what it made of them, hated for the choosing and never once thanked, until they can no longer tell their resolve from a callus.

## Tell

Says "everyone gains a little if this ends, and one group loses a lot, and that is exactly why it will not."
Is diagnosing their own defeat in the act of proposing the cut, because the sentence names the asymmetry that dooms it: a little to the many cannot outfight a lot to the few, so the reformer who sees the trap most clearly is the one who can least escape it, the truth of the arithmetic and the certainty of the loss stated together and neither able to move the other.
