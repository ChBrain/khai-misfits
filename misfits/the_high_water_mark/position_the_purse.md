# Position: The Purse

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The funding seat, which can raise a commitment easily against a present need and lower it only against a formed constituency, the office where up is cheap and down is dear.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The power to grant, and the cost of it spread thin.
The seat holds the means to fund a need when a crisis presents one, and grants the commitment against little resistance, because the outlay's cost is diffused across everyone who pays and deferred onto those who will pay later, borne partly by taxpayers not yet arrived, while its benefit is felt sharply and now by those it helps.
Ease of motion in one direction only, since the seat can lift a commitment with the light friction of a spread cost but cannot lower it without the heavy friction of a concentrated loss.

## Orders

Fund the need the crisis shows.
Meet the present emergency with the present means, granting what the moment demands because the need is real and the cost, spread across the many and pushed onto later payers, draws little objection (Buchanan and Wagner).
Do not price the exit when you grant, since at the moment of granting there is no constituency yet to resist a future cut, and the difficulty of ending the commitment is invisible precisely when the commitment is made.

## Loses

The power to lower, which the seat forfeits the instant a commitment forms a constituency.
The Purse can raise a floor with ease and cannot lower it with the same, because granting spreads a cost no one will fight and cutting concentrates a loss someone will, so the seat's two directions are not symmetric and its freedom is one-way: what it took a light hand to lift takes a force it does not possess to bring down. What the seat forfeits is reversibility, given up not by any single choice but by the structure of the choices, since each grant that was easy because its cost was diffuse becomes, once relied upon, a commitment dear to cut because its loss is concentrated, so the same asymmetry that lets the seat answer every crisis is the asymmetry that keeps it from ever undoing the answer, and the office that can always say yes is the office that can almost never take the yes back.

## Drives

Toward a floor that rises at need and will not fall at leisure,
and a granting hand that cannot become a lowering one,
so the seat that meets every crisis is the seat that keeps every commitment, its ease in the raising the exact measure of its helplessness in the cut.
