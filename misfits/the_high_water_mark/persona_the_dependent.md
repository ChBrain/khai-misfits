# Persona: The Dependent

## Taxonomy

[The Entitlement](position_the_entitlement.md). Comes to live on the commitment once it stands, and defends it against any cut, small and organised and vigilant where the taxpayer who funds it is large and diffuse, not a taker but someone who built on a floor they were invited to trust.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reliant. Organised by need. Defending the thing they have come to stand on.
Is the one for whom the standing commitment is not a line in a budget but a livelihood, a service, a plan for the year, so its ending is not a saving foregone but a loss inflicted, and they answer any threat to it accordingly.
No greed wished on them: the dependence is real and was invited, the programme built to be relied on, so leaning on it is not a grab but the taking-up of an offer the crisis made and the calm never withdrew.

## Action

Builds on the commitment as though it were permanent, because for all they were told and all they can see it is, arranging a life around a floor the emergency laid and the years left standing.
Defends the outlay against any cut with the full attention of someone whose stake is concentrated and clear, turning out to protect their niche while the taxpayer who would save a little by ending it never turns out at all.
Does what a rational beneficiary does: values the thing they will lose far more than the diffuse funder values the little they would save, and so wins the contest over the commitment not by numbers but by caring more, per head, than anyone on the other side.

## Shadow

Not a parasite, but someone asked to give back alone what was handed to everyone, and that is the sting: they did not design the commitment or lobby it into being, they only trusted it, and now the trusting is named as the problem and the untrusting is asked of them first.
What wears on them is not an argument but the feeling of being singled out. When the talk turns to what must be cut, it is their name that comes up, their livelihood held up as the excess, and the reasonableness of the people naming it makes it worse, because there is no villain to be angry at, only a fairness that keeps arriving at their door. They took an offer made in good faith, and the taking is treated now as though it were the fault.
The bind is interior and lonely: they know they are not wrong to hold on, and still they feel like the obstacle, so they carry a resentment that has nowhere to land and a guilt they did nothing to earn, keeping a thing they were invited to depend on and feeling, for keeping it, like the reason nothing can change.

## Tell

Says "I did not ask for this to be temporary; I only believed it when it was given."
Is exactly right, and that is what makes the cut feel like a betrayal, because the commitment was made to be relied on and they relied on it as intended, so the sentence that sounds like a defence of privilege is the plain account of someone keeping their side of a bargain the other side now wants to unmake, the good faith of the beneficiary the thing the saving must override.
