# Persona: The Provider

## Taxonomy

[The Purse](position_the_purse.md). Raises the spending to meet the crisis, rightly, and finds the commitment cannot be unmade once the crisis has passed, the one who said yes for good reasons and now owns a floor it cannot lower.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Responsible. Moved by a real need. Funding the emergency because the emergency is real.
Is the hand that holds the purse when the crisis arrives, and answers it the way a prudent body should, standing up the programme and bearing the tax the moment demands.
No profligacy wished on them: the outlay is not waste but the sound response to a present need, and the trouble is not that they spent but that what they raised to meet a crisis outlives the crisis and becomes theirs to keep.

## Action

Meets the crisis by raising the spending to it, funding what the emergency demands and bearing the cost calm would refuse, each outlay the correct answer to the need in front of them.
Grants the commitment when it is asked for and easy to grant, the need real and the cost spread thin, and does not weigh, because there is no reason at the moment to weigh, how hard the thing will be to end once it stands.
Does what a responsible hand does: answers the present need with the present means, and discovers only later that a commitment made is a commitment kept, the floor it raised in good faith now a floor it cannot bring back down.

## Shadow

Not that they overspent, but that every yes was right and the sum is a state they never chose, and that is the quiet of it: there is no single outlay they would take back, and yet the accumulation is a weight they carry without having willed it.
What sits with them is the discovery that granting is not the same act reversed as cutting. Each commitment felt, in the making, like a bounded answer to a bounded need, and only afterward showed its other face, that it had gathered a constituency and become permanent, so the hand that gave in good faith is the hand that finds it cannot take back, and the good faith is precisely what makes the taking back feel like a betrayal.
The bind is interior and cumulative: they cannot point to the outlay that was the mistake, because none was, and so they cannot find the place to undo it, the floor they stand on built entirely of decisions each of which they would make again, its height the one thing they would change and the one thing no single choice will lower.

## Tell

Says "it was the right thing to fund, and now it is simply what we fund."
Is right on both halves and undone by the join, because the outlay was sound when the crisis called for it and is now a standing line in the accounts that no crisis calls for, so the sentence that begins as a defence of a good decision ends as the description of a floor that will not fall, the rightness of the granting and the permanence of the grant admitted in one breath.
