# Piece: The Watermark

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The floor that climbs far more than it falls, the line the receding crisis leaves on the wall and the next crisis climbs from, the whole phenomenon of which every other piece is a part.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Set at [the Plateau](place_the_plateau.md) where the water settles, recorded in [the Accounts](place_the_accounts.md) as the standing floor, and climbed from at each new [Surge](place_the_surge.md).
Flows as the level that never falls, present after every crisis and higher than before it, the mark that turns a series of emergencies into a rising floor.

## Load Bearing

Without the Watermark there is no trap, only a set of separate crises each met and each subsiding: it is the Watermark that ties the cresting to the bedding-in to the entrenchment, the one line that turns a raised level into a permanent one and a permanent one into the base of the next rise.
The Watermark is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole, the displacement that lifts the floor and rarely gives it back, sound in each rise and unplanned in the sum (Peacock and Wiseman). It carries the entire misfit: the crest that answered the crisis is the Watermark forming, the level that would not recede is the Watermark holding, the ascent of the plateaus is the Watermark climbing, so the Watermark is not one more piece beside the others but the shape they all compose, the mark whose every motion is another of the play's pieces at work. It is the residue of good decisions, which is why it cannot simply be erased: the floor is made of commitments each of which met a real need, so lowering the mark means unmaking a soundness, taking from the people who stand on it what a true crisis once rightly gave.

## Apparent

Just the current level of spending, the ordinary size of the state.
Seen at the surface, the Watermark looks like a plain fact, the amount a body spends, and the truth that it is a mark and not a ground, that each stroke of it was a crisis crest the water never took back, that it climbs because up is easy and down is dear and not because anyone chose a larger state, stays hidden behind how completely a floor of accumulated marks resembles the way things simply are.

## Yearbook

Raised by [the Provider](persona_the_provider.md) crisis by crisis, stood upon by [the Dependent](persona_the_dependent.md) at every level, and faced by [the Cutter](persona_the_cutter.md) who would lower it and mostly cannot.
It is the whole shape of the ratchet: the Watermark is what [cresting](process_cresting.md) raises and [bedding in](process_bedding_in.md) sets, what [digging in](process_digging_in.md) defends and [climbing](process_climbing.md) stacks, the permanent line [the outlay](piece_the_outlay.md) composes and [the sunset](piece_the_sunset.md) was meant to lower, the floor that climbs far more than it falls.
