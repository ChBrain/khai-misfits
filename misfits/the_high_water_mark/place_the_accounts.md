# Place: The Accounts

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The standing books, where every commitment sits as a line that recurs, the ledger of the floor read by whoever would fund it further or bring it down.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A tidy record, each outlay a settled line that simply repeats.
Shown as order: the books balance, the commitments are itemised and funded, and the accounts look like nothing but the neutral bookkeeping of a going concern, each line as ordinary as the last.

## Holds

Holds every past crisis as a present line.
The Accounts are where the commitments become permanent by becoming routine: each outlay a crisis once raised now sits as a recurring entry, indistinguishable in the ledger from the ones that were always there, so the books hold the whole history of surges as a flat list of standing costs, the marks recorded as though they were the floor.

## Offers

Offers a clear tally to fund from, and a wall of standing lines to any cut.
Here the record is honest and every commitment legible, so the Accounts offer a true account of what is spent, and by rendering each outlay a settled line among settled lines they offer the cutter not a crest to lower but a baseline to attack, every entry looking equally load-bearing and equally permanent.

## Withheld

Withholds which lines are floor and which are still crest.
What the Accounts hide is the age and the origin of each commitment: the ledger shows what is spent but not that this line was a temporary crisis measure and that one a founding purpose, so the reader cannot see, from the books alone, which outlays a passed emergency no longer justifies, the marks and the floor written in the same hand and read as one.
