# Process: Digging In

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The gathering of a constituency around the standing commitment, the beneficiaries organising to hold the floor, concentrated and vigilant where the funders are dispersed and inattentive.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Dependent](persona_the_dependent.md), at [the Plateau](place_the_plateau.md), coming to rely on the settled commitment and organising to protect it.
Begins once an outlay has bedded in and a group has built their plans around it, so that its continuation becomes their stake and its ending their loss.

## Direction

Turns a standing commitment into an entrenched one.
Because the benefit of the commitment falls sharply on a few while its cost is spread thin on the many, the few organise to defend it and the many do not organise to end it, so digging in converts a bedded outlay into a defended niche, the floor now held by the concentrated interest of those who stand on it (Olson).

## Lever

The concentration of the stake.
Because those who depend on the commitment would each lose much from its cut while those who fund it would each save little from it, the beneficiaries act and the funders abstain, so digging in works through the asymmetry of intensity: the few who care greatly outfight the many who care slightly, and the commitment is held not by numbers but by who is willing to fight.

## Echo

A floor no longer freely lowered, held by the people on it.
The echo of digging in is a commitment cleared only by an upheaval as large as the one that raised it, defended by a constituency that will spend everything to keep it against a saving no one will spend anything to win, the mark now a fortified line and the ratchet's pawl the people standing on the floor.
