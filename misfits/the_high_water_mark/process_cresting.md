# Process: Cresting

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The lifting of the level to meet the crisis, the spending rising fast and far under an emergency that loosens what calm would refuse.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Provider](persona_the_provider.md), at [the Surge](place_the_surge.md), raising the spending to answer a real and present need.
Begins when a crisis presents a need the ordinary budget cannot meet, and the emergency makes bearable an outlay the calm years would never permit.

## Direction

Turns a present crisis into a raised level.
Because the emergency is real and the need immediate, the spending lifts to meet it, so cresting converts a bounded crisis into a commitment funded to the hilt, the level rising to whatever height the need demands, each outlay of it the correct answer to the moment (Peacock and Wiseman).

## Lever

The permission a crisis grants.
Because an emergency loosens the resistance that calm maintains, cresting works through the licence of necessity: taxes are borne and programmes stood up that would be refused in ordinary times, so the lever is the plain force of a real need, which makes the raising not only possible but obligatory, the right thing to do at the moment it is done.

## Echo

A level lifted past the old line, and a mark waiting to be left.
The echo of cresting is spending at a height the calm years never reached, sound for the crisis and already carrying, unseen, the commitment that will outlast it, the crest that answered the emergency poised to become the mark the receding water sets.
