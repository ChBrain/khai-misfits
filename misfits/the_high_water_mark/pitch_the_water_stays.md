# Pitch: The Water Stays

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md). The single hook: the spending rises to meet a crisis and does not fall when the crisis ends, so the temporary becomes the permanent and the floor climbs, mark by mark, on an asymmetry no single outlay can see.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A crisis, and the spending that rises to meet it.
The tenor is responsibility: the emergency is real, the need is present, so the outlay is made and the taxes borne, each commitment the sound and obvious answer to the moment, exactly what a prudent hand should fund.

## Undertow

The water recedes, and the mark stays on the wall.
Underneath, the level raised for the crisis does not fall when the crisis passes, because the programme has a people now and money sticks where it landed, so the temporary measure beds in as the standing baseline and the floor settles above the old line, higher than the calm years would ever have reached.

## Nerve

Up is easy and down is dear, and no one decided the state should grow.
The nerve is the asymmetry: raising a commitment spreads its cost thin across everyone and lowering it takes everything from someone, so the beneficiaries defend their niche while the saving goes unchampioned, and the floor climbs not by any plan or greed but by the plain fact that a cut has a victim and a grant does not.

## Echo

The only way down is to take a standing thing from the people who stand on it.
What lingers is that the cure is a sunset enforced against its constituency, a visible loss inflicted on identifiable people for an invisible gain to everyone, so lowering the floor means being the one who ended the thing they depended on, and leaving the mark where it stands is what every reasonable party, crisis after crisis, is content to do.
