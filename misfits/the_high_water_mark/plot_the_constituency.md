# Plot: The Constituency

## Taxonomy

[The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Plateau](place_the_plateau.md), a standing commitment and the group that has come to live on it.
An outlay bedded into the baseline, and the people whose plans, livelihoods, and services now rest on its continuing, gathered around it without being called.

## Action

[Digging In](process_digging_in.md): the beneficiaries organise to defend the commitment, concentrated and vigilant where the taxpayer who funds it is diffuse and inattentive, the floor now held by the people on it.

## Stage

[The Dependent](persona_the_dependent.md) gathers around [the outlay](piece_the_outlay.md) at [the waterline](piece_the_waterline.md), on [Keep What's Given](plan_keep_whats_given.md), defending the commitment with the full force of a concentrated stake while no one of equal force turns out for the diffuse saving of ending it.
They did not design the commitment or lobby it into being; they only trusted it, and now the trusting is what holds it up.

## Tension

The beneficiaries' defence is honest and proportionate, and it is exactly what makes the floor immovable.
There is no villainy in the digging in: the dependence is real and was invited, so defending the commitment is only keeping to a bargain the crisis offered and the calm never withdrew. But the few who would lose much from a cut outfight the many who would each save little, so the plot closes with the commitment entrenched, held not by any plan but by the concentrated stake of its constituency, the floor now a fortified line and the saving of lowering it left with no one willing to fight for it.
