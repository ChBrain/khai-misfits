# Plan: Meet the Need

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Purse](position_the_purse.md), inside [The High-Water Mark](play_the_high_water_mark.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Purse](position_the_purse.md)

## Direction

Fund the crisis with the means it demands, granting the commitment because the need is real and the cost, spread across everyone who pays, draws little resistance (Buchanan and Wagner). Answer the present emergency with the present outlay, and do not weigh the difficulty of ending the commitment later, since at the moment of granting no constituency yet exists to resist a cut and the exit costs nothing to leave unpriced. Accept that the outlay is easy to make precisely because its cost is diffuse, and that the same diffusion which lets you grant it without a fight is what will let a beneficiary, once formed, defend it without an equal one.

## Orders

([The Provider](persona_the_provider.md) raises the spending to meet the emergency, funding what the moment demands and bearing the tax calm would refuse, each outlay the sound answer to a real and present need and none of them priced for the exit.)

## Implementation

([The Surge](place_the_surge.md); through the [cresting](process_cresting.md) process; the Provider grants [the outlay](piece_the_outlay.md) against the crisis, lifting the level toward a height the calm years would never reach, sound in the raising and blind to [the watermark](piece_the_watermark.md) it will leave.)

## Targets

- [ ] the commitment is funded to meet a real and present need
- [ ] the cost is spread across the many, so the grant draws little resistance
- [ ] the difficulty of a future cut is left unpriced, invisible at the moment of granting
- [ ] the ease of the raising is accepted as the same ease that will defend the floor
