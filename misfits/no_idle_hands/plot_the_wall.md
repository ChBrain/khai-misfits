# Plot: The Wall

## Taxonomy

[No Idle Hands](play_no_idle_hands.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Line](place_the_line.md), the server near saturation, still reliable, still taking the next job.
A last small increment of utilisation, the most efficient-looking of all, to be squeezed from the last of the slack.

## Action

[Serving](process_serving.md): [The Server](persona_the_server.md), holding [the server's seat](position_server.md), takes the next job at its steady rate as the busy fraction nears one, its idle time all but gone.

## Stage

The Server, on [Take the Next Job](plan_take_the_next_job.md), holds its reliable rate to [the saturation](piece_the_saturation.md), and the last increment of [the utilisation](piece_the_utilisation.md) sends [the wait](piece_the_wait.md) toward infinity as one minus the fraction nears zero.
The most virtuous-looking squeeze, the finish of the manager's job, is the move that meets the wall, and the server never once fails.

## Tension

Taking the next job at a reliable rate is blameless, and the blamelessness is what hides that the last increment is the choke.
There is nothing to fault: the server serves exactly as well as ever and simply stands free less. But the wait diverges as the fraction nears one, so the plot closes with a tiny efficiency gain buying an unbounded rise in delay, the wall built from the arithmetic of variability alone in a resource that never broke.
