# Plot: The Climb

## Taxonomy

[No Idle Hands](play_no_idle_hands.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Line](place_the_line.md), the busy fraction rising as the slack thins.
The variability with no idle time left to sit in, waiting behind the loaded server.

## Action

[Queuing](process_queuing.md): [The Queue](persona_the_queue.md), holding [the queue's seat](position_queue.md), grows as the utilisation over one minus itself, gently at first and then steeply.

## Stage

The Queue, on [Wait in Line](plan_wait_in_line.md), takes up [the variability](piece_the_variability.md) the buffer used to hold and grows [the wait](piece_the_wait.md) as [the utilisation](piece_the_utilisation.md) rises, modest while slack remains and steeper as it thins.
The line grows behind the server where the metric does not look, the delay no longer proportional to the loading that drives it.

## Tension

The wait rising with the fraction is the plain arithmetic of the law, and the arithmetic is what hides how steep the last stretch will be.
There is nothing to fault: the queue grows because a loaded resource can only absorb its variability in the time it stands free. But the curve bends, so the plot closes with each point of efficiency now buying more wait than the last, the line climbing faster than the loading and still out of the metric's sight.
