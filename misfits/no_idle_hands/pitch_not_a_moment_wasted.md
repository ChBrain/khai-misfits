# Pitch: Not a Moment Wasted

## Taxonomy

[No Idle Hands](play_no_idle_hands.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Busy, lean, well run.
The plain good sense of a resource kept fully occupied, every idle minute wrung out of it, the metric on the wall reading utilisation high and reading it good.
Nothing false in it on its face: idleness genuinely costs, driving it out is the manager's job done well, and the server never once fails.

## Undertow

Under the leanness, the idle time that was squeezed out was the buffer the whole flow needed to absorb its variability.
Because a resource fed by ordinary variation can only absorb it in the time it stands free, the wait behind the server rises as the utilisation over one minus itself, without bound as the busy fraction nears one, so the last increments of efficiency each buy an unbounded rise in delay.
What makes a resource look well run, that it is rarely idle, is exactly what leaves the flow no slack.

## Nerve

The feeling of a resource kept fully occupied, the gaps closed and the metric high,
sure the leanness is the flow run well, because a busy resource is what a good manager makes,
while behind the busiest hands the line swells without bound, the wait diverging on blameless variation alone, in a server that never breaks.

## Echo

After the run: the busiest resource revealed as the one whose queue grows without bound, the leanness on the floor and the jam behind it the same fact from two ends,
no accident and no failure, only idle time squeezed out that was the buffer, and a wait that diverged from the arithmetic of variability meeting a resource with no slack left,
and the one move that would have kept the flow fast, holding utilisation below saturation and keeping planned slack, forgone because it reads as tolerating the waste the manager was right to hate.
