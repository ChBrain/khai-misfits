# Persona: The Reserver

## Taxonomy

[Reserver](position_reserver.md). The one who would hold utilisation below saturation and keep planned slack, leaving the resource idle on purpose so the buffer stays to absorb the variability, at the cost of looking like they tolerate waste. The archetype of the costly remedy.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Deliberate. Willing to leave capacity free. Suspicious of a fully loaded resource.
Is the one who sets a target utilisation below one, the planner who protects slack, the lead who refuses the last efficient-looking increment.
No sloth in it: the Reserver does not undervalue the resource, only knows that the idle time is the buffer the flow's variability needs.

## Action

Holds the busy fraction below saturation on purpose, keeping planned slack so the variability has idle time to sit in and the wait stays bounded.
Defends the free capacity against the standing pressure to load it, and refuses the last increment that reads most efficient and buys the choke.
Does what keeps the flow fast: it leaves hands idle deliberately, and deliberate idleness is the one thing that keeps the line from diverging.

## Shadow

Not a free corrective, a costly and unwelcome restraint, and that is the whole difficulty: the slack that keeps the flow fast is exactly the idle capacity the Manager is right to read as waste.
Because the busy fraction reads as leanness and the queue it spares leaves no trace, the Reserver must defend visibly idle capacity against every signal that a busy resource is well run, be answerable for an idle hour whose worth is a jam that never happens, and refuse the increment that would have read best on the wall, so keeping the flow fast is bought against the plain and constant pressure to load the resource, the choke it prevents leaving nothing to point to and the idleness it holds plain for all to see.
It keeps the buffer to spare the flow, and keeping it is read as tolerating the waste the manager was right to hate.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "leave it below full, keep the slack, that idle time is the buffer," into a room that sees only waste.
Says it against a metric that reads utilisation and rewards it high, and is heard, often, as protecting idleness, undervaluing the resource, the one who will not let a good server be fully used.
