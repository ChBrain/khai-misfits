# Piece: The Saturation

## Taxonomy

[No Idle Hands](play_no_idle_hands.md). The wall at full utilisation where the wait goes to infinity, the point the busy fraction is driven toward and cannot reach without the flow choking.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Approached on [the floor](place_the_floor.md), as the busy fraction is driven toward one.
Met at [the line](place_the_line.md), where the wait diverges as the fraction nears it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the wait rises with no ceiling to explain, and the trap has no edge.
With it the flow has its wall: the point at full utilisation where one minus the busy fraction reaches zero and the wait, dividing by it, goes to infinity (Kingman, on the divergence at the heavy-traffic limit, the wait unbounded as the utilisation approaches one). The Saturation is load bearing because it is where the last increment lives: the move from very busy to almost fully busy is the move that sends the wait toward the wall, so the piece carries the edge the manager drives toward, the point where the most efficient-looking gain buys an unbounded loss and the metric reads best right up to the choke.

## Apparent

A target, the last of the idle time, one hundred parts busy, the goal a good manager drives toward.
Seen on the floor, the Saturation looks like the finish of the job, the fraction driven the last stretch to full, and the truth that the wait diverges as it nears, that the last increment buys an unbounded delay, that the wall is built by the arithmetic alone in a resource that never fails, stays hidden behind how virtuous the final squeeze toward full looks.

## Yearbook

Approached under [the manager](persona_the_manager.md) through [loading](process_loading.md), held back from only by [the reserver](persona_the_reserver.md) through [reserving](process_reserving.md).
It is the wall of the trap: the Saturation is where [the utilisation](piece_the_utilisation.md) drives one minus itself to zero and [the wait](piece_the_wait.md) goes to infinity on [the variability](piece_the_variability.md) alone, the edge at which the line becomes [the jam](piece_the_jam.md).
