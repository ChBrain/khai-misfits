# Piece: The Slack

## Taxonomy

[No Idle Hands](play_no_idle_hands.md). The idle capacity, read as waste on the floor but the buffer that absorbs the variability, the thing the manager is right to hate and the flow cannot do without.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Squeezed out on [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where the idle time is priced as waste.
Kept on purpose at [the reserve](place_the_reserve.md), where it is held as the buffer the flow needs.

## Load Bearing

Without it the variability has nowhere to sit but the line, and the wait diverges the moment the fraction climbs.
With it the flow has its buffer: the idle time in which a loaded resource absorbs its own variation, the free minutes that keep the queue from growing (Hopp and Spearman, Factory Physics, on capacity buffered against variability, the slack that a loaded flow needs to stay fast). The Slack is load bearing because it is the buffer read as waste: the one thing the flow needs to absorb its variability is exactly the idle capacity the metric prices as loss, so the piece carries the double face of the idle hour, genuine cost on the floor and necessary buffer to the flow, spent by good management and missed only at the choke.

## Apparent

Waste, an idle resource, capacity paid for and not used.
Seen on the floor, the Slack looks like exactly the loss it is priced as, an idle hour bought and thrown away, and the truth that the idle time is the buffer the variability needs, that spending it moves the variation to the line, that the flow stays fast only while it is kept, stays hidden behind how plainly an unused resource reads as waste.

## Yearbook

Spent by [the manager](persona_the_manager.md) through [loading](process_loading.md), kept by [the reserver](persona_the_reserver.md) through [reserving](process_reserving.md).
It is the buffer of the trap: the Slack is the idle time that holds [the variability](piece_the_variability.md), and when [the utilisation](piece_the_utilisation.md) squeezes it out the variation floods [the wait](piece_the_wait.md), the buffer read as waste whose loss becomes [the jam](piece_the_jam.md).
