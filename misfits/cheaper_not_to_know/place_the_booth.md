# Place: The Booth

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The private moment where the cost-return calculus resolves and the cue is marked, the site of [The Long Odds](plot_the_long_odds.md), [The Shortcut](plot_the_shortcut.md), and [The Homework](plot_the_homework.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A citizen alone with a ballot, a curtain or a screen, a pencil or a touchscreen waiting.
Free, in principle, to have learned as much as they liked before arriving: nothing here bars the studying, it is simply already over, whatever amount of it was done.

## Holds

The vote, and the sum that decided it.
Here the citizen marks [the Vote](piece_the_vote.md), so the Booth holds the point where the weighing of [the Cost](piece_the_cost.md) against [the Return](piece_the_return.md), gated all along by a near-zero [Pivot](piece_the_pivot.md), finally resolves into one motion of the hand, cue-cast or homework-cast, whichever the citizen brought in with them.

## Offers

A private, unwatched moment to decide, and a ballot that counts exactly the same whichever way it was reasoned.
Because the Booth asks nothing of the citizen but the mark, it offers the same clean vote to the one who spent a real cost learning and the one who spent none, so it hands out one vote each, indifferent to what stood behind it.

## Withheld

Any way for the single booth to see the hundred million others like it.
The Booth can only ever show one citizen one sound private choice, so it withholds the view from outside, the one from which a hundred million equally reasonable declinings would sum to a half-blind electorate, a fact no booth, taken alone, is built to hold.
