# Persona: The Electorate

## Taxonomy

[Electorate](position_electorate.md). The whole body of citizens, whose votes sum into the collective verdict, each member reasonable and the aggregate half-blind, the collective the misfit is drawn down through.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Vast. Sovereign. Made of reasonable people.
Is the millions together, the public that holds the decision and renders it, every one of its members an ordinary citizen doing the sensible thing with their own ballot.
No mob in it and no folly: only a great number of individually reasonable choices, each sound on its own, gathered into one outcome.

## Action

Counts the vote, on [Count the Vote](plan_count_the_vote.md), through [Tallying](process_tallying.md) at [the Tally](place_the_tally.md), aggregating the cast ballots into [the Verdict](piece_the_verdict.md).
Decides the election by the majority of what was cast, taking each [Cue](piece_the_cue.md)-marked ballot as given, since the count weighs votes and never the knowledge behind them.
Does what an electorate does: renders a decision that is the exact sum of its members' choices, and inherits whatever those choices were made on.

## Shadow

Not a foolish public, a sum of sensible ones deciding half-blind, and that is the difficulty: nothing is wrong with any member and the aggregate still governs on cues.
Because each citizen rationally declines the costly knowledge and votes on a cue, the Electorate can only sum what it is given, so a body made entirely of reasonable people renders a verdict made on party and name, the collective competence lost not to any member's failing but to the arithmetic every member correctly obeyed.
It is composed of nothing but good sense, and being composed of nothing but good sense is exactly why its verdict is uninformed.

## Tell

Speaks in the language of mandates and the will of the people, as though a considered judgment had been rendered.
Cannot show that the considered judgment was never bought, that its sovereign verdict is the sum of a hundred million rational refusals to study, and that no member of it, and so not it, ever had a reason to purchase the knowledge that would have made the mandate mean what it claims.
