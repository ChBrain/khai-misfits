# Pitch: Not Worth the Hours

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Reasonable, civic, well-meaning.
A citizen who wants to vote well, a ballot honestly cast, a campaign fought in the open, an election that anyone is free to study before deciding.
Nothing false in it: the knowledge is available, the voter is willing in principle, and no door is closed to anyone who wants to learn.

## Undertow

Under the civic surface, the arithmetic that makes learning the issues a losing investment.
To vote well costs real hours, and the benefit lands only if the better-informed vote changes the outcome, so with one vote among millions and a vanishing chance of deciding anything, the return on all that study is near zero, and a real cost that buys a near-zero return is a cost a reasonable person does not pay.
So the citizen stays ignorant, not from apathy but from sound economics, and votes on the cue instead.

## Nerve

The feeling of knowing you could learn it all and knowing, just as clearly, that it would change nothing,
that your one studied vote would be exactly as decisive as the most careless one, which is to say hardly at all,
so the hours of homework buy you the private satisfaction of knowing and buy the result nothing, and the sensible thing is not to spend them.

## Echo

After the run: an electorate that decides on party and name, not because its members are fools but because each is sensible,
the diligent few who did the homework changing no outcome, the earnest call to be informed running into the same arithmetic it means to beat,
and the good that would have made the choice sound, an informed public, going unbought, because no single member of it had any reason to buy it.
