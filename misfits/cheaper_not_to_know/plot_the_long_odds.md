# Plot: The Long Odds

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Booth](place_the_booth.md), before the ballot is marked.
The reckoning a citizen runs, unwritten, on whether learning the candidates and the measures is worth the hours it costs.

## Action

[Weighing](process_weighing.md): [The Voter](persona_the_voter.md), holding [the voter's seat](position_voter.md), opens [Vote on the Cue](plan_vote_on_the_cue.md) and prices [the Cost](piece_the_cost.md) of mastering the records and the measures against the benefit gated behind [the Pivot](piece_the_pivot.md), so [the Return](piece_the_return.md) comes out close to nothing.

## Stage

The Voter totals the hours it would take to read the measures and check the candidates' records, a real and specific cost, and sets it against what that learning would buy: a better outcome, worth something only if the informed vote is the one that decides. In an election of millions the chance of that is vanishingly small, so a genuine benefit multiplied by a near-zero pivot returns almost nothing. The Voter runs the sum the way anyone would run it on any costly thing that pays back almost nothing, without needing to write it down.

## Tension

A real cost sits on one side, a vanishing return on the other, and no reasonable person pays a real price for a return that small. Nothing here is a failure of will or curiosity: the arithmetic itself declines the purchase before the Voter has decided anything else, and the beat closes on a citizen who has correctly priced the knowledge and found it not worth its price.
