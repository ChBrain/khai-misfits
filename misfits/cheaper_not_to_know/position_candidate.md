# Position: Candidate

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The seat that competes for a cue-deciding public, and so is rewarded for supplying cues and penalized for the deep case no one is paid to weigh, meeting the electorate where the arithmetic has left it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A record to run on and a public that will not study it: the standing to seek the vote, and a true account of positions and history that the electorate has rationally declined to learn.
A hard case: the substance is real and available and the seat could lead with it, but the votes turn on cues, so leading with substance spends effort on what wins nothing.
No power to make the public weigh the record, because the public's not weighing it is the same rational refusal that governs every other seat, so the seat can only meet the vote where it already turns.

## Orders

Campaign on the cue.
Give the public the party, the name, the slogan it decides on, and supply the cue clearly and often, because that is what wins a vote cast on cues.
Place the full record where the studious can find it, but do not lead with what no one is paid to hear, since the contest is fought on the ground the electorate actually stands on.

## Loses

The contest of substance it might have run, and loses it to the arithmetic, not to any choice to cheapen.
Because the vote turns on cues and a rival will always supply them, the seat cannot win by leading with the deep record without ceding the cue-deciding public to one who does, so what it surrenders is the campaign of records, given up because the electorate rationally declined to come weigh it.
It keeps the votes it can win on cues and loses the contest that would have turned on knowledge, because the knowledge was never bought on the other side.

## Drives

Toward a campaign fought on cues because the vote is,
the slogan led with and the record filed where few will look,
so the seat that meets the public where it is supplies the cue the half-blind verdict turns on, no cynic and no villain, only a competitor shaped by the arithmetic across from it.
