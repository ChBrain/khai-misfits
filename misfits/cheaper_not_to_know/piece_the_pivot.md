# Piece: The Pivot

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The near-zero probability that the single vote is decisive, the vanishingly small chance of being the ballot that tips the election.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Set at [the Booth](place_the_booth.md), before a mark is ever made, the standing condition every weighing there runs against.
Not a place a citizen visits so much as a number that is already true of the election before anyone arrives: in a contest of millions, the chance that any one ballot is the ballot that tips it.

## Load Bearing

The Pivot is load bearing because it is the driver of the whole misfit, not the cost of learning, not the citizen's patience, but the near-zero probability that a single vote is decisive in a mass election (Downs). Everything else in this structure follows from it: the Return is near zero because the Pivot gates it, the Cost stays real because nothing about the Pivot lowers it, and the Cue gets chosen because the Pivot leaves nothing else worth buying with the saved effort. State it carefully: in a mass election this probability is vanishingly small, on the order of one in millions, not a single fixed number claimed for every election everywhere. It is the Pivot that drives the arithmetic, not the price of information, so even knowledge that cost nothing to acquire would still be gated behind the same near-zero chance of mattering.

## Apparent

A technicality. A footnote to the real decision.
It looks like a minor statistical fact, tucked under the more visible business of learning the candidates and marking the ballot, easy to skip past as background. That reading has it backward: the Pivot is the load-bearing member the entire calculus stands on, the reason the Cost never gets covered no matter how the citizen prices the learning.
