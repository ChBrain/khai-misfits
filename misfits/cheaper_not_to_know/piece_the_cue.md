# Piece: The Cue

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The cheap heuristic decided on instead of knowledge: party, ideology, name recognition, endorsement, a low-cost proxy that roughly sorts the choice.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Reached for at [the Booth](place_the_booth.md) when the costly knowledge has been declined, and supplied on [the Trail](place_the_trail.md), where the Candidate campaigns to meet it.
Not a substitute manufactured to deceive: the party label, the recognized name, the trusted endorsement, already sitting to hand, cheap to read and carrying some real signal about which side a candidate stands on.

## Load Bearing

The Cue is load bearing because it is what the Voter decides on once the costly knowledge is declined: ideologies and party labels function as rational information-saving shortcuts (Downs), the low-cost proxies a citizen reasonably relies on in a low-information electorate (Somin). It is precise to say the Cue is a sound shortcut of someone who has priced the deep knowledge correctly, not a failure of curiosity or a lazy stand-in: choosing it is the same act of good judgment that declined the Cost in the first place, applied to the question of how to vote anyway. Guard against the reading that the Cue is worthless: it carries real signal, a party label does correlate with a set of positions, an endorsement does reflect someone's judgment, it simply carries far less signal than the knowledge it stands in for, and that gap is the whole of what the electorate loses.

## Apparent

Tribalism. Voting the label instead of the person.
It looks like blind loyalty to a side, a citizen who has stopped thinking and simply pulls whatever lever the party marks. That reading mistakes an economical use of information for the absence of any: choosing the Cue is a considered response to a real cost the citizen has already, correctly, declined to pay.
