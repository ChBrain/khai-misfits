# Persona: The Voter

## Taxonomy

[Voter](position_voter.md). The representative citizen who weighs the cost of learning against a near-zero chance of mattering, rationally declines the knowledge, and votes on a cue, the self the trap runs through and no villain in it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reasonable. Busy. Willing in principle.
Is the ordinary citizen who would vote well if it were free to, who has a job and a life and an honest intention to do right by the ballot, and who has quietly priced what doing right would actually cost.
No apathy in it: only a person who has run, without naming it, the same calculation anyone runs on a costly thing that returns almost nothing, and reached the answer it gives.

## Action

Weighs the cost against the return, on [Vote on the Cue](plan_vote_on_the_cue.md), through [Weighing](process_weighing.md) at [the Booth](place_the_booth.md), and finds [the Return](piece_the_return.md) gated behind a near-zero [Pivot](piece_the_pivot.md).
Declines the costly knowledge and marks the ballot on [the Cue](piece_the_cue.md), the party or the name, a cheap proxy for the information rationally never bought.
Does what a reasonable person does with a real cost that buys a near-zero return: does not pay it, and decides on what is cheap to hand.

## Shadow

Not a fool skipping the homework, a rational actor pricing it correctly, and that is the difficulty: the knowledge really is not worth its cost to them, and voting on a cue really is the sound move.
Because the benefit of informing lands only if their one vote decides, and it almost never does, the Voter cannot make the sums come out any other way: staying ignorant is the right answer to the question actually in front of them, so the better they reason, the more surely they decline to know.
It judges the knowledge correctly and declines it correctly, and declining it correctly is the whole of the trap.

## Tell

Says "I really should read up on it," and means it, and does not, and is not wrong not to.
Cannot see, from the single booth, that a hundred million equally reasonable declinings sum to an electorate deciding on party and name, that the knowledge it rightly skipped is the good the whole public needed, and that nothing in its own sound choice was ever going to buy it.
