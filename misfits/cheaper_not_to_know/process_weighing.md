# Process: Weighing

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The reckoning before the vote, run by [the Voter](persona_the_voter.md) on [Vote on the Cue](plan_vote_on_the_cue.md), at [the Booth](place_the_booth.md), in [The Long Odds](plot_the_long_odds.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A citizen with a ballot ahead of them and a real choice about how much to learn first.
The records unread, the measures unstudied, and an honest question standing in front of both: whether the hours it would take are hours worth spending.

## Direction

Toward the sum the arithmetic actually gives.
[The Cost](piece_the_cost.md) of mastering the records and the measures is priced against the benefit of a better-informed vote, and the benefit is found gated behind [the Pivot](piece_the_pivot.md), the near-zero chance that this one vote decides anything, so [the Return](piece_the_return.md) comes out close to nothing and the weighing resolves toward the cue rather than the knowledge.

## Lever

A real cost meeting a near-zero return.
Because the benefit of informing lands only if the vote is decisive, and in a mass election that chance is vanishingly small, a real cost that buys a near-zero return is a cost a reasonable person does not pay, so ignorance is rational here, not apathy, not incuriosity, the correct answer to the sum actually in front of the Voter (Downs).

## Echo

The deep knowledge declined, and the cue reached for instead.
Nothing was skipped by accident: the Voter priced the records and the measures correctly and found them not worth their cost, so what is left standing in the calculation's place is [the Cue](piece_the_cue.md), the cheap proxy for the knowledge that was never going to pay for itself.
