# Persona: The Diligent

## Taxonomy

[Diligent](position_diligent.md). The rare citizen who masters the issues at real cost and casts one vote as unpivotal as any other, changing no outcome, the one who tries the obvious cure and finds it buys the result nothing.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Conscientious. Thorough. Doing it properly.
Is the citizen who reads every ballot measure, checks every record, follows what the offices do, and comes to the booth genuinely informed, at real cost to their evenings.
No fault in the effort: the homework is done well and the knowledge is real, exactly the knowledge the electorate as a whole was missing.

## Action

Does the homework, on [Do the Homework](plan_do_the_homework.md), through [Informing](process_informing.md), paying [the Cost](piece_the_cost.md) in full to master the records and the measures.
Casts [the Vote](piece_the_vote.md) at [the Booth](place_the_booth.md), a single ballot as unpivotal as the most ignorant, so [the Return](piece_the_return.md) on all that study lands, for the outcome, at near zero.
Does what a conscientious citizen does: learns it all, votes well, and moves the result by exactly the weight of one vote among millions.

## Shadow

Not a hero who fixes the electorate, a citizen who paid for a cure that changes nothing, and that is the difficulty: the diligence is real and the outcome is unmoved.
Because one informed vote is as unlikely to decide the election as one ignorant vote, the Diligent buys the private satisfaction of knowing and buys the result none of the good it needed, so the homework that should have been the answer is a personal expense that secures no public good, and doing it well changes only the doer.
It does everything right, and doing everything right leaves the verdict exactly where it was.

## Tell

Says "if everyone just did their homework," and is right that they would, and cannot make everyone, or themselves, be more than one vote.
Does not quite see that its own effort, however complete, ran into the same arithmetic as everyone's, that being informed and being pivotal are not the same thing, and that the knowing it paid so dearly for was worth having and worth, to the outcome, almost nothing.
