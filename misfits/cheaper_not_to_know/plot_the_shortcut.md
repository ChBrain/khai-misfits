# Plot: The Shortcut

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Booth](place_the_booth.md), the ballot still to be marked.
[The Long Odds](plot_the_long_odds.md) has already ruled out the costly knowledge; the vote itself was never declined along with it.

## Action

[Weighing](process_weighing.md) resolves into the mark itself: [The Voter](persona_the_voter.md), following [Vote on the Cue](plan_vote_on_the_cue.md) from [the voter's seat](position_voter.md), decides on [the Cue](piece_the_cue.md), the party, the name, the endorsement, in place of the knowledge rationally never bought, and casts [the Vote](piece_the_vote.md).

## Stage

Having priced the deep knowledge and declined it, the Voter still needs a mark to make, and reaches for what is cheap to hand: a party label recognized on sight, a name familiar from somewhere, the word of someone trusted. Each cue is a low-cost proxy standing in for the study that was never worth its hours, chosen not because it is easy but because it roughly sorts the choice at a price the Voter can actually pay. The ballot is marked, one vote among millions, cast on the shortcut rather than the study.

## Tension

Nothing about the choice is careless: the cue is a sound instrument in the hands of someone who has already priced the alternative and found it too dear. The deep knowledge is skipped, not from folly, but because [The Long Odds](plot_the_long_odds.md) already settled the matter, and the beat closes on a ballot honestly cast, on the one basis it was ever going to be cast on.
