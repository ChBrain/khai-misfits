# Plot: The Homework

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Booth](place_the_booth.md), where one citizen arrives having chosen differently.
Where [The Voter](persona_the_voter.md) priced the knowledge and declined it, [The Diligent](persona_the_diligent.md) prices the same knowledge and pays.

## Action

[Informing](process_informing.md): [The Diligent](persona_the_diligent.md), holding [the diligent's seat](position_diligent.md), carries out [Do the Homework](plan_do_the_homework.md), paying [the Cost](piece_the_cost.md) in full to read every measure and check every record, then casts [the Vote](piece_the_vote.md), as unpivotal as any other.

## Stage

The Diligent spends the evenings the Voter declined to spend, working through the ballot measures and the candidates' records until the knowledge is genuinely held, a real cost paid in full rather than priced and refused. That knowledge changes what the Diligent believes and how the ballot is marked, but it does not change how much the ballot weighs: one vote, cast among millions, carrying the same near-zero chance of deciding the outcome that an uninformed ballot carries. The homework is real, the ballot is informed, and the count does not ask how it was arrived at.

## Tension

The diligence buys the private good of knowing and buys the outcome nothing, because being informed and being pivotal were never the same thing. No one did anything wrong here either: the Diligent paid a real cost for a real good, and the good it bought was not, and was never going to be, a changed result.
