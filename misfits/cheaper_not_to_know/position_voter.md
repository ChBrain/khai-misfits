# Position: Voter

## Taxonomy

[Cheaper Not to Know](play_cheaper_not_to_know.md). The seat of the representative citizen, who weighs a real cost of learning against a near-zero chance of mattering and rationally decides on a cue, sound in every choice and half-blind in the sum.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

One ballot and a busy life: the standing to cast a single vote, and a real, finite budget of time and attention it would cost to learn enough to cast it well.
A clean case: the knowledge is available and the seat is willing in principle, so nothing bars it from informing except the price, weighed against what the informing returns.
No power to make its one vote more than one among millions, so no way to raise the return on learning above the near-zero its own pivotality sets.

## Orders

Weigh, then vote on the cue.
Price the cost of mastering the records and measures against the benefit, find the benefit gated behind a vanishing chance of deciding, and decline a cost that buys a near-zero return.
Then cast the vote anyway, on the cheap proxy at hand, the party or the name, because the ballot must be marked and the deep knowledge was correctly judged too dear.

## Loses

The knowledge that would have made its vote a good one, and gives it up correctly.
Because the seat can only realize the benefit of learning if its one vote decides, and its one vote almost never decides, it rationally surrenders the costly knowledge, so what it loses is not to any failing but to a sound judgment that the price exceeds the return.
It keeps its time and its good sense and loses the informed choice, and loses it again every election, because the arithmetic is the same each time.

## Drives

Toward a citizen reasonable in every particular and uninformed in the aggregate,
declining the costly knowledge as the sums require and voting on the cheap cue instead,
so the seat that does nothing wrong is one of the hundred million whose rational refusals sum to a half-blind electorate, at no fault of its own.
