# Piece: The Lapse

## Taxonomy

[Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md). The permitted worse act, charged to the credit, that brings the balance back to level.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the indulgence](place_the_indulgence.md), where the pass is spent.
The lesser thing, taken as a treat, often far from the good deed's domain.

## Load Bearing

Without it the license would be an unused permission and the good deed would stand.
With it the permission is realised as an act: the lapse is the lesser thing done on the credit, the withdrawal that draws the balance back down to the set point the good deed lifted it above. The Lapse is load bearing because it is where the virtue is actually spent (Mazar and Zhong on green-then-cheat): it is the moment the good deed is undone, and because it arrives feeling earned, a reward rather than a fall, its cost is never charged against the virtue that funded it.

## Apparent

A small, earned treat.
An act waved through as deserved looks like a harmless indulgence, so the lapse reads as a reward, and the truth that it is a withdrawal against a real virtue, the good deed cancelled and the balance returned to level, stays hidden behind the warmth of feeling it was owed.
