# Piece: The Wash

## Taxonomy

[Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md). The net: the good deed and the lapse it funded cancelling to nothing, so the virtue adds no good.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the indulgence](place_the_indulgence.md), where the account totals.
Where the good deed and the lapse are set, at last, against each other.

## Load Bearing

Without it the licensing would be harmless, a good deed followed by a treat with no cost.
With it the misfit has its harm: the wash is the netting of the good deed against the lapse it licensed, so the virtue does not raise the conduct but buys permission to lower it, and the total good ends level, or below. The Wash is load bearing because it is the finding that stings (Merritt, Effron and Monin): being good frees us to be bad, so a good deed can reduce the good a person does, and the more a virtue is banked and spent, the more reliably it comes, at the end, to nothing.

## Apparent

A person who is, on balance, decent.
A good deed and a small lapse look like the ordinary mix of any life, so the wash reads as simply being human, and the truth that the two were causally linked, the virtue spent precisely to license the vice, that the good added nothing because it paid for the fall, stays hidden behind how normal the mix appears.

## Yearbook

Totalled through [cashing](process_cashing.md), the net [the lapse](piece_the_lapse.md) makes of [the good deed](piece_the_good_deed.md).
It is the harm the misfit lands: the Wash is virtue spent to zero, the reason [moral licensing](piece_moral_licensing.md) means a good deed can buy permission to do less good, not more.
