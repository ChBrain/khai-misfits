## Shown

A real good thing being done, for its own sake, and helping where it lands.
The doer at it sincerely, and the warm certainty, afterward, of having been good.

## Holds

The good deed and its credit.
Here the virtue is genuine and here it becomes a proof, so the good turn holds both the real act that helps and the credential it mints, the deed and the deposit it will be read as.

## Offers

A true good, and the honest warmth of having done it.
The deed helps where it falls and proves the doer good, so the good turn offers real virtue and the deserved feeling of it, an end that is genuinely an end.

## Withheld

Any sign that the proof is a deposit.
The deed feels like an end, not a credit, so the good turn withholds the fact that its warmth is about to be banked and spent, and hands its proof to the account with no mark that it can be drawn down.
