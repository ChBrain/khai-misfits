# Moral Licensing: Reference

The research warrant for [Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md). The title is Monin and Miller's
moral licensing; the play runs on the account of moral self-regulation, from the credential studies
through the sinning-saints balancing model, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging moral licensing as a **cognitive system**: a moral self kept to a set level rather than a
maximum, so that a genuine good deed is banked as a credit, read as a surplus above the line, and
spent as a licence for a later lapse, often in an unrelated domain, so the virtue does not add to the
good but buys permission to do less of it. The play models the documented mechanics, the good deed
that is real and earned, the balance that treats morality as a level to maintain, the license that
turns a surplus into a pass, the lapse that spends it and brings the balance back down, the wash by
which the two net to nothing, and the earned rest that is a true due indistinguishable from the
licensed fall, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of hypocrisy. The
governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: establishing one's moral
credentials, doing or affirming a good, licenses subsequent behaviour that would otherwise threaten
the self-image, so being good frees us to be bad, and a good deed can reduce the total good a person
does. It is a self-regulation misfit, distinct from the house's concealment misfits: nothing is
hidden here, the books are honest, and it is distinct too from the house's Moral Hazard, which shifts
risk to another rather than spending one's own virtue.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the doer
  as a hypocrite, because the source finding is precisely that the licensing is not conscious deceit:
  the good deed is genuine, the credit is really earned, and the balancing is the ordinary machinery
  of a moral self-image, which keeps books and feels entitled to spend a surplus. The misfit is that
  this honest accounting of a true virtue is exactly what lets it be spent, so a good person lets
  themselves off, this once, on the strength of a good that was real.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that
  works is to hold the standard regardless of the credit, and it saps the will it depends on, since a
  bar that credits virtue with nothing wearies the motive to be virtuous, curdles into a joyless
  self-watching, and cannot tell a licensed lapse from a rest that was honestly earned. The reckoning
  is a good made to add at the price of the motive and the rest, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the moral credential, the self-licensing
  synthesis, and the self-regulation set-point are theirs, and the condition the effect actually
  requires, a genuine good deed and a self held to a level rather than a maximum, is carried as
  carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Good Deed](piece_the_good_deed.md)**: carries the genuine moral act (Monin & Miller), the real virtue whose
  earned credit the self banks.
- **[The Balance](piece_the_balance.md)**: carries the self-regulation set-point (Sachdeva, Iliev & Medin), morality held
  to a level, so a rise above becomes spendable slack.
- **[The License](piece_the_license.md)**: carries the moral credential (Monin & Miller; Khan & Dhar), the permission the
  credit issues for a later lapse.
- **[The Lapse](piece_the_lapse.md)**: carries the licensed behaviour (Mazar & Zhong), the worse act taken on the credit,
  often in an unrelated domain.
- **[The Wash](piece_the_wash.md)**: carries the net effect (Merritt, Effron & Monin), the good deed and the lapse
  cancelling, so being good frees the doer to be bad.
- **[The Earned Rest](piece_the_earned_rest.md)**: carries the legitimate slack after real good, the true due indistinguishable
  from the licensed fall, and the hazard it poses to the cure.
- **[Moral Licensing](piece_moral_licensing.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Monin & Miller), the good deed banked and
  spent as a licence, and its remedy in a held standard at a cost.
