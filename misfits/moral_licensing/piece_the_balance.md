# Piece: The Balance

## Taxonomy

[Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md). The moral self kept to a set level: the accounting that turns a good deed into spendable credit.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the account](place_the_account.md), where the books are kept.
In the set point the self holds itself to, neither saint nor sinner.

## Load Bearing

Without it a good deed would simply be good, with nothing to draw against.
With it the good becomes a surplus: the balance is the self-image regulated toward a level rather than a maximum, so a rise above the line is slack to spend and a fall below is a debt to repair. The Balance is load bearing because it is the engine of the whole misfit (Sachdeva, Iliev and Medin on moral self-regulation): it treats morality as a homeostat, a level to maintain and not a direction to climb, so the very mechanism that keeps a person decent is the mechanism that lets a good deed license a lapse.

## Apparent

Just a fair sense of oneself.
A self that keeps rough books, correcting when low and easing when high, looks like ordinary moral balance, so the balance reads as healthy self-regulation, and the truth that a set point spends surpluses rather than banking them, that it holds virtue level instead of letting it climb, stays hidden behind how reasonable keeping a balance seems.
