# Plot: The Reckoning

## Taxonomy

[Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The reckoning](place_the_reckoning.md), a move to stop the good deed buying a pass.
The proposal is to hold the standard flat, grant no slack for virtue, and treat the self as a work in progress rather than an account to spend.

## Action

[Holding](process_holding.md): [The Consistent](persona_the_consistent.md), holding [the standard's seat](position_standard.md), refuses to credit the good deed as licence, while [The Motivator](persona_the_motivator.md), holding [the motive's seat](position_motive.md), warns that virtue uncounted goes undone and the earned rest is real.

## Stage

[The Consistent](persona_the_consistent.md) tables [Hold the Line](plan_hold_the_line.md) and answers [moral licensing](piece_moral_licensing.md) by refusing [the license](piece_the_license.md), so the good can add.
[The Motivator](persona_the_motivator.md) tables [Keep It Worth Doing](plan_keep_it_worth_doing.md), defending [the earned rest](piece_the_earned_rest.md) and, with it, the door the licence needs.

## Tension

The one move that makes virtue add cannot be made without a price it cannot see clearly.
Holding the standard flat is the only way the good stops netting, but it drains the very motive that counting virtue would feed, since a bar that credits goodness with nothing wearies the will to be good. And it cannot cut clean: the earned rest and the licensed lapse are the same shape, so refusing the pass risks refusing a due. It closes with the good made to add, or the will sapped by a joyless vigilance, or a true rest denied as though it were a fall.
