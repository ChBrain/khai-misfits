# Piece: Moral Licensing

## Taxonomy

[Moral Licensing](play_moral_licensing.md). The phenomenon whole: a good deed banked and spent as a licence for a later lapse, and its remedy in a held standard at a cost.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the reckoning](place_the_reckoning.md), where the standard is held or the licence stands.
And across the whole self, in every good deed that becomes a credit and every lapse it pays for.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would be a heap of deeds and treats with no shape.
With it the moves become one law: the good deed, the balance, the license, the lapse, the wash, and the earned rest are facets of a single mechanism, in which a moral self kept to a level banks a true good deed as credit and spends it as permission, so the virtue does not add to the good but buys leave to do less of it. Moral Licensing is load bearing because it is the whole finding with its whole remedy attached (Monin and Miller; Merritt, Effron and Monin): the only answer is to hold the standard regardless of the credit, a refusal that makes virtue add but saps the will that counting it would feed and denies a rest it cannot tell from a lapse.

## Apparent

Either a decent person having a small treat, or a hypocrite exposed.
Seen from one side the good deed and the lapse are just the ordinary mix of a life; from the other, the lapse unmasks the virtue as a sham; and both miss the structure, that the good deed was genuine and the balancing unconscious and the credit really earned, so neither indulgence nor accusation answers it, only holding the standard so the good cannot be spent, which is the one thing the self-image that keeps a balance is built not to do.

## Yearbook

Answered by [the consistent](persona_the_consistent.md) through [holding](process_holding.md), held whole by nobody.
