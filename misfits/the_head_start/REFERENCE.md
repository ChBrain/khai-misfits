# The Head Start: Reference

The research warrant for [The Head Start](play_the_head_start.md). The title names the mechanism;
the play runs on the Matthew effect and the theory of cumulative advantage, from Merton's naming
through the network and inequality accounts, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Matthew effect as a **systemic system**: an early advantage, sometimes earned by a hair
and sometimes by luck, attracts the credit, resources, and attention that produce the next advantage,
so a small initial lead compounds into a large one independent of any widening difference in merit.
The play models the documented mechanics, the conferred lead, the halo that reads it as quality, the
flow of resources to the visible, the credit accruing to the already-eminent, the widening gap, the
equally able overlooked, and the blind allocation that alone breaks the loop, as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of pure talent. The governing finding of the source literature
is the play's governing law: to those who have, more is given, because having is read as deserving and
becomes the resource that makes the next having.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the star as
  a fraud or the patron as corrupt, because the source finding is precisely that each choice is rational:
  the leader really was, at first, a hair better or merely luckier, and backing the proven, attending to the
  visible, and crediting the known name are the safe, sensible bets every time. The misfit is that the sum of
  sensible bets manufactures a gap and then reads it as merit.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the deserving simply rising. No burst of talent
  from the overlooked overtakes a self-sustaining lead, because the lead is fed by visibility, not by the
  contest; the one move that works is structural, blinding the allocation so reward decouples from standing,
  and it costs the real signal that following a proven leader carried. The gap does not close on merit; it is
  reset, at a price.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the naming, the cumulative-advantage mechanism, and the
  network law are theirs, and the condition the Matthew effect actually requires, an advantage read as a signal
  and convertible into the next advantage, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Lead](piece_the_lead.md)**: carries the small early advantage (DiPrete & Eirich), the initial difference,
  earned or lucky, that the rest will compound from.
- **[The Halo](piece_the_halo.md)**: carries the lead read as quality (Merton), status taken as a proxy for merit.
- **[The Flow](piece_the_flow.md)**: carries resources and attention accruing to the visible (Price; Barabási &
  Albert), the preferential attachment of the field.
- **[The Compounding](piece_the_compounding.md)**: carries success breeding success (Price; DiPrete & Eirich), the
  loop that turns a lead into a larger lead.
- **[The Gap](piece_the_gap.md)**: carries the widening inequality (DiPrete & Eirich), the distance between comparable
  performers grown far past their difference in ability.
- **[The Overlooked](piece_the_overlooked.md)**: carries the equally able who get less (Merton; Zuckerman), the
  forty-first chair and the uncredited peer.
- **[The Reset](piece_the_reset.md)**: carries the remedy (Goldin & Rouse; practitioner), blind allocation that
  decouples reward from standing.
- **[The Matthew](piece_the_matthew.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Merton; DiPrete & Eirich), to those who have
  more is given, and its remedy in blinding.
