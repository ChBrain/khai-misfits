# The Tyranny of Small Decisions: Reference

The research warrant for [The Tyranny of Small Decisions](play_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md).
The title is Kahn's; the play runs on the account of aggregated choice and market failure, from Kahn's
essay through Schelling's micromotives and the option-value literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the tyranny of small decisions as a **social system**: a large outcome that is never put to
anyone as a question, settled instead by the sum of small choices each of which is individually
rational and about itself, so an option no one would have chosen to end is thinned past its threshold
and lost for everyone, including those who valued it. The play models the documented mechanics, the
small choice that is free and correct, the missing forum where the aggregate is never decided, the sum
that settles the whole by default, the option value that no single choice protects, the
irreversibility that makes the loss permanent, and the free choice that most small decisions rightly
are, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of foolishness or greed. The
governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: a large decision that is never
made as a single choice can be settled by the accumulation of many small ones that were never about
it, producing an outcome that no one chose and that many would have rejected had it been put to them
directly. It is distinct from the house's Commons, where a shared resource is overdrawn because each
taking's cost is split among all: here nothing is shared and overdrawn, an aggregate is settled by
default for want of a forum, and what is often lost is an option's mere availability. It is distinct,
too, from the house's Everybody's Business, where a single decision everyone could own is dropped
because each assumes another will: here everyone is deciding, freely and well, and the undecided thing
is the whole their choices sum to.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  chooser as foolish or greedy, because the source finding is precisely that each small choice is free
  and correct: the convenient option is genuinely the better one, cheap and reversible and its own, and
  no single choice is decisive or wrong. The loss is authored by no one and assembled by all, emerging
  from the structure, the missing forum, and not from any bad act, so the misfit is that a thing no one
  chose is settled by choices each of which was exactly right.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that
  could keep a thing worth keeping is to convene the aggregate, price the option, and put the whole to
  a real decision, and it overrides the free small choices it is made of, cannot know in advance which
  sums will cost something irreplaceable, and most small decisions should stay small and free, so the
  cure for a tyranny of small decisions is a tyranny of large ones. The reckoning is a liberty gathered
  up to save an option, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the tyranny of small decisions, the
  micromotives account, the ecological application, and option value are theirs, and the condition the
  trap actually requires, a large outcome with no forum to decide it and an option whose value no small
  choice protects, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Small Choice](piece_the_small_choice.md)**: carries the individually rational decision (Kahn), free, correct, and
  about itself, from which the loss is assembled.
- **[The Missing Forum](piece_the_missing_forum.md)**: carries the absent aggregate decision point (Kahn), the large question
  that is never put to anyone.
- **[The Sum](piece_the_sum.md)**: carries the emergent collective outcome (Schelling), the whole settled by the residue of
  choices never about it.
- **[The Option Value](piece_the_option_value.md)**: carries the worth of availability (Weisbrod), held even by non-users and
  protected by no single choice.
- **[The Irreversibility](piece_the_irreversibility.md)**: carries the permanence of the loss (Odum), the threshold crossed
  unnoticed and not cheaply undone.
- **[The Free Choice](piece_the_free_choice.md)**: carries the genuine good of leaving small decisions small, the liberty the
  remedy must override.
- **[The Tyranny of Small Decisions](piece_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Kahn), a large
  outcome no one chose settled by right small choices, and its remedy in a convened decision at a cost.
