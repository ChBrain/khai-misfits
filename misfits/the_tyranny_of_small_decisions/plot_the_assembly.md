# Plot: The Assembly

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of Small Decisions](play_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The assembly](place_the_assembly.md), a move to decide the whole in time.
The proposal is to build the forum the small choices never pass through: put the large question to a vote, price the option, bundle the small into the big.

## Action

[Convening](process_convening.md): [The Convener](persona_the_convener.md), holding [the convener's seat](position_convener.md), would decide the aggregate on purpose, while [The Free Hand](persona_the_free_hand.md), holding [the liberty seat](position_liberty.md), warns what the forum takes.

## Stage

[The Convener](persona_the_convener.md) tables [Put the Whole to a Vote](plan_put_the_whole_to_a_vote.md) and answers [the tyranny of small decisions](piece_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md) by convening the whole before it settles.
[The Free Hand](persona_the_free_hand.md) tables [Leave It Free](plan_leave_it_free.md), holding that overriding [the free choice](piece_the_free_choice.md) is a tyranny of its own.

## Tension

The one move that could keep a thing worth keeping smothers the liberty it is made of.
Convening the aggregate is the only way to decide the whole on purpose, and it works by taking the small choices from the people making them and betting on which sums are worth the taking. The free hand is right that most small decisions should stay small, and the convener is right that a few of them sum to an unchosen ruin, and neither can tell in advance which is which. It closes with the option kept on purpose, or free choice smothered to save it, or the aggregate convened too late, or for a sum that never needed it.
