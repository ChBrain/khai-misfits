# Plot: The Fork

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of Small Decisions](play_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The junction](place_the_junction.md), a small choice offered at each turn.
The convenient option against the one that keeps the alternative alive, and the convenient one is, plainly, the sensible pick.

## Action

[Choosing](process_choosing.md): [The Chooser](persona_the_chooser.md), holding [the chooser's seat](position_chooser.md), takes the convenient option, freely and well, while [The Option](persona_the_option.md), holding [the option's seat](position_option.md), loses one more unit of the custom it lives on.

## Stage

[The Chooser](persona_the_chooser.md) tables [Take the Convenient One](plan_take_the_convenient_one.md) and makes [the small choice](piece_the_small_choice.md), right and light and its own.
[The Option](persona_the_option.md) tables [Stay Available](plan_stay_available.md) and thins, its [option value](piece_the_option_value.md) unprotected by any single pick.

## Tension

The right choice is made, and the alternative is a little closer to gone.
Nothing here is a mistake: the convenient way is genuinely better for the chooser, cheap and reversible, and no one owes the option a trip they do not want. But the option is worth more than any single use and is defended by none of them, so each free, correct choice withdraws a share of a thing valued for being there, and the fork, repeated by everyone, is quietly the whole trap in miniature.
