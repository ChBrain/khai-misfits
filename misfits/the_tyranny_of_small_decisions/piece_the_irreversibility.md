# Piece: The Irreversibility

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of Small Decisions](play_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md). The permanence of the option's closing: once gone, it cannot be cheaply revived.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the vacancy](place_the_vacancy.md), where the loss is already permanent.
In the gap between a threshold crossed and any way back.

## Load Bearing

Without it the aggregate could be undone once seen, and the small choices reversed at leisure.
With it the loss locks: the irreversibility is the fact that the option, once dropped past its threshold, cannot be cheaply brought back, so a gradual thinning becomes a sudden and permanent absence. The Irreversibility is load bearing because it is why the missing forum stings and the vote comes too late: each small choice was reversible, but their sum is not, so the harm crosses, unnoticed, from a thing anyone could have changed into a thing no later choice can, and the large question can be asked only over the space where the answer no longer matters.

## Apparent

Just the way things end.
An alternative that closes and does not come back looks like the ordinary mortality of things, so the irreversibility reads as nature taking its course, and the truth that it turns a sum of reversible small choices into an unrecoverable loss, that the point of no return was crossed with no one deciding to cross it, stays hidden behind how normally closed things stay closed.

## Yearbook

Felt by [the bereft](persona_the_bereft.md) through [losing](process_losing.md), tabled as [Miss It Too Late](plan_miss_it_too_late.md).
It is the lock on the loss: the Irreversibility makes [the option value](piece_the_option_value.md) unrecoverable once the threshold is crossed, so the vote that [Put the Whole to a Vote](plan_put_the_whole_to_a_vote.md) would call comes, if at all, too late.
