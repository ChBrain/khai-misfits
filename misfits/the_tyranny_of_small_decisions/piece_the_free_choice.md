# Piece: The Free Choice

## Taxonomy

[The Tyranny of Small Decisions](play_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md). The genuine good of leaving small decisions to the people making them, what the remedy threatens.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the assembly](place_the_assembly.md), where the remedy would gather it up.
In every small choice that rightly belongs to the one making it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the cure would be simple: convene every aggregate and decide the whole.
With it the cure is a hazard: the free choice is the real value of leaving small decisions small, made by individuals rather than voted on, and it is a good, not a flaw, so the convener's forum overrides something worth keeping. The Free Choice is load bearing because it is what makes the remedy costly and the misfit have no hero: most small decisions should stay small and free, and the convener cannot know in advance which sums will cost something irreplaceable, so the cure for a tyranny of small decisions is itself a tyranny of large ones, gathering up liberties that ought to have stayed loose.

## Apparent

Either sacred liberty or reckless anarchy, depending who is asked.
To the free hand it can look like all small choices to be left alone; to the convener, all sums to be gathered and decided; and both are wrong, because most small choices should stay free and a few of them sum to ruin, so the free choice reads as one or the other, and the truth that the same liberty is mostly a good and occasionally the road to an unchosen loss stays hidden behind whichever case is being pressed.

## Yearbook

Defended by [the free hand](persona_the_free_hand.md) within [convening](process_convening.md), the good [Leave It Free](plan_leave_it_free.md) guards and [Put the Whole to a Vote](plan_put_the_whole_to_a_vote.md) must override.
It is the liberty tangled in the cure: the Free Choice is why convening the aggregate is never clean, the reason answering [the tyranny of small decisions](piece_the_tyranny_of_small_decisions.md) risks a tyranny of its own.
