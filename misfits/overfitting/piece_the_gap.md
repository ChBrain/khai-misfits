# Piece: The Gap

## Taxonomy

[Overfitting](play_overfitting.md). The distance between the model's error on the sample and its error on the world, the whole of the overfitting made into one number.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the field](place_the_field.md), where new data is met and the two errors stand side by side.
Rehearsed early at [the holdout](place_the_holdout.md), where held-back data reads it before the field can.

## Load Bearing

Without it the overfitting would have no measure, only a vague sense that the model does worse than it promised.
With it the finding is exact: the gap is the training error subtracted from the true error, the memorised noise counted in a single figure, and it is invisible where the fit is made and legible only where new data arrives. The Gap is load bearing because it is the overfitting itself, quantified (Geman, Bienenstock and Doursat; Vapnik): the wider the fit reached past the signal, the larger the gap, so it is both the symptom read in the field and the exact size of what the fitting should not have learned.

## Apparent

A disappointment, a model that tested well and then let everyone down.
Seen from use, the gap looks like a surprise or a fluke, the model simply not living up to its training score, and the truth that it was written the whole time, growing turn by turn as the fit crossed into noise and only unreadable until new data came, stays hidden behind how sudden the failure feels.

## Yearbook

Read by [the unseen](persona_the_unseen.md) through [failing](process_failing.md), the verdict [the score](piece_the_score.md) could not give.
It is the overfitting counted: the Gap is what [the fit](piece_the_fit.md) opened by learning [the noise](piece_the_noise.md) past [the signal](piece_the_signal.md), the reading [the holdout](place_the_holdout.md) offers early, and the quantity [the regularizer](persona_the_regularizer.md) works at [the retuning](place_the_retuning.md) to close.
