# Piece: The Signal

## Taxonomy

[Overfitting](play_overfitting.md). The true pattern beneath the data, the part that holds from one draw to the next and is worth learning.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the training set](place_the_training_set.md), mixed through the sample with the noise.
The real regularity the model is meant to catch, present in this draw and every other.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing to learn, and overfitting could not be told from mere error.
With it the difficulty has its shape: the signal is the pattern that generalises, the thing catching it is genuine learning and the whole point of the fit, so the trouble is not that fitting is wrong but that the signal cannot, inside the sample, be cleanly separated from the noise beside it. The Signal is load bearing because it is what makes the fitting worth doing and the memorising a betrayal of it (Vapnik): a model that caught only the signal would generalise perfectly, and the misfit is exactly that no in-sample measure can stop the fit at the signal's edge.

## Apparent

Just the low training error, indistinguishable from the noise's share of it.
Inside the sample the signal shows only as the part of the fit that happens to hold up later, and nothing marks it as it is learned, so catching the signal looks identical to memorising the noise, and the truth that only one of them will return in the next draw stays hidden until that draw arrives.

## Yearbook

Carried by [the sample](persona_the_sample.md), caught by [the fitter](persona_the_fitter.md) through [fitting](process_fitting.md).
It is the thing worth learning: the Signal is what [the fit](piece_the_fit.md) should capture and stop at, what [the score](piece_the_score.md) cannot separate from [the noise](piece_the_noise.md), and the real pattern that a heavy hand at [the retuning](place_the_retuning.md) risks cutting into as [the underfit](piece_the_underfit.md).
