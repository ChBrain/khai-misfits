# Piece: Overfitting

## Taxonomy

[Overfitting](play_overfitting.md). The phenomenon whole: a model fit so closely to its sample that it learns the noise as signal, explains the past and predicts nothing, and its remedy in a pulled-back fit at a symmetric cost.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the retuning](place_the_retuning.md), where the whole is weighed and the cure and its cost are held together.
And across the sample and the world, in every turn of a fit that learns the pattern and then keeps going.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit would be a scatter of curves and errors with no single law.
With it the moves become one mechanism: the signal, the noise, the fit, the score, the gap, and the underfit are faces of a single finding, in which a model with capacity to spare, guided by a score that cannot tell learning from memorising, fits its sample past the signal into the noise, so the closer it matches the data in hand the worse it does on the world it was built for. Overfitting is load bearing because it is the whole finding with its whole remedy attached (Geman, Bienenstock and Doursat; Vapnik): the only answer is to pull the fit back, penalise complexity, and test on data never fitted, and that scores worse on the sample, cannot locate the true optimum from a finite draw, and pressed too far becomes the underfit, so the cure for a fit too tight is the risk of one too loose.

## Apparent

Either a model that simply needs more tuning, or one that simply failed.
Seen from the training set the low error looks like success and the answer looks like fitting harder; seen from the field the failure looks like a fluke or a bad model; and both miss the structure, that the fitting was right and the score honest and the loss written by the very capacity that let it learn, so neither more fitting nor a better sample answers it, only a fit deliberately held back, tested on data it never saw, and stopped at a point no in-sample measure can find.
