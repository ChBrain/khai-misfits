# Position: Operations

## Taxonomy

[Held Under](play_held_under.md). The seat that can break the loop the system cannot break itself, holding the one lever that ends the hold and the whole of its cost: fail fast, back off, shed load.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The switch and the standing to throw it: the breaker that makes failing calls fail fast, the backoff that spaces the retries, the shed that drops load below capacity.
A hard case: the lever works, and every setting of it throws away real work, so the seat holds the only exit and no version of the exit that keeps every request.
No costless option anywhere in reach, only the choice of when to pay and how much, and so the seat owns the escape and owns the bill for it.

## Orders

Break the loop, and pay for it.
Trip the breaker so the failing calls fail fast instead of feeding the queue, jitter the retries so they stop arriving together, shed load until the sum falls below capacity and the queue can drain.
Do it knowing each move rejects requests that would have succeeded and drops real users to save the rest, because the load holding the system under is correct retries, and the only way to stop them is to refuse the work they carry.

## Loses

The real work it sheds to drain the queue, and the fiction that recovery could be free.
Because the loop is held open by correct behaviour, the seat cannot break it without stopping that behaviour, and stopping it means failing good calls, abandoning wanted work, and dropping users who did nothing wrong, so what it loses is exactly the load it throws overboard to lift the rest.
It surrenders real requests on purpose, and surrendering them on purpose is the price of the only exit there is.

## Drives

Toward a system lifted at a cost or left under at a greater one,
the breaker thrown and the queue draining and the shed work gone, or the switch untouched and the hold going on,
so the seat that alone can end it must choose between the work it throws away now and the work the hold keeps swallowing for as long as it waits.
