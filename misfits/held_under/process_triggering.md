# Process: Triggering

## Taxonomy

[Held Under](play_held_under.md). The single crossing that tips [the Service](persona_the_service.md) out of health, run by [the Surge](persona_the_surge.md) on [Pass Through](plan_pass_through.md), at [the Steady State](place_the_steady_state.md), in [The Spike](plot_the_spike.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A moment where headroom runs thin against ordinary variance: a load spike, a garbage-collection pause, a stalled dependency, nothing exotic. At [the Steady State](place_the_steady_state.md), demand or latency ticks past what the remaining margin can absorb.

## Direction

Toward the threshold, once, and past it. The Surge does not linger and does not escalate: it is a transient, in and gone, and its only lasting effect is to move [the Service](persona_the_service.md) from the healthy equilibrium into the failing one, laying down [the Trigger](piece_the_trigger.md) as the record of the crossing. Once across, what happens next belongs to [Retrying](process_retrying.md) and [Sustaining](process_sustaining.md), not to the Surge; the trigger's size need not match the failure's size, since nothing about it requires duration, only mistiming against a margin that happened to be thin.

## Lever

An ordinary transient that any real system meets, sooner or later, in the normal run of operating it (Bronson, Charapko, Aghayev, and Zhu). It requires no severity and no malice, only bad timing against headroom that was thin at that moment, so nothing about the trigger itself is unusual or blameworthy.

## Echo

The threshold crossed and the cause already gone: by the time the failing state is visible on [the Dashboard](place_the_dashboard.md), the Surge has left nothing behind but the shape it started, and the shape has since become self-sustaining.
