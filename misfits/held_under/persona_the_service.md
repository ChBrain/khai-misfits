# Persona: The Service

## Taxonomy

[Under](position_under.md). The sound system tipped out of health by a passing trigger and held in the failing state by nothing but the correct retries of its own clients, the self the trap is drawn down through.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Healthy. Provisioned. Answering everything that comes.
Is the system that ran with headroom to spare a minute ago, latency low and the board green, built well and overloaded by no one, doing exactly what a service is meant to do.
No fragility in it: only a sound system that was knocked once past a line it never usually crosses, and cannot climb back over it while the load keeps arriving.

## Action

Serves what comes, on [Serve What Comes](plan_serve_what_comes.md), accepting every request and answering as many as it can.
Falls into [the failing state](piece_the_metastable_state.md) when [the Surge](persona_the_surge.md) tips it past the line, then handles the fresh load plus the retries and fails the excess, so more retries come.
Does what a sound system does under a load above its capacity: serves at its limit, fails the overflow, and holds in the bad equilibrium because the overflow is retried straight back onto it.

## Shadow

Not a broken system, a sound one held under, and that is the whole difficulty: nothing about it needs fixing, and it still cannot lift itself.
Because the load pinning it is the sum of its clients each rightly retrying, the Service cannot recover on its own correct behaviour: serving what comes is exactly right, and serving what comes is what keeps it under, so the healthier and more dutiful it is, the more completely it stays failing, held down not by a fault but by the correct recovery of everyone calling it.
It answers every request it can, and answering every request it can is what leaves it buried.

## Tell

Shows a dashboard that reads "overloaded" while the trigger that started it is long gone.
Was slammed, really, only for the second the spike lasted, and cannot see that what is burying it now is not demand but the echo of its own failures, retried back at it by clients doing exactly the right thing.
