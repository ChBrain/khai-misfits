# Persona: The Speculator

## Taxonomy

[The Table](position_the_table.md). Plays for the prize on matching, sets the fundamental aside, and forecasts where the crowd will go rather than what the thing is worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Shrewd. Detached. Reading the room.
Is the trader who knows the game is not to be right about the thing but to be right about everyone else, and who plays it well.
No recklessness in it: only a player answering the payoff as it is set, buying what the room will bid up and selling before it turns.

## Action

Sets aside what the thing is worth and asks the only question that pays: what will the others do, and what do they think the others will do.
Guesses the crowd's guess, then the crowd's guess about the crowd's guess, and takes the position that the tower of expectations points to.
Does what the payoff rewards: anticipates the room, rides the rise it expects the room to make, and calls it reading the market.

## Shadow

Not a gambler, a sound forecaster, and that is the whole difficulty: when the prize is matching the crowd, guessing the crowd is the rational move and valuing the thing correctly against it is the losing one.
Because the reward is the match and not the truth, the Speculator rightly lets go of the fundamental and turns to the others' expectations, so the sharpest, soundest play, best anticipating the room, is the very move that unmoors the price from worth, and the more skilfully the crowd is read the further the price floats from the thing.
It plays the game exactly as the payoff sets it, and playing it well is what prices the thing by opinion about opinion.

## Tell

Says "I am not saying it is worth it: I am saying it is going up, and everyone can see it is going up."
Is right that it is going up, and cannot see that the going-up is the whole of the value now, a price rising because everyone expects everyone to keep buying.
