# The Beauty Contest: Reference

The research warrant for [The Beauty Contest](play_the_beauty_contest.md).
The dynamic is Keynes's beauty contest and the higher-order expectations beneath it: the newspaper-contest
metaphor and the third degree, the guessing-game experiments that measure the levels of reasoning, the
asset-pricing model of iterated expectations, the theory of speculative bubbles, and the limit of arbitrage that
keeps value from disciplining the game, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Keynesian beauty contest as a **higher-order-expectations structure**: when the payoff turns on
matching the crowd rather than on the thing itself, each participant stops asking what a thing is worth and starts
asking what the average opinion expects the average opinion to be, so judgement climbs from the first degree to the
second to the third and beyond, price and choice come to track opinion about opinion, and the market floats free of
any fundamental it is meant to price. The play models the documented mechanics, the payoff rule that rewards
matching the room and not being right, the fundamental value set aside because valuing it correctly against the
crowd loses, the tower of iterated expectations each rational player must climb because everyone else is climbing
it too, the self-referential price that rises because everyone expects everyone to keep buying, and the anchor on
value that alone could discipline the game but is left to bear the divergence, as the load-bearing vectors of the
structure, not as a tale of a mania or a fool. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law:
where the reward is matching the crowd rather than the fact, rational players forecast one another's forecasts in
an unbounded regress, so price is set by opinion about opinion and can detach from value for as long as the game
runs.

It is distinct from the house's Information Cascade, and the distinction is the whole point of the cut: a cascade
runs on sequential inference, each actor reading the actions of those before as private signals and rationally
updating their own belief, so information is the currency and the sequence is the engine, whereas the beauty
contest needs no inference and no sequence, the player need not believe the crowd knows anything, only guess where
the crowd will go, because the payoff is the match and not the truth, a simultaneous regress of belief about belief
rather than a chain of learning. It is also distinct from the house's The Stag Hunt, where a group hedges from a
cooperative prize to a safe payoff under doubt: there the game has two equilibria and doubt selects the inferior
one, here there is no cooperative prize and no fixed equilibrium to select, only the self-referential tower that
produces bubbles and fads, the outcome never settling because each level of the regress moves with the next.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the speculator as a
  fool or a manipulator, because the source finding is precisely that each is playing soundly: when the reward is
  matching the crowd, guessing the crowd is the rational move and valuing the fundamental correctly against it is
  the losing one, so the player who tracks opinion about opinion is doing exactly what the payoff asks. The
  detachment is authored by no one and emerges from a payoff on matching met by rational forecasting, so the misfit
  is that a market of sound forecasters, each rightly anticipating the others, prices a thing by opinion about
  opinion and floats it free of worth, no mania and no fraud required.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into value simply winning out. The one move that could
  discipline the game is to anchor on the fundamental and trade against the crowd, but arbitrage against a
  self-referential price is risky and capital-constrained, so the anchor can be carried out on the tide before the
  price corrects, the mispricing running against them for as long as the game does. The reckoning is a price
  disciplined only by an anchor that may not outlast the divergence, not a fundamental that reasserts on cue, so
  the cure for a market pricing opinion about opinion is a bet on value that can go broke being right too early.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the beauty contest and the third degree
  are Keynes's, the guessing-game measure of bounded reasoning is Nagel's, the iterated-expectations asset model is
  Allen, Morris and Shin's, the feedback theory of bubbles is Shiller's, and the limit of arbitrage that keeps
  value from disciplining the game is Shleifer and Vishny's, with the honest limit, that the fundamental reasserts
  in the long run and the detachment holds only where the game permits, carried as carefully as the finding itself.
