# Piece: The Asymmetry

## Taxonomy

[Vetocracy](play_vetocracy.md). The imbalance that blocking needs one and acting needs all, so the equilibrium tilts to inaction.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the gauntlet](place_the_gauntlet.md), where a proposal meets every gate.
In the gap between the price of a single no and the price of every yes.

## Load Bearing

Without it many checks would still leave many paths, and action could find one.
With it every check is a gate on the only path: the asymmetry is that stopping a thing costs one veto and a reason while moving it costs the assent of all, so the cost of acting rises with each gate and the cost of blocking stays at one. The Asymmetry is load bearing because it is the engine of the whole misfit (Buchanan and Tullock on the consent threshold): it makes the many legitimate checks compound rather than merely add, so a system optimised to prevent bad action becomes one unable to take any, and the tilt toward inaction is structural, not chosen.

## Apparent

A sensible bias toward caution.
A system that is easy to stop and hard to move looks like prudent conservatism, a high bar that only good proposals clear, so the asymmetry reads as care, and the truth that the same bar stops the good proposals too and prices action out entirely stays hidden behind how wise it seems to make change hard.

## Yearbook

Felt by [the mover](persona_the_mover.md) through [paralysing](process_paralysing.md), the imbalance [Get It Through](plan_get_it_through.md) runs against.
It is why the checks compound: the Asymmetry prices acting at every yes and blocking at one, turning [the thicket](piece_the_thicket.md) into a wall and sending most proposals to [the foregone](piece_the_foregone.md).
