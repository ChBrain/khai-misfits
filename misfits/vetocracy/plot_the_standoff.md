# Plot: The Standoff

## Taxonomy

[Vetocracy](play_vetocracy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The chamber](place_the_chamber.md), the paralysis questioned at last.
Someone asks why nothing passes, and each veto-holder answers with the good reason its check was built to serve.

## Action

[Vetoing](process_vetoing.md) defended: [The Sentinel](persona_the_sentinel.md), holding [the sentinel's seat](position_sentinel.md), names each veto as a legitimate check against haste and overreach, while [The Vetoer](persona_the_vetoer.md), holding [the vetoer's seat](position_vetoer.md), holds its own, one guard among many.

## Stage

[The Sentinel](persona_the_sentinel.md) tables [Guard the Checks](plan_guard_the_checks.md) and defends [the guard](piece_the_guard.md), the true protection each veto affords.
[The Vetoer](persona_the_vetoer.md) tables [Block to Protect](plan_block_to_protect.md) and stands on [the veto](piece_the_veto.md), so the block and the guard prove to be one act.

## Tension

The paralysis is made entirely of legitimate checks, and that is exactly why it cannot be undone.
Every no in the chamber is defensible, earned by some past abuse it was built to prevent, so the sentinel is right about each one, and the vetoer is right to hold. But a wall of legitimate guards is still a wall, and because the check that protects and the veto that only obstructs speak in the same words, no one can point to the obstruction to remove it, and the gridlock stands, unanswerable, on the strength of every reason for it.
