# Plot: The Toll

## Taxonomy

[Vetocracy](play_vetocracy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The gauntlet](place_the_gauntlet.md), a veto that can be traded instead of merely cast.
One gate will open, but not for nothing: its keeper knows what a yes is worth to a proposal that needs every one.

## Action

[Vetoing](process_vetoing.md) turned to leverage: [The Holdout](persona_the_holdout.md), holding [the holdout's seat](position_holdout.md), withholds its assent until a price is paid, while [The Mover](persona_the_mover.md), holding [the mover's seat](position_mover.md), needing every yes, pays it.

## Stage

[The Holdout](persona_the_holdout.md) tables [Extract the Price](plan_extract_the_price.md) and works [the extraction](piece_the_extraction.md), trading its yes for a concession.
[The Mover](persona_the_mover.md) tables [Get It Through](plan_get_it_through.md) and pays, bending the proposal to buy the assent it cannot do without.

## Tension

What passes survives only by being bent to every toll it paid.
The holdout's concern is genuine and its price defensible, so the toll cannot be called extortion, just a stakeholder protecting its interest. But the leverage comes from the structural power to halt everything, so each yes is sold and each sale bends the proposal, and by the time it clears, the thing that passes is so laden with concessions that it may no longer be worth passing, the rare success as compromised as the many failures are absent.
