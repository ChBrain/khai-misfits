# Play: Vetocracy

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Vetocracy.
Over time a system grows checks: a review here to guard against haste, a sign-off there to protect a stake, a veto for each interest that might be run over. Every one is added for a good reason, and every one is a legitimate guard. Then a decision must be made, and it has to pass all of them: any single hand can stop it, and no single hand can move it, so the proposal runs a gauntlet where blocking is cheap and cheap again, and acting needs everyone at once. The system keeps its checks and loses its capacity to act ... and the cost is paid not in any visible wreck but in the roads unbuilt, the reforms unpassed, the decisions that quietly never get made, and no one is ever blamed for a thing that never happened.

## Arc

The bend is this: the guards against bad action, each one legitimate, sum to an inability to take any action at all.
It runs on an asymmetry.
To block, you need one veto and a reason; to act, you need every veto-holder to say yes at once. So the cost of stopping a thing is borne by one and the cost of moving it is assembled from all, and as the veto points multiply, the many small legitimate checks compound into one large emergent paralysis.
None of it is malice. Each veto was created to guard something real, against haste, against unaccountable power, against an interest being trampled, and each is used, most often, exactly as intended. Some are held to extract a price, but even those defend a genuine stake, so the paralysis is built entirely of legitimate blocks, chosen by no one as a whole.
Cut the thicket, remove veto points, concentrate the authority to act, and the system can decide again: but every check removed is a guard removed, and the power consolidated to act is the very power the checks existed to restrain, so the cure for a system that cannot act is a step toward one that cannot be stopped, and no one can cleanly tell a veto that is a vital check from a veto that is mere obstruction.

## Triggers

**[The Gauntlet](plot_the_gauntlet.md)**
A decision must be made, and it has to pass every check in turn. The Mover tries to move it through, and the Vetoer, guarding a real stake, blocks it, one legitimate no among many. Closes when the proposal is caught in a gauntlet where any single hand can stop it and no single hand can move it.

**[The Toll](plot_the_toll.md)**
Cue: a veto can be traded as well as cast. The Holdout withholds its assent until a price is paid, and the Mover, needing every yes, pays it, so the proposal survives only by being bought concession by concession. Closes when the veto has been spent as leverage and the thing that passes is bent to the tolls it paid.

**[The Limbo](plot_the_limbo.md)**
Cue: most proposals never assemble every yes. The decision the Mover carried dies unmade, and the Foregone, the road unbuilt and the reform unpassed, joins a pile no one counts. Closes when the cost has been paid in a thing that simply never happened, and no one is blamed for it.

**[The Standoff](plot_the_standoff.md)**
Cue: the paralysis is questioned. The Sentinel defends each veto as a legitimate check, against haste and unaccountable power, and the Vetoer holds, so the block and the guard prove to be the same act. Closes when the paralysis is shown to be built entirely of legitimate checks, indistinguishable from obstruction.

**[The Redesign](plot_the_redesign.md)**
Cue: someone moves to restore the capacity to act. The Consolidator would cut veto points and concentrate authority, against the Sentinel's warning that every check removed is a guard removed and the power to act is the power the checks restrained. Closes when the system can decide again ... or the checks hold and it stays frozen, or a vital guard is cut and the power it held is loosed.

## Stakes

Whether a system can ever act, or only ever be stopped.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the block, which one hand can cast for a real reason, and away from the decision, which needs every hand at once. The production cannot make acting as cheap as blocking, because each veto is a legitimate guard and their sum is a paralysis no one chose, so the system keeps every check and loses the capacity the checks were meant to make safe. It can only show that no one is a villain, that each veto guards something real and is mostly used as meant, that the cost falls invisibly on the decisions never made and lands on no one to blame, and that the one move that restores the capacity to act, cutting the thicket and concentrating authority, removes the guards and loosens the power they held, with no clean line between the check that protects and the veto that only obstructs.
