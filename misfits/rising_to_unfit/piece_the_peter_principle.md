# Piece: The Peter Principle

## Taxonomy

[Rising to Unfit](play_rising_to_unfit.md). The accumulated whole: every post tending to be held by someone risen just past their fit.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

The gap between the rung a person fits and the rung they hold.
Across the whole org chart, seat by seat: each filled by someone who succeeded their way up to it and stopped, so the distance between fitness and position is not one manager's bad luck but the standing shape of the ladder.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of fair promotions and a few people in over their heads.
With it those are one law: a hierarchy that promotes on present success and never demotes on future failure tends, over time, to fill every post with an employee risen to their level of incompetence, held whole by nobody, because the seat that promotes is never the seat that lives the result, and no seat is answerable for the fit of the whole. The competent work, meanwhile, is done by those still rising, who have not yet reached the rung that stops them.

## Apparent

A meritocracy.
From every seat it is fair: each person was promoted for real excellence, each rise was earned, the system rewarded the best. The trap is invisible from within because there is no unfair promotion to point to, only a fair rule that reads the wrong signal, applied honestly, all the way up.

## Yearbook

The ladder built it in good faith: [the mastery](piece_the_mastery.md) rewarded, [the wrong signal](piece_the_wrong_signal.md) read, [the one-way ladder](piece_the_one_way_ladder.md) holding, [the stranded master](piece_the_stranded_master.md) left in place.
[The next](persona_the_next.md) is already climbing the same slope, promoted for the job below toward the job above, so the principle does not resolve, it recurs. Nobody had a name for the shape of the chart, because every seat on it was filled by someone who deserved to rise ... to exactly one rung too far.
