# Play: Rising to Unfit

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Rising to Unfit.
A career climbing on merit, rung by well-earned rung, each promotion a reward for doing the last job brilliantly ... until the rung whose job needs something the last one did not, where the climbing stops and the person stays, exactly as high as they are no longer good.

## Arc

The bend is this: every promotion is deserved, and the ladder fills with people in the wrong jobs.
It runs on two lines that the promotion couples wrongly.
One line is mastery of the current role, which earns the move up: do the job well, and be rewarded.
The other line is fitness for the next role, which is a different job needing different gifts, and is not what the reward was measured on.
So the promotion reads the first line and installs against the second, and because mastery of one rung barely predicts fitness for the next, a person rises as long as each new role happens to suit them and stops at the first that does not. There they lodge, because a ladder that lifts on success cannot lower on failure without calling the last promotion a mistake, so no one is moved back, and the post is held, from then on, by someone risen just past their fit.
Reward people for the job they will do, not the one they have done, and the ladder stops installing its own misfits: the promotion is the story.

## Triggers

**[Wrong Signal](plot_wrong_signal.md)**
Someone is brilliant at their craft, and the calibration reads that brilliance as the signal to promote. The Natural masters the role, the Rewarder moves to reward it with a bigger one. Closes when the best is promoted on the strength of the job they are leaving.

**[Level of Incompetence](plot_level_of_incompetence.md)**
Cue: the new role needs different gifts than the old one. The fit runs out, the numbers regress, and the risen person reaches the rung they cannot do. Closes when the promotion has landed a master in a job that is not theirs, and the executive who blessed it cannot unwind it.

**[One-Way Ladder](plot_one_way_ladder.md)**
Cue: the ladder that lifts on success will not lower on failure. No demotion follows, the reports route around the stuck manager, and the post is held by someone past their fit. Closes when the role is permanently occupied by a misfit, and the work goes on around them.

**[Backfill](plot_backfill.md)**
Cue: the real work needs doing, so the next star is pulled up to do it, and set on the same track. Closes when the Peter Principle is named ... or the next Natural is promoted, and rises to their own unfit.

## Stakes

Whether anyone is ever rewarded for the job ahead instead of the job behind.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward fit, toward the next rung. The production cannot make a promotion predict a different role from the one it measured. It can only show that every rise was earned, that no one was foolish and no one malicious, and that the one design that would have worked, reward people for the job they will do, was never the one the ladder ran on, because the job they had done was the one that could be seen.
