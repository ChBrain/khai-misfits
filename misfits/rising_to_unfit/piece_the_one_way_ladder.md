# Piece: The One-Way Ladder

## Taxonomy

[Rising to Unfit](play_rising_to_unfit.md). A structure that lifts on success and will not lower on failure.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On [the org chart](place_the_org_chart.md), all arrows pointing up.
The rule nobody wrote and everybody follows: you can be promoted for doing well, and you cannot be moved back for doing badly, because moving back is a punishment the ladder does not carry.

## Load Bearing

Without it a misfit would slide back to the rung they fit and the damage would self-correct.
With it the misfit is trapped in place, because the One-Way Ladder makes the level of incompetence a terminus and not a stopover: rising rewards success, but nothing demotes on failure, since a demotion would humiliate the person, indict the promotion, and admit the whole system placed them wrong. So the seat that would fix the misfit, a step back down to competence, is the one step the ladder refuses to offer, and the person stays exactly where they no longer belong.

## Apparent

Dignity, and stability.
It looks humane: we do not demote people, we do not shame them for reaching too far. Not shaming people is decent. The same decency welds them into the wrong seat, because kindness about the fall becomes a life sentence at the ceiling.

## Yearbook

Enforced by [the ratchet](persona_the_ratchet.md), who promotes readily and demotes never.
It is what turns a bad fit into a permanent one: without it the play would be a stumble and a recovery, with it it is [the stranded master](piece_the_stranded_master.md), held at the level of incompetence by a structure that can only ever move people the way that put them there.
