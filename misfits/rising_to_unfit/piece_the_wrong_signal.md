# Piece: The Wrong Signal

## Taxonomy

[Rising to Unfit](play_rising_to_unfit.md). Past performance in one job, used to predict fitness for a different one.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the calibration](place_the_calibration.md), where the decision to promote is made on the record of the last role.
The ratings, the numbers, the reputation: all measures of how well the person did the job they are in, read as evidence for a job they are not.

## Load Bearing

Without it the promotion could look at fitness for the next role and the ladder might place people right.
With it the promotion looks backward, because backward is what can be measured: the Wrong Signal is performance in role N used to select for role N plus one, and the two roles need different gifts, so the signal is not false, it is simply about the wrong thing. It is the exact hinge of the play: the reward is real, the record is accurate, and it answers a question, how good were they at this, that is not the question being asked, which is, how good will they be at that.

## Apparent

Evidence-based promotion.
It looks rigorous: promote on demonstrated performance, not on favour or guesswork. Demonstrated performance is the fair thing to use. It just demonstrates fitness for the role being left, and there is rarely any measure of fitness for the role being entered, so the fair measure and the needed one are not the same.

## Yearbook

Read by [the rewarder](persona_the_rewarder.md), who promotes on the best evidence available, which is evidence about the wrong role.
It is why no one is at fault: [the mastery](piece_the_mastery.md) is true, the record is honest, and the promotion is defensible on every count except the only one that matters, whether the gift transfers, which nothing on the table measures.
