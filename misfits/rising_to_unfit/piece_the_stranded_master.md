# Piece: The Stranded Master

## Taxonomy

[Rising to Unfit](play_rising_to_unfit.md). The person taken off what they were best at, lodged in what they are not.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In [the corner office](place_the_corner_office.md), doing a job they cannot do and cannot leave.
The former best-in-the-room, now a struggling manager: too high to go back to the craft, too stuck to go forward, marooned at the one rung that does not suit them.

## Load Bearing

Without it the misfit is an abstraction about ladders and no one is hurt.
With it there is a person in it, and the double loss is plain: the Stranded Master is both a bad manager and a lost maker, because the promotion did not just install someone unfit above, it removed someone excellent below, so the organisation is worse in two places at once, short its best craftsman and saddled with a reluctant boss who used to be them. They are not incompetent as people. They are competent, stranded, in the single seat where their competence does not reach.

## Apparent

A senior person who has plateaued.
It looks like an ordinary career that levelled off, a manager who is fine, nothing to see. The plateau hides the waste: the best hands in the building are folded, and the seat they vacated is filled by whoever rises to it next, on the same wrong signal.

## Yearbook

Lived by [the stuck](persona_the_stuck.md), routed around by the team that must deliver despite the seat, and never returned to the bench.
It is the human cost the play refuses to hide: a reward that punished excellence by ending it, a master kept from mastery by the very ladder that honoured it, stranded at the top of a climb that should have stopped one rung sooner.
