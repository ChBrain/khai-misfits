# Process: Policing

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The disciplining of the terms by informed custom, the switching that would hold the print in line, a force that acts above a critical margin and idles below it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Shopper](persona_the_shopper.md), at [the Aisle](place_the_aisle.md), reading the clauses and threatening to walk.
Begins whenever a buyer reads the terms, compares them across sellers, and would take custom from the seller who wrote a worse one.

## Direction

Turns informed custom into a discipline on the print, when there is enough of it.
Because a seller who cannot tell the reader from the many must hold decent terms to keep the readers it would lose, policing converts the reading of a minority into a floor under everyone's clause, so it would arrest the drift, but only where the readers are numerous enough to be worth keeping, the same process idling wherever the margin is thin (Schwartz and Wilde; Salop and Stiglitz).

## Lever

The custom the seller stands to lose.
Because the discipline is the seller's fear of losing informed buyers, policing works through the weight of the readers as a bloc: a seller worsening a clause forfeits the custom of everyone who would notice, so the lever scales with their number, strong where the margin is thick and absent where it is thin, a threat that bites in bulk and vanishes in ones.

## Echo

A term held where the readers are many, and let go where they are few.
The echo of policing is a discipline that engages above a threshold and disengages below it, so the print stays honest exactly where enough buyers read and drifts everywhere else, the process real and conditional, holding the terms only in the markets the search cost has not thinned past the line.
