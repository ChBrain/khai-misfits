# Pitch: Too Few Read to Police It

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The single hook: a competitive market holds standard-form terms in line only if an informed minority reads and compares them, but reading costs more than it saves, so the minority stays too thin to discipline, and the fine print drifts toward whoever drafts it, unread and unpoliced.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Trust the market to keep the terms honest, the way it keeps the price honest.
The tenor is competition working as promised: a seller who writes a harsh clause loses buyers to one who does not, so the market should discipline the fine print as surely as the price, and no buyer need read the boilerplate because the market reads it for them.

## Undertow

The market reads the terms only through a minority that is not there.
Underneath, the discipline runs on an informed few who read and compare, and reading a standard form costs more than the rare bad term is likely to take, so the rational buyer skips it, the few thin toward none, and the terms are policed only where enough people read, which is nowhere.

## Nerve

Below a margin of readers, the seller stops holding its own print.
The nerve is that the discipline is a threshold and not a guarantee: keeping decent terms to retain a handful of readers costs more than the handful is worth, so once the informed demand falls past the line the seller writes toward itself, and a harsher clause with a trimmed price beats a generous one, because buyers choose on the price they read and not the term they do not.

## Echo

One form clears to all, so the drift falls on the reader too.
What lingers is that the seller posts a single contract to reader and non-reader alike, so the careful buyer is bound by the same degraded clause as the careless one, and the obvious remedy of forcing the term into the open discloses what no one attends, leaving the informed few as thin as before, the print available and still unpoliced.
