# Plan: Drift the Term

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Boilerplate](position_the_boilerplate.md), inside [Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Boilerplate](position_the_boilerplate.md)

## Direction

Move each non-salient term to the point that wins the sale, holding a clause generous only where enough buyers compare it to punish a worse one, and drifting it toward the seat wherever the comparing few are too few to notice, then pass the saving to the visible price where buyers will see and reward it (Kessler; Schwartz and Wilde). Compete on the axis buyers read, since the sale turns on the posted number, and recover in the unread clause what the price concedes, treating the drift not as predation but as the sound answer to a rival who will take the same notch first: a seller who keeps the term whole and prices it in is undercut by one who thins it and cuts the price, so writing toward the drafter is what a market that reads only the price rewards.

## Orders

([The Drafter](persona_the_drafter.md) moves a clause a notch toward itself, trims the price to match, and matches first the rival's certain move, worsening the print because the market leaves no way to be paid for keeping it whole.)

## Implementation

([The Fine Print](place_the_fine_print.md); through the [drifting](process_drifting.md) process, unresisted by the [policing](process_policing.md) that would hold it; the Drafter moves [the clause](piece_the_clause.md) where [the discipline](piece_the_discipline.md) has lifted, each notch tightening [the ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md) the buyers never read.)

## Targets

- [ ] each non-salient term is set to the point that wins the sale
- [ ] a clause is held generous only where the comparing buyers are numerous
- [ ] the term drifts toward the drafter wherever the readers are too few
- [ ] the saving is passed to the visible price and the ratchet turns a notch
