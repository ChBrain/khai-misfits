# Process: Skipping

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The not-reading of the terms, the rational skip that spends attention on the price and none on the clause, thinning the informed margin by one buyer at a time.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Signer](persona_the_signer.md), at [the Counter](place_the_counter.md), reading the price and passing the print.
Begins whenever a buyer weighs the cost of reading a page of clauses against the small chance one of them bites, and finds the reading not worth it.

## Direction

Turns a rational buyer into an uninformed one.
Because the expected benefit of reading a single form falls short of its cost, the buyer searches the salient price and leaves the buried terms unread, so skipping converts a sound allocation of attention into a subtraction from the reading margin, each correct skip removing one more buyer from the few who would police the print (Stigler).

## Lever

The gap between a spread benefit and a borne cost.
Because the cost of reading falls wholly on the one buyer and the benefit of a policed clause is spread across the whole market, skipping works through the plain asymmetry of the sum: no buyer captures enough of the discipline they would supply to pay for the reading it takes, so the lever is the ordinary economy of attention, which makes skipping the right move for each and the loss of discipline the result for all.

## Echo

A market of buyers each soundly uninformed on the print.
The echo of skipping is an informed minority thinned toward nothing, every buyer having correctly declined the reading, so the discipline the terms need is withdrawn not by neglect but by arithmetic, the print left unpoliced by a market whose every member was right to skip it.
