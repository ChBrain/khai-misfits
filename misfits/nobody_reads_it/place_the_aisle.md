# Place: The Aisle

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The comparison floor, where custom moves between sellers and the discipline would be applied, the place a reader could walk from a worse form to a better one, if enough readers walked.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Rival forms side by side, and prices to compare.
Shown at the surface is the competition itself: sellers ranged against sellers, each with a price and a form, the buyer free to move between them, a floor arranged so that custom can switch and switching can discipline.

## Holds

Holds the switching that disciplines, and the threshold it needs.
The Aisle is where the informed custom would police the terms, the readers walking from the harsher clause to the better one so the seller must hold its print, so it holds the whole mechanism of discipline, and holds with it the condition the mechanism runs on: that enough readers walk to be worth keeping, a floor of protection that is here only when the margin is thick.

## Offers

Offers the power to punish a bad term, redeemable only in numbers.
Here a buyer can act on a clause, taking custom from the seller who worsened it, so the Aisle offers the discipline the terms depend on, and offers it as a threshold good: one reader who walks is absorbed without a flinch, so the power is real only in bulk, present to the margin as a whole and worthless to any single member of it, a discipline the place can supply only when enough arrive to claim it together.

## Withheld

Withholds the discipline from the thin margin, and the numbers from the search cost.
What the Aisle withholds is its own mechanism whenever the readers are too few: the floor engages above a critical mass and the search cost keeps the readers below it, so the place that holds the power to police the terms holds it out of reach, the switching available and the switchers absent, the discipline present in principle and withheld in fact from a market whose buyers each found the reading not worth its cost.
