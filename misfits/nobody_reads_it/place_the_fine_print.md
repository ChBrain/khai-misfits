# Place: The Fine Print

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The unread region of the form, the clauses folded below the price where the drift happens, present in full and attended by almost no one, the place a term can move without resistance.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Everything, in small type, in full.
Shown here is the whole of the terms, complete and disclosed, the warranty and the arbitration line and the fee schedule set down exactly, a place that hides nothing and is read by no one, its openness and its neglect the same fact.

## Holds

Holds the non-salient terms, and the room for them to move.
The Fine Print is where the clauses live that no buyer prices, so it is the slack in the contract, the margin the seller can recover what the price gave away, and because attention does not reach here the terms it holds are free to drift, the place defined less by what it contains than by how little of it is ever read.

## Offers

Offers the whole truth of the deal, at a reading cost that keeps it unclaimed.
Here is everything a buyer would need to police the terms, laid out and available, so the Fine Print offers full disclosure and, in the same breath, offers it at a price in time and effort that exceeds its worth on any single purchase, the truth present and unpurchased, so complete that reading it is dear and so dear that reading it is rare.

## Withheld

Withholds nothing and protects nothing, disclosure without attention.
What the Fine Print withholds is not information but its uptake: it conceals by revealing at length, since a term set down in full small type is available to all and read by none, so the place that hides no clause guards no buyer, the drift running through terms that are open on the page and dark in every decision, withheld not behind secrecy but behind the cost of the reading that would find them.
