# Process: Drifting

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The one-way movement of a non-salient term toward the drafter, the notch a clause takes wherever no reader resists, the print worsening under a competition on the price.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Drafter](persona_the_drafter.md), at [the Fine Print](place_the_fine_print.md), moving a clause and trimming the price to match.
Begins whenever a term is unread enough that worsening it loses no buyer and the saving passed to the price wins some.

## Direction

Turns a competitive market into a source of degrading terms.
Because a seller who thins a non-salient clause and cuts the visible price beats one who keeps the clause whole and prices it in, the competition itself moves the terms toward whoever writes them, so drifting converts the discipline of the market into the engine of the decline, the same rivalry that holds the price honest driving the print down (Kessler; Schwartz and Wilde).

## Lever

The visible price bought with the unread clause.
Because buyers choose on the number they read and not the term they do not, drifting works through the sound competition on the salient axis: every notch taken from the clause funds a cut in the price that wins the sale, so the lever is the rivalry itself, which rewards the seller who recovers margin in the print and punishes the one who leaves it whole, the drift a rational answer to a rival who will drift first.

## Echo

A term one notch worse than the last, unresisted because unread.
The echo of drifting is a clause moved toward the drafter with no buyer the wiser, so the print degrades one increment at a time, each step a competitive response and none of them seen, the terms bad by the working of the market rather than against it, and worse next season than this.
