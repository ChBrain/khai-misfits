# Persona: The Drafter

## Taxonomy

[The Boilerplate](position_the_boilerplate.md). Writes the standard form and, where no informed minority polices it, moves the non-salient terms toward itself and trims the visible price to match, because a rival who does the same undercuts a form that keeps its clauses generous.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Attentive to what buyers actually compare. Competing hard on the price. Unsentimental about the clause.
Is the one who knows the sale is won or lost on the number the buyer reads, so it prices to the bone and finds the room to do so in the terms the buyer does not read, treating the clause as a place to recover margin the price gave away.
No villain wished on them: the drift is not greed but the shape of the competition, since a seller who kept the warranty generous and priced it in would be undercut by one who thinned the warranty and cut the price, and buyers choosing on price would take the cheaper, cruder form, so writing toward oneself is the move the market rewards.

## Action

Moves a non-salient term a notch toward itself, then passes the saving to the visible price where buyers will see and reward it, recovering in the clause what it concedes at the counter.
Watches the readers, not the moralists: holds a term only where enough buyers compare it to make holding it pay, and lets it drift wherever the comparing few are too few to notice.
Does what competition on a read price and an unread term compels: writes the form toward the drafter one clause at a time, each notch a rational answer to a rival who will take the same notch if it does not.

## Shadow

There is a version of the form it would rather write, and it knows the fair clause and does not use it, and that is the quiet weight: not the guilt of a predator but the resignation of someone who can see the decent term clearly and cannot afford to offer it, because the market it sells into will punish the decency it cannot see.
What gnaws is a kind of pre-emptive defeat. It drafts toward itself not in triumph but because it has run the rival's move in its head and knows the rival will make it, so to keep the generous clause is to volunteer to lose, and it drafts the meaner term with the flat feeling of a hand forced, the fairness it would prefer priced out of reach by a competition that reads only the price.
The bind is interior and airless: it must worsen the print to survive the aisle, knows the buyer will never see what it took, and lives with the private discomfort of writing what no one will read and everyone will be held to, a decency it can picture and a market that will not let it spend.

## Tell

Says "if we don't, the next shop will" over the clause it is trimming.
Is the whole drift in one line, since the sentence is not an excuse but a forecast, a true reading of a competition that rewards the harsher term and the lower price, so the words that sound like a rationalisation are the sound of a drafter naming the rival's certain move and matching it first, worsening the print because the market has left it no way to be paid for keeping it whole.
