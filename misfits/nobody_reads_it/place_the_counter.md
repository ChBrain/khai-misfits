# Place: The Counter

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The point of sale, where the form is signed and the one contract meets every buyer, the price posted large and the clauses folded small, and a queue behind that prices a reading no one has time to do.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The price, in large plain figures, and the form to sign.
Shown at the surface is everything the sale turns on and nothing the sale hides: the number the buyer compares, the pen, and the space for a name, a transaction laid out to be completed quickly and read at a glance.

## Holds

Holds the meeting of one form and every buyer, reader and non-reader alike.
The Counter is where the seller posts a single contract to all comers, unable to tell the buyer who read from the buyer who did not, so it is the place the free ride is taken and the place the free ride collapses, the informed and the uninformed handed the identical clause across the same counter.

## Offers

Offers a fast, fair sale on the price and a skipped reading of the terms.
Here the buyer can do the whole rational transaction, compare the number, judge the value, and sign, so the Counter offers a competent purchase on everything it shows, and offers, in the same motion, the standing invitation to skip the print, since the queue and the glance and the posted number all price a full reading out of the moment, making the skip the natural act the place is built for.

## Withheld

Withholds the weight of the clause, and the time to find it.
What the Counter hides is not the terms, which are present, but their reading: the place is arranged for speed, so the cost of stopping to parse the print rises with the queue and the moment, and the term that would take an hour to understand is withheld behind the seconds the sale allows, present on the page and absent from the decision, disclosed in full and read by no one because the Counter is where reading costs the most.
