# Plot: The Nudge

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Fine Print](place_the_fine_print.md), a term almost no one reads and a rival who will move it.
A non-salient clause sitting unpoliced, and a market that rewards the lower price the clause can fund.

## Action

[Drifting](process_drifting.md): the Drafter moves the clause a notch toward itself and trims the price to match, recovering in the term what it concedes at the counter.

## Stage

[The Drafter](persona_the_drafter.md) acts from [the Boilerplate](position_the_boilerplate.md) on [Drift the Term](plan_drift_the_term.md), moving [the clause](piece_the_clause.md) where [the discipline](piece_the_discipline.md) has lifted, unresisted because unread.
The move is competitive and the price is better for it, and the print is one notch worse.

## Tension

The seller competes and the terms degrade by the competing, and no buyer choosing on price will see it.
There is no predation: a drafter who kept the clause whole and priced it in would be undercut by one who thinned it and cut the price, and buyers choosing on the number would take the cheaper, cruder form, so holding the term is volunteering to lose. The plot closes with a clause moved toward the drafter by the ordinary working of the market, the price genuinely better and the print genuinely worse, the drift a rational answer to a rival's certain move and invisible to everyone it binds.
