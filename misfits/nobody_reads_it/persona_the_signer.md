# Persona: The Signer

## Taxonomy

[The Sign-Off](position_the_sign_off.md). Reads the price and skips the terms, because reading and understanding the clauses would cost more than the rare bad term is likely to take, and signs the form on trust, one more buyer added to the uninformed many.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Sensible about their own time. Trusting the market to have done the reading. Quick with the pen.
Is the one who treats the standard form as settled furniture, glances at the price because the price is what varies and what bites today, and takes the clauses as given because a competitive market surely would not let them be too bad.
No fool wished on them: the skipping is not carelessness but a correct sum, since the cost of reading a page of clauses at the counter, understanding them, and comparing them across sellers is real and the bad term is rare, so the buyer who skips has done the arithmetic right.

## Action

Reads the salient price, runs an eye past the boilerplate, and signs, spending attention on what is cheap to know and varies by seller and none on what is dear to know and looks the same everywhere.
Trusts by default: takes the terms on the assumption that competition polices them, since that assumption is right about the price and there is no cheap way to check it about the print.
Does what a rational buyer does with a cost that outruns its benefit: skips the reading, saves the search cost, and accepts the clause unread, joining the many whose non-reading is each individually sound.

## Shadow

There is a small, swallowed unease in signing what you did not read, and they know it and sign anyway, and the knowing is the part that stays: not fear of the term, which is probably fine, but the bare fact of having put a name to words they let themselves not understand, a consent given past the edge of what they actually consented to.
What sits under it is a cornered feeling dressed as convenience. They tell themselves they chose not to read, and they did, but the choice was between reading a wall of clauses with a queue at their back and getting on with their day, which is no choice at all, so the ease is a little false, a rationality that feels from the inside less like freedom than like being quietly out of options and calling it fine.
The bind is interior and low and constant: they must trust to function, cannot check without a cost they will not pay, and carry, under the ordinary ease of signing, the faint private knowledge that they have agreed to something they declined to know, and that declining was the only sensible thing to do.

## Tell

Says "I'm sure it's fine" with the pen already moving.
Is the whole skip in one phrase, since the sureness is not knowledge but a substitute for it, a trust stood in for a reading that did not pay, so the sentence that sounds like confidence is the sound of a buyer deciding, correctly, that checking would cost more than it is worth, and signing on the strength of a market they hope has read what they did not.
