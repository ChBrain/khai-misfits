# Position: The Sign-Off

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The buying seat, which holds the pen and the price and closes the sale, able to act on the salient number and lacking any instrument to value the buried term for the cost of one purchase.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The pen, the price, and the power to complete or refuse the sale.
The seat holds the visible number, which it can read and compare at once, and the authority to sign, so it can act fully on everything the market makes cheap to know.
Command over the salient and none over the buried, since the seat can price what is posted plainly and has no tool to price a page of clauses against the small chance one of them bites.

## Orders

Read the price, weigh it, and sign.
Compare the number that varies by seller, decide whether the sale is worth it on the terms that show, and complete the purchase, treating the clauses as a fixed backdrop the market is presumed to have set.
Spend attention where it pays, since the seat's whole economy is to buy well on the axis it can measure, and the price is that axis, so the sign-off allocates its scarce reading to the number and skips the print as a matter of sound procedure.

## Loses

The pricing of the term, which the seat has no instrument to perform per transaction.
The Sign-Off can value the posted price but cannot value the buried clause, because the cost of reading and comparing the terms exceeds their expected weight on any single purchase, so the seat forfeits the print as a matter of arithmetic, not attention. What the position loses is any per-sale defence of the non-salient term: every act available to the seat, the reading, the comparing, the walking, costs more than it returns at the scale of one buyer, so the seat's rational procedure is to skip exactly the terms whose discipline the market depends on, and no operation the seat can run on a single transaction reverses this, because the benefit of policing a clause is spread across a market and the cost of reading it falls on the one buyer, so the seat that acts soundly on its own account withdraws precisely the scrutiny the terms require.

## Drives

Toward a sale closed well on the price and blind on the print,
and a buyer efficient on every axis the market prices and absent on the one it does not,
so the seat that spends its attention most rationally is the seat that polices the terms least, its very economy of effort removing the scrutiny the fine print needs, each sound sign-off thinning the discipline by one.
