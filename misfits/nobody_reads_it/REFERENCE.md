# Nobody Reads It: Reference

The research warrant for [Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md).
The dynamic is standard-form term degradation under the informed-minority hypothesis: Schwartz and Wilde's finding
that competitive terms require enough informed buyers to make policing worthwhile, Stigler's economics of costly search
beneath it, Salop and Stiglitz's equilibrium of informed and uninformed buyers, Kessler's contract of adhesion, and
Bakos, Marotta-Wurgler and Trossen's measure of how few actually read, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the informed-minority hypothesis as a **discipline-needs-readers-it-does-not-have structure**: a competitive
market is supposed to hold standard-form terms in line the way it holds the price, because a seller who writes a harsh
clause loses buyers, but that discipline runs on an informed minority reading and comparing the terms, and reading them
costs more than it saves on any single purchase, so the minority stays too thin and the non-salient terms drift toward
whoever drafts them. The play models the documented mechanics, the search cost that makes non-reading rational, the
informed margin whose thinness lifts the discipline, the one form that clears to reader and non-reader alike, and the
disclosure remedy that makes the terms visible without making them read, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure,
not as a tale of a predatory seller or a careless buyer. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing
law: a market can offer competitive terms to all buyers if a sufficient fraction is informed and sellers cannot
distinguish the informed from the uninformed at the point of sale, so the uninformed free-ride on the informed, but when
the informed fraction is too small the terms are not disciplined, and the non-salient terms degrade. It is a tendency
under stated conditions and not an iron rule, holding where the informed margin is thin and the terms non-salient, and
lifting where enough read or the terms are prominent, so the play stages the drift a thin margin permits, not a law that
degrades every term everywhere.

It is distinct from the house's The Market for Lemons, where hidden quality of the good drives good sellers out through a
pooling price and the market can unravel by exit: there the concealed variable is the quality of the thing sold, the
mechanism is adverse selection, and the failure is a spiral in which good units withdraw until the market thins toward
collapse. Here the good's quality is not hidden and no one exits: buyers keep buying and sellers keep selling, and it is
the contract terms, not the goods, that degrade, because the buyer-side discipline that would police them is priced too
thin to engage. The engine is an informed-minority failure on a non-salient term, a discipline that lifts below a
threshold of readers, not an adverse selection on a hidden quality, and the outcome is a one-way drift of the print
rather than an exit of the good, the market intact and clearing and its terms sliding, not unravelling.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the seller as a predator or
  the buyer as careless, because the source finding is precisely that no bad faith and no deception is needed: each buyer
  skips the terms because reading costs more than the rare bad clause is likely to take (Stigler), and each seller writes
  toward itself because a rival who thins a non-salient term and cuts the visible price will undercut a form that keeps
  the term whole, so holding the clause is volunteering to lose (Kessler; Schwartz and Wilde). One form clears to reader
  and non-reader alike, so the drift falls on all. So the misfit is that a discipline resting on an informed minority
  lifts when the minority is priced too thin, and competitive terms degrade, no predation and no neglect required, the
  skipping and the drafting each sound and the bad term the sum, the buyer who skips a reading that does not pay as
  reasonable as the seller who competes on the price its buyers actually read.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into terms held by a diligent reader or an easy rule. The lone
  reader reads correctly and disciplines nothing, because the discipline is a threshold and one buyer is below it
  (Salop and Stiglitz), and the obvious remedy of mandating disclosure makes the terms visible without making them read,
  since the cost that kept the print unread was the reading and not the availability (Bakos, Marotta-Wurgler and
  Trossen). The move that would hold the terms is to manufacture an informed minority thick enough to make policing pay,
  which means making the reading worth the buyer's while against the very cost that made skipping rational, or writing the
  terms directly rather than trusting a discipline with no readers to run on, so the correction is dear and partial, not
  a way to read the fine print rightly and be spared.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the informed-minority hypothesis and the term
  degradation it explains are Schwartz and Wilde's, the economics of costly search beneath it is Stigler's, the
  equilibrium of informed and uninformed buyers is Salop and Stiglitz's, the contract of adhesion is Kessler's, and the
  empirical measure of how few read is Bakos, Marotta-Wurgler and Trossen's, each carried as carefully as the finding
  itself: the misfit stages terms drifting for want of readers and credits that no one cheated and the discipline lifts
  by arithmetic, holding it a tendency under stated conditions and not a universal law.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md)**: carries the informed-minority hypothesis whole (Schwartz and Wilde), the
  standard form as a one-way mechanism whose terms tighten wherever too few read to police them and never loosen.
- **[The Discipline](piece_the_discipline.md)**: carries the market pressure that holds the terms (Schwartz and Wilde),
  qualified as a threshold that engages above a critical margin of readers and lifts below it (Salop and Stiglitz).
- **[The Search Cost](piece_the_search_cost.md)**: carries the economics of costly information (Stigler), the price of
  reading that falls on the one buyer while the benefit spreads across the market, keeping the reading margin thin.
- **[The Clause](piece_the_clause.md)**: carries the non-salient dictated term (Kessler), the boilerplate provision the
  buyer does not price and the seller can move, the object the drift acts on.
- **[The Mandate](plot_the_mandate.md)**: carries the empirical measure of attention (Bakos, Marotta-Wurgler and
  Trossen), the disclosed term that stays unread, so making the print visible does not manufacture the informed margin.
