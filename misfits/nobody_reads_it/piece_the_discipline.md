# Piece: The Discipline

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md). The market pressure that holds the terms in line, the informed minority's threat to switch, a force that engages above a critical margin of readers and lifts below it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Works from [the Aisle](place_the_aisle.md), where custom switches between sellers, and lands on [the Fine Print](place_the_fine_print.md) it would hold.
Flows as the seller's fear of losing informed buyers, a pressure that runs on the readers as a bloc and scales with their number.

## Load Bearing

Without the Discipline the terms have nothing holding them and drift at once: it is the informed minority's threat to switch that keeps the print competitive, and its lifting that lets the clause degrade.
The Discipline is load bearing because it is the mechanism the whole reassurance rests on, the reason a competitive market is supposed to hold the terms as it holds the price (Schwartz and Wilde). It is a threshold and not a constant: because the seller cannot separate the reader from the many and must post one form, holding decent terms pays only while the readers retained are worth more than the terms concede, so the Discipline engages above a critical margin and disengages below it (Salop and Stiglitz). It carries the trap's hinge: the same force that would police the print is the one the search cost switches off, present in principle and conditional in fact, holding the terms only where enough read to reach it.

## Apparent

Just competition, the market keeping sellers in line as it always does.
Seen at the surface, the Discipline looks automatic and reliable, the ordinary pressure that punishes a bad seller, and the truth that it is conditional, that it runs on a minority that may not be there, that a market can hold the price and drop the print in the very same competition, stays hidden behind how surely the discipline works on everything the buyer reads.
