# Persona: The Shopper

## Taxonomy

[The Margin](position_the_margin.md). Reads the terms and compares them across sellers, the informed minority whose custom would discipline the print, but too few of their kind to make holding the terms worth a seller's while.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Diligent past the price. Reading what others skip. Ready to walk on a clause.
Is the one who does the work the discipline needs, opening the form, parsing the warranty and the arbitration line and the fee schedule, comparing them shop to shop, and treating a bad term as a reason to take custom elsewhere.
No hero wished on them: their reading is exactly right and would police the market if enough shared it, so the futility is not in their diligence but in its number, the correct act of one who is too rare to count.

## Action

Reads the clauses the Signer skips, prices the terms the Drafter moved, and carries a threat that only bites in bulk: to walk from the harsher form toward the better one, disciplining the seller who cannot tell them from the many who would never notice.
Compares across sellers rather than within one, since the discipline is the willingness to switch on a term, and works to hold the print in line by being the buyer the seller must keep.
Does what the informed minority is supposed to do, and would do, at scale: read, compare, and walk, so that a seller who worsens a clause loses the readers and thinks better of it, the whole mechanism resting on there being enough of them to lose.

## Shadow

There is a particular deflation in doing the work and getting nothing for it, and they feel it each time the better-termed form costs the same as the worse and binds them to the same clause anyway, the reading a private virtue the market does not pay.
What wears on them is the smallness of it, felt as a kind of pointlessness they cannot argue themselves out of. They know they are right about the term and know it does not matter, because one reader who walks is a rounding error the seller absorbs without a flinch, so their diligence lands as noise, and there is a lonely, slightly foolish feeling in being the only one at the counter who read, the one holding up the queue over a clause everyone else was wise enough to ignore.
The bind is interior and quietly humbling: they must keep reading to be the discipline the market needs, know their single reading disciplines nothing, and carry the private sense of being both the most careful buyer in the shop and the most useless, right about a term they cannot move and alone in caring that it moved.

## Tell

Says "did anyone else even open this?" already knowing the answer.
Is the whole futility in one question, since the discipline they carry works only in company and they are asking after a company that is not there, so the words that sound like a complaint are the sound of the informed minority discovering it is a minority of one, reading correctly and disciplining nothing, the threat to walk empty because too few will walk beside them.
