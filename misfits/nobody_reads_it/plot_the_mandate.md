# Plot: The Mandate

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

The remedy, a rule that would fix the terms by forcing them into the open.
A disclosure mandate that posts the clause plainly and makes it free to read, on the theory that a visible term is a policed term.

## Action

[Clearing](process_clearing.md): the market re-settles under the mandate, the seller posting the disclosed clause to all, and the term everyone takes is no better than before.

## Stage

The mandate makes [the clause](piece_the_clause.md) accessible and leaves [the search cost](piece_the_search_cost.md) untouched, so [the discipline](piece_the_discipline.md) is unthickened and [the ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md) turns on, the disclosed print no more read than the folded one (Bakos, Marotta-Wurgler and Trossen).
The terms are open and the readers are as few, and the drift runs on.

## Tension

The obvious fix discloses what no one attends, and the print stays unpoliced.
There is no bad rule: making the terms visible is the sensible remedy, and the mandate delivers it, the clause posted plainly and free to read. But the cost that kept the print unread was never its availability, it was the reading, so a term one is free to read and still will not read is disclosed and still unpoliced, the informed margin as thin after the mandate as before. The plot closes with the remedy paid and the drift unarrested, disclosure having supplied the availability the market already had and not the attention it lacked, and the dearer correction that would thicken the margin, making the reading worth the buyer's while against the very cost that made skipping rational, left unattempted.
