# Plot: The Skim

## Taxonomy

[Nobody Reads It](play_nobody_reads_it.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Counter](place_the_counter.md), a form to sign and a queue behind.
The price posted plain and the clauses folded small, and a buyer with seconds to spend and a page that would take an hour to read.

## Action

[Skipping](process_skipping.md): the Signer reads the price, runs an eye past the terms, and signs, spending attention where it pays and none on the print.

## Stage

[The Signer](persona_the_signer.md) acts from [the Sign-Off](position_the_sign_off.md) on [Sign and Go](plan_sign_and_go.md), saving [the search cost](piece_the_search_cost.md) and taking [the clause](piece_the_clause.md) on trust, one more buyer subtracted from the reading margin.
The skip is sound and the sale is fair, and the discipline is a fraction thinner for it.

## Tension

The buyer does the right thing and weakens the terms by doing it, and nothing at the counter shows the cost.
There is no error: the price is read well, the value judged, the reading correctly declined, since parsing a page of clauses against the small chance one bites is not worth the buyer's hour. But the benefit of that reading would have fallen on the whole market and its cost on the one buyer, so the plot closes with a sound skip that removes one reader from the few who police the print, the buyer efficient on their own account and, without meaning to or seeing it, a fraction of the reason the terms are not held.
