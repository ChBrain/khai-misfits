# Piece: The Conservation

## Taxonomy

[The Balloon Effect](play_the_balloon_effect.md). The total roughly unchanged across the whole, the trade neither cut nor grown but moved, the sum the patch-count cannot see.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Holds across the whole, readable only from [the map](place_the_map.md), where the trade is totalled across the patches.
Invisible on [the patch](place_the_patch.md), where only the local clearance shows.

## Load Bearing

Without it the clearances would add up to a reduction, and the trap would be no trap.
With it the whole story turns: a hundred real local wins summing to a trade merely relocated, the total held roughly steady because the driver was never drained. The Conservation is load bearing because it is the sum the trap hides (Windle and Farrell): the trade neither cut nor grown across the whole, so the piece carries the conserved total that no patch-level count can show, the cost that lives only in the sum, and, where the softest spots are the worst, a total not merely held but worsened into harder hands.

## Apparent

An accident, an unlucky run of new outbreaks, nothing to do with all the patches cleared.
Seen at the surface, the Conservation does not appear as a total at all: each bulge reads as a fresh local problem, and the truth that the whole trade is roughly what it was, that the hundred clearances moved it and did not cut it, that the steady total is the signature of a driver left untouched, stays hidden behind a board of quiet patches that never adds itself up.

## Yearbook

Held by [the pressure](piece_the_pressure.md) across [displacing](process_displacing.md), the total the patch-count cannot see.
It is the cost of the trap: the Conservation is the sum of every [clearance](piece_the_clearance.md) undone by every [bulge](piece_the_bulge.md), the steady total that [counting](process_counting.md) never takes, the whole that only [draining](process_draining.md) would move, the trade the [balloon effect](piece_the_balloon_effect.md) conserves.
