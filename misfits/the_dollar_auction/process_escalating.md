# Process: Escalating

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). Each bid by the one who is second the smaller loss, so the bids climb and the lead swaps back and forth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Chaser, second and facing the forfeit, with one move that turns the loss around.
At [the floor](place_the_floor.md), the bidder who is behind raises just above the leader, and in doing so becomes the leader, handing the other the same reason to bid.

## Direction

Toward higher bids by sound steps.
Because the runner-up loses their last bid for nothing, escalating points each raise upward: bidding a little over the leader wins the prize and escapes the forfeit, the smaller loss, so the one who is second always raises, and since every raise makes the other second, the bids climb by a chain of correct choices with no misstep in it.

## Lever

The forfeit that makes the next bid worth its cost.
Because coming second means paying the whole last bid for nothing, escalating runs on the plain arithmetic that one more bid is the lesser loss, so no stubbornness and no sunk-cost error is needed to drive the climb, only the forward-looking choice the all-pay rule sets in front of whoever is behind, correct every time it is made.

## Echo

The bids risen and the lead traded, the climb toward the prize.
What leaves this process is a pair of bidders who have each raised soundly and swapped the lead between them, the numbers up near the prize and every step behind them right, so the escalation arrives at the prize by correct choices alone, with the lock about to make the same logic hold past it.
