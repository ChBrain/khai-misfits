# Process: Locking

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The reason to bid only strengthening as the bids near and pass the prize, so the escalation finds no floor.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The bids themselves, once they climb toward the prize's worth.
At [the floor](place_the_floor.md), the runner-up's forfeit has grown as large as the prize, so quitting now means losing a stake as big as the dollar for nothing, and the reason to bid one more only tightens.

## Direction

Toward an escalation with no natural stop.
Because the forfeit grows with every raise, locking points the logic past the prize: the larger the last bid, the more quitting costs, so the case for one more bid strengthens as the numbers climb, and at the dollar it does not break and past the dollar it holds, each bidder choosing to lose a little more to win over losing everything to quit.

## Lever

A forfeit that rises with the bid.
Because the stake the runner-up would lose is exactly the bid they just made, locking runs on a cost that climbs alongside the escalation, so the higher the bidding the harder it is to stop, no new motive needed, only the same smaller-loss arithmetic now applied to numbers past the prize, correct and inescapable.

## Echo

The bidding risen past the prize, and no rung to stop on.
What leaves this process is an escalation carried past the dollar's worth by the same logic that started it, the forfeit now larger than the prize and the reason to bid stronger than ever, so the two are past saving by the structure and can be stopped only by an empty pocket, the trap holding all the way down.
