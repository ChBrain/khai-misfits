# Process: Baiting

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The prize offered cheap, the free-looking gain that draws two bidders across the threshold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The Auctioneer, with a prize of known worth and a small opening bid.
At [the threshold](place_the_threshold.md), a dollar is held up for a nickel, and a bidder steps in for the bargain, a second stepping in to take it or deny it.

## Direction

Toward two bidders in for a small stake.
Because a dollar going for a nickel is plainly worth chasing, baiting points the newcomer at the entrance: the opening bid is trivial and the gain large, so stepping in is the obvious move, and the structure needs only that two step in, the escalation to follow supplied by them and not by the bait.

## Lever

A real bargain at the door.
Because the prize is genuine and the opening price tiny, baiting runs on no trick, only a true and tempting gap between a nickel bid and a dollar prize, so no deception is needed to draw the bidders in, and the one costly move of the whole game, entering at all, is the one that looks the cheapest.

## Echo

Two bidders on the floor, the trap entered by consent.
What leaves this process is a pair of rational people each in for a small stake and a large-looking prize, the door crossed for a nickel and the forfeit not yet felt, so the escalation has its two players and the only avoidable step, the entry, is already behind them.
