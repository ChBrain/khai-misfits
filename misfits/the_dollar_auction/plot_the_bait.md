# Plot: The Bait

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Threshold](place_the_threshold.md), a dollar held up and a rule read aloud.
An auctioneer offering a real bargain, and a bidder seeing free money and no cost yet in sight.

## Action

[Baiting](process_baiting.md): the prize is offered cheap under the all-pay rule, and two bidders step in, one for the bargain and one to take it or deny it.

## Stage

[The Auctioneer](persona_the_auctioneer.md), on [Run the Sale](plan_run_the_sale.md), offers [the prize](piece_the_prize.md) under [the all-pay](piece_the_all_pay.md) and states the terms plainly, and two bidders cross into the game for a nickel and a large-looking gain.
Nothing is hidden and the bargain is real, and stepping in is the one move that cannot be undone at the price it cost.

## Tension

Entering for a dollar going cheap is the obvious move, and the obvious move is the only costly one there is.
There is no trick: the prize is genuine, the opening bid trivial, and stepping in for free money is what anyone would do. But the door is free to cross and ruinous to recross, so the plot closes with two rational bidders on the floor, the one avoidable step already behind them, taken because it looked the cheapest of all.
