# Place: The Floor

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The auction floor, where the bids climb by sound steps and the lead swaps back and forth toward ruin.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The open bidding, the numbers rising, the two stakes called out over each other, the lead passing from one bidder to the other and back with every raise.
The Chaser bidding just past the leader, the leader becoming the chaser, the climb going up through the dollar and beyond, each bid a small sound sum.

## Holds

The escalation, and the lock that only tightens as it climbs.
Here the all-pay rule is in motion, so the Floor holds the engine of the trap: whoever is second finds the next bid the smaller loss, and each raise makes the other second with the same reason, so the two trade the lead up a ladder that passes the prize and finds no rung where stopping beats bidding.
The Floor is where every step is right and the sequence of right steps has no floor beneath it.

## Offers

The prize, always one raise away, and a reason to take it.
Because the leader is a single bid from the dollar and the chaser a single bid from the lead, the Floor offers each bidder a prize that seems just within reach, handing out the pull that keeps them bidding, and in the same motion it offers the forfeit that makes not-bidding the larger loss, the prize and the trap held out together at every rung.

## Withheld

A stop, and any floor beneath the next bid.
The Floor can carry the bidding as high as the two will take it, but it withholds a price at which quitting beats raising, since the runner-up's forfeit only grows with the climb, so it withholds the natural end an ordinary auction has, and lets two sound bidders rise past the prize with nothing in the structure to halt them but an empty pocket.
