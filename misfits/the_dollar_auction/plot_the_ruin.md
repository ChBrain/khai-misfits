# Plot: The Ruin

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Reckoning](place_the_reckoning.md), one bidder out of budget or nerve.
A climb that reason would not stop, halted at last by an empty pocket.

## Action

[Ruining](process_ruining.md): the one who can bid no higher quits and forfeits, the winner takes the prize for far more than its worth, and the auctioneer collects both stakes.

## Stage

One bidder can raise no further and quits, paying [the forfeit](piece_the_forfeit.md) for nothing, while the other takes [the prize](piece_the_prize.md) for the [overbid](piece_the_overbid.md) it cost, and [the Auctioneer](persona_the_auctioneer.md) collects both stakes under [the all-pay](piece_the_all_pay.md), the house alone ahead.
The auction ends where a budget ran out, not where reason found a stop, and both bidders leave worse than had they never entered.

## Tension

The escalation is stopped by exhaustion, not by judgement, so the ending is a limit reached and not a mistake corrected.
There is no error to point to: both bidders chose the smaller loss at every step, right up to the edge of what they had. But the sum of the right steps is a winner who overpaid and a loser who paid for nothing, so the plot closes with both ruined and the house paid, a mutual loss no one misjudged and no one could stop, the trap sprung by nothing but correct choices and an all-pay rule with no floor.
