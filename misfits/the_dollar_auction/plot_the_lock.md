# Plot: The Lock

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Floor](place_the_floor.md), the bids climbed near the dollar and the forfeit grown as large as the prize.
Two bidders each facing a loss the size of the reward if they stop.

## Action

[Locking](process_locking.md): quitting now forfeits a stake as big as the prize, so the reason to bid one more only strengthens, and past the dollar it holds.

## Stage

As the bids near the prize, [the forfeit](piece_the_forfeit.md) grows to match it, so stopping means losing as much as winning would gain, and each bidder raises again, the smaller loss carrying [the bid](piece_the_bid.md) past [the prize](piece_the_prize.md) and turning it to [overbid](piece_the_overbid.md), [the all-pay](piece_the_all_pay.md) leaving no rung to stop on.
The logic that started the climb does not break at the dollar, and past it the case for one more bid is only stronger.

## Tension

The reason to bid one more grows as the bids climb, so the escalation is most binding exactly where it has already passed the prize.
There is nothing new in it: the same smaller-loss arithmetic that opened the bidding now runs past the value, because the forfeit that would be paid for quitting has grown with every raise. So the plot closes with the two bid past the prize's worth and the structure offering no stop, the trap tightest where the bidding makes least sense, and only an empty pocket left to end it.
