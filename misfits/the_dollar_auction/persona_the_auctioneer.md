# Persona: The Auctioneer

## Taxonomy

[The Block](position_the_block.md). Offers a prize of known worth under a rule stated plainly, that the runner-up pays too, and collects from both.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Open. Fair-dealing. Running a clear sale.
Is the one who holds up the dollar and names the rule aloud: highest bidder wins it, second-highest pays their last bid and gets nothing, all of it on the table for anyone to read.
No trickery wished on them: the game is transparent, the prize is exactly what it looks like, and nothing is hidden from the bidders who choose to step in.

## Action

Offers the prize and states the all-pay rule, then lets the bidding run, taking each raise as it comes and collecting both stakes at the end.
Answers no one's ruin into being: the Auctioneer only opens a structure and holds it steady, the escalation supplied entirely by the bidders' own sound choices.
Does what running the sale requires: names the terms clearly, keeps the floor open, and lets two rational people do to each other what the rule allows.

## Shadow

Not a swindler, but the still centre a ruinous structure turns on, and that is the quiet of it: the profit is real and no trick was played to earn it.
Because the rule forfeits the runner-up's bid, the Auctioneer collects from the loser as well as the winner, and the more soundly the two bid the more the house takes, so the Auctioneer, having deceived no one, is paid from a spiral that only the bidders' correct choices drive, the beneficiary of a trap that needs no trickster.
The game is honest and the winnings are a ruin split two ways, so the fair dealer is the one the structure pays.

## Tell

Says "the rule is plain and everyone heard it: I am only running the sale."
Is right that nothing is hidden and cannot be blamed for a bid, so the transparency is genuine and the profit is drawn from a loss the bidders chose step by sound step to hand over.
