# Piece: The All-Pay

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The phenomenon whole, the structure in which both bidders pay and only one wins, and the escalation it makes rational.

## Owner

## Place

Runs across [the threshold](place_the_threshold.md), [the floor](place_the_floor.md), and [the reckoning](place_the_reckoning.md) as the whole rule, the structure the bidders enter, climb, and settle under.
Flows through the entire game, from the bargain at the door to the ruin at the tally, the mechanism seen entire.

## Load Bearing

Without it there are only parts: a prize, a raise, a forfeit, a loss, with no name for the rule that binds them into a trap.
With it the parts become one structure: both bidders pay their last bid and only one wins the prize, so whoever is second always has reason to bid again, the escalation runs with no floor, and the ruin is shared while the house is paid. The All-Pay is load bearing because it is the phenomenon entire (Shubik; Maynard Smith): the loser-pays rule that makes each raise rational and the climb endless, so the piece carries the trap whole, the same structure the war of attrition wears, in which correct play by both parties is exactly what ruins them.

## Apparent

Just an auction, a lively sale of a prize to the highest bidder, spirited bidding and a fair close.
Seen at the surface, the All-Pay looks like an ordinary auction with an unusual rule, and the truth that the rule removes the floor every auction has, that the runner-up's forfeit turns sound bidding into a spiral, that a transparent structure can ruin two rational people without a single misstep, stays hidden behind how much a brisk sale looks like a fair one.
