# Position: The Chase

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The seat of the one who is second, facing the forfeit, where bidding on is always the smaller loss.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A last bid about to be forfeited, and one move that turns the loss around.
The arithmetic of being second: pay the whole stake now for nothing, or bid one step higher and take the prize, the step higher plainly the lesser loss.
No stubbornness in it, since the choice is forward-looking, weighing the two outcomes ahead and not the stake already laid.

## Orders

Bid to escape.
Raise just above the leader, choosing to pay a little more and win over paying the last bid and losing, and take the prize the forfeit was about to cost.
Make the sound raise, since being second gives the next bid a positive value at every price.

## Loses

The stop, because the raise is correct and correct has no floor.
Because bidding past the leader is always the smaller loss, the Chase is right at every step and right past the dollar too, so the very soundness of the move is what carries the escalation on, and the seat that is always one bid from the prize is never a bid from the end.
It chooses the lesser loss each time and loses the ladder's top rung, right at every price and ruined by the sequence of being right.

## Drives

Toward a raise that is always correct,
and a climb the correctness never stops,
so the seat that chooses the smaller loss at every step chooses it past the prize and into ruin.
