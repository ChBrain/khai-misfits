# Place: The Reckoning

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The settling, where the escalation stops at a limit and both bidders count their losses while the house collects.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The final tally, one bidder out of budget or nerve, the last stakes paid over, the dollar handed to the winner and nothing to the runner-up.
The Auctioneer collecting both bids, the winner holding a dollar bought for many, the loser holding nothing and paying for it, the numbers all come due.

## Holds

The shared ruin, and the house paid from it.
Here the climb has met a limit that is not the structure's but a pocket's, so the Reckoning holds the outcome the sound steps summed to: the winner has overpaid and the loser has paid for nothing, both worse off than had they never bid, and the auctioneer richer by both stakes, the ruin split two ways and the profit whole.
The Reckoning is where the arithmetic that never erred is added up and found to have ruined everyone who played.

## Offers

A prize to the winner, and a settlement to the loser.
Because the auction does end, the Reckoning offers the winner the dollar and the loser an exit, handing out a resolution, but the dollar costs the winner far more than a dollar and the loser's exit costs them their last bid, so what it hands out is two losses wearing the shapes of a win and a release.
The only party it pays is the one that made no bid.

## Withheld

Any winner who came out ahead, and any stop the structure itself supplied.
The Reckoning can end the auction, but it withholds a bidder who profited, since the winner overpaid and the loser forfeited, and it withholds the sense that the ending was the structure's doing rather than a budget's, so it hands over a close that looks like resolution and was only exhaustion, the trap stopped not by reason but by running out.
