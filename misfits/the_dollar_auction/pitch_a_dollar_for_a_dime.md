# Pitch: A Dollar for a Dime

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Brisk, rational, arithmetic.
The plain sense that each bid is a sound bit of maths: a dollar going for a dime is free money worth chasing, and once two are bidding, paying a little more to win the prize beats paying your last bid for nothing, so every raise is the smaller loss chosen with open eyes.
Nothing foolish in it: no one misjudges the dollar, no one throws good money after bad, and each step is the forward-looking, correct choice of the lesser cost.

## Undertow

Under the arithmetic, a rule with no floor.
Because the runner-up forfeits their last bid, being second always makes one more bid worth its cost, and there is no price at which that stops being true, so the sound step repeats past the dollar, past two, past five, two reasonable people climbing toward ruin on a ladder of correct choices.
The maths that reads as prudence at every rung is a structure that turns each right move into a shared loss, the smaller loss chosen so many times it exceeds the prize, and the house paid from both.

## Nerve

The feeling of a move so obviously right it cannot be the wrong one, a dollar too cheap to pass and a next bid too sensible to refuse,
because no bidder is fooled and no bidder is stubborn, only choosing the lesser loss the rule sets in front of them,
while underneath the same rule that makes each bid sound makes the last bid a forfeit, so the arithmetic that never errs walks two clever people past the prize and splits the ruin between them.

## Echo

After the run: two bidders past the prize and out of pocket, the winner holding a dollar bought for many, the loser holding nothing and paying for it, the house richer by both stakes,
no swindle in the story and no fallacy, only a transparent rule and two sound sequences of choices,
and the one move that would spare them, never to bid at all, refused because the bait is a gift and the entry looks free, so the ruin lands where every step was right and the only winning move was the one that looked like walking away from money.
