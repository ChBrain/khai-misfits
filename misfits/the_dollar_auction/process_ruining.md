# Process: Ruining

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The escalation ending at a limit, one bidder forfeiting and the winner overpaying, the house collecting both.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A bidder running out of budget or nerve, the only limit the structure did not supply.
At [the reckoning](place_the_reckoning.md), one of the two can bid no higher and quits, forfeiting their last bid, while the other takes the prize for far more than it is worth.

## Direction

Toward a shared ruin and a paid house.
Because the climb stops only when a pocket empties, ruining points the outcome at loss for both: the loser pays their last bid for nothing and the winner pays past the prize for a dollar, so both end worse than if they had never bid, and the auctioneer, who bid nothing, collects both stakes, the ruin split two ways and the profit whole.

## Lever

A limit that is a budget, not a reason.
Because no price makes quitting beat raising, ruining runs on exhaustion rather than judgement, so the auction ends not where reason found a stop but where a bidder ran out, and the halt that looks like a decision is only the floor of a pocket, the two having bid correctly right up to the edge of what they had.

## Echo

Both bidders down, the house up, the arithmetic added and found ruinous.
What leaves this process is a winner holding a dollar bought for many and a loser holding nothing and paying for it, the auctioneer richer by both, so the sequence of sound choices closes on a mutual loss no one misjudged and no one could stop, the whole trap sprung by correct steps and paid for by the two who took them.
