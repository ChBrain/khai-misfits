# Place: The Threshold

## Taxonomy

[The Dollar Auction](play_the_dollar_auction.md). The door, where a dollar going cheap draws the bidder in and refusing to enter is the only escape.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The edge of the floor, the dollar held up, the rule read aloud, and a bidder weighing whether to step in for what looks like free money.
The Auctioneer naming the terms and the newcomer seeing only the bargain, a dollar to be had for a nickel and no cost yet in sight.

## Holds

The entry, the one costly move dressed as the cheap one.
Here nothing has escalated and no stake is laid, so the Threshold holds the only place the trap can be avoided: to enter is to accept a structure that has no floor, and to decline is to walk away from an apparent gift, so the door is where the whole ruin is chosen or refused, before a single bid makes refusing impossible.
The Threshold is where the trap is only a rule on the table, still costless to leave and already too tempting to.

## Offers

A dollar for a nickel, and a place in the game.
Because the prize is real and the opening bid is small, the Threshold offers the bidder a genuine bargain and an easy way in, handing out exactly the free-looking gain the structure needs, and in the same motion it offers a seat in an escalation with no natural stop, the bargain and the trap passed across the same doorway.

## Withheld

Any exit as cheap as the entrance, once the step is taken.
The Threshold can be crossed for a nickel, but it withholds a way back at that price: the moment a second bidder is in, every leaving forfeits the last bid, so it withholds the symmetry between coming in and getting out, and lets the bidder step over a line that is free to cross and ruinous to recross.
