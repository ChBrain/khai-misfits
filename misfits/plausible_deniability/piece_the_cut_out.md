# Piece: The Cut-Out

## Taxonomy

[Plausible Deniability](play_plausible_deniability.md). The insulating layer: the intermediary that keeps the principal's hands off the deed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the corridor](place_the_corridor.md), one layer down from the wish and one above the hand.
Where the chain is built to break cleanly if pulled.

## Load Bearing

Without it the wink would pass straight from principal to agent, and the trail would run in one step.
With it there is distance by design: a broker, an intermediary, an arm's length, so that intent is relayed and no single link joins the principal to the deed, and the point past the last cut-out is where any trace ends. The Cut-Out is load bearing because it is the structural insulation the doctrine describes (Arendt; Church Committee): the apparatus arranged so that responsibility is dispersed through it and no hand seems to hold the whole, the seam engineered so the deed cannot be traced back through it.

## Apparent

An ordinary chain of command, or a layer of management.
A cut-out looks like the normal distance between a leader and the front line, a delegation like any other, so its role as a deliberate break in the trail hides inside the ordinary shape of a hierarchy.

## Yearbook

Manned by [the broker](persona_the_broker.md) through [winking](process_winking.md), the layer [the wink](piece_the_wink.md) travels through.
It is the seam where the trail dies: the Cut-Out is why [the denial](piece_the_denial.md) can hold, the distance that leaves the principal untouched by the deed.
