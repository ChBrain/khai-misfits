# Play: Plausible Deniability

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Plausible Deniability.
A principal wants something done that cannot be asked for aloud, so it is not asked for: a wish is let fall, a wink is passed down a chain, an agent who understands does the deed at arm's length, and the principal takes care not to be told how ... so that when the thing surfaces, as such things do, there is no order to produce, no knowledge to admit, and the principal can say, truthfully, that it did not know, while the one who understood the wink is left holding the deed alone.

## Arc

The bend is this: the way to answer for nothing is to arrange, in advance, to know nothing.
It runs on a gap built on purpose between the wish and the hand.
A wish that could never survive being written down is conveyed instead by a look, a phrase, a handle-it, so that intent travels and no order does. Between the principal and the deed are laid one or more cut-outs, a broker, an intermediary, an arm's length, so the principal's own hands never touch it. And over the whole arrangement the principal draws a blindfold on purpose, does not ask, does not want to be told, structures the reporting so the knowledge never arrives, so that the ignorance is real and therefore the denial is true.
None of it need be a lie. When the deed surfaces there is genuinely no order, genuinely no knowledge, so the principal denies and the denial holds, and the agent who read the wink correctly is cut loose to bear it alone, having done exactly what was wanted and nothing that can be traced to the wanting. The structure works precisely because everyone in it behaved as designed: the wish unspoken, the order unwritten, the knowledge unheld.
Pierce the shield, hold a principal answerable for what it arranged not to know, name willful blindness as a kind of knowing and command as a kind of ordering, and the deniability fails: but the wink leaves no proof, the arrangement looks exactly like honest delegation, and the same doctrine that catches the one who blinded itself on purpose can punish the one who simply, honestly, did not know.

## Triggers

**[The Wish](plot_the_wish.md)**
A principal wants a result that cannot be asked for aloud: a corner cut, a rule bent, a thing done that no memo could survive. The Principal holds the wish and knows it must never become an order. Closes when the wanting is clear and the asking is impossible.

**[The Wink](plot_the_wink.md)**
Cue: intent must travel without an order travelling with it. The Principal lets the wish fall as a look or a phrase, and the Broker carries it down the chain as a hint, adding a layer of arm's length, so a wish moves and no instruction is written. Closes when everyone who needs to understand does, and nothing is on the record.

**[The Blindfold](plot_the_blindfold.md)**
Cue: knowledge is a liability the principal must not hold. The Principal declines to be told, does not ask, and structures the reporting so the doing never reaches it, and the Broker filters the chain to keep it clean. Closes when the ignorance is real, built on purpose, so the future denial will be true.

**[The Denial](plot_the_denial.md)**
Cue: the deed surfaces and someone comes asking. The Agent, who read the wink and did it, is found holding it alone, and the Principal says, truthfully, that it gave no order and had no knowledge, while the Investigator finds no thread to pull. Closes when accountability fails to attach and the agent is cut loose.

**[The Piercing](plot_the_piercing.md)**
Cue: someone moves to make arranged ignorance itself a fault. The Reformer names willful blindness as knowing and command as ordering, so the shield can be pierced, against a wink that left no proof and an arrangement that looks like honest delegation. Closes when the principal is held for what it arranged not to know ... or the proof cannot be found, or the doctrine catches the honestly ignorant along with the deliberately blind.

## Stakes

Whether the one who wanted a deed can ever be made to answer for it, or only ever the hand that did it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: away from the principal, whose wish is unspoken and whose hands are clean, and toward the agent, who read the wink and holds the deed alone. The production cannot make an unwritten order appear in the record, nor make a built ignorance false, so the denial is true and the shield holds by design. It can only show that no single step was a crime, that a wish is not an order and not-asking is not lying and doing as one understood is not being told, and that the one thing that would attach the deed to the wanting, treating a purpose-built ignorance as a kind of knowledge, is a doctrine hard to prove, easy to resist, and unable to tell cleanly the one who blinded itself from the one who simply did not know.
