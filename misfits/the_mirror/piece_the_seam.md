# Piece: The Seam

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The fault line where the system's boundaries settle onto the org's, and the integration bugs collect.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the integration](place_the_integration.md), in the merge, the staging failure, the 2 a.m. page.
Where two well-built halves are supposed to meet and almost do: the mismatched assumption, the field one team versions and the other doesn't, the retry the caller never expected. The bug that is nobody's code and everybody's problem.

## Load Bearing

Without it the mirroring would be a diagram curiosity, not a cost anyone pays.
With it the org's shape shows up as defects, measurably, exactly on the boundaries between teams: the mirroring hypothesis made concrete, where loosely coupled teams produce loosely coupled parts and the couplings that remain fail at the org lines. The Seam is load bearing because it is where the law is felt, the place the system reveals which shape it actually took.

## Apparent

An integration bug. Bad luck at the edges.
It looks like ordinary friction, a defect to be triaged and owned by whoever is nearest, nothing structural. That reading is the trap, because the seam is not scattered at random: it lands on the boundary every time, and calling it luck is how the org's fingerprint goes unread.

## Yearbook

Forced together by [the splicer](persona_the_splicer.md) through [integrating](process_integrating.md), and held, after, by [the maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md).
It is where the play's cost lands: the Seam settles onto [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md), proves [the mirror](piece_the_mirror.md) true, and stays until [the reorg](piece_the_reorg.md) moves the boundary out from under it.
