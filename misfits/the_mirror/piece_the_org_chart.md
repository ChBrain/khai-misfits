# Piece: The Org Chart

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The communication structure that actually exists: who talks to whom, and how easily.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the two rooms](place_the_two_rooms.md), made real in seating, reporting lines, and standing invitations.
Not the diagram on the wall but the live graph of communication: the people who share a manager, a channel, a lunch, and the people who file a ticket to reach each other. Dense inside a team, thin between teams.

## Load Bearing

Without it a design could be built by whoever, wherever, and the shape would answer only to the drawing.
With it the system inherits a structure nobody drew for it: work flows easily where communication is dense and jams where it is thin, so the software's real boundaries form on the org's real boundaries. The Org Chart is load bearing because it, not the Architecture, is what the system will end up a copy of.

## Apparent

Just how the company is arranged.
It looks like org design, an HR concern, a matter of managers and headcount with nothing to say about software. That is the disguise, because the arrangement of who can talk to whom is the arrangement the product will be built in, and the chart is a system diagram no one reads as one.

## Yearbook

Inhabited by [the lead](persona_the_lead.md) and [the neighbor](persona_the_neighbor.md), through [building](process_building.md) inside their teams.
It is the shape the system copies: the Org Chart draws [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md) the software will inherit, and its imprint on the delivered system is exactly what [the mirror](piece_the_mirror.md) reflects.
