# Plot: The Split

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The two rooms](place_the_two_rooms.md), where the work must be handed to the teams that exist.
The design is split into parts, and the parts are dealt out along the org chart, not the architecture: this team owns what is near it, that team owns the next.

## Action

[Building](process_building.md): [The Lead](persona_the_lead.md), holding [the team lead's seat](position_team_lead.md), builds the nearest module densely and well; [The Neighbor](persona_the_neighbor.md) at [the sibling's seat](position_sibling.md) builds the next on their own schedule and reading.

## Stage

[The Lead](persona_the_lead.md) works [Own the Module](plan_own_the_module.md) and [The Neighbor](persona_the_neighbor.md) works [Ship Our Half](plan_ship_our_half.md): two strong parts, each coordinated tightly inside its team.
The interface between them crosses [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md), so the ownership follows [the org chart](piece_the_org_chart.md) and not the design, and the one contract that spans the wall belongs to no single team.

## Tension

Both teams are right, and the split is already the shape.
Dealing the work along the org chart is the only practical move, because those are the teams there are; but it means the seams of the system are being placed on the seams of the org before a line of the far interface is written. The design's boundaries and the org's boundaries have quietly diverged, and the software will keep the org's.
