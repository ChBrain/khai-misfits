# Piece: The Boundary

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The line between two teams, where communication thins and coordination gets dear.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the two rooms](place_the_two_rooms.md), on the wall between them, and in every place a message has to cross to be heard.
The edge where a quick word becomes a scheduled sync, where shared context stops and translation starts. Inside it, a team agrees without trying; across it, agreement is work.

## Load Bearing

Without it every part of the system would be coordinated as cheaply as a team coordinates with itself.
With it there are places where coordination is expensive and rare, and the design must cross exactly those places to hold together. The Boundary is load bearing because it is where the mirroring bites: a module split across a boundary is a module whose halves rarely talk, and the software remembers every conversation that did not happen.

## Apparent

An ordinary reporting line.
It looks like structure doing its job, teams with clear ownership and clean scopes, the sensible division of a large effort. And it is sensible, right up to the moment the architecture asks two teams on opposite sides of it to share one interface.

## Yearbook

Drawn by [the org chart](piece_the_org_chart.md), and crossed, thinly, by [the liaison](persona_the_liaison.md) through [coordinating](process_coordinating.md).
It is where the trouble lives: the Boundary is the line [the seam](piece_the_seam.md) settles onto, and the reason [the handoff](piece_the_handoff.md) is the weakest contract in the system.
