# Piece: The Handoff

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The thin, expensive communication across a boundary that stands in for a shared owner.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the sync](place_the_sync.md), the scheduled cross-team meeting where context is translated rather than shared.
The status update, the interface review, the Slack thread that spans two teams: the substitute for sitting in one room, carrying a fraction of the bandwidth a single team has with itself, at several times the cost.

## Load Bearing

Without it the two halves of a split interface would never talk at all and the seam would gape.
With it they talk a little, expensively, and the seam narrows without closing: the Handoff is the coordination that crosses [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md), and it is always thinner than the coordination inside a team, so the contract it maintains is always the weakest one in the system. It is load bearing because it is what the org offers in place of a shared owner, and why that offer is never quite enough.

## Apparent

Good collaboration.
It looks like teams working well together: regular syncs, clear updates, cross-functional alignment, all the ceremonies of coordination. And it is real effort, honestly made, which is what makes its insufficiency so hard to see: the meeting happened, the seam still failed.

## Yearbook

Carried by [the liaison](persona_the_liaison.md) through [coordinating](process_coordinating.md), against the thinness of [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md).
It is the effort that cannot win: the Handoff is what [the interface](piece_the_interface.md) leans on across a boundary, and its limits are why coordinating harder never moves [the seam](piece_the_seam.md).
