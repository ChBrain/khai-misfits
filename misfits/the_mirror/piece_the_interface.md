# Piece: The Interface

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The intended contract between modules: narrow, explicit, and strong only where a team owns both sides.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the whiteboard](place_the_whiteboard.md) as a clean line, and at [the integration](place_the_integration.md) as whatever the two teams actually agreed.
The place where one module meets another: a signature, a schema, a protocol. On the design it is a single tidy edge; in delivery it is as sharp as the conversation that defined it.

## Load Bearing

Without it modules cannot compose at all, and the architecture is just boxes.
With it composition depends on agreement, and agreement is cheap inside a team and dear across a boundary, so an interface within one team's ownership is crisp and an interface split between two is vague exactly where it matters. The Interface is load bearing because it is where the design's cleanliness meets the org's communication, and loses.

## Apparent

A well-specified contract.
It looks settled: the types line up, the endpoint is documented, the diagram shows one clean arrow. And within a team it is settled; across a boundary the same arrow hides two different readings that will not meet until [the seam](piece_the_seam.md).

## Yearbook

Specified by [the drafter](persona_the_drafter.md), and honoured unevenly through [building](process_building.md) on each side.
It is the design's hope and the org's tell: an Interface owned by one team holds, one split by [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md) frays, and the fraying is what [the splicer](persona_the_splicer.md) is left to force together.
