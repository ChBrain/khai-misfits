# Plot: The Mirror

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The integration](place_the_integration.md), where the shipped system can finally be held against the org chart.
Outage after outage, each on a boundary between two teams. Someone lays the incident map over the reporting lines and the two shapes are the same.

## Action

[Mirroring](process_mirroring.md): [The Maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md), holding [the on-call seat](position_on_call.md), keeps the seams from failing and sees, from the pager, the pattern no single team sees.

## Stage

[The Maintainer](persona_the_maintainer.md) works [Hold the Seam](plan_hold_the_seam.md): patch it, restart it, trace each failure to the two teams it lives between, and name that [the mirror](piece_the_mirror.md) is real.
The architecture never had a chance the org was not shaped for: the system is a copy of the communication structure that built it.

## Tension

The pattern is legible, and the seat that sees it cannot change it.
To name the mirror is to say the shape came from the org, not the code, which is the hardest finding to act on, because acting means reorganising and the easy move is to call it weak engineering and demand more coordination. So the maintainer holds a structural problem together with operational effort ... and the demand for another sync buys nothing the seam will feel.
