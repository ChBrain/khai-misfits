# Piece: The Mirror

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The phenomenon whole: the system a copy of the org that built it, and the reshaping that could change the reflection.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the integration](place_the_integration.md), where the shipped system can finally be held against the org chart and seen to match.
Not one seam but the pattern of all of them: the fact, stated by Conway and measured since, that a design's structure copies the communication structure of the organisation that produced it, so the product is a portrait of the org.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of unrelated integration bugs and one awkward reorg.
With it there is a named law the production stands on: the shape of the system mirrors the shape of the org, so the architecture you can ship is the one your communication structure is built for, and the seams sit on the team lines because that is where the talking stops. The Mirror is load bearing because it holds both the law and its one remedy, that to change the reflection you must change the thing reflected, the org, not the diagram.

## Apparent

A coincidence. A run of bad integrations that better engineers would have avoided.
It looks like a quality problem, or a coordination problem, anything but a structural law, because no single seam announces the pattern and the org chart is not where anyone looks for the cause. That dismissal is the last disguise, because the same law that makes the toy case inevitable is the one that put every seam on a boundary.

## Yearbook

Assembled from [the architecture](piece_the_architecture.md) meeting [the org chart](piece_the_org_chart.md), and answered by [the reorg](piece_the_reorg.md).
It is the law the play runs on: the Mirror is what [the seam](piece_the_seam.md) proves, what [coordinating](process_coordinating.md) cannot escape, and what [reorganizing](process_reorganizing.md) is the only shown way to turn, a design shipped by the shape of its builders unless the builders are reshaped.
