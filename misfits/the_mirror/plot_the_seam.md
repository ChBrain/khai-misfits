# Plot: The Seam

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The integration](place_the_integration.md), where the two well-built halves are supposed to meet.
The merge, the staging failure, the end-to-end test that will not pass: the parts are each correct and do not quite fit.

## Action

[Integrating](process_integrating.md): [The Splicer](persona_the_splicer.md), holding [the integrator's seat](position_integrator.md), forces the halves together, while [The Liaison](persona_the_liaison.md) at [the coordinator's seat](position_coordinator.md) syncs across the boundary to align the readings.

## Stage

[The Splicer](persona_the_splicer.md) works [Make It Meet](plan_make_it_meet.md) and [The Liaison](persona_the_liaison.md) works [Sync at the Boundary](plan_sync_at_the_boundary.md): an adapter written by hand, a meeting held to align the terms.
[The seam](piece_the_seam.md) settles onto the boundary, and [the handoff](piece_the_handoff.md) proves too thin to close it: the glue holds, the sync narrows the gap, the fault line stays.

## Tension

The parts meet, and the meeting is a monument to the boundary.
Coordinating harder narrows the gap and never closes it, because the seam is set by who reports to whom, not by who met on Tuesday; the adapter makes it work by writing the org's boundary into the code. Everything is well-built, and the integration bugs live exactly on the line between two teams that no one was assigned to hold.
