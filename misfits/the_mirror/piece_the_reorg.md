# Piece: The Reorg

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The reversal: reshaping the teams to the architecture, so the communication structure produces the design.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the reorg room](place_the_reorg_room.md), where team boundaries are redrawn instead of code.
The inverse move: take the architecture you want, and build the org to match it, so the dense communication falls inside each intended module and the thin communication falls on the edges the design meant to be thin. Reshape who talks to whom until the shape you get is the shape you drew.

## Load Bearing

Without it the seams are fixed wherever the org already put them, and the design is stuck.
With it the seams can move, because they follow the boundaries, and the boundaries are now the ones the architecture chose: the inverse Conway maneuver, aligning communication structure to intended structure so the mirror finally reflects the drawing. The Reorg is load bearing because it is the only move in the play that changes the shape rather than fighting it.

## Apparent

Disruption. Churn. Moving people around instead of shipping.
It looks like the expensive, morale-costing thing every org avoids: reporting lines redrawn, teams broken up, ownership reassigned, all with no feature to show for it. That reading is why it is the last resort, because reshaping the org is real cost paid now against a payoff that is only ever the absence of seams.

## Yearbook

Made by [the reorganizer](persona_the_reorganizer.md) through [reorganizing](process_reorganizing.md), against the settled weight of [the org chart](piece_the_org_chart.md).
It is the remedy the play holds: the Reorg moves [the boundary](piece_the_boundary.md) so [the seam](piece_the_seam.md) has nowhere to fall, and it is the one thing that makes [the mirror](piece_the_mirror.md) reflect the architecture instead of the org.
