# Piece: The Architecture

## Taxonomy

[The Mirror](play_the_mirror.md). The design intended: clean modules and interfaces, drawn as though the teams were interchangeable.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the whiteboard](place_the_whiteboard.md), where the boxes and arrows are clean and the coupling is low.
The target shape: modules that hide their internals, interfaces that are narrow and explicit, a decomposition chosen for the problem and not for the people, information hidden behind boundaries drawn where the design wants them.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no intended shape for the org to fail to produce, and no gap between design and delivery.
With it there is a clean target the communication structure will be measured against, and quietly defeat: the Architecture is drawn as if any team could build any module, which is the one assumption the org chart will not honour. It is load bearing because it is the shape the play keeps promising and the org keeps failing to ship.

## Apparent

Good design, plainly.
It looks like exactly what should be built: low coupling, high cohesion, boundaries in the right places, a diagram anyone would approve. And it is good design, for a team shaped like the diagram, which is the team that does not exist.

## Yearbook

Drawn by [the drafter](persona_the_drafter.md) through [designing](process_designing.md), and split, in a moment, along lines it did not choose.
It is the promise the play opens on: the Architecture sets [the interface](piece_the_interface.md) where the design wants it, and [the org chart](piece_the_org_chart.md) sets it somewhere else, and only [the mirror](piece_the_mirror.md) names why the second wins.
