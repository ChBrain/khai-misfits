# Piece: The Common Mode

## Taxonomy

[Belt and Braces](play_belt_and_braces.md). The shared cause the supposedly independent backups rest on, the one power or crew or premise or environment that fells the redundant set at a stroke, the reckoning that shows the many guards were one.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Rests latent in [the system](place_the_system.md), where the backups share what they share.
Traced early, if ever, at [the review](place_the_review.md), before the shared cause has fired.

## Load Bearing

Without it the backups differ in their causes and the redundancy holds, and no single fault takes the set.
With it the trap has its reckoning: a cause the parallel backups share, so the independence the arithmetic assumed is not there and one fault reaches all of them at once (Sagan; Perrow, on common-mode failure, the shared dependency that defeats parallel redundancy at a stroke, the second pump dead of the same water as the first). The Common Mode is load bearing because it is what the redundancy finally costs: not many independent chances but one chance wearing the face of many, so the piece carries the shared fault that fells the belt and the braces together, the many guards revealed to have been one and no one having been reckless.

## Apparent

A freak event, an unlucky fault that somehow took every backup at once.
Seen at the surface, the Common Mode looks like extraordinary bad luck, redundant systems that improbably failed together, and the truth that the backups were never independent, that one power or crew or premise or environment underlay them all, that the joint failure was certain the moment they shared a cause, stays hidden behind how much a shared-cause collapse looks like a run of coincidence.
