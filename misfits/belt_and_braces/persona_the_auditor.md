# Persona: The Auditor

## Taxonomy

[Auditor](position_auditor.md). The one who would count the true independence before another backup is added, tracing the shared power, crew, premise and environment and pricing the complexity, at the cost of arguing against a spare that reads as safe.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Sceptical. Willing to refuse a backup. Suspicious of a figure that only climbs.
Is the one who asks whether the spare is really independent, the reliability engineer who traces the common cause, the reviewer who prices the coupling each addition brings.
No obstruction in it: the Auditor does not doubt that the backup covers its gap, only doubts that a part sharing the cause of what it backs is the independent chance the figure counts.

## Action

Traces whether the backup shares power, crew, premise or environment with what it backs, prices the complexity each addition brings, and counts the true independence rather than the number of spares.
Treats a reliability figure that climbs with every unit as suspect precisely because it climbs, and looks for the coupling and the shirking the arithmetic cannot see.
Does what catches the trap: it separates the parts that share a cause from the parts that do not, and only that separation shows whether a backup adds a chance or adds a coupling.

## Shadow

Not a free corrective, a costly and unwelcome refusal, and that is the whole difficulty: the count that would catch the turn is exactly the argument against a defence in depth everyone can see is prudent.
Because each backup reads as more safety and the figure rewards it, the Auditor must refuse a spare that looks sound, spend to find a shared cause no incident has yet exposed, and doubt a number that climbs, so counting the true independence is bought against every signal that another guard is safer, the coupling it prevents leaving no trace and the backup it refuses looking, afterward, like safety declined.
It counts the independence to hold the reliability, and counting it is read as standing in the way of safety.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "before we add another, show me it does not share a cause with the last."
Says it into a room that can see the figure climbing, and is heard, often, as arguing against defence in depth, the one who would refuse a backup that any prudent engineer would add.
