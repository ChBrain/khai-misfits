# Piece: The Unshrouding

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The cure that does not pay: the true total advertised, and answered with avoidance rather than reward.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Attempted by [the Honest Rival](persona_the_honest_rival.md) in [the Market](place_the_market.md), through [Unshrouding](process_unshrouding.md).
It is offered openly, not smuggled in: a total price, stated plainly, where every other firm states only the base.

## Load Bearing

The Unshrouding is load bearing because it is the point at which the misfit turns: it is the one move that looks, on its face, like it should win the market, and its failure is the entire finding. A firm that advertises the true total and warns buyers about the add-on does not gain their custom, because a buyer who is debiased by the warning and who has a close substitute for the add-on simply avoids the add-on rather than rewarding the firm that named it: the warning turns a myope into a sophisticate, and a sophisticate needs only the cheap base every firm already posts, not the honest total only one firm bothers to state, so the honest firm pays the cost of educating the market and captures none of the gain, which is the curse of debiasing (Gabaix and Laibson). This result is conditional and the condition is the point: it holds specifically when the add-on has a close substitute for the buyer to dodge into. Where no substitute exists, an unshrouding firm could in principle capture the debiased buyer's custom; the misfit's ink, overdrafts, and unused gym months are all cases with a substitute at hand, and that is precisely what makes honesty the losing move here, not a general claim that transparency never pays anywhere.

## Apparent

The obviously right move, the one that should be rewarded: tell the truth, compete on honesty, let the informed buyer choose well.
The disguise is that it looks costless to the firm and purely beneficial to the buyer, a clean win for transparency. It is neither: it costs the firm the sale to a shrouding rival, and it benefits the buyer only by letting them route around the add-on elsewhere, never by routing them toward the teller.
