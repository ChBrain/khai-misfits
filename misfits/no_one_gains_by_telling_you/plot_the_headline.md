# Plot: The Headline

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Shelf](place_the_shelf.md), where a price gets set before any buyer arrives to read it: the sticker to be printed, the number to be posted first and biggest.

## Action

[Shrouding](process_shrouding.md): [The Firm](persona_the_firm.md), holding [the seller's seat](position_seller.md), tables [Shroud the Add-On](plan_shroud_the_add_on.md) and sets [the Base](piece_the_base.md) low on [the Shelf](place_the_shelf.md), holding [the Add-On](piece_the_add_on.md) behind [the Shroud](piece_the_shroud.md), off the number any buyer will compare.

## Stage

The base goes up cheap, sometimes below what it costs the Firm to make, because the lowest visible price is what wins the sale in a market where a rival is always ready to undercut. The add-on that will fund the difference is not hidden in the sense of erased: it is disclosed, usually, somewhere in the terms, true and findable, only never placed beside the number a buyer weighs against another shelf's number. Nothing is printed that is false. The Firm does what a firm in a competitive market does, and the Shelf reads, honestly, as a good deal.

## Tension

The arrangement is in place before it needs defending: a genuinely cheap base and a genuinely shrouded add-on, nothing on the sticker that could be caught as a lie. The Headline is not a trick sprung on a buyer who has not yet arrived; it is the ordinary placement of a low price where attention will fall, done because a firm that placed the total there instead would simply lose the sale. The beat closes with a shelf that looks like nothing but competition, because that is exactly what it is.
