# Plot: The Choice

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Shelf](place_the_shelf.md), where a buyer arrives with a set of visible prices to compare, the base the only number any shelf actually shows.

## Action

[Underweighting](process_underweighting.md): [The Myope](persona_the_myope.md), holding [the myope's seat](position_myope.md), tables [Buy the Base](plan_buy_the_base.md) and chooses on [the Base](piece_the_base.md), weighing [the Add-On](piece_the_add_on.md) too little from [the Myopia](piece_the_myopia.md), the documented present bias that makes a deferred cost genuinely harder to feel than a near price (DellaVigna and Malmendier).

## Stage

The Myope compares the numbers actually in front of them and picks the lowest, exactly as attention and time incline anyone to do. The add-on is not unknown or unread; it is weighed, at the moment of choice, against a base that can be felt right now and a bill that cannot, and the near number crowds out the far one. The choice lands on the cheap sticker, sound on everything it was made against.

## Tension

The base is bought and the add-on, unweighed, is still waiting, off at the shelf's edge where it was never asked to be priced. Nothing here is a failure to read: the terms are available, the Myope simply weighs a real cost too little at the one moment it would have mattered, the way a documented bias always does. The beat closes on a purchase that is reasonable on everything the buyer looked at, and short on everything they did not.
