# Piece: The Myopia

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The documented bias that underweights the deferred add-on at the moment of choice: present bias, not stupidity.

## Owner

## Place

It travels with the buyer rather than sitting in any one place: the same underweighting shows up wherever a salient price meets a deferred one.

## Load Bearing

The Myopia is load bearing because it is the opening the whole arrangement exploits: without a buyer who weighs the near, salient base more heavily than the far, deferred add-on, shrouding the add-on would win a firm nothing, since an unbiased comparison shopper would price the two together regardless of where each number sits. The bias is real and measured, present bias and overconfidence about one's own future self-control that leads buyers to choose contracts they later overpay under, evidenced in the gap between the flat-rate and pay-per-visit gym plans people actually pick and what their own attendance would have made economical (DellaVigna and Malmendier), and it is the same underweighting of a shrouded, deferred attribute that lets firms design profitable base-and-add-on pricing around it in the first place (Gabaix and Laibson). It is not a failure to read the terms: the add-on is knowable, often printed plainly, and the Myope could in principle compute the true total. The bias sits in the weighing, not the reading, at the moment the choice is made, when the near number crowds out the far one.

## Apparent

Distraction, or trust, or simply being busy: the ordinary reasons a shopper doesn't do the full arithmetic.
The disguise is that it looks like an individual lapse, one buyer not trying hard enough. It is not: it is a documented regularity general enough that firms can design contracts around it the way an engineer designs around a known force, which is exactly what distinguishes it from carelessness.
