# Persona: The Honest Rival

## Taxonomy

[Rival](position_rival.md). The firm that would compete on honesty, advertising the true total and unshrouding the add-on, and gains nothing for it, the one who tries the obvious cure and finds it does not pay.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Straight. Transparent. Sure the truth will win.
Is the firm that reads the arrangement clearly and decides to break it by telling the buyer the real total, warning them that the cheap printer means dear ink, competing on the honest number.
No naivety in the reading: the diagnosis is right, the buyer really is being taxed, and honesty really would serve them, so the move looks like the winning one.

## Action

Tells the truth, on [Tell the Truth](plan_tell_the_truth.md), advertising the true total in [the Market](place_the_market.md) and unshrouding the add-on through [Unshrouding](process_unshrouding.md).
Carries [the Unshrouding](piece_the_unshrouding.md), the cure that does not pay, and wins no one, because a warned buyer answers by avoiding the add-on rather than by rewarding the teller.
Does what an honest competitor does against a shrouded market: pays to educate the buyers, and watches them take the lesson and their custom elsewhere.

## Shadow

Not a hero who fixes the market, a firm that pays for a cure it cannot capture, and that is the difficulty: telling the truth turns myopes into sophisticates, and a sophisticate does not need the truth-teller.
Because a debiased buyer with a close substitute for the add-on avoids the add-on rather than switching to the honest firm, the Rival bears the whole cost of the education and captures none of the reward, so the one move that would clear the trap is the losing one, and honesty is punished not by any cheat but by the plain arithmetic of who benefits from being warned.
It tells the truth to win the market, and telling the truth to win the market is exactly what wins it nothing.

## Tell

Says "we will show you the real total, so you can choose," and believes it will be enough.
Learns, too late, that a buyer shown the real total thanks them and then buys the cheap base anywhere and dodges the add-on, that the warning made a sophisticate and not a customer, and that no one, itself least of all, gained by the telling.
