# Piece: The Shroud

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The suppression of the add-on's salience: kept off the compared number, disclosed only where no one is weighing it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Held at [the Shelf](place_the_shelf.md) by [the Firm](persona_the_firm.md), through [Shrouding](process_shrouding.md).
Not a locked drawer: a placement, the add-on's true cost sitting exactly where a comparison shopper never looks.

## Load Bearing

The Shroud is load bearing because it is the mechanism that keeps the base and the add-on from ever being weighed together: in a competitive market, no firm is rewarded for making the deferred attribute salient, since the rival that keeps it off the sticker will always look cheaper and take the sale, so information about the add-on is suppressed by ordinary competitive pressure rather than by any single firm's decision to deceive (Gabaix and Laibson). It is not a lie and not concealment of a fact the firm denies: the add-on is usually disclosed somewhere in the terms, true and findable. What the Shroud withholds is not the fact but its salience, the weight the fact would carry if it sat beside the price the buyer is actually comparing.

## Apparent

Fine print. Standard terms. The ordinary business of disclosure done, technically, in full.
The disguise is that nothing printed is false, so the Shroud looks like paperwork rather than strategy: no clause hidden, no number altered, only a fact placed where weighing it costs more attention than a buyer spends at the moment of choice.
