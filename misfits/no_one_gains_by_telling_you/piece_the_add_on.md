# Piece: The Add-On

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The high, deferred cost where the margin sits: the dear ink, the overdraft fee, the minibar, the months paid for and never used.

## Place

Lands at [the Bill](place_the_bill.md), off the number compared at [the Shelf](place_the_shelf.md).
Not hidden in the sense of concealed: disclosed, usually, somewhere in the arrangement, only never where the buyer is weighing price against price.

## Load Bearing

The Add-On is load bearing because it is where the firm's margin actually lives: the base can be priced at or below cost only because the add-on is priced high enough to cover the gap and more, the shrouded attribute that subsidizes the salient one (Gabaix and Laibson). Take the add-on out and the base cannot stay this cheap; put the add-on on the sticker and the base loses the comparison to a rival who keeps its own add-on off. Crucially, the add-on nearly always has a close substitute sitting beside it: ink sold by someone else, an overdraft avoided by simply not spending past the balance, a gym session actually attended. That substitute is what makes the add-on avoidable to a buyer who sees it coming, and its presence is exactly what the misfit's honest-firm turn depends on: an add-on with no substitute to dodge into would not curse the truth-teller the same way.

## Apparent

A minor extra, easy to overlook, the fine print of an otherwise good deal: ink, an occasional fee, a service charge.
The disguise is scale read as triviality. Any one instance looks small; across the arrangement it is the whole of the firm's return, the number the base's cheapness was always paying for in advance.
