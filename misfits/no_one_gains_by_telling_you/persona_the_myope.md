# Persona: The Myope

## Taxonomy

[Myope](position_myope.md). The present-biased buyer who chooses on the salient base and underweights the deferred add-on, not from stupidity but from an ordinary, documented bias, the self the trap draws its tax from.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Ordinary. Reasonable. Choosing the cheaper one.
Is the buyer comparing the numbers in front of them and picking the lowest, weighing the price now more than the cost that lands later, exactly as attention and time incline everyone.
No foolishness in it: only a person subject to a real, measured present bias, the deferred cost genuinely harder to feel at the moment of choice than the price on the sticker.

## Action

Buys the base, on [Buy the Base](plan_buy_the_base.md), choosing on [the Base](piece_the_base.md) at [the Shelf](place_the_shelf.md) and underweighting [the Add-On](piece_the_add_on.md) through [Underweighting](process_underweighting.md).
Meets [the Add-On](piece_the_add_on.md) later at [the Bill](place_the_bill.md), and pays [the Tax](piece_the_tax.md), more across the whole than an honest single price would have cost.
Does what a present-biased buyer does under a shrouded add-on: chooses on the number they can feel and meets the number they could not, and pays for the difference.

## Shadow

Not a dupe who was fooled, a buyer weighing a real cost too little, and that is the difficulty: nothing corrected the bias at the moment it mattered, and the choice was sound on everything that was salient.
Because the add-on lands later and the present bias is real, the Myope is not tricked into a bad choice but inclined into one by how attention weighs a near price against a far cost, so the loss is not the mark of a swindle worked on them but of an ordinary disposition met by a market built to price around it.
It chooses reasonably on what it can feel, and choosing reasonably on what it can feel is exactly what the tax is levied on.

## Tell

Says "look how cheap," pointing at the sticker, and means it.
Cannot see, at the shelf, that the cheapness on the sticker is funded by the cost off it, that the printer is cheap because the ink is dear, and that the number it is choosing on is the one number the arrangement was built to make it choose.
