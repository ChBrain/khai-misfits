# Plot: Telling the Truth

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Market](place_the_market.md), where a firm reads the arrangement clearly and decides it can win by breaking it: compete on honesty instead of on the lowest visible number.

## Action

[Unshrouding](process_unshrouding.md): [The Honest Rival](persona_the_honest_rival.md), holding [the rival's seat](position_rival.md), tables [Tell the Truth](plan_tell_the_truth.md) and advertises the true total in [the Market](place_the_market.md), warning buyers that the cheap base means a dear extra, carrying [the Unshrouding](piece_the_unshrouding.md) as the cost of the telling.

## Stage

The diagnosis behind the move is correct: the buyer really is taxed, the total really is worth knowing, and telling it looks like the obvious cure. So the Rival unshrouds, names the add-on plainly, and waits for the market to reward the one firm that did not shroud. What arrives instead is a buyer who says thank you, takes the lesson, and buys the cheap base anywhere, because the substitute for the add-on was always available to anyone who knew to look for it, and knowing to look for it is exactly what the warning supplied.

## Tension

A debiased buyer with a close substitute for the add-on avoids the add-on rather than rewarding the firm that told them about it; the education turns a Myope into a Sophisticate, and a Sophisticate needs no honest seller. The Rival pays the whole cost of unshrouding and captures none of the reward, having made the market one sophisticate richer and itself no customers at all. The beat closes with the cure tried in good faith and found, by the plain arithmetic of who benefits from being warned, to be the losing move.
