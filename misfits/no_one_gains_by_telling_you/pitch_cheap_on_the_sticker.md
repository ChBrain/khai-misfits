# Pitch: Cheap on the Sticker

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Knowing, matter-of-fact, sensible.
A low price on the sticker, a market of firms each doing the obvious profitable thing, a shopper choosing on the number in front of them.
Nothing false in it: the firms are competing honestly, the sticker price is real, and the buyer is choosing exactly as attention and time make sensible.

## Undertow

Under the good deal, the part that was never on the sticker.
The base is cheap because the add-on is dear, and the add-on lands later, weighed too little at the moment of choice by an ordinary present bias, so the buyer pays more across the whole than an honest single price would have cost, no lie told and no fool made.
The low number where the buyer looks is paid for by the high number where they do not.

## Nerve

The feeling of a firm that could win by simply telling the truth, advertising the real total, warning the buyer,
and gains nothing for it, because a warned buyer learns to dodge the add-on rather than to reward the teller,
so the honest move is the losing one, and the sticker stays cheap because no one is paid to complete it.

## Echo

After the run: a stable market in which the shroud holds, no firm defecting because defection does not pay,
the tax flowing from the myopic who meet the add-on to the sophisticated who dodge it and ride the discount they fund,
and the one cure, information, cheap to tell and worth nothing to any firm that tells it, so the loss stands with no cheat to catch and no dupe to blame.
