# Piece: The Base

## Taxonomy

[No One Gains by Telling You](play_no_one_gains_by_telling_you.md). The low, salient headline price: the number the buyer compares on, posted cheap and funded by the add-on that never makes the sticker.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Posted on [the Shelf](place_the_shelf.md) by [the Firm](persona_the_firm.md), through [Shrouding](process_shrouding.md).
The cheap printer, the free account, the low room rate, the monthly fee that reads as nothing a day: wherever a buyer's eye lands first, the Base is the number waiting there.

## Load Bearing

The Base is load bearing because it is the number every rival is judged against, and the number that wins the sale: in a competitive market the firm that posts the lowest visible price takes the custom, so the base is driven down, sometimes below what it costs to make, and the shortfall is recovered on the attribute the buyer is not weighing (Gabaix and Laibson). Without a base priced this low there is nothing to compare on and no sale to win first; the add-on can only be dear because the base has already made the sale cheap. It is not a false price or a bait switched at the register: the base is honestly, verifiably that cheap, and stays that cheap for as long as the add-on keeps subsidizing it.

## Apparent

A good deal, plainly. The cheapest printer, the lowest room rate, the free account: it reads as the firm competing well and the buyer choosing well.
The disguise is that the number is entirely true and entirely real, so there is nothing in it to catch. What it is not is the whole price, and nothing on the sticker says so.
