# No One Gains by Telling You: Reference

The research warrant for **No One Gains by Telling You** (play and company not yet staged;
this warrant is drafted first, to prove the science and the distinction before authoring).
The dynamic is the shrouded-attribute account of consumer exploitation: Gabaix and Laibson's
shrouded-attribute equilibrium and curse of debiasing, with DellaVigna and Malmendier's field
evidence of present-biased contract choice, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging shrouded-attribute pricing as a **stable-exploitation-equilibrium structure**: a market of
competing firms selling a base good and an add-on to consumers who are present-biased or myopic about
the add-on's cost, so firms set a low, salient base price and a high, shrouded add-on price, and no firm
can profit by unshrouding, advertising the true total and educating consumers, because a debiased
consumer who has a close substitute for the add-on simply avoids the add-on rather than rewarding the
transparent firm, so shrouding is a stable competitive equilibrium that extracts a myopia tax from the
biased while the sophisticated, who already avoid the add-on, are cross-subsidized by them. The governing
law is not that any firm swindles or any consumer is a fool, but that **a documented consumer bias,
present bias or myopia about a deferred or add-on cost, is exploited not by a cheat but by an ordinary
competitive equilibrium in which transparency does not pay, so the exploitation is stable, no firm
defecting to honesty and no informed shopper disciplining it**. The play models the documented mechanics:
the base-and-add-on equilibrium in which a shrouded high-priced add-on subsidizes a low base price, and
the curse of debiasing by which unshrouding fails to pay when the add-on has a close substitute (Gabaix
and Laibson), and the field evidence that firms optimally design contracts around consumers' documented
present bias, the biased overpaying under the very contracts they choose (DellaVigna and Malmendier), as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a con or a dupe.

It is distinct from the house's Time Inconsistency, where the misfit is intrapersonal, a present self
breaking the plan a past self made, one person's conflict between what they want now and what they wanted
for now, with nothing between agents and no market anywhere in it: there the bias plays out inside a
single self and betrays a future self, here the same present bias is only the input, and the trap is a
two-sided market equilibrium of firms and consumers in which the bias is priced around and exploited in a
stable competitive equilibrium, nothing promised and no future self betrayed, the failure living between
agents rather than within one. It is distinct from Nobody Reads It, where contract terms drift against
the buyer because too few read them, an informed-minority and search-cost mechanism in which a larger
informed readership would discipline the terms for everyone: there the cure is more attention and more
reading, and it works because informed shoppers police the terms, here the add-on cost is knowable and
even the sophisticated understand it, more information does not discipline the equilibrium, and unshrouding
fails by the curse of debiasing because the educated consumer answers by avoiding the add-on rather than
by rewarding the transparent firm, so the informed minority that fixes Nobody Reads It cannot fix this.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a swindler
  or a fool, because the source finding it stages is precisely that neither is required: each firm is
  competitive and profit-maximizing, doing exactly what the equilibrium rewards; the shrouding is not a
  lie but the ordinary suppression of a non-salient attribute that no competitor is rewarded for making
  salient (Gabaix and Laibson); and the consumer is not stupid but subject to a real, documented present
  bias or myopia about a deferred cost (DellaVigna and Malmendier). No firm defects to transparency
  because transparency does not pay, and no bait-and-switch or fraud is anywhere required, so the misfit
  is that an ordinary competitive market, each firm optimizing and each consumer merely present-biased,
  settles into a stable exploitation with no cheat to catch and no dupe to blame.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one obvious cure,
  unshrouding, advertising the true total and educating consumers, does not pay, because a debiased
  consumer who has a close substitute for the add-on avoids the add-on rather than switching to the
  transparent firm, so the honest firm bears the whole cost of the education and captures none of the
  reward (Gabaix and Laibson: the curse of debiasing). The sophisticated, who already avoid the add-on
  and are cross-subsidized by the myopic, have no interest in ending it, and even a mandate to disclose
  meets the same substitute-avoidance rather than a switch of custom, so the reckoning is that the single
  clearest remedy, information, is exactly the move the equilibrium is built to make unprofitable, not a
  free correction anyone can reach for.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the shrouded-attribute equilibrium and the curse of
  debiasing are Gabaix and Laibson's, the field evidence of present-biased contract choice is DellaVigna
  and Malmendier's, and the condition the trap actually requires, a documented consumer bias, a
  base-and-add-on structure, and an unshrouding that fails to pay, is carried as carefully as the finding
  itself. The scope is held to competitive markets with a shroudable add-on and a real consumer bias, and
  not stretched to every high price or every hidden fee that is in fact a swindle.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element. The play and its company are not yet authored; these are the
proposed load-bearing pieces the warrant commits the staging to carry, one per vector, no filler.

- **The Base**: carries the low, salient headline price (Gabaix and Laibson), the loss-leading base good
  that draws the consumer, priced down and subsidized by the add-on it hides.
- **The Add-On**: carries the high, deferred, shrouded cost (Gabaix and Laibson), the extra where the
  profit sits, the minibar and the ink and the fee, not advertised and not salient.
- **The Shroud**: carries the suppression of the add-on's salience (Gabaix and Laibson), the information
  not made plain that no competitor is rewarded for lifting, so it stays down across the market.
- **The Myopia**: carries the documented consumer bias (DellaVigna and Malmendier; Gabaix and Laibson),
  the present bias or inattention that underweights the deferred or add-on cost, real and measured and no
  fool's error.
- **The Unshrouding**: carries the cure that does not pay (Gabaix and Laibson), the transparency that a
  debiased consumer answers by avoiding the add-on rather than rewarding the honest firm, so the curse of
  debiasing keeps shrouding a stable equilibrium.
- **The Tax**: carries the transfer the equilibrium runs on (Gabaix and Laibson), the myopia tax paid by
  the biased, with the sophisticated who avoid the add-on cross-subsidized by them, the cost no one is
  cheated into and everyone competitive produces.
