# Plot: The Efficiency

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), a resource to be made to go further per unit, in good faith to save it.
The gain worked out, the saving multiplied from it, everything resting on a fixed count of units.

## Action

[Improving](process_improving.md): [The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md), holding [the efficiency engineer's seat](position_efficiency_engineer.md), doubles the efficiency, while [The Forecaster](persona_the_forecaster.md) at [the analyst's seat](position_analyst.md) books the saving that follows if demand holds still.

## Stage

[The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) tables [Improve the Efficiency](plan_improve_the_efficiency.md): cut the resource each unit costs, so [the efficiency](piece_the_efficiency.md) delivers a real per-unit gain.
[The Forecaster](persona_the_forecaster.md) tables [Book the Saving](plan_book_the_saving.md), holding demand fixed, so [the expected saving](piece_the_expected_saving.md) is projected clean, and neither of them prices the fact that the leaner unit is also a cheaper service.

## Tension

Both are right, and the rightness lays the trap.
The efficiency is a genuine saving per unit and the projection is sound arithmetic, so nothing here is a mistake. But the gain aimed at the unit is a price cut on the service, and the projection that isolates the gain does so by assuming away the one thing the gain will move, demand, so the saving is booked exact and false in the same breath.
