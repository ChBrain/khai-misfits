# Piece: The Jevons Paradox

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The phenomenon whole: efficiency spending the resource it was meant to save, held whole by nobody.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Across the whole run, from [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md) to [the cap](place_the_cap.md).
And wherever a resource is used more efficiently and its total use is expected to fall.

## Load Bearing

Without it the parts are a scatter: an efficiency, a price drop, a rebound, a new use, a meter that rose.
With it they are one law: efficiency lowers the price of a service, a cheaper service is more demanded, so a saving booked at the unit is spent, and can be over-spent, at the whole. The Jevons Paradox is load bearing because it is the whole read at once (Jevons; Alcott), and it names the only exit: not a better efficiency but a ceiling, a cap or price on the resource itself, so the gain is kept at the level it is always spent. It is efficiency's own catch: to make a thing go further is to make it cheaper, and to make it cheaper is to make more of it wanted.

## Apparent

Progress doing exactly what progress should.
It looks like a world getting more efficient everywhere and therefore, surely, using less: leaner engines, brighter bulbs, greener grids. Nothing on the surface is a failure, and that is the whole of it, because the resource is spent not by any waster but by a chain of genuine improvements and rational responses to the prices they cut.

## Yearbook

Held whole by nobody: assembled from [the efficiency](piece_the_efficiency.md), [the cheaper service](piece_the_cheaper_service.md), and [the rebound](piece_the_rebound.md), and answered only by [the capper](persona_the_capper.md).
It is the misfit named: thrift per unit, left unpriced at the whole, is an invitation to use more, so the saving is kept only by capping the resource the efficiency made cheap.
