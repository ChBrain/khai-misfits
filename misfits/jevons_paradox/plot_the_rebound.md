# Plot: The Rebound

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The market](place_the_market.md), where the efficiency has made the service cheaper, not just leaner.
A lower price meeting buyers free to do more and builders free to make more.

## Action

[Rebounding](process_rebounding.md): [The User](persona_the_user.md), holding [the consumer's seat](position_consumer.md), travels the now-cheaper mile more often, while [The Pioneer](persona_the_pioneer.md) at [the new entrant's seat](position_new_entrant.md) finds uses that were never worth the resource before.

## Stage

[The User](persona_the_user.md) tables [Use It More](plan_use_it_more.md): answer the lower price with more of the service, so [the cheaper service](piece_the_cheaper_service.md) pulls demand up and [the rebound](piece_the_rebound.md) begins to eat the saving.
[The Pioneer](persona_the_pioneer.md) tables [Find New Uses](plan_find_new_uses.md), and [the new use](piece_the_new_use.md) opens the extensive margin the forecast held fixed, adding demand that never existed to be counted.

## Tension

Every extra use is rational, and their sum spends the saving.
The User is only answering a cheaper price and the Pioneer is only creating real value, so no one is wasting anything, and both feel thrifty and productive. But the fixed demand of the drawing board comes unfixed here, in old uses and new at once, and the reduction that was promised is quietly consumed by the very cheapness the efficiency produced.
