# Piece: The Efficiency

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The gain: more service per unit of resource, made in good faith to save it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), in the leaner design that does the same for less.
The genuine improvement, real and measured, per unit of service.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no saving to spend, and no paradox, only ordinary use.
With it there is a real per-unit gain, and the whole misfit is that this gain, aimed at the unit, does not stay at the unit: it lowers the cost of the service and so invites more of it. The Efficiency is load bearing because it is the honest good that the paradox turns (Jevons), the improvement that is exactly what it claims per unit and exactly the opposite of what it claims in total, so the trap is set not by waste but by success.

## Apparent

An unambiguous win.
It looks like pure progress: the same service for half the resource, cleaner, cheaper, greener. Nothing about a genuine efficiency announces that it is also a price cut, which is why the win is trusted to be a saving.

## Yearbook

Made by [the engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) through [improving](process_improving.md), and turned by [the cheaper service](piece_the_cheaper_service.md).
It is the good the paradox needs: the Efficiency is real, and its reality is precisely what makes [the rebound](piece_the_rebound.md) rational, because a truly cheaper service truly is worth using more.
