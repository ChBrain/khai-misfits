# Plot: The Cap

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The cap](place_the_cap.md), where someone puts a ceiling or a price on the resource itself, not on the unit.
The lever moved from the efficiency of use to the amount there is to use.

## Action

[Capping](process_capping.md): [The Capper](persona_the_capper.md), holding [the regulator's seat](position_regulator.md), sets a quota or a price on the total resource, above the efficiency, and holds it.

## Stage

[The Capper](persona_the_capper.md) tables [Cap the Resource](plan_cap_the_resource.md): make the whole deliberately scarce, so a leaner unit becomes less resource used rather than more service bought, and [the jevons paradox](piece_the_jevons_paradox.md) is answered by pricing the whole so [the backfire](piece_the_backfire.md) has no room to form.
For once the efficiency saves the total, because the rebound meets a ceiling it cannot push through.

## Tension

The one move that works makes the cheap thing dear again.
Capping the resource keeps the saving, but only by spending the cheap abundance the efficiency produced, so it feels like taxing a success and lands as a burden on every buyer the lower price had pleased. It holds only while it is held ... or the cap is judged too costly and lifted, the resource goes cheap once more, and the rebound floods straight back in.
