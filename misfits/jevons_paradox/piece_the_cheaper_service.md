# Piece: The Cheaper Service

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The efficiency, seen from the buyer's side: a service that now costs less.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Between the unit and the market, where a leaner unit becomes a lower price.
In the pass-through from resource saved to money paid.

## Load Bearing

Without it the efficiency would be a saving and stop there, banked as less resource used.
With it the saving becomes a discount, and a discount is not a reduction in use but an invitation to more of it, because a cheaper thing is a more-wanted thing. The Cheaper Service is load bearing because it is the hinge of the whole paradox (Greening et al.): it is the step, invisible in the per-unit framing, where conserving the resource turns into repricing the service, so that everything downstream, the rebound, the new uses, the backfire, follows from a price that quietly fell.

## Apparent

Just a better deal.
It looks like the happy consumer face of efficiency: the mile is cheaper, the light is cheaper, good news all round. Nothing marks it as the exact mechanism that will undo the saving, because a lower price feels like the reward for thrift, not the cause of excess.

## Yearbook

Produced by [cheapening](process_cheapening.md), out of [the efficiency](piece_the_efficiency.md), and answered by [the rebound](piece_the_rebound.md).
It is the pivot: the Cheaper Service is where a saving becomes a signal, the point at which using less per unit is read by the whole market as: use more.
