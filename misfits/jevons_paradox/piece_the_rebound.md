# Piece: The Rebound

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The direct rebound: more of the service used because it is now cheaper.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the market](place_the_market.md), in every choice to do more of a thing now that it costs less.
The further mile, the warmer room, the longer-running leaner machine.

## Load Bearing

Without it a cheaper service would be used exactly as much as before, and the saving would hold.
With it demand answers price: the lower the service costs, the more of it is taken, so the per-unit gain is partly, wholly, or more than wholly returned to consumption. The Rebound is load bearing because it is the effect that spends the saving (Khazzoom; Sorrell), the plain elasticity that turns a leaner unit into more units, measured in study after study and never zero, so that thrift per unit reliably buys use in total.

## Apparent

Just people responding to prices.
It looks like ordinary, rational economics: a thing got cheaper, so it is used more, exactly as a market should behave. Each extra use is sensible and cheap, so the rebound wears the face of thrift even as it spends the saving.

## Yearbook

Driven by [the user](persona_the_user.md) through [rebounding](process_rebounding.md), out of [the cheaper service](piece_the_cheaper_service.md).
It is the saving's fate: the Rebound is where [the expected saving](piece_the_expected_saving.md) goes, use by rational use, and with [the new use](piece_the_new_use.md) beside it, where the total begins to climb.
