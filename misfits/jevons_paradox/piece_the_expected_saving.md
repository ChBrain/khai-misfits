# Piece: The Expected Saving

## Taxonomy

[Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The projected reduction, booked on the assumption that demand holds fixed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), in the spreadsheet that multiplies the per-unit gain across a fixed number of units.
The clean total the efficiency is promised to deliver.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no expectation for the rebound to betray, and no reckoning at the meter.
With it there is a promised number, exact and false, that the whole intervention is justified by and measured against. The Expected Saving is load bearing because it is the naive projection every efficiency case rests on (Greening et al.): it holds demand fixed to isolate the gain, which is the one thing that guarantees it will be wrong, because the gain itself is what moves demand. It is the saving that was always going to be spent.

## Apparent

Sound arithmetic.
It looks like exactly the right calculation: units times resource-per-unit, before and after, difference saved. The maths is correct, which is what makes the assumption inside it so easy to miss, since no one doubts a sum that adds up.

## Yearbook

Booked by [the forecaster](persona_the_forecaster.md), and never met at [the meter](place_the_meter.md).
It is the promise the paradox breaks: the Expected Saving is what [the rebound](piece_the_rebound.md) quietly spends and [the backfire](piece_the_backfire.md) finally exposes, a reduction real on the page and gone in the world.
