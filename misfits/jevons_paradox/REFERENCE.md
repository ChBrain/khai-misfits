# Jevons Paradox: Reference

The research warrant for [Jevons Paradox](play_jevons_paradox.md). The title is Jevons's own;
the play runs on the energy-economics literature on the rebound effect and the Khazzoom-Brookes
postulate, from the original coal question through the modern rebound surveys, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Jevons paradox as a **systemic system**: a gain in the efficiency with which a resource
is used lowers the effective price of the service it provides, so demand rebounds toward the lower
price and the total resource consumed can rise rather than fall, without a single wasteful act. The
play models the documented mechanics, the efficiency improvement, the effective price of the service
falling, the saving projected on demand held fixed, the direct rebound of more use at the lower
price, the new uses that become viable and open the extensive margin, the backfire where total
consumption climbs above its start, and the cap or price on the resource that alone secures the
saving, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of waste. The governing finding
of the source literature is the play's governing law: efficiency cuts the resource per unit of
service and thereby the price of the service, and a cheaper service is more demanded, so a saving
booked at the unit is spent, and sometimes over-spent, at the level of the whole. It is a cousin of
the house's Extra Road, but a different engine: not a routing equilibrium on a network, a
price-elastic rebound of demand.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  engineer as a greenwasher or the user as a glutton, because the source finding is precisely that
  neither is needed: the efficiency is a genuine per-unit saving, and the extra use is a rational
  response to a lower price, and the new uses are real value. The misfit is that a saving on the unit,
  unpriced at the whole, is an invitation to use more.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a better gadget saving the resource on its
  own. Only a cap or a price on the resource itself keeps the gain, and that means making the resource
  deliberately scarce or dear, spending the cheap abundance the efficiency produced, so the reckoning
  is a ceiling imposed, not a saving that arrived by cleverness alone.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the paradox, the postulate, and the
  rebound taxonomy are theirs, and the condition the paradox actually requires, an efficiency that
  lowers the price of a service whose demand is elastic, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Efficiency](piece_the_efficiency.md)**: carries the efficiency gain (Jevons), more service per unit
  of resource, made in good faith to save it.
- **[The Cheaper Service](piece_the_cheaper_service.md)**: carries the price mechanism (Greening et al.), the
  efficiency lowering the effective price of the service the resource buys.
- **[The Expected Saving](piece_the_expected_saving.md)**: carries the naive projection (Greening et al.), the
  reduction booked on the assumption that demand holds fixed.
- **[The Rebound](piece_the_rebound.md)**: carries the direct rebound effect (Khazzoom; Sorrell), more of the
  service used because it is now cheaper.
- **[The New Use](piece_the_new_use.md)**: carries the extensive margin (Sorrell), uses that were never viable
  at the old price and become so at the new.
- **[The Backfire](piece_the_backfire.md)**: carries the Khazzoom-Brookes backfire (Khazzoom; Brookes), total
  consumption rising above where it began though every unit is leaner.
- **[The Jevons Paradox](piece_the_jevons_paradox.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Jevons; Alcott), efficiency
  spending the resource it was meant to save, and its remedy in a cap or price on the resource, held whole by nobody.
