# Persona: The Steward

## Taxonomy

[Steward](position_steward.md). The one who would put the cue and the quality back in step, restoring the ground so the signal tracks again, or dulling the cue so it no longer fires over the sink, at the cost of correcting against a site that still looks right and animals that still choose it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Watchful. Willing to alter the ground. Suspicious of a site that draws well and returns poorly.
Is the manager, the conservationist, the one who reads not the cue but the fitness behind it, and looks for the places where an honest-looking signal now sits over poor quality.
No blame in it: the Steward does not fault the settler for choosing or the people for changing their ground, only sees that the cue and the quality have come apart and moves to put them back.

## Action

Restores the ground so the cue tracks quality again, or removes or dulls the cue so it no longer fires over the sink, or buys the lineage time to re-learn what to read.
Matches signal to fitness: makes the attractive site either genuinely good or no longer attractive, so the draw stops leading settlers below replacement.
Does what dampens the trap: it works on the correlation itself, on the link between what the animal can read and what the ground delivers, the one thing neither the settler nor the cue can repair from inside.

## Shadow

Not a free rescue, a costly and unthanked correction, and that is the whole difficulty: the fix must be made against a site that shows every mark of quality and animals that go on choosing it willingly.
Because the sink still looks right and the settlers still prefer it, the Steward must change ground or signal with no visible failure to point to, find every altered place where the cue outran the quality, and hold the fix as new human change lays new traps under the same old cues, so the realignment is bought continuously and the sink it drains leaves no sign of the loss it would have taken.
It puts cue and quality back in step to spare the sinking, and putting them back is read as meddling with ground that looked perfectly good.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "the ground has changed under the signal, and the signal has not; put them back in step."
Says it against a site that still draws well, and is heard, often, as altering good ground for no reason a reading of the cue could ever show.
