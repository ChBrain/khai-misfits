# Process: Decoupling

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The ground altered under the cue by human forces, the fast change that breaks the old link between signal and quality and leaves the signal firing over the loss.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

Human change to the ground, and a feature the cue keeps reading.
At [the ground](place_the_ground.md), the land altered for reasons that have nothing to do with the animal, faster than the lineage can re-learn.

## Direction

Toward the link broken and the cue left firing over it.
Because the change alters the fitness and leaves the feature, decoupling breaks the correlation the cue rode, so the quality falls while the signal stays, and the same cue that once meant a good place now fires as strong over a poor one, the promise gone and the promise still spoken.

## Lever

The speed of the change against the slowness of the lineage.
Because human alteration outruns selection and learning, decoupling runs on a mismatch of rates, so the trap is assembled from no malice, only from ground changed faster than the chooser can re-read it, the people altering their own land for their own ends and mostly never thinking of the animal at all.

## Echo

A ground gone bad, and a cue still firing over it.
The change has altered the fitness and left the feature, so what reads as land improved or built or put to use is, in truth, a signal outliving the quality it tracked, the link broken and the cue about to draw settlers to a place that no longer keeps the promise.
