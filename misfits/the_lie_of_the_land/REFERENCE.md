# The Lie of the Land: Reference

The research warrant for [The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md).
The dynamic is the ecological trap: a cue that historically indicated habitat quality is decoupled from quality by
rapid, human-induced environmental change, so a now-poor site still fires the good cue and is preferred, an
attractive sink drawing settlers to below-replacement fitness. Schlaepfer, Runge and Sherman's ecological and
evolutionary traps, Robertson and Hutto's framework of trap mechanisms, Battin's attractive sink, and Dwernychuk
and Boag's empirical case, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the ecological trap as a **broken cue-quality correlation**: an organism selects habitat by a cue that for
a very long time correlated with quality, so choosing by the cue was adaptive and earned, and then human change
alters the ground faster than selection or learning can track, decoupling the cue from the quality while the cue
goes on firing at full strength, so a now-poor site keeps every mark the chooser is built to read and is preferred,
in a severe case above the true habitat, drawing settlers into an attractive sink below the rate at which they
replace themselves while the good ground stands empty for want of a cue. The play models the documented mechanics,
the cue an organism selects habitat by, the true quality it once tracked and can no longer be read from it, the
decoupling of the two under human-induced rapid environmental change, the maladaptive preference for the poor site,
the apparent fit the cue still reports over lost fitness, and the attractive sink that fills by faithful choice, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a foolish animal or a lying land. The governing finding
of the sources is the play's governing law: a habitat choice fully adaptive given the cue becomes maladaptive when
human change breaks the cue's link to quality, so no individual errs and the population is drawn down by a signal
no one controls.

The pattern runs regardless of which organism is inside it, a gull-led duck, a mayfly over asphalt, a bird in a
mown field, a turtle toward a lit shore: it is a property of a decoupled cue-quality correlation, not of any
chooser's psychology, and any competent settler shaped to trust the cue walks into it the same way.

The effect is bounded, and the boundary is carried as carefully as the finding. A trap requires the cue to be both
still attractive and now decoupled from quality: if the organism can update its cue, learning or coming to assess
quality directly, or if the change is slow enough for selection or plasticity to track it and re-tune the reading,
the correlation is restored and no trap forms. The lie of the land holds only where cue-based settlement meets
change faster than the chooser can re-learn the cue; give the lineage a way to read the quality, or time enough to
re-earn the cue, and the ground stops lying.

It is distinct from any misfit about a word whose agreed meaning drifts among the people who use it: there the
drift is in a shared symbol negotiated between communicators, a meaning that lives only in the community that holds
it, and the loss is a divergence of sense between speakers. Here there is no communication at all and no symbol and
no community: only a natural cue that once tracked environmental quality and now does not, read by an organism that
no one is speaking to, so the lie is in the world's changed correlation and not in any negotiated meaning. And it is
distinct from any misfit about a measure that becomes a target, gamed once it is watched: there an agent controls
the metric and acts strategically to move it, and the number decays because it is optimised against. Here nobody
games the cue and nobody controls it, the chooser is not strategic but faithful, and the signal decays not because
it is pushed on but because the world changed underneath it, so the trap is a broken correlation under blameless
change and not a metric bent by the one it measures.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the settler as a
  foolish chooser, the cue as a deceiver, or the people who alter the ground as culprits, because the source finding
  is precisely that no one errs: the reading is adaptive and earned, the cue reports the same feature it always
  reported, and the ground is changed for reasons that have nothing to do with the animal. The trap is authored by
  no one and emerges from a correlation broken faster than a lineage can re-learn, so the misfit is that a cue
  trustworthy because it tracked quality for so long is dangerous exactly once the ground is changed beneath it, and
  a signal still firing is heard as a promise still kept.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that dampens the trap
  is to put cue and quality back in step, restoring the ground so the signal tracks again, or removing or dulling
  the cue so it no longer fires over the sink, or buying the lineage time to re-learn, and it means correcting
  against a site that still shows every mark of quality and settlers that still choose it willingly, finding every
  altered ground where an honest-looking cue now sits over poor quality, and holding the fix as new change lays new
  traps under the same cues. The reckoning is a correlation mended only by paying, continuously, the cost of
  altering ground that looks perfectly good, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the ecological and evolutionary trap and the decoupling of cue from
  quality under human-induced rapid environmental change are Schlaepfer, Runge and Sherman's, the framework of trap
  mechanisms and the preference for the poor site are Robertson and Hutto's, the attractive sink drawing animals
  below replacement is Battin's, and the gull-led ducks are Dwernychuk and Boag's; the honest boundary, that an
  updatable cue or slow enough change forms no trap, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.
