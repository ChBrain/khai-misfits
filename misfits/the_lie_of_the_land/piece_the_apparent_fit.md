# Piece: The Apparent Fit

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The goodness the cue still reports, the site's quality as the signal reads it, the deceptive metric that stays high after the thing it measured has gone.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read at [the sign](place_the_sign.md), where the settler takes it for the quality.
Shown over [the ground](place_the_ground.md), where it stays high above a fitness that has fallen.

## Load Bearing

Without it the fallen quality would show, and no settler would choose the sink over the source.
With it the trap has its face: the apparent quality of a site as its cue reports it, kept high by a signal that still fires while the true quality beneath it has dropped, the metric intact and the thing it measured gone (Robertson and Hutto, on the attractive cue that persists after the quality it once indicated has declined). The Apparent Fit is load bearing because it is the reading the settler is left with: not the fitness of the ground but the signal's report of it, so the piece carries the deceptive measure that says good over a place gone bad, the one number the chooser can read and the one that no longer tells the truth.

## Apparent

The quality of the site, plain and legible, exactly as high as the cue is strong.
Seen at the surface, the Apparent Fit looks like the fitness itself, a good measure of a good place, and the truth that it is only the cue's report and not the ground's return, that a measure can stay high while its object collapses, that reading the metric is not reading the quality, stays hidden behind how naturally a strong signal reads as a good site, which is the whole of the lie.
