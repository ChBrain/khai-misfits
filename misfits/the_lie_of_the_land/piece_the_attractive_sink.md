# Piece: The Attractive Sink

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The phenomenon whole, the attractive low-quality site drawing settlers to below-replacement fitness, a place that gathers by its cue what it cannot return by its quality.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Filled at [the ground](place_the_ground.md), where the strong cue draws more settlers than the poor quality can keep.
Drained, if ever, by [restoring](process_restoring.md), where the steward matches the signal to the fitness.

## Load Bearing

Without it the decoupled cue and the drawn preference are just a bad choice, and no population is pulled down by them.
With it the trap is complete and authorless: a site so attractive by its cue and so poor by its quality that it draws settlers faster than they can replace themselves, an attractive sink into which good animals are pulled by loving a bad habitat (Battin, on when good animals love bad habitats, the attractive sink that a population is drawn into below replacement by the very strength of its draw). The Attractive Sink is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole: not one false reading but a place that fills by faithful choice and empties by poor return, the cue drawing what the quality cannot hold, so the piece carries the trap entire, the settlers gathered below the rate at which they replace themselves and the true source standing empty beside the sink that took them, the classic field case a gull colony leading ducks to nest where they fare worst (Dwernychuk and Boag).

## Apparent

A thriving site, a place full of settlers, the busiest and most chosen ground around.
Seen at the surface, the Attractive Sink looks like the best habitat there is, crowded exactly because it draws so well, and the truth that its crowd is its casualty, that a full site can be a failing one, that the draw which fills it is the draw that sinks it, stays hidden behind how a place everything chooses looks like a place worth choosing, right up until the young are counted.
