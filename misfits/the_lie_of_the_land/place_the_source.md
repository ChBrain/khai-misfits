# Place: The Source

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The true good habitat, quieter in cue though richer in fact, the site that would return more than it takes and stands empty for want of a signal to fire.

## Owner

## Shown

The place the quality is real and the cue is weak, the unmown meadow off the settler's path, the dark quiet shore, the plain wet marsh that does not glare, the ground that keeps the promise but does not shout it.
The Source holding its fitness while the sink holds the signal, the good habitat passed over because nothing about it fires the cue as strongly as the altered ground does.

## Holds

The true quality, and the cue too quiet to draw.
Here the ground still keeps the promise the cue once made, so the Source holds the point where fitness and signal have come apart the other way: a good place with a weak cue, high quality and low apparent fit, so it is where the settlers do not go, and where the ground that would return more than it takes stands open beside a sink that draws them all.

## Offers

A place that would keep its settlers, and no signal to bring them.
Because the Source has the quality and not the strong cue, it offers the return the sink cannot, the young that would fledge and the nests that would hold, so it hands out the fitness a good site holds, and hands it out to no one, the settlers drawn past it to the louder ground, the good place empty for want of a cue the chooser can read.

## Withheld

Any draw to match its worth.
The Source shows quality but cannot fire a signal to bring the settlers, because the cue reads a feature and the Source is rich in fitness and quiet in feature, so it withholds the pull that fills the sink, letting a good place stand empty while a poor one fills, and, all true quality and no strong cue, it withholds its return from the settlers who chose the ground that shouted louder.
