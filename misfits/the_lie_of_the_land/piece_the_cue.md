# Piece: The Cue

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The signal the settler chooses by, the feature that once tracked quality and still fires over the ground, the reading the lineage earned and cannot now un-earn.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read at [the sign](place_the_sign.md), where the settler meets it and prefers where it fires.
Fired over [the ground](place_the_ground.md), where it still reports the feature after the quality has changed.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no reading and no choice, and no way for a good site or a bad one to be preferred.
With it the trap has its instrument: a signal the settler selects habitat by, a feature that historically correlated with quality so that choosing by it was adaptive (Schlaepfer, Runge and Sherman, on habitat selection by cues that once reliably indicated the quality of a site). The Cue is load bearing because it is the whole of what the chooser can read: not the fitness of the ground but a feature standing in for it, so the piece carries the signal whose long reliability is exactly what makes it trusted, and trusted still after the ground it tracked has been changed beneath it.

## Apparent

A reliable sign of a good place, the mark the lineage learned because it worked.
Seen at the surface, the Cue looks like the quality itself, the feature and the fitness so long joined that reading the one is knowing the other, and the truth that the Cue reports a feature and never a fitness, that a signal can outlive the correlation it rode, that being strong is not being true, stays hidden behind how faithfully the cue tracked quality for as long as anyone can remember.

## Yearbook

Read by [the settler](persona_the_settler.md) through [cueing](process_cueing.md), fired by [the cue](persona_the_cue.md) over changed ground.
