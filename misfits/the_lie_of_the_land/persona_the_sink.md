# Persona: The Sink

## Taxonomy

[Sink](position_sink.md). The altered low-quality habitat that still fires the good cue and so draws settlers to it, the attractive site that returns fewer than it takes and looks, to every reading, like the best place to be.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Inviting. Convincing. Every mark of quality on show.
Is the ground the Settler is drawn to, the mown hayfield still green and tall in spring, the lit shore glaring like open water, the bright drained lawn, the verge where the grass grows high beside the road.
No malice in it: the Sink does not lure, it simply keeps the feature the cue reads while the quality behind that feature has fallen away, so it presents good and delivers poor without any intent to deceive.

## Action

Shows the good cue and takes in the settlers the cue sends, holding every mark the chooser is built to read while giving back less than the settlers put in.
Fills with nests that will be mown, eggs that will bake, young that will starve, drawing more animals than it can support and drawing them by the very strength of its signal.
Does what an attractive sink does: it presents high apparent quality and delivers low true quality, so it gathers the settlers a good site would gather and returns them below the rate at which they replace themselves.

## Shadow

Not a trap laid, a poor place still wearing the mark of a good one, and that is the whole difficulty: the Sink is attractive precisely because the feature the cue reads survived the change that ruined the quality.
Because the Sink keeps the cue while losing the quality, it draws settlers as strongly as the true source and returns them worse than an empty field would, so in a hard case it is preferred over the good ground and fills while the source stands open, a site whose whole danger is that it looks, to the only reading available, exactly like the best choice.
It keeps the good cue in good faith, and the kept cue is exactly what turns a poor site into an attractive one.

## Tell

Looks like the best ground and keeps like the worst, all the marks of quality and none of the return.
Draws the settlers a good site would draw and gives back less than empty ground, and cannot show, to any cue the chooser reads, that its quality has gone.
