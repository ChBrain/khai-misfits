# Piece: The Preference

## Taxonomy

[The Lie of the Land](play_the_lie_of_the_land.md). The maladaptive attraction to the poor site, the settler drawn to the sink and, in a hard case, choosing it over the good, the reading that once filled the best place now filling the worst.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Formed at [the sign](place_the_sign.md), where the strong cue is read and preferred.
Acted at [the ground](place_the_ground.md), where the preference settles the sink and passes the source.

## Load Bearing

Without it the decoupled cue is only a false sign, and nothing chooses on it, so nothing is lost.
With it the trap has its motion: the settler preferring the site where the cue fires strongest even after the quality has gone, an equal-preference trap where good and poor are chosen alike and a severe trap where the poor is preferred over the good (Robertson and Hutto, on the mechanisms of the trap and the settler's preference for the low-quality site, up to and including preference above the high). The Preference is load bearing because it is what turns a broken cue into a filled sink: not a mistake but an adaptive attraction pointed by the signal, so the piece carries the pull that draws the settler to the worst place as surely as it once drew it to the best, the choice faithful and the destination inverted.

## Apparent

A good choice, an animal going where the signs are strongest.
Seen at the surface, the Preference looks like sound habitat selection, a settler drawn to the best cue exactly as it should be, and the truth that the pull now points at a poor site, that the attraction can run stronger to the sink than to the source, that a faithful preference can prefer the ground that cannot keep it, stays hidden behind how right it looks to go where the cue is strong.
