# Piece: The Rising Salinity

## Taxonomy

[The Dead Sea Effect](play_the_dead_sea_effect.md). The reinforcing loop: each good departure worsening the team and driving out the next of the able.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the basin](place_the_basin.md), the concentration climbing with every exit.
Where a saltier sea drives off more of what fresh water is left.

## Load Bearing

Without it the effect would settle after one round, a team lightened once and then stable.
With it the sorting feeds itself: each departure of the able raises the average helplessness, which sours the place further, which gives the next tier of the able its own reason to go, so the salinity climbs round after round and the decline compounds rather than settling. The Rising Salinity is load bearing because it is the self-reinforcing spiral the effect turns on (Webster): the loss of talent is not a one-time subtraction but a loop, each good leaving making the next likelier, so a team can drain from the top down until almost nothing able is left.

## Apparent

A run of bad luck in hiring and holding.
Each further departure looks like an unrelated misfortune, a string of people who happened to move on, so the rising salinity reads as coincidence, when it is one loss making the next, the compounding of a spiral mistaken for a sequence of accidents.

## Yearbook

Climbing through [settling](process_settling.md), grown from [the residue](piece_the_residue.md) concentrating.
It is the loop that hollows: the Rising Salinity turns a single evaporation into a spiral, and drives the team toward [the repulsion](piece_the_repulsion.md) that guards the salt.
