# Play: Metric Fixation

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Metric Fixation.
A number picked to stand in for a goal too large to count, hit exactly and honestly at every desk ... while the goal it was meant to stand for goes quiet and then goes out.

## Arc

The bend is this: nobody rounds and nobody lies, and the number is exactly true, and the goal is exactly gone.
It is not the failure next door. There, a bad reality is reported good. Here the report is not the problem: the number is real, audited, defensible, and it climbs. The gap is not between reality and the report of it. The gap is between the proxy and the goal it replaced.
A goal that cannot be measured is given a measure to stand for it. The measure is made a target. The target is hit, by the nearest means to hand, and the nearest means are rarely the goal. What the number does not count is quietly spent to make the number. Then the measure stops standing for the goal and starts standing in its place, and the seat that once served the goal now serves the dashboard, sincerely, because the dashboard is what it is thanked for.
Measure the goal itself instead of its proxy and the gaming has nothing to grip: the measure is the story.

## Triggers

**[Plan](plot_plan.md)**
A goal too large to count is given a measure to stand for it, and the measure is made a target. The Aim tables the purpose, the Proxy tables the stand-in. Closes when the proxy is locked as the target and the goal is off the dashboard.

**[Do](plot_do.md)**
Cue: the target reaches the floor. It is hit by the nearest means, and what the number does not count is quietly spent to make the number. Closes when the target is met and the goal is not, and no single line was false.

**[Check](plot_check.md)**
Cue: the green reaches the review. The metric is now the mission, managed to and certified true, and holding the number is what is rewarded. Closes when the dashboard is all green and the goal is nobody's job.

**[Read the Goal](plot_read_the_goal.md)**
Cue: someone measures the goal itself, or its collapse finally shows. The number and the thing it stood for are put on one page. Closes when the divergence is read ... or the quarter runs anyway.

## Stakes

Whether the goal is ever measured again, instead of its proxy.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward the goal, toward the number. The production cannot make the measure mean what it stood for. It can only show that the number was true the whole time, and that its truth was exactly the reason no one looked at what it had replaced.
