# Piece: The Share

## Taxonomy

[Not Worth My While](play_not_worth_my_while.md). The individual's slice of the benefit, a fraction of a percent off a risk already low, the negligible personal payoff that a real cost outweighs.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Weighed at [the consulting room](place_the_consulting_room.md), where it is set against the dose and found too small.
Invisible in [the distribution](place_the_distribution.md), where it is spread so thin across the many that no individual's slice can be felt.

## Load Bearing

Without it the personal benefit is large and the measure pays, and there is no paradox.
With it the trap has its arithmetic: the benefit that reaches any one person is negligible, a fraction of a percent off a risk already low, because the sum is spread across the whole population so thinly that each slice is nothing (Rose, on the population versus individual distinction, a large aggregate benefit offering little to each participant). The Share is load bearing because it is the tiny side of the scale, the true and negligible personal payoff, so the piece carries what makes the sound refusal sound, a benefit really too small to repay the dose.

## Apparent

A reason to comply, a real benefit anyone should want.
Seen at the surface, the Share looks like enough to act on, so declining looks like carelessness about one's own good, and the truth that the personal benefit really is negligible, that a fraction of a percent cannot repay a real cost, that the slice is small because the sum was never in any one part, stays hidden behind how a benefit named in a population sounds when offered to a person.
