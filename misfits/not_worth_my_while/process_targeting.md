# Process: Targeting

## Taxonomy

[Not Worth My While](play_not_worth_my_while.md). The Screener aiming the measure at the few at highest risk, the efficient and individually sound strategy that reaches the tail and misses the bulk of the cases.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A measure not worth the many's while, and a few for whom it is worth it.
At [the distribution](place_the_distribution.md), the screener reading the tail of the curve and aiming the dose where the personal benefit is large enough to repay the cost.

## Direction

Toward the few targeted and the bulk missed.
Because the personal benefit is large for the few at highest risk and negligible for the many at modest risk, targeting concentrates the measure on the tail where each unit does the most personal good, so it helps its few genuinely and completely, and because most of the cases arise from the modest middle and not the dangerous tail, it misses the bulk of the harm even as it aims perfectly at the few.

## Lever

The efficiency of aiming where the benefit is large.
Because concentrating the measure on the high-risk few makes each dose individually worth it, targeting runs not on any misjudgement but on the sound logic of spending where the personal payoff is largest, so the missed sum is assembled from no error, only from an efficient aim at the few while most of the cases sit in the many it did not target.

## Echo

A few helped completely, and the bulk of the cases untouched.
The screener has aimed efficiently, so what reads as the sensible strategy is, in truth, a precise hit on the small tail that leaves the vast middle unmoved, useful and insufficient at once, the sum still ungathered because it lived in the many at modest risk.
