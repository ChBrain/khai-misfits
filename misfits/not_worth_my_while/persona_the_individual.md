# Persona: The Individual

## Taxonomy

[Individual](position_individual.md). The one who is offered the measure, weighs the personal benefit against the personal cost, finds the benefit negligible, and rationally declines. The archetype of the many at low or moderate risk who supply most of the cases and gain almost nothing each.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Sensible. Thrifty. Right on the numbers they can see.
Is any one of the many the measure is offered to, the patient, the participant, the ordinary person at low or moderate risk, of whom there are so many.
No selfishness in it: the Individual weighs the offer fairly and finds the personal benefit genuinely too small to repay the cost, so the refusal is arithmetic and not greed.

## Action

Hears the measure offered, counts the personal expected benefit, finds it a fraction of a percent off a risk already low, sets it against a real and certain cost, and declines or half-complies.
Refuses a dose whose personal payoff cannot be felt, and is correct to, because the sum was never in their share.
Does what a rational person must: it weighs the offer on the numbers that reach it, and a negligible benefit against a real cost does not pay.

## Shadow

Not a free-rider, a sound calculator of a share that really is negligible, and that is the whole difficulty: the refusal is rational, and the personal benefit really is too small to repay the dose.
Because the sum lives only in the adding up and no individual holds it, the Individual can see only their own tiny slice, so weighs a real cost against a negligible benefit and declines correctly, one of the many whose sound refusals each subtract the slice that was their part of a sum only the population could keep, right to the last that there was nothing in it for them.
It reasons impeccably about its own share, and the soundness of that reasoning is exactly what loses the sum that lived only across all the shares at once.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "the benefit to me is too small to bother," and is telling the truth.
Says it having weighed a real cost against a fraction of a percent, and cannot see the sum, because the sum is not in any share to see, so declines a negligible benefit soundly and forfeits a slice of a total no one holds.
