# Position: Individual

## Taxonomy

[Not Worth My While](play_not_worth_my_while.md). The seat that is offered the measure and weighs it, where a real personal cost meets a negligible personal benefit and the sound move is to decline.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A measure offered and a scale to weigh it on: the dose with its real cost, and the personal benefit it promises.
A sound calculus: the weighing is fair, the cost is genuine, and the personal expected benefit is truly a fraction of a percent off a risk already low.
No hold on the sum, because the sum lives only across the whole population and no share of it is large enough to feel, so the seat can see only its own negligible slice.

## Orders

Weigh your share.
Take the measure offered, count what it gives you against what it costs you, and act on the balance, declining when a real cost outweighs a negligible benefit.
Refuse the bad bargain, because on the numbers that reach you it is a bad bargain.

## Loses

The sum, exactly because the seat reasons soundly about its own share.
Because the aggregate benefit is real only across the many and no individual holds a felt piece of it, the seat weighing its negligible slice against a real cost declines correctly, one of the many whose sound refusals each subtract the slice that was their part of a total only the population could keep, and loses the sum to its own correct arithmetic.
It gains a sound refusal of a bad personal bargain, and loses a slice of a benefit that lived only in all the slices summed.

## Drives

Toward the share weighed and rationally declined,
the negligible benefit set against the real cost and correctly refused,
so the seat that weighs its share keeps its dose and forfeits its slice, right to the last that there was nothing in it for it.
