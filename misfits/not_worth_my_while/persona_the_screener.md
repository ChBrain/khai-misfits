# Persona: The Screener

## Taxonomy

[Screener](position_screener.md). The one who runs the high-risk strategy, targeting the few for whom the measure is individually worth it, efficient and sound, and missing the many at modest risk who supply most of the cases. The archetype of the targeted approach that is worth each chosen person's while and cannot reach the sum.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Efficient. Targeted. Reasonable about where the benefit is worth it.
Is the one who finds and treats the high-risk few, the screening programme, the risk score, the clinic that identifies those most likely to gain.
No error in it: the Screener aims the measure where the personal benefit is largest, and for those few it genuinely pays, so the targeting is efficient and individually correct.

## Action

Identifies the few at highest risk, offers the measure where the personal benefit is large enough to repay the cost, and helps them genuinely.
Targets the dose at the people for whom it is worth their while, and spares the many the bad bargain, and misses most of the cases anyway.
Does what a targeted strategy must: it concentrates the measure where each unit does the most personal good, and the most personal good is not where most of the harm is.

## Shadow

Not a poor aim, a precise aim at the wrong bulk, and that is the whole difficulty: the targeting is efficient and the few are helped, and most of the cases arise not from the few at highest risk but from the many at modest risk.
Because there are so many at modest risk, a small risk times a large number is most of the harm, so the Screener, helping its high-risk few completely, still leaves the bulk of the cases untouched, useful and insufficient at once, the strategy that is worth each chosen person's while unable to reach a sum that lives in the many it did not target.
It aims the measure where it is individually worth it, and where it is individually worth it is exactly not where most of the cases are.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "target the ones who really need it," and helps them, and misses the many.
Says it aiming at the few at highest risk, where the personal benefit is large, and leaves the many at modest risk untargeted, so the efficient strategy that pays for each chosen person cannot gather the cases that arise from all the others.
