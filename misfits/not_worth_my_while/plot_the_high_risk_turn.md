# Plot: The High-Risk Turn

## Taxonomy

[Not Worth My While](play_not_worth_my_while.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Distribution](place_the_distribution.md), then offer it only to the few for whom it is worth it.
The Screener reading the tail of the curve, where the personal benefit is large enough to pay.

## Action

[Targeting](process_targeting.md): [The Screener](persona_the_screener.md), holding [the screener's seat](position_screener.md), aims the measure at the high-risk few and helps them genuinely.

## Stage

The [screener](persona_the_screener.md), on [Screen the High-Risk](plan_screen_the_high_risk.md), aims [the dose](piece_the_dose.md) at the few in the tail where it is worth their while, and spares the many the bad bargain, and misses [the bulk](piece_the_bulk.md) in the middle where most of the cases arise.
Targets efficiently and helps its few completely, and leaves the many at modest risk untouched, because most of the harm is a small risk borne by very many.

## Tension

Aiming the measure where it is individually worth it is efficient and sound, and the efficient aim misses most of the cases.
There is nothing to fault: for the high-risk few the measure genuinely pays, and targeting them is correct. But most of the cases arise from the many at modest risk, so the plot closes with the targeted strategy having helped its few genuinely and missed the many who supply most of the cases, useful and insufficient at once, the bulk of the harm untouched.
