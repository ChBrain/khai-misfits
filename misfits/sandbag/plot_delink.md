# Plot: Delink

## Taxonomy

[Sandbag](play_sandbag.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The benchmark](place_the_benchmark.md), and someone who has stopped setting the target from your own last result.
The question has changed: not how much more than last year, but how does this compare to the field, and what would an honest forecast be worth.

## Action

[Benchmarking](process_benchmarking.md): [The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), holding [the delinker's seat](position_delinker.md), cuts the tie between a seat's history and its target, setting the number from the outside and rewarding an accurate call over a beaten one.

## Stage

[The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md) tables [Cut the Tie](plan_cut_the_tie.md): unhook the baseline from the person it measures, benchmark it to the peer group and the market, and pay for a forecast that proves true rather than one that is beaten, so [the ratchet effect](piece_the_ratchet_effect.md) loses its grip and holding capacity back stops paying.
With the bar no longer set from the last hit, [the true ceiling](piece_the_true_ceiling.md) is finally safe to show, because showing it no longer raises next year's number.

## Tension

The sandbags come out, and the comfortable deal goes with them.
Cutting the tie works, and it costs: an external benchmark is harder to set and harder to argue than last year plus a bit, an honest forecast has to be defined and defended, and the implicit bargain everyone relied on, deliver the number and keep your slack, is broken for a system that asks people to reveal what they can really do. It trades a simple, gameable rule that everyone understood for a fairer one that no one can quietly work ... or the old way is too easy and too familiar to give up, the benchmark is judged too much trouble, and the sandbags stay where they are.
