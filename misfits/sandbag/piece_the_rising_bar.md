# Piece: The Rising Bar

## Taxonomy

[Sandbag](play_sandbag.md). The target set from your own last result, so it only ever climbs.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the review](place_the_review.md), in the line that reads last year's actual and adds a stretch.
The rule that ties tomorrow's goal to today's outcome: whatever you delivered becomes the floor, plus a little, and that becomes next year's number. The baseline is you, last time.

## Load Bearing

Without it a target is a fixed measure and beating it is pure gain.
With it a target is a moving debt, because the Rising Bar sets the goal from the goal-holder's own history: every result you post is read back as your new minimum, so the bar rises exactly as fast as you climb, and there is no altitude at which it stops chasing you. It is the whole engine of the misfit, the reason effort is self-defeating: the harder you clear the bar, the higher the bar you have set yourself to clear.

## Apparent

Sensible, ambitious goal-setting.
It looks like exactly how targets should work: build on what you achieved, always push a little further, never settle. And in a world without memory of who delivered it would be fine, which is the trap, because the same rule that drives improvement also punishes it, and the two are invisible to tell apart at the moment the number is set.

## Yearbook

Applied by [the setter](persona_the_setter.md) through [target-setting](process_target_setting.md), learned the hard way by [the beater](persona_the_beater.md).
It is the root the whole play grows from: the Rising Bar is what turns [the reward for beating](piece_the_reward_for_beating.md) into a punishment and makes [the hidden capacity](piece_the_hidden_capacity.md) the only safe place to keep anything spare.
