# Sandbag: Reference

The research warrant for [Sandbag](play_sandbag.md). The title names the behaviour;
the play runs on the economics of the ratchet effect and the older field studies of output
restriction and budget gaming, from Taylor's soldiering through Weitzman's ratchet to the
corporate budgeting critique, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the ratchet effect as a **systemic system**: when tomorrow's target is set from today's
result, agents suppress today's performance to keep tomorrow's target soft, so an incentive meant
to drive output quietly teaches everyone to cap it, without a single idler or cheat. The play models
the documented mechanics, the target set from the seat's own past, the prize for beating it that is
a harder target, capacity parked off the record, the commit lowballed at planning time, the good
period banked to smooth the line, the peer norm that cools the rate-buster, and the delinking of
target from history that alone corrects it, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a
tale of laziness. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: if the
standard you are held to next period rises with what you deliver this period, the rational response
is to hold back, so a firm that rewards good performance with a stiffer goal is training its people
to hide how good they are.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as an idler or a cheat, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the
  setter is doing textbook goal-setting, the holder hits the number in full, the lowballer's caution
  is unprovable, and the shop floor is only protecting a pace that keeps all its bars low. The misfit
  is that rewarding good performance with a harder target makes hiding excellence the rational move.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the target simply being set higher.
  Cutting the tie can end the game, but only by giving up the simple, familiar rule of last year plus
  a bit for a harder-to-set external benchmark and a forecast that must be defined and defended, so
  the reckoning is a comfortable, gameable deal traded for a fairer, costlier one, not a system that
  reformed itself.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, from the shop
  floor to the plan to the boardroom budget.
