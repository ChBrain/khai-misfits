# Piece: The Soldiering

## Taxonomy

[Sandbag](play_sandbag.md). The peer norm that holds everyone to the safe pace.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the shop floor](place_the_shop_floor.md), in the cool that meets whoever works too fast in the open.
The unwritten rule among equals: keep to the pace the group has settled on, do not make the rest look slow, and do not hand the setter a reason to lift the bar for all. The quiet policing of the one who over-delivers.

## Load Bearing

Without it each person sandbags alone and a keen newcomer can still spoil it.
With it the whole room sandbags together, because the Soldiering makes holding back a shared norm enforced by peers: the rate-buster who sprints does not just raise their own bar, they raise everyone's, so the group has a common interest in cooling them off, and it does, with a word, a look, a withdrawal of warmth. It is the social half of the ratchet, older than any spreadsheet, and it turns private prudence into collective discipline that a single eager worker cannot break.

## Apparent

Team spirit, and not showing off.
It looks like ordinary group feeling: do not be the flashy one, do not throw your colleagues under the bus by sprinting, pull with the team. The framing is sincere and even decent, which is why the rate-buster half-accepts it and slows down, and why the pressure never has to be made explicit to be understood.

## Yearbook

Enforced on the floor through [soldiering](process_soldiering.md), suffered by [the hare](persona_the_hare.md) who ran too hard too openly.
It is the play's collective lock: where [the hidden capacity](piece_the_hidden_capacity.md) and [the reserve](piece_the_reserve.md) hide the surplus one seat at a time, the Soldiering makes the hiding a rule the whole room keeps, and keeps on each other.
