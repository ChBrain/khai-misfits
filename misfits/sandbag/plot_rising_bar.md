# Plot: Rising Bar

## Taxonomy

[Sandbag](play_sandbag.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The review](place_the_review.md), last year's actual on the page, and a pencil about to add a stretch to it.
The number to beat next year is about to be set from the number just delivered, and the person who delivered it is in the room.

## Action

[Target-Setting](process_target_setting.md): [The Setter](persona_the_setter.md), holding [the target-setter's seat](position_target_setter.md), reads this year's result and writes next year's target as that plus a little more.
[The Beater](persona_the_beater.md) at [the striver's seat](position_striver.md) has just posted a record, and watches it become his new floor.

## Stage

[The Beater](persona_the_beater.md) tables [Beat the Number](plan_beat_the_number.md): give it everything and post a record, because that is what a keen performer does the first time.
[The Setter](persona_the_setter.md) tables [Stretch the Target](plan_stretch_the_target.md): build next year on this year's high, so [the rising bar](piece_the_rising_bar.md) climbs to meet the record and [the reward for beating](piece_the_reward_for_beating.md) is handed over as praise.

## Tension

Nothing here is unfair, and it teaches the worst possible lesson.
The setter is doing textbook goal-setting, building on success and asking for a little more, and the beater is being rewarded for a genuine record, thanked and congratulated in front of the room. But the thanks come stapled to a higher number that the beater alone must now defend, so the lived experience of over-delivering is that it bought a harder year, and a rational person learns from that exactly once. The record is the last one he will ever post in the open.
