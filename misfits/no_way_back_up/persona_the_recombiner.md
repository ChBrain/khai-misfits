# Persona: The Recombiner

## Taxonomy

[The Cross](position_the_cross.md). Reproduces by mixing, taking the good stretches of two mutated genomes and combining them into one cleaner than either, so it can reconstitute the least-loaded class the ratchet destroyed, and pays for it in the full cost of sex.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Mixing rather than copying. Paying to pair. Rebuilding a clean genome from parts.
Is the one that does not merely transmit a genome but recombines two, so an offspring can inherit the fewest-mutations stretches of each parent and end lighter than both, the one reproduction that can lower a load rather than only carry it.
No heroism wished on them: the mixing is not a sacrifice made on purpose but a way of reproducing that happens to reassemble clean genomes, its benefit real and its cost paid whether or not anyone means it as a remedy.

## Action

Combines two genomes into one, letting the good parts of each meet in a single offspring, so a class cleaner than the current best can be built from the variation already present.
Halts and reverses the climb where it operates, since a genome assembled from the least-mutated stretches of two parents can restore a least-loaded class the draw had dropped, the one move that lowers the floor rather than raising it.
Does what recombination does, and pays what it costs: produces offspring that carry only half of each parent, makes males who bear no young, and breaks up good combinations as freely as bad, buying the escape from the ratchet at a steep and immediate price.

## Shadow

The hardest part is being the fool in every short run: right beside a copier that fills the world twice as fast, the recombiner spends half of every offspring on a partner and makes sons who bear nothing, so in any single season it is plainly out-reproduced, and the thing it is buying, a ratchet that does not turn, is a benefit it will never point to and never personally collect.
What wears on it is paying now for a nothing later. The copier's advantage is visible and immediate and the recombiner's is a decline that does not happen, spread across a lineage and a scale of time no individual lives to see, so it carries a real and present cost for a statistical gift to its descendants, and there is a loneliness in doing the expensive, dilutive, partner-needing thing while the cheaper neighbour surges ahead and looks, for as long as any season lasts, entirely vindicated.
The bind is interior and long: it must pay the whole cost of sex in the present to spare a lineage a decline in a future it cannot witness, feels itself out-competed in every generation it can see, and holds the one remedy that works knowing the remedy is dear and its payoff invisible, the mixing that alone lowers the floor being the mixing that always looks, in the moment, like the losing move.

## Tell

Says "it costs double, and it is the only thing that works."
Is the whole remedy in one sentence, since the two-fold cost is real and the escape from the ratchet is real and both are true at once, so the words that sound like a hard bargain are exactly that, the recombiner naming a cure it cannot make cheap, paying in the visible present for a floor held down in an invisible future.
