# No Way Back Up: Reference

The research warrant for [No Way Back Up](play_no_way_back_up.md).
The dynamic is Muller's ratchet: his finding that an asexual population accumulates mutations irreversibly, Haigh's
quantitative model of the least-loaded class, Felsenstein's account of recombination as the remedy, Maynard Smith's
two-fold cost of sex, and Lynch, Bürger, Butcher and Gabriel's mutational meltdown, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging Muller's ratchet as an **irreversible-loss-of-the-least-loaded-class structure**: in a finite population that
reproduces without recombination, the class of individuals carrying the fewest deleterious mutations is by the shape of
the distribution the rarest, so in a finite generational draw it is eventually lost by chance, and because reversing a
mutation is vanishingly rare and copying combines no genomes, the lost class cannot be reconstituted, so the minimum
mutational load steps up and can only ever step up. The play models the documented mechanics, the least-loaded class that
is also the smallest, the blind finite draw that drops it, the shut exits of back-mutation and reassembly that make the
loss permanent, the floor that climbs one click at a time, and the recombination that alone can lower it at the cost of
sex, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of selection for decay or an agent of decline. The
governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: in an asexual population of finite size the least-loaded
class is repeatedly lost by drift and cannot be regained, so the mutational load accumulates irreversibly, a ratchet that
turns one way and, in a small population, can accelerate to extinction. It is a tendency under stated conditions and not
an iron law, holding where the population is finite, recombination absent, and the mutations deleterious, and halting
where recombination operates or the population is large enough to hold its least-loaded class, so the play stages the
one-way climb those conditions produce, not a fate of every lineage.

It is distinct from the house's Model Collapse, where a generative model retrained on its own synthetic output erodes the
tails of a distribution and converges toward a narrow, degenerate one: there the engine is information loss through
recursive resampling of an imperfect approximation, a continuous distribution thinning at its extremes as each round
learns from the last round's outputs. Here the loss is of a discrete class by chance in a finite population, not a tail
eroded by resampling, and the irreversibility is the specific structural fact that an asexual genome cannot be
reassembled cleaner than its best without recombination, so the remedy is recombination, the mixing of two lineages, and
not the reinjection of real data. Both degrade the best or rarest over generations, but Model Collapse thins a
distribution by learning from its own outputs while Muller's ratchet drops a fittest class by drift and cannot rebuild
it. It is distinct too from the one-way degradation Nobody Reads It stages, which is a market discipline that lifts for
want of readers rather than a chance loss of a fittest class, and from The Survivors, whose survivorship bias misreads a
filtered sample, in that here the least-loaded class is genuinely gone from the population and the load genuinely climbs,
no misreading involved.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any agent of the decline,
  because the source finding is precisely that none is needed: no individual is selected against for being clean, the
  least-loaded class is lost to the blind luck of a finite draw and not to any disadvantage (Haigh), and the copying that
  carries the rest forward is faithful and efficient (Muller). So the misfit is that a finite population reproducing
  without recombination loses its best class by chance and cannot rebuild it, and the minimum load climbs irreversibly,
  no selection for decay and no agent of it required, the reproducing and the losing each only what they are and the
  rising floor their sum, the copier that fills the colony fastest as blameless as the draw that drops its cleanest.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a lineage saved cheaply. The one force that lowers the floor is
  recombination, the mixing of two genomes into one cleaner than either (Felsenstein), but it charges the two-fold cost
  of sex, a partner, and the breaking of good combinations, so an asexual copier out-reproduces it in every present
  season and is forever tempted to abandon it (Maynard Smith). And even paid, the remedy reaches only the variation still
  present, preventing the next click better than it undoes the last, since a mutation fixed in the whole population sits
  in every genome the mixing draws on. So the cure is real, dear, and forward-facing, out-competed in the short run and
  unable to recover what is already lost, not a way to copy on and be spared the climb.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the ratchet and its irreversibility are Muller's,
  the quantitative model of the least-loaded class and its loss by drift is Haigh's, the advantage of recombination is
  Felsenstein's, the two-fold cost of sex is Maynard Smith's, and the mutational meltdown of small asexual populations is
  Lynch, Bürger, Butcher and Gabriel's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages a floor that
  climbs one way and credits that no one selected for it and the loss is chance's, holding it a tendency under finite,
  non-recombining conditions and not a law that fixes every lineage on the climb.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Rising Floor](piece_the_rising_floor.md)**: carries the ratchet whole (Muller), the minimum mutational load that
  steps up with each lost class and by every available means never down.
- **[The Least-Loaded](piece_the_least_loaded.md)**: carries the quantitative model (Haigh), the fewest-mutations class
  that is also the smallest and so the one a finite draw is bound eventually to drop.
- **[The Back-Mutation](piece_the_back_mutation.md)**: carries the closed exit (Muller), the reversal too rare to rebuild
  a lost class, the reason the loss is permanent rather than a passing dip.
- **[The Cost of Sex](piece_the_cost_of_sex.md)**: carries the two-fold cost (Maynard Smith), the price that makes the
  recombination remedy refusable and the ratchet able to run.
- **[The Cross](position_the_cross.md)** and **[Recombining](process_recombining.md)**: carry the remedy (Felsenstein),
  the mixing of two genomes into a cleaner one that alone lowers the floor, reaching only what is not yet fixed.
- **[The Narrows](place_the_narrows.md)**: carries the mutational meltdown (Lynch, Bürger, Butcher and Gabriel), the small
  population where the click comes fast and the rising load spirals toward extinction.
