# Pitch: Only Mixing Brings It Back

## Taxonomy

[No Way Back Up](play_no_way_back_up.md). The single hook: a finite population that copies without recombination loses its least-loaded class by chance and has no way to reassemble it, so the minimum mutational load climbs one irreversible notch at a time, and the only force that can lower the floor again is the costly mixing of two lineages a faithful copier does without.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Copy faithfully, reproduce efficiently, and carry the line forward.
The tenor is sound reproduction: a lineage passes on its genome intact, wastes nothing on finding a mate, and keeps its best individuals doing what they do, exactly the efficient faithful copying that fills a population fastest.

## Undertow

The cleanest class is the rarest, and chance drops the rare.
Underneath, the fewest-mutations individuals are by their nature the smallest class, so the leading edge of the whole population's fitness rests on its rarest members, and in a finite crowd a small class is eventually missed by the luck of who reproduces and simply lost.

## Nerve

Once the best is lost, nothing asexual rebuilds it.
The nerve is that copying can transmit a genome and never combine two, and back-mutation is far too rare to count on, so an asexual population cannot reassemble a class cleaner than its current best, and the minimum load, having lost its lowest, can only hold or climb, never fall.

## Echo

The one cure is the mixing a copier abandons.
What lingers is that recombination alone can take the good stretches of two mutated genomes and make one cleaner than either, halting and reversing the climb, but it costs the two-fold cost of sex and a partner and the breaking of good combinations, so the floor keeps rising wherever the efficient copier does without the dear mixing that could lower it.
