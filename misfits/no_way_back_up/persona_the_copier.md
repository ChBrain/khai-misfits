# Persona: The Copier

## Taxonomy

[The Clone](position_the_clone.md). Reproduces asexually and faithfully, passing its genome to its offspring intact and mateless, carrying whatever mutations it holds and able to add them but never shed one, filling the population fastest and lowering its load never.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Efficient and self-sufficient. Copying without a partner. Filling the world with itself.
Is the one that reproduces by making copies, needing no mate and wasting nothing on courtship, so it fills a population twice as fast as anything that must pair, every offspring its own faithful likeness.
No sloth wished on them: faithful copying is exactly what reproduction is, and the copier that keeps its best individuals reproducing and asks no partner is doing the plainest, most efficient thing a lineage can do.

## Action

Transmits its genome whole to the next generation, mutations and all, adding whatever new ones arise and removing none, since copying carries a genome forward and has no means to repair it.
Reproduces without mixing, so each offspring descends from one parent and inherits that parent's load exactly, the line a chain of faithful likenesses that can only match or exceed the mutations before it.
Does what an efficient reproducer does: copies fast, pairs never, and passes the load on intact, gaining the whole advantage of asexual reproduction and with it the one thing it cannot do, lighten what it carries.

## Shadow

From the inside it feels like nothing but success: more offspring, sooner, each one its own, no partner needed and no half of anything given away, so the copier experiences its reproduction as pure winning and has no sense at all of a cost being paid.
What it cannot feel is the slowness of the loss. Each generation looks exactly like the last, the load creeping up by amounts too small to register in any single copying, so there is no moment where the decline announces itself, no felt tick of a floor rising, only an unbroken run of ordinary successful reproduction, and the copier is as content in the thousandth degraded generation as in the first, because from within a single act of copying there is simply nothing to notice.
The bind is interior and blank: it must copy to reproduce at all, feels each copying as a plain success, and carries no inner register for a decline distributed across a whole lineage and a thousand generations, so it does the efficient and faithful thing with real contentment and cannot, from inside, tell a rising line from a level one.

## Tell

Says "more of me, and faster, and no one else needed."
Is the whole advantage in one phrase, since needing no one and copying fast is exactly the asexual edge, and it is real, so the sentence that sounds like plain triumph is the sound of the copier counting the advantage it has and blind to the price folded inside it, the faithful efficiency that fills the population being the same faithful efficiency that can never lower its load.
