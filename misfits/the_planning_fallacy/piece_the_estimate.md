# Piece: The Estimate

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). The forecast that goes out, the date and cost promised, low by optimism and lower by the incentive that meets it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), where it is summed from the plan and shaded toward the number that clears the gate.
Carried to [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md), where it is laid down as dates for the world to beat.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing promised and nothing to be missed, only a plan and a hope.
With it the fallacy has its artefact: a single figure, committed to, that reads as knowledge and is optimism. The Estimate is load bearing because it is where the two drivers meet (Lovallo and Kahneman): honest optimism sets it below the likely, and where a low number wins the approval the incentive sets it lower still, believed and pitched at once, so the piece carries a promise that is sincere and unmeetable together, worst exactly where the stakes are highest.

## Apparent

A commitment, a firm date arrived at by careful work.
Seen from the promise, the estimate looks like a professional's considered word, and the truth that it was built from the smooth run and shaded for the gate, and so was never a date the world would keep, stays hidden behind the confidence with which it is given.
