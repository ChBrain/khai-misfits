# Piece: The Overrun

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). The distance between the estimate and the actual, the whole of the fallacy made into one number.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md), where the promised date passes and the finish lands later, the gap counted in spent days.
Foretold at [the archive](place_the_archive.md), where the same gap stands recorded against every task that came before.

## Load Bearing

Without it the fallacy would have no measure, only a vague sense that the work took longer than it should.
With it the finding is exact: the overrun is the actual minus the estimate, the omitted reality counted in a single figure, invisible while the plan is drawn and legible only once the work is done. The Overrun is load bearing because it is the planning fallacy itself, quantified (Flyvbjerg): the further the estimate was set below the truth, the larger it runs, one-directional across tasks and enduring across years, so it is both the cost read in the world and the exact size of what the estimate should have allowed.

## Apparent

A one-off, this task's particular bad luck, to be explained and moved past.
Seen from the finish, the overrun looks like a surprise specific to this run, and the truth that it is the same gap as last time and the time before, written in the base rate the whole while and only unreadable until the work concluded, stays hidden behind how singular each task's troubles felt as they came.
