# Plot: The Overrun

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Calendar](place_the_calendar.md), the work begun against the promised date.
The plan's smooth run meeting the week's real texture, and the days going faster than the work.

## Action

[Overrunning](process_overrunning.md): [The Unforeseen](persona_the_unforeseen.md), holding [the unforeseen's seat](position_unforeseen.md), arrives as the swarm of small surprises, while [The Planner](persona_the_planner.md), holding [the planner's seat](position_planner.md), watches the date it set come and go.

## Stage

[The Unforeseen](persona_the_unforeseen.md), having tabled [Bring the Unforeseen](plan_bring_the_unforeseen.md), spends the days on the slip, the bounce, the sick day, the change, none of them nameable in advance and all of them taking their time.
[The Planner](persona_the_planner.md), still on [Plan the Work](plan_plan_the_work.md), sees the estimate beaten not by any one blow it could have foreseen but by the sum of many it could not, and [The Sponsor](persona_the_sponsor.md) begins to carry the slip it priced.

## Tension

The date is missed by exactly the thing no date could have held.
There is no failure of effort: the team works, the plan was sound as a plan, each surprise was genuinely unforecastable. But a plan is the run where nothing in particular goes wrong, and the unforeseen is the certainty made of unpredictable parts, so the work runs long by the one factor the estimate was built to leave out, and the promised date passes with the task, like its kind before it, unfinished.
