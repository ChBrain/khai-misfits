# Piece: The Unforeseen

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). The swarm of small surprises the plan omits by construction, each unnameable in advance and all certain in the sum.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md), where the disruptions arrive one by one and spend the days the estimate did not hold.
Absent from [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), where none of them can be listed, and so none priced.

## Load Bearing

Without it the plan would come true and the estimate would hold, and there would be no fallacy at all.
With it the inside view is beaten by the reality it left out: the slip, the bounce, the sick day, the change, each too particular to foresee and their total too sure to escape. The Unforeseen is load bearing because it is the exact reason a plan runs low (Buehler, Griffin and Ross): a plan is the run where nothing in particular goes wrong, and this is the thing in particular that does, different every time, so it cannot be planned away by pricing the last one, only expected in the aggregate it never announces.

## Apparent

Bad luck, a run of surprises that happened to land on this task.
Seen from the calendar, the unforeseen looks like misfortune anyone would forgive, and the truth that some such swarm was certain from the start, that its total was written in the base rate before the work began, stays hidden behind how genuinely unpredictable each single member of it was.

## Yearbook

Brought by [the unforeseen](persona_the_unforeseen.md) through [overrunning](process_overrunning.md), the reality the plan omitted.
It is what the estimate was built to leave out: the Unforeseen is the departure from [the plan](piece_the_plan.md)'s smooth run, the days spent past [the estimate](piece_the_estimate.md), and the substance that fills [the overrun](piece_the_overrun.md), already counted in [the base rate](piece_the_base_rate.md) as the average it will match.
