# Place: The Calendar

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). Where the work is actually done and the days really spent, and where the promised date arrives and passes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The estimate laid down as dates, and the work moving against them.
The plan's smooth run meeting the week's real texture: the slip, the bounce, the sick day, the change, each taking its days, and the promised date coming closer with the work still short of done.

## Holds

The one true test of the estimate, paid in real time.
Here the inside-view number meets the reality it left out, and the calendar holds the running total of the unforeseen, day by day, so it is where the plan stops being a drawing and becomes a schedule the world is free to beat, and where every surprise the board could not name spends itself against the date.

## Offers

The overrun, delivered on the dates themselves.
Because the calendar counts real days and not planned ones, it offers the gap as it opens: the promised date passing, the work unfinished, the finish landing later, so it is the place the estimate is finally priced against the world, in the only currency that was ever going to settle it.

## Withheld

Any second date that would hold.
The calendar answers the estimate but does not repair the method, so it withholds the correction: it shows this task ran long, as its kind always does, but not why the next will too, and it withholds the record's counsel, offered on the board it never reached and read here only as the same surprise once more.
