# Piece: The Padding

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). The buffer added too freely to the estimate, and consumed by a task that expands to fill it, the symmetric waste on the far side of the fault.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the reforecast](place_the_reforecast.md), where the outside view is applied and, pressed past the truth, tips into a margin larger than the record warrants.
Spent on [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md), where the padded date is met not early but exactly, the slack drawn down by work that spreads to fill it.

## Load Bearing

Without it the remedy would look free, and the reckoning would be a clean win instead of a bet.
With it the correction shows its far arm: an estimate corrected too hard is not thereby safe, because a date set with room to spare invites the work to take the room. The Padding is load bearing because it is the symmetric cost that keeps the remedy honest (Parkinson): the answer to a promise too soon is not any later promise but the right one, and a buffer beyond the base rate is waste of the opposite sign, so the piece marks the far edge past which fixing the fallacy becomes feeding it.

## Apparent

Prudence, a sensible margin against the overruns of the past.
Seen from the reforecast, the padding looks like the lesson learned, and the truth that a margin past what the record shows will be filled rather than banked, that work expands to the time allowed, stays hidden behind how reasonable a little extra room appears after a run of lateness.

## Yearbook

Risked by [the forecaster](persona_the_forecaster.md) through [reforecasting](process_reforecasting.md), the remedy overshot.
It is the fault's other arm: the Padding is what [the reforecast](place_the_reforecast.md) yields when [the base rate](piece_the_base_rate.md) is not trusted but exceeded, the slack [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md) draws down in full, and the mirror of [the overrun](piece_the_overrun.md), a date wrong now by too much rather than too little.
