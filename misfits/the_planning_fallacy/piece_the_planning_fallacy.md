# Piece: The Planning Fallacy

## Taxonomy

[The Planning Fallacy](play_the_planning_fallacy.md). The phenomenon whole: a task estimated from its own plan and promised too soon, and its remedy in a forecast taken from the record at a symmetric cost.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the drawing board](place_the_drawing_board.md), where it is born as an inside-view estimate, and on [the calendar](place_the_calendar.md), where it is proved by the overrun.
Answered at [the reforecast](place_the_reforecast.md), where the outside view prices the task by its class, at its own cost.

## Load Bearing

Without it the other pieces are parts without the mechanism that binds them.
With it the whole trap stands in one object: an estimate built from the plan of a singular task ignores the distribution it belongs to, and because a plan traces the successful run, the estimate is optimistic in one direction and the promise is beaten by the reality it left out. The Planning Fallacy is load bearing because it is the structure entire (Kahneman and Tversky): not a slip of arithmetic but a way of seeing, the inside view natural and vivid and wrong, correctable only by the deliberate, uncomfortable turn to the record, which prices the task right and gives up the very detail that made the plan feel sure.

## Apparent

A discipline problem, teams that should simply pad their estimates and try harder to hit them.
Seen from outside, the fallacy looks like something better project management would cure, and the truth that it survives knowing, that the inside view returns for every new task even to those who have overrun before, and that its remedy costs the case's own detail, stays hidden behind how obvious the fix appears to anyone not inside the estimate.

## Yearbook

The trap entire, staged across the run.
The Planning Fallacy is [the plan](piece_the_plan.md) built into [the estimate](piece_the_estimate.md) while [the base rate](piece_the_base_rate.md) is set aside, the promise [the unforeseen](piece_the_unforeseen.md) turns into [the overrun](piece_the_overrun.md), and the fault [the forecaster](persona_the_forecaster.md) answers at [the reforecast](place_the_reforecast.md) only by risking [the padding](piece_the_padding.md) on the far side.
