# Place: The Surgery

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The consulting room where the well are screened, the finding delivered, and the treatment given, where a disease is caught and a survivor is made in the same encounter, and neither the lethal nor the harmless kind announces which it is.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A test, a finding, and a treatment, the whole of care in one room.
Shown at the surface is everything reassuring about medicine: a person seen, a disease found early, a plan made and carried out, a life attended to, the encounter that turns worry into action and sends the patient home cared for.

## Holds

Holds the catch and the crediting, the moment a survivor is made.
The Surgery is where the disease is found before the body would report it and where the person is told it was caught in time, so it holds both halves of the conversion: the detection that finds the harmless kind alongside the lethal, and the diagnosis that lets the person read their later survival as a rescue, the two kinds handled identically because nothing in the room can tell them apart.

## Offers

Offers a rescue felt and a harm unfelt, in the same act.
Here the patient receives real care, a genuine finding and a genuine treatment, so the Surgery offers everything a saving offers, and offers, in the same motion and indistinguishably, an overdiagnosis: the harmless kind found and treated exactly as the lethal is, the person sent home grateful whether they were rescued or relabelled, the room unable to hand them anything that would tell which.

## Withheld

Withholds the counterfactual, and with it the meaning of the finding.
What the Surgery hides is the one thing that would give the encounter its truth: what would have happened to this person had they never been screened, the course of the disease left alone, which the room cannot run and cannot show. So the place that delivers the finding withholds its worth, present with every fact of the case and absent the only comparison that reads them, sending home a survivor who cannot know, because the Surgery cannot tell them, whether there was anything to survive.
