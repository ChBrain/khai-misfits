# Piece: The Harmless Kind

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The indolent disease that screening finds and that would never have surfaced, real disease to the pathologist and no threat by its nature, the overdiagnosis at the root of every grateful survivor the loop makes.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Found at [the Surgery](place_the_surgery.md) alongside the lethal, counted the same and treated the same.
Flows as a diagnosis indistinguishable from a dangerous one, a disease caught early that was never going to cause symptoms or death, entering the record as a case addressed.

## Load Bearing

Without the Harmless Kind there is no overdiagnosis and no false rescue: it is the indolent disease, found and treated and survived, that supplies the grateful survivors the whole loop runs on.
The Harmless Kind is load bearing because it is the overdiagnosis itself, the detection of disease that would never have harmed (Welch and Black). It cannot be told from the lethal at the moment of the catch: the test sees the tumour and not its future, so the harmless kind is diagnosed, treated, and survived exactly as the dangerous kind is, and the person who carries it lives and credits the test. Every distortion downstream follows from this one object: the survival rate rises because the harmless kind is counted as a cure, the testimony forms because its survivor feels rescued, and the constituency grows because that survivor advocates, so the harm the programme does is made of the harmless kind found and treated as though it were the lethal.

## Apparent

Just a disease caught early, the same good news as any other catch.
Seen at the surface, the Harmless Kind looks like a save: a real disease found before it spread, dealt with in time, another life protected, and the truth that it would never have harmed, that finding and treating it did the person only harm, that it is the overdiagnosis and not the rescue, stays hidden behind how exactly it resembles the catch that saves.
