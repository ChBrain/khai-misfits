# Plot: The Thank-You

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Surgery](place_the_surgery.md), a person treated and living.
Told the disease was caught early and dealt with, the patient walks out well, and reads the walking out as a rescue.

## Action

[Crediting](process_crediting.md): the Patient survives, draws the line from finding to treatment to living, and thanks the test for the life it was never established to have saved.

## Stage

[The Patient](persona_the_patient.md) acts from [the Diagnosis](position_the_diagnosis.md) on [Tell My Story](plan_tell_my_story.md), reading [the survival rate](piece_the_survival_rate.md) in their own body and forming [the testimony](piece_the_testimony.md), unable to tell a saving from a relabelling.
The gratitude is sincere and the survival is real, and neither can show the test did the saving.

## Tension

The survivor thanks the test in perfect good faith and cannot know whether it earned the thanks.
There is no delusion: the person is genuinely alive, genuinely was found and treated, genuinely feels rescued. But nothing in a single life distinguishes the tumour that would have killed from the one that would have sat still, so the plot closes with a gratitude owed to a rescue that may never have happened, the strongest evidence in the room, a living body, standing for exactly the thing it cannot prove, the harmed thanking, sincerely, the thing that may have harmed them.
