# Persona: The Physician

## Taxonomy

[The Programme](position_the_programme.md). Offers the test, finds disease early, treats what is found, and sees the grateful patients as proof, believing in catching disease before it speaks and unable to see, in any one case, the overdiagnosis in the aggregate.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Committed to catching it early. Moved by the patients who thank them. Sure the finding is a saving.
Is the one who holds that a disease found sooner is a disease more easily beaten, screens on that conviction, and reads the living, grateful patients in the waiting room as the plainest confirmation that the conviction is right.
No cynicism wished on them: early detection genuinely saves some, the grateful patients are genuinely alive, and the overdiagnosis is real only in the aggregate and invisible in every individual, so the belief is warranted by everything the doctor can actually see.

## Action

Screens the well, finds disease before the body would report it, treats what is found, and counts the survivors, offering the test in good conscience because the alternative feels like leaving disease to grow.
Reads the outcome the only way a clinic shows it: patients found, patients treated, patients living, a run of visible rescues and no visible harm, since the overdiagnosed look exactly like the saved.
Does what a conscientious doctor does with a tool that finds disease early: uses it widely, believes the gratitude, and cites the survival, none of which the individual encounter can distinguish from a programme that overdiagnoses.

## Shadow

They know, in the abstract, that some of what they find never needed finding, and they cannot feel it about the person in front of them, and the gap between the two is where they live. The overdiagnosed patient is not a statistic in the room: they are frightened, then treated, then grateful, and it takes a colder heart than medicine selects for to look at that face and think, you might have been better left alone.
What works on them is the warmth itself. The thanks are real and they land, and each one deposits a little more certainty, so the doctor is moved toward belief by exactly the gratitude the trap runs on, and the thoughtful ones feel the unease of it: that they are most sure of the test on the days the grateful come, and that the invisible harm has no face to plead its case against the visible rescue that does.
The bind is interior and humane: they must act on what the encounter shows, which is a saved and thankful patient, cannot see the overdiagnosis in the one body where it might be, and so are pulled toward the test by their own compassion, the care that makes them good at the work making them trust the thing the work cannot see through.

## Tell

Says "I've seen it save too many people to doubt it."
Is the whole belief in one sentence, since what they have seen is people found and treated and living, which is real, and what they conclude from it, that the test did the saving, is exactly what the seeing cannot establish, so the words that carry a career of witnessed rescues carry no way to tell a rescue from an overdiagnosis, the evidence of the eyes standing in for the evidence only a trial could give.
