# Process: Randomising

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The building of the comparison the clinic cannot, splitting a population by chance into screened and unscreened and counting to death, the correction that separates a saving from a relabelling.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Trialist](persona_the_trialist.md), at [the Long Run](place_the_long_run.md), following two arms for years and counting the dead.
Begins when a reader refuses the survival rate and the testimony and asks whether fewer died among the screened than among the unscreened.

## Direction

Turns an unanswerable question into a measured one.
Because the counterfactual no single life can run is exactly what a randomised split supplies, comparing like populations screened and unscreened, randomising converts the impossible individual comparison into a population one, so the disease-specific mortality can at last tell a life saved from a life merely found earlier, reading the deaths the survival rate concealed (Bailar).

## Lever

The chance that makes the two arms alike.
Because randomisation balances the screened and the unscreened in everything but the screening, the process works through the comparison it manufactures: the difference in their deaths is the screening's effect and nothing else, so the lever is the random split itself, which builds the counterfactual the single case lacks and lets the mortality speak where survival could not.

## Echo

A true answer, late and often null, and quieter than a story.
The echo of randomising is a disease-specific mortality that resolves only after years, frequently little moved, with the screened accruing the extra diagnoses that measure the overdiagnosis, so the correction arrives cold and slow against a constituency already formed, right about the harm and outweighed by a single grateful survivor.
