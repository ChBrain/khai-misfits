# Piece: The Survival Rate

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The five-year survival counted from diagnosis, the statistic that rises when screening finds disease earlier and when it finds harmless disease at all, climbing with the overdiagnosis and cited as proof the programme works.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Reported by [the Programme](position_the_programme.md), read at [the Surgery](place_the_surgery.md) and carried to the public.
Flows as a percentage that looks like success, the share of the diagnosed alive at five years, rising as the programme screens more and finding both kinds.

## Load Bearing

Without the Survival Rate the programme has no number to point to: it is the statistic that appears to prove the test works and in fact climbs with the harm, the metric that makes overdiagnosis look like success.
The Survival Rate is load bearing because it is the measure everyone trusts and it moves the wrong way, inflated by two biases at once (Welch, Schwartz and Woloshin). Counting from the moment of diagnosis, it rises when disease is found earlier even if no death is postponed, the lead time added to the survival and not to the life (Zelen and Feinleib), and it rises again when the harmless kind is found, since a person who was never going to die of the disease counts as a five-year survivor. So the statistic climbs precisely as the programme overdiagnoses, and because it is honest arithmetic it cannot be caught out from inside the programme's own numbers, the metric most cited to defend the test being the one most corrupted by the harm.

## Apparent

Just a survival rate, higher every year, the plainest sign of progress.
Seen at the surface, the Survival Rate looks like the scoreboard of success, more of the diagnosed living longer, and the truth that it counts from diagnosis and not from birth, that finding disease earlier and finding harmless disease both lift it without saving a soul, that it can rise while not one death is postponed, stays hidden behind how naturally a rising survival reads as lives saved.
