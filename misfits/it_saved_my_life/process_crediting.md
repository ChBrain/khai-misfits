# Process: Crediting

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The reading of a survival as a rescue, the grateful attribution by which a person who lived after being found credits the test, unable from inside one life to tell a saving from a relabelling.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Patient](persona_the_patient.md), at [the Surgery](place_the_surgery.md), told the disease was caught early and living on after.
Begins whenever a person treated for a screen-found disease survives, and reads the survival as the test having saved them.

## Direction

Turns a survival into a rescue, and an overdiagnosis into a gratitude.
Because a diagnosis followed by treatment followed by survival reads as cause and cure and outcome, the person draws the line through the three points they hold and credits the test, so crediting converts a life that continued into a life the test rescued, the harmless kind's survivor as grateful as the lethal kind's and unable to tell which they are (Welch and Black).

## Lever

The counterfactual no single life can run.
Because nothing in one body distinguishes the tumour that would have killed from the one that would have sat still, crediting works through the missing comparison: the person cannot observe their own unscreened course, so the lever is the impossibility of the counterfactual, which leaves the survival open to only one reading from the inside, a rescue, whether or not there was anything to be rescued from.

## Echo

A gratitude sincere and unfalsifiable, owed to the test whichever kind was found.
The echo of crediting is a survivor sure the test saved them, their certainty resting on a living body that a rescue and a relabelling would have produced alike, so the more the programme overdiagnoses the more such gratitude it generates, each grateful survivor a real feeling attached to a rescue that may never have happened.
