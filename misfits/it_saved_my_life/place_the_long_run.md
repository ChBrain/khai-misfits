# Place: The Long Run

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md). The span of years over which the truth of the programme finally shows, where deaths are counted rather than diagnoses, and a life saved is at last told from a life merely found earlier.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Little, and slowly: a count of the dead accumulating over years.
Shown here is nothing dramatic, no rescue and no face, only a rate assembling as the years pass, the disease-specific deaths among the screened set beside those among the unscreened, a comparison that resolves too slowly to feel.

## Holds

Holds the mortality, the one measure that separates saving from relabelling.
The Long Run is where survival stops hiding the answer, because over enough years a life truly saved shows as a death that did not happen, and a life merely relabelled shows as a diagnosis that changed nothing, so this is where the two kinds finally part, held in a count that the moment of diagnosis could never give.

## Offers

Offers the truth of the programme, at a price in years and in warmth.
Here lies the finding that would tell whether the test saves lives, the mortality of the screened against the unscreened, so the Long Run offers the answer the Surgery and the Rally cannot, and offers it late and cold: a rate that takes years to resolve and arrives without a face, worth less in the room than a single living survivor though it holds the only truth of the matter.

## Withheld

Withholds its answer until long after the demand is set.
What the Long Run withholds is timeliness: the mortality it holds is unknowable until the years have passed, by which time the programme has grown, the constituency has formed, and the testimony has done its work, so the place that holds the truth releases it too late to catch the demand it would correct. The answer is kept behind time, present in principle and absent when it is needed, so the programme is judged by its survivors long before the Long Run can be asked who actually lived.
