# Persona: The Patient

## Taxonomy

[The Diagnosis](position_the_diagnosis.md). Screened, told a disease was caught early, treated, and living, credits the test with a rescue, unable to tell from inside a living body whether the disease found was ever going to kill.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Grateful, and sure of what they are grateful for. Alive and crediting the test. Ready to tell others.
Is the one who went in well, was found to have something, had it treated, and came out the far side living, and reads that living as a rescue because from where they stand a rescue is exactly what it looks like.
No naivety wished on them: they cannot know they were overdiagnosed, because nothing in a single body distinguishes the tumour that would have killed from the one that would have sat still, so the gratitude is not a mistake they could have avoided but the only reading their own life offers them.

## Action

Thanks the test, tells the story, and presses it on everyone they love, turning a private survival into a public case for screening.
Carries the testimony that moves rooms: not a statistic but a face and a name and a life, the single most persuasive thing anyone can bring to the question of whether the test is worth it.
Does what a grateful survivor does, and would do whether saved or overdiagnosed, since the two feel identical from the inside: campaigns, raises money, and adds one more grateful voice to the demand that the programme reads as proof.

## Shadow

The gratitude is not a position they hold but a floor they stand on, and that is why any doubt cast on the test lands not as a statistic but as a hand reaching for the floor. To be told the test may not have saved them is to be told the second chance they have built a life on top of might be a story, and no one hears that quietly.
What lives under it is a tenderness they cannot afford to examine. Somewhere they know the survival could have other explanations, and they do not go there, because the meaning is doing real work: it turned a frightening year into a rescue, gave the fear a shape and a hero, and let them be someone who was saved rather than someone who was frightened for nothing. The story is load bearing for the self, and they protect it the way anyone protects a thing that holds them up.
The bind is interior and gentle and immovable: they must believe it saved them to make sense of what they went through, cannot from inside their own life tell whether it did, and so meet the cold measure not with argument but with the whole weight of a person defending the meaning of their own survival, sincere past the reach of any evidence.

## Tell

Says "I'm living proof" with a hand on their own chest.
Is the whole conversion in one phrase, since the proof they offer is their living body, which is real, and the thing it is offered to prove, that the test saved them, is exactly what a living body cannot show, so the sentence that sounds like the strongest evidence in the room is a survival standing in for a rescue, the harmed speaking, in perfect good faith, for the thing that may have harmed them.
