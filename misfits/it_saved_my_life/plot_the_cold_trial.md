# Plot: The Cold Trial

## Taxonomy

[It Saved My Life](play_it_saved_my_life.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Long Run](place_the_long_run.md), someone who would measure it truly.
A reader unwilling to take the survival rate or the testimony for proof, ready to split a population by chance and count the dead over years.

## Action

[Randomising](process_randomising.md): the Trialist compares screened against unscreened, counts to death rather than to diagnosis, and reads a mortality barely moved and an incidence inflated.

## Stage

[The Trialist](persona_the_trialist.md) acts from [the Endpoint](position_the_endpoint.md) on [Measure the Mortality](plan_measure_the_mortality.md), setting the count of the dead against [the survival rate](piece_the_survival_rate.md) and the whole [constituency](piece_the_constituency.md), the harmless kind showing at last as the diagnoses the screened accrued and the unscreened did not.
The measure is right and the finding is cold, and it arrives years after the demand was set.

## Tension

The one true measure comes back a null and persuades no one it was meant to save.
There is no flaw in it: the randomisation is sound, the mortality the only figure that separates a saving from a relabelling, the incidence the clean fingerprint of the harm. But the answer takes years, arrives without a face, and lands in a room already full of grateful survivors, so the plot closes with the truth outvoted, a quiet null against a warm testimony, the trialist cast as the enemy of the people the finding would spare, and the programme, judged long ago by its survivors, running on undisturbed, or the trial never funded at all and the gratitude left to stand as proof.
