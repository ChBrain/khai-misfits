# Debt-Deflation: Reference

The research warrant for [Debt-Deflation](play_debt_deflation.md).
The theory is Fisher's; the play runs on his debt-deflation account of depressions, through Bernanke's credit
channel and the balance-sheet-recession literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging debt-deflation as a **monetary system**: an over-indebted economy in which each debtor's prudent move
to pay down debt, when made by all at once, forces distress selling that lowers the price level, so that the
debt, being fixed in money, rises in real weight as prices fall, and the more the debtors pay the more, in
real terms, they owe. The play models the documented mechanics, the debt fixed in nominal money whatever that
money comes to be worth, the distress sale that is the only way to pay, the deflation that a general
liquidation drives, the rising real burden that is the paradox at the centre, the self-reinforcing spiral in
which heavier debt begets more selling and lower prices beget heavier debt, amplified as failing banks stop
lending, and the reflation or debt relief that alone halts it, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the
structure, not as a tale of profligacy. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing
law: when nominal debt is high and the price level falls, the real value of debt rises, so attempts to
deleverage by liquidation are self-defeating in aggregate, driving the deflation that raises the burden they
sought to reduce. It is distinct from the house's The Paradox of Thrift, where a general urge to save cuts
aggregate demand and income: there the channel is spending and income, here it is the price level and the real
value of a fixed debt. And it is distinct from the house's The Bank Run, where a self-fulfilling panic and a
first-come queue fell a solvent institution: here there is no run, only a slow spiral of prices and the real
weight of debt.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the debtor as
  profligate or the creditor as heartless, because the source finding is precisely that each move is right:
  paying down debt in hard times is prudent, calling a shaky loan is a lender protecting itself, and selling to
  raise cash is the only way to pay. The spiral is authored by no error and emerges from the structure, a debt
  fixed in money and a price level that falls when all sell at once, so the misfit is that an economy doing the
  responsible thing, paying down its debts, drives itself deeper into them.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that halts the
  spiral is to raise the price level or cut the nominal debt, to reflate or forgive, and it asks a hand to
  create money and run deficits exactly when caution screams the opposite, asks creditors to swallow a loss and
  rewards the borrower who took the risk, and cuts against the deep instinct to let the debt be paid and the
  bust clear itself. The reckoning is a spiral broken by acting against every sound instinct of the moment, at
  the creditor's expense, not a free correction, so the cure for an economy paying itself deeper into debt is
  the one thing prudence forbids.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the debt-deflation theory, the credit channel, financial
  instability, and the balance-sheet recession are theirs, and the condition the trap actually requires, high
  nominal debt meeting a falling price level so that deleveraging raises the real burden, is carried as
  carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Debt](piece_the_debt.md)**: carries the fixed nominal obligation (Fisher; Minsky), owed in a set number
  of dollars whatever those dollars come to be worth.
- **[The Distress Sale](piece_the_distress_sale.md)**: carries the forced liquidation (Koo), the selling that is
  the only way to pay and that, done by all, drives prices down.
- **[The Weight](piece_the_weight.md)**: carries the rising real burden (Fisher), the paradox that as prices fall
  the fixed debt grows heavier, so the more is paid the more is owed.
- **[The Spiral](piece_the_spiral.md)**: carries the self-reinforcing descent (Fisher; Bernanke), heavier debt
  begetting more selling and lower prices begetting heavier debt, amplified as banks fail and lending stops.
- **[The Reflation](piece_the_reflation.md)**: carries the remedy (Fisher), the lift of the price level or the
  cut of the nominal debt that halts the spiral, against every instinct of the moment.
- **[Debt-Deflation](piece_debt_deflation.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Fisher), an economy paying itself
  deeper into debt, and its remedy in a reflation the moment forbids.
