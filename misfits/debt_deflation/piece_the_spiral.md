# Piece: The Spiral

## Taxonomy

[Debt-Deflation](play_debt_deflation.md). The self-reinforcing descent, heavier debt begetting more selling and lower prices begetting heavier debt, amplified as banks fail.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the slump](place_the_slump.md), where prices and net worth and credit fall together and the real debt climbs through it all.
Traced from [the market](place_the_market.md), where each round of selling drives the next fall in prices.

## Load Bearing

Without it the first fall in prices would be a one-off, and the burden would rise once and settle.
With it the loss compounds: heavier debt forces more distress selling, which lowers prices, which raises the debt again, and as firms fail below their fixed debts and the banks that lent them break, the credit that could arrest the fall dries up too. The Spiral is load bearing because it is why a modest deflation becomes a depression (Fisher; Bernanke): the mechanism closes on itself and the credit channel amplifies it, so the piece carries the self-feeding descent that no single payment can halt.

## Apparent

A worsening depression, bad news compounding, a downturn spinning out of control.
Seen as it runs, the spiral looks like a run of misfortune gathering pace, and the truth that it is the sum of prudent moves, each debtor paying and each creditor calling, that the descent is made of good sense closed into a loop, stays hidden behind how much it feels like catastrophe striking rather than prudence turning on itself.

## Yearbook

Driven through [spiralling](process_spiralling.md), the loop of falling prices and rising debt.
It is the descent that feeds itself: the Spiral is [the weight](piece_the_weight.md) forcing more [distress sale](piece_the_distress_sale.md) and more selling deepening the deflation on [the debt](piece_the_debt.md), the loop that only [the reflation](piece_the_reflation.md) can break.
