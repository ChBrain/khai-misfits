# Plot: The Weight

## Taxonomy

[Debt-Deflation](play_debt_deflation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Ledger](place_the_ledger.md), the debt fixed in money and the money now worth more.
The payments made, the assets sold, and the figure owed exactly as large as before.

## Action

[Deflating](process_deflating.md): [The Debt](persona_the_debt.md), holding [the debt's seat](position_debt.md), holds its sum as prices fall, while [The Debtor](persona_the_debtor.md), holding [the debtor's seat](position_debtor.md), finds the loan against its half-price field undiminished.

## Stage

[The Debt](persona_the_debt.md), having tabled [Hold the Sum](plan_hold_the_sum.md), stays owed in dollars though the dollars buy more, rising in real weight without a figure moving.
[The Debtor](persona_the_debtor.md), still on [Pay It Down](plan_pay_it_down.md), has sold and paid and yet owes as much in truth as ever, the burden grown as the effort to clear it grew.

## Tension

The borrower paid, and owes more for having paid.
There is no miscalculation to blame: the debtor sold and settled exactly as prudence advised. But the selling drove the prices down and the debt is fixed in money, so the real burden rose as the payments were made, and the plot closes on Fisher's paradox laid bare, the more the debtors pay the more they owe, the one figure on the ledger that a falling world makes larger.
