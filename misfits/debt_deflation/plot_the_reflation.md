# Plot: The Reflation

## Taxonomy

[Debt-Deflation](play_debt_deflation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Mint](place_the_mint.md), the spiral running toward the floor and one actor weighing whether to act against it.
The real burden climbing, and the levers of reflation and relief at hand.

## Action

[Reflating](process_reflating.md): [The Reflator](persona_the_reflator.md), holding [the reflator's seat](position_reflator.md), loosens money and runs deficits or writes the debt down, while [The Creditor](persona_the_creditor.md), holding [the creditor's seat](position_creditor.md), faces the loss and the instinct to let the debt be paid instead.

## Stage

[The Reflator](persona_the_reflator.md), having tabled [Lift the Level](plan_lift_the_level.md), raises the price level or cuts the nominal debt, halting the rise in the real burden.
[The Creditor](persona_the_creditor.md), still on [Call the Loan](plan_call_the_loan.md), must swallow the loss reflation or forgiveness imposes, and every sound instinct of the bust argues to let the paying-down run instead.

## Tension

The one move that halts the spiral is the one the bust condemns.
There is no easy remedy: reflation plainly stops the descent, yet it means creating money and running deficits when caution screams the opposite, and forgiveness means the creditor's loss and the borrower's reward. So the play closes where it must, the price level lifted or the debt cut and the burden's rise arrested ... or the paying-down goes on, austerity holds, and the spiral runs to the floor.
