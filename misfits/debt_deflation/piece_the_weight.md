# Piece: The Weight

## Taxonomy

[Debt-Deflation](play_debt_deflation.md). The rising real burden of the fixed debt as prices fall, the paradox that the more is paid the more is owed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the ledger](place_the_ledger.md), where the fixed sum, unchanged, comes to represent an ever greater share of everything.
Felt across [the slump](place_the_slump.md), where the rising burden crushes firms below their debts and drives the next round of selling.

## Load Bearing

Without it a general deleveraging would simply lighten the load, and paying down would work.
With it the central paradox lands: because the debt is fixed in money and prices fall, its real value rises, so the borrower who sells and pays finds the burden greater, not less. The Weight is load bearing because it is Fisher's paradox itself (Fisher): the more the debtors pay, the more, in real terms, they owe, so the piece carries the perverse arithmetic that turns the act of clearing debt into the cause of its growth.

## Apparent

Bad luck, a debt that somehow feels heavier though payments are being made.
Seen by the borrower, the weight looks like misfortune or miscalculation, and the truth that the paying itself caused it, that a whole economy selling drove the prices down that raised the debt, that the harder all pay the heavier it grows, stays hidden behind how plainly wrong it seems that paying a debt could enlarge it.

## Yearbook

Read at [the ledger](place_the_ledger.md) through [deflating](process_deflating.md), the burden risen as prices fell.
It is the paradox counted: the Weight is what [deflating](process_deflating.md) does to [the debt](piece_the_debt.md) that [the distress sale](piece_the_distress_sale.md) was meant to shrink, the rising burden that drives [the spiral](piece_the_spiral.md), and the figure that only [the reflation](piece_the_reflation.md) can bring back down.
