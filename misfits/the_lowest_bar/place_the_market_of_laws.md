# Place: The Market of Laws

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The field of competing standards, where each jurisdiction sets its bar against the others and the base can move between them, and where the lowerings answer each other into the descent.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The field the jurisdictions compete in, a market of standards laid side by side, where each sets its bar and each watches the others, and the base moves to whichever is lowest.
The Jurisdiction lowering to attract, the Rival matching to keep, each cut visible to the next, and the base flowing to the laxest terms across the field.

## Holds

The competition, and the descent it runs.
Here the standards are set against one another with a mobile base free to move between them, so the Market of Laws holds the point where each lowering is answered: a cut to attract the base met by a rival's cut to keep it, and a further cut to stay ahead, so it is where the standard ratchets down not by any jurisdiction's wish but by none being able to hold it high while the base can find it low next door, the descent running as competition runs.
Each lowering is a rational answer to the last, and the field carries them toward the lowest bar on offer.

## Offers

Competitiveness on every side, each jurisdiction winning the base by asking less.
Because the Market of Laws is where the standards are set against each other, it offers each jurisdiction the base and the revenue for lowering furthest, the field of terms in which asking less is the way to win, so it hands out competitiveness all round, and hands it out as a descent, each cut a local win and the sum a bar no one chose.
The wins are on the field for all to see, and the floor the field settles at is one none of them wanted.

## Withheld

Any hand that can hold the bar high alone.
The Market of Laws lets each jurisdiction set its standard but cannot let any hold it above the others while the base can move, because a bar kept high loses the base to a bar kept low, so it withholds the unilateral standard, exacting a match from anyone who would hold firm, and, all competition and no floor, it withholds from the field the one thing none can supply alone, a common minimum the base cannot slip beneath.
