# Process: Undercutting

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The Rival matching or beating a laxer neighbour's standard, the defensive lowering that answers each cut and deepens the descent.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A neighbour that lowered first and a base leaving for the laxer terms.
At [the market of laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), the rival watching the base register elsewhere and cutting its own standard to keep it.

## Direction

Toward the neighbour matched and the base kept.
Because a standard no base will sit under protects no one, undercutting lowers the rival's own bar to match or beat the neighbour, so the base stays, and the match forces the first jurisdiction to cut again to stay ahead, so each lowering answers the last and the standard ratchets down, the descent carried by jurisdictions each doing only what the neighbour made rational.
The match is defensive and reasonable, and it is the next notch down.

## Lever

The loss of the base to a bar the rival cannot beat by holding firm.
Because a higher standard the base will not sit under buys only the loss of the base and its revenue, undercutting runs on a forced choice and not on eagerness, so the deepened descent is assembled from no aggression, only from a rival choosing to keep the base it would otherwise lose, its match the reasonable answer to a neighbour's cut.

## Echo

A neighbour matched, and the bar a notch lower than before.
The rival has kept the base by cutting its own standard, so what reads as staying competitive is, in truth, the descent ratcheting, the rival's forced match forcing the next cut, and the immobile party it once protected keeping the lower bar the rival never wanted to set.
