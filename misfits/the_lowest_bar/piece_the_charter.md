# Piece: The Charter

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The prize each jurisdiction competes for and the franchise revenue it carries, the registration of the base that makes lowering the standard worth money to the jurisdiction that wins it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Granted at [the register](place_the_register.md), where the base enters and the fees begin.
Competed for across [the market of laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), where each jurisdiction bids for it by lowering the bar.

## Load Bearing

Without it winning the base is worth nothing and the jurisdiction has no reason to lower the standard, and the descent has no motive.
With it the trap has its incentive: the registration and the franchise fees it brings, which make holding the base a source of real revenue, so lowering the standard to win the charter is the rational pursuit of money and not a whim (Cary, on Delaware's franchise tax, the revenue that rewards the state for chartering the most corporations and so for pleasing the mobile chooser). The Charter is load bearing because it is why the jurisdiction competes: the prize that turns a mobile base into a revenue worth lowering the bar for, so the piece carries the franchise income that makes each lowering locally rational, the fee that follows the base to whoever asked the least.

## Apparent

A reward for good governance, a jurisdiction earning registrations by being a good place to do business.
Seen at the surface, the Charter looks like revenue well won, a jurisdiction paid for offering what bases want, and the truth that what bases want is a lower bar, that the fee rewards the state precisely for lowering the protection, that the revenue and the externalised harm are two faces of one charter, stays hidden behind how much a franchise fee looks like an honest return on being attractive.
