# Plot: The Match

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Market of Laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), a rival that held a higher standard watching the base leave for a laxer neighbour.
The Rival weighing its bar against the base it is losing.

## Action

[Undercutting](process_undercutting.md): [The Rival](persona_the_rival.md), holding [the rival's seat](position_rival.md), lowers its own standard to match or beat the neighbour and keep the base.

## Stage

[The Rival](persona_the_rival.md), on [Undercut](plan_undercut.md), matches [the standard](piece_the_standard.md) down to keep [the charter](piece_the_charter.md), surrendering the higher bar it had held because that bar now only loses the base.
Its match forces the first jurisdiction to cut again to stay ahead, and the bar drops a notch with no one having chosen it low.

## Tension

Matching a laxer neighbour to keep the base is defensive and rational, and the rationality is what deepens the descent.
There is nothing aggressive: a standard no base will sit under protects no one. But each match forces the next cut, so the plot closes with the rival's bar lowered, the descent ratcheting, and the party the rival once protected keeping the lower standard the rival never wanted to set.
