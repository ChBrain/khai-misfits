# Piece: The Bottom

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The phenomenon whole, the standard driven to the lowest bar on offer, a floor no jurisdiction chose and none can lift alone, the level everyone would rather was higher and the competition will not let stay there.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Settled across [the market of laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), where the descent comes to rest at the lowest bar.
Lifted, if ever, at [the floor](place_the_floor.md), the one arrangement that raises the bar the competition drove down.

## Load Bearing

Without it the descent is just a series of cuts and no one is trapped at a level they all dislike, and there is no misfit.
With it the trap is complete and authorless: a standard settled at the lowest bar any jurisdiction will offer, chosen by none and held by all, because each lowered rationally and none can raise the bar while the base can move to whoever keeps it lower (Cary, on the race to the bottom, the destination the competition reaches; Winter and Romano, on the boundary, the bottom being where the chooser's interest diverges from the borne). The Bottom is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole: not one cut but a floor no one wanted and none can unilaterally leave, the standard everyone would prefer higher and the mobility of the base will not let them keep there, so the piece carries the lowest bar as the destination of a competition in which every step was a local win.

## Apparent

An equilibrium, a settled competitive market of laws at its efficient level.
Seen at the surface, the Bottom looks like a market at rest, jurisdictions and bases having found their optimum, and the truth that the level is one no jurisdiction chose and all would raise if they could, that the immobile party bears it and cannot leave it, that the competition holds them all at a floor against their own preference, stays hidden behind how much a bottom reached by rational steps looks like an efficient result.
