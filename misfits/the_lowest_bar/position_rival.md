# Position: Rival

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The seat that held a higher standard and must match or undercut a laxer neighbour, where keeping the bar high buys only the loss of the base.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A higher standard it had held and a base it is losing: the bar it set above the neighbour's and the revenue leaving for the laxer terms.
A forced choice: a standard no base will sit under protects no one, so matching the neighbour is the rational move and not an eager one.
No way to keep both the standard and the base, because the base sits where the bar is lowest.

## Orders

Undercut.
Lower your standard to match or beat the laxer neighbour, keep the base and its revenue, because holding the bar higher only loses the base to whoever cut first.
Answer the lowering with your own, since a standard the base will not sit under shields no one anyway.

## Loses

The higher standard it had held, and with it the immobile party that standard protected.
Because a bar the base will not sit under buys only the loss of the base, the seat matches the neighbour and cuts its own standard, and its match forces the first jurisdiction to cut again to stay ahead, so the seat both answers the descent and deepens it, and the third party it once protected keeps the lower bar the seat never wanted to set.
It gains the base kept, and loses the standard it had held and the protection that standard gave.

## Drives

Toward the neighbour matched and the base kept,
the higher bar surrendered because holding it loses the base,
so the seat that undercuts answers each lowering with the next, the descent ratcheting through jurisdictions each doing only what the last made rational.
