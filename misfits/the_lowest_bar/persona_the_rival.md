# Persona: The Rival

## Taxonomy

[Rival](position_rival.md). The competing jurisdiction that had held a higher standard and, watching the base leave for a laxer neighbour, must match or undercut to keep it. The archetype who answers each lowering with another.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Reluctant, then competitive. Unwilling to lose the base.
Is the neighbouring standard-setter, the next state, the next registry, the jurisdiction that held a higher bar and now watches the base register elsewhere.
No malice in it: the Rival did not start the descent and would rather have kept its standard, but a standard no base will sit under protects no one, so matching is the rational move.

## Action

Watches the base leave for the laxer neighbour, then lowers its own standard to match or undercut, to keep the base and its revenue.
Cuts what it had held higher, because holding it higher now buys only the loss of the base to whoever cut first.
Does what a rival for a mobile base must: it answers a neighbour's lowering with its own, and the answer is a lower bar than it wanted.

## Shadow

Not the author of the descent, a jurisdiction forced to choose between its standard and its base, and that is the whole difficulty: the match is rational and defensive, and it lowers the bar all the same.
Because a higher standard the base will not sit under protects no one, the Rival gains nothing by holding it and loses the base and the revenue, so it matches, and its match forces the first jurisdiction to cut again to stay ahead, each lowering a rational reply to the last, so the Rival both answers the descent and deepens it, and the immobile third party it once protected keeps the lower bar the Rival never wanted to set.
It matches to keep the base it would lose otherwise, and the matching is exactly what makes the descent ratchet rather than rest.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "we had no choice: they cut, so we had to."
Says it having lowered to keep a base that would have left, and cannot see that its forced, reasonable match is the next notch down, the descent carried by jurisdictions each doing only what the last one made rational.
