# Piece: The Standard

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The level each jurisdiction sets, the bar the base must meet, the protection that exists for someone and the thing the jurisdictions compete over by lowering.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Set across [the market of laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), where each jurisdiction pitches its bar against the others.
Held, if ever, at [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where a common minimum would keep it from falling further.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing to compete over and nothing to lower, and no protection to remove.
With it the trap has its object: the level a jurisdiction requires of the base, which is at once a cost to the mobile base and a protection to the immobile party, so lowering it wins the base and strips the protection at one stroke (Cary, on the states setting the protections owed by corporations and competing by lowering them). The Standard is load bearing because it is the two-faced thing at the centre: what the base experiences as a cost and the third party relies on as a shield, so the piece carries the bar whose lowering is a win to whoever moves and a loss to whoever cannot, the level every jurisdiction can only set against what its rivals set.

## Apparent

A neutral requirement, a rule of business set at whatever level keeps a place competitive.
Seen at the surface, the Standard looks like a dial of competitiveness, higher or lower as the market prefers, and the truth that its level is a protection for someone who is counting on it, that lowering it to win the base removes that protection, that the party who bears the cost of the low bar is not the base that chose it, stays hidden behind how much a standard looks like a mere term of trade.
