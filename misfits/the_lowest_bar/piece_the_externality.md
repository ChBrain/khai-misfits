# Piece: The Externality

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The harm the lowered standard lays on the immobile third party the standard protected, the divergence of interest between the mobile chooser and the party who cannot follow it, the hinge on which the race turns down rather than up.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Borne wherever the immobile party is, across [the market of laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md) and outside every register.
Lifted, if ever, at [the floor](place_the_floor.md), where a common minimum keeps the protection the descent removed.

## Load Bearing

Without it the lowered standard costs its chooser what it saves and competition can raise the bar, and there is no race to the bottom.
With it the trap has its condition: the lowered standard the base prefers imposes its cost not on the base but on the immobile party the standard protected, so the base gains a saving whose harm it does not bear, and the descent runs down (Cary, on the shareholder protections lowered to please the incorporating managers). And here is the hinge: where the mobile chooser itself bears the cost of a lax standard, the same competition raises the bar instead, a race to the top (Winter, on state law and shareholder protection, competition improving standards where the mobile party internalises the cost; Romano, on the empirical debate over which race a competition for charters runs). The Externality is load bearing because it is what decides the direction: the divergence of interest between the chooser and the borne is the whole condition of the descent, so the piece carries the honest hinge, the race to the bottom being an externality condition and not a law of competition.

## Apparent

A cost of doing business, an unfortunate side effect of a competitive and efficient market.
Seen at the surface, the Externality looks like an incidental loss the market will net out, and the truth that the loss falls by structure on the party who cannot move, that the base is spared exactly the cost the third party keeps, that this divergence is what turns the race down when it might have turned up, stays hidden behind how much an externalised harm looks like a price worth paying for a competitive field.

## Yearbook

Falls on [the bound](persona_the_bound.md), whom [harmonising](process_harmonising.md) alone would spare, the divergence of interest at the heart of the trap.
