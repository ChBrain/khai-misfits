# Position: Jurisdiction

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The seat that sets the standard and competes for the base, where lowering the bar the base dislikes is the lawful instrument for winning it and its revenue.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A standard to set and a franchise revenue to win: the level the base must meet and the fees the base pays wherever it sits.
A real gain: the base is mobile and the revenue genuine, so lowering the standard to attract the base is not corruption but ordinary lawful competition.
No account that carries the cost of the lowering, because the harm falls off its ledger onto the immobile third party the standard protected.

## Orders

Draw them in.
Lower the standard the mobile base would rather not meet, win the registrations and the fees, and read the rising revenue as competitiveness, because a base that can move sits where the standard costs it least.
Cut what the base dislikes, since keeping it high buys only the loss of the base to a laxer rival.

## Loses

The protection the standard gave, exactly by lowering the bar to win the base.
Because the base is mobile and its revenue real, the seat lowers to attract it, and the cost of the lower standard falls not on the seat and not on the base but on the party the standard protected and who cannot follow the base out, so the win the numbers show is booked and the loss they do not show is borne off the ledger.
It gains the base and the revenue, and loses the standard that shielded someone who cannot move.

## Drives

Toward the base won and the standard given away,
the lowering booked as competitiveness and the harm off the ledger,
so the seat that draws them in wins the base and reads the revenue as proof it was right, the cost resting on the immobile third party it never counted.
