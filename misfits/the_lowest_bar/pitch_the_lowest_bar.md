# Pitch: The Lowest Bar

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Competitive, open, good for business.
The plain good sense of a jurisdiction that lowers a standard to attract a base that could go elsewhere, and watches the registrations and the revenue rise, a place that reads as competitive and welcoming.
Nothing false in it on its face: the base is genuinely mobile, the franchise revenue is genuinely real, and each lowering is a rational answer to a rival that lowered first.

## Undertow

Under the competitiveness, a standard that protected someone who cannot move, cut to keep a base that can.
Because a rival that holds a higher standard loses the base to a laxer one, each jurisdiction must match or undercut, so the standard ratchets down toward the lowest, and the harm lands on the shareholders and crews and public who cannot follow the base out.
What reads as attractiveness, a place that asks less of the base, is a protection removed from the immobile third party the standard was for.

## Nerve

The feeling of winning the base, the registrations up and the revenue up, a jurisdiction competitive and open,
sure the lowering is a victory, because every number it watches says so,
while the standard settles at a floor no one chose, and the party the standard protected keeps the cut it never asked for and cannot move away from.

## Echo

After the run: a standard driven to the lowest bar on offer, each jurisdiction having cut rationally to keep a mobile base, none able to hold the standard high while the base could find it low elsewhere,
no villain and no bad faith, only a base free to move, a revenue that follows it, and a harm externalised onto those who cannot follow,
and the one move that would have stopped the descent, a common floor no jurisdiction may cut below, forgone because any jurisdiction can win the base back by quietly cutting under it.
