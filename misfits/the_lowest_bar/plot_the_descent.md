# Plot: The Descent

## Taxonomy

[The Lowest Bar](play_the_lowest_bar.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Market of Laws](place_the_market_of_laws.md), each cut answered by the next across the field.
The lowerings compounding, no jurisdiction able to hold the bar high while the base can find it low next door.

## Action

The competition come to its destination: the answered cuts ratchet the standard down toward the lowest on offer, [the descent](piece_the_descent.md) settling into [the bottom](piece_the_bottom.md).

## Stage

Each jurisdiction, having lowered [the standard](piece_the_standard.md) rationally to hold [the charter](piece_the_charter.md), finds the bar dropped again by a rival, and the ratchet runs down until the standard rests at the lowest any will offer.
Only here does it show that the sum of the local wins is a floor no one chose, the harm settled on the immobile parties the standard had protected and no villain anywhere in the fall.

## Tension

Every cut is a rational answer to the last, and the rationality is exactly why the bottom is reached without anyone choosing it.
There is no fault in the field: each jurisdiction lowered to keep a base that would otherwise leave. But nothing could hold the bar high alone, so the plot closes with the standard settled at the lowest bar, the level everyone would rather was higher, and the mobility of the base the reason none can raise it.
