# Persona: The Mobile

## Taxonomy

[Mobile](position_mobile.md). The chartered or registered base that can move, and sits where the standard costs it least, carrying its revenue to whichever jurisdiction asks the least of it. The archetype whose freedom to move is the engine of the descent.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Free. Rational. Choosing the best terms on offer.
Is the base that can relocate, the company choosing where to charter, the ship choosing whose flag to fly, the firm choosing where to book its capital, the pool of capital choosing its jurisdiction.
No wrongdoing in it: the Mobile is entitled to register where it likes and reasonable to prefer the terms that cost it least, and moving to better terms is lawful and ordinary.

## Action

Registers where the standard asks the least of it, and carries its revenue there, moving to whichever jurisdiction offers the laxer terms.
Compares the standards on offer and sits under the lowest, because the lower standard costs it less to meet.
Does what a mobile base does: it chooses its jurisdiction by the standard it prefers, and the choice of jurisdiction is a choice of standard.

## Shadow

Not a bad actor, a base exercising an ordinary freedom to sit where its terms are best, and that is the whole difficulty: the freedom is legitimate, and the exercise of it is what forces each jurisdiction to compete on the standard.
Because the Mobile will sit where the standard costs it least, every jurisdiction that wants it must lower the standard to hold it, so the base's lawful choice is the pressure that ratchets the standard down, and the cost of the lower standard the base prefers is borne not by the base but by the immobile third party who cannot move with it, whose interest in the standard diverges from the Mobile's.
It moves to the best terms in good faith, and the good faith is exactly what makes the descent no one's fault and everyone's pressure.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "we went where the terms were best, as anyone would."
Says it having moved lawfully to the laxest standard, and cannot see that its ordinary choice is the engine that makes each jurisdiction lower the bar, the cost of the terms it prefers landing on those who cannot follow it out.
