# Pitch: The Defence Does the Damage

## Taxonomy

[In Its Own Defence](play_in_its_own_defence.md). The single hook: the immune response escalates in correct proportion to a threat, but its weapons damage the host's own tissue and it amplifies past the invader, so the body can be destroyed by the very defence that clears the infection, and the only brake also disarms the guard the threat still needs.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A strong response to a real threat is what keeps the patient alive.
The tenor is sound defence: an infection met with too little force is an infection that wins, so the immune system's escalation, more force for more invader, is exactly right, the strength of the response the measure of its fitness for the danger.

## Undertow

The weapons cannot spare what they defend.
Underneath, the inflammation, the cell-killing, and the flood of signals act on the host as on the pathogen, so the same force that clears the invader wrecks the tissue around it, the collateral not a malfunction but the ordinary working of the guard doing its job.

## Nerve

The response amplifies past the threat that triggered it.
The nerve is that each wave of defenders summons the next and each signal raises the signal, a positive feedback with no fast brake, so a defence proportioned at the start runs past proportion, and the host can die of the response with the invader all but cleared, killed by the force it raised.

## Echo

The only brake also disarms the guard.
What lingers is that the mechanism that wrecks the tissue is the mechanism that clears the pathogen, so suppressing the harm blunts the protection, on a knife-edge where too much lets the infection win and too little lets the tissue burn, the defence never made safe, only weaker against an unbeaten threat.
