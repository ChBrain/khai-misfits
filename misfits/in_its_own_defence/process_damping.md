# Process: Damping

## Taxonomy

[In Its Own Defence](play_in_its_own_defence.md). The suppression of the response to spare the tissue, the one brake that acts on the whole defence, so it lowers the protection with the harm.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Clinician](persona_the_clinician.md), at [the Ward](place_the_ward.md), titrating the suppression between two failures.
Begins when a reader of the case would slow the response to save the tissue, and reaches for the only lever there is.

## Direction

Turns a runaway defence into a weakened one, harm and protection together.
Because suppression acts on the whole response and the response is both the harm and the clearing, damping lowers the storm only by lowering the guard, so it converts an overshooting defence not into a safe one but into a lesser one, sparing the tissue exactly to the degree it disarms the body against a threat that may not be beaten (Tisoncik, Korth, Simmons, Farrar, Martin and Katze).

## Lever

A brake on the whole, and none on the part.
Because the harmful force and the protective force are the same force, damping works through a lever that cannot select: every measure that eases the tissue blunts the clearing, so the lever is the suppression itself, which reaches the collateral only by reaching the protection, no dose dividing the response into a part to keep and a part to stop.

## Echo

A tissue spared and a guard weakened, on a setting with no safety in it.
The echo of damping is a response held lower, the storm eased and the infection given room, so the remedy buys the tissue at the cost of the clearing and leaves the case balanced between two failures, the right amount unknown until too late and shifting as the battle does. Decades of trials aimed at blocking the harmful response have not found the dose that spares the host without disabling its defence (Angus and van der Poll), so the echo is a trade paid and never a harm undone.
