# Persona: The Clinician

## Taxonomy

[The Brake](position_the_brake.md). Would damp the response to spare the tissue, and holds only one lever, suppression, that blunts the protection along with the harm, so it must titrate a remedy that is also a disarming, blind and against the clock.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Watching two harms at once. Reaching for a brake that is also a poison. Dosing against the clock.
Is the one who sees the response wrecking the tissue and the pathogen not yet cleared, and holds a suppression that would slow the first only by weakening the second, with no setting that does one without the other.
No cruelty and no genius wished on them: the harm is not the Clinician's doing and there is no dose that escapes the trade, so the one who would help arrives holding a lever that helps and harms in the same motion, and must choose how much of each.

## Action

Titrates the suppression between two failures, easing the storm without lifting the guard so far that the infection wins, judging by the hour and the sign because the right dose cannot be known in advance and shifts as the battle does.
Trades one harm for a risk of the other, since the only brake acts on the whole response, so every milligram that spares the tissue also blunts the clearing, and the Clinician spends the difference with no safe place to stand.
Does what managing a defence-turned-harm requires: not a cure but a balance held under time, dampening enough to save the tissue and not so much as to lose the patient to the pathogen, a knife-edge walked in the dark.

## Shadow

The particular dread of it is that there is no move that only helps: whatever the Clinician does, some harm follows, so the choice is never between right and wrong but between two ways to lose, and the weight of it is having to choose anyway, by the hour, with a life on the scale.
What wears on them is the blindness of the timing. The dose that is too little now is too much in an hour, and the sign that would tell them which comes late or not at all, so they must act before they can know and will learn whether they were right only from what happens next, carrying the whole responsibility for a call that no amount of skill can make safe, only less unsafe.
The bind is interior and exposed: they must intervene because the defence is killing the tissue, hold a lever that disarms the body it would spare, and answer for the outcome whichever way it breaks, blamed for the infection if they suppress and for the ruin if they do not, standing between two harms with a hand that cannot reach one without feeding the other.

## Tell

Says "I can slow the fire or keep the guard, not both."
Is the whole knife-edge in one line, since slowing the fire is lifting the guard and keeping the guard is feeding the fire, so the sentence that sounds like a hard choice is the plain shape of the only lever there is, a brake that damps the harm and the protection together, leaving the Clinician to pick a point on a scale where every point costs.
