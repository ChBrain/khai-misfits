# Place: The Tissue

## Taxonomy

[In Its Own Defence](play_in_its_own_defence.md). The living ground of the body, both the thing the defence protects and the field it is fought across, where the pathogen is met and the host is wrecked by the same force in the same place.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A body under defence, the fight brought to where the infection sits.
Shown at the surface is protection in progress: the response arrived where it is needed, the invader engaged in the very tissue it threatened, the guard doing its work at the site of the danger, exactly where a defence should be.

## Holds

Holds the invader and the host in one place, and the force that strikes both.
The Tissue is where the pathogen and the body's own cells sit together and where the response falls on them together, so it holds the meeting of threat and defence and the impossibility of aiming one without the other, the field on which protecting and wrecking are the same act because they happen in the same ground.

## Offers

Offers the defence exactly where it is needed, and the damage exactly there too.
Here the response reaches the infection at its site, so the Tissue offers the protection of a guard that fights where the threat is, and offers, in the same place and the same blow, the ruin of the host: the weapons that clear the invader from the tissue clear the tissue with it, so the ground gets its defence and its wound as one thing, delivered to the same cells.

## Withheld

Withholds any separation of the protected from the field.
What the Tissue cannot offer is a place to stand apart from the battle: the body is the ground, so there is no part of it that is defended without being fought across, no cell the response can spare while striking the invader beside it. The place that holds the defence holds no shelter from it, present as both the prize and the terrain, so the harm cannot be moved off the host because the host is where the threat is, and the guard fights the only place it can, which is the body itself.
