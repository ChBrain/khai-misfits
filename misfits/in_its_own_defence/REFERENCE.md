# In Its Own Defence: Reference

The research warrant for [In Its Own Defence](play_in_its_own_defence.md).
The dynamic is immunopathology: Zinkernagel and Doherty's finding that the immune response, not the pathogen, can
kill the host, Rouse and Sehrawat on the balance between protection and immunopathology, Tisoncik and colleagues on the
cytokine storm, Medzhitov, Schneider and Soares on tolerance versus resistance, and Angus and van der Poll on the
dysregulated host response of sepsis, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging immunopathology as a **the-defence-does-the-damage structure**: an infection triggers an immune response scaled
to the threat, more invader met with more force, which is correct because a danger under-met wins; but the weapons of
the response, the inflammation, the cell-killing, the flood of signals, act on the host's own tissue as on the pathogen,
so the same force that clears the invader wrecks the body, and because the response amplifies itself through a positive
feedback with no fast brake, a defence proportioned to a real threat can run past it and destroy the host beyond what the
pathogen would. The play models the documented mechanics, the proportioning that makes a large response a correct one,
the collateral the weapons deal by their ordinary working, the storm that carries the response past its cause, and the
suppression that can lower the harm only by lowering the protection, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as
a tale of a careless clinician or a malfunctioning guard. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing
law: in some infections the host's own immune response, rather than the pathogen, is the proximate cause of tissue damage
and death, the defence overshooting into the harm it was raised to prevent. It is a tendency under stated conditions and
not an iron rule, holding where the response is strong, its weapons non-specific, and its feedback unchecked, and sparing
where the defence is measured or the pathogen mild, so the play stages the overshoot a proportioned defence can run into,
not a fate of every immune response.

It is distinct from the house's First Do Harm, which stages iatrogenesis, the harm done by a remedy under the pressure to
act: there the damage comes from an external intervention, a treatment delivered by a clinician, creating a new pathology
in a new place, and the engine is the drive to do something. Here nothing is done to the body from outside: the harm is
the body's own defence, aimed at the same threat, falling in the same tissue, so the engine is the response overshooting
and not a remedy misfiring, and the clinician is not the cause of the harm but the one facing a suppression that cannot
spare the tissue without disarming the guard. It is distinct too from Alarm Fatigue, whose engine is a true signal
dulled by over-warning into under-response, in that immunopathology is the mirror, a response correctly triggered and too
strong rather than a warning tuned out, the harm of too much defence and not too little.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a careless clinician or a
  malfunctioning guard, because the source finding is precisely that no error and no malice is needed: the response
  escalates because a threat under-met wins (Zinkernagel and Doherty), the weapons harm the tissue because that is what
  they are, acting on host and pathogen alike (Rouse and Sehrawat), and the amplification is the summoning mechanism
  working, not a fault (Tisoncik and colleagues). So the misfit is that a defence proportioned to a real threat destroys
  the host with the same weapons it clears the invader, no misfire and no malice required, each escalation correct and
  each weapon doing its job and the ruin their sum, the guard that meets a threat most fully the one that wounds the body
  most.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a body saved by a clean intervention. The one move that would
  stop the harm is to suppress the response, but the response is also what clears the pathogen, so damping the harm blunts
  the protection, on a knife-edge where too much loses the patient to the infection and too little to the storm, the right
  dose unknown until too late and shifting as the battle does (Tisoncik and colleagues). Decades of trials aimed at
  blocking the harmful response have failed to spare the host without disabling its defence (Angus and van der Poll), so
  the remedy is a balance held between two failures and never a cure, the defence made weaker but never safe, not a way to
  keep the protection and lose the harm.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the finding that the immune response can be the
  cause of the damage is Zinkernagel and Doherty's, the protection-versus-immunopathology balance is Rouse and
  Sehrawat's, the cytokine storm and its therapeutic knife-edge are Tisoncik and colleagues', the resistance-versus-
  tolerance framing is Medzhitov, Schneider and Soares's, and the dysregulated host response of sepsis is Angus and van
  der Poll's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages a defence that destroys what it defends
  and credits that no one was careless and the guard did its job, holding it a tendency under stated conditions and not a
  law that fixes every response at the harm.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Overkill](piece_the_overkill.md)**: carries the finding whole (Zinkernagel and Doherty), the response
  overshooting into the harm it was raised to prevent, the host failing of its own defence with the invader all but
  cleared.
- **[The Proportion](piece_the_proportion.md)**: carries the scaling of force to threat (Zinkernagel and Doherty), the
  rule that makes a large response correct and the reason it cannot stop short of the harm.
- **[The Collateral](piece_the_collateral.md)**: carries the host damage of the defence (Rouse and Sehrawat), the
  weapons acting on the tissue as on the pathogen, and the cost of clearing that tolerance would limit (Medzhitov,
  Schneider and Soares).
- **[The Storm](piece_the_storm.md)**: carries the cytokine storm (Tisoncik and colleagues), the positive feedback that
  amplifies the response body-wide past its cause with no fast brake.
- **[The Brake](position_the_brake.md)** and **[Damping](process_damping.md)**: carry the therapeutic knife-edge
  (Tisoncik and colleagues; Angus and van der Poll), the suppression that lowers the harm only by lowering the protection.
