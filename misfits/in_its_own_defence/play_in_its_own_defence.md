# Play: In Its Own Defence

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

In Its Own Defence.
An infection arrives, and the body defends itself. The immune system senses the threat and mounts a response scaled to it: the greater the invader, the greater the force brought against it, which is exactly right, because a threat met with too little force is a threat that wins. But the weapons of the defence are not precise. The inflammation that walls off the infection also swells and starves the tissue around it; the cells sent to kill the infected kill infected cells, which are the body's own; the signals that summon reinforcements, in summoning them, make the vessels leak and the organs fail. And the response feeds itself: each wave of defenders calls the next and each signal raises the signal, so a defence proportioned to a real threat can amplify past it, until the body is harmed not by the invader but by the force raised against it. The pathogen may be all but cleared and the patient failing still, dying of their own defence. No cell did wrong. Every part of the response did precisely its job, met the threat it was built to meet, and the sum of all those correct responses was the wrecking of the thing they defended. And the only way to stop it is to call off the defence, which means disarming the body against an invader that may not yet be beaten, so the one move that spares the tissue is the move that lets the infection back in, and there is no dose that stops the harm without also stopping the protection, because the harm and the protection are the same act.

## Arc

The bend is this: the immune response, escalating in correct proportion to a threat, damages the host's own tissue with the same weapons it turns on the pathogen, and because the response amplifies itself, a defence proportioned to a real threat can destroy the host beyond what the pathogen would, the defence itself the disease (Zinkernagel and Doherty).
It runs on a response scaled to the threat.
Because a threat met with too little force wins, the immune system escalates in proportion to what it detects, more invader answered with more defence, so the response is correct exactly insofar as it is large, its strength the measure of its fitness for the threat (Medzhitov, Schneider and Soares). The scaling is sound, and it is what makes a large response a right one.
And the weapons are not precise.
Because the means of defence, the inflammation, the cell-killing, the flood of signals, act on the host as well as the pathogen, the response damages the tissue with the same force it turns on the invader, so the collateral is not a malfunction but the ordinary working of the guard, the body harmed by its own defence doing exactly its job (Rouse and Sehrawat). The imprecision is built in, and it makes every defence a cost.
And the response feeds itself.
Because each wave of defenders summons the next and each signal raises the signal, the defence can amplify beyond the threat that triggered it, a positive feedback with no fast brake, so a response proportioned at the start runs past proportion, the storm outgrowing its cause (Tisoncik, Korth, Simmons, Farrar, Martin and Katze). The amplification is the same mechanism as the summoning, and it has no quick off-switch.
And the defence can kill what the pathogen would not.
Because the amplified response destroys tissue faster and wider than the infection, the host can fail with the invader all but cleared, the cause of the ruin the force raised against the threat and not the threat itself, so the defence overshoots into the very harm it was raised to prevent (Zinkernagel and Doherty). The overshoot is real, and it is made of nothing but successful defending.
And calling it off means disarming the host.
Because the only way to stop the damage is to suppress the response, and the response is also what clears the pathogen, damping the harm blunts the protection, so any remedy trades the immunopathology for uncontrolled infection on a knife-edge of timing and dose (Tisoncik, Korth, Simmons, Farrar, Martin and Katze). The trade is unavoidable, and it has no safe setting.
And the harm and the protection are one act.
Because the mechanism that wrecks the tissue is the mechanism that clears the pathogen, there is no dose that stops the one without stopping the other, so the defence cannot be made safe, only weaker, and weaker against a threat that may not yet be beaten (Rouse and Sehrawat). The identity is the trap's floor, and it is why there is no clean save.
None of it is a fix. The response escalates because a threat needs meeting, the weapons harm the tissue because that is what they are, the signal amplifies because that is how reinforcements are called, so each part of the defence does exactly its job, and the wrecking of the host emerges from the sum, no misfire and no malice required. Halt it, and the halt is dear: it means suppressing the very defence the body needs, on a knife-edge where too much lets the pathogen win and too little lets the tissue burn, and even timed well the suppression weakens the guard against an unbeaten threat, so the one move that spares the tissue is the move that disarms the host. Short of it the defence, correct in every part, runs past the threat it answers and destroys what it defends, the body harmed in its own defence not by any failure of the guard but by the guard succeeding, wave on correct wave, past the point where success and ruin are the same.

## Stakes

Whether a strong immune response to a real threat is the protection it appears, or is in part the harm, the body wrecked by the same defence that clears the invader because the two are one act.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the overkill, which the Defender is right to escalate against a real threat, and the response right to summon the reinforcements a danger needs, and away from the suppression that would spare the tissue only by disarming the guard. The production cannot make the defence a malfunction, because each part of the response does exactly its job and the weapons harm the tissue by their ordinary working and the amplification is the summoning itself, so mounting the response and calling the reinforcements and clearing the pathogen are each only the guard succeeding. It can only show that a defence proportioned to a threat can amplify past it and destroy with the same weapons it protects with, so the host tends to be harmed by the very response that saves it and can die of the defence with the invader all but gone, and that the one move which would stop the harm, suppressing the response, is the move that also stops the protection, on a knife-edge with no safe setting, so the body is wrecked in its own defence not by any failure of the guard but by the guard doing exactly what a threat requires, past the point where the saving and the ruin are the same act.
