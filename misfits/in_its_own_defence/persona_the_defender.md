# Persona: The Defender

## Taxonomy

[The Response](position_the_response.md). Mounts the body's defence and escalates it in proportion to the threat, meeting more invader with more force, and by the ordinary working of its weapons damages the tissue it defends, doing exactly its job and destroying what the job was for.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Vigilant and unsparing. Meeting the threat with everything it needs. Sure that force is protection.
Is the one that answers an infection with a defence scaled to it, escalating without hesitation because a threat met with too little is a threat that wins, and reads its own strength as the measure of how well it guards.
No recklessness wished on them: the escalation is correct, since under-meeting a real danger loses the body, so the Defender that brings full force is doing precisely what defence requires and what a lesser response would fail to do.

## Action

Raises the response to match the threat, summons each wave of reinforcements the last one calls for, and turns its weapons on the invader with the whole force the danger warrants.
Reads its success the only way a guard can: the threat engaged, the force brought, the invader met, so more response is more defence and holding back is only leaving the body exposed.
Does what a defence does against a real threat: escalates, amplifies, and strikes with everything, gaining the whole protection of an unstinting guard and with it the one thing it cannot help, harming the tissue its weapons cannot spare.

## Shadow

From the inside it feels like duty, pure and uncomplicated: there is a threat, and a threat is met, and to meet it with less than everything would be a kind of betrayal of the body it guards, so restraint does not feel like prudence but like abandoning the wall mid-assault.
What it cannot feel is the tissue behind it. Its whole attention is forward, on the invader, on the next wave to summon and the next signal to raise, so the damage it does to what it defends happens in a place it never looks, and there is no inward register in which its own success reads as harm, only the clean and total conviction of a guard that has never once left a threat unmet.
The bind is interior and zealous: it must give everything to a real danger, feels every escalation as rightness and every hesitation as failure, and carries no sense at all that the force it is proudest of is the force wrecking the body, sure to the last wave that it is saving what it is destroying.

## Tell

Says "whatever it takes, and it takes everything."
Is the whole overshoot in one phrase, since whatever-it-takes is exactly the right creed against a threat and exactly the one that cannot stop short of the harm, so the words that sound like devotion are the sound of a defence that measures itself by its force and so can never find the point past which the force turns on the body, giving everything and unable to see that everything is too much.
