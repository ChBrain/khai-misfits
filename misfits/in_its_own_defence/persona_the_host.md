# Persona: The Host

## Taxonomy

[The Besieged](position_the_besieged.md). The body the defence is raised to protect and the ground it is fought on, defended and damaged by the same response, unable to command or call off the guard that is destroying it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Under siege from within. Depending on the defence that harms it. Unable to call it off.
Is the one whose tissue is both the thing protected and the field the battle wrecks, needing the response to clear the invader and suffering that same response as it does, with no lever to raise or lower the guard.
No weakness wished on them: the body is not failing at defence, it is defending too well, so the Host is harmed not by a guard that fell short but by one that gave everything, the very success of the response the source of the ruin.

## Action

Endures the response it cannot direct, absorbing the collateral of its own defence as the price of being defended, since the force clearing the pathogen is the force wrecking the tissue and the body has no way to take one without the other.
Bears the siege from both sides at once, the invader and the guard, with the guard often the heavier, and cannot stand the response down because command over it was never the body's to hold.
Does what the defended can do, which is little: it hosts the battle, needs the defence, suffers it, and waits on an outcome it cannot steer, alive only if the guard wins before the guard has ruined too much.

## Shadow

There is a particular terror in being destroyed by your own protector, worse than an enemy's harm because it wears the face of rescue and cannot be resisted without dying the other way: the body cannot hate the guard that is killing it, because the guard is right, and is itself, and is all that stands between it and the invader.
What sits under it is helplessness of the most intimate kind. The harm is not done to the Host by something outside it but by the Host's own defence, so there is no separation to take shelter in, no distance from the thing destroying it, only the strange grief of failing by one's own success, of being the ground on which one's own guard wins a battle that leaves nothing standing.
The bind is interior and inescapable: it must be defended to live and is wrecked by the defending, holds no command over the response that is its own, and can only endure a rescue that is also a ruin, dying, if it dies, not of the enemy but of the force raised to save it, unable even to call that force wrong.

## Tell

Says "it is saving me, and I cannot survive the saving."
Is the whole trap in one sentence, since both halves are true at once and neither cancels the other, so the words that sound like a contradiction are the plain condition of the defended body, kept alive by a response that is killing it, needing more of exactly the thing that is too much, with no way to want less of its own defence and live.
