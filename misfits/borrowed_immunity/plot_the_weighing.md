# Plot: The Weighing

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Clinic](place_the_clinic.md), a shot offered and a herd already covering the one deciding.
The Individual about to weigh the vaccine's cost against a protection the coverage already supplies, the disease nowhere in sight.

## Action

[Weighing](process_weighing.md): [The Individual](persona_the_individual.md), holding [the individual's seat](position_individual.md), sets the shot's personal cost against the marginal protection it would add, given the herd.

## Stage

[The Individual](persona_the_individual.md), on [Weigh the Shot](plan_weigh_the_shot.md), sets [the private cost](piece_the_private_cost.md) of the vaccine against [the herd effect](piece_the_herd_effect.md) that [the coverage](piece_the_coverage.md) already supplies past [the threshold](piece_the_threshold.md).
With the herd high the marginal benefit of their own shot is small and its cost is real, so the honest reckoning finds the shot's cost the heavier side, the disease held off by the others invisible on the scale.

## Tension

Weighing a real cost against a benefit the herd already supplies is not recklessness, and the honest reckoning is what makes the shot look like a cost worth avoiding.
There is nothing careless: the calculation is sound and the herd genuinely covers them. But a benefit already supplied is a benefit not worth paying twice for, so the plot closes with the shot weighed and its cost found the heavier side, the individual right about their own case and the protection they are counting on the very thing they are declining to renew.
