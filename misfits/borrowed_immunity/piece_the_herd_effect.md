# Piece: The Herd Effect

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md). The protection the coverage confers on those not themselves immunised, the good that reaches contributor and abstainer alike and, by reaching the abstainer, makes abstention rational.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Lives across [the population](place_the_population.md), where the coverage shields everyone behind the line.
Borrowed at [the clinic](place_the_clinic.md), where the individual counts on it and declines the shot.

## Load Bearing

Without it a shot protects only its taker and there is nothing to borrow, and no reason to decline but one's own risk.
With it the trap has its good: the protection the coverage confers on those who did not take a shot, the shield that, past the threshold, reaches the unvaccinated as fully as the vaccinated (Fine, on herd immunity, the indirect protection of the susceptible by the immunity of those around them). The Herd Effect is load bearing because it is the benefit that is borrowed: the good the individual receives whether or not they contribute, so the piece carries the protection whose non-excludability is the whole engine, a shield that reaches the abstainer exactly as it reaches the contributor and thereby makes the free-ride the sound choice.

## Apparent

Simple safety, a disease that cannot reach you, protection you can count on.
Seen at the surface, the Herd Effect looks like a guarantee, a protection securely in place and owed to no one in particular, and the truth that it is produced by contributions each can decline, that borrowing it is what makes each decline rational, that a shield reaching everyone is a shield each is free to stop holding up, stays hidden behind how solid a protection feels while enough others are still paying for it.
