# Persona: The Susceptible

## Taxonomy

[Susceptible](position_susceptible.md). The one who cannot be vaccinated at all and so depends wholly on the threshold, protected only for as long as the herd stays high, and the first the disease reaches when it slips. The one who bears the cost of a calculation they are not in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Exposed. Dependent. Doing everything asked and able to do nothing.
Is the one who can take no shot, the newborn too young, the immunocompromised whose body cannot bear the vaccine, the one for whom the shot is not an option to weigh.
No choice in it: the Susceptible does not decline the shot, they are refused it by their own body, and their protection is entirely other people's coverage.

## Action

Depends on the threshold, protected precisely as long as the herd stays above it and exposed the moment it slips.
Takes no shot, weighs no cost, and can do nothing to raise the coverage they live on.
Does what the wholly dependent do: it relies, having no other option, on a line held up by contributions everyone else is free to withhold.

## Shadow

Not a victim of anyone's malice, a person wholly reliant on a protection others may each rationally decline to renew, and that is the whole difficulty: they are safe only for as long as enough strangers keep giving a shot they themselves can never receive.
Because the Susceptible's whole protection is the herd's coverage, they are carried, invisibly, by every contribution the others make, and when the coverage thins to the point of slipping below the threshold, the disease returns to them first and hardest, so the one who could do nothing bears the cost of a calculation that was never wrong for anyone who made it.
It depends on the line entirely, and the line depends on shots that everyone else is free, and rational, to stop giving.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "I took every shot I was allowed and could take none of the one that matters."
Says it wholly dependent on the herd's coverage, and cannot raise it, guard it, or be told it is slipping, because the herd doing its job is a disease no one can see, right up until it reaches the one who was never protected by anything but the others.
