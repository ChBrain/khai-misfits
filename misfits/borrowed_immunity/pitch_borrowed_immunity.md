# Pitch: Borrowed Immunity

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Reasonable, reassured, unalarmed.
The plain good sense of a person who sees the disease gone, the neighbours already covered, and weighs one small shot against a protection the herd already supplies.
Nothing false in it on its face: the shot carries a real cost, the herd really does cover them, and declining is the honest arithmetic of a benefit already in hand.

## Undertow

Under the good sense, a threshold public good whose own success is what makes each further contribution feel redundant.
Because a herd high enough to protect everyone makes each individual's shot buy little to that individual while its cost stays real, rational self-interest points to sitting out, and one by one the coverage thins until it slips below the line and the disease returns, first to the ones who could never be vaccinated at all.
What makes the protection worth having, that it covers everyone once enough contribute, is what makes each contribution feel unnecessary once enough have made it.

## Nerve

The feeling of declining a shot with the disease nowhere in sight, the neighbours covered and the risk of the shot the only visible risk,
sure you lose nothing, because the herd already shields you and your one shot would add so little,
while every other individual reasons the same in good faith, each right about their own case, and the coverage that held the line thins shot by unrenewed shot toward the threshold it needs to stay above.

## Echo

After the run: coverage fallen quietly below the line and the disease come back, first and hardest to the infant and the immunocompromised who could take no shot at all,
no one reckless and no one a cheat, only a protection whose success made each contribution to it feel redundant exactly when it was most collectively needed,
and the one move that would have held the line, sustaining the coverage by outreach and mandate and trust, forgone because it costs continuously to chase a coverage no visible threat is asking for.
