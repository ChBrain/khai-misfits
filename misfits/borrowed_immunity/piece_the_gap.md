# Piece: The Gap

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md). The structural shortfall between the coverage self-interested individuals choose and the coverage that would protect the group, a distance built into the incentives and not into anyone's ignorance.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Grows across [the population](place_the_population.md), where the voluntary coverage settles below the protective level.
Read at [the ledger](place_the_ledger.md), where the shortfall shows as a number before it shows as an outbreak.

## Load Bearing

Without it the voluntary coverage would reach the level that protects the group, and there would be no trap to stage.
With it the trap has its measure: the distance between the coverage a population of rational individuals settles at and the coverage that would keep the disease out, a shortfall that persists because each individual's incentive to vaccinate falls exactly as others' coverage rises (Bauch & Earn, on the structural gap between self-interested and group-optimal coverage; Fine & Clarkson, on the divergence of individual and public priorities in optimal vaccination). The Gap is load bearing because it is the shortfall that no one chooses: not a failure of will but the settled distance between the private optimum and the public one, so the piece carries the space between what is best for each and what is best for all, built into the arithmetic and closed only from outside it.

## Apparent

A small margin, a coverage nearly high enough, a shortfall that looks like almost there.
Seen at the surface, the Gap looks like a minor deficit, a coverage a few points shy that a little more effort would close, and the truth that it is structural and not incidental, that it reopens as fast as it is closed because the incentive that opens it is fixed in the arithmetic, that voluntary choice can never on its own reach the protective level, stays hidden behind how close to enough a coverage just short of the line looks.
