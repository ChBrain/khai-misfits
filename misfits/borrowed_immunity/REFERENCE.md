# Borrowed Immunity: Reference

The research warrant for [Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md).
The dynamic is the free-rider problem of voluntary vaccination over a threshold public good: Bauch and Earn's
game theory of vaccination, Fine and Clarkson's divergence of individual and public optima, Fine's herd immunity,
and Anderson and May's herd-immunity threshold, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging voluntary vaccination as a **self-erasing public good**: shots build a shared protection, herd immunity,
that above a coverage threshold stops the pathogen spreading and shields even the unvaccinated, and because the
herd's protection is a good each individual receives whether or not they contribute, once coverage is high the
marginal benefit of one's own shot falls toward nothing while its personal cost, real if slight, stays exactly
what it was, so rational self-interest points to declining, coverage settles below the socially optimal level, and
as more decline it slips below the threshold and the disease returns. The play models the documented mechanics,
the coverage that is the fraction immunised, the threshold above which transmission cannot be sustained, the herd
effect that confers protection on those not themselves immunised, the private cost of the shot that makes
free-riding rational once the herd is high, the structural gap between the coverage individuals choose and the
coverage that would protect them, and the return of the disease as success thins the coverage that success
required, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of ignorance or anti-vaccine malice. The
governing finding of the sources is the play's governing law: under voluntary vaccination and any perceived
vaccine cost, self-interested individuals can never reach the group-optimal coverage, because each individual's
incentive to vaccinate falls exactly as others' coverage rises, so the voluntary equilibrium sits structurally
below the coverage that protects the group, and the programme's own success is what dissolves the incentive that
produced it.

It is distinct from the house's The Commons, and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: there each
user draws down a depletable shared stock, a pasture or a fishery, and the loss is over-extraction of something
finite that everyone takes from; here nothing is drawn down and the shared thing is a threshold public good that
each abstainer withholds a contribution to rather than depletes, so the engine is not consumption of a stock but
the withholding of a contribution the herd's own success has made feel redundant, a protection that erases the
incentive that built it. It is distinct from Social Loafing, where each member of a shared effort quietly reduces
their input as the group grows and their marginal contribution feels lost in the whole: there the slackening is a
dilution of motivation across a joint task with no threshold and no self-erasing success, here the abstention is a
strict cost-benefit over a public good with a sharp threshold, driven by the good's own high coverage and biting
only in the low-prevalence regime that success creates. And it is distinct from Rose's prevention paradox, which
turns on a preventive measure benefiting a whole population greatly while offering little to each individual who
adopts it: there the small per-person benefit is the whole story, here the engine is threshold free-riding on a
protection others produce and the dissolving of the incentive by the coverage's own success, not the smallness of
the personal benefit alone.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the individuals as
  reckless or anti-vaccine or the herd as a shirking mob, because the source finding is precisely that no one errs:
  each individual weighs a real personal cost against a benefit the herd already supplies, and chooses as any
  rational person would. The falling coverage is authored by no one and emerges from a threshold public good and
  the inverse relation between coverage and the marginal private incentive, so the misfit is that a protection high
  enough to shield everyone is high enough to make each further contribution feel redundant, and a disease held off
  by the herd is a disease unseen by the one deciding whether to add to it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that holds the line
  is to keep coverage above the threshold by outreach, reminder, mandate, and the building of trust that carries
  people past their own arithmetic, and it means paying, continuously, to sustain a contribution each individual
  can always rationally decline, against a disease that stays invisible precisely while the effort is working. The
  reckoning is a line held only by carrying, forever, the cost of asking people to give a shot the herd has already
  made feel unnecessary, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the game theory of voluntary vaccination and the structural gap between
  self-interested and group-optimal coverage are Bauch and Earn's, the divergence of individual and public optima
  is Fine and Clarkson's, herd immunity and the protection of the unvaccinated are Fine's, and the herd-immunity
  threshold 1 minus 1/R0 is Anderson and May's; the honest boundary, that the trap bites only in the low-prevalence
  regime and that altruism, mandates, or trust can close the gap at a cost, is carried as carefully as the finding
  itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Coverage](piece_the_coverage.md)**: carries the fraction immunised (Fine), the shared protection built
  shot by shot and thinned shot by unrenewed shot.
- **[The Threshold](piece_the_threshold.md)**: carries the herd-immunity threshold 1 minus 1/R0 (Anderson & May),
  the coverage above which the pathogen cannot spread.
- **[The Herd Effect](piece_the_herd_effect.md)**: carries the protection of the unvaccinated (Fine), the good the
  coverage confers on those who did not contribute it.
- **[The Private Cost](piece_the_private_cost.md)**: carries the perceived cost of the shot (Bauch & Earn), the
  real if slight personal cost that makes free-riding rational once the herd is high.
- **[The Gap](piece_the_gap.md)**: carries the structural shortfall (Bauch & Earn; Fine & Clarkson), the distance
  between the coverage individuals choose and the coverage that would protect them.
- **[The Return](piece_the_return.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Bauch & Earn), coverage slipping below the
  threshold as success breeds free-riding, and the disease coming back to the ones who could never be covered.
