# Process: Free-Riding

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md). The Individual declining the shot under the herd's protection, the rational abstention that borrows a benefit others produced and, summed, thins the coverage toward the threshold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A herd high enough to make the shot feel redundant, and a cost still real.
Across [the population](place_the_population.md), the individual taking the protection the coverage supplies and declining the shot that would add to it.

## Direction

Toward the protection borrowed and the contribution withheld.
Because the herd already shields the individual, free-riding takes the benefit the coverage confers and skips the cost of the shot, so each abstainer is no worse protected while the line holds, and as the visible risk falls more decline, the coverage thinning shot by unrenewed shot, the sound choice of each summing into the fall of the protection all were counting on.

## Lever

The rationality of declining a benefit already supplied.
Because the herd's protection reaches the abstainer whether or not they contribute, free-riding runs not on any bad faith but on the plain sense of not paying for what one already has, the falling coverage assembled from no cheating, only from each individual rationally borrowing a protection the herd supplies and declining, in good faith, to renew it.

## Echo

A shot skipped, and the coverage thinned.
The individual has taken the herd's protection and kept the shot's cost, so what reads as one harmless decline is, in truth, one of the many that sum, each sound on its own, into a coverage sliding toward the threshold it needs to stay above, the free-ride costing nothing to the rider and, in aggregate, the herd its height.
