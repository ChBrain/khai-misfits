# Play: Borrowed Immunity

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Borrowed Immunity.
Someone declines the shot, and it is a reasonable thing to decline: the disease is nowhere to be seen, the neighbours are already covered, and the shot, however small, carries a real cost, a sore arm, a lost afternoon, a risk that is tiny but not nothing. And here is the thing about a vaccine: it does two jobs at once, it protects the one who takes it, and, once enough have taken it, it protects everyone, because a pathogen that cannot find a susceptible host cannot spread, and coverage passes a threshold above which the chain of transmission breaks and even the unvaccinated are carried, and this is herd immunity, and it is real, and it is a public good. So the individual weighing the shot weighs it honestly: if almost everyone around them is covered, the herd already shields them, and the marginal good their own shot would add to their own protection is small, while the cost of the shot, real if slight, stays exactly what it was. So for that individual, in that moment, declining is not folly, it is arithmetic: take the protection the herd supplies, skip the cost the shot demands, and be no worse protected for it, because the threshold is already crossed and the disease is already gone. And each individual reasons the same, in good faith, and each is right about their own case, and so, one by one, coverage that was high enough to hold the line begins to thin, because the very success that raised it, the disease vanished from sight, the risk of the shot the only visible risk left, is exactly what makes each next person's shot look unnecessary. And it holds, invisibly, right up until enough have sat out that coverage falls back below the threshold, and then the chain of transmission, which needed only a gap, finds one, and the pathogen returns, and it returns first and hardest to the ones who could never be vaccinated at all, the infant, the immunocompromised, the one whose body cannot take the shot and who was living, this whole time, on the coverage the others were quietly declining to renew. Nobody was reckless. Nobody was a cheat: each simply weighed a real cost against a benefit the herd already supplied, and chose as any rational person would. It is only that a protection which works by covering everyone is a protection that makes each individual's own contribution feel redundant exactly when it is most collectively needed, so the coverage that success produces is the coverage that success dissolves, and the shield the herd holds up is held up by shots that, one by one, everyone can rationally decline to give.

## Arc

The bend is this: vaccination builds a shared protection that, once high, makes each individual's own shot look redundant, so rational self-interest thins the coverage that success produced, and the disease returns to the ones who could never be covered.
It runs on a protection that is a public good.
Vaccination past a certain coverage confers herd immunity: the pathogen cannot find a chain of susceptible hosts, transmission breaks, and even those not immunised are protected, so the good one person's shot produces is not kept by them alone but spread across the whole population (Fine, on herd immunity, the protection the coverage confers on those not themselves immunised). The benefit is collective and the cost is private, and this is what makes it a trap and not a fault, because there is no shot whose good stays wholly with its taker, and each contribution genuinely spills onto the rest.
And the protection has a threshold, and above it each further shot buys less. There is a coverage, 1 minus 1/R0, above which the disease cannot spread at all, so once the herd is past it the marginal benefit of any one further shot to its own taker falls toward nothing while the disease is held off by the others (Anderson & May, on the herd-immunity threshold, the coverage above which each infection produces on average fewer than one further case). The line, once crossed, makes the next shot look surplus to the one asked to give it.
And so declining becomes individually rational. For a person weighing their own shot, once the herd already covers them, the small personal cost of the shot, real if slight, outweighs the marginal protection it would add, so self-interest, honestly reckoned, points to sitting out, and the voluntary choice settles below the coverage the group would choose (Bauch & Earn, on the game theory of vaccination, self-interest never reaching the group-optimal coverage when the vaccine carries any perceived cost). No one cheats: each weighs a real cost against a benefit the herd supplies, and each is right.
And the individual optimum and the public optimum come apart. What is best for each, taking the others' coverage as given, is not what is best for all, so the coverage a population of rational individuals settles at is structurally short of the coverage that would protect it, a gap that is a property of the incentives and not of anyone's ignorance (Fine & Clarkson, on the divergence of individual and public priorities in the determination of optimal vaccination policy). The shortfall is built into the arithmetic, not chosen by anyone in it.
And the success is the engine. The disease is unseen precisely because coverage is high, so the shot looks least necessary exactly when it is most load-bearing, and as more sit out, coverage slips back below the threshold and the pathogen returns, first to the ones who could never be vaccinated and were living wholly on the herd (the protection erasing the very incentive that produced it). The programme's own success is what dissolves the coverage that success required.
None of it is recklessness or malice. Each weighs a real cost against a real, herd-supplied benefit and chooses rationally, so no one errs and the coverage falls anyway. What makes the protection valuable, that it covers everyone once enough contribute, is exactly what makes each further contribution feel redundant once enough have made it, and a disease held off by the herd is a disease unseen by the one deciding whether to add to the herd.
Hold the coverage above the threshold, and the disease stays gone: keep it there by outreach, by reminder, by mandate, by building the trust that carries people past their own arithmetic, and the line can be held: but this means paying, continuously, to sustain a contribution each individual can always rationally decline, against a disease that stays invisible precisely while the effort is working, chasing a coverage no visible threat is asking for, so the line is held only by carrying, forever, the cost of asking people to give a shot the herd has already made feel unnecessary.

## Triggers

**[The Weighing](plot_the_weighing.md)**
An individual at the clinic weighs their own shot against a herd that already covers them, and the Individual, reckoning honestly, finds the small personal cost of the shot set against a protection the others already supply. Closes when the private calculation has been made in good faith and the shot has come to look, to the one asked to give it, like a cost with little marginal benefit, the herd's protection borrowed and the shot's cost declined.

**[The Cover](plot_the_cover.md)**
Cue: coverage rises as shots are given and crosses the herd-immunity threshold. The disease can no longer spread and vanishes from view, the herd effect established and the only visible risk left the risk of the shot itself. Closes when the threshold is crossed and the disease is gone from sight, the protection at its height and the incentive to add to it at its lowest, success and its own undoing arriving together.

**[The Sit-Out](plot_the_sit_out.md)**
Cue: with the disease unseen and the herd high, the shot looks least necessary. One by one, rational individuals decline, free-riding on a protection others produced, each right about their own case. Closes when free-riding has spread as the visible risk fell, coverage thinning shot by shot with no one cheating and no one alarmed, the herd quietly stopping being renewed.

**[The Slip](plot_the_slip.md)**
Cue: enough have sat out that coverage falls back below the threshold. The chain of transmission finds the gap it needed, and the pathogen returns, first and hardest to the ones who could never be vaccinated. Closes when the disease has come back to the population that let its coverage fall, the shortfall between voluntary and protective coverage surfacing at last as an outbreak, no single choice to blame.

**[The Hold](plot_the_hold.md)**
Cue: the Officer would keep coverage above the threshold before it slips. Through outreach, reminder, mandate, and trust, the Officer works to sustain a contribution each individual can rationally decline, against a disease no one can see. Closes when the line is held and the disease kept gone ... or the effort is judged unneeded against an invisible threat, the coverage left to the individuals' arithmetic, and the gap left to widen toward the slip.

## Stakes

Whether a population can hold a protection that works by covering everyone, when covering everyone is what makes each person's own contribution feel unnecessary.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the shot declined, which each individual is right to decline once the herd already covers them, and away from the coverage sustained, which asks each to pay a real cost for a benefit the herd already supplies against a disease no one can see. The production cannot make the private incentive match the public need, because a protection high enough to shield everyone is high enough to make each further shot redundant to its taker, and a disease held off by the herd is a disease invisible to the one deciding whether to add to the herd, so the coverage that falls is self-interest working as self-interest does over a threshold public good, not recklessness: no one errs and every choice is rational. It can only show that each weighed a real cost against a real, herd-supplied benefit and chose in good faith, that the programme's own success is what dissolves the coverage that success required, and that the one move that holds the line, keeping coverage above the threshold by outreach and mandate and trust, must be made by paying, continuously, to sustain a contribution each individual can always rationally decline, against a disease that stays invisible precisely while the effort is working, so the population is protected and each is rational and the protection it borrowed from the herd is the one thing the herd, one by one, was declining to renew.
