# Piece: The Private Cost

## Taxonomy

[Borrowed Immunity](play_borrowed_immunity.md). The individual's perceived cost of the shot, real if slight, the one thing on the scale that does not fall as coverage rises, and so what makes free-riding rational once the herd is high.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Weighed at [the clinic](place_the_clinic.md), where the individual sets it against the shot's marginal benefit.
Left untouched across [the population](place_the_population.md), where every rising point of coverage lowers the benefit but not the cost.

## Load Bearing

Without it the shot is costless and everyone takes it, and there is no free-rider trap at all.
With it the trap has its lever: the real if slight personal cost of the vaccine, a sore arm, a lost afternoon, a small perceived risk, which stays exactly what it is while the herd's coverage drives the shot's marginal benefit toward nothing (Bauch & Earn, on the game theory of vaccination, self-interest never reaching the group-optimal coverage when the vaccine carries any perceived cost). The Private Cost is load bearing because it is why declining is rational: the fixed weight on one side of a scale whose other side the herd's success keeps lightening, so the piece carries the cost that need not be large to tip the choice, only real, and real it is.

## Apparent

A trivial inconvenience, a small risk barely worth naming.
Seen at the surface, the Private Cost looks minor, a slight and reasonable thing set against a serious disease, and the truth that it need not be large to matter, that it is the one weight the herd's success cannot reduce, that a cost this small tips the choice precisely because the benefit facing it has been driven so near to nothing, stays hidden behind how minor the shot's cost seems until it is the only cost left on the scale.
