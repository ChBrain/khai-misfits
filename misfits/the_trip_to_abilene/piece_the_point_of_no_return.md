# Piece: The Point of No Return

## Taxonomy

[The Trip to Abilene](play_the_trip_to_abilene.md). The line past which reversing costs more face than proceeding.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Somewhere out on [the road](place_the_road.md), too far along to turn back easily.
The stretch where the car is committed, the plans are made, the reservations held, and stopping now would mean admitting the whole thing was a mistake out loud.

## Load Bearing

Without it a late objection could still save the group, and someone might risk it.
With it the window shuts: the Point of No Return is where the cost of speaking flips from awkward to humiliating, because now the objector is not just spoiling a mood, they are indicting a decision already acted on, and everyone who went along with it. The later the truth, the more it accuses, so the truth gets later, and the later it gets the more it costs, until proceeding is simply cheaper than confessing.

## Apparent

Commitment.
It looks like a group seeing something through, staying the course, not flip-flopping. Follow-through is a virtue. It is wearing the virtue's coat.

## Yearbook

Passed unspoken while [the goer](persona_the_goer.md) drives on and everyone watches the miles.
It is the sunk-cost turn of the play: each seat now has its own going-along invested in the trip being fine, so each seat has a fresh reason to keep the objection swallowed, and the very momentum of the mistake becomes the argument for finishing it.
