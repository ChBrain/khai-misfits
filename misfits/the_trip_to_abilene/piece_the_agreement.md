# Piece: The Agreement

## Taxonomy

[The Trip to Abilene](play_the_trip_to_abilene.md). The accumulated whole: owned by every seat in public, wanted by none in private.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

The gap between the two rooms: the one on the record and the one in the heads.
On the record, a unanimous decision, warmly carried. In the heads, seven people each hoping to be overruled. The Agreement is the standing distance between them.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of polite nods and one long drive.
With it those are one object: a consensus that is total and hollow, held whole by nobody, because the seat that voices the yes is never the seat that holds the wish, and no seat holds both. It is the mirror of a real agreement, identical from outside and empty within, and it commits the group as bindingly as the real thing, on the authority of a want that does not exist in a single chest at the table.

## Apparent

Unanimity.
From any seat the room is agreed, and by every visible sign it is. The trap is invisible from within because there is no dissent to point to: no argument, no vote against, no scene, only seven yeses and a car full of quiet regret.

## Yearbook

The table built the skin: [the nod](piece_the_nod.md), [the read](piece_the_read.md), [the manufactured mandate](piece_the_manufactured_mandate.md), each true as a public act.
The chests held the truth: [the swallowed objection](piece_the_swallowed_objection.md), seven times over.
[The breaker](persona_the_breaker.md) was the first to say the private thing out loud, on the way home, and the whole unanimous agreement fell in a single sentence, because it had never been standing on anything but everyone's fear of being the first to sit down. Nobody had a name for the trip until it was already taken.
