# Piece: The Swallowed Objection

## Taxonomy

[The Trip to Abilene](play_the_trip_to_abilene.md). The private no that is never said, and so never counts.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Behind the teeth of nearly everyone at [the table](place_the_table.md).
The full sentence, formed and ready: I don't actually want this. Held there, unspoken, while the head nods along.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no misfit, only a group that wants the trip.
With it the room is full of dissent and empty of it at once: the objection exists in every seat and is voiced by none, because each seat, seeing the nods already given, files itself as the lone holdout and decides the cost of saying so is too high. The Swallowed Objection is the play's dark matter: it is the true state of the room, and it registers nowhere, because the one place it lived was a mouth that stayed closed.

## Apparent

Agreement.
From the outside a swallowed objection and a genuine yes are identical: a calm face, a nod, a moment passing. The room cannot tell the difference, which is the whole trouble, because the room is what everyone is reading.

## Yearbook

Held by [the withholder](persona_the_withholder.md), who counts the nods and loses the nerve.
It is the cheapest thing in the play to voice and the most expensive to keep, and everyone keeps it, because to speak it is to risk being the one who broke the lovely accord that, unknown to all, none of them felt.
