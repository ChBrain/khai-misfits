# The Trip to Abilene: Reference

The research warrant for [The Trip to Abilene](play_the_trip_to_abilene.md). The name
is Jerry Harvey's parable; the mechanics the play runs on are grounded in the
social-psychology literature on agreement, conformity, and public silence cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Abilene paradox as a **systemic system**: a group in which every member
publicly assents to a course they privately oppose, and the unwanted decision carries
anyway because each assent is read by the others as the wish none of them holds. The
play models the documented mechanics, the mismanagement of agreement, conformity to an
imagined majority, self-censorship, pluralistic ignorance, and the spiral of silence, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of cowards. The governing
finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: the failure is the
inability to manage agreement rather than conflict, and it is one-directional, because a
voiced yes adds to the apparent consensus while a swallowed no never subtracts from it,
so the misread compounds toward unanimity that no seat privately shares.

## Origin

The phenomenon in practice, and the research spine beneath it.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                                                                                                      | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Harvey**         | "The Abilene Paradox: The Management of Agreement in Organizations", _Organizational Dynamics_ (1974); book (1988)    | The parable and the thesis: organisations fail as often from the inability to manage **agreement** as from the inability to manage conflict. The play's spine and its name.                    |
| **Katz & Allport** | "Students' Attitudes" (1931); the naming of **pluralistic ignorance**                                                 | Each member privately rejects a norm while assuming most others accept it, so all conform to a consensus none of them holds. The Read, formalised.                                             |
| **Asch**           | "Effects of Group Pressure upon the Modification and Distortion of Judgments" (1951); the conformity monograph (1956) | **Conformity**: individuals will publicly agree with a group against their own plain perception, to avoid standing alone. The Nod, measured.                                                   |
| **Janis**          | "Victims of Groupthink" (1972); "Groupthink" (1982)                                                                   | **Groupthink**: self-censorship and the illusion of unanimity, where silence is read as consent and doubts go unvoiced to preserve cohesion. The Swallowed Objection and the Mandate.          |
| **Noelle-Neumann** | "The Spiral of Silence: A Theory of Public Opinion" (1974); book (1984)                                               | The **spiral of silence**: fear of isolation makes those who sense they are in the minority go quiet, which emboldens the apparent majority, which quiets them further. The Silence Travels.   |
| **Kuran**          | "Private Truths, Public Lies: The Social Consequences of Preference Falsification" (1995)                             | **Preference falsification**: people misrepresent their wants under perceived social pressure, so public consensus can diverge completely from private conviction. The gap the play stands on. |
| **Practitioner**   | committee decision-making, boards, and the "disagree and commit" counter-practice (management practice)               | The naming in organisational life: a unanimous decision that every participant privately regrets, and the deliberate norms built to prevent it.                                                |

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to
  cast any seat as a coward or a liar, because the source finding is precisely that each
  member is being considerate: every yes is meant kindly, offered to spare the group a
  friction the giver imagines. The misfit is that the considerate move is the fatal one.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into brave dissent in time. The
  reckoning is recrimination after the road has run out, when the private truth is finally
  cheap to say because the trip is already taken, not courage arriving to prevent it.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed
  here, never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Trial Balloon](piece_the_trial_balloon.md)**: carries Harvey's origin, the unowned
  offhand suggestion that starts the trip because no one is committed enough to stop it.
- **[The Nod](piece_the_nod.md)**: carries conformity (Asch), assent given to the group rather
  than to the proposal, and read as a vote.
- **[The Swallowed Objection](piece_the_swallowed_objection.md)**: carries self-censorship (Janis)
  and preference falsification (Kuran), the private no that is never voiced and so never counts.
- **[The Read](piece_the_read.md)**: carries pluralistic ignorance (Katz & Allport), each seat
  misreading the others' identical silence as a contentment it alone lacks.
- **[The Manufactured Mandate](piece_the_manufactured_mandate.md)**: carries the illusion of
  unanimity (Janis), the consensus no seat holds cited as the authority all must defer to.
- **[The Point of No Return](piece_the_point_of_no_return.md)**: carries public commitment and the
  rising cost of a later confession, the social sunk cost that makes finishing cheaper than admitting.
- **[The Silence Travels](piece_the_silence_travels.md)**: carries the spiral of silence
  (Noelle-Neumann), each public assent emboldening the apparent majority and quieting the next doubt.
- **[The Agreement](piece_the_agreement.md)**: carries the Abilene paradox itself (Harvey), a
  unanimous decision wanted by no one, held whole by nobody.
