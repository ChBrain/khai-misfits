# Piece: The Nod

## Taxonomy

[The Trip to Abilene](play_the_trip_to_abilene.md). The assent given to the others, not to the thing.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the table](place_the_table.md), the first agreeable yes.
Given quickly, warmly, to keep the room easy: sounds good to me, whatever everyone wants.

## Load Bearing

Without it the balloon deflates, and the room moves on.
With it the balloon becomes a proposal with support, and the support is the misunderstanding: the Nod is not agreement with the idea, it is agreement with the imagined wish of the room. It answers a question nobody asked, are we all getting along, with a yes that is then read as answering the question on the table. A courtesy is entered into the record as a vote.

## Apparent

Enthusiasm, or near enough.
It looks like a person on board, maybe even keen. It is a person being kind, and kindness photographs exactly like consent.

## Yearbook

Given by [the obliger](persona_the_obliger.md), who would rather agree than weigh.
It is the friendliest move in the play and the most misread: offered as a gift to the group's mood, received as a commitment to the group's course, and impossible to take back once the next person has nodded to it.
