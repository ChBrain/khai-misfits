# Piece: The Descope

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md). The reversal: cutting the promise, or staffing early, instead of adding people to a late project.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the backlog](place_the_backlog.md), where scope is removed rather than capacity added.
The features cut, the release narrowed, the honest re-plan against what a held team can truly ship: the retreat that makes the date a date again.

## Load Bearing

Without it the only lever the schedule believes in is headcount, and the slip has no honest floor.
With it there is a move that actually shortens the work instead of lengthening the team: cut the scope so the remaining work fits the time and the team that already knows it, or add the people early enough that ramp-up finishes before the deadline matters. The Descope is load bearing because it is the one lever in the play that pulls toward the date instead of away from it.

## Apparent

Giving up. Admitting defeat.
It looks like failure: shipping less than promised, telling the customer the scope shrank, conceding the plan was wrong. That reading is why it is the last resort, because cutting the promise is visible and swallowing the slip is not, and a project would rather add people than say it will do less.

## Yearbook

Made by [the cutter](persona_the_cutter.md) through [descoping](process_descoping.md), against the pull of [the deadline](piece_the_deadline.md).
It is the remedy the play holds: the Descope answers [the slip](piece_the_slip.md) by shrinking the work, and it is the only move that makes [the man-month](piece_the_man_month.md) balance, a smaller promise the team can actually keep.
