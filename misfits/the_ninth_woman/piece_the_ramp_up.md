# Piece: The Ramp-Up

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md). The training drain: a new hand is a net loss before it is a gain, and the loss is paid by the ones who knew the work.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the onboarding](place_the_onboarding.md), in the weeks a newcomer spends learning the system and the veteran spends teaching instead of building.
The pairing sessions, the questions, the context that lives in heads and must be transferred one conversation at a time: real assimilation, which takes as long as it takes.

## Load Bearing

Without it added people would be productive the day they arrive and Brooks's Law would not bite.
With it every addition subtracts twice at first: the newcomer produces little, and the person who could have been producing is teaching instead, so the project's output drops exactly when it needed to rise. The Ramp-Up is load bearing because it is why capacity added late is capacity lost first, and lost from the people who were the project's speed.

## Apparent

Investment in the team.
It looks like the right thing, growing capability, bringing people up, a cost that pays back later. And it does pay back later, which is precisely the problem, because later is on the far side of the deadline the additions were meant to save.

## Yearbook

Paid by [the veteran](persona_the_veteran.md) and [the newcomer](persona_the_newcomer.md) through [onboarding](process_onboarding.md).
It is the first cost of [the reinforcements](piece_the_reinforcements.md): the Ramp-Up drains the project before it feeds it, and its bill comes due inside the exact window [the deadline](piece_the_deadline.md) could least afford.
