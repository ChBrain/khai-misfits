# Plot: The Cut

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The backlog](place_the_backlog.md), and someone who has stopped adding people and started removing work.
The question has changed: not how many more hands will save the date, but how much less promise the date can actually hold.

## Action

[Descoping](process_descoping.md): [The Cutter](persona_the_cutter.md), holding [the product owner's seat](position_product_owner.md), cuts the scope to what a held team can truly ship and re-plans against it.

## Stage

[The Cutter](persona_the_cutter.md) tables [Cut the Scope](plan_cut_the_scope.md): shrink the promise rather than swell the team, so [the descope](piece_the_descope.md) makes the work fit the time and [the man-month](piece_the_man_month.md) finally balances.
The people who know the work are protected from dilution, and the date becomes real against a smaller promise.

## Tension

The date is saved, and the promise it was made on is smaller.
Cutting works, and it costs: it ships less than was promised, tells the customer the scope shrank, and looks like conceding while adding people looked like fighting harder. It trades the whole promise for a real one ... or the cut is refused as defeat, the crowd grows again, and the date recedes by the same move that moved it before.
