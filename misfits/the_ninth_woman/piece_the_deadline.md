# Piece: The Deadline

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md). The fixed date a late project is measured against, and told to save.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the gantt](place_the_gantt.md), a hard line drawn down the calendar that the work keeps failing to reach.
The launch, the release, the promise to the customer: a commitment made when the plan looked achievable, now standing over a project that has fallen behind it.

## Load Bearing

Without it lateness is just a rate, and no one reaches for a lever.
With it there is a fixed point the schedule must hit and is missing, so pressure builds toward the one move that looks like more speed: add people. The Deadline is load bearing because it is what turns a slip into a crisis, and a crisis into the decision that deepens it.

## Apparent

A reasonable commitment.
It looks like discipline, a date to rally around, the thing that keeps a project honest. And it is, until it becomes the reason to add hands to work that cannot take them.

## Yearbook

Held over the project by [the sponsor](persona_the_sponsor.md), and missed through [slipping](process_slipping.md).
It is the pressure the play opens under: the Deadline is what [the slip](piece_the_slip.md) measures against, and the promise [the descope](piece_the_descope.md) must shrink to make real.
