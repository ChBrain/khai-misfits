# Piece: The Man-Month

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md). The phenomenon whole: the myth that people and months are interchangeable, and the smaller promise that is the way out.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the gantt](place_the_gantt.md), where headcount and time are laid on the same axis and treated as one currency.
Not one slip but the unit itself: the man-month, the assumption that ten people for one month equals one person for ten, held by every plan that converts people into time and time into people.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is one unlucky project that hired badly.
With it there is a named law it stands on: adding manpower to a late software project makes it later, because effort and progress are the same only when work partitions without communication, and real work does neither. The Man-Month is load bearing because it holds both the law and its remedy, that the schedule's error is the unit, and the fix is to spend less scope rather than more people.

## Apparent

A sensible planning unit.
It looks like arithmetic: budget in person-months, add capacity to buy time, the oldest move in project management. That is the disguise, because the unit quietly assumes the one thing the work refuses, that a month can be filled with any number of people, and the assumption is invisible right up to the slip.

## Yearbook

Assembled from [the ramp-up](piece_the_ramp_up.md), [the cross-talk](piece_the_cross_talk.md), and [the partition](piece_the_partition.md), and answered by [the descope](piece_the_descope.md).
It is the law the play runs on: the Man-Month is why [the reinforcements](piece_the_reinforcements.md) deepen [the slip](piece_the_slip.md), and why the only way out is a smaller promise, not a larger crowd.
