# Piece: The Cross-Talk

## Taxonomy

[The Ninth Woman](play_the_ninth_woman.md). The communication overhead: each added person multiplies the channels faster than the capacity.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the standup](place_the_standup.md), grown long, in the syncs and threads that now take a share of every day.
The meetings that used to be quick, the decisions that now need more people in the room, the n paths becoming n times n-minus-one over two: coordination that scales as the square of the team.

## Load Bearing

Without it a bigger team would simply be more output and adding people would always help.
With it output grows linearly at best while coordination grows quadratically, so past a point each new person costs more in cross-talk than they add in work. The Cross-Talk is load bearing because it is the second tax on size: even after ramp-up, the team is slower per head than it was small, and the slowdown is structural, not slack.

## Apparent

Good communication.
It looks like a well-run team: everyone in sync, decisions aligned, no one left out of the loop. And it is real coordination, honestly needed, which is why its cost is so hard to cut, the meeting that must happen is also the meeting eating the day.

## Yearbook

Felt most by [the lead](persona_the_lead.md) through [communicating](process_communicating.md), across a team that keeps growing.
It is the second cost of [the reinforcements](piece_the_reinforcements.md): the Cross-Talk eats the capacity [the ramp-up](piece_the_ramp_up.md) did not, and together they are why [the man-month](piece_the_man_month.md) does not add up.
