# Play: The Ninth Woman

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Ninth Woman.
A project is late, so more people are added to bring it back ... and the bearing of the thing takes its own time no matter how many are assigned, so the newcomers must be taught, the channels multiply, and the date the help was meant to save moves further away.

## Arc

The bend is this: add people to a late project, and you make it later.
It runs on a split between headcount and progress, which the schedule treats as the same and the work does not.
The date slips, so hands are added, because hands are what a manager can add. But a new hand is a net drain before it is a gain: it must learn the system, and the learning is paid by pulling the people who know it off the work. Meanwhile every added person multiplies the channels that must be kept in sync, so coordination grows as the square of the team while delivery does not. And the task on the critical path was never divisible: nine people cannot bear in one month what one bears in nine.
Hold the team and cut the scope, or add the people early and never late, and the date can be made real: men and months are not interchangeable, and the schedule that trades one for the other buys delay with the coin it thought was speed.

## Triggers

**[The Slip](plot_the_slip.md)**
The project is behind and the date will not move on its own. The Sponsor sees the slip and demands it be saved. Closes when the answer chosen is capacity: bring in more people.

**[The Reinforcements](plot_the_reinforcements.md)**
Cue: heads are added to the late project. The Manager brings them on, and the Veteran stops delivering to teach them. Closes when the team is bigger and the people who knew the work are now training instead of building.

**[The Cross-Talk](plot_the_cross_talk.md)**
Cue: every added person multiplies the channels to keep in sync, and the critical task will not divide. The Lead drowns in coordination while the Newcomer is still getting up to speed. Closes when the added capacity is eaten by communication and ramp-up.

**[The Man-Month](plot_the_man_month.md)**
Cue: the date has moved the wrong way. The Estimator re-forecasts and finds that adding people made the project later, though everyone worked hard. Closes when the law is named ... or blamed on the new hires, and still more people are demanded.

**[The Cut](plot_the_cut.md)**
Cue: someone stops adding people and starts removing work. The Cutter cuts the scope, holds the team, and re-plans against what can truly ship. Closes when the date becomes real against a smaller promise ... or the cut is refused and the crowd grows again.

## Stakes

Whether a schedule is ever paid in progress, or only ever in headcount.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward more people, which the date reads as more speed, and away from the work, which cannot be hurried by a crowd. The production cannot make a month hold more people's worth of the one task that will not divide. It can only show that no one was idle and no one unskilled, that every added hand was added in good faith, and that the single thing that would have saved the date, cutting the promise or staffing early instead of late, is the move a project in crisis is least willing to make.
