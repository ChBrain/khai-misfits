# Piece: The Promise

## Taxonomy

[Time Inconsistency](play_time_inconsistency.md). The plan optimal ex ante: the sincere announcement meant to induce the agent's action.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the podium](place_the_podium.md), declared and meant.
Where the plan best to make is made.

## Load Bearing

Without it there would be no inducement and no trap, only a world where nothing was offered.
With it there is a plan genuinely optimal to announce: keep prices stable, honour the patent, pay no ransom, and it would, if believed, move the agent to do the thing the good outcome needs. The Promise is load bearing because it is the ex-ante optimal plan at the root of the finding (Kydland & Prescott), the sincere and well-chosen pledge whose whole worth depends on being believed, and the thing the second moment will make it optimal to break.

## Apparent

A commitment, plainly.
It looks like a binding undertaking, because it is offered as one and meant as one, so nothing about the promise at the moment of making announces that the same reasoning which recommends it now will recommend breaking it later.

## Yearbook

Made by [the authority](persona_the_authority.md) through [promising](process_promising.md), the pledge the play opens on.
It is the good plan with the fatal condition: the Promise is worth everything if kept and is what [the temptation](piece_the_temptation.md) will make it optimal to abandon.
