# Piece: The Commitment

## Taxonomy

[Time Inconsistency](play_time_inconsistency.md). The credibility device: the rule, delegation, or tied hand that makes reneging impossible.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the mast](place_the_mast.md), the discretion surrendered on purpose.
Where reneging is put out of reach.

## Load Bearing

Without it the promise would stay empty and the outcome lost for good.
With it the break is removed, not by willpower but by force: the choice is delegated to a rule or a body that cannot renege, the hand lashed to the mast before the song, so the agent, seeing reneging impossible, acts at last and the good outcome forms. The Commitment is load bearing because it is Schelling's paradox and the delegation cure in one (Schelling; Rogoff), the counterintuitive move by which giving up the freedom to choose is what makes the promise worth believing, and the only thing that answers the trap.

## Apparent

A strange self-defeat, or a loss of nerve.
Tying one's own hands looks like weakness or foolishness, a refusal to keep options a wise actor would hold, so the commitment reads as surrender, when it is the precise source of the strength the promise needed: a hand that cannot move cannot be pushed.

It is credibility bought with freedom: the Commitment removes [the temptation](piece_the_temptation.md) and, in the same stroke, sets up [the rigidity](piece_the_rigidity.md).
