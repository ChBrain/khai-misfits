# Time Inconsistency: Reference

The research warrant for [Time Inconsistency](play_time_inconsistency.md). The title is Kydland and
Prescott's; the play runs on the theory of dynamic inconsistency and the commitment devices that
answer it, from Strotz and Schelling through the rules-versus-discretion literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging time inconsistency as a **systemic system**: a promise that is optimal to announce, because
it induces investment, invention, restraint, or trust, becomes optimal to break once the inducement
has done its irreversible work, so a rational counterparty foresees the break, withholds the induced
action, and the good outcome never forms, undone by a foreseen reneging rather than an actual one.
The play models the documented mechanics, the promise optimal ex ante, the temptation to renege ex
post, the anticipation by which the counterparty prices the break in, the unravelling of the outcome
before it exists, the commitment device that restores credibility by removing the discretion, and the
rigidity that is its price, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of bad faith.
The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: when the plan that is
optimal at the first moment is not optimal at the second, discretion cannot sustain it, and good
outcomes require binding rules or commitment that give up the very flexibility that discretion
prized. It is the engine beneath the house's Moral Hazard and Bank Run: the no-bailout pledge is
time-inconsistent, promised to induce prudence and broken to stop the panic, so the promise that
would prevent the trouble is exactly the one no one believes.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  authority as a liar, because the source finding is precisely that no lie is needed: the promise is
  sincere when made, the reneging is genuinely optimal when the moment comes, and the counterparty who
  expects the break is reasoning correctly, not cynically. The misfit is that a reasonable, flexible
  authority cannot make a promise worth believing, because its own good judgement is what will break it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a promiser simply keeping their word by
  willpower. Credibility is restored only by surrendering the discretion, tying the hands so reneging
  is impossible, and a hand that cannot break its promise cannot break it when a genuine shock says it
  should, so the reckoning is flexibility traded for credibility, and the rule that buys the outcome is
  the rule that binds in the storm.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the inconsistency result, the precommitment
  insight, and the delegation-and-reputation cures are theirs, and the honest tension between a rule's
  credibility and its rigidity is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Promise](piece_the_promise.md)**: carries the plan optimal ex ante (Kydland & Prescott), the sincere
  announcement meant to induce the counterparty's action.
- **[The Temptation](piece_the_temptation.md)**: carries the ex-post optimal deviation (Strotz; Kydland &
  Prescott), the break that becomes right once the inducement's work is done.
- **[The Anticipation](piece_the_anticipation.md)**: carries the rational foresight (Barro & Gordon), the
  counterparty pricing the break in before it comes.
- **[The Unravelling](piece_the_unravelling.md)**: carries the outcome destroyed by foresight (Barro & Gordon),
  the induced action withheld and the good result never formed.
- **[The Commitment](piece_the_commitment.md)**: carries the credibility device (Schelling; Rogoff), the rule,
  delegation, or tied hand that makes reneging impossible.
- **[The Rigidity](piece_the_rigidity.md)**: carries the price of commitment (Rogoff), the bound hand that cannot
  move when a genuine shock says it should.
- **[The Time Inconsistency](piece_the_time_inconsistency.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Kydland &
  Prescott), the plan best made and worst kept, and its remedy in surrendered discretion at a cost.
