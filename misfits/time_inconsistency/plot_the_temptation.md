# Plot: The Temptation

## Taxonomy

[Time Inconsistency](play_time_inconsistency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The podium](place_the_podium.md) the next day, the inducement's work done and impossible to undo.
The invention made, the capital sunk, the hostage taken, and the promise now costing and buying nothing.

## Action

[Reneging](process_reneging.md): [The Authority](persona_the_authority.md), holding [the authority's seat](position_authority.md), finds that keeping the word no longer pays, while [The Adviser](persona_the_adviser.md), holding [the adviser's seat](position_adviser.md), names the plain fact that breaking it is, at this second moment, the right call.

## Stage

[The Authority](persona_the_authority.md) tables [Do What's Best Now](plan_do_whats_best_now.md) again, and this time doing what is best now means to renege, so [the temptation](piece_the_temptation.md) turns the plan best to make into the plan best to break.
[The Adviser](persona_the_adviser.md) marks that the break is not betrayal but an update, the same reasoning that made the promise now unmaking it.

## Tension

The right thing to have promised and the right thing to now do are opposites.
There is no villainy: the pledge was sincere, the acting it induced cannot be reversed, and with its purpose spent the promise is pure cost, so breaking it is simply better. That is the inconsistency itself, laid bare: a plan whose optimal announcement and optimal execution point in different directions, and a reasonable hand that will, correctly, follow the second.
