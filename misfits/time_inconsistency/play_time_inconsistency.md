# Play: Time Inconsistency

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Time Inconsistency.
A promise is made that is exactly right to make: keep prices stable, honour the patent, never pay the ransom, tax no capital that comes ... and it is meant to move someone to act, to invest, to invent, to trust, to build. But when tomorrow comes the promise is exactly right to break, because by then the acting is done or the hostage is taken, and the best thing to do now is not the thing that was promised then. And because the one who was to act can see all this coming, they never act, so there is nothing to keep the promise about, and the good it was meant to buy is gone before it was ever offered.

## Arc

The bend is this: the plan that is best to announce is not best to carry out, and everyone knows it.
It runs on two moments and one temptation.
At the first moment, the promise is genuinely optimal: it induces the invention, the investment, the restraint, the trust, and everyone is better for it. At the second moment, the inducement has done its work and cannot be undone, so the promise now costs something and buys nothing, and breaking it is genuinely optimal too. The one who holds the discretion, reasonable and responsive, will do at the second moment what is best at the second moment, which is to renege.
None of it is dishonesty. The reneging is the right call when it comes, keeping your options open is prudence, and the other party, seeing that you will rightly break the promise, is right not to rely on it. So they do not invent, do not invest, do not trust, and the good outcome never forms, destroyed not by a broken promise but by a foreseen one, a credibility that discretion cannot hold.
Tie your own hands, take the discretion away, delegate the choice to a rule or a body that cannot renege, lash yourself to the mast before the song, and the promise becomes worth believing and the good returns: but a hand that cannot break its promise cannot break it when it should, so the rule that buys the outcome is the rule that binds you in the storm you did not foresee.

## Triggers

**[The Promise](plot_the_promise.md)**
An authority announces a plan that is exactly right to make: stable prices, an honoured patent, no ransom, no tax on capital that comes. The Authority means it to move the Agent to act, and the Agent hears a promise that would, if kept, be worth acting on. Closes when the promise is made, optimal today and meant to induce.

**[The Temptation](plot_the_temptation.md)**
Cue: tomorrow arrives, and the inducement has done its work and cannot be undone. The Authority finds that the promise now costs and buys nothing, and the Adviser names the plain fact that breaking it is, at this second moment, the right call. Closes when the plan best to make is shown to be best to break.

**[The Unravelling](plot_the_unravelling.md)**
Cue: the Agent, reasoning it through, sees the break coming before it comes. The Agent does not act on a promise it knows will rightly be broken, so there is nothing left to keep the promise about, and the Adviser shows the good outcome was gone before it formed. Closes when the promise is disbelieved and the inducement fails, by foresight and not by any broken word.

**[The Commitment](plot_the_commitment.md)**
Cue: someone moves to make the promise worth believing by making it impossible to break. The Binder ties the Authority's hands, delegates the choice to a rule or a body that cannot renege, lashes the mast before the song, and the Agent, seeing reneging removed, acts at last. Closes when the discretion is surrendered and the good outcome returns.

**[The Rigidity](plot_the_rigidity.md)**
Cue: a storm arrives that no one foresaw, and the right thing to do is exactly the thing the rule forbids. The Bound holds the line because breaking it now would unmake all the credibility the binding bought, and bears the cost of a hand that cannot move. Closes when the rule that secured the promise is the rule that traps the promiser ... or it is broken for the storm, and the credibility goes with it.

## Stakes

Whether a promise is ever worth acting on, or only ever worth making.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the second moment, when the promise is best broken, and away from the first, when it was best made and best believed. The production cannot make a reasonable authority keep a promise it will be right to break, so the promise is empty the instant the other party can reason, and the good it would have bought is lost to foresight alone. It can only show that no one lied, that reneging was right when it came and expecting it was right before, and that the one thing that would make the promise hold, a hand tied against its own future judgement, is the same tie that cannot loosen when the judgement, for once, should have been trusted.
