# The Open Cage: Reference

The research warrant for [The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md).
The dynamic is learned helplessness: Seligman and Maier's finding, reformulated attributionally by Abramson,
Seligman, and Teasdale, and reversed at its root by the neuroscience of Maier and Seligman, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging learned helplessness as a **conditioning structure**: an actor facing a stretch in which its actions are
severed from their outcomes learns, correctly, that effort does not pay, and the learned expectation of no-control
generalises beyond the room that taught it and outlives the conditions, so the actor goes on not trying once
control has returned and effort would now work, staying in a situation it could now escape. The play models the
documented mechanics, the objective uncontrollability in which outcomes are independent of responses, the
accurate giving-up that is a well-calibrated update and not a failure of will, the generalisation and persistence
of the expectation past its cause, the available control left untested because the expectation forbids the test,
the misattribution of the passivity to character that deepens the trap, and the graded outside demonstration and
restored expectation of control that alone reverse it, at a cost, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure,
not as a tale of a lazy or spineless actor. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law:
exposure to uncontrollable aversive events produces a learned expectation that responding is futile, which
transfers to new situations and impairs later escape even when escape has become possible.

The house is careful with the strength of the claim, because the source itself was reversed: Maier and Seligman's
2016 review of fifty years of neuroscience concludes that passivity in the face of prolonged aversive events is
not learned but is the unlearned default, and that what is actually learned is the detection of control, which
inhibits that default. The play credits this: it stages the behavioural phenomenon, the true lesson kept past its
expiry and the escapable trap not escaped, and treats the reversible engine as the expectation of control that
must be installed rather than a helplessness that must be unlearned, so the remedy is framed as supplying control,
not as removing a belief.

It is distinct from the house's The Dead Sea Effect, where the ablest members of a souring team leave first
because they can, so talent evaporates and the less mobile settle and stay: there the engine is differential
exit, a sorting by who can move, here it is a learned expectation that keeps an actor from acting or leaving at
all even when it now could, so the staying is not the residue of a departure but a taught passivity in the face
of an open door. And it is distinct from the house's The Bounce, where an extreme outcome is followed by a milder
one and the reversion is misattributed to whatever was done between: there the trap is a statistical artefact of
noisy measurement misread as cause, here it is a conditioning history that severs action from outcome and teaches
a futility that outlives its truth.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the Subject as lazy
  or the Overrider as a tormentor, because the source finding is precisely that the trap is structural: the
  powerlessness was often real and often no one's design, the giving-up was the correct response to a genuine
  contingency, generalising a learned truth is what learning does, and reading passivity as character is the
  natural inference. The staying is authored by no one and emerges from a true lesson carried past its expiry, so
  the misfit is that an actor who learned accurately that effort did not pay goes on not trying where effort would
  now save it, and is blamed for a passivity that was taught.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that frees the
  actor is an outside hand arranging a graded, unmissable demonstration that action now works and crediting the
  passivity to its cause, and it means spending the effort the Subject will not spend for itself, working against
  its settled certainty and the observer's verdict of laziness, and installing the expectation of control rung by
  rung rather than summoning it whole. The reckoning is a cage opened by someone else walking the caged one
  through the door again and again, never in a single step, not a free correction, so the cure for a taught
  helplessness is the control that must be supplied, not demanded.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's, and its own reversal is carried. The staging is the house's; the learned-helplessness finding
  is Seligman and Maier's, the attributional reformulation is Abramson, Seligman, and Teasdale's, and the
  neuroscience reversal, that passivity is the default and control is what is learned, is Maier and Seligman's
  2016 review, carried as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages the behavioural phenomenon and
  frames the remedy as supplying control, not as unlearning a helplessness.
