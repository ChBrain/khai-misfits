# Position: Judge

## Taxonomy

[The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md). The seat that reads the Subject's passivity as a flaw of character and blames them for it, the misattribution that deepens the trap.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

A clear view of the not-trying and a standard to hold it to: the standing to observe the Subject, see the open door go unwalked, and judge the person who will not reach for it.
A clear case: the opportunity is real and visible, and holding people to a standard of effort is only fair.
No sight of the stretch that taught the passivity, and no instrument to tell a taught giving-up from a chosen one.

## Orders

Call it character.
Read the passivity as laziness or want of grit, attribute it to the person and not the situation, and hold the Subject responsible for the door they will not try.
Judge the disposition by the behaviour, and name the failure to try a failure of will.

## Loses

The cause of the passivity, exactly by reading it as character.
Because the seat sees the not-trying and not the stretch behind it, it credits a stable flaw where there is a taught expectation, so its fair-seeming verdict misses the cause entirely, and the blame it lays lands on the Subject as one more outcome no effort can change, teaching again that nothing they do alters how they are seen and deepening the very passivity the seat names.
It gains a tidy explanation of the behaviour and loses its truth, and its verdict becomes another lever the Subject presses in vain.

## Drives

Toward the passivity credited to character and the blame laid,
bought against the cause it cannot see,
so the seat that would judge the giving-up fairly deepens the thing it misnames.
