# Persona: The Coach

## Taxonomy

[Coach](position_coach.md). Would arrange a graded, unmissable demonstration that action now works and walk the Subject through the open door, at the cost of spending the effort the Subject will not.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Patient. Deliberate. Building the small win.
Is the one who does not accept the passivity as character, who sets up a step small enough that the Subject cannot fail to see their action land, and then another.
No cheerleading in it: only the discipline of knowing that an expectation of no-control is changed by evidence, not by exhortation.

## Action

Arranges a graded demonstration that action now works, small enough to be unmissable and repeated until the expectation shifts.
Credits the passivity to its cause rather than to character, and supplies from outside the experience of control the Subject will not seek for itself.
Does what restores an expectation of control: it engineers a felt, undeniable link between what the Subject does and what results, one rung at a time.

## Shadow

Not a soft touch excusing sloth, a clear-eyed restorer, and that is the whole difficulty: the one move that frees the Subject is to spend, from outside, the effort the Subject will not spend for itself.
Because the Subject expects no control and will not test for it, the Coach must arrange the test on their behalf, against the Subject's settled certainty and the Judge's verdict of laziness, and even then can only install the expectation of control step by step, never summon it whole, so the freeing move is slow, effortful, easily mistaken for coddling, and never finished in one demonstration.
It would open the door and walk the Subject through, and walking them through means carrying, again and again, the effort the cage taught them not to spend.

## Tell

Says "here is one thing you do that changes one thing: watch it land, and now again."
Says it, and is told they are coddling the workshy, because the demonstration that would free the Subject looks, to the Judge, like an excuse.
