# Process: Restoring

## Taxonomy

[The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md). The rebuilding of the expectation of control by graded demonstration, the Coach supplying from outside the felt success the Subject will not seek, at a cost and never in one step.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A refusal to take the passivity as character and a step small enough to land.
At [the ramp](place_the_ramp.md), the Coach arranging a win the Subject cannot miss, showing an effort of theirs actually change something.

## Direction

Toward the expectation restored, if the outside effort can be carried.
Because the Subject expects no control and will not test for it, restoring arranges the test on their behalf, a felt, unmissable link between action and outcome, so it drives toward the expectation rebuilt: but a taught helplessness gives way only rung by rung, against the Subject's certainty and the Judge's verdict, so the process frees the actor only by the Coach spending, again and again, the effort the actor will not.

## Lever

The truth that expectation shifts on evidence, not exhortation.
Because a taught expectation of no-control is changed by a felt success and not by being told to try, arranging the small win is the one move that reaches it, so restoring runs on a sound method: yet the same move that frees the Subject requires supplying the control they will not seek and repeating it until it holds, so the process works only at the cost of the outside effort it demands, and never in a single step.

## Echo

An expectation rebuilt rung by rung, or a demonstration waved off as coddling.
Either the Subject is shown their action landing, small and felt and again, until the expectation gives and they try, bought against the Coach's repeated effort, or the demonstration is dismissed as excusing the workshy, the laziness pronounced once more, and the cage stays open and unwalked.
