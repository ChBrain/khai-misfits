# Place: The Ramp

## Taxonomy

[The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md). Where control is shown small and felt and the expectation rebuilt, the graded scaffold of unmissable wins the Coach walks the Subject up.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the escape is made in steps too small to fail, a graded scaffold where the Subject is shown their action landing, once, and then once more, each rung a felt link between what they do and what results.
The Coach arranging the small win, the Subject watching an effort of theirs actually change something, the expectation of no-control beginning to give.

## Holds

The restoring, and the evidence it builds.
Here the Subject's action is made to land where they can feel it, so the Ramp holds the point where control is given back: a step small enough that the effort cannot fail to matter, so it is where the expectation is rebuilt from evidence, one felt success at a time, the only ground on which a taught helplessness gives way.

## Offers

A felt link between action and outcome, restored in steps.
Because the Ramp makes each rung small enough that the Subject cannot miss their action landing, it offers the experience of control the Subject would not seek for itself, so it hands out the one thing that shifts the expectation, an undeniable, felt success, and hands it out only through the Coach's effort, arranged from outside and repeated until the ground holds.

## Withheld

A cure in one step, or an expectation summoned rather than built.
The Ramp can restore control, but it cannot install the whole expectation at once or spare the Coach the repetition, because a taught helplessness gives way only rung by rung, so it withholds the single decisive demonstration, exacting instead a patient sequence of small wins, and, all graded evidence and no leap, it withholds any restoration that does not cost the Coach the effort the Subject cannot yet spend for itself.
