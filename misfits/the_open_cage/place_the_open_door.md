# Place: The Open Door

## Taxonomy

[The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md). The changed situation where control has returned and goes untested, the escape a step away that the Subject does not try and the Judge condemns.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the situation has changed and a way out has opened, a new manager who would welcome the initiative, a lever that now works, a door unlocked and standing open.
The Subject not reaching for it, the Judge watching the open door go unwalked and drawing the natural conclusion about the one who will not step through.

## Holds

The judging, and the untested control it condemns.
Here the returned control meets the expectation that denies it, so the Open Door holds the point where the trap outlives its cause: a way out plainly available and a Subject who will not test it, so it is where the passivity is seen and misjudged, the open door read by the Judge as proof of a flaw and by the Subject as a wall.

## Offers

A real way out, and a stage for the misjudging of it.
Because the Open Door is where control has genuinely returned, it offers the Subject an escape a single act would confirm, and offers the Judge the plain sight of that escape refused, so it hands out both the available freedom and the evidence, to the observer, that the Subject simply will not take it, the same open door reading as opportunity to the one and as a locked wall to the other.

## Withheld

The felt knowledge that the door is open.
The Open Door stands unlocked, but it cannot make the Subject feel that it is, because feeling it would take the test the expectation forbids, so it withholds from the Subject the very perception that would move them, leaving the freedom real and unfelt, and, all open way and no felt opening, it withholds from the Judge any sign that the refusal they condemn is a taught blindness and not a chosen sloth.
