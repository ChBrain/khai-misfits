# Persona: The Judge

## Taxonomy

[Judge](position_judge.md). The observer who reads the Subject's passivity as a flaw of character, laziness or want of grit, and blames them for the thing that was taught.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Fair-minded. Observant. Calling it as they see it.
Is the one who watches the Subject not try, the manager, the teacher, the onlooker, and draws the natural conclusion about the person who will not reach for the open door.
No malice in it: only someone reading passivity the way passivity reads, and holding people to a standard of effort.

## Action

Reads the giving-up as a stable trait, and attributes the passivity to the Subject rather than to the stretch that taught it.
Calls it laziness, want of drive, a failure of will, and holds the Subject responsible for not trying the door that stands open.
Does what an observer does: infers the disposition from the behaviour, and judges the person by what they fail to do.

## Shadow

Not a bully, a fair observer drawing the obvious inference, and that is the whole difficulty: passivity does look like character, and reading it so is natural and wrong.
Because the Judge sees the not-trying and not the stretch that produced it, they credit a stable flaw where there is a taught expectation, so the verdict of laziness misses the cause entirely, and, worse, it lands on the Subject as one more outcome no effort can change, teaching again that nothing they do alters how they are seen, and deepening the very passivity it names.
It calls the giving-up a flaw, and the naming becomes another lesson that trying will not help.

## Tell

Says "they just don't apply themselves: the opportunity is right there."
Sees the opportunity clearly, and cannot see that to the one who will not take it, the opportunity is a locked door the eye reports as open.
