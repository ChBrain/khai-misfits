# Process: Judging

## Taxonomy

[The Open Cage](play_the_open_cage.md). The reading of the passivity as character, the Judge crediting the giving-up to a stable flaw and blaming the Subject for the thing that was taught.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A visible not-trying and an observer with a standard.
At [the open door](place_the_open_door.md), the Judge watching the Subject leave the way out unwalked and drawing the natural conclusion about the person who will not step through.

## Direction

Toward the passivity credited to character and the trap deepened.
Because the observer sees the not-trying and not the stretch that produced it, judging attributes the passivity to a stable flaw in the Subject rather than to its cause, so it names the giving-up laziness or want of grit, and the blame lands as one more outcome no effort can change, teaching the Subject again that nothing they do alters how they are seen and deepening the very passivity it names.

## Lever

The naturalness of inferring the person from the behaviour.
Because passivity really does look like character and holding people to a standard of effort is only fair, reading the not-trying as a flaw is the ordinary, reasonable inference, so judging runs on fair-minded observation, the misdiagnosis assembled from no malice, only from an eye that sees the refusal and not the room that taught it.

## Echo

A tidy verdict of laziness that misses the cause and feeds it.
The passivity has been credited to character, so what reads as a fair judgement of a person who will not try is, in truth, a misattribution that mistakes a taught expectation for a flaw, and lands on the Subject as another uncontrollable outcome, the blame becoming one more lever pressed in vain.
