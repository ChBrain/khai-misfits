# Pitch: Good Blocks Better

## Taxonomy

[The Good Move](play_the_good_move.md). The single hook: a good answer found first does not merely beat the search for a better one, it forecloses it, holding the attention that the better move would need to be found at all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A practised mind, a familiar problem, and the answer that has always worked rising at once.
The tenor is competence: the method fits, the solution is correct, the speed is earned, and applying what has solved this a hundred times is exactly what a skilled hand should do.

## Undertow

The good answer, found first, closes the search before the better one is reached.
Underneath, the solution that arrives fast takes the attention, and the mind that already holds a working answer stops looking, its gaze held on the ground of the move it found, so the better move present on the same problem is left in the region the eye never reaches, blocked not by argument but by where the looking goes.

## Nerve

The solver searched for something better and swears they looked, and the better move was there the whole time.
The nerve is that the blindness is not laziness: asked to find a better answer, the mind sets out honestly and finds nothing, because its attention returns again and again to the features of the first solution, so the search for better is spent on the ground of the worse, and the better move sits unlooked-at not for want of trying but because the good move already took the eye.

## Echo

Expertise is no escape from the block, and the only cure is to distrust the answer that has never failed you.
What lingers is that a ready answer is what does the blocking, so the master, who holds one, is caught where the empty-handed beginner is not, no mere novice's stumble but a cost of competence; and though deeper skill lifts the strongest past it more often, none are wholly immune, so the one move that would find the better answer is to treat every good one as a suspect, look again past a solution that already works, and be slower at everything easy to be better, sometimes, at the rare thing that was hiding.
