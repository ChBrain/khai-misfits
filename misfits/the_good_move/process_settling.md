# Process: Settling

## Taxonomy

[The Good Move](play_the_good_move.md). The mind coming to rest on the workable answer, the search closing because a solution that solves the problem is a reason to stop, the better move never costed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Master](persona_the_master.md), at [the Board](place_the_board.md), holding a correct answer and judging the problem solved.
Begins when the good solution is in hand and works, the very sufficiency of a correct answer telling the mind the case is closed.

## Direction

Turns a working answer into a stopping point.
Because the solution solves the problem, there is no felt reason to keep looking, so settling converts sufficiency into closure, the search ended at the first answer that works rather than pushed on to the best available, the good enough standing in for the better because the good enough was reached first (Luchins).

## Lever

The sufficiency of a correct answer.
Because a solution that works genuinely does end the problem, settling leans on sound sense: there is no ordinary reason to search past a right answer, and the effort saved by stopping is real, so its lever is the plain economy of a working solution, the same economy that makes stopping usually wise being what stops the search one move short of better.

## Echo

A closed case and an uncosted alternative.
The echo of settling is a problem marked done at a good answer, the better move never weighed because the search that would have weighed it never ran, so the solver keeps the economy of stopping and loses only the thing they never saw, the better solution left uncosted behind a correct one that was reason enough to rest.
