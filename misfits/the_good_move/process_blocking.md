# Process: Blocking

## Taxonomy

[The Good Move](play_the_good_move.md). The found answer holding the attention, the search for better spent on the ground of the worse, so the better move sits unlooked-at while the mind believes it looked.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The First Answer](piece_the_first_answer.md), at [the Blind Spot](place_the_blind_spot.md), occupying the attention the search for a better move would need.
Begins the moment a good solution is in hand and the mind turns to look for more, the looking drawn back to the features of the answer already found.

## Direction

Turns an honest search into a misdirected one.
Because the first solution holds the gaze, the attention that sets out to find something better returns again and again to the ground the good answer occupies, so blocking converts a real intention to look into a search spent where the better move is not, the eye held on the first answer's squares while the better one waits elsewhere (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet).

## Lever

The pull of the answer already found.
Because a solution in hand draws the attention to itself, blocking works through capture rather than choice: the mind does not decide to ignore the better move, it simply cannot get its gaze off the good one, so the lever is the plain fact that a found answer holds the eye, and the search is defeated not by too little looking but by looking that cannot leave.

## Echo

A better move left in the dark, and a solver sure they searched.
The echo of blocking is a complete-feeling search that never reached the better answer, so the mind comes away certain it looked and found nothing, the better move still sitting on the board and the looking that would have found it spent, honestly and entirely, on the ground of the answer already held.
