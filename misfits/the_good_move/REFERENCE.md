# The Good Move: Reference

The research warrant for [The Good Move](play_the_good_move.md).
The dynamic is the Einstellung effect: Luchins' naming and water-jar demonstration of the set, the Luchinses' fuller
account of its breadth and its remedy, Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet's isolation of the attentional mechanism and its
quantification in chess masters, and Wertheimer's reproductive-versus-productive frame, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the Einstellung effect as a **good-solution-blocks-a-better-one structure**: repeated success installs a set, a
readiness to meet the new problem as the old one, and when a familiar problem arrives the primed solution fires at once,
correct and fast, so the mind holds a working answer before any search begins and stops, because a solution that solves
the problem is a reason to stop. The trap is that a better solution is present on the same problem, and the good answer
blocks it, not by argument but by attention: the search for a better move returns to the features of the found one, so
the better move sits present and legal in the region the gaze never reaches. The play models the documented mechanics,
the set installed by practice, the good first answer it fires, the attentional block that holds the gaze on that answer,
and the better move left unseen in the blind spot, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a
blunder or a worse answer chosen over a better one. The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: a
good solution, found first, does not merely lose a comparison to a better one, it forecloses the search that would find
the better one at all, by capturing the attention that search would need, and the block catches the skilled and not the
novice alone, since it is a ready familiar answer that blocks a better one and only the practised hold that answer, so
expertise is no immunity to the set, though susceptibility falls as skill rises and the strongest solvers are caught
least, inflexibility in the expert real and the more a myth the deeper the skill. The set is invisible while the
familiar method keeps working and undeniable only on the problem that method cannot solve, where the set-bound expert
fails and the unset beginner, meeting the board whole, sees the only path plainly.

It is distinct from the house's The Law of the Instrument, where a mastered tool reshapes the problem to fit its
striking face, the reaching coming before the diagnosis so the aspect the tool can address is promoted to the whole and
the rest trimmed off: there the problem itself is bent to the solution, the case redefined to what the instrument can
strike, while here the problem is seen correctly and a correct solution is found, and what is lost is a better solution
to the same, unaltered problem, blocked from view by the first. The one trap bends the definition of the problem to a
tool; the other leaves the problem intact and closes the search for a better answer to it, a redefinition of the case
against a foreclosure of the search.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the solver as careless or
  the set as a failing, because the source finding is precisely that no blunder and no worse answer is needed: the first
  solution is genuinely correct, the method that fires it is sound, and the set that made it ready is the honest return
  on real practice, right nearly everywhere it is applied (Luchins). The mind does not choose a bad answer over a good
  one; it finds a good one and stops, because a working solution is a reason to stop, and the better move is lost not to
  an error but to a correct answer that arrived first and closed the search. Even the attentional block is not
  negligence: the solver searches honestly and believes they searched, and only the direction of the looking is captured
  (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet), so the misfit is that a good move, found first, blocks a better one by holding the
  attention, no mistake and no bad faith required, the miss the sum of a sound method, a correct answer, and a sensible
  stop.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a mind freed by better sense alone. The one move that would
  find the better answer is to distrust the good one, to look again past a working solution and come to the problem as
  though unpractised, but that costs on every problem to pay off on a few, since the set is invisible from inside and the
  good method is right nearly everywhere, so guarding against the block means treating all one's competence as suspect,
  forgoing the speed practice earned, and being slower and worse at the ordinary problems to be better at the rare one,
  and even then the deliberate look is leaky, reducing the set but never quite erasing it, so the second look buys the
  better move more often, not always (Luchins; Luchins and Luchins). The naive eye that finds the better move wins only
  by lacking the method that wins everywhere else, an advantage it cannot claim as skill and cannot keep once it learns.
  The reckoning is a mind that looks past every good move it makes, or one that keeps the good move and never learns what
  it missed, not a way to hold the ready answer and escape the block that having it brings.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the water-jar demonstration, the naming of the
  effect, and the warnings remedy that reduces but never erases the set are Luchins', the fuller account of its breadth
  across tasks the Luchinses', the attentional mechanism shown by eye-tracking and its quantification in masters, where
  the block is reality and myth at once and more a myth the deeper the skill, are Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet's, and the
  reproductive-versus-productive distinction is Wertheimer's, each carried as carefully as the finding itself: the
  misfit stages a good answer that blocks a better one and credits that no solver blundered and the block is the set's
  own, the pitch's phrase a paraphrase of Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet's title, not a coinage of the house.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Set](piece_the_set.md)**: carries the Einstellung effect whole (Luchins), the disposition installed by repeated
  success that meets the new problem as the old and ties the priming, firing, and block into one phenomenon, sound where
  the old answer fits and blinding where a better one is present.
- **[The First Answer](piece_the_first_answer.md)**: carries the good solution the set fires ready (Luchins), correct and
  fast, whose very success closes the search, the good move that blocks the better precisely by being good.
- **[The Fixation](piece_the_fixation.md)**: carries the attentional mechanism (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet), the gaze held
  on the found answer's features so the search for a better move is spent where the better move is not, the block a
  capture of attention rather than a lazy stop.
- **[The Simpler Way](piece_the_simpler_way.md)**: carries the better path present on the same problem (Wertheimer), the
  productive solution the set-bound mind runs past while the reproductive one holds, available the whole time and left
  unreached.
