# Play: The Good Move

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Good Move.
A mind that has solved a kind of problem many times meets one more of the kind, and the method that has always worked rises at once and works again. This is not a fault: the answer is correct, the practice that produced it real, the speed earned. The trouble is that a better answer is sitting on the same problem, plainer and shorter, and the good move blocks it: not by argument but by attention, the mind's eye held on the ground of the solution it already has, so the better move goes unlooked-at in the region the gaze never reaches. Ask the solver whether they searched and they will say yes, honestly, and mean it, while their attention never left the first answer's squares. The set is the disposition the practice installed, the readiness to meet the new problem as the old one, and it is worth having, because most of the time the old answer is the right one and the readiness is what expertise is. But where a better path is present, the good move is exactly the thing that hides it, the found solution spending the search that would have found the better, so the mind stops one move short of better and never knows it stopped. Show it a problem the old method cannot solve at all and it fails outright, while a beginner with no method to reach for sees the only path plainly. Nobody blundered. The solver answered a real problem with a sound method they had every reason to trust, and the miss is the sum, a good answer found fast closing the search on a better one it never let the eye reach.

## Arc

The bend is this: a good solution, found first, does not sit beside the better one and lose to it; it blocks the better one from being found at all, by holding the attention that the search for better would need (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet).
It runs on practice turned to a set.
Solving a kind of problem again and again installs a disposition to meet the next one the same way, and the disposition is sound, because the method really does solve the problems of its kind and the readiness to apply it is what mastery is (Luchins). The set is the honest return on practice, and it fires before any search begins.
And the firing precedes the looking.
Faced with the new problem, the primed solution presents itself at once, fast and unbidden and correct, so the mind has an answer in hand before it has searched, and the answer works, which is precisely the reason to stop (Luchins). The first answer is good, and its goodness is what closes the case.
And the good answer captures the attention.
The mind believes it goes on to look for something better, and reports that it does, but its gaze returns to the features of the solution it already found, so the search for the better move is spent on the ground of the worse one, and the better move, present and legal on the same board, sits in the region the eye never reaches (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet). The looking is real and misdirected, and the blindness is not laziness but capture.
And the set catches the expert, not the novice alone.
Having a familiar answer ready is the very thing that blocks a better one, so the block is a cost of competence rather than a failing of the beginner: the practised, who hold a ready answer, are caught where the empty-handed cannot be, and masters shown a position with a familiar answer present can still miss a better one that is there (Bilalic, McLeod and Gobet). Mastery is not immunity to the set: the expert is caught too, no mere novice's stumble. But nor does more skill deepen the block, for the greater the mastery the more often it lifts the solver past it, the strongest caught least, so inflexibility in the expert is real and grows more a myth the deeper the skill runs.
And the set only shows its cost where the old method fails outright.
Give the primed mind a problem the familiar method cannot solve at all and it fails where a beginner, holding no method to reach for and so meeting the problem fresh, sees the only path at once, the naive eye passing exactly where the practised one is blind (Luchins). The set is invisible while the old answer keeps working, and undeniable only in the case it cannot reach.
None of it is a blunder. The solver meeting a real problem with a trusted method, finding a correct answer fast, and stopping because it works: each step is sound, and the miss emerges from the sum, no error and no worse answer chosen over a better one, only a better one never found. Distrust the good move, look again after the answer is in hand, come to the problem fresh, and the better move can more often be found, though never surely, the deliberate look reducing the set without wholly erasing it (Luchins; Luchins and Luchins): but that means treating a correct answer as a suspect, spending effort past the point a good solution has already solved it, and being slower and worse at the ordinary problems the set solves at a glance, to be better only at the rare one where a better move was hiding, so the cure for a set is a deliberate refusal to trust the answer that has never failed you, and the reckoning is a mind that looks past every good move it makes, or one that keeps the good move and never learns what it missed.

## Triggers

**[The Priming](plot_the_priming.md)**
A run of problems all yielding to one method, each success deepening the groove. The Master learns the method and learns to trust it, the set installed by the very practice that makes them expert. Closes when the disposition is set, sound and earned and ready to fire before the next problem is even read.

**[The Lock](plot_the_lock.md)**
Cue: a problem that fits the primed pattern and also admits a better path. The set fires, the familiar answer rises at once and works, and the mind locks onto it, correct and worse in the same stroke. Closes when the good move is taken as the move, the search closed on a solution that solved it and blind to the better one on the same board.

**[The Unseen](plot_the_unseen.md)**
Cue: the solver, sure there may be more, sets out to find a better move. They search, and find nothing, and report they looked. Closes when the better move is still sitting in the blind spot, unseen not for want of looking but because the looking never left the first answer's ground, the gaze held where the good move was found.

**[The Extinction](plot_the_extinction.md)**
Cue: a problem the old method cannot solve at all. The Master fails where the Novice, holding no method to reach for, sees the only path plainly, and the cure is shown at its price: distrust the good move, look again, come fresh. Closes when the remedy is taken at the cost of trusting nothing you know, or declined for the comfort of a method that has never failed before.

## Stakes

Whether a practised mind can find the better move on a problem it already knows how to solve, or is held by the good answer it found first from ever looking where the better one waits.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the good move that blocks the better, which the Master is right to trust because the method has always worked and right to apply because it works again, and away from the second look that would find the better move at the cost of distrusting a good one. The production cannot make the miss a blunder, because the first answer is genuinely correct and stopping at a correct answer is sensible, and the set is the honest fruit of practice that serves well nearly everywhere: reaching for the method that has always worked is the sound act, the expert doing what expertise is for. It can only show that a good solution found first spends the attention a better one would need, that the better move sits present and legal in the blind spot the gaze never reaches, and that the one move that would find it, looking past a good answer as though it were a suspect, means being slower and worse at the ordinary problems to be better at the rare one, so the miss turns not by anyone's error but by the ordinary good sense of a mind that trusts the good move it has every reason to trust.
