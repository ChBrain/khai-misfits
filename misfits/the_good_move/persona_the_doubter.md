# Persona: The Doubter

## Taxonomy

[The Second Look](position_the_second_look.md). Treats the good answer as a suspect rather than a stop, searches past it on purpose, and pays for the discipline in the many cases where the first answer was already best.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Distrusting by discipline. Slow on purpose. Refusing the answer that arrives too easily.
Is the solver who has learned that the good move can hide a better one, and so will not let a working solution close the search, holding the case open past the point it feels solved.
No gift wished on them: the doubt is not intuition but a practised refusal, a decision to keep looking after a correct answer, taken against the pull of a solution that already works.

## Action

Finds the good answer as fast as anyone, and then, instead of stopping, makes themselves look again, deliberately away from the ground the first answer holds, into the part of the board the good move would have them skip.
Warns themselves off the easy path with the answer already in hand, checking the region the set says is empty, so the search does not close on the first solution but is spent evenly across the whole.
Does what a burned mind does: assumes every good move may be blocking a better one, and buys the better move, when there is one, with the effort of distrusting a solution that did not deserve distrust.

## Shadow

Not a clearer eye, but a tiredness: the discipline of never trusting a good answer is exhausting, and most of the time the second look confirms the first, so the effort buys nothing and must be spent anyway, because the one time it matters cannot be told from the many when it does not.
What wears on them is the waste they cannot avoid. The set is invisible from inside, so there is no signal that says look harder here and not there, and the only defence is to doubt everything, which means paying the full cost of the second look on every problem to catch the rare one with a better move hiding, a tax on all their correct answers for the sake of the few that were not best.
The interior bind is the loss of the ease that skill is supposed to buy: the Master's speed is the reward of practice, and the Doubter forgoes it on principle, treating their own competence as untrustworthy, so the more skilled they become the more they must distrust themselves, and the doubt that protects them from the set is the same doubt that denies them the confidence they earned.

## Tell

Says "it works, which is exactly why I do not trust it yet."
Is sound and sounds perverse, because the working answer is the very thing that would close anyone else's search, and to treat its success as a reason to keep looking is to refuse the plainest signal that the problem is solved, so the sentence that sounds like second-guessing is the discipline the set requires, a mind that has learned a good move is where a better one goes to hide.
