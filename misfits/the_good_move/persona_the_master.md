# Persona: The Master

## Taxonomy

[The Repertoire](position_the_repertoire.md). Meets the new problem with the method a hundred solved problems have made ready, finds the good answer at once, and is held by it, most blind exactly where most practised.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Practised. Fast by earning it. Sure of the method because the method has always worked.
Is the solver whose repertoire fires before the search begins, so the familiar answer is in hand almost as the problem is read, correct and quick and honestly come by.
No cleverness wished on them and no laziness charged: the speed is the return on real practice, and the trust in the method is warranted nearly everywhere, which is precisely why it holds where it should not.

## Action

Reads the new problem as one more of a kind they know, and the method that fits the kind rises at once and solves it.
Takes the good answer it produces, because it works, and moves on, the search closing on a correct solution before a better one is ever costed.
Does what a skilled hand does: applies what has solved this before, trusts the answer it trusts for good reason, and, asked whether more might be there, looks, and returns satisfied, having searched the ground the first answer already holds.

## Shadow

Not that they are careless, but that the looking felt complete and was not: the certainty of having searched, while the eye never left the move already found, is the part that stings when the better one is finally shown.
What gnaws is the discovery that skill did not free them. Each time the better move is revealed, it was present the whole while, on the same board, and the practice that should have found it was the very thing that hid it, so the mastery they trust is exposed as partly the blinkers, and there is no felt difference from the inside between a search that was free and one that was already spent.
The bind is interior and quiet: the more they have practised, the more sure the good answer feels and the less a better one seems possible, so the confidence that is the fruit of their expertise is the same confidence that keeps them from doubting it, and they cannot tell, in the moment, which of their sure answers is the one with a better move hiding behind it.

## Tell

Says "I looked for something better; there was nothing there."
Is being honest, and is wrong, because the search was real and misdirected, spent on the features of the answer already found while the better move sat in the part of the board the gaze never reached, so the sentence that sounds like due diligence is the exact report of the set at work, a mind certain it searched by a mind that never left where it started.
