# Play: Saving Face

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Saving Face.
A capable team, courteous and professional, that protects everyone in the room from the small embarrassment of being wrong ... and pays for it with the one thing the protection costs: the correction it needed, kept off the table to spare the face it would have cost.

## Arc

The bend is this: the harder a team works to keep everyone safe from looking bad, the less it can learn, because learning is looking bad on purpose.
It runs on two facts that pull apart.
The first: to be corrected is to be, for a moment, wrong in front of others, and being wrong in front of others carries a real cost, you look ignorant for asking, incompetent for erring, negative for doubting, difficult for pressing. So the sound, social move is to manage the look: keep the question in, work around the error, meet the hard point with a smooth one, and never, ever make it personal.
The second: that same move, done well, is the thing that blocks the learning. The doubt kept in is a lesson not taken. The error worked around is a cause not found. The hard point smoothed over is a governing assumption never tested, so the symptom is patched, and patched again, and the frame that produces it is never touched.
And the move seals itself. The correction is undiscussable, and that it is undiscussable is undiscussable too, so the one signal that would reveal the trap is the signal the trap is built to suppress. The team is not lying: it is being kind, and skilled, and professional, and the sum of the kindness is a room that has quietly stopped learning and started defending, and cannot say so.
Make the error discussable and the team learns before the world teaches it: but discussing it costs exactly the face the whole room was working to save.

## Triggers

**[Set the Tone](plot_set_the_tone.md)**
A capable team brings its first real problem to the one who leads it, and watches how it lands. The Verdict meets the news with a judgement, not a question, and the room reads the price of speaking off a single face. Closes when the cost of being the bearer of bad news is set, and the next bearer has already done the sum.

**[Bite the Tongue](plot_bite_the_tongue.md)**
Cue: someone sees the flaw and reaches for the question. The Careful weighs the look of asking against the look of staying quiet, and quiet wins, because the room is moving and the point might be nothing and being the one who slows it is a cost paid alone. Closes when the doubt stays behind the teeth and the record shows a room with no objections.

**[Smooth It Over](plot_smooth_it_over.md)**
Cue: a hard point reaches the table anyway. The Smoother, skilled and kind, meets it with a softer one, keeps it constructive, spares the face it would have cost ... and folds the deflection so neatly that naming the deflection would itself be the rude thing. Closes when the correction is off the table and the fact that it was taken off is off the table too.

**[Patch It](plot_patch_it.md)**
Cue: the same failure returns, wearing a new symptom. The Patch fixes it, cleanly and fast, inside the frame that keeps producing it, and calls it resolved, because the fix is real and the frame is not the sort of thing one questions here. Closes when the symptom is closed for the nth time and the cause is filed as bad luck.

**[Name It](plot_name_it.md)**
Cue: a failure lands too large to smooth, and someone puts the undiscussable on the table as information, not indictment. The Namer reframes the error as the thing to learn from and holds the room in the discomfort it was built to avoid. Closes when the frame is questioned at last and the lesson is taken ... or the Namer is thanked, called brave, and quietly learned from about what not to raise.

## Stakes

Whether the error is ever made discussable before it is made expensive.
Every plot moves the same direction: toward the face, which is protected freely, kindly, and at once, and away from the correction, which is costly, exposing, and paid for by the one who dares it. The production cannot make a considerate team want to embarrass itself. It can only show that no one lied and no one was a coward, that the courtesy was real and the professionalism sincere, and that the room's great skill at keeping everyone comfortable was exactly the skill that kept it from learning the one thing it could not afford not to know.
