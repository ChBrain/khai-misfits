# Saving Face: Reference

The research warrant for [Saving Face](play_saving_face.md). The title is the old idiom for
protecting one's standing in front of others; the mechanics the play runs on are grounded in the
organizational-learning and psychological-safety literature cited below, where the routines that
protect face are shown to be the same routines that block learning.

## Line of Work

Staging the defended organisation as a **systemic system**: a capable, courteous team in which every
member protects self and others from the small threat of being wrong, and the aggregate is a group
that cannot learn, because each act of protection removes a correction. The play models the
documented mechanics, the interpersonal risk of speaking up, the Model I governing values that keep
control and avoid embarrassment, the skilled defensive routine that deflects a threat and blocks its
examination, the undiscussable that seals itself, single-loop correction that fixes symptoms and
never the frame, and the double-loop inquiry that alone corrects it, as the load-bearing vectors of
the structure, not as a tale of cowards or fakes. The governing finding of the source literature is
the play's governing law: the behaviour that is skilled at protecting face is the same behaviour that
is skilled at preventing learning, and because it makes its own operation undiscussable, it is
self-sealing, so the correction it suppresses is the one signal that could reveal it.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as a coward, a liar, or a fool, because the source finding is precisely that none is needed:
  the withheld question is courtesy plus a real image cost, the smoothing is genuine kindness and
  skill, the patch is a competent fix, and the leader's verdict is ordinary decisiveness. The misfit
  is that behaviour skilled at protecting people is behaviour skilled at stopping them learning.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into candour arriving and winning the room.
  Naming the undiscussable can test the frame, but only by spending the very face the routine was
  built to save, and only while someone keeps renewing it against the constant pull back to comfort,
  so the reckoning is a face traded for a lesson, not a team that grew open on its own. The Namer is
  as often thanked and quietly shelved as heard.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and the
  distinction between espoused values and values-in-use is kept, not smoothed over.
