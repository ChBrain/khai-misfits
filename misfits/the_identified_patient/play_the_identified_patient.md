# Play: The Identified Patient

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Identified Patient.
A family is under strain, and the strain is real: a marriage gone quiet, a loss unspoken, a pressure no one can name, a fracture running through the whole that no member can bear to face, because facing it might break the thing apart. And then one of them begins to struggle, the child who acts out, the one who will not eat, the member who falls ill or falls apart, and the trouble is real too, a genuine symptom in a person genuinely suffering. And the family gathers around them, worried and loving, and says: here is our problem, here is the one who is unwell, and it is a relief to say it, because now there is a trouble everyone can see and name and rally to, a shared thing to organise around, and the quiet fracture that no one could face slips out of view behind the loud symptom everyone can. So the family takes the suffering one to be treated, and the clinician treats them, skilfully and in good faith, because the symptom is real and treating it is the right and caring thing ... and treating the one confirms that the one is the problem, and holds the fracture shut, and the family, steadied for now around its patient, stays whole on the strength of a member kept unwell. And the strangest part is what happens when the patient gets better: the system, deprived of the trouble that held it together, grows anxious, and pulls them back toward the role, or finds another to carry it, because the symptom was doing the family's work, and a family that needs a patient will keep one. No one chose this and no one is cruel: the strain is real, the symptom is real, the love is real, the treatment is right, and still the group has quietly settled its whole trouble onto one member and called that member the problem, and the treating that would heal them is the treating that keeps the system that made them ill.

## Arc

The bend is this: a system under a strain it cannot face settles that strain on one member, who turns symptomatic, and the symptom stabilises the whole by giving it a visible trouble to rally round instead of the fracture, so treating the patient as the problem heals the one the system needs ill and preserves the rift that made them so.
It runs on a symptom that is functional for the system.
A family under strain has a fracture it cannot face, and when one member becomes symptomatic the whole is relieved: now there is a shared, nameable, visible trouble to organise around, and the loud symptom draws every eye off the quiet fracture (Vogel and Bell; Jackson). The symptom is not the family's failure to cope, it is the family coping: it detours the conflict, unites the members around a common concern, and holds the system together (Minuchin), so the trouble that surfaces in one is doing work for all.
And the trouble settles on one by no one's design. Under strain the system's anxiety seeks a place to land, and it lands on the member most available to carry it, who becomes the identified patient, the symptom-bearer through whom the whole discharges its tension (Bowen). It is a projection, not a plot: no one selects the patient, the anxiety selects them, and the more the family treats the one as the trouble, the more the one becomes it.
And treating the patient confirms the patient. Because the symptom is real and the suffering genuine, treating it is the right and caring thing, and the clinician who heals the one, in good faith and with skill, confirms the family's frame that the one is the problem, and so the treatment aimed at the patient holds the fracture shut and preserves the system that produced the symptom. And when the patient improves, the system, losing the trouble that steadied it, grows anxious and reasserts: it pulls the recovered member back toward the role, or recruits another to carry it, because a system that needs a patient keeps one (Jackson).
None of it is cruelty or design. The strain is real, the symptom is real, the love and worry are real, and treating a suffering member is the right thing to do: no one scapegoats on purpose, and the trap emerges from the sum of a family that cannot face its fracture, an anxiety that must land somewhere, a symptom that happens to steady the whole, and a treatment that is genuinely called for. What is meant to heal the one keeps the one the system it serves.
Treat the system rather than the member, name the fracture the symptom hides, hold the whole as the unit that is unwell, and the trouble can be met where it lives: but treating the system means surfacing the very conflict the family formed the symptom to avoid, letting the fracture into the open where it might break the thing apart, and it means taking from the patient the role that gave them care and place and a name for their pain, and taking from the family the shared trouble that held it together, so the escape is bought by facing the rift the whole system was built to keep shut, and by asking the one the family needs ill to give up being the one it gathers around.

## Stakes

Whether a group can hold together without a member kept unwell to hold it.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the patient, whose symptom each member is right to worry over and treat and all are steadied by treating, and away from the fracture, which would meet the trouble where it lives and which asks a family to face the very conflict it formed the symptom to avoid. The production cannot make the treatment find the fracture, because the symptom's whole function is to hide it, and the suffering in the one is real and visible where the rift in the whole is neither, so the settling of the trouble on one member is the system stabilising itself, not failing. It can only show that no one is cruel or scheming, that the strain is real and the symptom real and the love and the treatment genuine, and that the one move that meets the trouble, treating the system and surfacing the fracture, must be made by letting the hidden conflict into the open where it might break the whole apart, and by taking from the patient the role that gave them care and from the family the trouble that held it together, against every instinct that runs toward the suffering one, so the love is real and the illness is real and the treatment that would heal the patient is the treatment that keeps the system needing them ill.
