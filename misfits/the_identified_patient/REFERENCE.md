# The Identified Patient: Reference

The research warrant for [The Identified Patient](play_the_identified_patient.md).
The dynamic is the family-systems account of the symptom-bearer: Bowen's projection, Minuchin's structural detour,
Vogel and Bell's family scapegoat, and Jackson's family homeostasis, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the identified patient as a **family-systems structure**: a group under a strain it cannot face, in which
the anxiety of the whole settles on one member who becomes symptomatic, the symptom stabilises the system by
detouring the conflict and uniting the members around a shared trouble, and treating the patient as the problem
confirms the frame and preserves the fracture that produced the symptom, so a whole that needs a patient keeps
one. The play models the documented mechanics, the unfaceable conflict in the system, the projection by which the
strain of the whole settles on the available member, the symptom's homeostatic function of detouring the conflict
and holding the family together, the frame that locates the trouble in the one, the care and role that hold the
patient in the position from their own side, the system's resistance to the patient's recovery and its recruiting
of a new bearer, and the systemic treatment that alone meets the trouble where it lives, at a cost, as the
load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a cruel family or a scheming scapegoater. The governing
finding of the source is the play's governing law: the symptom of the identified patient is functional for the
family system, expressing and stabilising a dysfunction that belongs to the whole, so that treating the member in
isolation preserves the system, and lasting change requires treating the family as the unit.

It is distinct from the house's Shifting the Burden, where reliance on a symptomatic fix lets the capacity for the
fundamental solution atrophy from disuse: there the engine is dependence and the withering of an ability, here it
is displacement and homeostasis, a whole locating its trouble in one member whose symptom stabilises the system,
so the unit that is unwell is the group and the cost of the cure is the group's own balance. It is distinct from
the house's The Paper Trail, where blame-avoidance drives each actor to make their work defensible rather than
effective: there the trouble is diffused across everyone until it is no one's fault, here it is concentrated onto
one until it is only theirs, the whole's dysfunction worn by a single bearer rather than dissolved among all. And
it is distinct from a simple story of blame or bullying, because the identified patient is genuinely symptomatic
and genuinely suffering, the family genuinely loving, and the treatment genuinely called for: the misfit is that
real care for a real symptom preserves the system that produced it, not that anyone is cruel.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the family as
  cruel or the kin as scheming scapegoaters, because the source finding is precisely that each move is sound: the
  strain is real, the symptom is genuine and the suffering real, the love and worry are real, and treating a
  suffering member is the right and caring thing to do. The settling of the trouble on one is authored by no one
  and emerges from the structure, an anxiety that must land somewhere and a symptom that happens to steady the
  whole, so the misfit is that a loving family meeting a real illness in one member keeps the member the system
  needs unwell, and locates in the one a trouble that belongs to all.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that meets the
  trouble where it lives is to treat the system rather than the member and surface the fracture the symptom
  hides, and it means letting the hidden conflict into the open where it might break the whole apart, taking from
  the patient the role that gave them care and a name for their pain, and taking from the family the shared
  trouble that held it together, against every instinct that runs toward the suffering one. The reckoning is a
  fracture faced at the cost of the whole's balance and the patient's place, not a free correction, so the cure
  for a member kept unwell is the conflict the family formed the symptom to avoid.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed
  as the house's. The staging is the house's; the family scapegoat is Vogel and Bell's, the projection and the
  emotional unit are Bowen's, the structural detour is Minuchin's, and the family homeostasis is Jackson's, and
  the condition the trap actually requires, a symptom functional for the system, expressing and stabilising a
  dysfunction of the whole, so that treating the member alone preserves it, is carried as carefully as the
  finding itself.
