# Play: The Anticommons

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Anticommons.
A resource that belongs to so many that it belongs, usefully, to no one, where every owner holds a right to exclude and none holds a right to use ... so a shop that would feed a family, a drug that would save a life, a block that would house a street, needs every last owner to say yes, and is stopped forever by any single one who says no, and the thing that too many people own sits empty, wanted by all and moved by none.

## Arc

The bend is this: everyone is within his rights, and the resource sits idle.
It runs on an inversion of the commons.
Where the common field had no owner and no one could be shut out, so it was used to death, the anticommons resource has too many owners and each can shut everyone out, so it is used not at all.
To put it to work, a single yes is not enough: every owner must agree at once, and each, knowing the whole deal dies without him, holds his one piece as if it were the whole, and names a price to match. Add those prices up and the tolls stack past what the resource is worth, so the deal that would have served everyone never closes, and the one owner who names a fair price to let it live is simply the one who gets less than the holdout beside him.
None of it is theft. Each owner only holds what is his and only says no to what is his to refuse, the fragmentation is lawful, and the gridlock is the sum of a hundred lawful noes.
Gather the pieces back into one hand, pool the rights, or override a veto by a taking, and the resource can be used again: but each of those must buy out or break a right that was perfectly good, so the cure is an owner's veto overridden, and the resource that too many owned is freed only by unmaking the ownership.

## Triggers

**[The Fragmenting](plot_the_fragmenting.md)**
One valuable resource passes into many hands at once, and the registry records each owner with a clear right to exclude. The Holder takes his piece and means no harm by it, and the Reckoner notes that every piece is now a veto over the whole. Closes when the title is divided and each fragment can stop all the rest.

**[The Holdout](plot_the_holdout.md)**
Cue: someone wants to put the resource to use and must gather every owner's yes. The Assembler goes owner to owner, the Holdout knows the deal dies without his one piece and names a price to match, and the Reckoner adds the asking prices and finds they stack past the whole's worth. Closes when the tolls sum above the value and the deal cannot close.

**[The Idle Resource](plot_the_idle_resource.md)**
Cue: the deal has failed and the resource stands unused. The Seller named a fair price to let it live and got less than the holdout beside him, and the Holder keeps his piece while the shop stays dark and the kiosks crowd the pavement outside. Closes when cooperation is punished and the resource sits idle.

**[The Pool](plot_the_pool.md)**
Cue: the Consolidator moves to gather the fragments into one hand, pool the rights, or override a veto by a taking, so the resource can be used again. The scattered title becomes a single one. Closes when the resource is freed under one owner ... or no holder will sell and no taking will come, and it stays empty for good.

## Stakes

Whether the pieces can be gathered before the resource is wasted for good.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward use, toward one more no. The production cannot make a single owner's fair price close the deal, because the holdout beside him will always ask for more and still be within his rights. It can only show that no one stole, that each owner only held and only refused what was his, that the owner who named a fair price lost most, and that the one thing that would have freed the resource, all the pieces gathered into one hand, is the very ownership that too many owners, each lawfully clutching his own, could not let go.
