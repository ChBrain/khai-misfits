# Piece: The Holdout Premium

## Taxonomy

[The Anticommons](play_the_anticommons.md). The extraction: the essential owner pricing his one piece as the whole, and the fair seller left worse off beside him.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the table](place_the_table.md), in the price named last and highest.
Where the owner who knows the deal dies without him asks accordingly.

## Load Bearing

Without it each owner would take a fair share and the deal would close.
With it the owner whose yes is needed last holds out for the difference between fair and desperate, and the one who named a fair price early finds his restraint only funded the holdout's take. The Holdout Premium is load bearing because it is the strategic core of the anticommons (Heller), and it carries the play's cruel mirror of the commons' punished steward: there the one who spared the grass fed the takers, here the one who prices fairly feeds the holdout, so cooperation is not rewarded but harvested.

## Apparent

A hard but honest bargain.
The holdout is asking only what his essential piece is worth to a deal that genuinely needs it, which is a fair market price, so the premium looks like shrewd dealing rather than the thing that kills the deal.

## Yearbook

Charged by [the holdout](persona_the_holdout.md) through [holding out](process_holding_out.md), enabled by [the veto](piece_the_veto.md), suffered by [the seller](persona_the_seller.md).
It is the correct price that ruins: every essential owner charging the Holdout Premium at once is what builds [the stacked toll](piece_the_stacked_toll.md).
