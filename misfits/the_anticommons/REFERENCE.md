# The Anticommons: Reference

The research warrant for [The Anticommons](play_the_anticommons.md). The term is Michelman's, the tragedy framing Heller's,
the mirror of Hardin's; the play runs on the economics of fragmented ownership, the complementary-monopoly
math beneath it, and the symmetry that makes it the exact inverse of the commons, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the tragedy of the anticommons as a **systemic system**: a resource whose ownership is
fragmented into many rights to exclude goes unused, not destroyed, because putting it to work needs
every owner's consent at once, and each owner, holding a veto over the whole, can block the rest,
so the resource is underused exactly as a commons is overused. The play models the documented
mechanics, the single resource divided into many exclusion rights, the veto each fragment carries,
the holdout who prices his one piece as the whole, the stacked tolls that sum past the resource's
worth, the transaction cost of gathering every yes, and the consolidation that alone restores use at
the cost of the ownership that defined it, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale
of greed. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: where a
resource is owned by too many with the right to exclude and no single right to use, individually
lawful refusals sum to collective waste, and the remedy is neither inevitable ruin nor a free market
but the pieces gathered back into one hand. It is the exact mirror of the house's Commons: not a
field with no owner used to death, but a resource with too many owners left to sit.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  owner as a thief, because the source finding is precisely that no theft is needed: each owner holds
  only what is his, refuses only what is his to refuse, and the holdout who prices his piece as the
  whole is within his rights to ask. The misfit is that a hundred lawful noes over one resource are a
  resource no one can use.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the market clearing itself. Consolidation
  can free the resource, but only by buying out or overriding a right that was perfectly good, closing
  what was owned by many into what is owned by one, so the reckoning is a veto overridden, not a
  costless recovery, and the pieces stay gathered only while no one splits them again.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and Heller's
  inversion of Hardin, with Buchanan and Yoon's proof of the symmetry, is carried as carefully as the
  Commons carries Hardin himself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Divided Title](piece_the_divided_title.md)**: carries the fragmented resource (Michelman; Heller),
  one valuable thing split into many separate ownership rights.
- **[The Veto](piece_the_veto.md)**: carries the right to exclude (Michelman), the power each fragment holds
  to block the use of the whole.
- **[The Holdout Premium](piece_the_holdout_premium.md)**: carries the holdout problem (Heller), the essential
  owner pricing his one piece as the whole, and the fair seller left worse off beside him.
- **[The Stacked Toll](piece_the_stacked_toll.md)**: carries complementary monopoly (Cournot; Buchanan & Yoon),
  the independently set tolls that sum past what the resource is worth.
- **[The Assembly Cost](piece_the_assembly_cost.md)**: carries the transaction cost of assembly (Heller;
  Heller & Eisenberg), rising with the number of owners until it exceeds the value.
- **[The Idle Resource](piece_the_idle_resource.md)**: carries the underused resource (Heller), the empty
  storefront and the shelved drug, and the cruel term that the fair seller is left worst off.
- **[The Anticommons](piece_the_anticommons.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Heller; Buchanan & Yoon), the
  overowned resource sitting idle, and its remedy in consolidation at a cost, the veto that must be
  overridden to free what too many owned.
