# Piece: The Stacked Toll

## Taxonomy

[The Anticommons](play_the_anticommons.md). The math: the independently set prices that sum past what the resource is worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the table](place_the_table.md), in the running total that climbs above the value.
Where the asking prices are added in the open and found too large together.

## Load Bearing

Without it the deal's failure would be one owner's fault, and could be argued down.
With it no owner is at fault: each toll is a fair price, and it is the sum that is ruinous, because complementary owners each setting a price independently overshoot the figure a single owner would set. The Stacked Toll is load bearing because it is Cournot's complementary-monopoly result standing under the whole play (Cournot; Buchanan & Yoon), the proof that the anticommons overprices exactly as the commons underprices, so the gridlock is arithmetic and not greed, and cannot be talked out of any single seat.

## Apparent

Just the total of a set of fair offers.
Each price in the stack is reasonable and defensible on its own, so the sum looks like an unlucky addition rather than a structural certainty, and the table keeps hoping the next round will bring it down.

## Yearbook

Summed by [the reckoner](persona_the_reckoner.md) through [holding out](process_holding_out.md), built of [the holdout premium](piece_the_holdout_premium.md) charged by each.
It is the number that kills the deal: the Stacked Toll is what leaves the resource as [the idle resource](piece_the_idle_resource.md), priced correctly out of use.
