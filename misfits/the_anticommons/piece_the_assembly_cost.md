# Piece: The Assembly Cost

## Taxonomy

[The Anticommons](play_the_anticommons.md). The friction: the cost of gathering every owner's yes, rising with their number until it exceeds the value.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the table](place_the_table.md), in the rounds, the lawyers, the search for the last owner.
Where reaching everyone is itself expensive, before any price is paid.

## Load Bearing

Without it a resource with many owners could be reassembled whenever it was worth it.
With it the mere work of finding, reaching, and closing with every owner climbs with the count, so even absent any holdout the transaction cost alone can pass the worth, and a resource split finely enough cannot be gathered at a profit. The Assembly Cost is load bearing because it is the transaction-cost half of Heller's tragedy (Heller; Heller & Eisenberg), the reason a patent thicket or a fractioned estate stalls even among willing owners: not a bad actor, just too many yeses to buy.

## Apparent

The ordinary cost of doing a deal.
Negotiation takes time and lawyers cost money, which is true of every transaction, so the assembly cost hides as overhead, and its growth with each added owner passes for the normal price of complexity.

## Yearbook

Borne by [the assembler](persona_the_assembler.md) through [holding out](process_holding_out.md), rooted in [the divided title](piece_the_divided_title.md).
It is the toll that needs no holdout: the Assembly Cost adds to [the stacked toll](piece_the_stacked_toll.md), so that even fair, willing owners can leave the resource unassembled.
