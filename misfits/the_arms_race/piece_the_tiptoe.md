# Piece: The Tiptoe

## Taxonomy

[The Arms Race](play_the_arms_race.md). The deadweight, the cost paid in full by all for a standing not one of them improved, the whole field risen and no one seeing better.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the tally](place_the_tally.md), where the cost is summed and stands whole against a gain of zero.
Felt across [the field](place_the_field.md), where every contender bears the strain of a spend that bought the group nothing.

## Load Bearing

Without it the cancellation would be merely futile, effort wasted but no one worse off.
With it the futility becomes loss: the standings unchanged and the cost still paid, everyone poorer for a rank no one raised. The Tiptoe is load bearing because it is the deadweight the structure produces (Frank): a crowd that stands on tiptoe to see a parade sees no better than seated, but now all are strained, so the piece carries the real, unrecovered price of a competition that changed nothing, the harm that makes the race not just pointless but costly.

## Apparent

The price of staying competitive, a cost each had to bear to keep its place.
Seen by each contender, the tiptoe looks like a necessary expense, and the truth that the whole field could sit down together and lose nothing but the strain, that the cost buys no one anything the group did not already have, stays hidden behind how real the threat of falling behind feels to each one alone.

## Yearbook

Borne by all through [ratcheting](process_ratcheting.md), the strain no single contender can set down.
It is the loss the race leaves behind: the Tiptoe is the price paid for [the cancellation](piece_the_cancellation.md)'s empty result, the cost [the ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md) forbids anyone to escape alone, and the burden [the truce](piece_the_truce.md) alone can lift, by letting the whole field sit down at once.
