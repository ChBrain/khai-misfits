# Play: The Arms Race

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Arms Race.
You want to get ahead, so you spend a little more than the others: more arms, more hours, more polish, more show, and it works, you pull in front ... except the prize is not a thing you can hold but a place in a line, and the others, facing the same sense, spend a little more too. When everyone has raised their effort by the same measure, the line is exactly as it was: the same ones ahead, the same ones behind, and every one of them poorer by what the climb cost. Nobody blundered and nobody cheated, and still the whole field stands on tiptoe to see a parade no better than before, unable to sit down because whoever sits first sees nothing at all.

## Arc

The bend is this: the effort that would put one ahead puts none ahead when all make it, and yet each must make it, because the one who does not is the only one who truly falls.
It runs on a prize that is a position, not a good.
Some things are wanted for themselves, and more of them is more for everyone. Rank is not one of them: to be ahead is to be ahead of someone, so the top is scarce by its nature, and no amount of effort can make more of it, only redistribute who holds it. When one contender spends to rise, the gain is real but it is taken from the others' places, so their answer is not envy but arithmetic: match the spend, or drop.
And so they match, and the spending cancels. Every contender is higher in effort and none is higher in rank, the gains netted exactly to zero across the field, while the cost is netted to nothing of the sort: it is paid, in full, by all, for a standing not one of them improved.
None of it is folly. Getting ahead is a fair aim, spending to compete is rational, and matching a rival who spends is simple prudence, because the field is a ratchet: to stand still while others climb is not to hold your place but to lose it, so no one can stop alone, and the one who tries is not the wise one but the sucker, the only player who both pays nothing and falls.
Bind every contender to the same limit at once, an arms-control treaty, a cap, a tax on the spending itself, and the race can be halted where all are freed of the cost and none has lost a rung: but the limit must catch everyone or it catches no one, each has a live reason to slip it and pull ahead, and it caps a freedom every contender would rather keep for themselves and deny the rest, so the peace is a thing built against every private incentive that made the race.

## Triggers

**[The Climb](plot_the_climb.md)**
A contender spends a little more than the rest to get ahead, and it works: the effort buys a place further up the line. The Contender escalates, and the Rank yields a higher place, taken from someone else's. Closes when the spend has bought a real gain in position.

**[The Match](plot_the_match.md)**
Cue: the rival faces the same choice and makes the same move. The Rival matches the spend, and the two gains cancel, so both stand higher in effort and neither in rank, the line restored exactly as it was. Closes when the escalation is general and the standings are unchanged.

**[The Ratchet](plot_the_ratchet.md)**
Cue: someone reasons that if all stopped, all would be better off, and tries to stop. The Holdout sits out the next round and does not fall level but falls behind, so the others cannot follow without dropping too, and the race locks on. Closes when it is plain that no one can stand down alone.

**[The Tally](plot_the_tally.md)**
Cue: the whole spend is added up against the whole gain. The Rank shows the line unmoved while the Tally shows the cost paid in full, the effort netted to zero and the price netted to everything. Closes when the race is seen for a cost all bore and none was bettered by.

**[The Truce](plot_the_truce.md)**
Cue: a limit is proposed to bind every contender at once. The Broker would cap the spend for all, freeing the field of the cost at no one's loss of rank, against each contender's live reason to slip the cap and pull ahead. Closes when the limit holds and the race is stood down ... or one player defects and the spending resumes.

## Stakes

Whether effort spent to get ahead ever leaves the field ahead, or only ever higher and poorer in the same order.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the spend, which each contender is right to make and all are ruined by making, and away from the standing down, which each is right to want and none can risk alone. The production cannot make rank grow to reward the effort poured at it, because rank is a position and a position is scarce by definition, so more effort only moves who holds it and never how much there is. It can only show that no one erred, that getting ahead is fair and matching a rival is prudent and stopping alone is merely to lose, and that the one move that frees the field, a limit laid on all at once, must be built against every private incentive that drives the race, so the cost is real and the gain is zero and the peace is the hardest thing of all to hold.
