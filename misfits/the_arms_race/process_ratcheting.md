# Process: Ratcheting

## Taxonomy

[The Arms Race](play_the_arms_race.md). The lock that lets the spend rise and never fall, so no contender can stand down alone.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A contender reasoning that if all stopped, all would gain, and moving to stop.
At [the field](place_the_field.md), the holdout holding its effort steady while the others keep raising theirs.

## Direction

Toward a spend that only climbs.
Because standing still while the field rises is losing rank, the one who stops does not pull the others level but drops behind, so the ratcheting drives restraint into sacrifice, letting the effort rise freely and refusing to let it fall, until the level reached is the level kept and the only safe move is to match.

## Lever

The penalty on stopping first.
Because unilateral restraint costs the restrained and helps no one, each contender is held in the race by the plain fact that leaving it alone means falling, so the ratcheting works not by forcing anyone to spend but by punishing the one who does not, and the lock holds on that asymmetry.

## Echo

A race that cannot be left by one.
The holdout has fallen, the others have their proof that stepping off is dropping, and the spend sits at its new height with no way down, so what began as one player's clear judgement ends as the whole field's evidence that no one can be first to stop.
