# Piece: The Ratchet

## Taxonomy

[The Arms Race](play_the_arms_race.md). The lock that lets the spend rise and not fall, the compulsion that makes stopping alone a loss.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the field](place_the_field.md), where the holdout tries to lower its effort and drops a place instead of levelling the line.
Answered only at [the table](place_the_table.md), where a limit on all at once can do what no contender can do alone.

## Load Bearing

Without it a clear head could end the race by simply stopping, and the trap would not hold.
With it the waste is inescapable from within: because standing still while others climb is falling, restraint is punished and the effort can only go up. The Ratchet is load bearing because it is why the race persists against everyone's interest (Dawkins and Krebs): each is kept in not by force but by the penalty on being first to stop, so the piece carries the asymmetry that locks the spend at its height and leaves no unilateral way down, the reason the tally can be read by all and change nothing.

## Apparent

Simple necessity, the plain fact that you cannot afford to stop.
Seen by each contender, the ratchet looks like an obvious constraint rather than a trap, and the truth that it is the very thing making a shared, wanted peace unreachable, that it turns each one's prudence into everyone's cage, stays hidden behind how reasonable it is not to be the one who falls.

## Yearbook

Met by [the holdout](persona_the_holdout.md) through [ratcheting](process_ratcheting.md), the lock that punishes stopping first.
It is why the race cannot be left by one: the Ratchet is what forbids lowering [the escalation](piece_the_escalation.md) alone, what keeps [the tiptoe](piece_the_tiptoe.md)'s cost in place, and the reason only [the truce](piece_the_truce.md), a limit on all together, can undo what any single contender cannot.
