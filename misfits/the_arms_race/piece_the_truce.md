# Piece: The Truce

## Taxonomy

[The Arms Race](play_the_arms_race.md). The mutual limit, the cap laid on all at once that frees the field at the price of binding each against its own incentive.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the table](place_the_table.md), where the limit is struck to bind every contender together.
Set against [the field](place_the_field.md), whose every pull is toward breaking it and getting ahead.

## Load Bearing

Without it the race has no exit and the play no remedy, only a cost that renews each round.
With it the misfit is answered, and answered at a price: a cap that catches all at once, keeping the standings and lifting the cost. The Truce is load bearing because it is the one correction the structure allows and the hardest to hold (Schelling; Frank): it works only if it binds everyone, runs against each player's live reason to defect, and fences a freedom each would keep for itself, so the piece carries both the escape and the fragility of it, a peace built against every drive that made the race.

## Apparent

An obvious agreement, a deal any sensible field would simply sign and keep.
Seen from outside, the truce looks easy, a matter of everyone agreeing to stop, and the truth that it must catch all or none and stands only on trust and enforcement, that each signatory keeps a standing reason to break it, stays hidden behind how plainly better the peace is than the race.

## Yearbook

Brokered by [the broker](persona_the_broker.md) through [capping](process_capping.md), the limit that frees the field.
It is the exit and its cost: the Truce is what lifts [the tiptoe](piece_the_tiptoe.md) from all at once, what undoes the lock of [the ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md) that no contender could undo alone, and the peace that leaves [the rank](piece_the_rank.md) exactly as it was because stopping the spend never threatened the order.
