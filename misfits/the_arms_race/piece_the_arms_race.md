# Piece: The Arms Race

## Taxonomy

[The Arms Race](play_the_arms_race.md). The phenomenon whole: a field spending real resources to move no one, and its remedy in a limit held against every private drive that made it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Born on [the field](place_the_field.md), where the escalation is spent, and read at [the ladder](place_the_ladder.md), where it moves nothing.
Reckoned at [the tally](place_the_tally.md) and answered at [the table](place_the_table.md), where the only exit is built.

## Load Bearing

Without it the other pieces are parts without the mechanism that binds them.
With it the whole trap stands in one object: a prize that is position, an escalation that is rational, a cancellation that nets it to zero, a cost paid regardless, a ratchet that forbids stopping alone, and a truce that frees the field only by binding all. The Arms Race is load bearing because it is the structure entire (Hirsch; Schelling): not a failure of sense but its sum, each contender right to spend and the field ruined by the spending, correctable only by a mutual limit that stands against every incentive that drove it.

## Apparent

A contest of wills, to be won by whoever wants it most and spends hardest.
Seen from inside, the arms race looks like a competition with a winner waiting at the end, and the truth that it has no winner, that the effort nets to zero and only the cost is real, that the sole good outcome is for everyone to stop at once, stays hidden behind how surely each contender feels that one more push will do it.

## Yearbook

The trap entire, staged across the run.
The Arms Race is [the escalation](piece_the_escalation.md) that buys a rung of [the rank](piece_the_rank.md) and loses it to [the cancellation](piece_the_cancellation.md), the [tiptoe](piece_the_tiptoe.md) all pay and the [ratchet](piece_the_ratchet.md) none can leave alone, and the waste that only [the truce](piece_the_truce.md) can end, by a limit laid on every contender at once.
