# Piece: The Indispensable

## Taxonomy

[The Bus Factor](play_the_bus_factor.md). The reward for being the one who cannot be replaced.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Wherever the org shows it values the expert: the raise, the retention call, the please do not leave.
The standing the Keystone earns precisely by being the single point, valued in exact proportion to how much would break without them.

## Load Bearing

Without it the expert has every reason to share the load and lighten it.
With it the expert has a quiet reason not to, because the Indispensable is the incentive pointing the wrong way: an organisation rewards the person it cannot lose, so being irreplaceable pays, and the surest route to irreplaceable is to remain the only one who knows. It need not be cynical. The expert may long to offload it and still feel, correctly, that their standing rests on being the one asked, so the system that would most help the team is the system that would most reduce the expert, and no one has to choose that reduction on purpose for it never to happen.

## Apparent

Recognition of value.
It looks like an organisation rightly prizing its best people, keeping its stars happy. Valuing expertise is good. Valuing it as a single point, and paying most for what is least shared, quietly funds the fragility.

## Yearbook

Conferred on [the keystone](persona_the_keystone.md), and felt by [the understudy](persona_the_understudy.md) as a door that will not open.
It is the incentive twin of [the fast path](piece_the_fast_path.md): one makes not-sharing quick, the other makes it pay, so the concentration is pushed by convenience and pulled by reward, and the expert stays the only shelf in the library because both the day and the ladder ask them to.
