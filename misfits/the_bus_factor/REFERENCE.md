# The Bus Factor: Reference

The research warrant for [The Bus Factor](play_the_bus_factor.md). The term is practitioner
vocabulary for the number of people who would have to be lost before a project stalls; the
mechanics the play runs on are grounded in the tacit-knowledge and knowledge-concentration
literature cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the bus factor as a **systemic system**: a team that runs faster the more it leans on
one person's knowledge, and more fragile by the same motion, because the fast way to use the
knowledge is the way that does not share it. The play models the documented mechanics, tacit
knowledge that resists being written down, the concentration of a project's understanding into
one or two heads, the under-provision of documentation as thankless and never urgent, and the
key-person risk accepted because it is certain to cost now and only possible to cost later, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a hoarder. The governing finding of
the source literature is the play's governing law: knowledge routes to whoever already holds it,
because that is fastest, so each use deepens its concentration instead of distributing it, and
dependence and fragility rise together, one direction, until the holder is gone.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  expert as a hoarder, because the source finding is precisely that no one need hoard: answering
  directly is generous and fast, documenting is thankless and never urgent, and leaning on the one
  who knows is what a sensible team does. The misfit is that the helpful, fast move is the one that
  does not share.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into foresight closing the risk in time. The
  reckoning is the departure and a successor excavating an undocumented system, because writing it
  down was never quick, never urgent, and never anyone's job, not a conscientious act arriving to
  prevent the loss.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.
