# Piece: The Bus

## Taxonomy

[The Bus Factor](play_the_bus_factor.md). The accumulated whole: the day the one head leaves, and the system with it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

The gap between what the team runs on and what it can survive losing.
On one side, a system operating perfectly, fast and unblocked. On the other, a single chair, and everything the system needs to keep working sitting inside the person in it. The Bus is the standing distance between the two, and the certainty that one day it closes.

## Load Bearing

Without it the play is a run of sensible fast answers and one unlucky resignation.
With it those are one object: a team whose whole knowledge lived in a single uncopied head, held whole by nobody once that head is gone, because the seat that carried the tacit half was the only seat that had it, and no page, no backup, no second knower was ever actually made. It does not require a bus. A notice, a better offer, a burnout, a retirement: any exit empties the chair, and the knowledge does not resign, it simply is not there the next morning, and was never anywhere else.

## Apparent

An ordinary departure.
From the outside one person left a job, which happens constantly and recovers. The trap is invisible until the moment it is total: nothing looks fragile while the expert is answering, and everything is unmaintainable the day they are not.

## Yearbook

The team built it in good faith: [the fast path](piece_the_fast_path.md) taken, [the backlog item](piece_the_backlog_item.md) deferred, [the indispensable](piece_the_indispensable.md) rewarded, [the paper backup](piece_the_paper_backup.md) never made real.
[The excavator](persona_the_excavator.md) was the first to sit at the empty desk and try to read a system that was only ever spoken, and rebuilt a partial, wrong copy at a cost that dwarfed the writing-down never done. Nobody had a name for the risk while it was answering every question ... until the morning it could not.
