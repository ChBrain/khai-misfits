# Piece: The Paper Backup

## Taxonomy

[The Bus Factor](play_the_bus_factor.md). The named second person who was never made real.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On the org chart and the risk register, a second name beside the system.
The nominated backup, the also-familiar, the in-case: a redundancy that exists in the box and nowhere in anyone's actual knowledge.

## Load Bearing

Without it the single point is nakedly single and someone might act on the alarm.
With it the alarm is silenced without being answered, because the Paper Backup lets the organisation believe it is covered while it is not: a name is written where a second knower should be, and the writing is mistaken for the knowing. The backup was never given the hours to shadow, the tickets to struggle through, the failures that make expertise, because those hours always lost to delivery, so the redundancy is a label on an empty box, and it does its worst work by making the risk look handled.

## Apparent

Coverage.
It looks like the responsible thing, a bus-factor mitigation in place, a second pair of hands ready. Naming a backup sounds like having one. The gap between the name and the knowledge is the whole exposure, hidden behind a filled-in cell.

## Yearbook

Worn by [the understudy](persona_the_understudy.md), who is the backup on paper and knows they could not actually take the wheel.
It is the fiction that makes [the single point of failure](piece_the_single_point.md) survivable to look at: with a backup named, the risk owner can accept the risk, so the label that was meant to reduce the exposure is what lets everyone stop worrying about it.
