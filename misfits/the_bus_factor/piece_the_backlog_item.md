# Piece: The Backlog Item

## Taxonomy

[The Bus Factor](play_the_bus_factor.md). Write the documentation: filed, agreed, and never done.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On [the backlog](place_the_backlog.md), near the bottom, its priority a permanent someday.
The ticket everyone approves of and no one pulls: document the system, write the runbook, capture the tribal knowledge, always this quarter's intention and last quarter's too.

## Load Bearing

Without it the not-writing would be a visible choice and someone might make the other one.
With it the not-writing is disguised as a plan: the Backlog Item is how an organisation agrees the knowledge should be shared and ensures it never is, because a task that everyone endorses and nothing prioritises is a task that lives forever in the state of about-to-happen. It never loses the argument, it simply never wins the sprint, and there is always a fire that outranks it, so the copy that would have saved the team is perpetually one iteration away.

## Apparent

A plan to fix it.
It looks like the problem is handled: it is on the board, it is acknowledged, it will get done. Being on the board reads as progress. It is the opposite: it is the alibi that lets the fast path continue guilt-free, because the sharing is, officially, coming.

## Yearbook

Endorsed by everyone, pulled by no one, deprioritised each sprint by [the leaner](persona_the_leaner.md).
It is [the deferral](process_deferral.md) made visible: the one act that would break the concentration, written down and ranked below every delivery, so the knowledge stays in the head and the plan to free it stays on the list, and both are still there on the day the head walks out.
