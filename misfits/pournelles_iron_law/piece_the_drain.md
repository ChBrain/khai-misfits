# Piece: The Drain

## Taxonomy

[Pournelle's Iron Law](play_pournelles_iron_law.md). The mission-devoted losing influence, leaving, or let go, and the served receiving less.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the field](place_the_field.md), where the mission empties.
Where the missionary's say runs out and the served's portion thins.

## Load Bearing

Without it the capture would be a change of hands with no cost, and the mission might carry on.
With it the takeover has a price paid in the goal: the drain is the ebb of the goal-devoted from a house that pays them nothing, and the thinning of the service to the ones it is for. The Drain is load bearing because it is where the law is felt (Pournelle's "less and less influence, sometimes eliminated entirely"): it is not a purge but an attrition, the steady loss of those whose devotion earns no standing, so the mission is hollowed quietly, and the body can be at its strongest exactly as the goal recedes.

## Apparent

Ordinary turnover and tightening times.
People moving on and a service trimmed look like the normal churn and thrift of any organisation, so the drain reads as attrition and budgeting, and the truth that it is the mission bleeding out while the body fills, that the same purse pays for the strength and the loss, stays hidden behind the look of a place merely running lean.

## Yearbook

Suffered by [the missionary](persona_the_missionary.md) and [the served](persona_the_served.md) through [draining](process_draining.md), felt by [Come for the Service](plan_come_for_the_service.md).
It is the cost of the capture, paid in the goal: the Drain empties the side of [the divide](piece_the_divide.md) that served the mission, and is the near face of [the turn inward](piece_the_turn_inward.md).
