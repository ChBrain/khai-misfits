# Pournelle's Iron Law: Reference

The research warrant for [Pournelle's Iron Law](play_pournelles_iron_law.md). The title is Jerry
Pournelle's Iron Law of Bureaucracy; the play runs on the account of organisational oligarchy and
goal succession, from Michels through Downs and the public-choice literature, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging Pournelle's Iron Law as a **social system**: an organisation with two kinds of member, those
devoted to the goal it exists to serve and those devoted to the organisation itself, in which control
is made of the internal things, budget, headcount, process, standing, so that tending the body accrues
the currency of control and serving the mission does not, and the org-devoted therefore win command
from the mission-devoted whatever the organisation is for. The play models the documented mechanics,
the divide between the two devotions, the internal currency earned by tending and not by serving, the
capture by which a fair-looking promotion converts that currency into control, the drain by which the
goal-devoted lose influence and leave, the turn inward by which the body comes to work for its own
perpetuation, and the maintenance that is real upkeep tangled inseparably with self-service, as the
load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of villainy. The governing finding of the source
literature is the play's governing law: in any bureaucracy the people devoted to the benefit of the
bureaucracy itself come to control it, and those devoted to its goals have less and less influence,
and sometimes are eliminated entirely, because the levers of internal advancement reward devotion to
the body over service to the mission. It is a sibling of the house's Shirky Principle, and its distinct
engine: not a body that preserves the problem it was built to solve, but a body whose internal
selection hands control to the members loyal to it over the members loyal to its purpose.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  org-devoted as cynics, because the source finding is precisely that no cynicism is required: the
  sincere custodian who protects the institution rises for the same structural reason the gaming
  careerist does, so the law selects a devotion and not a vice, and cannot be cured by purging the bad
  actors. The mission-devoted are not spotless either, since the single-minded server of the goal
  neglects the real upkeep the body needs and hands the custodian a true reason to take the wheel. The
  misfit is that a fair promotion, read off a real record, hands the organisation to itself.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a clean purge or a costless reform. The one
  move that works is to re-aim what advancement rewards, from the body tended to the mission served,
  and it must be enacted by the very org-devoted the law has already installed, who are asked to
  disarm themselves, against a tending whose genuine maintenance cannot be told from its self-service,
  so the hand that strips the capture risks the scaffolding the mission stands on. The reckoning is a
  reform aimed through the captured and cut without a clean seam, not a villain removed.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the iron law, the oligarchy thesis, the
  goal-succession account, and the budget-and-headcount mechanics are theirs, and the condition the
  law actually requires, control made of the internal things one devotion accrues and the other
  cannot, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Divide](piece_the_divide.md)**: carries the two member types (Downs), devotion to the goal set against
  devotion to the organisation itself.
- **[The Internal Currency](piece_the_internal_currency.md)**: carries the coin of control (Niskanen on budget; Parkinson
  on headcount), earned by tending the body and not by serving the goal.
- **[The Capture](piece_the_capture.md)**: carries the rise of the ruling few (Michels), the fair-looking promotion that
  converts internal standing into control.
- **[The Drain](piece_the_drain.md)**: carries the loss of the goal-devoted (Pournelle; Downs), the ebb of influence
  and the members eliminated or gone.
- **[The Turn Inward](piece_the_turn_inward.md)**: carries the organisation's self-perpetuation (Michels; Parkinson), the
  body run for its own survival with the mission retained as the story.
- **[The Maintenance](piece_the_maintenance.md)**: carries the genuine institutional upkeep (Weber), the real care tangled
  inseparably with the self-serving tending, and the hazard it poses to the cure.
- **[Pournelle's Iron Law](piece_pournelles_iron_law.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Pournelle), control captured by
  devotion to the body over devotion to the goal, and its remedy in re-aimed levers at a cost.
