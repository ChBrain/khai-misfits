# Plot: The Reckoning

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Rolls](place_the_rolls.md), the eligible counted on one line and the enrolled counted on another.
The gap between them ordinary at first glance, until the names behind it are read.

## Action

[Deterring](process_deterring.md): [The Applicant](persona_the_applicant.md), holding [the applicant's seat](position_applicant.md), is one abandonment among many, and the many are not spread evenly across the rolls.

## Stage

[The Applicant](persona_the_applicant.md), on [Try to Claim](plan_try_to_claim.md), gives up alongside every other applicant who ran short of time, health, or ease, each abandonment small and each one adding to [the take-up gap](piece_the_take_up_gap.md).
The poorest, the sickest, the least practiced with a form give up soonest and most, so the gap widens into [the regressive load](piece_the_regressive_load.md), heaviest on the rolls where the programme was needed most.

## Tension

No single abandonment explains the pattern, and no clerk at any counter chose it.
There is nothing to fault at the scale of one applicant: each simply ran out of what claiming cost. But the sum is visible only in the rolls, so the plot closes with take-up fallen below eligibility, a programme built to serve having turned away the neediest it was built for, the exclusion authored by no one and owned by everyone who added a reasonable step.
