# Process: Adding

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The step laid onto the programme one defensible ask at a time, run by [the Administrator](persona_the_administrator.md) on [Add the Step](plan_add_the_step.md), the engine behind [The Requirement](plot_the_requirement.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A risk the programme must answer for: fraud, error, a claim paid to someone who should not have received it.
At [the Rolls](place_the_rolls.md), where the Auditor can point to the gap, and the duty to close it falls to whoever adds the next requirement.

## Direction

Toward one more verification, one more form, one more wait, each named for the risk just found.
Because every risk that can be named can be met with a requirement, and no requirement, once reasoned to, is easily refused, adding runs forward with no natural stop, a step for fraud followed by a step for error followed by a step for accuracy, each the last one's fair successor.

## Lever

The defensible reason meeting the duty to guard the funds.
Because the reason is real and the duty is real, each single step is sound on its own terms, and adding turns that soundness directly into motion: what can be justified gets added, and once added it stands beside every step justified before it.

## Echo

The interaction one step longer, and the desk's conscience clear.
Each requirement is judged only against the risk it answers, never against the pile it joins, so the Administrator can defend every step in the stack without ever totalling the stack, the ask reasonable in isolation and the sum, from the desk, simply not a question anyone was asked to answer.
