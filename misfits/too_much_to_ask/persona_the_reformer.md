# Persona: The Reformer

## Taxonomy

[Reformer](position_reformer.md). The one who would lower the burden so the benefit reaches its people, auto-enrolling, pre-filling, dropping a step, against the cost the Auditor names.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Willing. Practical. Ready to spend to reach people.
Is the one who would enrol the eligible automatically, pre-fill the forms, cut a verification, or staff a helper at the counter, so that claiming costs the applicant less.
No naivety in it: only someone who has counted the eligible turned away and decided that reaching them is worth what it costs.

## Action

Eases the burden, on [Ease the Burden](plan_ease_the_burden.md), auto-enrolling where it can, pre-filling what is known, dropping the step that deters more than it catches.
Spends real effort and money to reach people rather than filter them.
Does what a reformer does: buys back take-up by lifting the cost of claiming, and pays for it in the risk the steps controlled.

## Shadow

Not a reformer who denies the cost, one who pays it and eases anyway, and that is the whole difficulty: each step lifted lets back a measure of the fraud or error it guarded, and the Auditor can name that measure exactly.
Because the burden cannot be lowered without admitting more of what the steps controlled, the Reformer must justify letting some error back in against a benefit reaching people that no ledger tallies as cleanly, so easing the burden is not a costless correction but a standing choice to trade a countable risk for an uncounted good, made against every audit that shows the leak and no audit that shows the unclaimed.
It buys back the take-up the steps deterred, and buys it with the very risk the steps were there to catch.

## Tell

Says "yes, some error comes back, and I will take that to reach the people we are turning away."
Means it, and knows the leak is countable and the reached are not, easing the burden against a scoreboard that shows only what the easing costs.
