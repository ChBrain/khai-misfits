# Plot: The Ordeal

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Kitchen Table](place_the_kitchen_table.md), the papers spread out and the forms not yet begun.
The steps waiting together, not one at a time, and the evening already short.

## Action

[Burdening](process_burdening.md): [The Applicant](persona_the_applicant.md), holding [the applicant's seat](position_applicant.md), works through the asks at the table and at [the counter](place_the_counter.md), and each one costs before it is done.

## Stage

[The Applicant](persona_the_applicant.md), on [Try to Claim](plan_try_to_claim.md), finds out what is owed and what the rules demand, and pays [the learning cost](piece_the_learning_cost.md) before a form is filled.
Gathers the documents, keeps the appointments, and sits the wait at [the counter](place_the_counter.md), paying [the compliance cost](piece_the_compliance_cost.md) for doing what is asked.
Feels the stigma of asking and the stress of proving need, paying [the psychological cost](piece_the_psychological_cost.md) for needing the help at all.

## Tension

No single cost is much: an evening's reading, a folder of papers, an hour at the counter.
There is nothing to fault: the Applicant is only doing what is asked, one step after the next. But the steps are not paid singly, they are paid together, so the plot closes with the Applicant worn by the accreted learning, compliance, and psychological costs, the benefit not yet claimed and the effort still mounting.
