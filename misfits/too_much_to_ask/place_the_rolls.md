# Place: The Rolls

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The register where eligibility is set against take-up, the ledger [The Reckoning](plot_the_reckoning.md) is read from, kept by [the Auditor](persona_the_auditor.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A column of names enrolled, and a count of how many were thought eligible.
The take-up rate, printed to a decimal, and beside it the error rate the audit caught, the two figures the Rolls were built to report.
Everything counted here is counted precisely.

## Holds

The take-up figures, and the error the Auditor found and corrected.
Here the programme's account of itself is kept, so the Rolls hold what was claimed, by whom, and what was denied or clawed back, a record complete on its own terms: every enrolled name checked, every caught error logged.
It is the ledger the programme trusts to say whether it is working.

## Offers

A full view of who claimed, precise and auditable.
Because the Rolls count only what came through the Counter, they offer a clean, defensible account of the enrolled: exact numbers, checked against exact rules, ready for the report that says the funds were guarded.
On its own terms, this offer is kept in full.

## Withheld

Any line for the eligible who never claimed.
The Rolls have no column for the person who was owed the benefit and never appeared to ask for it, so the ledger that catches every wrongly-paid claim holds no entry at all for the rightly-owed one that was never made, and the take-up gap exists only for whoever thinks to set eligibility beside enrollment and look at what is missing between them.
The register is honest about everyone in it and silent about everyone it never met.
