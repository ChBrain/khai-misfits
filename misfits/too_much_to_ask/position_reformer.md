# Position: Reformer

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The seat of the one who lowers the burden to reach the eligible, spending effort and admitting risk to buy back the take-up the steps deterred.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The will to ease and a budget to spend: the standing to auto-enrol, pre-fill, drop a step, or staff a helper, and the resources to reach people rather than filter them.
A clear case: the eligible are being turned away, so lifting the cost of claiming is a real good worth buying.
No way to ease a step without admitting some of what it guarded, so the seat cannot lower the burden for free.

## Orders

Ease the burden.
Auto-enrol the eligible, pre-fill what is known, drop the step that deters more than it catches, and staff the help that carries the applicant through.
Spend real effort and money, and accept the risk the eased steps let back.

## Loses

The clean ledger, and the countable safety the steps bought.
Because the seat lifts steps whose easing lets back a nameable measure of fraud or error, it gives up the audit that showed no leak, so it holds its reform against a scoreboard that tallies the risk admitted and not the people reached, keeping the take-up it buys and paying in the one currency the ledger counts.
It surrenders the countable safety to reach the uncounted eligible, and pays for access in the risk the checks controlled.

## Drives

Toward the burden lowered and the benefit reaching its people,
bought with real effort and the risk the steps let back,
so the seat that would reach the eligible pays for them in the safety the checks were there to keep.
