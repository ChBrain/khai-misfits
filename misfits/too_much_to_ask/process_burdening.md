# Process: Burdening

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The steps landing on one applicant at once, felt at [the Kitchen Table](place_the_kitchen_table.md) and [the Counter](place_the_counter.md), the engine inside [The Ordeal](plot_the_ordeal.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The steps the Administrator added one at a time, arriving now as one pile.
At the Kitchen Table, where the applicant sits down to all of them together, not the single reasonable ask each was when it was added.

## Direction

Toward a learning cost, a compliance cost, and a psychological cost, rising as they stack rather than resetting between steps.
Because each requirement was added without regard to the ones beside it, the applicant meets them concurrently, working out the rules while gathering the documents while carrying the stigma of asking at all, so the load is not one step's weight repeated but every step's weight held at once.

## Lever

The requirements meeting the applicant's limited store of time, health, and ease.
Because the store is finite and the steps draw on it without asking how much is left, burdening runs on a debit no single step overdraws: each ask spends a little of what the applicant has, and the account is never refilled between asks.

## Echo

An applicant worn closer to giving up, the pile heavier than any card in it.
No single step in the stack would exhaust anyone, so the wearing looks, from outside, like nothing has gone wrong yet, but the cost is cumulative where the design is not, and what mounts unseen is exactly what will decide whether the claim is finished.
