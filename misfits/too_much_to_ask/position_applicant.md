# Position: Applicant

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The seat of the intended beneficiary, who bears the cost of every step and is deterred by their sum, the eligible person the programme is for.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

An entitlement and a limited store to spend on reaching it: the standing to claim the benefit, and only so much time, health, and ease to bear the cost of claiming.
A clear case: the person qualifies, and the programme is meant for them, so the claim is theirs by right.
No way to make the programme bear the burden, only to bear it themselves, and the less they have to spend the sooner it runs out.

## Orders

Try to claim.
Find out whether you qualify, gather the documents, fill the forms, sit the wait, and endure the questions, working through the asks with whatever you can spare.
Carry on until the benefit is claimed, or the cost is more than you can pay.

## Loses

The store of time, health, and patience each step spends, and, when it runs out, the benefit itself.
Because the seat bears every cost of claiming from its own limited store, it gives up hours and energy and standing to each step, so it loses most where it has least, the neediest occupant spending down soonest and abandoning the claim, the entitlement forfeited not by refusal but by exhaustion.
It surrenders its store to the steps and, too often, the benefit with it.

## Drives

Toward a claim begun and given up, the store spent before the benefit is reached,
the seat that has least to spend deterred soonest,
so the one the programme is most for is the one its cost most surely turns away.
