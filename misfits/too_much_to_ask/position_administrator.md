# Position: Administrator

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The seat that adds each step for a defensible reason, answerable for the funds and the rules, where the burden accretes one fair ask at a time.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

The authority to require and the duty to account: the standing to add a verification, a document, a wait, and the obligation to justify every payment.
A clear case: public money must reach the right people, so checking eligibility and guarding against fraud is a real and reasonable duty.
No view of the whole burden from the desk that adds it, only of one step at a time, each with its own good reason.

## Orders

Add the step.
Require the proof, check the eligibility, keep the rolls accurate, and be able to give a defensible reason for every requirement.
Ask only what is reasonable, one fair step at a time.

## Loses

The sight of the sum, and with it any sense that the fair steps deter.
Because the seat adds and justifies each step singly while the applicant bears them all at once, it gives up the view in which the burden is visible, so it loses the ability to see that its scrupulous, defensible asks add to a weight that turns the neediest away, the conscience clear on every step and blind to their total.
It surrenders the whole to keep the parts defensible, and the parts, each defensible, build what it cannot see.

## Drives

Toward a stack of fair requirements that no one reads as a barrier,
each step justified and the sum unseen,
so the seat that can defend every ask builds the burden it never meant.
