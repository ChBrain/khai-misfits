# Place: The Kitchen Table

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). Where the applicant works through the steps alone, the table [The Ordeal](plot_the_ordeal.md) is borne across, no clerk present to help and no queue to share the wait with.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A letter with rules that do not quite say who qualifies, and a pile of papers meant to answer it.
The forms spread out after the day's other work is done, the search for a document that may or may not still exist, the same paragraph read three times before it is understood, or given up on.
Nothing here is loud; it is just late, and tired, and alone.

## Holds

The eligibility rules to be worked out, the documents to be found, and the hours after work that both of those cost.
Here the learning cost and the compliance cost are paid in full, so the Kitchen Table holds the private half of the burden, the half the Counter never sees: not the form itself but the working-out of which form, not the document but the drawer it is searched for in.
It is where the applicant does, unpaid and unassisted, the labour the programme's design assumes will simply get done.

## Offers

The chance to work it out, in whatever hours are spare.
Because the Kitchen Table asks nothing of anyone but the applicant, it offers them the freedom to take as long as they need, to try again tomorrow, to ask no one and owe no one for the help.
That freedom is real, and for some it is enough.

## Withheld

The time, the health, and the ease the neediest do not have to spend on it.
The Kitchen Table does not lower the rules for the tired or the sick or the stretched; it demands the same reading, the same search, the same hours from whoever sits at it, so it withholds any adjustment for who has least to give, and what looks like an equal ask lands as an unequal one.
The papers still sit there tomorrow, and the day after, until the someday they are finished on does not arrive.
