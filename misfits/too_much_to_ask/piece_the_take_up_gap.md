# Piece: The Take-Up Gap

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The sum over the rolls: the eligible who never claim, take-up fallen below eligibility, the exclusion no single office ever sees because it lives only in the aggregate.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read only at [the rolls](place_the_rolls.md), where the claimed are counted and, by the gap beneath the eligibility estimate, the unclaimed are counted too, though never by name.

## Load Bearing

Without it every abandoned claim would be a private disappointment, a single applicant's bad day, with nothing to say about the programme that asked too much.
With it the private disappointments become a public fact: day after day an eligible person gives up somewhere in the middle of the learning, the complying, or the enduring, and given enough days the give-ups are no longer scattered but a rate, a share of the eligible who are never once counted among the served. The Take-Up Gap is load bearing because it is the accrued sum (Herd and Moynihan): small, isolated costs summing to a large effect on whether an eligible person applies at all, so the piece carries the reckoning that exists nowhere in any single case and everywhere in the total, the only place the trap can be seen as a trap at all.

## Apparent

A low take-up rate, a line item, a modest shortfall the annual report notes and moves past.
Seen at the surface, the Take-Up Gap looks like a measurement problem or a mild inefficiency, a number a little lower than hoped, and the truth that every point of the gap is a person who qualified and was turned away by nothing but the asking, that the shortfall is the misfit's entire evidence and its entire cost at once, stays hidden behind how bloodless a rate looks next to the refusal it never had to issue.

## Yearbook

Summed across [the Rolls](place_the_rolls.md) through [deterring](process_deterring.md), every abandonment [the Applicant](persona_the_applicant.md) never files a reason for.
It is the reckoning of the trap: the Take-Up Gap totals what [the learning cost](piece_the_learning_cost.md), [the compliance cost](piece_the_compliance_cost.md), and [the psychological cost](piece_the_psychological_cost.md) spent past bearing at every [step](piece_the_step.md) the Administrator added in good faith, weighted unevenly into [the regressive load](piece_the_regressive_load.md), and narrowed only where [easing](process_easing.md) lets the Auditor's guarded risk back in.
