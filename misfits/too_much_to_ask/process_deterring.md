# Process: Deterring

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The burden turning into an abandoned claim, the hinge of [The Abandonment](plot_the_abandonment.md), run out of [the Kitchen Table](place_the_kitchen_table.md) and never reaching [the Counter](place_the_counter.md) at all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

The burden built by Burdening, now heavier than the applicant has left to spend.
At the Kitchen Table, somewhere in the middle of the pile, where the next step still to do outweighs the strength still available to do it.

## Direction

Toward the claim abandoned, or never begun at all.
Because the applicant cannot spend more than the store they have, and the burden has already drawn that store down, deterring turns the shortfall into a stop: the form left half filled, the appointment not made, the renewal let lapse, take-up falling below eligibility one quiet non-claim at a time.

## Lever

The accreted cost meeting the ones with the least to spend on it.
Because time, health, and ease are unevenly held and the burden asks the same of everyone who holds them, deterring falls hardest where the store is thinnest, so it is not the burden alone that decides who is turned away but the burden set against exactly how little the neediest have left to meet it with.

## Echo

An eligible person unserved, and no one having refused them.
Nothing was denied and no door was shut; the applicant simply stopped where the cost outran what they could pay, so the gap this leaves is regressive by nature, widest among the poorest and sickest and most stretched, and it reads everywhere as absence rather than as the exclusion it is.
