# Plot: The Easing

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Rolls](place_the_rolls.md), the take-up gap now named and counted, and [the counter](place_the_counter.md) where the next step would be asked or not.
The steps still standing, each as defensible as the day it was added, and a proposal on the table to take one down.

## Action

[Easing](process_easing.md): [The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), holding [the reformer's seat](position_reformer.md), would auto-enrol, pre-fill, or drop [a step](piece_the_step.md), against [the Auditor](persona_the_auditor.md), holding [the auditor's seat](position_auditor.md), who prices what the step catches.

## Stage

[The Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md), on [Ease the Burden](plan_ease_the_burden.md), spends real effort and money to close [the take-up gap](piece_the_take_up_gap.md), reaching people rather than filtering them.
[The Auditor](persona_the_auditor.md), on [Guard the Funds](plan_guard_the_funds.md), names the fraud or error the eased step would let back in, and holds the easing to that cost.

## Tension

The gap is countable and so is the leak, but not on the same ledger: one shows in the rolls who were never claimed, the other in the funds that go astray.
There is nothing to fault on either side: the Reformer has counted the turned away and the Auditor has counted the risk. So the plot closes ambivalently: the burden lowered and the benefit reaching its people... or the risk judged too dear to admit, and the steps, each defensible, stay.
