# Process: Easing

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The burden lowered on purpose, run by [the Reformer](persona_the_reformer.md) on [Ease the Burden](plan_ease_the_burden.md), the engine behind [The Easing](plot_the_easing.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A Reformer who would reach the eligible person the accreted steps have been losing.
At [the Rolls](place_the_rolls.md), where the take-up gap sits beside the enrollment figures, evidence enough that the burden, not the eligibility, has been deciding who is served.

## Direction

Toward the burden lowered: auto-enrolling who is already known to qualify, pre-filling what the record already holds, dropping a verification, walking the applicant through the rest.
Because each step eased returns exactly the time, health, or patience that step had been costing, easing moves directly against Burdening, and every requirement lifted restores a portion of the applicants that requirement had been quietly turning away.

## Lever

The wish to serve meeting the fraud, error, or misallocation the eased step had been guarding against.
Because no step was added without a reason, none can be eased without giving something up, so easing runs on a trade struck one requirement at a time: the access bought back is paid for, precisely, in the risk that step had been built to catch.

## Echo

More people reached, and more of the guarded risk let back in.
Take-up rises exactly as far as the burden falls, and the fraud or error the lifted checks had screened out rises with it, not as a failure of the reform but as its stated price, so the gap the Rolls once hid is narrowed only by admitting, openly, the cost the steps were raised to avoid.
