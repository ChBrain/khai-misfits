# Place: The Counter

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). Where the applicant meets the programme in person, the window the steps are asked and answered across, the desk where [The Requirement](plot_the_requirement.md) is served and, sometimes, [The Easing](plot_the_easing.md) is felt.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A line of people and a stack of forms, each name called in turn.
The queue moving slowly, the verification demanded of whoever reaches the front, the wait stamped onto the ticket before the wait behind the door.
Nothing hidden here: every ask is posted on the wall, reasonable, numbered, in plain sight.

## Holds

The forms to be filled, the documents to be produced, and the minutes that pass before either is checked.
Here the eligibility rules meet the paperwork that proves them, so the Counter holds the point where the applicant's claim is tested against what the programme requires to believe it: a form for this, a document for that, a wait while both are confirmed.
It is the single surface where every step added elsewhere finally lands on one person at one time.

## Offers

The benefit, to whoever completes what is asked.
Because the Counter is where the steps are satisfied, it offers a real and unrigged door through: finish the forms, produce the documents, sit out the wait, and the claim goes through exactly as promised.
Nothing here is a trick; the offer is genuine, made in good faith, and kept for anyone who reaches the end of it.

## Withheld

The benefit, in practice, from whoever cannot reach that end.
The Counter cannot tell the applicant worn down by the fourth document from the one who never needed help at all, so it withholds nothing on purpose and withholds the benefit all the same, the queue that thins toward the back the truest census of who the steps were too much for.
What is offered to everyone is not received by everyone, and the gap between the two is invisible from the window, which sees only who reached the front.
