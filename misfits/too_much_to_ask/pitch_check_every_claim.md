# Pitch: Check Every Claim

## Taxonomy

[Too Much to Ask](play_too_much_to_ask.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Diligent, responsible, careful with the public purse.
The plain rightness of checking each claim: proving eligibility, stopping fraud, catching error, being accountable for money meant to reach the right people.
Nothing false in it: each verification is defensible, each step is added in good faith, and guarding public funds is a real and reasonable duty.

## Undertow

Under the diligence, the applicant worn down by the cost of finding out, the cost of complying, and the cost of the stigma, until the neediest give up.
Because the burden of claiming falls on the applicant and weighs most on those with the least to spend on it, take-up falls below eligibility, and the programme turns away the very people it was built to serve.
The care that guards the purse is the care that screens out the poor.

## Nerve

The clean conscience of the programme that checks every claim, sure it is doing right because every step is fair,
because no verification is unreasonable and each was added in good faith to stop fraud and catch error,
while at the kitchen table someone who qualifies gives up on the forms, and the benefit meant for them goes unclaimed.

## Echo

After the run: a programme built to serve the neediest that turns the neediest away, no gate built and no one refused,
only a hundred reasonable asks summing to a cost the poorest could not pay, take-up fallen below eligibility,
and the exclusion visible only in the aggregate, authored by no one and regretted, if at all, too late.
