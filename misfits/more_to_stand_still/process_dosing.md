# Process: Dosing

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The raising of the input to restore a faded effect, the sound answer to a flat result that gives the response a larger amount to move it again.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Driver](persona_the_driver.md), at [the Level](place_the_level.md), meeting a dose that has stopped moving the needle with a larger one.
Begins whenever the effect has faded to the point of a stalled result and the one driving the behaviour reaches for more to bring it back.

## Direction

Turns a faded effect into a restored one.
Because a larger dose clears the raised neutral by enough to register again, raising it brings the response back, so dosing converts a flat result into a working one, the increase the correct answer to a stalled effect and effective at each step because more genuinely does move a response that has adapted to less (Schultz, Dayan and Montague).

## Lever

The reliability of more.
Because a bigger amount always outruns the current adaptation for a while, dosing works through the plain fact that raising the dose works: there is no faded effect that a large enough increase will not restore, so the lever is the dependable short-run payoff of escalation, the very thing that makes the raise the obvious and defensible move each time it is made.

## Echo

A restored effect, resting on a higher floor.
The echo of dosing is the needle moving again at a larger amount, the result bought back and the dose now higher, so the restoration is real and momentary, the raised level already beginning to be adapted to and the next fade already loading beneath the effect just regained.
