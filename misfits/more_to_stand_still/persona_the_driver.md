# Persona: The Driver

## Taxonomy

[The Supply](position_the_supply.md). Applies the motivator to drive the behaviour, and raises the dose each time the old amount stops working, the one who keeps giving more and cannot tell when generosity became mere maintenance.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Responsive. Sensible about a flat result. Giving more because more is what the faded effect calls for.
Is the hand that drives the behaviour with a motivator, and reads a dose that has stopped moving the needle as a reason to raise it, exactly as good sense says.
No manipulation wished on them: the raise is not a trick but the correct answer to an effect that has genuinely faded, and the trouble is not that they give but that whatever they give becomes the level against which their next gift must be larger still.

## Action

Applies the motivator and watches it work, then, when it stops working, raises the dose to restore the effect, the increase the sound response to a flat result.
Keeps giving more as the response keeps adapting, matching each faded effect with a larger amount, so the dose climbs while the effect it buys stays where it was.
Does what a responsive hand does: answers a stalled result with a bigger push, and finds that the bigger push, having worked, becomes the new floor from which the next result stalls, the giving never done because the receiving never holds.

## Shadow

Not that they are indulgent, but the slow dread of a ceiling: they can raise the dose only so far, and each raise brings the day the dose cannot go higher and the effect cannot be held closer.
What gnaws is that they cannot find the moment it changed. There was a time the motivator was a gift and a time it became upkeep, and no single raise was the one that crossed the line, so they cannot point to where generosity ended and mere maintenance began, only feel that they are working harder than ever for a result that no longer improves.
The bind is interior and creeping: the more they give the less room they have left to give, so their own past raises narrow the future ones, and they carry the sense of having spent their leverage on standing still, of arriving at the end of what they can offer with nothing bought but the level they already had.

## Tell

Says "the old amount stopped working, so of course I gave more."
Is right in every instance and trapped by the sequence, because each raise was the correct answer to a faded effect and the sum of the correct answers is a dose climbing toward a ceiling for an effect that never rises, so the sentence that defends any single increase is the description of the treadmill, a hand giving more and more and buying, each time, only the ground it already stood on.
