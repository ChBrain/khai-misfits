# Play: More to Stand Still

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

More to Stand Still.
A motivator drives a behaviour: a bonus, a word of praise, a jolt of urgency, a threat, a stimulant, anything that moves the needle. The first time it lands with full force. But a response adapts to what it is repeatedly given, so the same dose, applied again, moves the needle less, and to restore the effect you raise the dose, which is the sensible thing to do when the old amount has stopped working. The larger dose works, for a while, and then it too becomes the level the response has adjusted to, the new neutral against which only more will register, so the effect fades again and the dose climbs again, and the system escalates not to get ahead but merely to keep the effect it started with. Nobody is being greedy. The one who raises the dose is answering a real and flat response, and the one who has adapted did not choose to: adaptation is not ingratitude but the way a response works, the body and the mind settling to whatever persists and reading only change. And the trap has a floor as well as a ceiling, because the same process that dulls the dose sharpens its absence: stop, and the level you climbed to is now the neutral you fall below, so quitting does not return you to where you began but drops you into a trough, the withdrawal the exact mirror of the tolerance. So the dose ratchets up, buying maintenance and calling it progress, and the only way off is to bear the trough that getting off costs. Nobody miscalculated. Each raise answered a faded effect and each adaptation was involuntary, and the escalation to stand still is the sum, a response that neutralises whatever it is given turning every sound increase into the baseline of the next.

## Arc

The bend is this: a response adapts to whatever it is repeatedly given, so a motivator loses its potency with use, and to hold the same effect the dose must rise, the system escalating merely to stand still (Solomon and Corbit; Brickman and Campbell).
It runs on a response that reads change, not level.
Because the body and the mind register difference from what they have adjusted to rather than absolute amount, a stimulus applied steadily fades, its opponent process strengthening with each use until the net effect shrinks toward nothing, so the same dose that once moved the needle comes to move it barely at all (Solomon and Corbit). The fading is not wear on the stimulus but growth of the adaptation, and it is involuntary.
And the sensible answer to a faded effect is more.
When the old dose stops working, raising it restores the effect, which is the correct response to a flat result, so the one who drives the behaviour gives a larger amount and the needle moves again, the escalation rational at every step because at every step it works (Schultz, Dayan and Montague). The larger dose is the right call, and it buys back the effect.
And the larger dose becomes the new neutral.
Because the response adapts to whatever persists, the raised level is itself adjusted to, settling into the baseline against which only more will register, so the gain is neutralised and the effect fades again from a higher floor, the hedonic treadmill returning satisfaction to zero no matter the height it is fed (Brickman and Campbell; Brickman, Coates and Janoff-Bulman). What was a raise becomes the level, and the level becomes the nothing.
And the climb buys maintenance and reads as progress.
Because each raise works before it fades, the escalation feels like motion, but the response keeps resetting its zero to the current dose, so the system spends more and more to hold the effect it began with, running to stand still while the rising numbers say it is getting somewhere (Frederick and Loewenstein). The dose is higher and higher and the effect is exactly where it started, the progress an illusion of the moving baseline.
And the same process makes the exit a trough.
Because the opponent that dulled the dose does not vanish when the dose stops, the level climbed to becomes the neutral fallen below, so quitting does not restore the beginning but drops the response into withdrawal, the after-reaction the mirror image of the tolerance and worse for how high the climb went (Solomon and Corbit; Frederick and Loewenstein). Getting off costs a low deeper than the flatness that made you want off, and the trough is the price of the reset.
None of it is a vice. The driver raising a dose that has genuinely stopped working, the response adapting because adaptation is what responses do, the climb that buys back a real effect each time: each is sound, and the escalation to stand still emerges from the sum, no greed and no weakness required. Let the dose rest so sensitivity returns, take the effect off the treadmill by stepping off it, and the trap is broken (the deliberate abstinence, the tolerated trough): but that means giving up the effect entirely for a while, dropping into the withdrawal the climb has loaded, and being worse before you are neutral, so the one move that resets the response is a low you must choose and endure, and the reckoning is a system that pays more and more to stand where it started, or a trough that must be borne to stop paying.

## Triggers

**[The First Hit](plot_the_first_hit.md)**
A motivator applied for the first time lands with full force, the effect at its height. The Driver sees it work and the Habituated feels it fully, the dose and the effect matched at their best. Closes when the first effect sets the standard, its height the mark every later dose will be measured against and fall short of.

**[The Bigger Dose](plot_the_bigger_dose.md)**
Cue: the old dose has stopped moving the needle. The Driver raises it, the sound answer to a faded effect, and the larger amount restores the response. Closes when the dose is raised and the effect is momentarily back, the increase correct and already becoming the level the response will adjust to.

**[The Flat Line](plot_the_flat_line.md)**
Cue: the raised dose has settled into the neutral. More is being applied than ever and the effect is exactly where it began, the escalation buying only maintenance. Closes when the climb is seen to hold its ground and no more, the rising dose and the flat effect exposing the treadmill under the motion.

**[The Fast](plot_the_fast.md)**
Cue: someone would step off the treadmill. The Abstainer withdraws the dose to let sensitivity return, and drops into the trough the climb has loaded, worse before neutral. Closes when the reset is bought by the low that getting off costs, or declined for the trough it demands and the effect it forgoes.

## Stakes

Whether a system can hold an effect without forever raising the dose that produces it, or is left to escalate by the plain fact that a response neutralises whatever it is steadily given.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the climb that buys only maintenance, which the Driver is right to raise when the dose has faded and the Habituated cannot help having adapted and each larger dose really does work before it levels, and away from the rest that would reset the response at the cost of the trough. The production cannot make the escalation a vice, because each raise answers a genuinely flat effect and adaptation is not a choice but the way a response works, so giving more when the old amount has stopped moving the needle is the sensible act: the driver maintaining the effect and the body adjusting to the dose are each only doing what the situation makes correct. It can only show that a response which reads change and not level neutralises every increase into its new zero, that the climb buys the effect it began with at a rising price and calls it progress, and that the one move that steps off the treadmill, letting the dose rest, means bearing the withdrawal the climb has loaded and being worse before neutral, so the system escalates not by anyone's greed but by the ordinary good sense of everyone who answers a faded effect with a little more.
