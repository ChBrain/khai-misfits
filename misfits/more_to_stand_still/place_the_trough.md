# Place: The Trough

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The withdrawal low, where the dose is stopped and the opponent that dulled it stands exposed, the level climbed to now the neutral fallen below.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A slump, a state plainly worse than the dose it followed.
Shown as a fall: the motivator is gone and the response is below where it sat, so the Trough looks like nothing but decline, a person or a system visibly diminished the moment the dose was withheld.

## Holds

Holds the opponent unmasked, the process that grew against the dose and outlives it.
The Trough is where the after-reaction runs: the opponent that shrank the effect does not stop when the dose does but is left with nothing to oppose, so the response drops below neutral in proportion to how far it was held above, the withdrawal roughly the mirror of the tolerance and deeper the higher the climb had gone.

## Offers

Offers the descent that resets the response, and every reason to end it by resuming.
Here the low is real and the recovery it enables is not yet felt, so the Trough offers the one path back to sensitivity and offers, in the same place, the easiest possible exit from itself, since a single return to the dose would lift the response out of the low at once, the reset and the temptation to abandon it occupying the same ground.

## Withheld

Withholds that the low is the cost of the height, not a new harm.
What the Trough hides is that it is not a fresh affliction but the bill for the climb, the opponent it exposes having been built dose by dose on the way up, so the slump that looks like the reset failing is the reset working, the descent concealing that it is proportional to the ascent and that passing through it, not avoiding it, is the only way the response comes back.
