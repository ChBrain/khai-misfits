# Pitch: More Just to Stay

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The single hook: a response adapts to whatever it is given, so the raised dose becomes the new neutral and the effect fades again, and the system escalates not to get ahead but merely to hold the ground it started on.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

A motivator that works, and the sensible raise when it stops.
The tenor is responsiveness: the dose drives the behaviour, and when the old amount stops moving the needle you give a little more, exactly what a flat result calls for, the increase the obvious and correct answer to an effect that has faded.

## Undertow

The raise becomes the level, and the level becomes the nothing.
Underneath, the response adjusts to whatever persists, so the larger dose is itself adapted to and settles into the neutral against which only more will register, the gain neutralised and the effect fading again from a higher floor, each raise quietly becoming the baseline of the next.

## Nerve

The dose is higher and higher and the effect is barely above where it began.
The nerve is that the climb buys maintenance and reads as progress: the rising numbers feel like motion, but the response keeps resetting its zero toward the current dose, so the system spends more and more to hold the effect it started with, running as hard as it can just to stand still.

## Echo

The only way off is a low deeper than the flatness that made you want off.
What lingers is that the same process which dulled the dose sharpens its absence, so stopping does not return you to the beginning but drops you into the trough the climb has loaded, and the reset that would free the response must be bought by bearing the withdrawal, worse before neutral, the exit priced by the very height you climbed.
