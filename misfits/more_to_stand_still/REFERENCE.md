# More to Stand Still: Reference

The research warrant for [More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md).
The dynamic is tolerance and the hedonic treadmill: Solomon and Corbit's opponent-process account of why a repeated
stimulus fades and its absence inverts, Brickman and Campbell's treadmill and Brickman, Coates and Janoff-Bulman's
adaptation to a set-point, Schultz, Dayan and Montague's fading of a predicted reward, and Frederick and Loewenstein's
synthesis of hedonic adaptation and its asymmetry, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging tolerance as an **escalation-to-stand-still structure**: a response adapts to whatever it is repeatedly given,
so a motivator loses its potency with use, and to hold the same effect the dose must rise, but each raise is itself
adapted to and becomes the new neutral, so the effect fades again from a higher floor and the system escalates merely to
keep the effect it began with. The play models the documented mechanics, the fading of a repeated stimulus, the sound
raise that restores it, the levelling that turns each raise into the baseline, and the withdrawal trough that the same
adaptation loads beneath the climb, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of greed or weakness.
The governing finding of the source is the play's governing law: because a response registers change from an adapted
level rather than absolute amount, a steadily applied motivator strengthens the opponent process against it until the
net effect shrinks, so holding an effect requires an ever-rising dose, and stopping drops the response below neutral by
the amount it was held above. The escalation is sound at every step, since each raise answers a genuinely faded effect
and adaptation is involuntary, so giving more when the old amount has stopped working is the sensible act, and the
running-to-stand-still is the sum of correct responses to a response that neutralises whatever it is fed.

It is distinct from the house's Alarm Fatigue, where repeated or false signals desensitise a responder until the signal
is tuned out and missed: there the output is a signal ignored, the response lost to a raised threshold, while here the
output is maintained by a rising input, the effect held rather than dropped, escalation rather than inattention. It is
distinct from Shifting the Burden, where leaning on a symptomatic fix lets the fundamental capacity atrophy from disuse:
there a capability withers from neglect, while here nothing withers, the response habituates to the stimulus itself and
demands a larger dose for a flat effect. And it is distinct from The Arms Race, where all run to stand still because a
rival escalates: there the treadmill is positional and driven by a competitor, while here there is no rival, the
escalation driven by the actor's own adapting response, the opponent internal and the standing-still a fact of one
system's habituation, not a contest between two.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the driver as manipulative
  or the habituated as greedy, because the source finding is precisely that no vice is needed: each raise answers a
  genuinely faded effect and restores it (Schultz, Dayan and Montague), and the fading is not ingratitude but the
  involuntary working of a response that reads change and not level (Solomon and Corbit). The one who raises the dose is
  maintaining an effect, not indulging a want, and the one who has adapted did not choose to and cannot choose to stop,
  so the misfit is that a response which neutralises whatever it is given turns every sound increase into the baseline of
  the next, no greed and no weakness required. The escalation is the sum of correct responses to a faded effect, not the
  product of anyone's excess, and the driver giving more is as reasonable as the body adjusting to it.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a system freed by restraint alone. The one move that resets
  the response is to withhold the dose and let sensitivity return, but the same opponent that dulled the dose is unmasked
  when it stops, so withdrawal drops the response below neutral into a trough deeper the higher the climb went (Solomon
  and Corbit; Frederick and Loewenstein). The cost is front-loaded, certain, and felt, while the recovery is deferred,
  invisible, and merely probable, and the same dose withheld would end the trough at once by resuming the climb, so the
  reset must be bought by bearing a low that looks to everyone like a fall and choosing it against the easy relief always
  to hand. The reckoning is a system that pays more and more to stand where it started, or a trough that must be endured
  to stop paying, not a way to hold the effect without the climb or to step off without the low.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as the
  house's, and its reach is not overstated. The staging is the house's; the opponent-process account of tolerance and
  its withdrawal mirror is Solomon and Corbit's, the hedonic treadmill is Brickman and Campbell's, the empirical
  adaptation to a set-point is Brickman, Coates and Janoff-Bulman's, the fading of a predicted reward is Schultz, Dayan
  and Montague's, and the synthesis of hedonic adaptation and its asymmetry is Frederick and Loewenstein's, each carried
  as carefully as the finding itself: the misfit stages a response that neutralises what it is given and credits that no
  one was greedy and the climb is the response's own.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Wheel](piece_the_wheel.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Solomon and Corbit; Brickman and Campbell), the
  response that reads change and not level neutralising whatever it is given, so the dose rises forever to hold a
  constant effect and the system escalates to stand still.
- **[The Kick](piece_the_kick.md)**: carries the fading reward (Schultz, Dayan and Montague), the effect that leaps on a
  fresh dose and barely registers on a predicted one, the diminishing return the climb keeps paying to recover.
- **[The Set-Point](piece_the_set_point.md)**: carries the moving neutral (Brickman and Campbell; Brickman, Coates and
  Janoff-Bulman; Frederick and Loewenstein), the baseline that resets to whatever dose persists so every gain is spent
  raising the floor rather than the height.
- **[The Trough](place_the_trough.md)**: carries the withdrawal after-reaction (Solomon and Corbit; Frederick and
  Loewenstein), the opponent unmasked when the dose stops dropping the response below neutral, the low that prices the
  reset.
