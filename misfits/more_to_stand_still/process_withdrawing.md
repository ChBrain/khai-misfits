# Process: Withdrawing

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The after-reaction when the dose stops, the opponent left with nothing to oppose dropping the response below neutral, the mirror of the tolerance and the price of the reset.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Abstainer](persona_the_abstainer.md), at [the Trough](place_the_trough.md), taking the dose away and leaving the opponent exposed.
Begins the moment the dose is withheld, when the counter-response that grew against it continues while the stimulus it opposed is gone.

## Direction

Turns a stopped dose into a low.
Because the opponent that shrank the effect does not subside as fast as the dose is removed, its unopposed action carries the response below neutral, so withdrawing converts the mere absence of the stimulus into an active trough, the after-reaction roughly the counterpart of the tolerance and deeper for how high the climb had built it (Solomon and Corbit; Frederick and Loewenstein).

## Lever

The persistence of the opponent.
Because the counter-response outlasts the stimulus it was built against, withdrawing works through a debt that comes due on stopping: the same growth that dulled the dose is now unopposed, so the lever is the lag between removing the dose and the opponent fading, a gap in which the response is dragged below where it began.

## Echo

A low that resets the response, and a standing temptation to end it.
The echo of withdrawing is a trough that both restores sensitivity and offers its own easiest exit, since resuming the dose would lift the response out at once, so the descent that would return a smaller amount to working is the descent the climb is always tempting one to abandon by climbing again.
