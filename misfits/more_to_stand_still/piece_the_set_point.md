# Piece: The Set-Point

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The neutral the response returns to, resetting upward to whatever dose persists, so every gain is spent raising the floor rather than the height.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Sits at [the Level](place_the_level.md), the zero the response settles to, raised each time a dose becomes routine and lowered only through [the Trough](place_the_trough.md).
Flows as the moving baseline of feeling, the point the response calls neutral, climbing to sit just beneath whatever amount is now ordinary.

## Load Bearing

Without the Set-Point resetting, a raise would stay a gain and the climb would end: one increase would lift the response and hold it lifted, and there would be no treadmill.
It is because the neutral moves to whatever level persists that no gain is kept, so the Set-Point is load bearing because it is the reset that neutralises every increase, the reason the response returns to zero however high it is fed (Brickman and Campbell; Brickman, Coates and Janoff-Bulman; Frederick and Loewenstein). Its motion is the whole trap: a fixed baseline would let a raise accumulate, but a baseline that climbs to meet each dose converts every gain into the new nothing, so the Set-Point is what makes standing still cost more each cycle, the floor rising to swallow the height and forcing the next dose to clear a neutral the last dose built.

## Apparent

Just how things normally feel, the ordinary baseline of the response.
Seen at the surface, the Set-Point looks like a fixed reference, the natural zero from which highs and lows are measured, and the truth that it moves, that today's neutral was yesterday's peak, that it climbs with every sustained raise to render each gain invisible, stays hidden behind how permanent a baseline feels to the response standing on it.

## Yearbook

Raised by [the Habituated](persona_the_habituated.md)'s adaptation, fed by [the Driver](persona_the_driver.md)'s every increase, and lowered only by [the Abstainer](persona_the_abstainer.md)'s withdrawal.
It is the moving zero that neutralises the climb: the Set-Point is what [levelling](process_levelling.md) raises and [withdrawing](process_withdrawing.md) at last brings down, the floor that turns each [dose](piece_the_dose.md) into the new neutral and each [kick](piece_the_kick.md) into a fading memory, the pawl that lets [the wheel](piece_the_wheel.md) climb and never coast.
