# Process: Fading

## Taxonomy

[More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md). The shrinking of the effect with repetition, the opponent strengthening against the dose until the same amount moves the response by less and less.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

[The Habituated](persona_the_habituated.md), at [the First Time](place_the_first_time.md) once it is repeated, whose response begins to adjust to the dose the moment it stops being new.
Begins the second time a stimulus lands and every time after, as the response that met it fully learns to meet it less.

## Direction

Turns a working dose into a fading one.
Because the opponent process grows with each application while the stimulus stays the same, the net effect of a constant dose shrinks, so fading converts a motivator that once moved the needle into one that barely does, the loss not in the dose but in the response's growing answer against it (Solomon and Corbit).

## Lever

The strengthening of the opponent.
Because repetition builds the counter-response faster than the stimulus can outrun it, fading works through an adaptation that compounds with use: the more a dose is given the stronger the opposition it raises, so the very steadiness that should make an effect reliable is what erodes it, the response tuning itself against whatever it is fed.

## Echo

An effect gone flat, and a reason to give more.
The echo of fading is a dose that no longer works though nothing about it changed, so the flat result presents itself as a call for a larger amount, the shrinking effect handing the driver the sound and immediate reason to raise the dose that will fade in its turn.
