# Plan: Raise the Dose

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the Supply](position_the_supply.md), inside [More to Stand Still](play_more_to_stand_still.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [The Supply](position_the_supply.md)

## Direction

Meet a faded effect with a larger dose, since more restores the response and restoring the response is the point (Schultz, Dayan and Montague). Read a stalled result as the signal to increase, and do not hold the dose steady expecting the effect to hold, because a constant dose fades and standing pat is slow decline. Give the amount the moment calls for, and accept that the amount which works now will become the level the response adapts to, so the raise that restores the effect today is the floor the effect will stall at tomorrow. Take the increase as correct at each step and the climb as the price of a response that keeps resetting its zero to whatever it is fed.

## Orders

([The Driver](persona_the_driver.md) applies the motivator and, when it stops moving the needle, raises the dose to restore the effect, matching each faded result with a larger amount and finding the larger amount become the new floor.)

## Implementation

([The First Time](place_the_first_time.md); through the [dosing](process_dosing.md) process, against the [fading](process_fading.md) it answers; the Driver raises [the dose](piece_the_dose.md) to bring back [the kick](piece_the_kick.md), and the raise feeds [the set-point](piece_the_set_point.md) that turns it into the new neutral, driving [the wheel](piece_the_wheel.md).)

## Targets

- [ ] a faded effect is met with a larger dose, not a repeated one
- [ ] a flat result is read as the signal to raise, not to hold
- [ ] the dose that restores the effect is expected to become its next floor
- [ ] the climb is accepted as the cost of a response that neutralises what it is given
