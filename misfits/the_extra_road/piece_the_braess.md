# Piece: The Braess

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md). The phenomenon whole: the gap between one driver's saving and everyone's cost.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the map](place_the_map.md), where the whole network is seen at once and the paradox becomes legible.
Not a single road but the relation between a road and the graph: the fact, provable and measured, that adding a link under selfish routing can raise every driver's time, and that removing it can lower it. The thing itself, held where it can be read.

## Load Bearing

Without it the other pieces are a story about traffic, not a law about networks.
With it there is a named, general result the production stands on: the price of anarchy, the distance between what selfish routing settles for and what a coordinated network could reach, and Braess's configurations as where that distance opens widest. The Braess is load bearing because it is the whole carried as one, the finding that the misfit is not bad luck or bad planning but a structural property of unpriced, load-dependent, selfishly routed networks.

## Apparent

A trick. A counter-intuitive curiosity for a textbook.
It looks like a puzzle, a surprising special case, the kind of thing that could not really matter on a real map. That dismissal is the last disguise, because the same gap that makes the toy example paradoxical is the gap that priced roads close and free roads leave open, and the curiosity is the mechanism.

## Yearbook

Named against [the network](piece_the_network.md), and answered by [the toll](piece_the_toll.md).
It is the law the play runs on: the Braess is what [the equilibrium](piece_the_equilibrium.md) obeys, what [the slowdown](piece_the_slowdown.md) proves, and what [pricing](process_pricing.md) is the only shown way out of, a price on the shared stretch the free road refused to charge.
