# Piece: The Selfish Route

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md). The minimum-time choice: each driver taking what is quickest for them, not for all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the junction](place_the_junction.md), in the moment a driver reads the roads ahead and picks the fastest one.
The turn taken because the sign, the app, the habit says it saves a minute. Not a plot, not a calculation about anyone else, just the ordinary, defensible act of choosing the route that gets you there soonest given how everyone else is already driving.

## Load Bearing

Without it drivers might hold to routes for the good of the whole, and the extra road could be left unused.
With it every driver responds only to their own travel time, so any link that is faster for them will be taken by them, and the network is routed by a million private optimisations that answer to no total. The Selfish Route is load bearing because it is the behaviour Wardrop's user equilibrium assumes and Braess's paradox requires: the network settles where no single driver can do better by switching, and that point is set by what is best for each, never by what is best for all.

## Apparent

Rationality. Good sense. Nobody's fault.
It looks like exactly what a driver should do: minimise your own time, take the quick way, respond to conditions. There is no better advice to give one driver, and that is the point, because the Selfish Route is not a vice, it is optimal play, and the misfit is that optimal play by each is not optimal for all.

## Yearbook

Driven by [the commuter](persona_the_commuter.md) through [routing](process_routing.md), and refused, for a while, by [the holdout](persona_the_holdout.md).
It is the engine under every reroute: the Selfish Route is what carries traffic onto [the extra road](piece_the_extra_road.md), and its sum over every driver is [the equilibrium](piece_the_equilibrium.md) the network cannot escape by itself.
