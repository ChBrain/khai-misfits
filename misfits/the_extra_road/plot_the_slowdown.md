# Plot: The Slowdown

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The commute](place_the_commute.md), and a clock that reads longer than it did before the road was built.
The bypass is open, the fleet is on it, and the trip takes twenty-five minutes where it took twenty ... for everyone, on more road.

## Action

[Measurement](process_measurement.md): [The Mayor](persona_the_mayor.md), holding [the steward's seat](position_steward.md), reads the network end to end and finds every driver worse off, though each drove well.

## Stage

[The Mayor](persona_the_mayor.md) tables [Keep the City Moving](plan_keep_the_city_moving.md): report the honest average rather than the ribbon's promise, so [the slowdown](piece_the_slowdown.md) is named as the cost of [the equilibrium](piece_the_equilibrium.md), the whole made worse by an improvement.
Or the average is called growth, the demand blamed, and the network's slower time is read as a reason to build another link.

## Tension

The one number that would open the cure is the one the ribbon-cutting cannot survive.
To read the slowdown truly is to admit the new road made the city slower, which is the hardest finding to sign; the easy move is to credit the road with relief and ask for one more. So the plot turns on a choice that is not about traffic at all: whether the steward names the paradox ... or buys the next road that will deepen it.
