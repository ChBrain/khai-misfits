# Plot: The Toll

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The toll plaza](place_the_toll_plaza.md), and someone who has stopped leaving the link free and started charging for the load.
The question has changed: not how do we add road, but what does the shared stretch cost the network, and who should pay the delay they impose on the rest.

## Action

[Pricing](process_pricing.md): [The Tollkeeper](persona_the_tollkeeper.md), holding [the pricer's seat](position_pricer.md), puts a price on the loaded link, or closes it, so each driver meets the cost they were handing to everyone else.

## Stage

[The Tollkeeper](persona_the_tollkeeper.md) tables [Price the Road](plan_price_the_road.md): charge the load rather than leave the link free, so [the selfish route](piece_the_selfish_route.md) and the system route come back into line and the network shifts off [the equilibrium](piece_the_equilibrium.md).
With a price on the shared stretch, [the braess](piece_the_braess.md) loses its grip, [the toll](piece_the_toll.md) undoes [the slowdown](piece_the_slowdown.md), and the flow recovers.

## Tension

The network speeds up, and the free road that slowed it is gone.
Pricing works, and it costs: it asks drivers to pay for a road that used to be free, or takes away a road that was built to help, and the relief it buys is invisible next to the charge it levies. It trades an open, unpriced, congested network for a charged, aligned, faster one that must keep defending the toll ... or the price is judged unfair and pulled, the link goes free again, and the slowdown returns.
