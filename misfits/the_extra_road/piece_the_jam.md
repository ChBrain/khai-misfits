# Piece: The Jam

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md). The congestion the new road is meant to relieve: time that climbs with the load.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the commute](place_the_commute.md), in the shared stretch that slows as more pour onto it.
The queue at rush hour, the link that takes ten minutes empty and thirty full, the road whose cost is not a fixed length but a function of how many are on it at once. This is the pressure everyone can see and everyone wants gone.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no problem to solve, and no reason to build the extra road at all.
With it there is a real, felt cost that a new link is offered to cure, and a load-dependent link is the mechanism the whole paradox needs: because the Jam gets worse the more use it, a driver who joins it imposes a cost on everyone already there, and that imposed cost, unfelt by the joiner, is the gap the free road will exploit. The Jam is not the villain; it is the honest congestion that makes adding capacity look obviously right.

## Apparent

A problem of too little road. Build more, and it clears.
It looks like scarcity: the jam is where demand meets a link too small for it, so the fix reads as capacity, another lane, another route, more supply against the load. That reading is exactly what invites the extra road, and exactly what the paradox turns against, because the jam was a load problem before it was a capacity one.

## Yearbook

Felt by every driver on [the old route](place_the_old_route.md), and cured, it seems, by [the extra road](piece_the_extra_road.md).
It is the setup the production opens on: the Jam is what [the planner](persona_the_planner.md) answers, and its relief is the promise that draws every driver onto [the selfish route](piece_the_selfish_route.md) and into the slower whole.
