# Piece: The Slowdown

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md). The raised travel time: the whole run slower than before the road was built.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the commute](place_the_commute.md), in the clock that reads longer than it did last year, on more road.
The trip that took twenty minutes before the bypass opened and takes twenty-five after, for everyone, though the bypass is faster than the road it replaced and nobody drove it wrong. The cost is real, measured, and shared out across every driver alike.

## Load Bearing

Without it the paradox is a curiosity, not a misfit: a network that added a road and simply held even.
With it there is a whole made worse by an improvement, the signature Braess result: capacity up, flow down, and every user's time increased. The Slowdown is load bearing because it is the harm the production exists to show, the thing that cannot be pinned on any driver, any planner, or any single road. It is what the equilibrium costs, paid in minutes by all, chosen by none.

## Apparent

Just more traffic. Demand, growth, the usual congestion.
It looks like the ordinary story of a busy network: more cars, more delay, nothing a paradox, and the natural conclusion is that the road helped and things would be even worse without it. That reading is the trap's last defence, because it credits the extra road with relief while the road is the cause, and it calls for another road to fix what a road did.

## Yearbook

Measured by [the mayor](persona_the_mayor.md) through [measurement](process_measurement.md), and unwound only by [the toll](piece_the_toll.md).
It is the reckoning of the play: the Slowdown is the price of [the equilibrium](piece_the_equilibrium.md), the fact that names [the braess](piece_the_braess.md), and the number that either indicts the extra road or is blamed on demand and answered with one more.
