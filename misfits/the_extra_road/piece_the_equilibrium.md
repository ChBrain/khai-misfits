# Piece: The Equilibrium

## Taxonomy

[The Extra Road](play_the_extra_road.md). The settled state: the routing no driver can beat by switching, and worse for all.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the commute](place_the_commute.md), in the daily pattern that stops changing because no one can gain by changing it.
The steady rush hour, the flows that have found their level, the state where every driver has already taken the best route left to them and none has a move that helps. It looks stable, and it is: stable is exactly the problem.

## Load Bearing

Without it the network could drift back to the fast balance on its own, and the paradox would not bite.
With it the bad routing is locked, because equilibrium means no unilateral improvement exists: the very stretch that would speed up if some drivers left it is the stretch each of them is on because leaving alone gains nothing. The Equilibrium is load bearing because it is the trap's floor, the reason the slowdown persists without a villain holding it in place. Everyone would be better off if many moved together, and no one is better off moving alone, so no one moves.

## Apparent

Stability. Order. The network working as it should.
It looks like a system at rest, sorted, efficient, each driver settled on their best option, so nothing is visibly wrong: there is no queue no one chose, no route left obviously better. That calm is the disguise, because the equilibrium is settled precisely at the slow point, and its stability is what a coordinated fix has to break.

## Yearbook

Formed by every [selfish route](piece_the_selfish_route.md) at once, and hardened by [the dispatcher](persona_the_dispatcher.md) routing the fleet the same way.
It is the state the production drives toward: the Equilibrium is where [the extra road](piece_the_extra_road.md) leads, [the slowdown](piece_the_slowdown.md) is what it measures, and only [pricing](process_pricing.md) can move the whole network off it at once.
