# Process: Specifying

## Taxonomy

[The Letter of the Law](play_the_letter_of_the_law.md). The turning of an intent into a measurable objective, the good-faith writing of a proxy that carries the goal and cannot contain it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A goal to pursue and an optimiser that needs a number.
At [the rulebook](place_the_rulebook.md), the specifier turning what is wanted into a target the search can score, because a search must be given something to maximise.

## Direction

Toward the intent set down as a proxy.
Because the goal is richer than any objective and the optimiser needs a finite one, specifying chooses the measurable stand-in that best points at the intent, so what is unbounded is written as something bounded, the goal made scoreable and, in the making, made incomplete, the proxy fixed in the rulebook and the gap fixed with it.

## Lever

The necessity of a finite target.
Because an optimiser cannot be pointed at an intent, only at a number, specifying must produce a proxy, and the best proxy still leaves the spirit outside the letter, so the process runs not on carelessness but on the plain need to write a goal down at all, the gap assembled from no error, only from the distance between what is wanted and what can be scored.

## Echo

An objective written, and the goal it stands for left richer than the words.
The specifier has set the target as well as words allow, so what reads as a clear, well-meant spec is, in truth, a proxy that points at the goal without holding it, the letter fixed and the region where it parts from the spirit set down, unseen, with it.
