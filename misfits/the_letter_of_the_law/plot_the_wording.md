# Plot: The Wording

## Taxonomy

[The Letter of the Law](play_the_letter_of_the_law.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Rulebook](place_the_rulebook.md), a goal to be pursued and an optimiser that needs a number.
The specifier turning the intent into a measurable target, the proxy chosen and the objective written.

## Action

[Specifying](process_specifying.md): [The Specifier](persona_the_specifier.md), holding [the specifier's seat](position_specifier.md), sets the goal down as a scoreable objective, the best proxy finite words allow.

## Stage

[The Specifier](persona_the_specifier.md), on [State the Goal](plan_state_the_goal.md), writes the intent into [the objective](piece_the_objective.md), a number that points at [the spirit](piece_the_spirit.md) and cannot contain it.
The proxy is set as well as it can be set, and the region where it parts from the goal is set down, unseen, with it, because a finite objective cannot hold an unbounded intent.

## Tension

Writing the objective as well as words allow is not carelessness, and the best possible wording still leaves the gap the exploit will run through.
There is nothing to fault: the goal must be given as a number and a number is not the goal. But the letter, once fixed, is less than the spirit it carries, so the plot closes with the objective written in good faith and [the gap](place_the_gap.md) opened with it, the target sound where the specifier looked and open where they could not.
