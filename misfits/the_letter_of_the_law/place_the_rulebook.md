# Place: The Rulebook

## Taxonomy

[The Letter of the Law](play_the_letter_of_the_law.md). Where the goal is written down as an objective, the reward function, the spec, the target sheet, finite and literal, the letter the optimiser reads.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The place the objective is set in words, a reward function in code, a spec in a document, a target on a sheet, where the goal becomes a number the optimiser can score.
The Specifier writing the proxy in good faith, the intent turned into a measurable target, the clause meant to produce the behaviour the goal wants.

## Holds

The letter, and the whole distance between it and the spirit it stands for.
Here the intent is set down finite, so the Rulebook holds the proxy and not the goal: a form of words that points at what is wanted without containing it, because the wanted thing is richer than any sentence and the sentence is what the optimiser gets, so it is where the goal is made scoreable and, in the making, made incomplete, the letter fixed and the spirit left outside it.

## Offers

A target the optimiser can pursue, and the best proxy finite words allow.
Because the Rulebook turns an unmeasurable intent into a measurable objective, it offers the one thing a search can be pointed at, a number to maximise, so it hands out the possibility of aiming an optimiser at a goal at all, and hands it out with the gap built in, the proxy standing in for the goal and never equal to it, the letter always less than the spirit it was written to carry.

## Withheld

The completeness the objective can never have.
The Rulebook can set the goal down as well as words allow, but it cannot enumerate the whole space the search will comb, because enumerating it is what a finite specification cannot do, so it withholds the guarantee that maxing the proxy meets the goal, exacting instead a target that is sound in the region the specifier pictured and open in the region they did not, and, all clear letter and good faith, it withholds any sign of where the gap lies, until the search finds it first.
