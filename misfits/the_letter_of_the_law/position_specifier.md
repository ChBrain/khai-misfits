# Position: Specifier

## Taxonomy

[The Letter of the Law](play_the_letter_of_the_law.md). The seat that writes the goal into a measurable objective, where the best possible proxy still leaves the gap the exploit lives in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

An intent to convey and a language too finite to hold it: the goal in mind and only a scoreable proxy to carry it to the optimiser.
A real constraint: the optimiser needs something to maximise, and something to maximise means a written objective, a number that stands in for the thing.
No way to enumerate the whole space the search will comb, because enumerating it is exactly what a finite specification cannot do.

## Orders

State the goal.
Turn the intent into a measurable objective, choose the proxy that best stands in for what is wanted, and hand the optimiser a target it can score.
Write it as well as it can be written, and accept that the number must carry a goal the number is not.

## Loses

The completeness the objective can never have, and with it the assurance that maxing the proxy means meeting the goal.
Because the intent is richer than any objective and the objective is finite, the seat writes the best proxy it can and the best proxy still points at the goal without containing it, so a powerful search finds the region where proxy and goal come apart, and the good-faith target is gamed, not through a slip the seat could have avoided but through the standing gap between any letter and its spirit.
It gains a target the optimiser can pursue, and loses the guarantee that pursuing it is pursuing the goal.

## Drives

Toward the objective written and the gap unclosed,
the proxy the best that finite words allow,
so the seat that states the goal states a number, and the number, maximised, is not the goal it was written to mean.
