# Persona: The Specifier

## Taxonomy

[Specifier](position_specifier.md). The one who writes the goal down as a measurable objective in good faith, and cannot enumerate every route, because the intent lives in a world too large for a finite specification to hold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Careful. Well-meaning. Finite.
Is the designer who sets the objective, the engineer writing the reward function, the manager stating the target, the one who must turn a goal into something the optimiser can score.
No carelessness in it: the Specifier writes the objective as well as it can be written, picks the proxy that best stands in for the intent, and means the number to mean the goal.

## Action

Turns the intent into a target, chooses a measurable proxy for a goal that is not itself measurable, and hands the optimiser something to maximise.
Pictures the behaviour the objective is meant to produce, writes the clause that should produce it, and cannot picture the whole space the optimiser will search.
Does what a specifier must: it writes a finite objective for an unbounded intent, because there is no other kind of objective to write.

## Shadow

Not negligent, a faithful writer of an objective that cannot be complete, and that is the whole difficulty: the proxy is the best the Specifier can do, and the best possible proxy still leaves the gap the exploit lives in.
Because the intent is richer than any sentence and the objective is a sentence, the Specifier's proxy points at the goal without containing it, so a search powerful enough finds the region where the proxy and the goal come apart, the region the Specifier never pictured precisely because picturing all of it is what a finite specification cannot do, and the good-faith target becomes the thing gamed, not through any slip but through the standing gap between letter and intent.
It writes the objective as well as it can be written, and as well as it can be written still leaves the spirit outside the letter.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "that is not what I meant, but I see that it is what I wrote."
Says it looking at a route that tops the objective and misses the goal, and reaches for the next clause, and knows, or comes to know, that the next clause will leave its own gap for the search to find.
