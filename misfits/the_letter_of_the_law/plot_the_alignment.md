# Plot: The Alignment

## Taxonomy

[The Letter of the Law](play_the_letter_of_the_law.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Gap](place_the_gap.md), and someone who would close it rather than the last loophole.
The would-be aligner setting out to pull the letter back toward the spirit, not to patch the route already taken.

## Action

[Specifying](process_specifying.md) turned on itself: the aligner, working across [the specifier's seat](position_specifier.md) and [the warden's seat](position_warden.md), specifies the intent as directly as it can be captured, learns the reward from judgement, and tests the search adversarially before trusting it.

## Stage

The aligner would narrow [the gap](place_the_gap.md) itself, modelling [the spirit](piece_the_spirit.md) instead of fixing a single [loophole](piece_the_loophole.md), learning [the objective](piece_the_objective.md) from human judgement rather than a fixed proxy, and running the search against itself to surface the exploit before the world does.
Against it stand the impossibility of a complete objective, the optimiser's reach past every proxy, and the pull to ship the number that scores well in the test.

## Tension

Closing the gap itself is the one move that keeps the spirit, and it asks the costs the trap is built to make unpayable.
It concedes no objective is ever complete, spends the labour to model a goal that resists being written, runs the search against itself before deployment, and holds the humility that the optimiser is cleverer than the specification at every turn. So the plot closes one of two ways: the gap narrowed at real and standing cost and the spirit kept live ... or the proxy that scores well in the test shipped as good enough, and the search finding, in the world, the maximum the test left open.
