# Social Loafing: Reference

The research warrant for [Social Loafing](play_social_loafing.md). The title is Latané's;
the play runs on the social-psychology literature on effort in groups, from Ringelmann's
rope through the collective-effort model, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging social loafing as a **systemic system**: individuals exert less effort on a task when
their contribution is pooled with others' than when it stands alone, so per-head effort falls as
the group grows, without a single shirker. The play models the documented mechanics, the pooled
output in which a single contribution cannot be identified, the effort quietly eased to a hidden,
dispensable level, the sense that one hand among many can hardly matter, the sucker effect by which
the hard worker pulls back rather than carry the rest, the Ringelmann decline of per-head effort
with group size, and the process loss between what the group could produce and what it does, as the
load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of loafers. The governing finding of the source
literature is the play's governing law: when effort is combined and cannot be individually
identified or felt to matter, people expend less of it, so more hands make less work each, and the
group underperforms the sum of the same people working alone. This is the effort-dilution twin of
its neighbour: the house's Everybody's Business stages a discrete duty that no one takes, and Social
Loafing the continuous effort that everyone eases while still taking part.

## Origin

The phenomenon in practice, and the research spine beneath it.

| **Ringelmann**                         | "Recherches sur les moteurs animés: Travail de l'homme", _Annales de l'Institut National Agronomique_ (1913) | **The rope**: per-person pulling force declines as team size grows, the original measurement of effort lost to the group. | **Latané, Williams & Harkins**         | "Many Hands Make Light the Work: The Causes and Consequences of Social Loafing", _JPSP_ (1979)               | **The naming**: effort per person falls when contributions are pooled and unidentifiable; reduced identifiability and dispensability as causes. The Pool, the Dispensable Hand.            |
| **Kerr & Bruun**                       | "Dispensability of member effort and group motivation losses: Free-rider effects", _JPSP_ (1983)             | **The free-rider and sucker effects**: members reduce effort when it seems dispensable, and the hard worker reduces effort to avoid being the sucker who carries them. ## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  hand as a shirker, because the source finding is precisely that none is: easing an effort that is
  pooled, hidden, and felt to be dispensable is a quiet, rational settling, not a decision to slack,
  and the hard worker who pulls back is only declining to be the sucker. The misfit is that effort no
  one can see, and no one hand can be felt to matter to, is effort that quietly falls.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into team spirit lifting the effort on its own.
  The pull returns only by making each hand visible and each part matter, naming the contribution and
  shrinking the group, which spends the easy, equal, pooled togetherness the group was formed for, so
  the reckoning is anonymity traded for accountability, not a group that pulled harder because it
  cared more.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the rope, the naming, and the collective
  effort model are theirs, and the condition social loafing actually requires, pooled and
  unidentifiable effort on a task each feels dispensable to, is carried as carefully as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Pool](piece_the_pool.md)**: carries pooled, unidentifiable output (Latané et al.), the sum in which no
  single contribution can be told from the rest.
- **[The Slack](piece_the_slack.md)**: carries the motivational effort loss (Ingham et al.), the pull quietly
  eased to the level a hidden effort is worth.
- **[The Dispensable Hand](piece_the_dispensable_hand.md)**: carries dispensability (Latané et al.; Kerr & Bruun),
  the sense that one grip among many can hardly matter to the whole.
- **[The Sucker's Guard](piece_the_suckers_guard.md)**: carries the sucker effect (Kerr & Bruun), the hard worker
  easing off rather than carry the coasters for an equal share.
- **[The Ringelmann Curve](piece_the_ringelmann_curve.md)**: carries the group-size decline (Ringelmann; Ingham et
  al.), per-head effort falling as the group grows.
- **[The Process Loss](piece_the_process_loss.md)**: carries process loss (Steiner), the gap between the group's
  potential output and what it actually delivers.
- **[The Social Loafing](piece_the_social_loafing.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Latané et al.; Karau &
  Williams), effort falling because it is pooled and dispensable, and its remedy in identifiability, held whole by nobody.
