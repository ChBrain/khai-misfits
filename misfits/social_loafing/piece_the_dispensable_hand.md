# Piece: The Dispensable Hand

## Taxonomy

[Social Loafing](play_social_loafing.md). The sense that one grip among many can hardly matter to the whole.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

In the head of every hand on [the rope](place_the_rope.md), weighing whether its own pull counts.
In the moment a person judges their effort against a total so large it seems to swallow them.

## Load Bearing

Without it the ease would be only about hiding, and identifiability alone would fix it.
With it there is a second, motivational engine: even a hand that could be seen may pull less if it feels its contribution is redundant, one of many, unlikely to be the one that matters. The Dispensable Hand is load bearing because it is the perceived dispensability the sources name beside identifiability (Latané et al.; Kerr & Bruun), the felt-pointlessness that lowers effort from the inside, so loafing lifts not only when a hand is seen but when its pull is felt to count.

## Apparent

Humility, or realism.
It looks like a modest, reasonable read of one's own small place in a big effort: what difference does my single pull make among so many? That reasonableness is the trap, because the same thought in every head is what makes every pull less.

## Yearbook

Felt by [the loafer](persona_the_loafer.md), and deepened by [the ringelmann curve](piece_the_ringelmann_curve.md).
It is the inner half of the ease: the Dispensable Hand is why [the slack](piece_the_slack.md) is taken even when it might be seen, and why a larger group loafs harder, since the bigger the crowd the smaller each hand feels.
