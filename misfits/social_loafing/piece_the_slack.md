# Piece: The Slack

## Taxonomy

[Social Loafing](play_social_loafing.md). The pull quietly eased to the level a hidden effort is worth.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

On [the rope](place_the_rope.md), in the grip that relaxes a shade no one can feel.
In the gap between the effort a hand gives alone and the effort it gives in a crowd.

## Load Bearing

Without it the pool would hide nothing worth hiding, and the group would simply match its members.
With it there is a real, repeated drop in effort, the motivational loss that survives even when coordination is perfect, so the group falls below the sum of its parts. The Slack is load bearing because it is the effort actually lost (Ingham et al.), the quiet, unmeant easing that makes social loafing a real cost and not a mere accounting artifact, the reason many hands make less work each and not just work harder to sum.

## Apparent

Being a team player.
It looks like nothing at all, or like ordinary, relaxed collaboration: no one is refusing to work, everyone is taking part, the ease is too small and too invisible to read as slacking. That invisibility is precisely what lets it happen and lets it spread.

## Yearbook

Taken by [the loafer](persona_the_loafer.md) through [easing](process_easing.md), hidden by [the pool](piece_the_pool.md).
It is the misfit in motion: the Slack is each hand giving less because [the dispensable hand](piece_the_dispensable_hand.md) feels its pull can hardly matter, summed into a rope slacker than it should be.
