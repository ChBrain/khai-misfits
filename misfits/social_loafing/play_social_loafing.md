# Play: Social Loafing

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Social Loafing.
A rope with eight hands on it, every hand pulling and none pulling as hard as it would alone ... because the force is summed and no single grip can be told from the rest, so each eases off a shade that no one can see, and eight hands pull less than four would have, and everyone is, honestly, pulling.

## Arc

The bend is this: put effort in a pool where no one hand can be seen, and every hand eases, so more hands make less work each.
It runs on a split between the effort a person gives alone and the effort they give in a crowd.
Alone, the output is theirs: every ounce shows, and slack shows too, so they pull their weight. In a group the outputs merge into one sum, and a single contribution cannot be picked out of it, so easing off costs nothing that can be seen, and feeling that one grip among eight can hardly matter, the hand relaxes. Not a decision to shirk: a quiet, unnoticed settling to the level that a hidden, dispensable effort is worth.
And it compounds two ways. Add more hands and each matters less and hides better, so the per-head pull falls as the group grows. And the one who does pull hard finds themselves carrying the easers, doing the group's work for a share of the group's credit, until, rather than be the sap who hauls while others coast, they ease too. So the pool fills with willing, participating, softly-pulling hands, and the total sits below what the same people would have made apart.
Make each hand visible and each part matter, keep the group small and the contribution named, and the effort returns: what cannot be seen is what quietly slackens.

## Triggers

**[The Pool](plot_the_pool.md)**
A task is set for the group and its output is summed, not split. The Grader assigns the work to the team and measures the team, so no single contribution can be told from the whole. Closes when effort goes into a pool where no one hand can be seen.

**[The Slack](plot_the_slack.md)**
Cue: hidden in the pool, a grip can ease with no one the wiser. The Loafer eases the pull a shade, feeling that one hand among many can hardly matter, while the Sucker pulls harder to take up the drop. Closes when the effort has quietly settled below what each would give alone, and everyone is still, honestly, pulling.

**[The Dilution](plot_the_dilution.md)**
Cue: the group is behind, so more hands are added to help. The Piler adds heads, and the per-hand pull falls as the group grows, so the total barely moves, and the Sucker, tired of hauling the easers for a shared credit, finally eases too. Closes when more hands have made less work each and the process loss is at its widest.

**[The Name](plot_the_name.md)**
Cue: someone makes each hand visible and each part matter. The Namer names the contribution, shrinks the group, and ties the effort to the person, so the pull returns. Closes when the loafing lifts because it can be seen ... or the naming is judged to spoil the team spirit, and the pool closes back over the hands.

## Stakes

Whether effort is ever seen by the hand, or only ever summed in the pool.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward the full pull, toward the hidden ease, which costs nothing that shows and saves the one who takes it. The production cannot make a hand pull its hardest for an output no one can trace to it. It can only show that no one shirked and everyone took part, that each ease was small and unseen and rational, that the sucker who pulled hard was punished by carrying the rest, and that the one thing that would have held the effort up, a contribution named and made to matter, is the very thing a pooled and equal group was built to blur.
