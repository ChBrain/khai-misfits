# Piece: The Ringelmann Curve

## Taxonomy

[Social Loafing](play_social_loafing.md). Per-head effort falling as the group grows.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the group task](place_the_group_task.md), in the roster that keeps growing and the output that does not.
On [the rope](place_the_rope.md), in the force per hand that Ringelmann measured dropping with each hand added.

## Load Bearing

Without it loafing would be a fixed tax, the same in a pair as in a crowd, and easily bounded.
With it the loss grows with the group: the more hands, the smaller each share of credit and blame, the more dispensable each pull feels and the better it hides, so per-head effort declines as size climbs. The Ringelmann Curve is load bearing because it is the group-size law the whole phenomenon was first measured by (Ringelmann; Ingham et al.), the reason adding people to a lagging task lowers the pull of each and the total barely moves, which turns a sensible fix into a deeper cause.

## Apparent

A bigger, stronger team.
It looks like more capacity: more hands on the rope, more people on the job, surely more pull. The count rises visibly and the per-head effort falls invisibly, so the growth reads as strength right up until the total refuses to follow.

## Yearbook

Driven by [the piler](persona_the_piler.md) through [diluting](process_diluting.md), deepening [the dispensable hand](piece_the_dispensable_hand.md).
It is the scaling law of the misfit: the Ringelmann Curve is why more hands make less work each, opening [the process loss](piece_the_process_loss.md) wider with every arm added.
