# Piece: The Process Loss

## Taxonomy

[Social Loafing](play_social_loafing.md). The gap between what the group could produce and what it does.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the group task](place_the_group_task.md), between the potential of the members and the output of the team.
In the difference no one budgeted for, between the sum of the parts and the whole.

## Load Bearing

Without it the loafing would be a private matter, a few hands taking it easy with no cost to the whole.
With it there is a measurable shortfall: the group delivers less than the same people would apart, the potential minus the losses of motivation and coordination, and that shortfall is the price the misfit charges. The Process Loss is load bearing because it is the outcome the phenomenon is measured by (Steiner), the actual-below-potential that makes social loafing a real cost to reckon, the reason a team is so often worth less than its members added up.

## Apparent

Just how groups are.
It looks like an unremarkable fact of teamwork: groups are a bit inefficient, meetings are slow, committees underdeliver, everyone knows that. Naming it as loss rather than nature is the whole move, because treated as normal it is never recovered.

## Yearbook

Widened by [the ringelmann curve](piece_the_ringelmann_curve.md), and read across [the group task](place_the_group_task.md).
It is the misfit's bill: the Process Loss is what [the slack](piece_the_slack.md) and the dilution sum to, the whole worth less than its parts, the cost of an effort no one could see.
