# Place: The Cross-Section

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The physical plane where every member bears the load side by side, where partings happen and are counted, where the divided load is passed on. Hosts [The First to Part](plot_the_first_to_part.md), [The Handoff](plot_the_handoff.md), [The Run](plot_the_run.md), and [Nothing Holds](plot_nothing_holds.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

A bundle of many parallel members, holding together, sound and unremarkable.
Cut through it at any instant and every member is there at once, each carrying its own fraction of the load, each well within its rating, nothing on the plane to mark one strand from the next except the ordinary spread of a strength a hair higher here and a hair lower there.

## Holds

The members, sound and surviving, and the load divided among them, and the tally of how many have gone.
Here stand [the Strand](persona_the_strand.md) and [the Survivor](persona_the_survivor.md), the same member under two names before and after a parting, and here [the Load](persona_the_load.md) sits divided across whoever remains, and here the count is kept, a running fraction of members lost against the whole, the plane where [Sharing](process_sharing.md) and [Cascading](process_cascading.md) both run.

## Offers

A clean read of the instant: every share, every rating, every parting, laid out at once.
Because the plane cuts through the whole bundle together, it offers an honest tally, nothing hidden and no member off the books, so a parting is counted the moment it happens and the raised shares on the survivors are, in principle, there to be read.

## Withheld

That surviving is only holding a heavier share than before.
The Cross-Section shows the members and the count but cannot show that the survivors carrying a raised share are being loaded toward their own limit, that the very act of taking up a parted neighbour's load is the act that makes each of them the next candidate, so what reads on the plane as a bundle intact and holding is, unshown, a bundle counting down toward a fraction past which the same plane records a rupture instead of a tally.
