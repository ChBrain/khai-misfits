# Plan: Size the Redundancy

## Taxonomy

A **standing plan**: the intent of [the engineer's seat](position_engineer.md), inside [Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md).

## Owner

- Owner: [Engineer](position_engineer.md)

## Direction

Divide the load across many members so no single one is a point of failure, widen the margin on each, break the local sharing where the design allows, and hold the bundle far below the critical fraction. Do it knowing the redundancy buys distance and not immunity, that a shared load rises on every survivor as members drop, and that past the fraction no arrangement of sound members holds.

## Orders

([The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) sizes [the Bundle](piece_the_bundle.md) far above [the Load](persona_the_load.md), buying distance from [the Critical Fraction](piece_the_critical_fraction.md) through [Sizing](process_sizing.md), at a cost in material and weight.)

## Implementation

([The Drawing](place_the_drawing.md) sets the members and margins; the [sizing](process_sizing.md) process builds the redundancy far above the demand; the distance to [the Critical Fraction](piece_the_critical_fraction.md) is bought at real cost, and [Sharing](process_sharing.md) still carries a failure through the bundle past the fraction, so the redundancy is held far off and never made proof.)

## Targets

- [ ] the load is divided across many members, no single one a point of failure
- [ ] the margins are widened and the whole built far stronger than the load
- [ ] the local sharing is broken where the design allows, so a parting need not fall on its neighbours
- [ ] the redundancy is understood to buy distance from the fraction and never to abolish the cascade
