# Plot: Built to Share

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The opening beat: the redundancy is set, sound and genuine, before any member has parted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

On [the Drawing](place_the_drawing.md), a load with nowhere yet to fall: a demand to be met, and the sheet still blank of members.

## Action

[The Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md), on [Size the Redundancy](plan_size_the_redundancy.md), runs [Sizing](process_sizing.md) over [the Drawing](place_the_drawing.md) and sets [the Bundle](piece_the_bundle.md): many parallel members, drawn wide enough that the load can be divided among all of them at once, each member's portion far below what that member can bear. No single path carries the whole demand. No member, checked alone, is asked for more than a fraction of its strength.

## Stage

The Engineer adds members, not one heroic strand oversized to carry everything, but many, ordinary, each rated honestly and each given only a share. The margins are wide and comfortable: strength summed across the bundle far exceeds the load it is meant to carry. Checked member by member, every one passes with room to spare, and checked as a whole, the bundle is stronger than any single part of it could ever be alone.

## Tension

The redundancy is real, not a single path dressed up as several, and the Engineer's work is sound. But the same rule that divides the load safely among many members, so that no one of them is a point of failure, is the rule that will one day divide a failed member's load among the rest. Built to share the load is built to share the failure too, and nothing on the Drawing marks the difference: that waits, unremarked, for the load to rise.
