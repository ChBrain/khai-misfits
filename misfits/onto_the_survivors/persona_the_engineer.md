# Persona: The Engineer

## Taxonomy

[Engineer](position_engineer.md). The one who sizes the bundle, adding members and margins so the whole far outstrengthens the load, and who cannot abolish the fact that the sharing which makes it redundant is the sharing that carries the failure.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Careful. Correct. Building in the redundancy.
Is the one who divided the load across many members so no single one is a point of failure, who widened the margins and made the whole far stronger than the demand, and did it exactly as sound design requires.
No negligence in it: only a person building genuine redundancy, more members than the load needs and each rated above its share, the structure honestly and provably strong.

## Action

Sizes the redundancy, on [Size the Redundancy](plan_size_the_redundancy.md), setting on [the Drawing](place_the_drawing.md) enough members and margin that [the Bundle](piece_the_bundle.md) far outstrengthens [the Load](persona_the_load.md).
Works [Sizing](process_sizing.md) against the cascade, adding members, widening margins, breaking the local sharing where it can, and buys the bundle a long distance from [the Critical Fraction](piece_the_critical_fraction.md).
Does what a good engineer does with a shared load: makes the whole far stronger than the demand, and cannot make the sharing that spreads the load stop spreading the failure.

## Shadow

Not a rescuer with a costless fix, a person paying in material for a distance the sharing will still, past a point, close, and that is the difficulty: more redundancy holds the cascade off and never removes it.
Because a shared load rises on every survivor as members drop, no amount of sizing changes that past a critical fraction each transfer breaks the next, so the Engineer can only hold the bundle far below the fraction at a real cost in members, weight, and capacity, and past it no arrangement of sound members holds.
It does everything right, and doing everything right buys a margin against the cascade and never abolishes it.

## Tell

Says "there is redundancy to spare," and is telling the truth: the whole is far stronger than the load, and no single member is a point of failure.
Reads the many members as safety bought, when what they buy is distance: a critical fraction held far off, never removed, because the sharing that divides the load across them is the sharing that will carry a failure through them once too many have gone.
