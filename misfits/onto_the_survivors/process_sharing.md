# Process: Sharing

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). No single persona owns it: the redistribution rule itself, enacted whenever [the Strand](persona_the_strand.md) parts and [the Survivor](persona_the_survivor.md) takes up what it sheds, at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), inside [The Handoff](plot_the_handoff.md) and every parting that follows it. Carries [the Share](piece_the_share.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A member parting, its load with nowhere else to go.
The instant [a Strand](persona_the_strand.md) exceeds its strength and lets go, its share does not vanish: it is passed on, equally among every survivor under global sharing, onto the parted member's nearest neighbours under local sharing (Daniels; Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti).

## Direction

Toward every remaining share raised, the next-weakest brought to its limit.
Each transfer lifts every recipient's burden by exactly the parted member's portion, so the process moves the whole bundle, share by share, closer to whichever survivor was already nearest its own strength.

## Lever

The rule that makes redundancy work, sharing the load across whoever holds, is the rule that carries a failure onward, sharing the failed member's load across whoever holds: the same rule doing both.
There is no second mechanism that turns on only once something has gone wrong; the division that keeps the bundle safe while every member is sound is identical to the division that, once a member parts, moves that member's burden onto the rest.

## Echo

A share taken up honestly, and every survivor a little closer to its own limit for having taken it.
What looks, at each transfer, like the redundancy doing exactly what it was built to do is the same act, repeated member by member, that narrows the distance between a bundle holding and a bundle whose next transfer breaks the next member.
