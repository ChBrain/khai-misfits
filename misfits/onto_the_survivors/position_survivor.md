# Position: Survivor

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The seat of the members still holding after a parting, who take up the shed load and carry a raised share, each closer to its limit and each the next that may part.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Has

Intact strength and a share that has just risen: the standing to take up a parted member's load and hold the whole together, the redundancy doing what it is for.
A sound case: the survivor is within strength and its raised share may still sit below its limit, so absorbing the shed load and holding is exactly what a shared load asks.
No power to refuse the load shed onto it, only to hold the raised share or, if it exceeds its strength, to part in turn, and so the seat cannot take up the slack without being carried nearer its own limit.

## Orders

Take the extra.
When a member parts, absorb the load it sheds and carry the raised share, because that is what surviving a parting means and what redundancy is built to do.
Hold if the raised share is still below your strength, and if it is not, part and pass it on, becoming the next member the failure travels through.

## Loses

The margin it had before the parting, and, past the critical fraction, everything.
Because every share rises when a member parts and the survivors are drawn from the same spread, taking up the shed load carries each survivor nearer its limit, so what the seat surrenders with every parting it absorbs is the distance between its share and its strength.
It holds the bundle together for a while and, in holding it, is loaded toward its own parting, until past the fraction the share it must take is one no survivor can hold.

## Drives

Toward a survivor that is only ever the next to go,
each parting raising its share and each raised share bringing it nearer its limit,
so the seat that takes up the slack to save the bundle is the seat the bundle breaks next, spared once and only until the load comes round.
