# Piece: The Critical Fraction

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The threshold fraction of members lost, equivalently the peak of the bundle's load-extension curve, past which an avalanche runs to completion instead of being absorbed.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Read at [the Curve](place_the_curve.md), the load-extension curve on which the peak, the bundle's critical load, appears as the point past which added extension no longer meets rising resistance.

## Load Bearing

The bundle's strength is the maximum, the peak, of its load-extension curve: the critical load at which the bundle fails as a whole, set by the strength distribution of its members under the sharing rule (Daniels). The Critical Fraction is load bearing because it is a determinate point, not a vague zone: below it, a parting's raised shares still sit under the surviving members' strengths and the avalanche is absorbed; past it, the raised share on every survivor already exceeds what the survivors can bear, so each transfer breaks the next member in turn. State it precisely and no further: this piece names the peak as set by the particular strength distribution and sharing rule of a given bundle, not a single universal numeric value that holds across every bundle regardless of its members or its sharing rule.

## Apparent

A generous margin, a distant limit that the bundle, holding comfortably at its ordinary load, is nowhere near.

Seen at [the Curve](place_the_curve.md), the Critical Fraction reads as a far-off ceiling, reassuring by its distance, and the subtler fact that the same fraction can be approached not only by raising the external load but by the load simply pressing on fewer and fewer survivors as members drop, so the peak is a state the bundle can near without the load itself ever climbing, stays hidden behind how safely remote a margin sounds while it is still wide.
