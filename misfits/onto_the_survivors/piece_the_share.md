# Piece: The Share

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The load-sharing rule itself: what a parted member's load is passed onto, equally across all survivors or onto its nearest neighbours, the single mechanism that carries both the redundancy and the failure.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Enacted at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), where a parted member's load is redivided the instant it lets go.
Read at [the Curve](place_the_curve.md), where the rising shares trace the bundle's load-extension path toward the peak.

## Load Bearing

Under equal load sharing, a parted member's load is divided evenly across every surviving member, the solvable mean-field rule of the probabilistic bundle (Daniels); under local load sharing, that load falls instead on the member's nearest neighbours, concentrating the burden and letting the cascade localize into a running front rather than dispersing across the whole set (Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti). The Share is the central piece of this misfit: it is exactly the same rule doing two things at once. Divide the load across whoever holds is what makes a bundle redundant in the first place, each member carrying only a fraction, and divide a failed member's load across whoever holds is what carries a parting into a raised share on every survivor. State it precisely and no further: this piece does not claim the two effects are separable, or that a different sharing rule would keep the first without the second. They are one rule, read two ways.

## Apparent

A safety valve, a way of making sure no single member is ever asked to carry more than its rated portion.

Seen at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), the Share reads as pure mitigation, the mechanism that spreads risk thin across many members so that no one part bears it alone, and the fact that this identical mechanism is what transmits a parting onward, raising every remaining share the instant one member gives, stays hidden behind how protective a load-sharing rule sounds on its own.
