# Piece: The Bundle

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The parallel, independent set of members carrying one load together: the redundancy itself, genuine and sound, before any member has parted.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Sized at [the Drawing](place_the_drawing.md), where [the Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) sets the members and the margins.
Read at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), where every member stands together at one instant, whole.

## Load Bearing

A bundle of parallel members carries a load together, each holding a share well below its own strength, so the structure is genuinely redundant and sound, and every member checked alone passes (Daniels). The Bundle is load bearing because it names what makes the redundancy real rather than nominal: many members, no member standing alone, no common cause running through the set, and the whole meant to outlast any one part. State it precisely and no further: the redundancy this piece carries is sound and thoroughly built, not thin, not skimped, not a single path dressed up as several. That soundness is exactly what makes the later trap possible, a structure this honest has nothing to blame when it goes.

## Apparent

A margin of safety, plain and comfortable: many members, one load, far more strength than the load requires.

Seen at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), the Bundle reads as simple abundance, member after member each carrying its own small share, and the true state, that this same soundness is the precondition for a rule which will carry a failure through the whole set, stays hidden behind how comfortably safe a wide, sound bundle looks.

Its members are drawn from [the Spread](piece_the_spread.md), so there is always a weakest; when one parts, [the Share](piece_the_share.md) it sheds runs onto [the Survivor](persona_the_survivor.md) and every remaining member in the Bundle takes a heavier load.
