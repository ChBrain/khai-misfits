# Place: The Curve

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). The analytic load-extension curve of the bundle, rising to a peak that is the bundle's own strength and turning over past it. Hosts [No Margin Left](plot_no_margin_left.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

The curve climbing steadily as the load rises, one single peak, and the point past which the line turns down.
Below the peak every extra strain the bundle takes is met by a bundle still capable of taking more; at the peak the capacity stops climbing; past it, the same curve that rose now falls, and the fall is the signature of a bundle whose survivors can no longer sustain what is asked of them.

## Holds

The bundle's whole load-extension relation, drawn as one line: the climb, the peak, the turn.
Here is where [the Engineer](persona_the_engineer.md) reads [the Critical Fraction](piece_the_critical_fraction.md), the single point at which the load the bundle can sustain stops rising and starts falling, the point Daniels derives as the critical load of the probabilistic bundle and the point past which Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti's avalanche stops being finite.

## Offers

A single, legible number: where the peak sits, and how far the current strain is from it.
Because the curve is analytic and drawn once for the whole bundle, it offers the Engineer the clearest possible reading of the critical point, a place to set the margin against, a number to build [the Drawing](place_the_drawing.md) around.

## Withheld

How close the dwindling survivors already are to the peak, as members drop.
Like a still diagram, the Curve shows where the peak is but not the bundle's live position on it: it cannot show that each parting on [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md) moves the bundle along its own curve toward that point, so a curve read once, safely below the peak, gives no warning of how few partings remain before the same curve is the one turning down.
