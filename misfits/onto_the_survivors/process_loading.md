# Process: Loading

## Taxonomy

[Onto the Survivors](play_onto_the_survivors.md). [The Load](persona_the_load.md) running [Raise the Load](plan_raise_the_load.md) at [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), the demand the bundle carries, raised until the weakest member parts and then held while the same weight rides onto the survivors, opening [The First to Part](plot_the_first_to_part.md) and, later, closing on [No Margin Left](plot_no_margin_left.md).

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A demand carried by the bundle, divided across its members, well within what the whole can bear.
At [the Cross-Section](place_the_cross_section.md), the load sits on every member as a fraction below that member's strength, ordinary and unremarkable, and begins to rise.

## Direction

Toward the weakest member's limit, then toward the same weight over fewer shoulders.
The load rises only until [a Strand](persona_the_strand.md) reaches its rating and parts; from there it does not grow, it is held, unchanged, while its total is redistributed across whoever remains, so the direction of the process is not upward in magnitude but downward in how many are left to carry it.

## Lever

The same total, divided among fewer survivors, presses harder on each with every parting, without the load itself growing (Daniels).
Nothing about the demand has to rise for the bundle to be pressed harder: the load only has to be held steady while the members thin, and the division alone does the rest.

## Echo

An ordinary demand, steady on the gauge, made fatal only by how few remain to hold it.
Read the load at any moment and it looks unremarkable, well inside its rating, never spiking; what has changed is not the weight but the number of shoulders under it, and the gauge that shows a load holding steady is the same gauge that, past a point, is showing a load no remaining member can bear.
