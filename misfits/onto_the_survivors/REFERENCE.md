# Onto the Survivors: Reference

The research warrant for **Onto the Survivors** (play and company not yet staged;
this warrant is drafted first, to prove the science and the distinction before authoring).
The dynamic is the fiber-bundle account of cascading failure: Daniels's probabilistic bundle model and
the load-redistribution avalanche reviewed by Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the fiber-bundle load-redistribution cascade as a **redistribution-cascade structure**: a set of
parallel, independent members sharing a load, each member sound within its own strength but each strength
drawn from a spread, so that when the load is raised the weakest member reaches its limit and fails
first, and its share of the load is passed to the survivors, which raises the burden on every one of
them, and if that raised burden now exceeds the next-weakest survivor's strength it fails in turn and
passes its share on again, a cascade of successive failures that below a critical fraction of the load is
finite and absorbed but past it runs, member by member, to the complete rupture of the whole bundle. The
governing law is not that any member was weak or that the structure lacked redundancy, but that **the
very rule that makes a shared, redundant load safe, distributing a failed member's load onto the
survivors, is the rule that carries the failure through them, so past a critical fraction of the load
each transfer breaks the next member and the redundancy destroys itself in a cascade that no single,
individually-safe transfer set out to cause**. The play models the documented mechanics: the probabilistic
bundle of many members with distributed strengths under a load-sharing rule, and the critical load at
which the bundle gives way as a whole (Daniels), and the redistribution avalanche whose size grows
without bound as the load approaches the critical point, sharpest under local load sharing, where a
failed member's load falls on its immediate neighbours and the cascade localizes into a running front
rather than dispersing across the whole set (Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti), as the load-bearing
vectors of the structure, not as a tale of a weak member or a skimped design.

It is distinct from the house's The Brittle Optimum, where slack is deliberately pared away to reach an
optimum and a later shock then finds no buffer left to absorb it: there something was removed and
something struck, here nothing is removed and nothing strikes, the redundancy is real and fully intact
and the members are independent, and the cascade is driven by the load-sharing rule meeting the spread of
member strengths, not by a cut buffer meeting a blow. It is distinct from Belt and Braces, where
redundant components are added and then defeated together by a common cause that takes them at one
stroke: there the redundancy fails because a single shared event reaches all of it at once, here the
members have no common cause and fail one at a time, the redundancy defeated not by a shared root event
but by the sequential transfer of load from each failed member onto the survivors. And it is distinct
from The Load It Always Carried, the house's fatigue, where a single monolithic part accumulates
invisible damage cycle by cycle until a crack runs: there one part is quietly damaged over time and the
failure is the sum of a hidden tally, here nothing accumulates over time and no single member is damaged,
the failure is the near-instantaneous redistribution of load across a population of sound members under
one raised load, driven by the spread of their strengths rather than by any hidden count in one of them.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast a weak
  member or a skimped design, because the source finding it stages is precisely that each part is sound:
  every member holds within its own rated strength, the load-sharing rule is exactly what makes a
  redundant structure redundant, and each individual transfer of a failed member's load onto the
  survivors is small and locally safe (Daniels). No slack was removed and no shock was struck, unlike The
  Brittle Optimum, and no common cause took the members together, unlike Belt and Braces: the cascade is
  authored by no one and emerges from the structure, the spread of member strengths meeting the
  redistribution rule, so the misfit is that a genuinely redundant, sound structure fails by precisely
  the load-sharing mechanism that made it redundant, each transfer correct and the sum a rupture.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The moves that hold it
  off, adding more members, widening the strength margins, breaking the local sharing so a failure cannot
  fall on its neighbours, designing the survivors for the load they will carry after the first failures,
  all cost real material, weight, and capacity, and none abolishes the fact that a shared load rises on
  every survivor as members drop away (Daniels; Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti). Past the critical
  fraction no arrangement of sound members holds, because the sharing that distributes the load is the
  sharing that distributes the failure, so the reckoning is redundancy overbuilt against a cascade the
  load transfer will always, past a point, carry through, not a fix that removes the danger.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never
  claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the probabilistic bundle, the equal-load-sharing
  rule, and the critical load are Daniels's, the redistribution avalanche and the local-load-sharing
  sharpening are Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti's, and the condition the trap actually requires,
  parallel independent members with distributed strengths, a rule that shares a failed member's load onto
  the survivors, and a critical load fraction past which the avalanche completes, is carried as carefully
  as the finding itself. The scope is held to load-bearing systems with genuine load sharing across
  parallel elements, sharpest under local sharing, and not stretched into every metaphorical cascade it
  does not govern.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element. The play and its company are not yet authored; these are the
proposed load-bearing pieces the warrant commits the staging to carry, one per vector, no filler.

- **The Bundle**: carries the parallel, independent, load-sharing set (Daniels), the genuine redundancy,
  many members holding one load together, no common cause among them.
- **The Spread**: carries the distribution of member strengths (Daniels), the variance that guarantees a
  weakest to fail first and a next-weakest waiting to be pushed over by the transfer.
- **The Share**: carries the load-sharing rule (Daniels; Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti), a failed
  member's load passed onto the survivors, locally onto its neighbours under local sharing, each transfer
  individually small and safe and the mechanism of the whole cascade.
- **The Avalanche**: carries the cascade (Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti), successive failures once a
  transfer breaks the next member, the run of breakings whose size grows without bound as the load nears
  the critical point.
- **The Critical Fraction**: carries the threshold (Daniels), the load fraction past which the avalanche
  runs to completion instead of being absorbed, the point at which the survivors can no longer take the
  redistributed load.
- **The Rupture**: carries the whole-bundle failure (Daniels; Pradhan, Hansen, and Chakrabarti), the
  complete giving-way once the cascade has run through every remaining member, the redundancy fully spent.
