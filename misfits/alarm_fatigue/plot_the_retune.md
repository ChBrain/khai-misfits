# Plot: The Retune

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Review](place_the_review.md), the channel gone deaf and one actor moving to give it back its meaning.
The flood laid out in numbers, the thresholds up for reconsideration, and the name of every danger a higher line would let pass in the room.

## Action

[Tuning](process_tuning.md): [The Tuner](persona_the_tuner.md), holding [the tuner's seat](position_tuner.md), raises the threshold and quiets the nuisance, while [The Setter](persona_the_setter.md), holding [the setter's seat](position_setter.md), faces the instinct that no danger may be missed.

## Stage

[The Tuner](persona_the_tuner.md), having tabled [Tune the Alarms](plan_tune_the_alarms.md), raises the line and prioritises the deadly so the few real cries can stand out, owning the miss the higher threshold concedes.
[The Setter](persona_the_setter.md), still on [Set It Low](plan_set_it_low.md), hears in every raised notch a danger let through, and the fear that set the alarm low reaches again for the dial.

## Tension

The one move that makes the alarm heard is the one the fear of a miss forbids.
There is no easy remedy: raising the threshold plainly restores the alarm's meaning, yet every notch up is a real event it will now let pass, and the instinct that no danger may be missed is exactly what will lower the line and flood the channel again. So the play closes where it must, the alarms retuned and the true cries standing out once more ... or the fear wins, the threshold drops, and the din returns.
