# Plot: The Setting

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Panel](place_the_panel.md), a rare and deadly danger and an alarm to be set against it.
The setter resolved that a missed danger is unforgivable, the threshold there to be drawn.

## Action

[Warning](process_warning.md): [The Setter](persona_the_setter.md), holding [the setter's seat](position_setter.md), draws the threshold low, while [The Alarm](persona_the_alarm.md), holding [the alarm's seat](position_alarm.md), is armed to cry on anything that crosses it.

## Stage

[The Setter](persona_the_setter.md), having tabled [Set It Low](plan_set_it_low.md), draws the line low to catch the rare danger, weighing the cheap false cry against the dreadful miss and erring toward the warning.
[The Alarm](persona_the_alarm.md), on [Cry Out](plan_cry_out.md), stands ready to sound the instant the low threshold trips, on the merest sign and the real emergency the same.

## Tension

The setting that guarantees no danger is missed guarantees a flood.
There is nothing to fault: for a single deadly-if-missed alarm, drawing the threshold low is plainly right, the false cry cheap and the miss catastrophe. But a line low enough to catch the rare danger sits below the common harmless, so the plot closes with the alarm armed to miss nothing, and the deluge of false cries that total coverage of a benign world must raise, unseen, about to begin.
