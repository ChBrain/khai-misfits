# Piece: The Threshold

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md). The detection criterion, the line that trades missed dangers against false alarms and, set low against a low base rate, fires mostly false.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the panel](place_the_panel.md), where it is set and the alarm fires the moment a reading crosses it.
Reconsidered at [the review](place_the_review.md), where raising it is the whole of the remedy and the whole of the cost.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is no trade to make, and the alarm is just a reading, neither eager nor deaf.
With it the whole trap has its fulcrum: a single line whose height sets both how few dangers are missed and how many false alarms are raised, so that lowering it to miss nothing raises the flood, and raising it to cut the flood concedes a miss. The Threshold is load bearing because it is the one dial the entire mechanism turns on (Green and Swets): every quantity in the trap, the coverage, the false-alarm rate, the responder's belief, the risk of a miss, moves as it moves, so the piece carries the criterion that makes catching the danger and being heard two ends of one setting that cannot both be had.

## Apparent

A technical setting, a number dialled in once and forgotten, a matter for the manual.
Seen alone, the threshold looks like a neutral configuration, set sensibly low to be safe, and the truth that this one line governs the whole trade between a miss and a flood, that setting it to catch every danger dooms it to cry mostly false and go unheard, stays hidden behind how ordinary and prudent a low safety setting appears.

## Yearbook

Set by [the setter](persona_the_setter.md) through [warning](process_warning.md), the low line drawn to miss no danger.
It is the fulcrum of the trap: the Threshold set low is what raises [the false alarm](piece_the_false_alarm.md) in flood, and only [the retune](piece_the_retune.md), raising it, can cut the flood, at the price of the very [miss](piece_the_miss.md) the low setting was drawn to prevent.
