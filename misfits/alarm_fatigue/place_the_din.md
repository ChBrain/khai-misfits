# Place: The Din

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md). The saturated channel, the flood of false alarms in which the responder is deadened.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Shown

Alarms upon alarms, hundreds a shift, beeping and shrieking and flashing until the sound is a wall.
The responder moving through it half-hearing, and each new cry landing on ears that have learned a cry means, almost always, nothing.

## Holds

The flooding and the deadening, the mass of false cries and the discounting they teach.
Here the alarm's constant sounding meets the responder's finite attention, so the din holds the point where a flood of warnings becomes a silence of belief: the cries come so thick and so nearly always false that no one of them can carry weight, so it is where the channel meant to compel a response empties of meaning.

## Offers

An excuse the responder is right to take.
Because the din is a flood the responder cannot chase and stay any use, it offers the sound reason to discount the whole channel, to silence and tune out and work through the noise, so it hands out the rational grounds for ignoring the alarm, and with it the habit that will ignore the true one too.

## Withheld

The weight a single cry should carry.
The din cannot let any one alarm stand out from the mass, so it withholds the significance a lone warning would have had, drowning each cry in the hundred beside it, and, making every cry cost the same nothing, it withholds from the true alarm the one thing it needs, to sound unlike the false.
