# Process: Tuning

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md). The raising of the threshold and quieting of the nuisance that restores the alarm's meaning, at the cost of a danger let pass.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A channel gone deaf, and someone who sees that an alarm heard by no one saves no one.
At [the review](place_the_review.md), the tuner laying out the flood and moving to raise the line, the setter's fear of the miss in the room.

## Direction

Toward fewer cries that carry more weight.
The tuner raises the threshold, prioritises the deadly, and quiets the nuisance, so tuning drives the channel from a flood no one heeds toward a trickle that might be, cutting the false cries so the true ones stand out, and cutting, with them, some of the true.

## Lever

The plain truth that an unheeded alarm protects no one.
Because a channel everyone ignores is worse than one that sometimes stays silent, the case to retune is sound, so tuning runs on the same care that set the alarm low, turned now to making it heard, the remedy driven by no less concern for the danger than the flood was.

## Echo

An alarm that means something again, and a danger it will now occasionally miss.
The channel has its meaning back and the true cries can stand out, and the higher threshold has quietly conceded some real event it will no longer catch, so what reads as the alarm fixed is, in truth, a trade, the deafness ended by consenting to sometimes not be warned, and the setter's fear already reaching for the dial.
