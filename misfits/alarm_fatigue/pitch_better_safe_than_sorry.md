# Pitch: Better Safe Than Sorry

## Taxonomy

[Alarm Fatigue](play_alarm_fatigue.md). The default key the whole production is read in.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Tenor

Careful, responsible, unwilling to gamble on a life.
The plain good sense of setting the alarm low so that no real danger slips past, and accepting a few false cries as the cheap price of catching the deadly one.
Nothing lax in it: only the sound instinct to warn on anything that might be the thing.

## Undertow

Under the caution, a channel so flooded with false alarms that the responder learns to hear none of them.
Because the danger is rare and the world mostly benign, a threshold low enough to miss nothing trips on the harmless, and the flood of false cries teaches the responder to discount, silence, and tune out, so when the true alarm sounds it is silenced with the rest.
The setting made so no alarm would be missed is the reason the one that matters is.

## Nerve

The feeling of doing the safe thing, setting the alarm to warn on the merest sign,
sure it is the way to miss no danger, because for a single alarm it is,
while every false cry spends a little more of the responder's belief, until the real one arrives sounding just like the hundred that were nothing.

## Echo

After the run: a monitor shrieking into a room that no longer hears it, the one true alarm silenced with the false,
no one careless and no one cruel, only a setter warning on everything and a responder rightly doubting a signal wrong every time,
and a retune that would have made the alarm mean something again, refused as long as the fear held, because raising the threshold means consenting to sometimes not be warned.
