# Play: The Commons

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Commons.
A field that belongs to everyone and so to no one, where each neighbour is free to graze one more animal ... and the gain of that animal is his alone, while the cost of the thinning grass is shared out among them all, so every neighbour adds, and the field that fed them all feeds none.

## Arc

The bend is this: everyone takes a fair share, and the field goes bare.
It runs on a gap between two ledgers.
On the private ledger, one more animal on the green is pure gain to the one who owns it, and the harm it does to the grass is split so many ways that his own share of the harm is almost nothing.
On the common ledger, every animal drawn down without limit outruns what the grass can grow back, so the field falls below the line where it renews, and keeps falling.
The one who grazes less to spare the field does not spare it: the grass he leaves is eaten by the next neighbour's herd, so restraint is a gift to the takers and a loss to the giver, and the sensible move, from every single seat, is to take now before the field is gone.
Bind the neighbours to a cap they cannot each undercut, and the field can hold: but the field is everyone's, so it is no one's to save.

## Triggers

**[Plan](plot_plan.md)**
The green stands full and open, every neighbour free to graze it. The Reckoner works the private math: one more animal is a whole gain to its owner, and its harm to the grass is split among all. The Grazer decides to add. Closes when the private ledger says take.

**[Do](plot_do.md)**
Cue: every commoner runs the same math at once. The Grazer adds one more, and the Taker draws on the field's upkeep while adding nothing to it, and the herd swells past what the grass can grow back. Closes when the draw outruns the renewal.

**[Check](plot_check.md)**
Cue: the grass thins and the bare patch spreads. The Steward grazes less to spare the field and watches the spared grass eaten by the others, and the Tally counts the herd and warns, and can bind no one. Closes when restraint is punished and the field keeps failing.

**[The Fence](plot_the_fence.md)**
Cue: the Bylaw sets a cap all agree is wise, and the Warden binds every neighbour to it with a watch and a real penalty, so the field can hold. The open green becomes a governed one. Closes when the field recovers under rule ... or no one will be bound, and the green goes to dust.

## Stakes

Whether the neighbours can bind themselves before the field is gone.
Every plot moves the same way: not toward the field, toward one more. The production cannot make a single herder's restraint pay, because whatever grass one spares, the next will take. It can only show that no one was greedy, that each took only a fair share, that the herder who took less lost most, and that the one thing that would have saved the field, a limit every neighbour was held to at once, was the one thing an open commons, left to itself, could not grow.
