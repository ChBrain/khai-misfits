# Plot: Check

## Taxonomy

[The Commons](play_the_commons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The bare patch](place_the_bare_patch.md), spreading, where the grass first gave out.
The herd is past what the field can carry, so the grass thins, and the neighbours can see it thinning. The question is whether anyone can stop it alone.

## Action

[Restraint](process_restraint.md): [The Steward](persona_the_steward.md), holding [the conserver's seat](position_conserver.md), grazes fewer animals to spare the field, and watches the grass he leaves eaten by the others.
[Depletion](process_depletion.md): [The Tally](persona_the_tally.md) at [the monitor's seat](position_monitor.md) counts the herd against the failing field and sounds the warning.

## Stage

[The Steward](persona_the_steward.md) tables [Graze Less](plan_graze_less.md): pull animals off to save the green, and lose, because the spared grass feeds the next herder's beasts and [the shared cost](piece_the_shared_cost.md) still lands on him in full.
[The Tally](persona_the_tally.md) tables [Walk the Field](plan_walk_the_field.md): count, measure, and warn that the herd has crossed the line, and bind no one, so [the overshoot](piece_the_overshoot.md) rolls on toward [the bare ground](piece_the_bare_ground.md) with the alarm ringing.

## Tension

The one who does the right thing is the one who loses by it.
The steward's restraint is real and costly and useless, because a field spared by one and taken by all is not spared at all, only redistributed to the takers. The monitor's count is accurate and unheeded, because seeing the tragedy is not the same as being able to stop it. So the field keeps failing, the giver falls behind the takers, and the plainest lesson on the green is that restraint is a private loss, which teaches the next herder not to try it.
