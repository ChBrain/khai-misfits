# Plot: Fence

## Taxonomy

[The Commons](play_the_commons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The meeting](place_the_meeting.md), the neighbours gathered at last around a field going bare.
The green cannot save itself, and no single herder can save it, so the only way left is one they must all take together: a limit each is held to, made and watched and enforced by the commoners themselves.

## Action

[Enclosure](process_enclosure.md): [The Bylaw](persona_the_bylaw.md), holding [the rule-maker's seat](position_rule_maker.md), writes a cap on the herd that all agree is wise, and [The Warden](persona_the_warden.md) at [the governor's seat](position_governor.md) gives it a watch and a penalty at [the fence line](place_the_fence_line.md).

## Stage

[The Bylaw](persona_the_bylaw.md) tables [Set the Cap](plan_set_the_cap.md): agree the number of animals the green can bear, the rule every neighbour would keep if only they trusted the others to keep it too.
[The Warden](persona_the_warden.md) tables [Fence the Green](plan_fence_the_green.md): bind each herder to the cap with a watch that sees who overgrazes and a penalty that bites, so [the tragedy](piece_the_tragedy.md) is halted and the field can hold, no longer an open commons but a governed one.

## Tension

The field is saved by taking away the freedom that made it a commons.
The cap works, and it works only because it is enforced: the same neighbours who could not each restrain themselves can, together, bind themselves, watch each other, and punish the overgrazer, and under that home-made rule the grass comes back. None of it is free. The green is now closed where it was open, watched where it was trusted, and ruled where it was shared, and every neighbour trades the liberty to take one more for a field that will still be there next year ... or the cap is set and no one will be bound to it, the watch goes unwatched, and the green finishes its slide to dust.
