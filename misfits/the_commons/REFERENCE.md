# The Commons: Reference

The research warrant for [The Commons](play_the_commons.md). The title is Hardin's phrase,
worn short; the play runs on the economics of common-property resources, the collective-action
problem beneath them, and the self-governance literature that answers Hardin, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the tragedy of the commons as a **systemic system**: a shared, rivalrous, non-excludable
resource destroyed without a single greedy act, because each user keeps the whole gain of one more
draw while the cost of it is split across all, so every user rationally draws, and the sum of the
fair shares outruns what the resource can renew. The play models the documented mechanics, the
gain kept private and the cost made common, the marginal unit always worth adding, the free ride
on upkeep no one can be billed for, the draw crossing the renewal rate, restraint punished because
what one spares another takes, and the self-governance that alone restores the resource at the cost
of the openness that defined it, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of
greed. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: where a resource
is held in common and its use is unpriced, individually rational use sums to collective ruin, and
the remedy is neither inevitable collapse nor a distant owner but rules the users make, monitor,
and enforce upon themselves.

## Origin

The phenomenon in practice, and the research spine beneath it.

| Source           | Key Work / Event                                                                                              | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Lloyd**        | _Two Lectures on the Checks to Population_ (1833)                                                             | The original **overgrazing** illustration: why cattle on a common are kept past what the ground can bear. The pasture Hardin borrowed. The Green and the Herd.                             |
| **Gordon**       | "The Economic Theory of a Common-Property Resource: The Fishery", _Journal of Political Economy_ (1954)       | **Open-access externality** formalised: under common property the resource is used until private gain meets private cost, far past the social optimum. The Shared Cost and the Overshoot.  |
| **Hardin**       | "The Tragedy of the Commons", _Science_ (1968)                                                                | **The spine and the name**: freedom in a commons brings ruin to all, because each herder's rational gain is whole and his share of the harm is a fraction. The play's governing law.       |
| **Olson**        | _The Logic of Collective Action_ (1965)                                                                       | **Free riding**: public goods are under-provided because their benefit cannot be withheld, so each does best to let others bear the upkeep. The Free Ride, the play's second engine.       |
| **Ostrom**       | _Governing the Commons_ (1990); Nobel Prize in Economics (2009)                                               | **The answer to inevitability**: enduring commons are governed by design principles, clear bounds, home-made rules, monitoring, graduated sanctions. The Bylaw, the Warden, the Enclosure. |
| **Practitioner** | fishery collapses and groundwater depletion; and irrigation, forest, and pasture commons under community rule | The tragedy and its cure in the field: where the resource fell to open access, and where self-governance held it for generations. The cost of the fence, and the proof it can work.        |

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any
  seat as greedy, because the source finding is precisely that no greed is needed: the grazer keeps
  only what his own animal earns, the free rider only declines an upkeep that is no one's job, and
  the steward who restrains himself is right that his restraint will not save the field. The misfit
  is that a fair share for each is a bare field for all.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the commons healing itself. Self-governance
  can restore the field, but only by closing what was open, watching what was trusted, and ruling what
  was shared, so the reckoning is a freedom traded for a fence, not a costless recovery, and the fence
  holds only while the neighbours keep enforcing it.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and Ostrom's
  correction to Hardin is carried as carefully as Hardin's tragedy.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Common Green](piece_the_common_green.md)**: carries the common-property resource (Lloyd; Gordon),
  rivalrous and non-excludable, everyone's to use and no one's to spare.
- **[The Private Gain](piece_the_private_gain.md)**: carries the whole benefit of one more draw (Hardin),
  kept undivided by the one who takes it.
- **[The Shared Cost](piece_the_shared_cost.md)**: carries the externality (Gordon; Hardin), the harm split
  so many ways that no single user meets a reason to stop.
- **[The One More](piece_the_one_more.md)**: carries the marginal unit (Gordon), always worth adding from one
  seat, ruinous only in the sum.
- **[The Free Ride](piece_the_free_ride.md)**: carries the free-rider problem (Olson), upkeep under-provided
  because its benefit cannot be withheld.
- **[The Overshoot](piece_the_overshoot.md)**: carries draw past the renewal rate (Gordon), the crossing of
  carrying capacity after which losses compound.
- **[The Bare Ground](piece_the_bare_ground.md)**: carries the depleted resource (Hardin), and the cruel term
  that the one who restrained himself is left worst off.
- **[The Tragedy](piece_the_tragedy.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Hardin), and its remedy in
  self-governance at a cost (Olson; Ostrom), the ungoverned commons that need not have been ungoverned.
