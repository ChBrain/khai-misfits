# Regulatory Capture: Reference

The research warrant for [Regulatory Capture](play_regulatory_capture.md). The title is the
economists' term; the play runs on the theory of economic regulation, the collective-action
account of concentrated and diffuse interests, and the modern work on cultural capture, all
cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging regulatory capture as a **systemic system**: an agency created to regulate an industry in
the public interest comes, over time, to advance the industry's interest instead, without a single
bribe, because the industry is concentrated and organised while the public is diffuse and absent,
and the agency depends on the industry for the expertise, the data, and the careers it runs on. The
play models the documented mechanics, the public-interest mandate, the asymmetry between a
concentrated interest that shows up and a diffuse one that cannot, the agency's dependence on the
regulated for facts and staff, the revolving door that aligns the regulator's future with the
regulated, the cultural capture by which the agency adopts the industry's worldview, and the served
industry that results, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of corruption. The
governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: regulation tends to be
captured by the industry it governs and operated for its benefit, not because regulators are venal
but because only the industry has the concentrated stake, the information, and the careers to shape
it. It is the guardian's mirror of the house's Shirky Principle: not a body preserving its own
problem, but a body coming to serve the ones it was set to watch.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the
  regulator as bought or the industry as a briber, because the source finding is precisely that
  neither is needed: the industry's voice is the informed one, the revolving door is where the
  expertise lives, and hearing one side for years is enough to make its frame feel like plain realism
  (Kwak). The misfit is that an agency left alone with the trade it judges is captured by proximity,
  not corruption.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into an incorruptible regulator holding the
  line. The capture is undone only by structure, walling the agency off, funding it apart, seating an
  advocate for the diffuse public, and cooling the door, each of which spends the very expertise,
  cooperation, and ease that closeness to the industry supplied, so the reckoning is independence
  bought at the price of access, not virtue winning the room.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the theory of regulation, the
  collective-action account, and the cultural-capture work are theirs, and the condition capture
  actually requires, a concentrated regulated interest and a diffuse public, is carried as carefully
  as the finding itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Mandate](piece_the_mandate.md)**: carries the public-interest charge (Bernstein), the agency created
  to guard the public against the industry.
- **[The Concentrated Interest](piece_the_concentrated_interest.md)**: carries the collective-action asymmetry
  (Olson), the industry organised to show up and the public too diffuse to.
- **[The Dependence](piece_the_dependence.md)**: carries information asymmetry (Laffont & Tirole), the agency's
  reliance on the industry for the facts, models, and staff it regulates by.
- **[The Revolving Door](piece_the_revolving_door.md)**: carries the career channel (Laffont & Tirole), the flow
  between regulator and regulated that aligns the agency's future with the industry's.
- **[The Cultural Capture](piece_the_cultural_capture.md)**: carries cultural capture (Kwak), the agency adopting
  the industry's worldview through relationship and status, with no quid pro quo.
- **[The Served Industry](piece_the_served_industry.md)**: carries the captured outcome (Stigler; Peltzman), rules
  written for the regulated and against new entrants, the public interest displaced.
- **[The Regulatory Capture](piece_the_regulatory_capture.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Stigler), the
  guardian serving the guarded, and its remedy in independence at a cost, held whole by nobody.
