# Play: Regulatory Capture

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Regulatory Capture.
An agency is founded to guard the public against an industry, and it does ... and then it learns the trade from the only people who know it, hears from the only people who show up, hires and is hired by the only people who understand the work, until the watchdog set over the industry is staffed, informed, and befriended by it, and comes, in perfect sincerity, to guard the industry against the public.

## Arc

The bend is this: the agency does not have to be corrupted to be captured, only to be left alone with the industry.
It runs on a split between two interests, one concentrated and one diffuse.
The industry has everything at stake in the rules and the expertise to shape them, so it shows up, every time, to every hearing, with the data, the drafts, and the people. The public has a little at stake each, spread across millions, so no one shows up for it, because no citizen's slice of the harm is worth a day of their life to fight. The agency needs to regulate a trade it cannot fully see, so it depends on the industry for the facts, the models, and the staff, and the only careers open to its experts run back into the industry it oversees.
None of it is a bribe. The industry knows the work best, so its voice is the informed one; the revolving door is just where the expertise lives; and the agency, hearing one side for years, comes to hold that side's frame as simple realism. So the rules are written with the industry, then for it, then to keep newcomers out, and the public interest the agency was built to serve is not betrayed but quietly unrepresented.
Wall the agency off, fund it apart, seat an advocate for the diffuse, and cool the door, and it can guard the public again: but each of those spends the expertise, the cooperation, and the ease that hearing only the industry supplied.

## Triggers

**[The Mandate](plot_the_mandate.md)**
An agency is founded to guard the public against an industry, and given the charge and the power. The Watchdog takes up the mandate, the Lobby lines up to be regulated, and the Public, each with a sliver at stake, does not come. Closes when the concentrated interest is in the room and the diffuse one is not.

**[The Dependence](plot_the_dependence.md)**
Cue: the agency must regulate a trade it cannot fully see. The Lobby supplies the data, the models, the draft rules, and the expert staff, because it alone has them, and the Watchdog comes to hold the industry's frame as plain realism. Closes when the only facts and the only worldview in the agency are the industry's.

**[The Revolving Door](plot_the_revolving_door.md)**
Cue: the experts the agency needs come from the industry and return to it. The Alumnus moves from firm to agency and back, so the regulator's next employer is the regulated, and the rules soften toward the hand that will pay next. Closes when the door has aligned the agency's careers with the industry's interest, and no one crossed a line to do it.

**[The Firewall](plot_the_firewall.md)**
Cue: someone moves to wall the agency off, fund it apart, seat an advocate for the diffuse public, and cool the door. The Reformer restores the independence the capture fed on. Closes when the agency can hear the public again, at the cost of the industry's expertise and ease ... or the firewall is judged to hobble expert oversight, and the room fills with the industry once more.

## Stakes

Whether the agency ever answers to the public it was built for, or only ever to the industry it lives among.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: not toward the diffuse public, whose interest no one carries into the room, but toward the concentrated industry, whose interest fills it. The production cannot make a million citizens each fight for a sliver, nor make an agency regulate blind a trade only the industry can see. It can only show that no one was bribed and no one betrayed a trust, that the industry's voice was the informed one and the revolving door was only where the expertise lived, and that the one thing that would have kept the watchdog the public's, an agency walled off from the trade it judges, is the very closeness that made it expert.
