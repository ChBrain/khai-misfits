# The Frozen Middle: Reference

The research warrant for [The Frozen Middle](play_the_frozen_middle.md). The term is
practitioner vocabulary for middle management that stalls change; the mechanics the play
runs on are grounded in the middle-management and organisational-change literature cited
below.

## Line of Work

Staging the frozen middle as a **systemic system**: an organisation in which a change set
with conviction at the top is never felt at the bottom, because the layer between them is
measured on continuity while charged with change, and rationally damps the disruptive
signal in both directions to protect the delivery it answers for. The play models the
documented mechanics, the middle's balancing of continuity and change, its sensemaking and
translation of strategy on the way down, its synthesising of reports on the way up, and the
active inertia by which an organisation meets disruption with more of its established
routine, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of obstructive
managers. The source literature shows the middle both drives change and buffers it, championing
upward as well as damping downward; the play stages the buffering as its governing law: the
middle is a buffer, and a buffer that protects delivery passes the small and safe while
attenuating the large, so the two signals that most needed to cross, the bold change and the
hard truth, are the two it can damp toward nothing.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast
  the middle as obstructive or self-serving, because the source finding is precisely that the
  middle's buffering is valuable: it keeps the company running while it changes, and shields
  both ends from each other's thrash. The misfit is that the buffering which protects delivery
  is the buffering which dampens change.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a brave manager breaking through. The
  top's answer is a reorg, which dissolves this middle and hands the next one the same continuity
  trap, so the reckoning relocates the layer rather than thawing it.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed
  here, never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Mandate](piece_the_mandate.md)**: carries the deliberate strategy set at the top (Floyd &
  Wooldridge), the change at full strength before the middle receives it.
- **[The Continuity Trap](piece_the_continuity_trap.md)**: carries the continuity-and-change tension
  (Huy), the middle measured on the quarter the change disturbs.
- **[The Dilution](piece_the_dilution.md)**: carries sensemaking (Balogun & Johnson), the change
  reinterpreted and softened a notch at each downward hand-off.
- **[The Husk](piece_the_husk.md)**: carries the implementation gap, the change reaching the frontline
  hollow, its disruption and its point filed off upstream.
- **[The Smoothing](piece_the_smoothing.md)**: carries the upward synthesising role turned reassuring
  (Floyd & Wooldridge), the frontline's alarm rounded off level by level.
- **[The On-Track Report](piece_the_on_track_report.md)**: carries the top's belief the change landed,
  the smoothing arriving as confident success.
- **[The Dampening](piece_the_dampening.md)**: carries the buffer as a two-way low-pass layer, the one
  reflex that attenuates both the bold change and the hard truth.
- **[The Frozen Middle](piece_the_frozen_middle.md)**: carries active inertia (Sull) and the knot that
  buffers instead of joining (Nonaka), the layer where change dies, held whole by nobody.
