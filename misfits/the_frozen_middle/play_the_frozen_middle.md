# Play: The Frozen Middle

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Frozen Middle.
A bold change announced at the top with real conviction, and a frontline that never feels it, because between them sits a layer whose whole job is to keep the machine running ... and a running machine is one that does not change.

## Arc

The bend is this: the middle does exactly its job, and the change dies.
It runs on two signals that both pass through the same layer, and the layer damps them both.
Downward, the change: bold at the top, softened by the first manager to fit this quarter, softened again by the next to protect delivery, until what reaches the floor is a husk, the disruption stripped out and the point with it.
Upward, the reality: alarming on the floor, smoothed by the first manager into a manageable issue, smoothed again into on track with actions, until what reaches the top is a report that the change is landing well.
The middle is not obstructing. It is buffering, which is what it is for: it shields the frontline from the top's thrash and shields the top from the frontline's panic, and it is measured on the one thing change most threatens, this quarter's delivery. So it protects delivery, sincerely, and protecting delivery means damping the change going down and the truth coming up.
Measure the middle on the change instead of the quarter and the layer thaws: the middle is the story.

## Triggers

**[Plan](plot_plan.md)**
At the off-site the executive sets a bold change, and the evangelist takes it up with conviction and no line authority. The director begins to translate it into something that will not threaten the quarter. Closes when the bold change has become a safe plan.

**[Do](plot_do.md)**
Cue: the change cascades down. Each layer softens it to protect delivery, the manager buffers the disruption, and what reaches the floor is a husk. Closes when the frontline receives the change with its point removed, and keeps doing today's job.

**[Check](plot_check.md)**
Cue: the reality climbs back up. Each layer smooths the floor's alarm into a manageable issue, the roll-up aggregates the good news, and the top hears the change is landing. Closes when the transformation is declared embedded and nothing has moved.

**[The Reorg](plot_the_reorg.md)**
Cue: the change plainly did not land, or a new executive arrives. The answer is to bypass the middle with a restructure. Closes when the frozen middle is named ... or the reorg dissolves this middle and grows the next one in its place.

## Stakes

Whether a change set at the top is ever felt at the bottom.
Every plot moves it the same direction: not toward the frontline, toward the quarter. The production cannot make the middle stop buffering, because buffering is what keeps the company running while it changes. It can only show that the layer which protects delivery is the same layer that dampens change, that no seat froze the middle on purpose, and that the one signal it was built to pass, cleanly, in both directions, is the one signal it cannot.
