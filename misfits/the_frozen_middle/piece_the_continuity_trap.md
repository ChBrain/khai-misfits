# Piece: The Continuity Trap

## Taxonomy

[The Frozen Middle](play_the_frozen_middle.md). The double-bind: measured on this quarter, asked to change how the quarter is made.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the operating review](place_the_operating_review.md), where the middle answers for the numbers.
The same manager praised on Friday for embracing the change is held on Monday to the delivery the change disrupts.

## Load Bearing

Without it the middle could back the change freely and the play never starts.
With it the middle is caught: the Continuity Trap is the structural conflict that makes damping rational, because a manager is rewarded on this period's output and asked, in the same breath, to disturb the very process that produces it. Told to change and to deliver, and measured only on delivery, it does the measured thing. The trap is not that the middle prefers the old way, it is that the old way is what its scorecard is built from, and the change is a threat to the score before it is a benefit to anything.

## Apparent

Discipline, and accountability.
It looks like a well-run organisation holding its managers to their commitments. Holding people to delivery is right. It just cannot be done at the same time as asking them to break delivery open, and the organisation asks for both and pays for one.

## Yearbook

Sprung on [the buffer](persona_the_buffer.md), who is told to transform and to hit the number, and can only hit the number.
It is the engine under the whole play: every dampening downstream is a rational response to this one contradiction, so the middle does not freeze from fear or laziness, it freezes because the incentive to keep delivering and the incentive to change point opposite ways, and only one is on the scorecard.
