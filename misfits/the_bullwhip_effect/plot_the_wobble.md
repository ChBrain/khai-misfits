# Plot: The Wobble

## Taxonomy

[The Bullwhip Effect](play_the_bullwhip_effect.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The Counter](place_the_counter.md), a few more shoppers than usual buying the thing this week.
A small ordinary shift in demand at the till, drawing the shelf down a shade further than before.

## Action

[Ordering](process_ordering.md): [The Shopper](persona_the_shopper.md), holding [the shopper's seat](position_shopper.md), buys near as ever, while [The Retailer](persona_the_retailer.md), holding [the retailer's seat](position_retailer.md), sees its shelf run low and readies to reorder.

## Stage

[The Shopper](persona_the_shopper.md), having tabled [Buy as Ever](plan_buy_as_ever.md), takes a couple more than usual for reasons of its own and will be back to normal next week.
[The Retailer](persona_the_retailer.md), on [Cover the Order](plan_cover_the_order.md), reads the drawn-down shelf and prepares to refill it, the small true change now registered as a slightly larger order.

## Tension

The whole swing begins with a demand that barely moved.
There is nothing to fault at the till: the shopper simply bought a little more, once, and will buy its usual again. But this small honest wobble is the only real signal the chain will ever get, and it is visible here and nowhere above, so the plot closes with the true demand already fading from the counter and the chain about to answer, for weeks, a change that has all but passed.
