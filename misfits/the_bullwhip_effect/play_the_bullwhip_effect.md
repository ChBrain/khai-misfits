# Play: The Bullwhip Effect

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

The Bullwhip Effect.
A few more shoppers than usual buy the thing this week, a small, ordinary wobble in demand. The shop, not wanting to be caught short and needing to refill its shelf besides, orders a little extra from its supplier. The supplier sees that larger order, cannot tell a wobble from a trend, and orders more still to cover itself, and the distributor above does the same, and the factory above that ... and by the time the ripple reaches the top of the chain it is a wave: the factory swings from overtime to idle and back, glut then shortage, all from a wobble that, down at the till, had already passed. Flick a whip at the handle and the tip cracks: a small motion at one end, a wild one at the other, and every link only did the sensible thing.

## Arc

The bend is this: each link, reacting prudently to what it can see, makes the swing bigger for the link above, and no one is reacting to the demand that actually moved.
It runs on a signal that distorts as it climbs.
No link sees the shopper. Each sees only the orders of the link just below it, arriving late and stripped of the reason behind them, so it cannot tell a one-week wobble from the start of a trend. To be safe it must cover two things at once: the rise it just saw, and a cushion against the next, the safety stock and the pipeline it takes to refill. So it orders not the wobble but the wobble plus its cover, and the order it sends up is therefore larger than the demand it received.
And that larger order is the only demand the next link ever sees. It reads its neighbour's caution as the market talking, adds its own cover on top, and passes something larger still upward, so the distortion does not fade as it climbs, it compounds: a small swing at the shopper becomes a big one at the shop, a bigger one at the distributor, a wild one at the factory. The lag makes it worse, because each link acts on old information and its correction lands after the wobble has already reversed, so it is forever chasing the last swing into the next, overshooting up and then overshooting down, like reaching for the tap in a shower that answers a beat too late.
None of it is error or greed. Keeping your shelves stocked is the job, covering against a stockout is prudent, and reading the only signal you have, your customer's orders, is all any link can do. No one games the chain and no one panics: each link optimises exactly what is in front of it, and the swing is the sum of that local good sense seen from no single vantage.
Let the links share the one thing they lack, the shopper's true demand, so every stage reacts to the wobble itself and not to the stage below, shorten the lags, smooth the orders, and the whip can be stilled: but sharing means independent firms opening their books to one another, trusting a rival's data over their own shelves, giving up the cover that felt like prudence, and holding to it when the next wobble tempts each back to protecting only itself, so the calm is real and costs the very local caution that felt like the responsible thing.

## Triggers

**[The Wobble](plot_the_wobble.md)**
A few more shoppers than usual buy the thing, a small ordinary shift in demand at the till. The Shopper buys near as ever, and the Retailer sees its shelf drawn down a little further than the week before. Closes when the small real change in demand has registered as an order.

**[The Cover](plot_the_cover.md)**
Cue: the retailer must refill the shelf and guard against being caught short. It orders not the wobble but the wobble plus a cushion, safety stock and the pipeline to refill, so the Signal it sends up is larger than the demand it saw. Closes when the order passed upward is bigger than the wobble that prompted it.

**[The Amplifying](plot_the_amplifying.md)**
Cue: the larger order is the only demand the next link sees. The Signal climbs, each link reading its neighbour's cover as the market and adding its own on top, so the swing grows at every stage. Closes when the distortion has compounded up the chain into a wild order at the top.

**[The Whip](plot_the_whip.md)**
Cue: the amplified order reaches the factory. It ramps to overtime then cuts to idle, glut then shortage, chasing on a lag a swing that down at the till had already passed. The Factory whipsaws while the Shopper barely moved. Closes when the top of the chain is swinging wildly on a wobble long since gone.

**[The Sharing](plot_the_sharing.md)**
Cue: someone moves to let every link see the shopper, not just the stage below. The Sharer would open the books, shorten the lags, smooth the orders, against the reluctance of rivals to share and the pull of local cover. Closes when the true demand is shared and the whip stills ... or each link goes back to covering itself and the swing returns.

## Stakes

Whether a chain can ever react to the demand that moved instead of to itself.
Every plot but the last moves the same way: toward the cover, which each link is right to add and all are whipsawed by adding, and away from the shared signal, which would still the swing and which every link's local prudence argues against. The production cannot make a link see past its own customer's orders, because the shopper's demand is visible only at the till and each link above reads the stage below as the market, so local good sense is all any link has and the sum of it is the wave. It can only show that no one blundered or gamed, that stocking the shelf and covering the stockout and trusting your one signal are each the responsible move, and that the one thing that stills the whip, every stage sharing the true demand and giving up its private cover, must be built across firms that would rather guard their own shelves, so the swing is made of prudence and the calm is the harder thing to hold.
