# Process: Ordering

## Taxonomy

[The Bullwhip Effect](play_the_bullwhip_effect.md). A link placing its order to cover the demand it saw plus a buffer, the order swelling past its cause.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Initiated by

A shelf drawn down and a link determined not to be caught short.
At [the counter](place_the_counter.md) the shopper's small wobble registers, and at [the line](place_the_line.md) the retailer moves to refill and to guard against the next rise.

## Direction

Toward an order larger than the demand that prompted it.
Each link orders the rise it saw and a cushion besides, safety stock and pipeline, and because it cannot tell a wobble from a trend, ordering drives the number above the demand, so what leaves each stage is the wobble swollen by that stage's own caution.

## Lever

The real cost of a stockout against the hidden cost of the cover.
Because being caught short loses the sale and a full shelf does not, each link is rewarded for covering and never charged for the distortion it adds, so ordering runs on plain prudence, the buffer sensible at every stage and invisible as the thing that feeds the swing.

## Echo

An order sent up, larger than the demand beneath it.
The shelf is safe and the cover is added, and that swollen order is now the only demand the next link will see, so what reads at one stage as careful replenishment is, unseen, the signal that will be mistaken for the market above.
