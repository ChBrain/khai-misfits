# Piece: The Divide

## Taxonomy

[The Common Object](play_the_common_object.md). The phenomenon whole, one object and many meanings across a boundary, coordination believed and not real, a shared thing that joined the groups on its face and split them on its sense.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

Grown across every [home ground](place_the_home_ground.md), where each community reads the one object its own way.
Undone, if ever, at [the crosswalk](place_the_crosswalk.md), where the several meanings are walked across the boundary and found never to have been one.

## Load Bearing

Without it the several readings are just specialised uses of a shared tool, and no one is misled about sharing a meaning.
With it the trap is complete and authorless: one object read correctly and differently by each community, the coordination genuine on the object and never on the sense, so the groups are joined by the artefact and divided by the meaning at once, and believe themselves joined in both (Star and Griesemer, on coordination without consensus, the boundary object that lets social worlds cooperate precisely by meaning different things in each; and the failure mode when the object substitutes for the conversation and the meanings are never reconciled). The Divide is load bearing because it is the phenomenon whole: not one misread artefact but a shared object whose plasticity coordinates the groups and hides that each reads it its own way, so the piece carries the coordination everyone believes and the divergence no one sees, the joined work the communities were sure they were doing and the one thing they were not.

## Apparent

Coordination, plainly: several communities working from one shared object, joined by the common thing, the collaboration moving as one.
Seen at the surface, the Divide looks like exactly what coordination looks like, a shared artefact doing its work, and the truth that the one object carries a different meaning in every community, that the coordination is on the object and the divergence on the sense, that the groups are joined and split by the same shared face, stays hidden behind how real a working collaboration feels, right up until the seam.
