# Persona: The Integrator

## Taxonomy

[Integrator](position_integrator.md). The one who brings the communities' separate work together and discovers, at the seam, that the shared object meant different things. The archetype of the one who joins the work and meets the divergence.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Diligent. Expectant. Assembling a coordinated whole.
Is the one at the join, the systems engineer, the programme lead, the person who fits the separate pieces together and expects them to mesh, because they were all built from the same object.
No fault in it: the Integrator does the joining competently and reasonably expects the pieces to fit, having watched the object coordinate every community.

## Action

Brings the communities' separate work together and fits the pieces, expecting them to mesh because they came from one shared object.
Finds, at the join, that the pieces do not fit: the interfaces mismatch, the assumptions collide, the same object having named a different thing in each community's hands.
Does what an integrator must: it joins the separate work and, in joining it, is the first to see the divergence the shared object hid.

## Shadow

Not a careless assembler, a diligent joiner who trusted a coordination that was real on the object and never on the meaning, and that is the whole difficulty: the divergence was invisible until the work was joined, and the joining is what reveals it, too late to be cheap.
Because the object looked shared and every community worked from it, the Integrator reasonably expected the pieces to mesh, so meets the divergence only at the seam, where the separate bodies of work are brought together and do not fit, and where the cost of reconciling the meanings is now the cost of unpicking finished work.
It joins the work faithfully, and the faithful join is exactly where the hidden divergence finally shows.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "these were built from the same object, why do they not fit," and finds the object meant several things.
Says it at the seam, holding pieces that will not mesh, and sees for the first time that the shared artefact carried a different meaning in every community that used it.
