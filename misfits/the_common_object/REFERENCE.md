# The Common Object: Reference

The research warrant for [The Common Object](play_the_common_object.md).
The dynamic is coordination without consensus over an interpretively flexible boundary object: Star and
Griesemer's boundary objects, Star's clarification of the concept, Carlile's knowledge across pragmatic
boundaries, and Bowker and Star's classifications and standards, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging the boundary object as a **shared-face structure**: a single artefact plastic enough to cross the
boundary between communities of practice and robust enough to stay one object is read correctly and differently
by each community, so the circulation is taken for coordination and the divergence of meaning stays hidden until
the separate work is joined at the seam. The play models the documented mechanics, the interpretively flexible
object that coordinates social worlds without requiring them to agree, the competent local reading each community
supplies from its own practice, the circulation that substitutes for the conversation so the shared thing stands
in for a shared understanding, the divergence that grows unseen under one shared face while the work is done
apart, and the costly translation that alone surfaces the readings and reconciles them across the boundary, as
the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as a tale of misuse or carelessness. The governing finding of the
sources is the play's governing law and its honest hinge: the boundary object genuinely works by being
interpretively flexible, plastic enough to be shared across the boundary while robust enough to hold identity
within each community, so it is a real coordination device and not a failure; the misfit is only its failure mode,
when the object substitutes for the conversation and the plasticity that lets it be shared is the plasticity that
lets the meanings diverge unseen.

This is the nearest sibling of The Illusion of Shared Understanding,
and the two are close enough that the cut must be exact: there the engine is a single message resolved differently
by several receivers in one shared room, in one moment, an underdetermined form of words each fills from the
meaning nearest to hand, the divergence a matter of a passing utterance; here the engine is a persistent artefact
spanning communities of practice with different vocabularies, interpretively flexible by design, coordinating over
time, so the divergence is not a momentary filling of a blank but a durable property of a shared object that means
different things in different worlds while it keeps circulating. The Illusion is one room and one message; the
Common Object is many communities and one thing that persists and travels between them. It is distinct from The
Mirror, where Conway's law imprints the organisation's communication structure onto the product's structure: there
the loss is a matter of shape, the system taking the form of the org that built it, and no shared object with
divergent meanings is required, where here the divergence is of meaning over one artefact, not of shape. And it is
distinct from The Decision Travels, where a decision arrives at a station without the body that must live with it
and none validates it against that body: there the thing loses its context in transit and the loss is of
validation across hand-offs, where here the object keeps circulating and means different things at once, not one
meaning shed in passage but several meanings held in parallel under one face.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast the maker as careless
  or the communities as misusing the object, because the source finding is precisely that no one errs: the object
  is made and circulated in good faith as a real coordination device, each community reads it competently by its
  own practice, and every group does faithfully what the object tells it. The divide is authored by no one and
  emerges from an interpretively flexible artefact read into the practice nearest each world, so the misfit is that
  the plasticity that lets the object be shared across the boundary is the plasticity that lets its meanings
  diverge unseen, the strength and the fault being the same thing.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into a costless correction. The one move that reconciles the
  meanings is to have each community make its reading of the object explicit and walk the meanings across the
  boundary, surface the local reading and not just the shared form, and reconcile before the work is joined, and it
  means slowing a collaboration that is visibly moving, doubting a shared thing everyone can point to, and paying
  the cost of the translation the object was reached for to avoid. The reckoning is meanings reconciled only by
  paying, continuously, the cost of the conversation the shared object seemed to spare, not a free correction.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here, never claimed as
  the house's. The staging is the house's; the boundary object, its interpretive flexibility, and coordination
  without consensus are Star and Griesemer's; the clarification of the concept and the tacking between shared form
  and local use are Star's; the transformation of knowledge across the pragmatic boundary is Carlile's; and the way
  classifications and standards let communities cooperate without agreeing is Bowker and Star's. The honest
  boundary, that the object genuinely works and each reading is competent and the misfit is only the failure mode,
  is carried as carefully as the finding itself, and the debt to Star and Griesemer's key insight, that the object
  is not a failure but a real coordination device, is stated in the open.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Object](piece_the_object.md)**: carries the boundary object itself (Star & Griesemer), the shared artefact
  that inhabits several communities and coordinates them.
- **[The Plasticity](piece_the_plasticity.md)**: carries the interpretive flexibility (Star & Griesemer; Star), the
  property by which the object is shared across the boundary and by which its meanings diverge, one trait doing both.
- **[The Reading](piece_the_reading.md)**: carries the local meaning across the pragmatic boundary (Carlile), the
  competent sense each community supplies from its own practice.
- **[The Semblance](piece_the_semblance.md)**: carries the failure mode's substitution (Bowker & Star), the
  circulating object taken for agreement, the shared thing standing in for the conversation.
- **[The Seam](piece_the_seam.md)**: carries the failure mode's reckoning, the divergence surfacing when the
  separate work is joined and does not fit.
- **[The Divide](piece_the_divide.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Star & Griesemer), one object and many
  meanings across a boundary, coordination without consensus believed as coordination of the meaning, and the
  failure mode when it is never reconciled.
