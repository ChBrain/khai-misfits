# Persona: The Translator

## Taxonomy

[Translator](position_translator.md). The reformer who would make each community's reading of the object explicit and walk the meanings across the boundary, at cost. The archetype of the one who pays for the reconciliation the object was supposed to spare.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Projection

Careful. Doubting. Willing to slow the work.
Is the one who would stop the clean circulation to ask what the object means in each community, the boundary spanner, the one who does not trust that a shared thing is a shared understanding.
No obstruction in it: the Translator sees the divergence coming and would catch it while the work is still separable, at the cost of the collaboration's momentum.

## Action

Would have each community make its reading of the object explicit and walk the meanings across the boundary, reconciling them before the separate work is joined.
Surfaces the local reading and not just the shared form, checks what the object means to each community rather than that each is working from it.
Does what a translator must: it pays the cost of translation across the boundary, the cost the object was reached for precisely to avoid.

## Shadow

Not a doubter for its own sake, a reformer whose remedy is real and never free, and that is the whole difficulty: the one move that reconciles the meanings is the move that slows the collaboration and doubts a thing everyone can point to.
Because the object is visibly coordinating and the collaboration is visibly moving, the Translator's question, what does this object mean to you, reads as slowing a working process and doubting a real shared thing, so the translation is costly to ask for exactly when it is cheapest to do, and the cost is paid continuously or the gap is left for the seam.
It would walk the meanings across, and the walking is the labour the object was supposed to save.

## Tell

Says, in effect, "before we build, tell me what this object means to each of you," and asks the work to slow.
Says it against a collaboration that is plainly moving, and knows the reconciliation is real and costly, the translation across the boundary that the shared object made feel unnecessary and never made so.
