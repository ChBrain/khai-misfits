# Piece: The Hoard

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The good work withheld from a system that will not pay for it.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the bench](place_the_bench.md), in the care kept back rather than spent for nothing.
The withdrawal of quality: the best effort saved for the side project, the client, the employer, the market that weighs it; the careful hand giving the gate only what the gate rewards and keeping the rest. Good coin, pulled from this circulation and held, or melted down and spent elsewhere.

## Load Bearing

Without it the good is spent anyway and the standard holds despite the bar.
With it the good leaves circulation, because the Hoard is Gresham's other half: since the dear coin earns no more than the cheap one here, the rational maker stops paying its price here and keeps its worth for where it is paid. The care does not vanish from the world, only from this shop, and that is the mechanism, a slow drain of quality out of a place that treats it as worthless, into places that do not. What is left behind is exactly the coin no one bothered to hoard.

## Apparent

Disengagement, or a worker coasting.
It looks like someone who has stopped trying: doing the minimum, phoning it in, saving their real energy for outside. Read from the gate, the hoarding maker is indistinguishable from a lazy one, when in truth they are a careful one responding correctly to a price of zero on their care, spending exactly what is bought and no more.

## Yearbook

Practised by [the craftsman](persona_the_craftsman.md) through [hoarding](process_hoarding.md), and reversed only when [grading](process_grading.md) makes care worth spending again.
It is how the good goes: the Hoard removes [the good coin](piece_the_good_coin.md) from circulation, leaving [the bad coin](piece_the_bad_coin.md) as all that changes hands, and every coin hoarded lowers the average of what remains.
