# Piece: The Good Coin

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The careful work, dear to make, worth more, and paid no more.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the bench](place_the_bench.md), in the piece that took the extra hour and shows it.
The full-weight work: the code written to last, the report checked twice, the joint cut true, the thing made properly. Real quality, plainly present to anyone who weighs it, and stamped at the gate as no better than the quick.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is only one grade of work and nothing to drive out.
With it there is a dearer coin to withdraw, because the Good Coin carries value the Face Value will not pay for: it costs more to mint and clears the same bar, so every time it is made and spent, its extra worth is given away for free. That surplus, unrewarded, is exactly what makes the Good Coin worth hoarding rather than spending, and its slow disappearance from circulation is the whole event the play stages.

## Apparent

A waste, or a luxury no one asked for.
It looks like gold-plating: effort beyond the spec, care the bar never required, an hour spent where a minute would have passed. Read against a gate that grades pass or fail, the Good Coin's surplus is indistinguishable from excess, so the very quality that makes it worth more makes it look like a maker who does not know when to stop.

## Yearbook

Made and then held back by [the craftsman](persona_the_craftsman.md), and priced at last only by [the assayer](persona_the_assayer.md).
It is the dear side of the split: set against [the bad coin](piece_the_bad_coin.md) at equal [face value](piece_the_face_value.md), the Good Coin is the one a rational maker keeps, so it becomes [the hoard](piece_the_hoard.md) and leaves the shoddy to circulate.
