# Piece: The Face Value

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The equal worth the bar assigns good and shoddy alike.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the gate](place_the_gate.md), stamped on every piece that clears it, the same for all.
The common value the acceptance rule confers: passed is passed, done is done, on time is on time. A pass/fail bar, a deadline, a checklist that says yes or no and never how well, so a careful piece and a quick one leave the gate worth exactly the same.

## Load Bearing

Without it good and shoddy trade at their true worth and the good is never driven out.
With it they trade as equals, because the Face Value is Gresham's condition: an authority fixing one worth on two unlike things. The bar grades pass or fail and refuses to grade quality, so the extra a careful piece carries is legal tender for nothing, and any maker comparing the two sees one price and two costs. It is the forced parity at the root of the whole misfit, the rule that makes exceeding the bar a gift the bar will not receive.

## Apparent

Fairness, and a clean standard.
It looks like the sensible thing: a clear bar, applied evenly, no favouritism, everyone held to the same line. And evenness is a virtue, which is the trap, because a bar that treats all passing work as equal is fair in the one way that guarantees the careful will not be paid for their care and the shoddy will not be docked for its shoddiness.

## Yearbook

Set by [the mint](persona_the_mint.md) through [acceptance](process_acceptance.md), and undone only when [the assayer](persona_the_assayer.md) puts a price back on quality.
It is the ground the play stands on: because the Face Value is equal, [the bad coin](piece_the_bad_coin.md) is cheaper to make at the same worth, so it circulates and [the good coin](piece_the_good_coin.md) is hoarded.
