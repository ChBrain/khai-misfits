# Play: Bad Coin

## Estate

[khai-misfits](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-misfits): the house that holds this production and answers for the whole run.

## Name

Bad Coin.
Two pieces of work pass the same bar and count the same: the careful one that cost a day and the quick one that cost an hour ... so the quick one is spent and the careful one is kept back, and coin by coin the careful is withdrawn from circulation until all that changes hands is the cheap stuff, stamped exactly as good as the rest.

## Arc

The bend is this: treat good and shoddy as equal, and the shoddy will drive the good out.
It runs on a common face value and an uncommon cost.
The bar accepts both: careful work and quick work clear the same gate, pass the same review, hit the same deadline, and are booked as the same done. Their face value is equal.
Their cost is not. The careful one takes longer and asks more, and earns no more for it, so producing it is a pure loss against producing the quick one, and a rational maker spends the cheap coin and hoards the dear: ships the quick version here, and saves the care for somewhere it is paid.
Then the good makers, watching the quick win the race on equal terms, convert, because holding to care is now a handicap. The standard sinks to the cheapest thing that still clears the bar, and the body of work fills with coin that passed and will not hold.
Grade the work, pay a premium for care, and the good returns to circulation: what you accept as equal is what you will be left holding.

## Triggers

**[Face Value](plot_face_value.md)**
The bar is set to pass or fail, not to grade: careful work and quick work clear the same gate and count the same done. The Mint stamps both alike. Closes when good and shoddy carry the same face value, at unequal cost to make.

**[Spend Bad](plot_spend_bad.md)**
Cue: the two coins are worth the same to spend and cost differently to mint. The Spender ships the quick version and passes, while the Craftsman does it right, earns nothing extra, and keeps his best back. Closes when the cheap coin circulates and the dear one is hoarded.

**[Match Pace](plot_match_pace.md)**
Cue: on equal terms, quick beats careful, and the Foreman rewards the pace. The Convert, watching care lose the race, drops to the quick to keep up. Closes when holding to care is a handicap and the good makers convert.

**[Debased Corpus](plot_debased_corpus.md)**
Cue: the body of work is now all coin that passed and will not hold. The Heir opens the corpus and finds it shoddy through, the standard sunk to the cheapest thing that clears the bar. Closes when the debasement is named as the cost of accepting at par ... or the bar is lowered again to match.

**[Assay](plot_assay.md)**
Cue: someone grades the work instead of passing it, and pays a premium for care. The Assayer restores a price on quality, and the good coin returns to circulation. Closes when care is paid for and worth making again ... or grading is judged too slow and dear, and the mint stamps all alike once more.

## Stakes

Whether care is ever paid for, or only ever accepted.
Every plot moves the same way: toward the cheap coin, which clears the bar for less, and away from the dear one, which clears it for more and is thanked no better. The production cannot make a maker give good work for a bad price forever. It can only show that no one was lazy and no one cheated, that every coin passed honestly, and that the one thing that would have kept the good in circulation, a price on the quality the bar refused to grade, was the one thing the bar was built to ignore.
