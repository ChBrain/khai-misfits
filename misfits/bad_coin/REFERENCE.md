# Bad Coin: Reference

The research warrant for [Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The title names the mechanism;
the play runs on Gresham's Law and the monetary economics of debasement, from its classical
statement through the modern account of the condition it needs, all cited below.

## Line of Work

Staging Gresham's Law as a **systemic system**: when unequal quality is accepted at equal value, the
cheaper drives the dearer out of circulation, so a standard debases not through fraud but through the
simple refusal to pay for a difference it can see. The play models the documented mechanics, the
common face value the bar confers, the good work dear to make and the bad cheap, the good hoarded and
the bad spent, the race that turns care into a handicap, the careful converting to compete, and the
grading that alone restores a price on quality, as the load-bearing vectors of the structure, not as
a tale of laziness. The governing finding of the source literature is the play's governing law: bad
money drives out good precisely when an authority fixes one value on two unlike things, so the coin
worth more than its stamp is withdrawn and the coin worth exactly its stamp is all that changes hands.

| **Rolnick & Weber**   | "Gresham's Law or Gresham's Fallacy?", _Journal of Political Economy_ (1986)                                                                              | **The condition**: the law operates only where an authority forces a fixed equal value on unequal monies; let quality be priced freely and it reverses. ## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and how it credits what it stages.

- **No villains**: the elements are reduced to load-bearing vectors. The play refuses to cast any seat
  as a cheat or a slacker, because the source finding is precisely that neither is needed: the mint's
  even bar is fair, the spender wastes nothing the standard asked, the craftsman is exact rather than
  lazy, and every coin passes honestly. The misfit is that accepting unequal work at equal value prices
  care at zero, so the careful stop spending it here.
- **No hero**: the play refuses to resolve the misfit into the standard simply recovering. Grading can
  bring the good coin back, but only by giving up the crisp, even, comfortable throughput that passing
  at par supplied, paying for a judge who can tell degrees and a shop willing to reward them, so the
  reckoning is speed and ease traded for a price on quality, not a standard that healed on its own.
- **Credit, not claim**: the research above is other people's work, packaged and attributed here,
  never claimed as the house's. The staging is the house's; the findings are theirs, and the condition
  Gresham's Law actually requires is carried as carefully as the law itself.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per element.

- **[The Face Value](piece_the_face_value.md)**: carries the forced equal value (Gresham / Macleod; Rolnick & Weber),
  the bar that grades pass or fail and prices quality at zero.
- **[The Good Coin](piece_the_good_coin.md)**: carries the full-weight piece (Gresham / Macleod), dear to make, worth
  more than its stamp, and paid no more.
- **[The Bad Coin](piece_the_bad_coin.md)**: carries the debased piece (Gresham / Macleod), cheap to make, worth its
  stamp exactly, and accepted the same.
- **[The Hoard](piece_the_hoard.md)**: carries the withdrawal of the good (Jevons), hoarded, melted, or spent where it
  is weighed, drained from a place that will not pay for it.
- **[The Race](piece_the_race.md)**: carries the competition under equal acceptance (practitioner), where the only
  visible axis left is speed and care becomes a handicap.
- **[The Debasement](piece_the_debasement.md)**: carries the sinking standard (Jevons), the good driving itself out as
  the careful convert to compete.
- **[The Debased Corpus](piece_the_debased_corpus.md)**: carries the accumulated stock of passed shoddiness (practitioner),
  brittle in sum and authorless, the bill for equal acceptance.
- **[The Gresham](piece_the_gresham.md)**: carries the phenomenon whole (Gresham / Macleod; Rolnick & Weber), and its
  remedy in grading (practitioner), a price on the quality the bar refused to see.
