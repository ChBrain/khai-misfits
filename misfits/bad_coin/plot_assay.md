# Plot: Assay

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The assay](place_the_assay.md), and someone who has stopped passing the work and started weighing it.
The question has changed: not did it clear the bar, but how good is it, and what is that worth, and how do we pay for the difference the gate refused to see.

## Action

[Grading](process_grading.md): [The Assayer](persona_the_assayer.md), holding [the grader's seat](position_grader.md), tests the work by degree instead of pass or fail, and puts a price back on the quality above the bar.

## Stage

[The Assayer](persona_the_assayer.md) tables [Grade and Pay](plan_grade_and_pay.md): reward care over the bar rather than accept it at par, so [the good coin](piece_the_good_coin.md) earns its surplus and becomes worth spending here again, and the hoard comes back into circulation.
With a price on quality, making the dear coin is no longer a pure loss, so [the gresham](piece_the_gresham.md) loses its grip and the standard can rise instead of only sink.

## Tension

The good returns, and the ease that debased it is gone.
Grading works, and it costs: weighing quality is slower than stamping pass or fail, it demands a judge who can tell degrees and a shop willing to pay for them, and it gives up the crisp throughput that passing at par supplied. It trades a fast, even, comfortable bar for a slow, discriminating, contestable one that must keep deciding what care is worth ... or grading is judged too dear and too subjective, the mint goes back to stamping all alike, and the good coin leaves again.
