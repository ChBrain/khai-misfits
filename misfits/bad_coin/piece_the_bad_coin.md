# Piece: The Bad Coin

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The quick work, cheap to make, worth less, and accepted the same.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the floor](place_the_floor.md), in the piece knocked out fast and pushed through the gate.
The light-weight work: the code that compiles and ships, the report that fills the template, the corner cut where no one checks, the thing done just well enough to pass. Real enough to clear the bar, and no more than that, by design.

## Load Bearing

Without it there is nothing cheaper to spend and the good is never displaced.
With it there is a coin that costs less and counts the same, because the Bad Coin is built precisely to the bar and not a grain above: it clears the gate at the lowest cost that clears it, so at equal face value it is the rational thing to make and to spend. It is not counterfeit, it passes honestly, and that is what makes it drive out the good rather than be caught, because a coin that really does clear the bar is a coin the bar has no grounds to refuse.

## Apparent

Efficiency. Exactly enough, and no waste.
It looks like discipline, even virtue: no gold-plating, no over-engineering, just the job done to spec and out the door. Against a gate that asks only pass or fail, meeting the bar exactly is the smart play, so the Bad Coin wears the face of the lean, sensible worker who does not waste a minute the standard did not demand.

## Yearbook

Spent by [the spender](persona_the_spender.md) through [circulation](process_circulation.md), and matched, in time, by every convert.
It is the cheap side of the split: at equal [face value](piece_the_face_value.md) with [the good coin](piece_the_good_coin.md), the Bad Coin is what gets spent, and coin by coin it becomes [the debased corpus](piece_the_debased_corpus.md) that is all that is left circulating.
