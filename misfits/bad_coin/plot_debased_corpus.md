# Plot: Debased Corpus

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Cue

[The corpus](place_the_corpus.md), the whole accumulated body of work, and someone finally asked to make it bear weight.
Every piece passed on its own and looked fine on its own, and now they are stacked into a mass that has to hold, and does not.

## Action

[Reckoning](process_reckoning.md): [The Heir](persona_the_heir.md), holding [the holder's seat](position_holder.md), opens the corpus and finds it shoddy through, brittle everywhere, expensive to touch, the standard sunk to the cheapest thing that ever cleared the bar.

## Stage

[The Heir](persona_the_heir.md) tables [Open the Corpus](plan_open_the_corpus.md): take true stock of the body of work, and find [the debased corpus](piece_the_debased_corpus.md), a stock of coin that all passed and will not hold, with no single piece to blame.
[The gresham](piece_the_gresham.md) is named at last: not a rot that crept in, not a lazy maker, but the exact result of a bar that accepted unequal work at equal worth.

## Tension

Every coin passed honestly, and together they will not carry the load.
There is no cheat to find and no slacker to sack, because each piece really did clear the gate and really did look fine alone: the ruin is in the sum, distributed across years and makers who each merely met the bar, so it is authorless, which is exactly why it is so hard to fix and so easy to repeat. The bill for a whole run of equal acceptance lands in one lump on whoever holds the work ... or the standard is quietly lowered again to match what the corpus can now do.
