# Piece: The Debased Corpus

## Taxonomy

[Bad Coin](play_bad_coin.md). The body of work left when only the cheap coin circulates.

## Owner

- Project: khai-misfits

## Place

At [the corpus](place_the_corpus.md), in the whole accumulated stock of what was passed.
The sum of the shortcuts: the codebase of quick fixes that now cannot be changed safely, the archive of templated reports no one trusts, the product built to spec and to nothing more. Everything that cleared the bar, and nothing that exceeded it, compounded into a mass.

## Load Bearing

Without it the debasement is an abstraction and costs nothing anyone can point to.
With it the cost is concrete and collective, because the Debased Corpus is where all the accepted shoddiness lives together: each Bad Coin passed on its own and looked fine on its own, but stacked into a body of work they make a thing that is brittle throughout, expensive to touch, and quietly failing, and no single coin can be blamed for it. It is the bill for the whole run of equal acceptance, presented as one lump, to whoever holds the work when it finally has to bear weight.

## Apparent

An ordinary inheritance, or a run of bad luck.
It looks like a codebase that just got messy, a function that just got sloppy, the normal decay of any old system, nothing that traces to a rule about how work was graded. The Debased Corpus hides its cause because its cause was distributed across years and makers, each of whom merely passed the bar, so the ruin looks authorless, which it is.

## Yearbook

Inherited by [the heir](persona_the_heir.md) through [reckoning](process_reckoning.md), after [the debasement](piece_the_debasement.md) has run its course.
It is the play's arrival at its cost: the Debased Corpus is [the bad coin](piece_the_bad_coin.md) summed over every cycle, the standing proof of what accepting at [face value](piece_the_face_value.md) leaves a shop holding.
