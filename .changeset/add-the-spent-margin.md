Add The Spent Margin misfit (risk compensation / the Peltzman effect). A safety measure that genuinely works hands the protected agent a felt margin; holding a roughly constant target of accepted risk, the agent reads the margin as slack and re-spends it as behaviour until the felt danger is back at target, so the engineered gain is consumed by the conduct the safety invites and part of the re-taken risk transfers onto the uncovered. Warranted on Peltzman (1975), Wilde's risk homeostasis, Adams's risk thermostat, and the Munich ABS study, with the partial-offset limit credited; cut explicitly against Moral Hazard (which needs a shifted cost) and Belt and Braces (where the device gains failure modes, not the operator the margin).
