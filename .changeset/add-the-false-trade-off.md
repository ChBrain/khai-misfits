Add The False Trade-Off misfit (Berkson's paradox / collider bias). Two qualities that are independent in the population both raise a unit's chance of clearing a selection gate, so the gate is a common effect of both, and conditioning on it, keeping only the units that passed, induces a spurious, usually negative association between the two: a unit low on one quality is in the sample only if it was high on the other. So among the admitted, beauty seems to cost wit and talent to spend diligence, a trade-off real in the sample and false in the world. No fraud and no fallacy: selecting on the sum is the fair way to take the best, reading a correlation off a clean sample is sound analysis, and acting on a peer-reviewed finding is what a decider should do, yet the acted-on trade-off spends the very compensations the gate had been admitting. Warranted on Berkson (1946), Pearl (Causality, the collider), Hernán, Hernández-Díaz & Robins (2004, selection bias as collider-stratification), Elwert & Winship (2014, endogenous selection), and Sackett (1979, the empirical footprint); cut explicitly against The Bigger Picture (Simpson's confounder, a common cause you must adjust for, the exact mirror of a collider you must never condition on) and The Survivors (attrition biasing one estimate, not a manufactured relationship between two). Fifth target of the warrant-mine slate.
