Add The Beauty Contest misfit (the Keynesian beauty contest / higher-order expectations). When the payoff turns on matching the crowd rather than on the thing itself, each rational player forecasts the others' forecasts in an unending regress, so price comes to track opinion about opinion and floats free of any value beneath it, held only by an anchor that arbitrage's limits can carry off before it is proved right. Warranted on Keynes (General Theory, 1936, ch. 12), Nagel's guessing-game experiments (1995), Allen, Morris & Shin's iterated-expectations asset model (2006), Shiller's feedback theory of bubbles (2000), and Shleifer & Vishny's limits of arbitrage (1997); cut explicitly against Information Cascade (sequential inference from actions, not simultaneous belief-about-belief) and The Stag Hunt (no cooperative prize and no fixed equilibrium, only the regress). First target of the season-slate refill.
