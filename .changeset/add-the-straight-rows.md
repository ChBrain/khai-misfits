Add The Straight Rows misfit (legibility and the loss of métis). A centre can act only on what it can see, so to govern a domain it reduces the tangle to a legible grid, a map, a table, a standard it can read from a distance, and because the grid records only what it can measure it clears away the tacit, local, practical knowledge, the métis, that actually ran the system and no survey could hold. The wild forest is replanted in countable rows for a legible yield; the first rotation is heavy and vindicates the method; the second comes in sick and the third dead, the cleared soil and web that fed the wood gone with the underbrush. No tyrant is needed: the planner means to improve the place, and a state must be able to read its domain to tax, plan, and feed it. Villain-less and remedy-costly: the cure, holding legibility below the point where it displaces the métis, runs against the very drive to govern that imposed the grid. Warranted on Scott (Seeing Like a State, 1998), Detienne & Vernant (the concept of métis, 1974/1978), Hayek (dispersed local knowledge, 1945), Jacobs (the living city, 1961), and Lowood (the scientific-forestry case, 1990); cut explicitly against The Abstraction Ladder (the territory actively remade, not a report thinning as it rises), Requisite Variety (the domain razed to fit the repertoire, not merely unmatched), and Metric Fixation (no number gamed; tacit knowledge cleared, not a measure corrupted). Fourth target of the warrant-mine slate.
