### Minor Changes

- 116dbfa: Add The Beauty Contest misfit (the Keynesian beauty contest / higher-order expectations). When the payoff turns on matching the crowd rather than on the thing itself, each rational player forecasts the others' forecasts in an unending regress, so price comes to track opinion about opinion and floats free of any value beneath it, held only by an anchor that arbitrage's limits can carry off before it is proved right. Warranted on Keynes (General Theory, 1936, ch. 12), Nagel's guessing-game experiments (1995), Allen, Morris & Shin's iterated-expectations asset model (2006), Shiller's feedback theory of bubbles (2000), and Shleifer & Vishny's limits of arbitrage (1997); cut explicitly against Information Cascade (sequential inference from actions, not simultaneous belief-about-belief) and The Stag Hunt (no cooperative prize and no fixed equilibrium, only the regress). First target of the season-slate refill.
- 2ad4ea4: Add The Dollar Auction misfit (Shubik's all-pay escalation). A prize of known worth is auctioned under one added rule: the runner-up, not only the winner, forfeits their last bid. So whoever is second faces losing their stake for nothing, and bidding just past the leader, to win the prize and escape the forfeit, is the smaller loss, a sound forward-looking choice at every price. Because there is always a one who is second, two reasonable bidders climb past the prize toward ruin: the winner overpays, the loser pays for nothing, and the auctioneer, who offered a transparent game and bid nothing, collects both stakes. No swindle and no fallacy: each raise is correct, and the all-pay rule leaves no floor beneath it, so being right at every step is exactly what ruins them, and the only winning move is not to enter. Warranted on Shubik (1971), Teger (Too Much Invested to Quit, 1980), O'Neill (1986, the game-theoretic solution), Leininger (1989), and Maynard Smith (1974, the war of attrition, the all-pay kin); cut explicitly against Good Money After Bad (the sunk-cost fallacy, backward-looking and by one actor, where this escalation is forward-looking and rational and needs two) and The Winning Bid (the winner's curse from a misjudged common value, where here the prize's value is known exactly and the overpayment is the rule's doing, not an estimation error). Sixth target of the warrant-mine slate.
- 775c71e: Add The False Trade-Off misfit (Berkson's paradox / collider bias). Two qualities that are independent in the population both raise a unit's chance of clearing a selection gate, so the gate is a common effect of both, and conditioning on it, keeping only the units that passed, induces a spurious, usually negative association between the two: a unit low on one quality is in the sample only if it was high on the other. So among the admitted, beauty seems to cost wit and talent to spend diligence, a trade-off real in the sample and false in the world. No fraud and no fallacy: selecting on the sum is the fair way to take the best, reading a correlation off a clean sample is sound analysis, and acting on a peer-reviewed finding is what a decider should do, yet the acted-on trade-off spends the very compensations the gate had been admitting. Warranted on Berkson (1946), Pearl (Causality, the collider), Hernán, Hernández-Díaz & Robins (2004, selection bias as collider-stratification), Elwert & Winship (2014, endogenous selection), and Sackett (1979, the empirical footprint); cut explicitly against The Bigger Picture (Simpson's confounder, a common cause you must adjust for, the exact mirror of a collider you must never condition on) and The Survivors (attrition biasing one estimate, not a manufactured relationship between two). Fifth target of the warrant-mine slate.
- 6c0e9de: Add The File Drawer misfit (publication bias / the file-drawer problem). A study is kept for the record not by how well it was done but by whether its result came out significant, so the significant enter the literature and the nulls file into the drawer, and the sample everyone reads and pools is filtered on the very outcome it is read to learn, overstating every effect it holds. No fraud is needed: the author writes up the finding where the citation is, the reviewer prefers the novel, and the editor spends a scarce page where it is read, and the sum of reasonable choices is a record surer than the science. Worse, the replication that is science's own self-correction is a null when it fails, drawered by the same filter, so the false positive stands. Villain-less and remedy-costly: the cure, pre-registration and publishing nulls, pays no author a citation and no editor a reader. Warranted on Sterling (1959), Rosenthal (1979, the file-drawer problem and the fail-safe N), Ioannidis (2005), Dickersin (1990, the medical evidence and the case for registration), and Simmons, Nelson & Simonsohn (2011, the p-hacking cousin, set apart); cut explicitly against The Survivors (an active filter on the result, not passive attrition, the missing evidence drawered rather than destroyed), Metric Fixation (no target set and gamed), and The Streetlight Effect (the bias is in which honest results the record kept, not where anyone searched). Third target of the warrant-mine slate.
- 1a8288d: Add The Proxy misfit (statistical discrimination). When an agent cannot cheaply see an individual's quality, it prices them by the average of their group, so the member of a low-rated group earns a discounted return on any costly signal, rationally invests less in it, and the group's real average falls to meet the rating, the assumed average becoming the produced average and the prior confirming itself, while the genuinely able member is priced by the group and cannot signal free of it because the signal is discounted too. Villain-less and remedy-costly: no bigot is needed, and subsidised entry can be discounted by the same rational updating into a patronising equilibrium. Warranted on Phelps (1972), Arrow (1973), Becker (1957, the taste-based contrast), Coate & Loury (1993), and Aigner & Cain (1977); cut explicitly against The Market for Lemons (self-fulfilling prior, not adverse selection driving the good out) and taste-based discrimination (no animus, no forgone profit). Second target of the season-slate refill.
- 9a356d1: Add The Spent Margin misfit (risk compensation / the Peltzman effect). A safety measure that genuinely works hands the protected agent a felt margin; holding a roughly constant target of accepted risk, the agent reads the margin as slack and re-spends it as behaviour until the felt danger is back at target, so the engineered gain is consumed by the conduct the safety invites and part of the re-taken risk transfers onto the uncovered. Warranted on Peltzman (1975), Wilde's risk homeostasis, Adams's risk thermostat, and the Munich ABS study, with the partial-offset limit credited; cut explicitly against Moral Hazard (which needs a shifted cost) and Belt and Braces (where the device gains failure modes, not the operator the margin).
- b489eae: Add The Straight Rows misfit (legibility and the loss of métis). A centre can act only on what it can see, so to govern a domain it reduces the tangle to a legible grid, a map, a table, a standard it can read from a distance, and because the grid records only what it can measure it clears away the tacit, local, practical knowledge, the métis, that actually ran the system and no survey could hold. The wild forest is replanted in countable rows for a legible yield; the first rotation is heavy and vindicates the method; the second comes in sick and the third dead, the cleared soil and web that fed the wood gone with the underbrush. No tyrant is needed: the planner means to improve the place, and a state must be able to read its domain to tax, plan, and feed it. Villain-less and remedy-costly: the cure, holding legibility below the point where it displaces the métis, runs against the very drive to govern that imposed the grid. Warranted on Scott (Seeing Like a State, 1998), Detienne & Vernant (the concept of métis, 1974/1978), Hayek (dispersed local knowledge, 1945), Jacobs (the living city, 1961), and Lowood (the scientific-forestry case, 1990); cut explicitly against The Abstraction Ladder (the territory actively remade, not a report thinning as it rises), Requisite Variety (the domain razed to fit the repertoire, not merely unmatched), and Metric Fixation (no number gamed; tacit knowledge cleared, not a measure corrupted). Fourth target of the warrant-mine slate.
