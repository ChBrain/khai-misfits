# Candidate Briefing: Impression Management

## Origin

A note on Substack asked: why does everyone here feel like they are
intellectually performing? The author wanted raw, messy emotion. They got
curated insight instead.

That observation is a symptom. The symptom has a structural name. The name
is not in the catalogue.

## The Gap

Goffman's dramaturgical theory (The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life,
1959) is the spine of the entire house. Every MISFIT that involves face,
candour, or group behaviour leans on it implicitly. But Goffman himself is
not the leading source for any staged misfit. His concept, impression
management, is not a warrant row in REFERENCES.md. No misfit in
`misfits/` is keyed to it.

The closest staged work:

- `saving_face` — warrants Argyris & Schon's Defensive Routines. Treats
  performance as a response to threat. Not the same as performance as the
  default condition of all interaction.
- `the_leadership_script` — warrants Fairclough, Bourdieu, Meindl. Treats
  a specific register (leadership discourse) as performed. Narrower than
  Goffman.
- `the_done_thing` — warrants DiMaggio & Powell's Institutional
  Isomorphism. Treats form-copying as a legitimacy move. Adjacent, not
  identical.

None of these stage the core Goffman claim: that the front stage / back
stage split is the universal structure of social interaction, not a
deviation from it. The trap that follows from that claim is also not staged:
the locally rational decision to present a processed version of oneself
costs the group the raw signal it actually needs, with no villain required.

## The Candidate

**Canonical name:** Impression Management (Goffman, 1959)

**Aliases:** dramaturgical theory; face-work; front stage / back stage;
self-presentation; the performance of self

**Leading source:** Goffman, Erving. The Presentation of Self in Everyday
Life. Anchor Books, 1959.

**Secondary sources (for the per-misfit REFERENCE.md):**
- Goffman, Erving. Interaction Ritual. 1967. (face-work mechanics)
- Hochschild, Arlie. The Managed Heart. 1983. (emotional labour as
  performed affect; the cost of sustained front-stage work)
- Leary, Mark R., and Robin M. Kowalski. "Impression Management: A
  Literature Review and Two-Component Model." Psychological Bulletin,
  1990. (empirical spine)
- Baumeister, Roy F. "A Self-Presentational View of Social Phenomena."
  Psychological Bulletin, 1982. (self-presentation as motivated cognition)

**The trap in MISFITS terms:** In any group where legibility is rewarded,
individuals converge on presenting processed, coherent versions of
themselves. Each move is locally rational: processed signal reads as
competent; raw signal reads as noise. The aggregate outcome is a group
that shares only what has already been resolved, never what is still
burning. The raw information the group most needs never enters the room.
No villain. Just structure.

**Structural trap type:** collective action; candour and face

**Proposed house title (working):** The Front

**Distinctness notes:**
- `saving_face` stages the moment of threat-response. Impression management
  stages the baseline: performance runs before any threat appears.
- `the_leadership_script` stages a specific discourse register. Impression
  management stages the universal mechanism that all registers, including
  leadership, run on top of.
- `watermelon_reporting` (MUM Effect) stages the suppression of bad news
  upward. Impression management stages the suppression of unprocessed
  emotion in all directions, including peer and self.
