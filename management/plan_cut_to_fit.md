# Plan: Cut to Fit

## Taxonomy

A **standard**: the shape a misfit takes on the page. The Playwright holds it
whenever a misfit is authored or restaged, so the cast a play carries is the cast
the concept needs and no more.

## Owner

- Owner: [The Playwright](position_playwright.md)

## Direction

One misfit is one **play**, and the play is the only fixed element. Everything
below it: the personas, the positions, the plans, the places, the processes, the
pieces, the plots ... is **cut to fit** the concept it stages, one element per
load-bearing vector and no other. A misfit is a mechanism, and a mechanism has
the moving parts it has: the tragedy of the commons and Braess's paradox do not
carry the same count, so their casts must not either. A fixed mould (seven
personas, seven positions, eight pieces, the same every time) is a tell that the
shape was copied, not read from the source. The house refuses the mould: the
warrant names the vectors, and the cast is exactly those vectors made flesh.

## Orders

Read the misfit's warrant first, list its load-bearing vectors, then cast one
element per vector: no filler seat, no piece that carries nothing, no plot that
does not turn. Fluid is not loose: **wire what you name.** Every element the play
lists in its Company must resolve, and every persona keeps its required section
and link, so a smaller cast is a tighter one, never a broken one. When restaging
an existing misfit, cut the elements that carry no vector and keep the play's
Company list true to what remains. Hold a floor so a cut does not hollow the
drama: at least three personas, and at least one plot for each beat the concept
turns on. The count is the concept's to set, inside that floor, never a quota to
fill.

## Implementation

The misfits under `misfits/**` and the warrant (`REFERENCE.md`) that names each
one's vectors. The play is the anchor and stays one per misfit; the pitch stays
the single hook. The judgement is the Playwright's and is reviewed, not gated by
count: the kit checks that what is named is wired, and the reading checks that
what is named is load-bearing.

## Targets

- [ ] each misfit carries exactly one play and one pitch
- [ ] every other element traces to a named vector in the warrant; no filler
- [ ] every element the play lists in its Company resolves, and every persona is wired
- [ ] the floor holds: at least three personas, at least one plot per beat
- [ ] element counts read from the concept, not copied from a sibling misfit
