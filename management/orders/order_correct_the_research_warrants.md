# Order: Correct the Research Warrants

## Direction

The house credits its sources, never claims them, and never overstates them. A
fidelity review read all 101 warrants against the literature they cite. Ninety
held: real works, correct authors and venues, scope claims the sources support,
governing laws kept within the sources' own bounds. Eleven carried errors: eight
factual miscitations and three overstated governing laws (one warrant carried
both). This order corrects the eleven and records the review.

## Orders

Fix what the review found, per warrant, and leave the ninety that held. Each
correction stays a fix to existing content: the misfit's staging is untouched,
only its credit to the source is set right. This order rides the corrections and
closes when they land.

## Implementation

The eleven warrants (`misfits/*/REFERENCE.md`, with two play Arcs), corrected:

**Factual miscitations.**

- **the_abstraction_ladder**: "dead-level abstracting" recredited from Hayakawa
  to its coiner, Wendell Johnson (_People in Quandaries_, 1946); a Johnson row
  added; the Arc parenthetical fixed.
- **pournelles_iron_law**: the "iron cage" phrase (Weber's _Protestant Ethic_, 1905) dropped from the _Economy and Society_ row, which keeps the accurate
  permanent-apparatus point.
- **vetocracy**: Rauch's 1994 book named correctly as _Demosclerosis_;
  _Government's End_ dated to its 1999 revised edition.
- **the_success_trap**: the naming of the competency and success traps recredited
  from March (1991), which supplies the mechanism, to Levitt & March (1988) and
  Levinthal & March (1993); a naming row added.
- **fit_to_fail**: the foraging-effort model cited to the correct paper (Matsuda
  & Abrams, "Timid consumers," _Theoretical Population Biology_, 1994), not the
  differently-titled asymmetrical-competition paper in the wrong venue.
- **social_loafing**: the sucker effect recredited from Kerr & Bruun (1983,
  free-rider) to Kerr's own 1983 paper.
- **the_winning_bid**: the Bazerman & Samuelson jar auction scoped correctly, the
  average bid below true value while only the winning bid overpays.
- **the_balloon_effect**: the five forms of displacement recredited from Cornish
  & Clarke to Reppetto (1976); Reppetto's own flagged limits noted; Guerette &
  Bowers scoped as displacement the minority outcome, not "common."

**Overstated governing laws.**

- **the_frozen_middle**: the two-way damping law marked as the play's staging of
  one failure mode, not the source literature's finding, which shows the middle
  both drives and buffers change.
- **watermelon_reporting**: "overwhelmingly one-directional... not by error"
  corrected to the measured ~2:1 optimistic tendency, driven by incentives and
  cognitive error alike.
- **the_paper_trail**: the Tetlock scope and the governing law reframed around
  blame for outcome and deviation, not "process accountability," which the
  accountability literature finds improves judgement; the internal tension with
  the just-culture cure resolved.

The eighty-nine unchanged warrants held on the reading and are recorded as read.
