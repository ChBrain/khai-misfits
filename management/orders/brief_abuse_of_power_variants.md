# Brief: Abuse of Power, The Variants Worth Carrying

Prepared by the Theatre Manager, for the Playwright and the Choregos, under the order
[Brief Abuse of Power Variants](order_brief_abuse_of_power_variants.md). This maps the field and cuts a clean
edge. It does not author.

## The lane, in house terms

A misfit is a structural trap: locally rational behaviour summing to a globally bad outcome, warranted by
research, with a costly remedy and no free hero and, crucially, **no villain needed**. Abuse of power fights
that last clause, because it carries a wrongdoer by default. So the lane only works if we cut on a single edge:

> The house stages the **enabling structure**, never the perpetrator. The trap is the system's locally rational
> response to a power-holder, the protection, the deference, the silence, the dependence, that lets harm persist
> and recur. A bad actor may stand in the frame (as the bounty-farmer stands in The Cobra Effect), but the
> misfit is the structure that keeps him, not the portrait of him.

This is the difference between a reusable misfit and a page from an ethics glossary. "A cruel boss is cruel" is
wrongdoing, not a trap. "A productive-but-cruel boss is kept because the output justifies the cost his
subordinates pay" is a trap: an externality borne by the powerless, rational for everyone deciding, ruinous in
aggregate. Every candidate below is judged on whether it can be cut to the second shape.

## The four gates

Each candidate is tested against the order's gates:

1. **Footing**: a stable research or documented-practitioner tradition to warrant it.
2. **Pattern**: a distinct causal mechanism, not merely a symptom or a mood.
3. **Stageable**: enough recurring roles, places, and processes to cast a play.
4. **Scope**: it belongs in a structural-trap house, not a general ethics glossary; it can be cut to the
   enabling structure with no villain load-bearing.

And one house gate on top, using the concordance: **distinct** against the sixty-seven staged misfits and the
current slate, deduped by canonical name in `REFERENCES.md`.

## The families

The fifteen candidates cluster into four research families.

### Family A: Power inside the legitimate lever

Power hiding within a sanctioned instrument (reward, sanction, measurement). Anchor: French & Raven's bases of
power (1959); procedural justice (Leventhal; Tyler).

- **The Reward Gatekeeper**: control of rewards and promotions used as leverage, so people serve the gatekeeper
  rather than the work.
- **The Coercive Performance Plan**: the improvement plan run not to develop but to manufacture an exit or
  extract compliance, a legitimate tool inverted.
- **The Metrics Enforcer**: power concealed inside ostensibly neutral numbers, whoever sets the measure rules
  the measured.

### Family B: The silencing of voice

The corrective signal suppressed, so error runs uncorrected. Anchor: Hirschman's exit-voice-loyalty (1970);
whistleblowing and retaliation (Near & Miceli; Rehg et al.); procedural justice.

- **The Retaliation Spiral**: each act of retaliation against a complainer is locally rational and chills the
  next complaint, until no one speaks and the harm compounds unseen.
- **The Psychological Hostage**: dependence (visa, vesting, sunk identity) blocks exit, so the trapped must
  absorb what they cannot leave or report.
- **The Credibility Trap**: the powerful cannot admit the early error without losing standing, so they double
  down and bury it, escalation dressed as resolve.

### Family C: The emergent abusive climate

No single villain, a normalised culture. Anchor: workplace bullying and mobbing (Leymann; Einarsen); abusive
supervision (Tepper, 2000); toxic leadership.

- **The Bullying Climate**: bullying tolerated becomes bullying normalised, each bystander rationally silent,
  each target unable to leave, the climate authored by no one and owned by no one.
- **The Tolerated Tyrant** (recast of "The Abusive Supervisor"): the abusive-but-productive manager kept because
  the numbers cover the cost, an externality on subordinates the org never books.
- **The Gaslighting Manager**: manipulation that makes a target doubt their own perception.

### Family D: Protection and the failure of oversight

The watch that will not watch, the shield around the harm. Anchor: governance failure and oversight capture;
institutional betrayal (Freyd); the "missing stair".

- **The Silent Board**: the oversight body defers, each director rationally quiet on information asymmetry and
  collegiality, so the check that exists on paper never fires.
- **The Protected Predator**: the institution shields a serial harm-doer because exposure is costlier than
  concealment, and the protection is what lets the harm recur.
- **The Toxic Protector**: the patron who shields a favoured subordinate from accountability, buying loyalty at
  the cost of the team.
- **The Policy Shield**: policy and process invoked as cover, "just following the rules", so accountability
  finds no one.
- **The Strong Boss**: a dominant leader whose presence suppresses dissent.
- **The Hostile Micromanager**: control exercised as constant intrusion.

## Shortlist (fit for development)

Ten carry, in rough order of readiness: **The Retaliation Spiral, The Silent Board, The Bullying Climate, The
Protected Predator, The Reward Gatekeeper, The Tolerated Tyrant, The Coercive Performance Plan, The
Psychological Hostage, The Credibility Trap, The Toxic Protector.** The last four carry overlap flags against
staged or slated misfits (noted in the table) and must clear the `REFERENCES.md` dedup before slating: The
Psychological Hostage against the slated **Loyalty Trap** (Hirschman), The Credibility Trap against **Good Money
After Bad** and **Saving Face**, The Coercive Performance Plan and The Policy Shield against **The Paper Trail**,
The Toxic Protector against The Protected Predator.

## The first three pilots

Chosen for cleanest no-villain cut, strongest evidence, highest staging value, and least risk of reading as
true-crime rather than structure.

1. **The Retaliation Spiral.** Each retaliation is locally rational (protect the unit, the reputation, the
   quarter) and its true cost is the complaint never made next time, so voice dies and harm compounds unseen.
   Strong footing (whistleblowing and retaliation literature). Distinct from **Watermelon Reporting** (passive
   upward filtering) and the slated **Loyalty Trap** (loyalty dampening voice at the source): here voice is
   actively punished, and the deterrent, not the reticence, is the engine. Rich cast: the complainer, the
   retaliator, the bystanders who read the signal, the silence that follows.

2. **The Silent Board.** The body built to check power defers instead, each director rational to stay quiet
   given information asymmetry, collegiality, and the reputational cost of the lone dissent, so the oversight
   that exists on paper never fires. Strong footing (governance failure, oversight capture). Distinct from
   **Closing Ranks** (a cohesive in-group repelling doubt) in that the failure is a principal that will not
   discipline its agent, and from **Vetocracy** (too many blockers) in that the problem is too few. Rich cast:
   board, executive, the dissenter who folds, the shareholders in the empty chair.

3. **The Bullying Climate.** Bullying tolerated once becomes the standard, bystanders rationally silent, targets
   unable to exit, so a culture of harm is authored by no one and sustained by everyone. Strong footing
   (mobbing and workplace-bullying research). Distinct from **The New Normal** (a drifting baseline of the
   acceptable in general) in that the specific engine is the bystander's rational silence plus the target's
   blocked exit, and it is the clearest demonstration that the lane can be staged with the villain removed and
   the structure left standing.

## Ruled out (at least three)

- **The Metrics Enforcer**: duplicative. This is **Metric Fixation** already staged, wearing a power hat; the
  power angle is a reading of that misfit, not a new one.
- **The Gaslighting Manager**: too moralised and thinly evidenced. Gaslighting is intentional manipulation
  centred on a wrongdoer; strip the villain and little structure remains. Belongs in the ethics glossary the
  scope gate excludes.
- **The Strong Boss**: too broad. It collapses into groupthink and **Closing Ranks**; "dominant leader,
  deferential room" is a mood, not a distinct causal pattern.
- **The Hostile Micromanager**: too narrow. It is a symptom of abusive supervision, not a trap in its own right,
  and cannot field a distinct cast.

## Recommendation

**Open the standing plan, but gated, and only after the pilots prove the cut.** The lane is real and
well-warranted, and the enabling-structure edge lets the house carry a charged subject without moralising, but
it is the house's highest-risk lane precisely because the no-villain discipline is hardest to hold here. So:

1. Author the three pilots first, in their own `misfit/` lanes, each deduped in `REFERENCES.md` and cut so the
   villain carries no load. Treat them as the proof that the framing holds.
2. If they land clean, open a standing plan (an "Abuse of Power" cluster, or a themed tier inside **Fill the
   Season**) whose admission gate is explicit and stricter than the general one: structural-not-perpetrator,
   deduped by canonical name, and cast so that removing the bad actor leaves the trap standing. Any candidate
   that cannot pass that last test is glossary, not house.
3. If the pilots read as portraits rather than structures, hold the lane closed and keep only what folds into
   existing misfits.

The edge to cut on, for the Playwright: **stage the room that keeps the harm, never the hand that does it.**
