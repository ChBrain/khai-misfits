# Order: Recut the Stamped Wave

## Direction

The mould is back. [Adopt Cut to Fit](order_adopt_cut_to_fit.md) retired the
uniform cast as copied, not read from the source; the standing plan holds that
the count is the concept's to set. Yet the house now carries 101 misfits in only
18 cast shapes, and a single shape (four personas, four positions, four plans,
three places, four processes, six pieces, five plots) covers 32 of them,
including every misfit of the latest authoring wave. Eighty plays carry exactly
five plots; seventy-two carry exactly four processes. The casts are honest in
content: no seat, name, or plot is copied from a sibling, and every element is
wired. The tell is in the counts, and the standing plan names a fixed count as
the tell that the shape was stamped, not read.

## Orders

Name the stamped wave and point the Playwright at it. This order records the
finding and assigns the review; it closes when the finding is on the record,
and the reading runs on under the standing plan, not under this order. The
review it stands up: for each misfit sharing the dominant shape, computed from
the cast counts, not chosen, read the warrant's load-bearing vectors and judge
whether the cast carries the concept's own count or the sibling's. Where the
count was stamped, recut under [Cut to Fit](../plan_cut_to_fit.md); where the
concept genuinely turns on four vectors, record that the reading held and leave
the cast alone. A recut is a restaging: it lands in its own `misfit/` lane under
the standing plan, an order riding that change, never this one reopened.

## Implementation

The review list is computed: the misfits whose cast counts match the dominant
signature, 32 at the time of writing. The warrant (`REFERENCE.md`) is the
measure; the vectors named there set the count, and the kit keeps checking that
what is named is wired. Two narrower norms are read wherever the review passes,
never inherited: the five-plot norm (80 of 101) and the four-process norm (72 of
101). One housekeeping seam rides the same review: the warrant of
`watermelon_reporting` traces four of its pieces only as a group ("the fragment
pieces"); the review names them per element.
