# Order: Brief Abuse of Power Variants

## Direction

The house will consider an abuse of power lane as a briefing order, not yet as a
committed build lane. The subject is broad and morally charged, and it sits at an
angle to the house ethic: a misfit is a structural trap where locally rational
behaviour sums to a globally bad outcome with no villain needed, and abuse of
power carries a wrongdoer by default. Before the house cuts any play, it needs a
sharp map of the variants worth carrying, each tied to a research tradition and
each cut so that the trap is the enabling structure, not the portrait of the bad
actor. This order commissions that map. It does not author the misfits.

## Orders

Produce a house brief that surveys abuse of power as a family of patterns,
separates the generic wrongdoing from the reusable misfit shape, and ranks the
candidates by clarity, evidence, and staging value. Test each candidate against
four gates: a stable research footing; a distinct causal pattern, not just a
symptom; enough recurring roles, places, and processes to stage a play; and a
scope that belongs in this house rather than in a general ethics glossary. Dedup
every candidate against the house-wide `REFERENCES.md` by canonical name.

## Implementation

The briefing note [Abuse of Power: The Variants Worth Carrying](brief_abuse_of_power_variants.md),
owned in voice by [the Theatre Manager](../position_theatre_manager.md) and
prepared for [the Playwright](../position_playwright.md) and
[the Choregos](../position_choregos.md). The note defines the lane in house
terms, clusters the variants by research family, shortlists those fit for
development, names the first three pilots, rules out the ones too broad or too
moralised or too thinly cut, and recommends whether to open a standing plan. No
misfit content moves under this order: it maps the field, and any authoring lands
in its own `misfit/` lane afterward.

## Targets

- [x] a briefing note exists under the order's lane
- [x] ten to fifteen abuse of power variants are distinguished with clear research anchors
- [x] the variants are grouped into a small number of coherent families
- [x] the first three pilot candidates are named and justified
- [x] at least three variants are ruled out as too broad, duplicative, or weakly cut
- [x] the brief ends with a recommendation on whether the house opens a standing plan
